Meta Ads Setup
Murarrie Campaign

Click each step to check it off. Your progress saves automatically.

01
Create the Campaign
ads.facebook.com → Create → Guided creation
Go to Ads Manager and click + Create
Top left of the Ads Manager dashboard. Green button.
ads.facebook.com
Select objective: Leads
Do NOT select Sales or Awareness. Leads is the 4th option in the list. It unlocks Instant Forms and optimises delivery to people who submit details.
Campaign level → Objective
Name the campaign: Murarrie — Property Valuation — Apr 2026
Keep the naming consistent so you can identify it later in reporting.
Campaign level → Campaign name
Leave A/B test and Advantage campaign budget OFF
You will set budget at the ad set level instead. Click Next.
Campaign level → bottom of page
02
Ad Set — Conversion & Audience
Where leads come from and who sees the ad
Conversion location: Instant Forms
Click the "Instant Forms" tile — NOT Website. This is the native Facebook form. No landing page needed. Meta pre-fills name from the user's profile.
Ad set → Conversion → Conversion location
Performance goal: Maximise number of leads
This is the default when Leads objective is selected. Leave it.
Ad set → Conversion → Performance goal
Budget: $30 / day
Daily budget, not lifetime. Start here for 7 days then review cost per lead before adjusting.
Ad set → Budget & schedule
Location: Add postcodes 4172 4173 4174
4172 = Murarrie. 4173 = Tingalpa. 4174 = Hemmant. Same homeowner demographic, all within your territory. Delete the default country/state that Meta adds.
Ad set → Audience → Locations
Age: 38 to 65+
Homeowners skew older. Under 38 are renters or not yet in the sell cycle.
Ad set → Audience → Age
Placements: Manual → Stories & Reels only
Click "Manual placements". Deselect everything. Re-select: Facebook Stories, Instagram Stories, Facebook Reels, Instagram Reels. Your 9:16 video is built for full-screen — do not run it in Feed on this test.
Ad set → Placements
03
Create the Instant Form
The form people fill in without leaving Facebook
Click + Create form
In the Instant Forms section that appears after selecting Instant Forms as conversion location.
Ad set → Instant form → Create form
Form type: Higher intent
NOT "More volume". Higher intent adds a review step before submitting. Fewer completions but far better lead quality — they actually meant to submit.
Form → Form type
Intro headline: What is your Murarrie home worth?
Short and direct. This is the first thing they read inside the form.
Form → Intro
Questions: First name + Phone number (pre-filled)
These are pre-filled from the user's Facebook profile. They just review and confirm. This is why native forms convert so much better than website forms.
Form → Questions
Add custom question: What is your property address?
Click "+ Add question" → Short answer. This is not pre-filled — they type it. You get phone to call them AND address to door knock if needed.
Form → Questions → + Add question → Short answer
Privacy policy URL: https://danielgierach.com/privacy
Required by Meta. Paste the URL into the privacy policy field.
Form → Privacy
Thank you screen message: Thanks. Daniel will be in touch within 24 hours.
Sets expectations so they are not surprised when you call.
Form → Completion
Click Save form then click Next
Form → bottom right
04
Ad Creative
Upload the video and write the copy
Format: Single video
Select Single image or video. Then click the video option (not image).
Ad → Format
Upload video: murarrie-v5-meta-ad-stories-and-reels.mp4
This is in your Downloads folder. Click "Add media" → "Upload" → select the file. It is 9:16 format at 4K resolution.
Ad → Media → Add media
Add captions: click Auto-generate
85% of people watch without sound. After uploading, click the captions option and let Meta auto-generate them. Review and correct if needed.
Ad → Media → Captions
Primary text: Most Murarrie homeowners underestimate what their property is worth. Based on real sales in your street, find out where yours sits. Takes 30 seconds.
Keep it to 3 sentences. No em dashes, no exclamation marks.
Ad → Primary text
Headline: What is your Murarrie home worth?
Short. Direct. Suburb-specific. Appears below the video in some placements.
Ad → Headline
CTA button: Get Quote
From the dropdown, select "Get Quote". This is the button text that appears on the ad before someone taps to open the form.
Ad → Call to action
05
Notifications — Get Leads Instantly
So you can call within the hour
Publish the ad — click Publish
The campaign goes into review. Meta typically approves within 1–24 hours.
Bottom right of Ads Manager
Go to Meta Business Suite → Instant Forms → your form → Notifications
Add your email: daniel.gierach@raywhite.com. Toggle on "Email me when I get a new lead."
business.facebook.com → Instant Forms → Notifications
Download leads: Ads Manager → Campaign → Leads Centre or export CSV
Lead data does not appear in your email — email just notifies you. Go to Leads Centre to see name, phone, and address for each lead.
Ads Manager → your campaign → Leads Centre
Review after 7 days — check cost per lead
A good cost per lead for this type of campaign is $15–$50. Under $15 is strong. Over $80 means something needs adjusting — usually the audience or the creative.
Ads Manager → Campaign → Results column

Campaign is set up.

All steps complete. Your ad is either in review or live. Check the Leads Centre daily.
