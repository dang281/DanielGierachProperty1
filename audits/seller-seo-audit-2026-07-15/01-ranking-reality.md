## What this says

1. **Invisible on all 10 generic seller queries.** Content topically exists and is indexed for several of them. The gap is authority, not relevance.
2. **Three walls to climb:** portals (unbeatable head-on, join them instead), competing agency/agent sites (beatable, paulapearce.com.au proves a solo agent site can rank for advice queries), calculator publishers (own the fee/cost niche, long game).
3. **His own brand surface is contested:** LinkedIn beats his site for his name, his employer's domain beats him for his office name, and US namesakes crowd the rest. The entity work (Wikidata, consistent profiles, disambiguation) is not optional.
4. **Query 7 insight:** "what is my home worth" is interpreted by Google as a licensed-valuation service query. Do not chase it head-on; target "free property appraisal Brisbane" phrasing instead, which matches agent intent.
5. **External citation check:** searching mentions of danielgierach.com off-site returns only LinkedIn plus a domain-registration log and namesake pages. Citation density is effectively zero, which is also what AI assistants read when deciding whether to cite him.
