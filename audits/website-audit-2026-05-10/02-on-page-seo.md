# On-Page SEO Audit, danielgierach.com

Date: 2026-05-10
Scope: page titles, meta descriptions, heading hierarchy (H1 uniqueness, H2/H3 structure), internal linking, keyword targeting for Brisbane inner east + south suburbs, image alt text, Open Graph and Twitter tags, breadcrumbs, keyword cannibalisation.
Source of truth: source files under `/src/pages/`, layouts under `/src/layouts/`, and the built output under `/dist/` (generated by `astro build`, 729 HTML pages).

## Summary

The fundamentals are mostly clean. Every page has a unique title (725 of 726 indexable pages) and a meta description (725 of 726), every page has exactly one H1 (725 of 726, the lone exception is the Search Console verification HTML), no real content image is missing alt text, OG and Twitter tags render on every page, breadcrumbs and BreadcrumbList JSON-LD render on every multi-segment URL, and the canonical URL helper in `Layout.astro` is consistent.

There are three substantial issues that are quietly suppressing SERP performance and a long tail of smaller cleanup items. First, internal linking is materially under-built: 414 of 574 insight articles (72.1%) contain zero in-body links to other insights, suburbs, tools, or resources, which is the single largest on-page opportunity for a site with 716+ pages and a TRAFFIC growth goal. Second, 91.5% of insight titles exceed 70 characters and will be truncated in Google's SERP, mostly because every article carries the 25-character "| Daniel Gierach Property" suffix on top of an already long headline. Third, 52 pairs of insight articles share keyword intent at Jaccard similarity ≥ 0.7 (with two pairs literally identical after normalisation, including one pair where both build to the same SERP title), creating cannibalisation that will throttle which version Google chooses to rank.

Smaller issues: title and H1 templates are inconsistent across the 47 suburb pages (three different title patterns, all with the same H1 pattern), all 56 tool pages title with "| Dan's Website" rather than "| Daniel Gierach Property" (this finding is also covered in TSEO-007 from a technical angle, included here for the on-page SEO impact), 716 of 729 pages share a single OG image with no per-page imagery, and 154 meta descriptions exceed 200 characters and will be truncated.

Nothing is critical to fix immediately, but addressing the internal linking and cannibalisation findings is the highest leverage available for organic traffic growth.

## Detail per finding

### OSEO-001, 72.1% of insight articles have zero in-body internal links (P0)

Evidence:
- Built output scan of 574 insight pages under `/Users/danielgierach/DanielGierachProperty/dist/insights/` extracting `<a href>` tags inside `<main>...</main>`:
  - 414 articles (72.1%) contain zero links to `/insights/*`, `/suburbs/*`, `/tools/*`, or `/resources/*` in body content.
  - 418 articles (72.8%) link to no other insight article from the body.
  - 525 articles (91.5%) link to no suburb page from the body.
  - 445 articles (77.5%) link to no tool from the body.
- Concrete example: `/Users/danielgierach/DanielGierachProperty/dist/insights/when-to-reduce-asking-price-brisbane/index.html` `<main>` contains exactly 2 hrefs: `/insights` (back link) and `/contact` (CTA box). No body links to any related guide, suburb, or tool.
- The source file `/Users/danielgierach/DanielGierachProperty/src/pages/insights/when-to-reduce-asking-price-brisbane.astro` has no internal-link anchors in any body paragraph (lines 30-62 are pure prose).

What is wrong: With 574 insight articles, 47 suburb pages, 56 tools, and 26 resources covering the same Brisbane inner east property topic universe, in-body cross-links should be the connective tissue carrying topical authority around the site. Currently most articles are topical islands. The Layout component does inject 1 `RelatedArticles` widget (3 links) after `</main>` and a footer-only `Suburb → Insight` cross-link block on `selling-in-*` slugs, but neither of these is in the article body where contextual linking matters most for topical signals.

Why it matters: For a TRAFFIC-led site, in-body internal links are the highest-leverage on-page lever after content quality. Each link tells Google "this article is part of a topic cluster about X" and passes equity to the linked URL. Articles with no internal links sit at the bottom of the equity distribution and rank slower. With 414 orphans of this kind, the long tail will index slowly and rank poorly even when content quality is strong. The site has the topology to be a definitive Brisbane inner east hub but is not signalling it.

Recommended fix:
1. Build a small post-processing or generation step that, for every insight article, identifies 3 to 5 contextually relevant other insights (by topic cluster), 1 to 2 relevant suburb pages (where the article mentions a suburb name), and 1 to 2 relevant tools (where the article mentions a calculation), and inserts those links into the body prose at natural moments.
2. Lower-effort interim: extend the Layout component to inject an "in-this-guide" block at the top of the article body and a "next steps: try the X tool, read the Y guide" block before the existing CTA box, both populated from a per-article links map.
3. Lowest-effort: after the existing footer email-capture block on insights, add a "Related tools and suburbs" section with 6 to 9 contextually-chosen links, populated by the same topic-cluster logic.

This is the single highest-leverage on-page SEO fix on the site.

### OSEO-002, 91.5% of insight titles exceed Google's display threshold (P1)

Evidence:
- Title length distribution across 725 indexable pages (built HTML, after HTML-entity decode):
  - <30 chars: 2
  - 30-49: 62
  - 50-60 (sweet spot): 73
  - 61-70: 39
  - >70: 549
- Insights only (574 articles): 525 titles exceed 70 chars (91.5%); 556 exceed 60 chars (96.9%).
- Longest title (155 chars): "Marketing Budget Allocation for a Brisbane Property Campaign: How to Split Spend Across Photography, Portals, Signage and Digital | Daniel Gierach Property" at `/Users/danielgierach/DanielGierachProperty/src/pages/insights/marketing-budget-allocation-brisbane-property-campaign-photography-portals-signage-digital.astro`.
- The brand suffix "| Daniel Gierach Property" is 25 characters. Even with a focused 50-character headline, the suffix pushes most titles to 75+ chars.

What is wrong: Google's SERP truncates titles at roughly 580px (around 60 to 65 characters depending on character widths). 549 of 725 pages (75.7%) will be displayed truncated, hiding the brand suffix and often hiding meaningful keywords from the right side of the title.

Why it matters: Truncated titles reduce CTR and can cost a position equivalent on borderline rankings. They also reduce the brand reinforcement in the SERP. With 175 currently-indexable insight pages and 400 more articles coming out of draft, fixing the template once is high leverage.

Recommended fix:
1. Drop the brand suffix from insight article titles. The `<title>` already gets brand reinforcement via the `og:site_name`, the global `Organization` schema, and the existing 558-of-574 consistency suggesting a template change is feasible.
2. Where possible, tighten headlines themselves. Several articles have long descriptive subtitles after the colon; consider moving those to the meta description and keeping the title under 60 chars.
3. Concrete change: in `Layout.astro` (or in a new `InsightLayout.astro` if separation is preferred), set the `<title>` directly from a `title` prop that does not append a brand. Update the per-article `title=` props to be the headline only (no "| Daniel Gierach Property" suffix).
4. Audit candidate (one-time): Run length check after change; flag any remaining title >65 chars for headline rewrite.

Worked example, before/after:
- Before: `Selling a Brisbane Property Mid-Renovation: Incomplete Works, Half-Finished Bathrooms, and the Finish-or-Sell Decision 2028 | Daniel Gierach Property` (149 chars)
- After: `Selling a Brisbane Property Mid-Renovation` (43 chars), with the rest moved to the description.

### OSEO-003, 52 pairs of cannibalising insight slugs (P0)

Evidence (slug Jaccard similarity ≥ 0.7, after singular/plural normalisation):
- Jaccard 1.00 (identical word set):
  - `selling-tenanted-property-queensland` vs `tenanted-property-selling-queensland`
  - `auction-vs-private-treaty-brisbane-seller` vs `auction-vs-private-treaty-brisbane-sellers` (the example called out in the audit brief)
- Jaccard 0.88:
  - `queensland-contract-of-sale-key-clauses-seller` vs `queensland-contract-of-sale-key-clauses-seller-guide`
  - `how-to-price-property-for-sale-brisbane` vs `how-to-price-your-property-for-sale-brisbane`
  - `how-to-choose-a-real-estate-agent` vs `how-to-choose-a-real-estate-agent-brisbane`
  - `2032-olympics-impact-inner-east-brisbane-property-values` vs `brisbane-2032-olympics-property-values-inner-east`
- Jaccard 0.86:
  - `when-to-reduce-asking-price-brisbane` vs `when-to-reduce-asking-price-brisbane-seller`
  - `when-to-reduce-asking-price-brisbane` vs `when-to-reduce-asking-price-brisbane-property`
  - `negative-gearing-selling-investment-property-brisbane` vs `negative-gearing-selling-investment-property-cgt-brisbane`
- 43 more pairs at 0.71 ≤ Jaccard < 0.86, including `cooling-off-period-property-queensland` vs `cooling-off-period-property-sale-queensland`, `selling-deceased-estate-queensland` vs `selling-deceased-estate-queensland-executor`, `gst-property-sale-queensland` vs `gst-property-sales-queensland-seller`, `selling-property-during-separation-divorce-queensland` vs `selling-property-separation-divorce-queensland`, `selling-queenslander-character-home-brisbane` vs `selling-queenslander-home-brisbane`, `pre-auction-offers-brisbane` vs `pre-auction-offers-brisbane-sellers`, `caveat-property-queensland-selling` vs `selling-property-caveat-brisbane-queensland`.
- Worst case: `negative-gearing-property-sale-brisbane` and `negative-gearing-selling-investment-property-brisbane` produce the identical `<title>` "Negative Gearing and Selling Your Investment Property in Brisbane | Daniel Gierach Property".
- Second worst: `how-to-choose-a-real-estate-agent` outputs `<title>What Does a Real Estate Agent Actually Do for You? | Daniel Gierach Property` while `what-does-a-real-estate-agent-do` outputs the same string. The first article's filename and URL no longer match its title.

What is wrong: Two articles competing for the same query split link equity, dilute Google's confidence in either as the canonical answer, and frequently cause Google to rank the "wrong" one or rotate between them. With 52 such pairs, this is a structural problem, not isolated incidents.

Why it matters: Cannibalisation is one of the most common reasons why content-rich sites underperform their content investment. Resolving each pair into a single canonical answer (with the loser either deleted, 301-redirected, or merged) consolidates ranking signals.

Recommended fix:
- For each of the 52 pairs, decide which slug is the canonical and which is redundant. Heuristics: prefer the more specific keyword phrasing, prefer the shorter slug, prefer the version with more existing inbound links.
- For the resolved pair, either:
  1. Delete the loser file from `src/pages/insights/` and add a redirect in `vercel.json` from the old URL to the canonical.
  2. Or, if both have unique content, merge content into the canonical and delete the loser.
- Run a similarity scan after fix and confirm no Jaccard ≥ 0.7 pairs remain.
- Recommended starting points (zero-risk dedupes):
  - Delete `negative-gearing-property-sale-brisbane.astro` (longer-form duplicate exists at `negative-gearing-selling-investment-property-brisbane.astro`).
  - Either delete or rename `how-to-choose-a-real-estate-agent.astro`, since its title already says "What Does a Real Estate Agent Actually Do for You?" and a separate `what-does-a-real-estate-agent-do.astro` exists with the same title; the "how-to-choose-a-real-estate-agent" slug is misleading.
  - Decide on plural for `auction-vs-private-treaty-brisbane-seller(s)`; redirect the loser.

These three alone close the most damaging duplicates.
