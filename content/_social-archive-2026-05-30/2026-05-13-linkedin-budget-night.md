## Caption

The federal budget delivered last night removes negative gearing from established residential properties for new investors, effective 1 July 2027.

Any property held before 7:30pm AEST on 12 May 2026 is grandfathered. New builds remain eligible for negative gearing. Investors buying established homes from now on will not be able to claim losses against other income once the change takes effect.

The capital gains tax discount also changes. The 50 per cent discount for individuals, trusts, and partnerships is replaced with cost-base indexation and a 30 per cent minimum tax rate for assets held more than 12 months, applying from the same date.

Investors holding established homes are now weighing their timeline. Some may look to exit before 2027. That can put additional stock on the market over the next 12 to 18 months, which is worth watching for buyers who have been competing in tight price ranges.

For owner-occupiers and first home buyers, investor demand on established homes may soften. The government's Help to Buy expansion also widens access to shared equity, which could bring more buyers into the market at the entry level.

New builds are protected from the negative gearing changes, which is likely to redirect some investment demand toward new construction. Suburbs undergoing development in Brisbane's inner east could see investor activity shift in that direction.

This is a significant policy change. What it does to prices will depend on how quickly investors act and how much new supply enters the market in response.
