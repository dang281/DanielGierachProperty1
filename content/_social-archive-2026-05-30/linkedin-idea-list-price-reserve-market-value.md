## Caption

List price, reserve, and market value are three different numbers. Buyers and sellers who understand the difference make better decisions at every stage of a campaign.

Market value is what a willing buyer and a willing seller agree on in an open and competitive market. It is not what the vendor needs to achieve, not what they paid, and not what a neighbouring property sold for three years ago. It is a current number derived from current comparable sales in similar condition, configuration, and position.

A list price (or price guide in Queensland) is the number used to attract buyers to the campaign. In a well-run campaign, it is set to be honest and to attract the widest relevant buyer pool. An inflated list price attracts the wrong buyers or no buyers. A list price that accurately reflects the market brings the buyers who can actually transact.

A reserve at auction is the minimum the vendor will accept under the hammer. It is set before auction day and is not disclosed to bidders. Setting the reserve correctly is one of the most important decisions in an auction campaign. A reserve set too high passes the property in; a reserve set at market value allows the auction to find the price the market is prepared to pay.

These three numbers should be close to each other in a well-run campaign. When they diverge significantly, the campaign usually encounters problems.
