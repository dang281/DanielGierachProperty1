## Caption

A buyer's agent works exclusively for the buyer. Their job is to find, evaluate, and negotiate a property on behalf of someone who is either too time-poor, too unfamiliar with the market, or too emotionally invested to negotiate effectively themselves.

For buyers, the value proposition depends on the situation. People with minimal time to inspect properties and track the market closely are among the clearest beneficiaries. Interstate buyers purchasing in a market they do not know well benefit significantly. First-home buyers who have not been through a negotiation before often find the fee worthwhile. Experienced local buyers who have done several transactions may not need one.

A distinct subset of buyer's agents specialises in building investment portfolios rather than sourcing owner-occupied purchases. These agents work through investment costs, projected yields, long-term capital growth, and where infrastructure spending in Brisbane is creating underlying demand. They develop a portfolio strategy based on your desired financial outcomes and source properties that fit it. If that is the brief, a specialist buyer's agent is worth the conversation.

Most buyer's agents in Queensland charge a flat fee or a percentage of the purchase price. That fee needs to be weighed against what they actually achieve in negotiation and access.

For sellers, a buyer's agent on the other side changes how you read an offer. Their brief is to minimise the price their client pays. Knowing that, and factoring it into your negotiation approach, is useful information.

Buyer's agents do not have access to anything a diligent buyer could not find themselves. What they have is time, market experience, and emotional detachment. Whether those are worth the fee depends on the buyer.
