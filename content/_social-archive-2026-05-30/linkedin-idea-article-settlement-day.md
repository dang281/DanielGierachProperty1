## Caption

Settlement day has a reputation for being stressful. For most vendors and buyers in Queensland, the actual day is quiet. The preparation in the weeks before it is where the real work happens.

In Queensland, property settlements are handled digitally through PEXA. The buyer's lender, the vendor's lender, and the solicitors on both sides coordinate the transfer of funds and the registration of title electronically. Most vendors find out their settlement has completed when they receive a text message from their solicitor.

What does require attention before settlement day is the pre-settlement inspection. The buyer is entitled to one inspection, usually within a few days of settlement, to confirm the property is in the same condition as when the contract was signed. Removalists who damage walls or floors, missing inclusions, and fixtures that have been removed all create disputes at this stage. Vendors who walk through their own property the day before the buyer's inspection avoid most of these problems.

The funds sequence also matters. The vendor's discharge of mortgage needs to be coordinated in advance. Most major lenders require notice well before settlement day, not on the day itself.
