## section: transport

Ascot has its own railway station on the Doomben line. Live timetables at translink.com.au/plan-your-journey.

Bus routes serve the suburb via Racecourse Road, Nudgee Road, and Sandgate Road.

By car, direct Gateway Motorway access handles airport and M1 routes. Brisbane Airport sits approximately 8 to 12 minutes off-peak via the Gateway. The CBD is around 12 to 16 minutes off-peak.

For active transport, the Kingsford Smith Drive bikeway connects Ascot to the broader inner-north cycling network.

For full timetables, route maps, and live updates: [translink.com.au](https://translink.com.au)

## section: schools

Ascot State School is the local primary state school. Catchment boundaries should be confirmed through the Queensland Government's catchment lookup at [qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap](https://www.qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap).

## section: pockets

Ascot breaks into three pockets buyers consider distinctly.

The premium ridge streets include hilltop blocks with grand homes. This is the premium pocket and one of the most established premium markets in Brisbane.

The Racecourse Road village pocket sits close to the dining strip. Walkability is the proposition for this segment of the market.

The Nudgee Road area sits closer to the arterial. Different character and a different price profile.

## section: lifestyle

Racecourse Road is the village dining strip with cafes, restaurants, and bars. Portside Wharf at Hamilton is a short drive for additional dining and the river precinct.

Open space includes the Eagle Farm Racecourse precinct and Crosby Park.

The lifestyle here is established premium inner-north. Heritage character, the racecourse precinct, and the airport proximity define the suburb.

## section: buyer profile

Three buyer types drive the Ascot market.

The largest is premium upgraders moving up to one of Brisbane's most established premium suburbs.

The second group is interstate professionals, particularly from Sydney and Melbourne, drawn by airport proximity and the heritage character.

The third group is established families targeting the local primary catchment and the private school options.
