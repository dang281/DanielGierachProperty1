## section: transport

West End's closest station is South Brisbane, walkable for parts of the suburb. Live timetables at translink.com.au/plan-your-journey.

For ferry, the West End CityCat runs to the CBD and South Bank.

Bus routes serve West End via Boundary Street, Vulture Street, and Montague Road.

By car, the M3 corridor and CBD access via Victoria Bridge put the CBD around 5 to 9 minutes away off-peak.

For active transport, the Brisbane Riverwalk and the Goodwill Bridge to South Bank and CBD connect West End to the broader inner-south cycling and walking network.

For full timetables, route maps, and live updates: [translink.com.au](https://translink.com.au)

## section: schools

West End State School is the local primary state school. Catchment boundaries should be confirmed through the Queensland Government's catchment lookup at [qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap](https://www.qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap).

For secondary state schooling, parts of West End may fall within the Brisbane State High School catchment. BSHS access is one of the most-checked catchments by buyers.

For private schooling, Somerville House and St Laurence's College in South Brisbane are accessible within a short walk or drive.

⚠️ VERIFY: BSHS catchment access varies by street. Use the QGSO lookup to confirm.

## section: pockets

West End breaks into three pockets buyers consider distinctly.

The Boundary Street precinct includes streets close to the Boundary Street strip. Walkability is the proposition.

The riverside / Orleigh Park pocket includes streets close to the river and the parkland.

The Montague Road side is a mixed-use redevelopment area with apartment market.

## section: lifestyle

Boundary Street is the suburb's defining cultural and dining strip with cafes, restaurants, bars, and independent retail. South Bank cultural precinct sits across the river.

For open space, Davies Park, Orleigh Park, and the Brisbane Riverwalk provide walking and weekend-park options.

The lifestyle here is cultural inner-south with the Boundary Street feel and the river precinct on the doorstep.

## section: buyer profile

Three buyer types drive the West End market.

The largest is cultural-precinct buyers attracted to the West End cultural and dining feel.

The second group is BSHS catchment buyers where the catchment applies for the address.

The third group is inner-CBD professionals wanting walk-to-CBD and walk-to-South-Bank lifestyle.

## section: selling approach

Selling in West End means matching the campaign to the pocket and whether the BSHS catchment applies. Boundary Street pocket properties lead with walkability and the cultural feel. Catchment-access properties lead with BSHS.

On method, both auction and private treaty work depending on the property and the buyer pool.
