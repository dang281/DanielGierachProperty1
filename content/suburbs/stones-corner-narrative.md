## section: transport

Stones Corner's closest stations are Buranda and Coorparoo, walkable for parts of the suburb. The South-East Busway is on the doorstep with the Stones Corner busway station providing high-frequency CBD connection. Live timetables at translink.com.au/plan-your-journey.

Bus routes serve Stones Corner via Logan Road and the South-East Busway.

By car, the M3 corridor handles southern travel and the CBD sits around 6 to 10 minutes away off-peak.

For active transport, the South-East Busway corridor and the Norman Creek bikeway connect through the suburb.

For full timetables, route maps, and live updates: [translink.com.au](https://translink.com.au)

## section: pockets

Stones Corner breaks into two pockets.

The Logan Road village pocket includes apartments and townhouses close to the strip. Walkability is the proposition.

The Norman Creek parkland side includes streets close to the parkland with character home stock.

## section: lifestyle

Logan Road through Stones Corner is one of the busier village strips in the inner south with cafes, restaurants, and bars. The Stones Corner library and shopping precinct anchor daily life.

For open space, Hanlon Park and the Norman Creek parkland provide walking and weekend-park options.
