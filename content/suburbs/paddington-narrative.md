## section: transport

Paddington does not have its own railway station. Milton and Auchenflower are the closest stations, walkable for some southern Paddington streets. Live timetables at translink.com.au/plan-your-journey.

Bus routes serve Paddington via Latrobe Terrace, Given Terrace, and Caxton Street.

By car, the Inner City Bypass and Western Freeway access put the CBD around 5 to 9 minutes away off-peak.

For active transport, the Western Suburbs bikeway and walking access to the CBD via Caxton Street are practical for many addresses.

For full timetables, route maps, and live updates: [translink.com.au](https://translink.com.au)

## section: schools

Petrie Terrace State School and Rainworth State School serve different parts of Paddington as primary state catchments. Catchment boundaries should be confirmed through the Queensland Government's catchment lookup at [qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap](https://www.qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap).

For secondary state schooling, Kelvin Grove State College is the common catchment.

For private schooling, Brisbane Boys' College in Toowong and Marist College Ashgrove are both within a short drive.

## section: pockets

Paddington breaks into three pockets buyers consider distinctly.

The Latrobe Terrace village pocket includes streets close to the village strip with heritage character queenslanders. Premium pocket of the suburb.

The hilltop / view streets are elevated streets with city outlooks.

The Caxton Street side includes streets close to Caxton Street and Suncorp Stadium.

Paddington's topography is hilly, and many streets sit on ridges with city or western outlooks.

## section: lifestyle

Latrobe Terrace and Given Terrace form the suburb's village shopping and dining strip with cafes, restaurants, boutiques, and bars. Caxton Street has additional bars and dining.

For open space, the Suncorp Stadium precinct and local pocket parks provide the closest options.

The lifestyle here is heritage character inner-west with the village strip on the doorstep.

## section: buyer profile

Three buyer types drive the Paddington market.

The largest is inner-CBD professionals wanting walk-to-CBD or short-drive lifestyle with character home stock.

The second group is renovators. Heritage queenslander stock attracts buyers looking for renovation projects.

The third group is established families targeting the catchment and the village amenity.

## section: selling approach

Selling in Paddington means leaning on the heritage character and the village walkability. Photography that captures the period detail and the street character matters disproportionately.

On method, both auction and private treaty work depending on the property and the buyer pool.
