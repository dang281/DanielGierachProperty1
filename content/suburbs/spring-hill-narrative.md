## section: transport

Spring Hill is one of the most CBD-walkable suburbs in Brisbane. Central railway station is walkable from much of Spring Hill, and Roma Street railway station is walkable from western streets. Live timetables at translink.com.au/plan-your-journey.

Bus routes serve Spring Hill via Wickham Terrace, Boundary Street, and the inner-CBD network.

By car, the Inner City Bypass handles travel beyond the CBD. The CBD itself sits around 2 to 5 minutes away off-peak.

For active transport, the inner-CBD walking and cycling routes connect through the suburb.

For full timetables, route maps, and live updates: [translink.com.au](https://translink.com.au)

## section: pockets

Spring Hill breaks into two pockets.

The heritage character pocket includes streets with heritage queenslander and worker's cottage stock.

The apartment market includes apartment buildings close to the CBD.

## section: lifestyle

Spring Hill has cafes and small dining clusters along Boundary Street and Leichhardt Street. The CBD and Fortitude Valley dining are within walking distance.

For open space, Wickham Park and Centenary Place provide the closest options.

The lifestyle here is heritage CBD-adjacent. Walk-to-CBD is the central proposition.

## section: buyer profile

Three buyer types drive the Spring Hill market.

The largest is inner-CBD professionals wanting walk-to-CBD apartment and character home options.

The second group is renovators. Heritage character stock attracts renovation buyers.

The third group is apartment downsizers attracted to apartment-led downsizing with CBD walkability.

## section: selling approach
