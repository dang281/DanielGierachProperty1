## section: transport

Brisbane CBD is the centre of the Translink network. Central railway station and Roma Street railway station serve all train lines, the Cultural Centre, King George Square, and Queen Street stations are the main bus interchanges, and CityCat terminals run along the river. Live timetables at translink.com.au/plan-your-journey.

For driving, the Inner City Bypass, Pacific Motorway, and Story Bridge converge in the CBD. Parking is the limiting factor rather than road access.

For active transport, the Bicentennial Bikeway, the Riverwalk, and the Goodwill Bridge to South Bank make the CBD one of the most pedestrian and bike-friendly inner cores in Brisbane.

For full timetables, route maps, and live updates: [translink.com.au](https://translink.com.au)

## section: schools

Most CBD residential addresses are apartments. Catchment boundaries still apply and should be confirmed through the Queensland Government's catchment lookup at [qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap](https://www.qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap).

For private schooling, All Hallows' School and St James College both sit within or adjacent to the CBD.

⚠️ VERIFY: CBD apartment buildings can fall within different catchments depending on the address. Use the QGSO lookup for any specific building.

## section: pockets

The CBD breaks into three pockets buyers consider distinctly.

The riverside precinct includes apartment stock close to Eagle Street, Howard Smith Wharves, and the river. Premium positioning with the strongest views and the most contested buyer pool.

The Spring Hill side covers northern CBD streets bordering Spring Hill. Mixed-use stock with character building conversions alongside contemporary apartments.

The South Bank-adjacent pocket includes southern CBD streets close to the cultural precinct and the river crossings.

## section: lifestyle

Queen Street Mall is the central retail spine. Howard Smith Wharves, Eagle Street, and the Riverside precinct anchor the dining experience. The Cultural Precinct sits across the river at South Bank.

For open space, the City Botanic Gardens, King George Square, and Wickham Park provide green-space access within walking distance of most addresses.

The lifestyle here is dense urban inner-CBD. The trade-off for the convenience is density, and that trade-off works for the buyer profiles drawn to the suburb.

## section: buyer profile

Three buyer types drive the CBD market.

The largest is inner-CBD professionals wanting walk-to-work convenience and inner-CBD lifestyle.

The second group is downsizers. Long-term Brisbane residents moving to apartment living for the convenience and the lock-up-and-leave lifestyle.

The third group is investors. Strong rental demand from professionals and students drives consistent investor activity.

## section: selling approach

Selling in the CBD means recognising the apartment market dynamics and pricing the building, the floor, and the view rather than just the suburb.

Photography that captures the view line, the layout flow, and the building amenity is non-negotiable. Generic apartment photography costs the campaign at every price level.

On method, both auction and private treaty work for apartments depending on the building, the floor, and the buyer pool depth.
