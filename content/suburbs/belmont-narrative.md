## section: transport

Belmont does not have its own railway station. The closest stations are Cannon Hill and Manly West on the Cleveland line, both a drive rather than a walk for most addresses. Live timetables at translink.com.au/plan-your-journey.

Bus routes serve Belmont via Old Cleveland Road and Belmont Road.

By car, the Gateway Motorway access via Carindale or Manly handles airport and M1 routes. The CBD sits around 18 to 22 minutes away off-peak.

For active transport, the Tingalpa Creek parkland and walking tracks are accessible from much of the suburb.

For full timetables, route maps, and live updates: [translink.com.au](https://translink.com.au)

## section: schools

Belmont State School is the local primary state school. Catchment boundaries should be confirmed through the Queensland Government's catchment lookup at [qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap](https://www.qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap).

For secondary state schooling, catchment varies by street.

For private schooling, Citipointe Christian College in Carindale and Iona College in Wynnum North are both within a short drive.

## section: pockets

Belmont breaks into two main pockets.

Belmont Hill includes elevated streets with grand homes on larger blocks. Premium pocket and the part of the suburb most buyers think of first.

The Old Cleveland Road corridor sits closer to the arterial and offers a more accessible entry point with a different character.

Belmont's larger block sizes and topography are the suburb's defining features.

## section: lifestyle

Belmont itself has limited retail. Westfield Carindale is a short drive for big-format retail and dining. The Cannon Hill K-Mart Plaza handles daily-life groceries.

For open space, Belmont Hill Reserve and the Tingalpa Creek parkland provide walking and weekend-park options.

The lifestyle here is leafy outer-east. Larger blocks, established trees, and a quieter residential feel than the inner east suburbs to the west.

## section: buyer profile

Three buyer types drive the Belmont market.

The largest is family upgraders wanting larger blocks and the leafy outer-east family suburb feel.

The second group is private school families. Iona College and Citipointe access drive a portion of the buyer pool.

The third group is acreage-adjacent buyers. Belmont's larger block sizes attract buyers who want space without leaving the inner east corridor entirely.

## section: selling approach

Selling in Belmont means leaning on the block size and the topography. The suburb's value proposition is space and elevation, and the campaign should reflect that.

Photography that captures the block dimensions, the established trees, and the views from the elevated streets matters more than in tighter inner-suburbs where lifestyle access leads.

On method, both auction and private treaty work depending on the property and the buyer pool.
