## section: transport

Annerley is served by Fairfield railway station on the Beenleigh and Cleveland line corridor, with Yeronga station closer for some southern streets. Off-peak travel to Central is short. Live timetables at translink.com.au/plan-your-journey.

Bus routes serve Annerley via Ipswich Road and Annerley Road. Routes change with Translink service updates.

By car, the M3 corridor handles southern travel and the Captain Cook Bridge or Story Bridge route puts the CBD around 8 to 12 minutes away off-peak.

For active transport, the Norman Creek bikeway and Veloway 1 access are nearby for confident riders.

For full timetables, route maps, and live updates: [translink.com.au](https://translink.com.au)

## section: pockets

Annerley breaks into three pockets buyers consider distinctly.

## section: buyer profile

The second group is first home buyers. Annerley remains accessible relative to Highgate Hill and West End on comparable stock.

The third group is investors. Train station and arterial proximity supports rental demand.

## section: selling approach

Selling in Annerley means matching the campaign to the pocket and the stock type. The suburb's character home market suits family-led campaigns; the renewal stock close to Ipswich Road suits investor and first-home-buyer campaigns.
