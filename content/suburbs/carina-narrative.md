## section: transport

Carina does not have its own railway station. The closest stations are Cannon Hill and Coorparoo on the Cleveland line, both a drive for most Carina addresses. Live timetables at translink.com.au/plan-your-journey.

Bus routes serve Carina via Old Cleveland Road and Creek Road.

By car, the M3 corridor handles southern travel and the Story Bridge route puts the CBD around 13 to 17 minutes away off-peak.

For active transport, the Bulimba Creek bikeway and parkland provide walking and cycling connections.

For full timetables, route maps, and live updates: [translink.com.au](https://translink.com.au)

## section: schools

Carina State School is the local primary state school. Catchment boundaries should be confirmed through the Queensland Government's catchment lookup at [qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap](https://www.qgso.qld.gov.au/maps/edmap).

For secondary state schooling, Whites Hill State College serves much of Carina. Whites Hill catchment access is one of the central buyer-decision drivers in the area.

For private schooling, San Sisto College sits within Carina, and Citipointe Christian College in Carindale is within a short drive.

## section: pockets

Carina breaks into three pockets buyers consider distinctly.

The Whites Hill catchment streets are the premium pocket for the family-home market. Catchment access drives buyer interest.

The Pacific Golf Club area includes established residential streets with character home stock.

The Old Cleveland Road corridor sits closer to the arterial and offers a more accessible entry point.

## section: lifestyle

Carina has the Pacific Golf Club precinct nearby. Westfield Carindale is a short drive for big-format retail and dining. Camp Hill Marketplace handles village dining within a 5 to 10 minute drive.

For open space, the Bulimba Creek parkland and Whites Hill Reserve provide walking and weekend-park options.

The lifestyle here is family-suburb east Brisbane. Catchment, parks, and an accessible entry point relative to the inner east suburbs to the west.

## section: buyer profile

Three buyer types drive the Carina market.

The largest is family upgraders targeting the Whites Hill catchment and the family-suburb feel.

The second group is buyers priced out of Camp Hill or Coorparoo who want similar family-suburb access at a more accessible price.

The third group is renovators. Character home stock attracts buyers looking for renovation projects.

## section: selling approach

Selling in Carina comes down to identifying whether the property is in the Whites Hill catchment and building the campaign accordingly.

A catchment-access property runs the family-catchment campaign with school access and the suburb's family-home character leading.

An out-of-catchment property runs the value campaign with proximity to the inner-east suburbs and the Westfield Carindale precinct as the central proposition.

On method, both auction and private treaty work depending on the property and the buyer pool.
