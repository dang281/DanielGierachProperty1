# Brisbane Auction Clearance Rate April 2026: 60.9%

**Category:** market
**Relevance:** 8
**Post worthy:** yes
**Suburbs:** 
**Source:** Cotality / propertyupdate.com.au, week ending April 11 2026
**Published date:** 2026-04-16

Brisbane recorded a preliminary auction clearance rate of 60.9% for the week ending April 11, 2026, across 116 homes. This is down from the March 2026 average of 65.55% and February's peak of 70.4%. The trend shows the market softening in response to the two cash rate hikes (February and March, totalling +50bp to 4.10%) but remaining firmly in sellers' territory above the 60% threshold.

**Key data points:**
- Week of April 11: 60.9% clearance rate, 116 auctions (up from 96 auctions same period last year)
- March 2026 average: 65.55%
- February 2026 average: 70.4%
- ABS QLD residential property price index: +8.1% year-on-year
- Brisbane monthly price growth (March 2026): +1.8%

**Angle for future post:** The clearance rate drift from 70% to 61% over 8 weeks is the story the data tells — rate hikes are biting at the margins but not breaking demand. Sellers who acted in February and March got the peak competition. Sellers in April and May are still operating in a healthy market, just with slightly less urgency from buyers. This frames the "act now vs wait" conversation well.
