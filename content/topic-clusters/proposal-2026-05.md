## Notes

**Cluster 1 dominance.** Suburb guides make up 46 of 117 live articles (39%). The hub page will be a directory-style index, not a long-form article. Consider alphabetical or geographic grouping on the hub.

**Cluster 8 is broader than it first appears.** The Brisbane property types cluster mixes architectural history (Queenslanders, prewar) with construction process articles (engage-right-builder, build-process-with-architect). These serve different readers but share a property-knowledge angle. If the hub page gets large, a future split into "Brisbane Home Styles" and "Building and Renovating" is sensible once more live articles accumulate in the draft pipeline.

**Articles that span clusters:**

- `buyers-agent` sits in cluster 4 (Marketing and Selling Methods) but could equally sit in cluster 5 (Legal/Contracts), as buyers agents influence the negotiation and due-diligence phase. Placed in cluster 4 because the primary reader question is "should I use a buyers agent" which is a campaign-phase decision.
- `first-home-buyer-help-history-and-current-options` sits in cluster 6 (Costs/Taxes) because stamp duty concessions and FHOG grants are its core content, but it is partly a buyer-audience article. Placed in cluster 6 because sellers commonly need to understand first-home buyer eligibility when assessing their buyer pool.
- `flood-mapping` sits in cluster 9 (Inner East Market) but contains disclosure-relevant content that overlaps with cluster 5 (Contracts/Legal). Placed in cluster 9 because its primary angle is understanding the Brisbane inner east property landscape, not the contract process.
- `land-tax-queensland-investment-property` sits in cluster 7 (Investment Properties) rather than cluster 6 (Costs/Taxes) because the tax applies specifically to investors and the reader context is "should I sell or hold."

**Clusters at the minimum threshold.** Clusters 6 and 7 each have exactly 6 articles. They justify a hub page but have limited depth. Prioritise staging 2 to 3 articles from the draft pipeline into each of these clusters before building hub pages, so the hub has room to grow after launch.

**Draft pipeline watch.** Several high-volume draft topics, including body corporate records, auction reserve prices, and CGT partial exemptions, align strongly with clusters 5, 6 and 7. As those drafts publish, the cluster balance will shift.
