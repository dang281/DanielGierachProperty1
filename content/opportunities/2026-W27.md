Equal lift for organic search because Google rewards low-bounce, multi-page sessions.

## 3. Site has zero internal search across 163 suburb pages, 773 insights, and 61 tools

**Category:** lead-gen
**Priority:** medium
**Why it matters:** A visitor arriving from a Facebook share asking "do they cover Tarragindi?" has to either guess the URL (`/suburbs/tarragindi`), find Tarragindi in a 163-row suburb list, or open the browser find dialog. A visitor looking for "pool safety" or "negative gearing" has to scroll the 773-article insights index. There is no search box anywhere on the site. Pagefind is a static-site search library that generates a search index at build time, runs entirely client-side (zero server dependency), works with Astro, and costs nothing to operate. It is purpose-built for exactly this scale of content site. Without search, the cost of finding any specific suburb or topic is too high, and the user defaults to bouncing back to Google rather than staying on-site, which kills the depth metrics that compound into rankings.
**Expected impact:** Adding Pagefind takes 2-3 focused hours including styling. Search adoption on content sites of this scale typically hits 8-15% of sessions. At even 8% adoption with a 60% better find rate vs scrolling, that is a meaningful reduction in dead-end sessions. Adds a real differentiator vs every other Brisbane agent site, none of which (verified Place, Ray White New Farm, McGrath, Belle) have content search.
**Next action:** Daniel to install Pagefind (`npm install pagefind`), add a `pagefind --site dist` step to the build, and drop a search input into the site header. Astro integration guide at pagefind.app. Self-contained, reversible, no API keys.

## 4. The site has no email capture anywhere: every visitor is a one-shot

**Category:** lead-gen
**Priority:** medium
**Why it matters:** A user can read an entire insights article, browse three suburb pages, use the commute calculator, and leave without ever giving the site an email address. There is no newsletter signup, no "monthly inner east market update" capture, no lead magnet, no triggered-popup, no footer subscribe field. The site builds organic search traffic well, ranks for real keywords, and serves serious content, but treats every visitor as a one-shot conversation. The Walkthrough and Buyers forms capture high-intent visitors at the bottom of the funnel; there is nothing to capture the much larger pool of mid-funnel visitors who are researching but not yet ready for a walkthrough. An owned email list compounds: an inner east seller who subscribes in July may not list until February but they remember the source if the monthly email arrives consistently in their inbox.
**Expected impact:** A single "Monthly inner east market update" subscribe field added to the footer and the bottom of every insights article, wired to a simple service (Buttondown $9/mo, MailerLite free up to 1000 subs, or a Formspree-to-CSV pipeline that Daniel exports manually), should capture 0.5 to 1.5% of organic visitors over time. At current site traffic that is a 30-60 net-new email subscribers per month, growing to a 500-1000 contact list by end of 2026. The list becomes a distribution channel for any future content drop, new listing, or open home invitation.
**Next action:** Daniel to decide on the service first (MailerLite free tier is the lowest-friction starting point). Then add a footer subscribe field and a "Get the monthly market update" block below the RelatedArticles component on insights articles. Single afternoon of work. Direction will sharpen the next 6 months of content strategy: every article ends with a capture, not a dead end.
