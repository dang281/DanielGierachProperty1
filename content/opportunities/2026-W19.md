# Growth Opportunities: Week 2026-W19

**Week start:** 2026-05-04

## 1. 506 insights articles, zero email capture

**Category:** lead-gen
**Priority:** high
**Why it matters:** The site now has 506 published seller-focused insights articles driving organic search traffic from Brisbane vendors at every stage of the decision. The only conversion path on those pages is the appraisal/walkthrough CTA, which only captures the small fraction ready to book a call today. A vendor researching "how to handle a four-week stalled campaign" or "selling with leased solar panels" is doing pre-decision homework. They will not book a walkthrough yet, but they are exactly the audience worth nurturing for the 6 to 12 weeks before they list. With no email capture anywhere on the site (confirmed: `newsletter|subscribe` appears only in `privacy.astro`), 100% of that pre-decision traffic exits without a way to be re-engaged.
**Expected impact:** A simple "Brisbane seller insights, one short email a fortnight" capture on the bottom of insights articles, plus a sidebar/inline placement on the index, would realistically convert 1 to 3% of insights traffic. Even at modest article traffic that compounds into a list of hundreds of warm vendors over the next 12 months. Each subscriber is a soft touchpoint Daniel controls, independent of Google ranking shifts or social platform reach.
**Next action:** Pick a managed email tool that can be embedded with a single form (ConvertKit, Buttondown, Beehiiv, or Resend Audiences), then ask the SEO & Content Agent to add a single shared `<NewsletterCTA />` component below the article body and on `/insights/index.astro`. One config decision, one component, broad coverage.

## 2. Insights articles have no author bio: E-E-A-T signal left on the table

**Category:** seo
**Priority:** medium
**Why it matters:** Google's E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust) framework explicitly rewards content that visibly demonstrates first-hand experience. Daniel's competitive advantage is exactly that: an actively-licensed Brisbane inner east agent writing from real transactions. Spot-checking `agent-fees-commission-brisbane.astro` confirms there is no author byline, no agent credentials, and no link to Daniel's bio at the article footer. To Google's quality algorithms (and to a vendor doing due diligence on whether the advice is trustworthy), these articles read as anonymous content. The competitor sites that outrank on commercial seller queries (realestate.com.au, Domain editorial, Smart Property Investment) all have visible author cards and credentials.
**Expected impact:** Adding a consistent author footer block (photo, name, REIQ licence number, "active Brisbane inner east agent since YYYY", link to /about) across all 506 articles materially strengthens the E-E-A-T signal in a single change. This is the kind of edit that pays off over months as Google re-crawls and re-evaluates. Past audits of similar additions on niche sites have shown 10 to 25% lift in long-tail commercial query positions over 60 to 90 days.
**Next action:** Ask the SEO & Content Agent to design a single `<AuthorBio />` Astro component (Daniel's photo, one-line credential, two-line bio, link to /about) and add it as the last block of every `src/pages/insights/*.astro` file. Verify on a handful first, then bulk-apply with a script.

## 3. Insights index has 506 articles and no search

**Category:** website
**Priority:** medium
**Why it matters:** The `/insights` index has a Buyers / Sellers / Buyers & Sellers / Owners tag filter, but no search input and no topic clustering. With 506 articles, a vendor who lands on the page looking for, say, "auction reserve price" has to scroll or use Cmd+F to find anything specific. The likely behaviour is bounce-back to Google. The same problem affects internal navigation from one article to another. A user finishing the storm-damage article has no easy way to discover the related "selling with insurance claim" or "presale repairs" pieces unless they were hand-linked in the body. This is conversion drag on traffic Daniel already paid for (in time and content cost).
**Expected impact:** A client-side search box at the top of `/insights` (Astro can ship a small Fuse.js index of titles + descriptions + tags) plus topic-cluster sub-pages (Auctions, Pricing, Presentation, Disclosure, Tax & Settlement, Tenanted Sales, etc.) would meaningfully extend session depth and pages-per-visit. Topic hubs also become powerful internal-linking targets for future SEO.
**Next action:** Ask the SEO & Content Agent to (a) add a client-side title/description search to `/insights/index.astro`, and (b) propose a 6 to 10 topic-cluster taxonomy for the existing 506 articles before building the hub pages.

## 5. Suburb pages have CTAs but no proof: missing trust layer

**Category:** lead-gen
**Priority:** medium
**Why it matters:** All 48 suburb pages now mention the appraisal/walkthrough offer (good, that gap is closed), but a vendor reading the Camp Hill or Bulimba page sees a polished suburb overview and a CTA, with no social proof tying the two together. The decision a vendor makes when picking an agent is heavily based on "have they actually sold properties like mine, in this suburb, recently?" Right now the answer on the suburb pages is implied at best. Competitors like Place Estate Agents and Ray White New Farm display recent sales tiles and vendor testimonials prominently on their suburb pages. That is the trust layer that converts a CTA view into an enquiry.
**Expected impact:** Adding even 2 or 3 recent verified sales (address, sale price band, days on market, brief testimonial quote where available) to each suburb page would meaningfully lift the appraisal-CTA conversion rate. Hard data from agency-side analytics typically shows social-proof additions on agent suburb pages lifting enquiry rate by 15 to 30%.
**Next action:** Ask Daniel for permission to surface 2 or 3 recent sales per priority suburb (Camp Hill, Bulimba, Morningside, Hawthorne, Coorparoo, Norman Park to start). Once the data is approved, the SEO & Content Agent can build a `<SuburbSocialProof />` component and apply it to those six suburb pages first as a measurable test before rolling out further.
