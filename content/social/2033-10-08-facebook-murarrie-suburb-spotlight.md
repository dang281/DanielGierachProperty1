## Caption

Murarrie offers one of the inner east's most accessible entry points, and buyers who understand the suburb relative to its neighbours tend to look at it very differently to buyers who have only skimmed the postcode.

The block sizes here are larger than what you find at comparable price points in suburbs closer to the city. That is a simple fact of the geography, and it matters to buyers who want land without paying Hawthorne or Bulimba prices. For first-time buyers and investors doing the comparison work carefully, Murarrie represents a proposition that is hard to dismiss.

The suburb's position near the Gateway Motorway is a practical advantage for residents who travel regularly or commute beyond the inner city. Morningside and Cannon Hill are close enough to draw on their retail and dining amenity without carrying those suburbs' price premiums. The infrastructure is there without the address cost.

Murarrie's character is still transitioning. Its industrial history is visible in parts, and the suburb is not fully residential in the way Hawthorne or Balmoral are. But the direction of that transition is clear, and buyers who are purchasing now are doing so ahead of a suburb that is becoming more residential with each development cycle.

The investor interest here is real. Rental demand from workers who need inner east access without inner east prices supports the yield case, and first-time buyers often compete with investors who have reached similar conclusions.
