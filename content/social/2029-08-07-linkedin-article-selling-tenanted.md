## Caption

If there is a fixed-term lease that runs past the settlement date, the buyer takes over that lease on settlement. They cannot require vacant possession on that day. This is one of the most common misunderstandings in tenanted property sales, and it catches buyers and sellers alike.

Owner-occupier buyers looking to move into the property need to understand this before signing. If the lease runs another four months past their intended settlement date, they are not moving in on settlement day. The contract must reflect the right expectation, or disputes follow.

For sellers, the obligations during a tenanted campaign add complexity. Every inspection requires at least 24 hours written notice to the tenant. The tenant has rights around reasonable access. A tenant who is cooperative and well-informed makes the campaign significantly smoother than one who has not been properly briefed on what is happening and why.

The most straightforward campaigns tend to be the ones where the seller has matched settlement timing to the lease expiry date, or has had an honest conversation with the tenant about the sale early in the process. Both take planning, and both are worth it.

The article covers the full picture of seller obligations in Queensland, how to structure the timeline, and what happens when the lease and the sale do not align cleanly.
