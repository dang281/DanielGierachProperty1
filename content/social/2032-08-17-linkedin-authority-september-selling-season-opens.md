## Caption

The city is returning to normal. The Games are done and September is three weeks away.

Here is what typically happens to the Brisbane inner east market at this point in the year.

Spring brings higher listing volumes, usually from September and into October. That means more choice for buyers and more competition for sellers. If you are planning to sell, you will not be the only one launching in spring. The sellers who do best are the ones who are prepared before the market gets crowded, not after.

The buyers who deliberately sat out the Games period are now making decisions. Some of them have been ready to move for months and have been waiting for the city to settle. That group is about to re-enter an inner east market that still has limited stock. The combination of returning buyer demand and an early spring listing wave will determine the tone of the market for the rest of the year.

For sellers: if you are planning a September or October campaign, the preparation work starts now. Photography, styling decisions, contract preparation, and pricing strategy all take time. A campaign launched unprepared into a competitive spring market is harder to run than one built deliberately over the next few weeks.

For buyers: the post-Games market is already opening. The window between now and the peak of spring listing volumes is worth using.
