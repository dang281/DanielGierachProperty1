## Caption

Q1 2034 is done. The inner east delivered a consistent quarter for properties that entered the market with realistic price expectations and solid presentation. The outliers in both directions were predictable: well-prepared properties with accurate pricing performed strongly; those priced above the comparable set or with presentation issues sat.

Heading into May, the buyer pool is shifting. The urgency buyers who needed to transact before the end of the financial year or ahead of a life event have largely made their decisions through March and April. The buyers who remain active in May are more deliberate. They are not in a rush and they know the winter market is quieter, which means they believe they may find better value by waiting.

For vendors going to market now, that shift matters. The spring urgency that pushes buyers to compete strongly does not apply in May. The campaign needs to create its own competition from within the active buyer pool rather than relying on seasonal momentum to do that work.

For buyers, May into June historically offers the opportunity to negotiate in circumstances that the autumn peak did not allow. The market does not fall apart in winter. But motivated sellers who have been on the market without a result are often more open to a direct conversation.
