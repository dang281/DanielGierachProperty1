## Caption

Cannon Hill's rail connection to the CBD is one of the suburb's most practical advantages and one that buyers from the commuter demographic weight heavily in their decision-making. Eight kilometres from the city with a direct train link, the suburb offers a genuine alternative to suburbs that require bus-only travel and can make the daily commute noticeably more variable.

Cannon Hill Anglican College draws families with school-age children from a wide catchment and contributes to an owner-occupier buyer pool that prioritises educational access in their property decisions. The school's presence also brings stability to the surrounding residential market, as families who move for the school tend to stay for the duration of schooling years, which keeps turnover measured rather than speculative.

DFO Brisbane at Cannon Hill is a practical retail asset for residents and also a local employer of note. The combination of train access, school proximity and retail convenience creates a liveable package that has been increasingly recognised in Cannon Hill's price performance over recent years. Current medians in the suburb reflect a market that has appreciated from its earlier position as a lower-profile inner east option.

The housing stock is varied: post-war homes, renovated character properties and medium-density development all appear within the suburb. Families tend to target detached homes with adequate yard space, while investors focus on properties near the station and school precinct.
