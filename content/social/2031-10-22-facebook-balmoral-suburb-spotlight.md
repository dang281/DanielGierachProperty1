## Caption

Balmoral sits above Bulimba, and the elevation makes a real difference. Some of the higher streets here have genuine CBD outlook that buyers from lower-lying suburbs don't always expect when they first come through. That view factor shifts price conversations noticeably.

Adjacent to Bulimba but with its own distinct character, Balmoral draws upsizers who want larger blocks and more space than the tighter streets closer to Oxford Street allow. Balmoral State School is well regarded, and the catchment is an active part of the family buyer conversation. River Street proximity shapes the southern end of the suburb, and blocks in that area attract a specific buyer profile looking for the Bulimba lifestyle with slightly more land.

The dominant stock type is the character home, often on blocks that feel generous by inner east standards. Well-presented homes on elevated streets with good presentation sell with very low days on market. Unrenovated homes in sound condition also move well here because the underlying land and location value gives buyers confidence in the renovation equation.

What takes time is the property that doesn't make the most of its position. Buyers in Balmoral have usually looked extensively in Bulimba first and shifted here for the extra space or the outlook. They are not easily impressed by presentation alone.
