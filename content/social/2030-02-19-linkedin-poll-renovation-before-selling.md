## Caption

Renovation before selling is one of those decisions that looks straightforward but rarely is.

In Brisbane's inner east, buyers are generally sophisticated. They can see through a fresh coat of paint on a property that hasn't been touched underneath. They can also identify value in a property that has been genuinely updated and presents without compromise.

The renovation question comes down to mathematics and timing. A full renovation that costs $80,000 and adds $120,000 to the sale price is worth doing. A full renovation that costs $80,000 and adds $60,000 is not, regardless of how much better it looks.

Light cosmetic work, paint, gardens, carpets, declutter, is almost always worth doing. It costs relatively little and consistently improves buyer perception and photography.

Major structural or kitchen-and-bathroom renovations require a detailed cost-benefit analysis before you commit. And time. In autumn, you may not have enough of either.

If you were selling your home in Brisbane's inner east, would you renovate before listing?

## Poll options
