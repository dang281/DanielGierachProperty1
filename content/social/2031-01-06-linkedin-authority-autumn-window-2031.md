## Caption

For homeowners planning a sale in the first half of 2031, the decisions that will shape the campaign result are being made now, not in February when the property goes live.

The preparation timeline for a well-run inner east campaign typically runs four to eight weeks. That preparation includes decluttering and minor repairs, any necessary touch-ups to paintwork or gardens, professional photography, and the preparation of the seller disclosure statement with your solicitor. For a property targeting a late February or early March launch, that preparation needs to start in January.

Waiting until February to start the conversation about selling in February does not leave enough time. The properties that launch in late February with strong photography, clear pricing based on current comparable sales evidence, and a disclosure statement ready to hand to a buyer's solicitor are the ones that convert early interest into early offers.

The other thing worth resolving before February is the sale method question. Auction, private treaty, or expression of interest: each has a different lead time for preparation and a different effect on how buyers experience the campaign. Having that conversation before you are under time pressure produces better outcomes than having it the week before the first open home.

If you are thinking about listing in the first half of 2031, January is the right time to have the appraisal conversation. Not to commit to a price or a date, but to understand what the preparation involves and what the current market supports. That groundwork changes what the campaign can achieve.
