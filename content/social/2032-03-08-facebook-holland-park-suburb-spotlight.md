## Caption

Holland Park sits at a price point that makes it one of the more active parts of the inner east buyer market. It consistently attracts buyers who have been searching in Coorparoo or Greenslopes, have done the maths, and are ready to commit to a suburb that gives them more land and a more liveable street scale for a comparable or better result.

The streets north of Logan Road, particularly those running toward the Holland Park West boundary, offer larger blocks and a quieter residential character. These have been among the most sought-after pockets for families over the past several years. Access to Whites Hill Reserve, cycling infrastructure, and the school catchment zones around Holland Park State School and Seville Road State School are all factors buyers raise at open homes.

March is a strong selling month here. The autumn buyer pool in Holland Park tends to include a high proportion of buyers who have already missed properties in nearby suburbs and are approaching inspections with a more decisive mindset. Delay fatigue is real, and vendors who present their homes well and price with reference to recent comparable sales are capturing that intent.

If you are thinking about selling in Holland Park this year, an autumn campaign timed before Easter gives you the best available buyer pool.
