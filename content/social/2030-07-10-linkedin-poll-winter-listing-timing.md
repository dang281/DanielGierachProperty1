## Caption

The spring vs winter listing debate comes up in nearly every vendor conversation I have in July. The instinct to wait for spring is understandable. More buyers, more open home foot traffic, more of the energy that makes a campaign feel competitive.

What the instinct underestimates is the quality of winter buyers. The inner east consistently attracts committed buyers year-round, and a July listing with less competition can work in a vendor's favour. The properties that sit are the ones priced incorrectly, not the ones that launch in winter.

If you were selling your inner east home this year, when would you list?
