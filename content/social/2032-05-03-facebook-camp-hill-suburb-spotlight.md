## Caption

Camp Hill has built a reputation in Brisbane's inner-east property market that is hard-won and well-deserved. The suburb sits on a ridge that delivers elevated outlooks across the city from streets like Boundary Road, Old Cleveland Road and the upper sections of Echlin Street, and buyers who have visited for the first time often describe the position as something they had not fully anticipated from a map.

The Old Cleveland Road café and retail strip through Camp Hill is one of the most genuinely used local strips in the inner east. Independent bakeries, wine merchants, and a range of food options that cater to the suburb's predominantly owner-occupier population make it a walkable destination rather than a drive-through. That streetscape quality is something buyers from suburbs without a comparable strip notice and factor into their decision-making.

Camp Hill State School is a well-regarded primary option, and the suburb sits within the Balmoral State High School catchment, which draws families from a wide area who want continuity across both school stages. That dual-school catchment profile is a material factor in the suburb's sustained buyer interest.

Character Queenslanders on elevated allotments on the western side of the suburb, particularly those with city views, attract competitive campaigns. Buyers who have attended auctions in Norman Park or Hawthorne without success frequently find that Camp Hill delivers the product they want, within a budget that works. If you are a Camp Hill homeowner thinking about what your property is worth in the current market, the details are at danielgierach.com/suburbs/camp-hill.
