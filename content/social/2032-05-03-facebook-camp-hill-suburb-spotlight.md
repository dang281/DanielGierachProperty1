## Caption

Camp Hill has one of the more distinct topographies in Brisbane's inner south-east, with the elevated ridge along Old Cleveland Road sitting above the creek flats and giving many properties a breezy outlook that buyers notice immediately. The suburb is well-established in the minds of the buying market as a family destination, and the competition at the upper end of its price range reflects that reputation.

The Martha Street and Old Cleveland Road café and dining strip at Camp Hill has become a genuine drawcard over the past decade, with the activity there reinforcing the suburb's appeal to younger families and professionals who want lifestyle amenity alongside a residential address. Proximity to Coorparoo and Greenslopes also gives Camp Hill residents access to a wider range of services without being in the middle of the higher-traffic suburbs themselves.

Camp Hill State School is the primary catchment, and its positioning within a walkable part of the suburb means families with school-aged children consider the street-level access to the school a genuine factor in choosing their property. Cavendish Road State High School is the secondary catchment, and its draw is consistent with most of the inner south-east corridor.

Camp Hill properties on the ridge with original character features and any outlook tend to generate strong buyer interest at inspection. If you own on one of those streets and have not had a current assessment done, the number may be higher than you expect. danielgierach.com/suburbs/camp-hill
