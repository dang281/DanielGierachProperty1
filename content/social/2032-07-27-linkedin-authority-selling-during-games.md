## Caption

The Brisbane 2032 Olympics opened four days ago. Here is an honest early read on what the first week has meant for property enquiry in Brisbane's inner east.

Open home attendance is genuinely lower. That is not a surprise and it is not a crisis. It is the environment.

What has changed is the nature of the enquiry. The active buyer pool right now is smaller, but the people in it are having more substantive conversations. When you are one of a smaller number of buyers inspecting a property, you get more time with the agent, more direct access to information, and a better read on vendor motivation.

That dynamic tends to produce cleaner transactions when a deal does come together.

For sellers who have been patient through this period, the position is reasonable. The buyers who commit during the Games window are doing so because they see value in acting when competition is thinner. Vendors who understand this and price accordingly are finding transactions.

The September return will bring buyer depth back. More open home traffic, more competing offers, and a market that looks and feels more active. For some properties, waiting for that is the right call. For others, the motivated buyer pool right now is the better opportunity.

The Games period is a transition. The inner east market does not stop. It recalibrates, and the sellers who understand the recalibration are positioned well for what comes next.
