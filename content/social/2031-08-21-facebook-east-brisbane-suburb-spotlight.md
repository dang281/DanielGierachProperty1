## Caption

East Brisbane's investment case is built on proximity, and that is the most durable case in property. The suburb sits inside the four-kilometre ring from the CBD, with South Bank and the Gabba precinct as immediate neighbours. That positioning means rental demand from CBD workers, university students, and inner-city professionals never slackens.

The character home market in East Brisbane attracts a different buyer to the apartment market, and those two markets coexist without competing in the way that might be expected. Character buyers are owner-occupiers looking for a long-term address. Apartment buyers and their investors are driven by yield. The result is a suburb with genuine depth across two distinct buyer groups, which supports values across different market conditions.

Flood-aware buyers do their research. Properties on higher ground in East Brisbane carry a clear premium, and the clarity of that distinction makes price comparison more nuanced than in suburbs without the flood variable. For owners of flood-free properties, that research-driven premium is very real and should be part of any appraisal conversation.

Pre-spring in East Brisbane sees buyer urgency build quickly. The suburb does not have a long listing period, and competition for well-positioned stock is genuine even in late August. If your property is flood-free and well-presented, the market conditions in August are more favourable than they might appear on the surface.
