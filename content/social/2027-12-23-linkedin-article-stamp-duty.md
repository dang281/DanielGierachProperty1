## Caption

Transfer duty in Queensland, what most people call stamp duty, is consistently one of the most underestimated upfront costs in a property purchase.

On a $1 million owner-occupier purchase, the transfer duty payable is approximately $38,025. That figure does not go into equity. It does not reduce the mortgage. It is a cost of transacting, paid upfront, that is simply gone.

Most first-time buyers have a rough sense that stamp duty exists. Fewer know the actual number until they are three weeks from settlement and their solicitor confirms the settlement statement. At that point, the impact on the deposit and the borrowing structure is fixed.

For owner-occupiers, Queensland does provide concessions: the rate is lower for owner-occupiers than for investors, and first home buyers purchasing under $700,000 receive a full exemption. Above that threshold, first home buyers receive a partial concession that reduces progressively.

The practical implication is simple: if you are planning a purchase in 2028, the transfer duty figure should be in your budget before you start inspecting properties, not discovered at the contract stage. A buyer who has budgeted $200,000 for a deposit on a $1 million property needs to know that approximately $38,000 of that deposit is spoken for before a dollar goes to the purchase.
