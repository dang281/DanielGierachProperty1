## Caption

The trigger for the first conversation with an agent is rarely a single thing. It tends to be a combination of signals arriving close enough together that the timing starts to feel right.

A sale in the street is the most common catalyst. When the house three doors down that everyone assumed was worth a certain amount sells for considerably more, the question of what this one is worth becomes harder to leave unanswered. That curiosity is usually the beginning of the process, not the end of it.

Life events are the second driver. A job relocation, a relationship change, a new family member, or the straightforward recognition that the house no longer fits the life being lived in it. These are not market-driven decisions. They are personal ones. But they create a timing that is often disconnected from whether the market is at a peak or a trough.

The third category is the decision that has been building slowly. The homeowner who has been thinking about downsizing for two years but has not yet had a reason to act. A strong market, a property they have seen and want to buy, or a conversation with an agent that gives them confidence in the process can complete that decision.

None of these require the market to be at its peak. They require the timing to make sense for the person in the house. If any of them resonate, the conversation is worth having.
