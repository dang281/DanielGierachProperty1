## Caption

March is historically the strongest month for inner east campaigns. The buyers are ready, the stock is manageable, and the urgency to transact before winter is real and growing.

Here is what sellers who are ready now should know.

Buyer engagement in March is at its peak. Open home numbers are higher than at any other point in the year. The people attending are not curious neighbours. They are buyers who have been searching for months and are ready to make a decision when the right property arrives.

Competition from other sellers exists but is still manageable. By April, more listings arrive. By May, the buyer who was ready in March has often already bought something else. The window is not permanent.

If you are ready now, launch now. Not in three weeks when you have done one more thing to the garden. The garden matters less than the timing.

For sellers who are close but not quite there, the question to ask is: what is the one thing stopping me from launching this month, and is that thing actually material to the result? In my experience, the answer is often no.

For sellers who genuinely are not ready, the right move is not to rush. A poorly presented property launched into the peak buyer pool and rejected is harder to recover than a well-prepared property launched six weeks later into a smaller pool. If March is not achievable, plan properly for mid-March or early April and do it right.

The window is open. It will not stay open indefinitely.
