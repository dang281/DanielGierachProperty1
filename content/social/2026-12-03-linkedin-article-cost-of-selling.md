## Caption

When a seller thinks about what they will net from a sale, the first calculation is usually sale price minus the remaining mortgage balance. That calculation is missing four or five line items that can add up to between 2.5% and 4% of the sale price.

On a $1.4 million property, that is $35,000 to $56,000 before you see the proceeds.

The main components: agent commission (typically 2% to 2.5% plus GST in QLD), conveyancing fees ($1,500 to $2,500 for a standard residential sale), marketing costs (photography, floor plan, online listing portals, print if used), mortgage discharge fee (set by your lender, typically $150 to $500), and any pre-sale preparation costs if you have styled, painted, or repaired the property.

Capital gains tax can apply in some circumstances, but most owner-occupiers selling their primary residence are exempt. That is a separate conversation with your accountant.

The reason this matters is simple: sellers who do not factor in the full cost of selling sometimes get a shock at settlement when the net proceeds are lower than expected. A realistic net proceeds calculation, done before you list, gives you clarity on whether the sale makes sense financially at the expected price range, and what price you actually need to achieve to reach your goal.

It is not a complicated calculation. But it needs to be done with all the numbers in it.
