## Caption

Most spring-selling advice is built around Melbourne and Sydney. Brisbane's inner east does not follow the same seasonal rhythm, and sellers who act on that advice are often selling into their most competitive window rather than their best one.

Autumn, roughly late February through to May, is historically the most consistent selling window in Brisbane's inner east. The weather is mild, buyers are motivated, and supply is typically lower than spring. That last point matters more than most sellers realise. Motivated buyers with fewer properties to choose from is the environment where prices hold firmly and campaigns run shorter.

Spring does produce strong results in Brisbane, but it also brings more competition. Stock levels rise, buyers spread their attention across more options, and campaigns that might have run for two weeks in autumn can stretch to five in October. That is not a disaster, but it changes the dynamic.

The honest answer to "when should I sell" is that preparation matters more than the calendar. A well-presented property with a well-executed campaign in July will outperform a poorly prepared one in September every time. The season is a tailwind, not a substitute for the fundamentals.

The article goes deeper on the Brisbane data, what the inner east specifically looks like across the year, and why sellers who focus only on timing often miss the bigger lever.
