## Caption

The 5-business-day cooling off period in Queensland cannot be waived by the vendor. It is a statutory buyer right, written into the Property Law Act, and the seller has no ability to remove it from a private treaty contract regardless of what the negotiation looks like.

Most buyers know this in a general way. What they often do not know: exiting during the cooling off period is not free. It costs 0.25% of the purchase price. On an $800,000 property, that is $2,000 the buyer forfeits to exit. It is not zero. Treating it as a cost-free escape hatch is a misunderstanding that catches people out.

The third thing: auction contracts have no cooling off period at all. If you are the winning bidder at auction, or you sign a contract before or after auction under auction conditions, you are bound immediately and unconditionally. There is no 5-day window. This is one of the reasons buyers need to complete their due diligence, building and pest inspections, title searches, and finance pre-approval, before auction day rather than after.

These three facts are not complicated, but they are not well understood even by buyers who have purchased property before. The article on my website works through the cooling off period in detail, including how the 5-business-day count works, what triggers it, and what the process looks like if a buyer decides to exercise it.

If you are buying in Queensland this spring, it is worth understanding the rules before you are in a situation where they matter.
