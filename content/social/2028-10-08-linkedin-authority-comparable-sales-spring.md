## Caption

Spring campaigns set the comparable sales evidence that sellers and buyers will rely on for the next six months.

What I am seeing right now in the inner east: the properties that have sold in October are resetting the benchmarks in a handful of streets. A strong October result in a good street gives the next vendor in that street a genuine evidence base to work from. A weak October result, one that came from an overpriced launch or a vendor who needed to move quickly, does the opposite.

This matters to sellers who are thinking about 2029. The comparable evidence that exists in your street by November or December of this year is the floor you will be working from if you list in the first quarter of next year. That floor is being set right now.

For buyers, the same logic applies. The October results tell you whether spring has actually delivered price growth or whether the market has been flatter than the spring narrative suggests. Looking at settled sales rather than asking prices gives you a far more accurate read.

If you want an honest assessment of what the comparable evidence looks like in your street, that is a conversation worth having before the spring window closes.
