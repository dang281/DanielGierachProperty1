## Caption

Carina sits squarely in the middle of Brisbane's eastern residential belt, bordered by Carindale to the east and Camp Hill to the north, with Old Cleveland Road running through it as the main arterial connecting the suburb to both the city and the Carindale shopping precinct. That central positioning means Carina draws from multiple buyer groups, and the suburb's consistent turnover reflects a healthy market rather than distressed selling.

The housing stock in Carina is dominated by post-war homes on lots that typically sit between 500 and 700 square metres. Many of these homes have been renovated or extended, and the result is a suburb where buyers on a realistic budget can find a full-size family home with genuine outdoor space. Bulimba Creek runs along the suburb's southern boundary and offers a linear green corridor that residents use for walking and cycling through to the Carindale and Eight Mile Plains areas.

Carina State School is the main primary catchment, and Cavendish Road State High School is the secondary draw, with the high school's consistent community reputation being a reliable driver of family buyer interest. The suburb also benefits from a local shopping strip at the intersection of Old Cleveland Road and Cavendish Road, with supermarkets and everyday services within walking distance for many residents.
