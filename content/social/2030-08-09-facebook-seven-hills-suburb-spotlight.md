## Caption

Seven Hills sits between Morningside and Murarrie, close to the Gateway Motorway, and within the Morningside State School catchment. For the buyers who have discovered it, those three facts are more than enough. For the buyers who have not yet looked here, they are missing one of the better-value pockets in the inner east.

The buyer profile arriving in Seven Hills is largely made up of families priced out of Morningside and Cannon Hill who are not willing to sacrifice school catchment or suburb quality to find room in their budget. They are buying post-war homes and newer builds on blocks that offer genuine space, and they are doing so at entry points that the neighbouring suburbs passed through some years ago.

What sells here tends to be homes that are well-presented and priced to reflect the suburb's position honestly. Buyers are informed and they are comparing Seven Hills against Morningside and Murarrie side by side. The ones who move quickly do so because they recognise value before it becomes consensus.

Seven Hills has stayed below the radar for longer than its access profile and school catchment would normally allow. Owner-occupier momentum is building, and with it, confidence in the suburb's long-term trajectory.

If you own in Seven Hills and want to understand where your property sits in this market, the conversation starts here.
