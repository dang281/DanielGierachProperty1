## Caption

The most common misunderstanding I see from sellers and buyers in Queensland is treating solicitors and conveyancers as interchangeable. They are not, and choosing the wrong one for your situation can cost you more than the difference in their fees.

In Queensland, both a solicitor and a licensed conveyancer can legally handle the transfer of property. The distinction is what happens when things get complicated. A conveyancer handles the transactional process. They are efficient and often excellent for a straightforward property sale with no title issues, no trust account complications and no disputes. A solicitor can do everything a conveyancer does, plus advise on the legal implications of what you are signing and act if the contract becomes a legal matter rather than a transactional one.

The mistake people make is hiring based on price alone. A cheap conveyancer on an uncomplicated sale makes sense. A cheap conveyancer on a property with a heritage overlay, an easement query, a deceased estate, or a vendor finance clause is a different calculation entirely. The few hundred dollars saved at the start can be far outweighed by the cost of sorting out something that a solicitor would have caught at contract review.

I have written a plain-language guide to how the two differ in Queensland, what each costs, and how to think about which one is right for your transaction. It is one of the more practical things you can read before signing anything.
