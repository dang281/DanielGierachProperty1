## Caption

Brisbane's inner east autumn, from late February through to May, is consistently the strongest selling window of the year. If you are thinking about selling in 2034 and you want to be part of that window, the preparation needs to start now.

The preparation phase for a well-run campaign typically runs six to ten weeks. That covers pre-sale repairs and maintenance, property styling, professional photography and floor plans, disclosure statement preparation, and the process of selecting and appointing an agent. When you work back from a late-February launch, six to ten weeks of preparation means starting in mid-December to mid-January at the latest.

A vendor who begins that process in late December is ready to launch by late February. They are at the front of the autumn window, competing against a buyer pool that is highly motivated after months of searching over the summer. Their campaign runs through March and April when buyer activity peaks.

A vendor who decides to start the conversation in March is launching in May at the earliest. The autumn window is already narrowing toward the quieter winter period. They miss the peak of buyer activity that drives competition and, in most cases, the strongest result.

The quiet weeks between Christmas and late January are not dead time. For a vendor who is serious about 2034, they are the most valuable preparation weeks of the year. The market is not watching. There is no pressure. It is the right time to make considered decisions about presentation, strategy, and timing without the urgency of a live campaign pushing those decisions.
