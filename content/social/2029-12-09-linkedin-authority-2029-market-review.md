## Caption

The inner east opened 2029 carrying reasonable momentum from late 2028. Buyer enquiry was steady, stock was tight, and competition at open homes in suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Balmoral was producing strong results for well-prepared vendors. That conditions held through autumn was not a surprise. That they held through the winter as well was.

Rate movements mid-year created a period where some buyers paused to reassess their borrowing position. That pause was real, but shorter than most commentators expected. By September, the active buyer pool had largely recalibrated and the spring market in the inner east performed solidly.

Freestanding homes in the $1.5 million to $2.5 million range remained the most contested segment throughout the year. Buyers in this bracket were serious and prepared. Properties that were well-presented and accurately priced moved quickly. Those that were not sat longer than vendors anticipated.

What surprised even experienced operators was how resilient land value held in the sub-1500 square metre category. The premium that buyers place on proximity to the river and walkable amenity did not compress the way some predicted it might.

Heading into December, stock remains below what the market could absorb. That constraint will carry into 2030. Whether it produces the same results depends on confidence and employment conditions, which are harder to forecast.

The year was not perfect. But the inner east gave vendors who approached it correctly a genuine opportunity.
