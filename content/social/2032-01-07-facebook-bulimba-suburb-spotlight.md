## Caption

Bulimba is the suburb that defines the inner east for most people outside it. The Oxford Street strip, the river frontage, the ferry access, the tree-lined streets behind Bulimba State School. It has the kind of liveability that does not need to be explained to buyers. They arrive already knowing what they want.

What makes Bulimba a different sell in 2032 is the combination of genuine prestige and Olympic proximity. The suburb sits within easy reach of the Games corridor, and buyer interest from interstate and international enquirers has been higher than in most inner east postcodes through 2031.

The housing mix covers substantial riverfront and near-river properties at the top end, character Queenslanders and post-war homes through the middle streets, and a growing townhouse market along the southern fringe. The school catchments, particularly Bulimba State School and the access to Anglican Church Grammar within a short commute, attract family buyers who prioritise education infrastructure alongside lifestyle.

For vendors in Bulimba, the key variable right now is timing relative to the autumn campaign window. Properties that come to market in March or April, well prepared and with a realistic pricing strategy, are competing for the strongest buyer pool of the year. Waiting for the Games period itself introduces noise and logistics that rarely benefit sellers.

If you are thinking about what your Bulimba property is worth in the current market, it is worth understanding that conversation now.
