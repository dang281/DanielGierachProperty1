## Caption

Most vendors assume spring means listing in October. The logic feels right: more buyers, warmer weather, gardens in bloom. But the part of that equation that gets ignored is supply competition. Spring brings more buyers, yes. It also brings more sellers, and they all arrive at roughly the same time.

Autumn told a different story. Buyer numbers were lower, but so was the competing supply. A well-prepared property that was the standout in its price range in March had less to fight against than the same property would face in a crowded October week. That matters. Buyers who are genuinely ready to move make decisions based on what they can see, and if there are six similar homes in front of them, they negotiate differently.

The window that actually works is the one where your property stands out rather than blends in. For inner Brisbane right now, that window is September. Buyer activity has been building since July, the school-holiday slowdown is clearing, and the listings that will eventually crowd the market haven't all hit yet. Vendors who are preparing now, in August, are set up to list into that window before it fills up.

The preparation phase is what most sellers underestimate. Styling, repairs, photography, and the legal paperwork all take time. Compressing that phase to chase a faster launch almost always shows in the result. If you're thinking about selling before Christmas, the planning conversation needs to happen now.
