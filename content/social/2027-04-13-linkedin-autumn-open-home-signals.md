## Caption

Two things tell you where the market is before any data report does: how many people turned up to the first open home, and what they asked at the door.

In Brisbane's inner east this autumn, attendance at well-priced open homes has been consistent. Not the post-lockdown numbers from 2021, but steady and purposeful. The buyers coming through are not window shoppers. They have typically been looking for six to twelve weeks, they know the street values, and they have done the finance work.

What they ask at the door matters more than how many showed up. When the questions are about the contract timeline, what's included, and whether the vendor would take subject-to-finance, that is a buyer who is ready. When the questions are about the street, the neighbours, and whether the vendor would consider a lower price, that is a buyer who is still testing value. Both are useful information. But only one of them closes quickly.

Right now in the inner east, the ratio is running toward the first type. The window between now and May is worth taking seriously if you have been considering a campaign. The buyer pool you are selling to in April is more motivated, more informed, and less distracted than the one you will be selling to in June.

If you want a straight read on what that looks like for your property, call me.
