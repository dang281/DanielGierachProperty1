## Caption

This year was unlike any other I have worked through, and it deserves an honest account rather than a polished summary.

The year opened with real momentum. Autumn in Q1 was active. Buyers who had been sitting on the sidelines through late 2031 came back with confidence, and the inner east responded. Stock moved quickly, competition at inspections was genuine, and the conversations I was having with vendors reflected a market that felt settled and purposeful.

That changed in Q2 as the Olympics drew closer. The hesitation was not dramatic, but it was real. Some buyers adopted a wait-and-see position. Some vendors chose to defer. The market did not fall, but it paused. Listings in May and June sat longer than they had in Q1, and the urgency that had characterised the autumn campaign window softened.

July and August were the quietest months I have seen in years. The city's attention was elsewhere, and the property market reflected that. Vendors with flexibility held. A small number of motivated buyers found an opening that will look very good in hindsight.

September changed everything. The Games concluded and the market restarted almost immediately. The spring selling season ran strong through October and November, with genuine depth in buyer activity. The inner east performed well. Families wanted to be settled before the school year, and that timing pressure produced real competition.

Where we end 2032: the Games are behind us, and the market has returned to what it has always been driven by. Land content. School catchments. Proximity to the river. Walkable streets. Those fundamentals did not change this year. They never do.
