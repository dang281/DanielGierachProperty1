## Caption

2034 delivered a more nuanced picture for Brisbane inner east property than the headline numbers suggest.

The strongest performance came from well-positioned family homes in the mid-tier suburbs. Camp Hill, Norman Park, and Carina Heights all saw competitive buyer pools through spring for three and four-bedroom homes in the $1.2 million to $1.8 million range. Properties that were well-presented and correctly priced were transacting within three to four weeks in that price bracket.

The character home market in Hawthorne, Balmoral, and Bulimba continued to attract buyers from outside the inner east, including buyers from interstate who were drawn by the lifestyle and the relative value compared to equivalent properties in Sydney and Melbourne. That interstate buyer influence has been a consistent theme.

The investment property segment performed more quietly, with investors managing the balance between rental yields and holding costs in an environment where both rates and land values have continued to move.

Looking at 2034 overall, the common thread across the strongest results was preparation. The vendors who invested in their presentation and got the pricing conversation right before going to market consistently outperformed those who launched with a test-and-adjust approach.

If you are assessing what your inner east property is worth today, or planning a sale in 2035, that conversation belongs in December, not January.
