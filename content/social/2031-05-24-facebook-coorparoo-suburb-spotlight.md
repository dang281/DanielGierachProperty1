## Caption

Coorparoo's position in the south-east corridor is rarely appreciated fully by buyers who look at a map rather than at the suburb's actual street-by-street character. The older sections of Coorparoo, particularly the character home streets that sit between Old Cleveland Road and the suburb's quieter residential interior, offer a quality of housing and streetscape that the post-war and infill sections do not.

That distinction matters for vendors. A character home on a good block in the quieter parts of Coorparoo is not selling the same thing as a post-war house on an equivalent-sized lot further toward the commercial strip. Buyers who have spent time in the suburb will have identified the streets they prefer, and they will pay a measurable premium to be on one of them.

The Coorparoo State School catchment continues to do the work of sustaining demand through periods when buyer sentiment elsewhere in the city has softened. School-catchment buyers are planning around a timeline that is not dictated by the property market. They need to be enrolled, and the property purchase is the means to that end. That functional urgency is a consistent feature of the Coorparoo buyer pool.

The bus corridors along Old Cleveland Road into the CBD are among the more reliable in the south-east, and for households where public transport is the primary daily commute, Coorparoo's frequency and coverage make it a genuine alternative to suburbs with train access.
