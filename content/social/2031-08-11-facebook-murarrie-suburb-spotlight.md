## Caption

Murarrie's investment dynamics are worth examining closely, because they are different from most inner-east suburbs and the difference matters for owners thinking about the year ahead.

Rental demand here is driven by price. As rents have climbed across Morningside, Cannon Hill, and Hawthorne, tenants have been pushed east into Murarrie, where yields remain stronger relative to purchase price. For investors, that compression of rental supply from the west has been a consistent source of demand. For owner-occupiers considering selling, a strong rental demand floor means investor buyers remain competitive bidders in the market.

The Gateway Motorway is an underrated asset for Murarrie. Workers based in the trade, logistics, and industrial sectors further north and south of the city find the suburb genuinely practical in a way that the more residential inner suburbs are not. That expands the effective buyer pool beyond the typical inner-east professional demographic.

New residential development has continued to add stock with a contemporary standard of finish. That new supply is filling a gap in the local market rather than depressing it, because demand from the west has consistently absorbed what has come to market.

If you own in Murarrie and have been watching the suburb develop around you, August is a good time to understand precisely what that development has done to your asset. See the current numbers at danielgierach.com/suburbs/murarrie
