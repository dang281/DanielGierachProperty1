## Caption

The volume at an open home is not random.

There are properties in the inner east where the first Saturday draws 40 groups through the door. There are others where three couples walk through and two of them leave in under five minutes. The difference is rarely about the property itself.

It comes down to a few things. Photography that makes the home look like the best version of itself. A price guide that is in the range buyers are searching, rather than above it. A first Saturday that hits when the suburb's serious buyers are actually looking, not during school holidays or a long weekend when the genuinely motivated groups are away.

Agents who talk about buyer databases and pre-qualified buyers are talking about the right thing. An agent who can call 15 people the day before and tell them a property in their target suburb just hit the market is going to produce a different first weekend than one who relies entirely on portals.

The outcome at auction or in private negotiations is built in the first two open homes. The numbers that show up in week one set the expectations and the competition for the rest of the campaign.
