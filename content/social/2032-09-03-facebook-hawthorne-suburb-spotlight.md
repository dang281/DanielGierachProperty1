## Caption

The streets east of Oxford Street in Hawthorne do not come to market often, and when they do, they do not stay there long.

Wybelenna Street and the elevated ridge blocks that sit above the river corridor are some of the most sought-after residential addresses in Brisbane's inner east. Owners hold them for decades. The decision to sell is rarely made quickly, and the buyer who purchases them is rarely buying on impulse either. They have usually been watching the suburb for six months or more before the right property appears.

Hawthorne Cinema is the suburb's cultural anchor. It draws people into the precinct from across the inner east and gives Hawthorne's village street character something that Oxford Street retail alone cannot deliver. Buyers who come from Sydney or Melbourne often identify with this: walkability, a local cinema, a street they can name. It matches the kind of suburb they left behind.

Lourdes Hill College adds a secondary school dimension to the buyer profile. Families who have mapped out their school years ahead of the purchase represent consistent demand at the family home price point.

Interstate buyers, particularly those relocating from Sydney and Melbourne in the post-Olympic period, have identified Hawthorne's walkability and its position in the corridor as a match for what they are looking for. They arrive with equity and a decision already mostly made.

If you own in Hawthorne and want to understand where your property sits in the current market, the conversation starts here.
