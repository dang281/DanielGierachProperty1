## Caption

Interstate buyers do not behave the same way as local buyers, and understanding the difference matters when you are preparing a campaign.

A buyer coming from Sydney or Melbourne is often working with a different reference point for value. They have done their research online, they know the Brisbane suburb names, and they have a figure in their head based on what their money buys at home. What they do not always have is a feel for which streets, which aspects and which block sizes actually matter in the inner east.

That creates an opportunity for a seller whose agent knows how to present those things clearly. The elevation that gives you the river glimpse. The street that sits back from the school traffic. The block that opens to the north. These are not features that write themselves in a listing description. They need to be explained to someone who did not grow up driving those streets.

In 2032, with the Games in July, I am also fielding more calls from interstate buyers who see Brisbane as a long-term play rather than a purely Olympic one. They are interested in the livability of the inner east, the school catchments, the proximity to the CBD and the airport. The Games are context, not the primary driver for most of them.

If you are selling in this window, knowing who your likely buyer is helps shape every decision from the marketing material to the open home presentation.
