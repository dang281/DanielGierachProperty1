## Caption

There is a clear difference between the people walking through an open home in July and the people walking through an open home in October. I notice it every year without exception.

In July, people are still deciding whether they want to move. They ask about the neighbourhood, the neighbours, what the street is like on a Saturday morning. They are genuinely curious, but they are still in the research phase. Half of them will not buy anything for six months. Some will not buy at all.

By October, that question has already been answered. The families who spent winter thinking about it have made the decision. Finance is approved, in some cases months ago. They have a building and pest inspector they have already used once or twice on other properties. They have a shortlist, and in many cases this is the third or fourth time they have been to an open home for this type of property. The questions at the door shift completely. It stops being about what the suburb is like and starts being about what a reasonable settlement date looks like and whether the vendor would consider a shorter finance clause.

I also see the repeat inspections happen faster. A buyer who comes back within four or five days in October is almost always a serious buyer working up the confidence to make an offer. In July, a return inspection might just mean they are still doing their due diligence broadly. In October, it means they are close.

If you are thinking about listing this spring, the buyer pool is ready. The question is whether you are.
