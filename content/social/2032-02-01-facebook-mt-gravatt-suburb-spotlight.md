## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits at an interesting intersection for buyers in 2032. It is far enough from the CBD to offer genuine land sizes, close enough to Westfield Mt Gravatt and the South East Freeway to feel connected, and positioned within a school catchment, Whites Hill State College, that buyers in neighbouring suburbs will cross a boundary to access.

The ridge along Mt Gravatt Lookout Road and Bunnerong Avenue captures views that are genuinely difficult to find at this price point in greater Brisbane. Properties on the elevated western face have consistently drawn competitive interest from buyers priced out of Coorparoo and Holland Park who are willing to move slightly further south for the size and the outlook.

The suburb's buyer profile is a mix of growing families, long-term owner-occupiers downsizing within the area, and investors who understand that proximity to Griffith University and the Princess Alexandra Hospital precinct creates a resilient rental demand base. That mix keeps the market stable and the days on market figures tight on well-presented homes.

As the autumn selling season opens, Mt Gravatt is one of those suburbs where correctly priced homes with good presentation are still attracting the right buyer quickly. The ones that are sitting are a pricing conversation, not a demand problem.
