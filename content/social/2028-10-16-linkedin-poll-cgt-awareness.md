## Caption

Capital gains tax is one of the costs of selling that sellers who have used a property as an investment often underestimate. The main residence exemption covers most owner-occupiers, but it is not automatic if the property was ever rented out, used partly for business, or held through a structure other than personal name.

For investment properties, the settlement date relative to 30 June determines which financial year the capital gain falls into. Getting that wrong by a few days can shift a significant tax liability.

Most vendors find out their CGT position at tax time, not before the campaign. That is the wrong sequence.

If you sold your Brisbane property today, how confident are you that you know your approximate capital gains tax position?
