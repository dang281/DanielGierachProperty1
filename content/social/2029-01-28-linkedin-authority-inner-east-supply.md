## Caption

One of the consistent features of Brisbane's inner east property market over the past several years has been constrained supply. Not in a dramatic way, but in a steady, structural way that shapes every campaign in suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, Balmoral, and Camp Hill.

The inner east has very limited land for new housing. What is built is mostly infill. The detached house stock in these suburbs does not grow meaningfully year on year. Demand from families wanting to be within 7km of the CBD with access to good schools, the river, and walkable high streets has not gone away.

What this means for sellers heading into the 2029 autumn window is that a well-prepared, correctly priced property in a sought-after part of the inner east is unlikely to wait long for the right buyer. The constraint is not demand. It is the willingness of existing owners to sell.

For buyers, the same supply dynamic means that when a quality property comes to market in the right suburb, the competition is real. Waiting for another one "just like it" in the same street often means waiting six to twelve months.

If you are a homeowner on the fence about selling this autumn, the market conditions are as supportive as they have been in this window. The constraint now is on the supply side, which is exactly where you sit.
