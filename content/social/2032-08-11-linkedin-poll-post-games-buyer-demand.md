## Caption

The 2032 Olympics closed two days ago. For years leading into the Games, there was no shortage of commentary about what the Brisbane property market would do before, during, and after.

Some analysts called a post-Games demand surge as buyers who had been waiting finally moved. Others flagged a correction risk once the Olympic spotlight moved on. Most of it was speculation built on limited comparable data, because Australia has not hosted a Summer Olympics since Sydney in 2000 and markets look very different now.

From what I have seen on the ground in the inner east, the Games period was quieter than a typical July. What happens from here is the open question.

How do you think post-Games buyer demand compares to what the market was expecting?
