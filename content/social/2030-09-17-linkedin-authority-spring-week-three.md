## Caption

Week one of a spring campaign is almost always the busiest. The property is fresh on the market, the photography is new, the buyers who have been waiting for something in this suburb and price range show up in numbers. It is the spike you hope for and plan around.

Week two is where the first real signal arrives. The buyers who attended in week one have had time to think, compare and consult. If there are offers, they tend to come now. If there is silence, that is a signal too, and a good agent is already reading it.

Week three is where the campaign is decided. The properties that maintain strong attendance in week three are the ones where pricing is credible, follow-up has been active and any early feedback has been absorbed and acted on. The properties that start to plateau in week three are usually dealing with one of three things: an asking price that buyers are marking above their ceiling, a presentation issue that surfaced in early feedback and has not been addressed, or a campaign that has not stayed in front of its buyer pool between open homes.

There is no mystery to what separates a strong week three from a slow one. It is whether the first two weeks were treated as active intelligence, not just foot traffic. The buyers who did not offer in week two have told you something. The question is whether you are listening.
