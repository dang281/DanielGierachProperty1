## Caption

We are at the point in the spring season where results start telling a clearer story.

The properties that entered the market in late September and October with realistic price expectations have been resolving. Some went at or above guide. A few well-positioned character homes in Hawthorne, Balmoral, and Norman Park attracted the competitive bidder pools that produce the results people talk about after the campaign ends.

The outlier in mid-November is the property that overpriced in October and has now reduced. That trajectory, starting strong and cutting price mid-campaign, almost always produces a worse outcome than launching correctly in the first place. Buyers track price reductions. A reduction signals that the market has tested the property and found it wanting at the original price. That signal is hard to undo even when the revised price is sensible.

On the demand side, the buyers who have been in the market since August are now at a decision point. They have seen enough stock, attended enough opens, and missed out at enough auctions to have a very clear sense of value. When a property lands at the right price, that experienced buyer group responds quickly. When it does not, they move on.

The last clean weekend before the Christmas slowdown is typically the final weekend of November or the first of December. After that, available buyer numbers shrink and settlement timelines start bumping against the holiday period.

If you have been watching the spring market and considering a move, the window is narrow but it is still open. More at danielgierach.com.
