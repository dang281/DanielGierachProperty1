## Caption

Carina offers a specific trade that buyers in Brisbane's inner east keep coming back to. The blocks are larger than anything available at the same price in Hawthorne, Bulimba, or Norman Park. The suburb is close enough to Carindale and the inner east network to feel connected. The creek frontage sections along Bulimba Creek add a natural boundary and green amenity that buyers from the denser suburbs notice when they first inspect.

The Belmont Hills corridor to the east of Carina shapes the suburb's northern and eastern edge and gives the suburb a landscape context that is less urban than its price point might suggest. Families upgrading from Murarrie or Cannon Hill often land in Carina because the step up in block size is significant and the price step from where they started is achievable.

The land-for-price ratio is the defining feature. In a Brisbane market where inner east land has been appreciating consistently, Carina represents a place where families can still get a four-bedroom home on a block that accommodates a pool, a shed, and some separation from the neighbours. That combination is not available at the same price in the suburbs directly to its west.

The autumn 2031 market in Carina will be driven by the same buyer type that has sustained the suburb through the previous cycle: families who have made a deliberate choice about value and space, and buyers who understand where this corridor is heading.
