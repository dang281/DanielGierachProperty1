## Caption

Investors selling a Brisbane property in the first half of 2034 have a decision to make that is not about the property itself. It is about which financial year the capital gain falls into.

The ATO uses settlement date, not contract signing date, to determine when a capital gain is realised. For an investor selling now, the difference between settling before 30 June and settling after 1 July is the difference between a capital gain falling into the 2033-34 financial year or the 2034-35 year. Depending on what the rest of the investor's income looks like in each of those years, that distinction can represent a meaningful difference in the total tax payable.

Getting this wrong by a few days is surprisingly easy. Standard Queensland settlement terms are 30 to 45 days from contract. If the contract is signed in late May without a specific eye on the settlement date, an intended June settlement can slip into July, shifting the entire tax liability into the following year, which may not be what the investor's accountant planned for.

The article steps through the full picture for investors considering a Brisbane property sale in the first half of 2034, including how to structure the settlement timeline in consultation with your accountant and solicitor.

This is the kind of planning decision that pays for itself in the conversation you have before the campaign launches, not after contracts are exchanged.
