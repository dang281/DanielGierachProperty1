## Caption

The finance condition in a Queensland contract does not wait for your bank. If the deadline lapses and you have not formally extended it or declared finance approved, the condition is gone, whether or not your approval has arrived.

That single fact catches buyers out more often than almost anything else in the purchase process. They assume the condition protects them until the bank gives a clear answer. It does not. The deadline is fixed from the contract date and it runs regardless of where the lender is in its process.

The inclusions question catches a different group. Light fittings, blinds, the built-in dishwasher, the garden shed bolted to the slab. In Queensland, if it is attached to the property it is assumed to stay, unless it is specifically excluded in the contract before signing. What that means in practice is that the seller who plans to take the chandelier they shipped from Europe needs to exclude it before you sign, not after.

These are not obscure technicalities. They are the parts of the standard contract that a solicitor catches in twenty minutes when you get them involved before you sign, rather than after.

The full article walks through the key clauses, the deadlines that matter, and what to check before you commit.
