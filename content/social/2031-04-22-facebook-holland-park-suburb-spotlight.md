## Caption

Holland Park has an unusual combination of features that buyers looking carefully at the inner south-east will identify: elevated land with no significant flood risk, block sizes that are generous relative to the inner-east average, and a school catchment that generates some of the most motivated buyer demand in the corridor.

The Cavendish Road State High School catchment is the structural demand driver. For families who are planning around secondary school enrolment, the catchment boundary is not flexible. Buyers who need to be inside it will stretch their budget to do so, and they will move quickly when the right property comes to market. That urgency is consistent regardless of the broader market mood, and it provides Holland Park vendors with a buyer pool that is not easily substituted.

The suburb's access to Old Cleveland Road gives residents practical connections to retail and public transport that work well for households without complete car dependence. The flat, drivable grid of streets in Holland Park is easy to navigate and functions well for families at any stage.

Properties on the higher streets, where the block sits above the surrounding rooflines and the aspect opens up, are the ones buyers identify when they first map Holland Park. Vendors on those streets are typically dealing with buyers who have specifically sought them out rather than arrived by default.
