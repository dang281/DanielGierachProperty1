## Caption

Three months of market observation from Brisbane's inner east. Here is what the July to September cycle actually delivered.

Winter produced fewer listings than spring, as it always does, but the properties that did come to market moved well when they were priced correctly and presented properly. The buyer pool in July and August was smaller in number but stronger in intent than many vendors expected. Upgraders who had already sold were the most active group, followed by investors who had completed their post-EOFY reassessment and were ready to deploy capital.

August was the critical preparation month for spring vendors. The ones who used it well arrived in September with campaigns that were ready to open at full strength. The ones who did not have been spending the first weeks of spring catching up, which is a real cost even if it does not show up in a dollar figure.

September confirmed the pattern I have seen in previous spring cycles in the inner east. Buyer attendance lifted sharply in the first two weekends, contract requests came early in well-priced campaigns, and the gap between prepared and unprepared properties widened. The market rewarded specificity: properties with a clear story, a defined buyer, and a price grounded in comparables attracted competition. Properties that were priced aspirationally and presented as generically as possible attracted hesitation.

Heading into Q4, the buyer pool remains active. School catchment urgency increases through October and November. Vendors who have been waiting will find a ready market, provided they enter it with realistic expectations and genuine preparation.
