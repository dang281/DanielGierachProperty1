## Caption

Griffith University's campus at Upper Mt Gravatt generates a sustained demand pool that most outer Brisbane suburbs can't access. Academic staff, researchers, and education-sector professionals regularly seek to buy close to the campus, and that keeps Mt Gravatt's buyer base broad in a way that doesn't always get enough attention.

Mt Gravatt Outlook gives the suburb its most distinct feature: streets at elevation with genuine views across the south. Properties on those high streets are a category of their own. Buyers who discover them often don't look anywhere else. The view factor, combined with the South East Freeway access for the drive to work, makes the suburb highly practical for professionals.

The Plaza shopping precinct handles the retail and dining needs without requiring residents to leave the suburb. The mix of long-term residents and newer arrivals gives Mt Gravatt an interesting social mix: established families who've been here for decades alongside buyers who've recently moved up from a smaller property or across from another inner suburb.

November is a strong month for Mt Gravatt. The weather is good, properties present well outdoors, and buyer activity picks up in the lead-up to December.

The homes that attract the most competition here are elevated blocks with views, well-presented interiors, and practical outdoor space. Flat properties near the main road corridors attract price-driven buyers rather than the emotional end of the market.
