## Caption

Net equity is not sale price minus loan balance. Most vendors do not factor in the full cost of selling, and the gap between what they expect and what actually lands in their account can be $40,000 to $60,000 on a $1.4 million sale.

Agent commission, conveyancing fees, marketing costs, and the mortgage discharge fee all come out of the sale proceeds before you see a dollar. These are not hidden costs. They are predictable costs that most vendors simply have not added up before they decide to list.

The mortgage discharge fee deserves particular attention. It is charged by your lender to close your home loan at settlement. For a variable rate mortgage, it is typically a flat administrative fee and is not prohibitive. For a vendor on a fixed rate loan, the calculation is very different. Fixed rate break costs are calculated based on the interest rate differential between your contracted rate and the current rate, multiplied by the outstanding loan balance and the time remaining on the fixed period. In a rate environment where that differential is meaningful, the break cost can add thousands of dollars to the cost of selling.

Vendors on a fixed rate who are considering listing should contact their lender for a break cost estimate before they accept an offer. Once the contract is signed, that cost is locked in.
