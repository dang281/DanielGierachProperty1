## Caption

East Brisbane sits at the edge of the CBD fringe and the Woolloongabba precinct, and that position shapes who buys here. The buyer profile is predominantly CBD-focused professionals who want to minimise their commute, alongside investors who understand the rental demand that comes with that location. It is a suburb with a clear use case rather than a broad one.

Public transport access is strong. Multiple bus routes and proximity to the Gabba precinct mean car dependence is low for the right buyer, and that matters increasingly to the professional households who are most active in this market. The housing stock mixes detached houses with townhouses, which broadens the entry price range and keeps a range of buyer types engaged.

Spring in East Brisbane tends to look different from the family-oriented suburbs further south. The October buyer here is often time-constrained by professional schedules rather than school calendars, and motivation tends to be high once they identify the right property. Properties that are well located within the suburb, close to transport corridors and the Woolloongabba activity, attract attention from the first open home.

If you own in East Brisbane and are considering a sale, understanding the professional buyer demographic and how to reach it is part of the conversation I am happy to have with you.
