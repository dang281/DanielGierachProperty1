## Caption

Most vendors skip January because they assume buyers are away. That assumption costs some of them money.

The buyers attending open homes in January are not casually curious. They have usually been searching since October, missed out on something in November or December, and are now motivated to act before the autumn season creates more competition. That profile, determined, pre-approved, and not distracted by other campaigns, is exactly what a seller wants in the room.

The inner east suburbs, Bulimba, Balmoral, Hawthorne, tend to see lighter stock levels in January. Fewer listings means your property is not one of twelve Queenslanders going to market on the same weekend. You get the full attention of an active buyer pool rather than a fraction of it.

The counterargument is presentation: January heat and school holidays can make properties harder to photograph and open homes trickier to schedule. That is fair. But for a property that photographs well in summer light and a vendor who is genuinely ready, January is a window that other sellers are not using.

The decision to sell should follow preparation, not the calendar. If your property is ready, if your legal work is in order, and if you have had a realistic conversation about price, January has more going for it than most people think.
