## Caption

Sellers in Queensland regularly accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one. This is not sentimentality. It is a rational financial decision.

A conditional offer, subject to finance and building and pest, opens a two to three week window where any number of things can unravel. Finance can fall over. The building and pest report can surface a surprise the buyer uses to renegotiate or exit. A buyer can change their mind and walk away for just 0.25% of the purchase price. On an $800,000 property, that exit costs the buyer $2,000 and costs the vendor weeks of lost time, a campaign that has gone cold, and the psychological weight of starting again.

An unconditional offer eliminates that window entirely. The contract becomes binding from exchange. For a vendor who needs certainty, that removal of risk has real dollar value.

In Queensland, an unconditional offer means the buyer has completed their due diligence before signing. That typically means finance pre-approval is solid, the building and pest has already been done on the property, or the buyer is a cash purchaser. It is not a decision to make casually.

Understanding what unconditional actually means, the risk the buyer absorbs, and when it genuinely makes sense to offer unconditionally is the subject of the article below.
