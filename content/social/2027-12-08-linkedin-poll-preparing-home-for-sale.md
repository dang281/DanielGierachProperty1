## Caption

The preparation phase before a property launches to market is where the campaign is largely won or lost. A property that launches well-presented and correctly priced tends to find its buyer in the first two to three weeks. A property that launches underprepared or at an aspirational price tends to sit, accumulate days on market, and sell for less than it would have with better preparation.

The question is always where to direct the preparation budget and effort. Not everything makes a measurable difference to buyers. Some improvements earn back their cost and then some. Others are satisfying to the vendor but invisible to the buyer pool.

When preparing a home for sale, which factor do you think matters most?

## Poll options
