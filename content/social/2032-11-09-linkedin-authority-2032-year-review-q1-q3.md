## Caption

With the year running into its final quarter, it is worth looking back at what 2032 actually delivered for the inner east.

The first quarter was a strong autumn campaign season. Buyers were active, stock was moving at a pace that sellers felt positive about, and the conditions through February and March were as good as the inner east had seen in a few years. That part of the year felt straightforward.

Then Q2 arrived, and it did not. Pre-Games hesitation is the honest description. Sellers held back. Some were waiting to see what the Olympics would do to values, to visitor activity, to the general feel of the city. Buyers anticipated some kind of shift without being sure which direction it would go. The result was a quieter listing environment than the strong Q1 had suggested was coming.

July and August were quiet, which should surprise no one. The Games ran through that period, and the inner east was at the centre of the activity. Open homes and property decisions were not the priority for most people. September then came back sharper than I expected. Once the closing ceremony was done, buyers who had been waiting refocused quickly.

The honest observation is that the market absorbed a major global event running through its middle and emerged in reasonable shape. That is not a trivial thing. The inner east has always had a depth of owner-occupier demand that insulates it from short-term disruption. This year tested that, and it held.

The final quarter now is being shaped by the buyers that patience produced.
