## Caption

Hawthorne shares the Oxford Street corridor with Bulimba and captures the same river access, the same school catchments, and the same professional and family buyer base. What distinguishes it within that corridor is a slightly more varied housing mix, with Queenslanders and post-war homes sitting alongside a growing number of townhouses that appeal to buyers who want the address without the maintenance of a character home.

Hawthorne Garage has become a genuine social anchor for the suburb, drawing residents from across the inner east on weekends and adding a food and coffee culture that reinforces the neighbourhood's appeal. River access points and the connection to the Hawthorne Ferry are lifestyle features that buyers factor into their decision-making, particularly those who work near the CBD and want to avoid driving.

Balmoral State School catchment covers the majority of Hawthorne, and that consistently channels a family buyer profile into the suburb's auction rooms. Well-presented Queenslanders on streets like Hawthorne Road and Ward Street generate strong competition. The townhouse market appeals to a different buyer: downsizers, single professionals, and couples who want low maintenance without sacrificing the inner east lifestyle.
