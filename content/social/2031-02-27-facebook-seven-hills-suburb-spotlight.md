## Caption

Seven Hills does not appear in many buyers' shortlists at the start of their search. By the end of it, a meaningful number of them are asking why they overlooked it.

The suburb sits on a ridge that runs above neighbouring Coorparoo and Holland Park, and the views from the upper streets across the city and surrounding district are the kind that buyers in more prominent suburbs pay a significant premium to access. Seven Hills offers those same views at a price point that still reflects the suburb's relative underrecognition. That gap is not infinite, and it has been closing steadily.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war character, with larger blocks than what buyers find in Hawthorne or Bulimba. The suburb is well-positioned for access to Chatsworth Road and the Pacific Motorway, which suits buyers whose work or lifestyle requires flexibility beyond the CBD commute corridor. Coorparoo State School catchment questions sometimes draw buyers to the area, though Seven Hills has its own school options worth understanding directly.

The buyer profile here tends to be the second-time buyer: someone who has owned property before, who understands that the suburbs with the most foot traffic and the most media coverage are not always the ones that deliver the strongest long-term performance. They arrive at open homes in late February prepared and ready to act.

For Seven Hills homeowners, this autumn is a market where being well-prepared matters.
