## Caption

By the second week of January, buyer enquiry in the inner east has typically returned to levels that would surprise most people who assume the market is still on holidays.

The buyers who resume their search in early January are not the ones who are casually considering a move. They are the ones who spent December making up their minds. Some attended an inspection or two before Christmas and used the break to discuss and decide. Others have been watching a suburb for months and are returning with a budget approved and a clear brief. These buyers are motivated in a way that is distinct from the broader market you see in the autumn peak.

The contrast is stark on the supply side. Most vendors are still weeks away from being ready. Some are waiting for tradespeople. Others are timing their campaign around school terms or waiting for what feels like the "right" moment to launch. That hesitation is understandable, but it means the market in January is often characterised by strong buyer competition for a limited pool of properties.

For a vendor whose home is in good order, or who has been preparing through December, this window is worth taking seriously. Fewer listings means less for buyers to compare you against. When the autumn volume arrives in March and April, the competitive landscape looks very different.
