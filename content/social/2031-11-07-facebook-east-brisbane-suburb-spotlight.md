## Caption

The Gabba is at East Brisbane's doorstep, and so is the Woolloongabba entertainment precinct that has been building around it for years. This is an inner-city address in all but name, and the buyer profile here reflects that clearly.

East Brisbane sits close enough to the CBD that buyers who work in the city treat it as a genuinely practical choice, not a compromise. East Brisbane State School anchors the family side of the market, but the dominant buyer group here is young professionals and downsizers who want the inner-city lifestyle without the density of South Brisbane or Fortitude Valley. The character homes that survive here are genuinely prized because there's limited land and development pressure keeps eating into the supply.

The mix of townhouses and older character homes means the market has multiple tiers operating simultaneously. Buyers who want freestanding character in this location will pay significantly above those who are open to a townhouse. That spread creates opportunities at entry level but also means understanding your specific product and its position is critical.

Properties with good light, indoor-outdoor connection, and parking sell fast. East Brisbane has limited street parking, and buyers know it. Off-street parking adds real money here.
