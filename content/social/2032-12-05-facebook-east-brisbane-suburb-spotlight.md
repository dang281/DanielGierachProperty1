## Caption

East Brisbane has a particular appeal to buyers who want CBD proximity without paying for Paddington or New Farm. The Woolloongabba precinct gives the suburb a genuine urban edge, and the public transport connections mean professionals can live here without relying on a car for the daily commute.

The buyer profile here tends to skew toward working couples and single professionals who know exactly what they want: a character home or well-positioned townhouse, manageable land, and fifteen minutes to the office. They research the suburb carefully before they start, so by the time they come through an open home they are ready to act.

December in East Brisbane has its own rhythm. Some owners wind down, but buyers in this market rarely stop. Many are professionals whose decision-making sharpened through the second half of the year and who want to settle before February. Listing ahead of the holiday pause puts your property in front of that motivated group before competition from January listings arrives.

If you own in East Brisbane and the idea of selling in 2033 has crossed your mind, an appraisal now gives you the clearest possible starting point.
