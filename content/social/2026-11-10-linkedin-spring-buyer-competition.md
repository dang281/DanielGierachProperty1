## Caption

By mid-November, open home attendance in Brisbane's inner east has settled into a pattern I recognise from the last few years. The numbers are not as high as the first week of October, when buyers who have been out of the market all winter come back all at once. What remains is a smaller, more focused group.

These are buyers who have been through enough open homes to know exactly what they want and what they will pay. A four-bedroom house in the Whites Hill State College catchment, presented well and priced correctly, draws five to eight qualified groups. Not thirty. But those eight are ready to write offers.

The implication for sellers is straightforward. Marketing at this point in the year is not about generating the widest possible audience. It is about making sure the right people see your property, form a clear impression of value, and have a reason to move.

What tends to underperform in this window: properties priced above the range of what buyers have seen settle over the past 60 days. What tends to perform: properties that are prepared, priced on market, and presented in a way that justifies the number being asked.

The pool is smaller than July. The quality of the buyers in it is higher.
