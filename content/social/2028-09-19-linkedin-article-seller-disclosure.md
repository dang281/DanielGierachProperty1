## Caption

Under the Property Law Act 2023 (Qld), sellers are required to provide a completed Seller Disclosure Statement before any contract is entered into. Not at signing. Before.

That requirement is more significant than it sounds. The disclosure statement covers matters including body corporate information for strata properties, unregistered encumbrances, pool safety certificates, smoke alarm compliance, and land use restrictions. If the statement is not provided before contract, the buyer has the right to terminate the contract at any point before settlement, without any penalty.

That is not a theoretical risk. It means a seller who skips the disclosure requirement or provides an incomplete statement has potentially given the buyer a walk-away right that extends all the way to settlement day. After weeks of open homes, negotiations, and preparation for settlement.

The practical response is straightforward: have the disclosure statement prepared as part of your pre-launch checklist, not as something you hand over when the contract is already being signed. Your solicitor or conveyancer can prepare it. Allow time to gather the relevant documents before the campaign starts.

The full article covers what must be included, when it must be provided, and what the consequences are if it is not.
