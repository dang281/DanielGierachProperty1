## Caption

Coorparoo is one of the larger inner-south suburbs and its diversity of housing stock reflects that scale. From the elevated streets on the western ridge near Cavendish Road through to the flatter residential blocks closer to Old Cleveland Road, the suburb offers a range of buyer entry points that keeps demand broad across market conditions. Streets like Cavendish Road, Chatsworth Road, and Mary Street each carry their own residential character, and buyers who know Coorparoo well target specific pockets rather than the suburb generally.

The Coorparoo train station, on the Cleveland line, is one of the suburb's practical anchors, putting the CBD within 10 to 15 minutes and making the suburb genuinely viable for daily commuters. The Coorparoo Square shopping centre provides a full retail and dining offer that has progressively matured, and the daily convenience of that precinct shapes how families and couples assess the suburb against alternatives further out.

Villanova College on Old Cleveland Road and Coorparoo State School both draw families into deliberate suburb selection, and the concentration of private school options across this part of Brisbane makes Coorparoo a practical central point for multi-child households with different schooling requirements. Post-war Queenslanders on the elevated western streets with city views generate the most competitive buyer fields, particularly where the aspect and outdoor living have been developed thoughtfully.
