## Caption

The finance condition in a Queensland contract lapses on the deadline date regardless of whether the bank has approved the loan.

This is one of the most commonly misunderstood points in a Queensland property transaction, and the consequences of misunderstanding it are significant.

A buyer whose finance approval is still in progress when the deadline arrives is not protected by the condition simply because the application is active. The condition has a deadline. If that deadline passes without formal approval in hand and the buyer has not formally extended the condition by agreement with the vendor, the condition lapses. From that point, the contract is either unconditional or it is at risk.

Most buyers who find themselves in this position assume they are still covered. They believe the condition protects them until the bank comes back with an answer. That is not how the contract works. The condition clock runs from the contract date regardless of what is happening at the bank.

This is one of the most avoidable moments of distress in a Queensland property transaction. A solicitor who reviews the contract before it is signed and monitors key dates during the condition period catches this before it becomes a problem.

The finance condition deadline, the building and pest condition deadline, and the settlement date are three dates in every Queensland contract that deserve attention from the moment the contract is signed. Missing any of them can have consequences that were entirely avoidable.
