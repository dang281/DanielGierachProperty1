## Caption

On a $1 million purchase, Queensland transfer duty adds approximately $38,025. Most buyers underestimate this figure by at least $10,000.

Transfer duty (still commonly called stamp duty) is one of the largest upfront costs in a property purchase and one of the most consistently underestimated. Buyers who budget for a deposit and legal fees and forget about duty run into a problem that is discovered at a difficult point in the process.

A few things worth knowing:

Transfer duty in Queensland is calculated on the higher of the purchase price or the dutiable value of the property. For a standard residential purchase these are usually the same, but it is worth confirming with your solicitor.

First home buyer concessions can reduce or eliminate transfer duty on eligible properties, but the thresholds and conditions apply. The first home owner grant operates separately and has its own eligibility criteria.

Investors pay the standard rate with no concessions. On a $1.2 million investment property the duty figure is materially higher than on the same purchase with owner-occupier eligibility.

The duty is payable on settlement, not on signing. But your solicitor and broker need to know it is in your budget from the beginning. It affects how much you can genuinely afford to offer.

For inner east properties where the median sits above $1 million, this is not a minor line item. It is a five-figure cost that needs to be in the budget before you start inspecting.
