## Caption

The method of sale is one of the most consequential decisions in a campaign, and it receives far less attention than photography or pricing.

Auction creates urgency. Buyers must act on a fixed date without conditions, and price discovery happens publicly. That competition, when the buyer pool is deep and the property is genuinely suited to bidding, produces the best outcomes the market can deliver. When the buyer pool is thin or the property's value is highly individualised, an auction can work against the vendor.

Private treaty gives buyers conditions and time. A finance clause, a building and pest inspection, a longer settlement. For properties where the right buyer is specific but might not exist within a three-week auction window, private treaty finds them. The risk is that without deadline pressure, negotiations can drag and conditional contracts can fall over.

Expression of interest suits properties where genuine buyers may place very different valuations on the same asset. A development site, a large landholding, a home with a feature that only certain buyers will value highly. An EOI process invites those buyers to show their hand without anchoring to a single advertised price.

The decision should follow the property and the buyer pool. What has worked two doors down is not necessarily the right answer for this house, this street, this season. Getting the method wrong means running the right property through the wrong process, and that costs vendors money they rarely recover.
