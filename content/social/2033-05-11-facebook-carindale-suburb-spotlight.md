## Caption

Carindale is a suburb built around family living, and the evidence of that is visible across the streetscape. Wide blocks, established gardens, good school access and proximity to Westfield Carindale create a lifestyle that suits families at the stage where space and practicality matter more than inner-city proximity. At around ten kilometres from the CBD, the suburb occupies a comfortable middle-ring position with road access that makes the commute manageable.

Westfield Carindale is one of Brisbane's larger shopping centres and it anchors the suburb's commercial identity. Having major retail, dining, medical services and entertainment within a short walk or drive is a convenience that residents value, and it is consistently cited by buyers as a reason the suburb appeals. The shopping centre also generates local employment that contributes to the suburb's stability as a community.

The housing stock in Carindale tends toward family-sized homes on reasonable land parcels, with a mix of original homes from the 1980s and 1990s alongside more recently built properties. Renovation and extension of existing homes is common, reflecting owners who have invested in the suburb for the long term. Current medians in Carindale offer a compelling comparison with inner suburbs, with buyers receiving more land and internal space for their money.

School options in and around Carindale support the family buyer demographic, and the suburb's quiet suburban feel is a deliberate choice for many who move there.
