## Caption

Morningside's market in 2030 has reflected something that is becoming a consistent feature of the inner east: buyers willing to pay a premium for the elevated streets with views, and buyers who find genuine value in the flatter sections where the price-to-size ratio works more in their favour.

The streets above Wynnum Road on the ridge that overlooks the bay corridor attract a buyer who has usually spent time in Hawthorne and Balmoral and has concluded that Morningside delivers comparable views and character at a price point that still leaves room to manoeuvre. Those buyers are sophisticated and their offer behaviour reflects that.

The Morningside café strip on Wynnum Road is one of the most underrated suburban commercial strips in the inner east. It has the café and restaurant depth of a strip three or four times its size, and the Saturday morning foot traffic reflects a community that genuinely uses it. Buyers from other suburbs often discover it at their first open home visit and mention it consistently in feedback.

Cannon Hill Anglican College's proximity, combined with Morningside State School for primary, gives families a school proposition that covers both private and state options in the one suburb.

For Morningside homeowners considering a November campaign, the buyer depth for well-positioned elevated properties remains solid through to the end of the month.
