## Caption

Old Cleveland Road runs through the middle of Coorparoo and brings with it the kind of transport access that makes inner east buyers stop and reconsider suburbs they'd previously dismissed. Buses into the city are frequent and the stop is within walking distance of most streets here. For buyers who don't want to drive to work, that matters.

Coorparoo State School anchors the family buyer side of the market. Greenslopes Hospital to the north gives the suburb the same professional buyer draw that benefits Mt Gravatt East. The food scene along the Old Cleveland Road strip has been developing steadily, and there's enough there now that residents rarely need to go further for a decent meal.

The housing stock is more varied here than in most inner east suburbs. Queenslanders, 1970s brick homes, and newer infill sit side by side. That variety means there are entry points across a wider price range, which broadens the buyer pool. First-home buyers, investors, and upsizers all compete for different parts of the market.

What moves quickly in Coorparoo is the well-located Queenslander on a quiet street. What takes longer is the 1970s brick home on a main road or one that needs significant capital work without a price that reflects it.
