## Caption

Hawthorne sits between Bulimba and Balmoral and shares the riverfront character that defines that stretch of the inner east. The streets running from Hawthorne Road toward the river, particularly those that access the Hawthorne Riverwalk and the Hawthorne Ferry terminal, are among the most consistently in-demand residential addresses in Brisbane.

The suburb's food and retail precinct along Hawthorne Road is a specific draw. The concentration of quality cafes, independent food retailers, and the Hawthorne Cinema makes it a suburb that functions as a complete neighbourhood rather than a dormitory. Buyers who have chosen Hawthorne tend to be specific about it, and that specificity shows up in the quality of buyer registrations at open homes.

The broader Hawthorne market includes a mix of elevated Queenslanders, postwar brick homes, and contemporary rebuilds. The Queenslanders on the elevated streets with river or city views represent the top of the suburb's market and attract interstate and international buyer interest alongside the local pool.

March is one of the strongest months to campaign in Hawthorne. The autumn buyer pool is at its most committed, and the suburb's combination of liveability factors means that well-positioned properties draw competition from buyers who have already decided this is where they want to be.

End of Q1 is also a useful moment to assess timing. If you are thinking about selling in the second quarter, the preparation conversation needs to start now.
