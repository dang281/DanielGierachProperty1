## Caption

Buyers who are still actively searching in late November have generally been at it since September. That means they have inspected many properties, tracked what has sold and at what price, and in a number of cases made an offer that did not work out. By the time they walk through a well-priced property in late November, they are not early in the process. They are in the final stages of a search that has already taken months.

That experience changes how they behave at an open home and at the negotiating table. They do not need to be convinced that the suburb is good. They already know the comparable sales. They have usually had a pre-approval sitting in place for weeks. When they find the right property, they do not need much time to make a decision, because they have already done most of the decision-making during all the properties they inspected before this one.

There is also a timing factor that sharpens their motivation. A buyer in late November can see the December-January slowdown coming. They know that if they do not secure something before Christmas, they are likely restarting in February with the rest of the market. That prospect concentrates the mind. Offers that come through in late November are often cleaner in terms and faster in execution than offers from the same buyers would have been earlier in the campaign season.

I find these buyers straightforward to work with, because the ambiguity of the early search period is gone. The questions are practical rather than exploratory. The decisions are made with real information rather than speculation.

If you have a property ready to go and are weighing timing, late November is not a soft window. It is a focused one.
