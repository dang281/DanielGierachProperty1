## Caption

The cooling-off period in Queensland is one of the most misunderstood parts of the residential contract. Both buyers and sellers regularly get the details wrong, and the misunderstanding can cost money.

Here is what actually applies under Queensland law.

Buyers of residential property have a five-business-day cooling-off period from the date the fully signed contract is received. During that window, a buyer can exit the contract, but it costs 0.25 percent of the purchase price. On a $1.2 million purchase, that is $3,000. Not zero.

The cooling-off period cannot be waived by the vendor. It is a statutory right for the buyer. Vendors sometimes believe they can negotiate it away. They cannot.

There is no cooling-off period for properties purchased at auction. A buyer who wins a property at auction is immediately bound by an unconditional contract. This is one of the reasons lenders require finance and building inspections to be completed before auction, not after.

The practical implication for buyers: the cooling-off period exists as a safety net, not a strategy. Using it costs money. The preparation that should happen before signing, finance pre-approval, solicitor review, building inspection, is designed to make the cooling-off period irrelevant.
