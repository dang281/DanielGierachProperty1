## Caption

Most people don't sell their home because the market is right. They sell because something changed in their life and the market is the context they are working within. I've worked with vendors selling because of a job relocation, a growing family, a divorce, a deceased estate, or simply because a neighbour sold and they realised they'd been sitting on more equity than they understood. The market data matters, but it rarely drives the decision on its own. I'm curious what actually moves people. What would most likely trigger you to sell your current home?

## Poll options

- A life change (family, work, relationship)
- Reaching a financial goal or equity milestone
- The right property to buy becomes available
- Sustained strong market conditions
