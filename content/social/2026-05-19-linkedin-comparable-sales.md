## Caption

When an agent gives you a price guide, they lean on comparable sales. Recent transactions of similar properties nearby. It is the most objective data available, and it is also the most misread.

Here is what comparables cannot do.

They cannot account for a market that has moved since the sale date. A comparable from six months ago in a rising market can be materially below where things are trading now. In the inner east, I have seen the gap between a six-month-old sale and current value run to 5% or more in streets with very low turnover.

They cannot account for what was not disclosed. A comparable might sit below the street average because the building report was difficult, or the vendor needed a fast settlement, or the buyer was the neighbour and paid below market to avoid a campaign. You often do not know which of these applies.

And they cannot account for what is different about your property. Orientation, setback, flood status, school catchment, and proximity to parkland all affect value in ways that a price-per-square-metre comparison misses.

Comparables set the context. A good agent reads beyond the surface of each transaction to understand what the number actually represents.

If you are getting a feel for the value of your home in Bulimba, Hawthorne, Camp Hill, or any of the inner east suburbs I work in, I am happy to walk through what the recent sales data actually means.
