## Caption

Having run open homes through two consecutive winters in the inner east, a comparison between this year and last is worth putting on paper.

The buyer pool composition in winter 2031 has been broadly similar to winter 2030 in one important respect: the buyers attending open homes are almost all genuine candidates. They are not window shoppers. They have usually been searching for several months and they come through properties with a specific checklist already formed. That pattern held in 2030 and it has held this year.

Where I have noticed a difference in 2031 is in the confidence with which buyers are making decisions. In winter 2030, there was more hesitation in the middle price range, more buyers who were present but stalling. This winter, the buyer who attends a well-presented property and finds it matches their requirements is moving faster to a conversation about price. That is partly a reflection of a buyer pool that has been active for longer without finding what they need, and partly a reflection of a market where the inventory of well-prepared properties in the inner east has remained constrained.

On listing competition, 2031 is consistent with 2030. Quality listings are not sitting for extended periods when they are priced accurately. The properties that have taken time are the ones where the price guide did not reflect the current comparable sales, or where the preparation was incomplete when the campaign launched.

The profile of a strong result in both winters looks the same: well-prepared property, realistic price guide from day one, method of sale matched to the buyer demographic, and a vendor who is ready to engage when the right buyer shows interest. That pattern does not change between seasons. What changes is how quickly it plays out.
