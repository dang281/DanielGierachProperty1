## Caption

Transfer duty on a $1 million property purchase in Queensland depends on who is buying. An owner-occupier using the home concession pays approximately $30,850. An investor or second-property buyer pays the standard rate of approximately $38,025. That gap of over $7,000 matters when you are working out how much cash you actually need at settlement.

Neither figure appears in the purchase price. Neither adds to your equity. And neither is optional. Transfer duty is payable within 30 days of the contract becoming unconditional, typically managed through your conveyancer at settlement.

For buyers focused on saving a deposit, this is the number that catches people off guard. A 10% deposit on a $1M property is $100K. Add transfer duty, legal fees, and building and pest inspection costs, and the real upfront number is closer to $140K to $150K for an owner-occupier, and slightly higher for an investor.

First home buyers purchasing a new home in Queensland pay zero transfer duty with no price cap (from May 2025). For existing homes, a full concession applies up to $700K and a partial concession up to $800K. Above $800K on an existing home, there is no first home buyer concession. Most first home buyers in the inner east are shopping above that threshold, which limits the benefit unless they are buying new.

Foreign buyers pay additional duty on top of all standard rates.

Understanding what you are actually budgeting for before you make an offer is basic preparation. The full breakdown is at the link.
