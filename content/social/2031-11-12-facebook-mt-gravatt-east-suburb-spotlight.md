## Caption

Buyers who've spent months looking in Holland Park and Camp Hill with a budget that keeps falling short often end up in Mt Gravatt East. The suburb has a strong renovation pipeline right now, which tells you something about where confidence sits among owners who bought in recent years.

The garden character of Mt Gravatt East appeals to a buyer who wants usable outdoor space. Blocks here tend to be practical: enough for a garden, a shed, and room for kids or a dog. The school catchment for Mt Gravatt State School gives families a clear primary option. Greenslopes Private Hospital's proximity means the professional healthcare workforce remains a consistent buyer group.

The streets closest to the Holland Park boundary have been seeing the most activity. Buyers who want the Holland Park feel with a slightly lower price point have been discovering that the practical differences between the suburbs are smaller than the address difference implies. That discovery process tends to lift prices in the border areas first.

Empty nesters also feature in the buyer mix here. The suburb's low-fuss, garden-oriented character suits people looking to scale back from a larger property without leaving the inner south completely.

A renovated home on a private block with good indoor-outdoor flow moves quickly in this market. An original home in sound condition also finds buyers, typically those with renovation intent and a realistic budget.
