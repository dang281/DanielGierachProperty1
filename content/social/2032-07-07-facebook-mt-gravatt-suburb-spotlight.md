## Caption

Mt Gravatt is anchored by three things that generate consistent buyer demand regardless of where the broader market is sitting: the Showgrounds, Mt Gravatt State High School, and Westfield Garden City.

The school is one of Queensland's largest public high schools, and families targeting that catchment drive a dependable segment of owner-occupier demand in the mid-price range. Post-war brick and tile homes on 600 to 800 square metre blocks are the dominant stock, and these properties suit the family buyer profile that arrives here specifically for the school.

The investor case runs parallel. Griffith University's Nathan campus is close enough to keep rental demand steady, and Princess Alexandra Hospital adds a second permanent employment anchor that supports that demand. Buyers who are thinking about yield rather than lifestyle find a different argument in Mt Gravatt than they do in the river corridor suburbs.

The Mains Road ridge offers elevated terrain with genuine views across Brisbane that buyers who have not explored the suburb's street grid sometimes miss.

Mt Gravatt is not a suburb that relies on one buyer type to hold its market. That breadth is a practical advantage for owners considering a sale.
