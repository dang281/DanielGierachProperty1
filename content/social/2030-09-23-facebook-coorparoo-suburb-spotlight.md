## Caption

Coorparoo's Old Cleveland Road strip has matured into one of the more complete café and retail corridors in Brisbane's inner south-east. Combined with a school catchment that includes Coorparoo State School, Villanova College, and Loreto College, the suburb draws a family buyer base that is broad, well-resourced, and consistent. Buyers here are rarely opportunistic: they have chosen Coorparoo specifically.

The housing mix is one of the most varied in the inner east. Character Queenslanders and post-war homes share the residential streets with a growing stock of apartments and townhouses, which means Coorparoo has genuine depth across price brackets. That breadth of product makes it a suburb where both first-home buyers entering the apartment market and established families stepping into a four-bedroom Queenslander are active at the same time.

The apartment and townhouse market deserves particular attention. Buyers who want an inner-east address without a freestanding home budget are finding Coorparoo competitive with Greenslopes and Stones Corner on price but stronger on school catchment appeal. That distinction shapes demand in ways that support property values across the board.

Sellers in Coorparoo have the advantage of consistent, multi-segment buyer demand. If you have been thinking about the timing of a sale, an accurate appraisal is the most useful tool you have right now.
