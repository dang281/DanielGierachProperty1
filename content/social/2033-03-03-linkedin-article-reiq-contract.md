## Caption

One of the most costly misunderstandings in Queensland property transactions involves the finance condition in the REIQ contract of sale. Buyers who miss the finance condition deadline frequently assume they are still covered because their bank approval is still in progress. They are not.

The finance condition in a standard REIQ contract has a specific deadline date. If the buyer has not formally given notice of finance approval by that date, the condition lapses. Once it lapses, the contract is at risk: depending on the circumstances, the buyer may have lost the protection the condition was designed to provide.

This is not a technicality that solicitors can resolve after the fact. The deadline is the deadline. And in a busy autumn campaign, where a buyer may have submitted their finance application to multiple lenders, or where a bank is processing a complex income assessment, the deadline can arrive before the bank has responded.

The solution is practical: when the finance condition is being negotiated, build in adequate time. If a lender typically takes twelve to fifteen business days to formally approve, a fourteen-day finance condition is not generous. And if the deadline is approaching and approval has not been confirmed, apply for an extension before the deadline, not after.

Your solicitor should be tracking this. But the buyer who understands the mechanism will not find themselves in the position of having to rely on that.
