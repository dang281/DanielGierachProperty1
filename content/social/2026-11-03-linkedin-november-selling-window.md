## Caption

There are roughly six weeks left before Brisbane's property market goes quiet for the summer break. In practical terms, that means a campaign launched this week or next week can still reach settlement before Christmas. A campaign launched in the last week of November is likely to settle in January, which is a different proposition.

This matters for sellers who have been watching and waiting.

The buyers active in the inner east right now have been in the market since at least August. They know the streets, they have their finance ready, and they are not browsing. A well-prepared property launching this week hits that pool at full readiness.

What I consistently see in this window is that the first weekend generates the most concentrated interest. Buyers who have been watching Morningside, Camp Hill, or Hawthorne for months will move quickly when the right property appears. The risk for a seller is not a shortage of buyers. It is launching a property that is not ready to compete.

If you are considering listing before Christmas, the preparation window is closing. Properties that go live the second or third week of November with strong marketing, correct pricing, and good presentation tend to perform well. Properties that rush to market at the end of November are competing for a rapidly thinning buyer pool.

The question for December is not whether the market is strong. It is whether you are positioned to capture it.
