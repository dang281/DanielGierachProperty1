## Caption

Two houses on the same street in Hawthorne can be $200,000 apart in value. Same number of bedrooms, similar land size, built in the same era. The difference is aspect, flood risk, the quality of what has been done inside, and whether the back yard gets afternoon sun.

No automated tool can see any of that. Online valuation models use settled sales data, which in Queensland is typically two to four months behind the contract date. In a market that has moved, that lag means the estimate you are looking at describes a different market than the one you are selling into.

What actually determines your property's value in Brisbane's inner east is land configuration, aspect and orientation, renovation quality and what it has actually solved, school catchment, and the level of buyer competition for your specific property type in your suburb right now. These factors interact in ways that vary street by street. A 600 square metre block with a north-facing rear yard and rear lane access is a different asset to a 600 square metre block that is irregular and south-facing, even if they are listed as comparable.

A proper appraisal is not a number pulled from a database. It is a comparison against three to five genuinely similar recent sales, conducted by someone who has been inside those comparable properties, who can explain exactly which sales they used and why. If an agent cannot walk you through the reasoning clearly, the figure they have given you is not worth much.
