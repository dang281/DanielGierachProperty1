## Caption

The main residence capital gains tax exemption is not automatic if you ever rented your home. Most vendors do not know this. Most find out mid-campaign.

Vendors who rented their property for any period, even briefly, may have a partial CGT obligation when they sell. The exemption applies only to the portion of ownership during which the property was your primary residence. If you bought in 2019, lived there for two years, rented it out for three years, and then moved back in, your CGT position is not the same as someone who lived there continuously.

The 6-year rule allows you to treat a property as your main residence for up to 6 years while it is rented out, provided you do not nominate another property as your main residence during that period. But there are conditions, and those conditions have a habit of not being met in exactly the right way without specific advice.

The inner east has a high proportion of vendors who rented their home at some point during ownership, given the transient nature of some early career moves and the fact that many owners held onto properties during periods of work interstate or overseas. For those vendors, a conversation with a tax accountant before the campaign launches is not optional. It is essential.

The article at the link below explains the main residence exemption, the 6-year rule, how partial exemptions are calculated, and what questions to ask your accountant before you list.
