## Caption

The buyers showing up at auctions in Brisbane's inner east this spring are not tentative. They have had seven weeks since the Olympic closing ceremony to re-engage with the market, and most have used that time well. Pre-finance approvals are in place. Second and third inspections have been done. By the time a property goes under the hammer, the serious bidders are ready.

What is translating into genuine competition at auction right now is largely about property type. Character homes with meaningful land on the Bulimba-to-Balmoral corridor are attracting multiple registered bidders. The combination of the character Queensland architecture, land that can accommodate a quality renovation or extension, and proximity to the river is a consistent draw for buyers who have been watching this corridor for some time.

School catchments are driving real competition in Norman Park and Balmoral. Buyers with families who are targeting specific catchments are not waiting for more stock. They know from experience that well-located homes in these streets do not come up often, and when they do, the field is competitive.

In Camp Hill, the detached house on a genuine block with separation from neighbours is what moves quickly. The buyers looking for this are often coming from smaller homes in the same suburb who have made a decision about what the next chapter looks like.

The properties that are sitting are the ones with a price that does not reflect what has actually sold nearby. This market is not indiscriminate. Buyers are doing the research and they know what comparable sales look like. Honest pricing and genuine presentation is what creates competition. Everything else is noise.
