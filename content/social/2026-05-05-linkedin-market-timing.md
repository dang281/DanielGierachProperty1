## Caption

The most common question I get from homeowners who are not yet clients is some version of this: should we wait?

Wait for rates to fall. Wait until after winter. Wait to see what the market does.

My honest answer is always the same: it depends on what you are waiting for.

If you are waiting for lower interest rates to bring more buyers into the market, that may already be happening. Buyer inquiry in the inner east is not sluggish. The people attending open homes are qualified and motivated. They are not waiting.

If you are waiting for more certainty, that is a reasonable instinct, but certainty has a cost in real estate. The months you spend waiting are months the property is not on the market, and in a suburb like Hawthorne or Norman Park where new listings are absorbed quickly, timing matters.

What waiting often buys you is not a better outcome. It buys you more time to make the same decision under slightly different conditions.

The sellers who do best are usually the ones who have a clear reason to move, a clear timeframe, and a plan for what happens after. Whether the market is at a peak or a trough matters less than whether the decision is right for the people making it.
