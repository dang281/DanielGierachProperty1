## Caption

The pre-Christmas listing decision comes down to a real trade-off. You are giving up the volume of the peak spring market and accepting a smaller buyer pool in exchange for something else: less competition from other sellers, more motivated buyers, and the possibility of a clean finish before the new year.

Whether that trade works depends on what you value most and whether your property is genuinely ready to campaign in November. Vendors who are prepared and have a clear next step often find the pre-Christmas window productive. Vendors who need more time, or who are waiting on a purchase before they can commit to selling, are usually better served by the autumn market starting in February.

The alternative is worth naming too. The full autumn window, February through May, consistently produces strong results in the inner east and gives more time for preparation. For some vendors that window is simply better.

If you were listing for a December settlement, what would you see as the real advantage?

## Poll options

- Less competition from other sellers at that time of year
- More motivated, serious buyers still actively searching
- The clean start that comes with settling before the new year
- No real advantage, spring earlier in the year is better
