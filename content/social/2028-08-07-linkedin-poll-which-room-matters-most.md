## Caption

Sellers preparing for a campaign often ask where to focus their presentation effort. The answer is not always the room you expect.

The kitchen has driven the conversation for years, and it still matters. But in the inner east, where buyers are primarily families and owner-occupiers, the experience of walking through the home is what they remember. Not just the kitchen on its own.

I am curious what the actual experience is from the buyer's side. When you walk through a property for the first time, which room forms your strongest first impression?
