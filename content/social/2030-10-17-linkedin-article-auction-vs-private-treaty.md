## Caption

Sale method is one of the most consequential decisions a vendor makes. It is also one of the most commonly made on the wrong basis.

Most vendors choose auction because their neighbour sold via auction, or private treaty because they are nervous about being "passed in." Neither of those is a good reason to choose a sale method.

The logic should run in the other direction. Auction works best when buyer interest is genuinely competitive and broad, when buyers cannot easily put a ceiling on value, and when the property has features that create emotional competition at the open home. A character Queenslander in Hawthorne on a full block with river glimpses fits that profile well.

Private treaty works better when the buyer pool is more specific, when conditions are likely, and when negotiation over a defined period is more practical than a fixed auction day. A two-bedroom unit in a large complex, where comparable sales are recent and clear, rarely benefits from the uncertainty of an auction day.

Expression of interest sits between the two and is best suited to unique properties where the genuine buyer value range is wide and opaque. It creates a structured competitive process without the pressure of an auction room.

The agent's job is to read the property and the likely buyer pool honestly and recommend accordingly. Not to default to the method that suits the agency's schedule.
