## Caption

Most Brisbane sellers still have a mortgage when they list. That is not a problem. What catches people out is what they thought they would receive and what they actually net at settlement.

Your net equity is not your sale price minus your loan balance. To get to a real number, you subtract the mortgage discharge amount, agent commission (typically 2 to 2.75% of the sale price plus GST in Brisbane's inner east), your conveyancer's fees, any pre-sale costs you have incurred, and outstanding rates or body corporate levies adjusted at settlement. On a $1.4 million sale, those costs can total $35,000 to $56,000 before you see a figure you can actually act on.

For sellers who are buying simultaneously, this matters more than they often realise. The equity available to you is not automatically cash in your account on settlement day. In a simultaneous settlement, it flows directly to the new purchase. That works seamlessly when both transactions are coordinated properly, and it becomes a problem when the timing is not managed well.

There is one other thing worth checking before you list: if you are on a fixed rate and selling before the fixed term ends, your lender can charge a break cost. This is separate from the standard discharge fee. Break costs vary widely and can range from a few hundred dollars to tens of thousands depending on how far into the fixed term you are and how rates have moved since you fixed.

The full article covers all of this in detail, including how PEXA works in Queensland and how to calculate what you will actually have available for your next purchase.
