## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits at the crossroads of several things that buyers in Brisbane's southern inner east consistently look for: Westfield Garden City retail access, solid school options anchored by Mansfield State High, elevated land with views, and a bus network that connects efficiently to the CBD.

The residential streets on the high ground above the Westfield precinct, around Bunny Street and McCullough Avenue, deliver the views and the block depth that motivate buyers to specifically target this suburb. Properties on these streets consistently outperform the suburb average, and buyers who understand the topography know which side of the ridge they are targeting before they attend the first open home.

Mt Gravatt's investor market has held firm through 2030. Proximity to Griffith University's Mt Gravatt campus generates a reliable rental demand pool that keeps vacancy rates competitive. That student and academic population base gives Mt Gravatt a rental market that is somewhat insulated from broader economic fluctuations in ways that purely family-focused suburbs are not.

For homeowners in the elevated streets of Mt Gravatt, the last active buyer period of 2030 before the summer pause is running now through November.
