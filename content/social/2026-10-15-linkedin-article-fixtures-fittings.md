## Caption

The most common minor dispute I see at pre-settlement inspection involves a light fitting.

A seller replaces a builder's fitting with a pendant they paid $800 for. They assume it travels with them. The buyer assumes it is included because it was there every time they inspected. Neither party raised it before signing. Now it is a problem.

In Queensland, light fittings are fixtures. Fixtures stay with the property on sale unless you explicitly exclude them in the contract. The same rule applies to ceiling fans, window blinds on tracks, dishwashers that are plumbed in, pool equipment, and built-in shelving.

The distinction is fixture versus chattel. A fixture is attached and stays. A chattel is freestanding and leaves with you. The grey area sits with things like TV wall mounts, outdoor pot plants on pedestals, and the wine fridge sitting in a kitchen nook.

The practical rule: if you are not certain whether something is included, list it as an exclusion before the contract is prepared. You cannot easily change this after signing without the buyer's agreement.

I have written a full breakdown of what stays and what goes, including the items that cause the most confusion.
