## Caption

March is the most reliable month of the year to read buyer intent in the inner east. Summer is done, Christmas money decisions are settled, and the people attending open homes right now are not browsing. They have been to 10 or 15 opens since January and they are ready to act.

What I notice every March is the quality of the buyer pool, not just the volume. By the time March arrives, the uncommitted buyers have usually dropped off. What remains is a concentrated group with finance pre-approved, a clear brief, and a frustration level that tells you they have already missed properties they wanted.

In the Olympic year, that pattern is amplified. There is a visible sense among buyers that the second half of 2032 will look different to the first half. Some of that is rational, some of it is noise, but the practical effect is that buyer urgency in March 2032 is sharper than in a typical autumn.

For vendors thinking about timing, the combination of peak buyer quality and genuine urgency is rarely replicated at this intensity later in the year. Easter sits in late April this year. A campaign launched in mid to late March has time to run its full cycle before the school holiday window compresses buyer attendance.

The opening week of a campaign is always a read on how prepared the vendor is. Buyers in this market notice presentation, pricing logic, and whether the selling agent actually knows the property. Those details matter more in March than in any other month.
