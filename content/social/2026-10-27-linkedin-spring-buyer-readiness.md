## Caption

By late October, most serious buyers in Brisbane's inner east have been in the market for two to four months. They have attended open homes through the tail of winter, watched what sold and at what price, and built a clear picture of what they will pay for the right property.

That preparation matters for sellers.

A buyer who has been watching Morningside or Camp Hill since August does not need to think about whether to make an offer. They already know the street. They have run their finance. The only question left is whether your property is the one they've been waiting for.

What I see consistently is that well-prepared properties going live in late October and early November hit that buyer pool at peak readiness. The first two weekends of a campaign in this window tend to generate the most competitive interest, because the buyers who have been waiting the longest are the first to act. A property that receives three or four serious enquiries in its first week is in a very different negotiating position from one that trickles along.

The flip side is that buyers in this position are also experienced. They know what constitutes a compelling offer and what is a waste of their time. Properties that are overpriced, poorly presented, or ambiguous about terms tend to get passed over quickly. The same buyers who would have paid well for a well-positioned home will not renegotiate indefinitely on one that needs work to justify its price.

If you are thinking about listing before Christmas, late October to mid-November is the window that matters most. The pool you are marketing to now has been watching and waiting. Whether you capture that interest depends largely on what happens in the first ten days on market.
