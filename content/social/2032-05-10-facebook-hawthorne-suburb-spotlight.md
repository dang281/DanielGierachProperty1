## Caption

Hawthorne's Riding Road precinct is one of those suburban strips where the quality of the food offer tells you something about the income level of the surrounding neighbourhood. The cafes, wine bars, and independent retailers that have settled along that stretch reflect the expectations of residents who have chosen Hawthorne specifically because of the lifestyle it provides at a short distance from the Brisbane CBD.

The suburb is bounded by Bulimba to the north and east and Norman Park to the south, and it shares the peninsula character of the Bulimba loop. The Brisbane River forms its western edge, with the riverfront reserve along Oxford Street extended into Hawthorne giving residents access to the water that most suburban addresses cannot offer. The CityCat at the Hawthorne ferry terminal is a practical commute option, and many residents prefer it to driving.

Hawthorne State School is the primary catchment and is known for its active parent community. The secondary public option is Coorparoo State Secondary College, though the suburb's demographic also includes a proportion of families who choose independent schooling nearby. Properties on the streets closest to the river and the Riding Road strip command a location premium that compounds over time.
