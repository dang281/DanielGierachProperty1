## Caption

A seller who accepts a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one is not being generous. They are being rational.

A conditional offer, even at a strong price, carries a 2 to 3 week window of genuine uncertainty. During that time the finance approval can fall through, the building and pest report can surface issues that prompt renegotiation, and the deal can collapse entirely. The vendor lives with that uncertainty for weeks, and then potentially has to re-launch the campaign.

An unconditional offer removes that window completely. The seller knows the deal is done. No re-inspection, no finance drama, no late renegotiation. The certainty has a real monetary value to a vendor who is also buying, has a settlement deadline to meet, or has simply been through a failed contract before.

This is important for buyers to understand because unconditional offers are often seen as something reserved for cash buyers or property developers. In practice, any buyer who has their finance formally approved and has done their due diligence before making the offer can put forward an unconditional position. It is a negotiating tool, not just a status.

The article covers what unconditional actually means in a Queensland context, when it makes sense to consider it, and what the risks of getting it wrong look like.
