## Caption

Sellers in Brisbane's inner east regularly accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one. Not because they are leaving money on the table, but because unconditional eliminates the two to three week risk window that comes with finance and building and pest conditions.

A conditional offer can still fall through. The finance condition can lapse if the buyer misses the deadline. The building and pest result can give a buyer grounds to renegotiate or walk. Every day a property sits under a conditional contract is a day the seller is off the market and cannot entertain competing interest.

An unconditional offer is done. From the moment the seller signs, the contract is binding.

In a spring market where there is buyer competition, a buyer who can go unconditional has a negotiating advantage that goes well beyond their offer price. They can offer a shorter settlement, more flexibility on access, and certainty that the vendor cannot put a price on after a long campaign.

Getting to unconditional as a buyer usually requires two things: formal finance pre-approval that is solid enough to convert to a full approval quickly, and an independent building and pest inspection completed before the offer is made rather than as a contract condition.

Both are achievable with preparation. Most buyers who cannot go unconditional simply have not planned for it.
