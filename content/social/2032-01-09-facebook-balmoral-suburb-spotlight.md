## Caption

Balmoral is one of those suburbs where the buyer brief tends to be very specific. River proximity, elevated positions along Lores Bonney Riverwalk access corridors, the Norfolk Island pines along Wynnum Road, the mix of renovated Queenslanders and prestige new builds on larger blocks. People who are looking for Balmoral are not hedging between it and somewhere else.

That specificity is an advantage for vendors here. Buyers arrive pre-qualified by the suburb itself. They have typically been watching for months and arrive at open homes knowing the comparable sales. Competition at the right property can be intense.

The housing stock is more varied than people expect. The prestige end sits along the river and the elevated northern streets. Further south, closer to Tingalpa Creek and the Morningside boundary, you find more modest post-war homes and renovators at lower price points. Both buyer groups are active, and they rarely compete for the same properties.

In an Olympic year, Balmoral's riverside character and proximity to inner east infrastructure is drawing enquiry from buyers who want a defined lifestyle asset rather than an investment play. Owner-occupier demand is strong.

Days on market here have historically been lower than surrounding suburbs for well-prepared homes in the prestige tier. The gap between a prepared campaign and a reactive one is meaningful in a suburb with a limited comparable sales pool.
