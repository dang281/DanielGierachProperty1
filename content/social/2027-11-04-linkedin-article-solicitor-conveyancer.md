## Caption

Most buyers in Queensland do the same thing: they sign the contract at the agent's office, then call their solicitor or conveyancer to let them know.

That sequence is backwards.

The contract is the document that sets every condition, deadline, and obligation for the next four to six weeks of your life. The finance condition deadline, the building and pest clause, the settlement date, the inclusions and exclusions, the special conditions negotiated during the offer process. All of that is already locked in by the time you pick up the phone to your legal representative.

A solicitor or conveyancer who reviews the contract before you sign it can catch things that cost nothing to fix in negotiation but become expensive problems after exchange. A finance condition that is too short for your bank's current processing times. An inclusion list that is missing the air conditioning unit you thought was staying. A special condition that creates an obligation you did not intend to accept.

The difference between a solicitor and a conveyancer in Queensland is scope: a solicitor can provide legal advice across the full transaction, including complex situations involving trusts, deceased estates, or commercial components. A conveyancer handles standard residential transfers competently but cannot advise on legal disputes or unusual structures.

For most straightforward residential purchases in the inner east, a good conveyancer is sufficient. For anything involving tenants, estate matters, or unusual contract conditions, a solicitor is the right call.
