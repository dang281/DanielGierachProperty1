## Caption

Camp Hill carries one of the most consistent buyer followings in the inner east, and the reasons are not complicated.

The Old Cleveland Road retail strip from Coorparoo through to Camp Hill has evolved into one of the better lifestyle precincts in Brisbane's southern suburbs. Archive Beer Boutique, independent cafes, and a cluster of locally-owned restaurants give the precinct a character that larger suburban centres do not have. Buyers who place daily lifestyle at the centre of their decision consistently arrive here.

Camp Hill State School is the suburb's most consistent sales driver. It is cited as a primary reason for choosing Camp Hill over Norman Park or Morningside across the full price range, and particularly in the bracket where family homes on 600 to 800 square metre blocks sit. The streets running south off Old Cleveland Road are where the suburb's ceiling product is concentrated.

The buyer profile arriving in Camp Hill tends to fall into two groups: first-movers coming from Coorparoo and Greenslopes with equity, and upgraders coming from Morningside and the eastern suburbs with a clear brief. Both groups are motivated and researched when they arrive.

For owners in Camp Hill, that depth of buyer interest is a significant advantage in any market.
