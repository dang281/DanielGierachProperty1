## Caption

We are at the mid-year point and the inner east market is telling sellers something worth hearing.

Stock levels in July are typically the lowest of the year. Buyers who missed out in autumn have stayed active. They are pre-approved, they have been looking for months, and they are not waiting for spring to make decisions.

This is the dynamic that makes a well-prepared winter campaign more effective than most sellers expect. You are not competing with every other property that launches in September. You are meeting the buyers who are already in the market and ready to commit.

The key phrase there is well-prepared. A July campaign that skips the preparation phase does not benefit from lower competition. It just suffers from lower presentation. The homes that sell well in winter are the ones where the owner treated July as a spring campaign with fewer competitors, not as a compromise.

If you are planning to sell before Christmas, July is not too early. In some price brackets it is exactly the right time.
