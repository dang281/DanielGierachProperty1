## Caption

The Easter long weekend marks the end of Brisbane's best selling window, but it does not mark the end of the market.

Properties that are still on the market after Easter face a changed environment. Buyer urgency softens. The sense of seasonal deadline that pushed buyers to act in March has passed. Open home attendance typically dips for two to three weeks after Easter before picking up again in May.

For sellers still on the market right now, the next few days matter. A well-priced property with strong buyer interest going into Easter is well positioned. For sellers who have not yet launched, the calculation is different.

Two paths forward for sellers who missed the pre-Easter peak. The first is to launch in May when the post-Easter market typically finds its footing again, particularly for well-presented properties in the $1M to $2M range. May and June in the inner east can produce strong results. The second is to wait until August or September, well clear of the winter slowdown, when the lead into summer brings buyers back.

Neither path is wrong. What matters is launching with the right strategy for the conditions at the time, not trying to force an autumn campaign into a May market.

If you are considering what comes next for your property, the best thing to do is have an honest conversation about your options without the pressure of a specific launch date. That conversation is worth having now, while you have time to plan rather than react.
