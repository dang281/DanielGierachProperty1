## Caption

The sequence of pre-sale preparation matters almost as much as the tasks themselves. Most vendors know they need to do something before listing. Fewer have a clear order of operations.

The most common mistake I see: vendors spend money on styling before minor maintenance is finished, and the result is a beautifully presented home with a dripping tap and a cracked window latch visible in the video walkthrough. Buyers notice.

The second most common mistake: leaving landscaping until the week the photos are scheduled, then discovering a project that needs four weeks to look right.

Getting the order right means each task sets up the one after it. The property should be clean and structurally sound before any styling decision is made. Street appeal work should be completed before photography. These are not complicated principles, but they require a plan that starts earlier than most vendors expect.

When preparing your home for a 2034 campaign, what would you tackle first?

## Poll options

- Minor repairs and maintenance (the basics first)
- Professional styling (presentation drives results)
- Landscaping and street appeal (first impression matters)
- Decluttering and deep clean (a fresh start before decisions)
