## Caption

Spring in Brisbane's inner east does not arrive as a single event. It builds across a window, and understanding how that window works matters for vendors deciding when to launch.

September is when the buyer side of the market begins to broaden. Buyers who have been active through winter are joined by a new group who emerge as school terms shift and daylight hours extend. These buyers arrive with energy and motivation. They are not yet fatigued by a long search season, and they are entering a market where supply has not yet peaked.

That combination, rising buyer numbers against a listing environment that has not yet reached its October and November crowding, is one of the more favourable conditions a vendor can enter. A property that launches in late September often has the benefit of buyers who are genuinely excited to be in the market, in a window where they are not yet comparing your listing against a dozen others at the same price point in the same suburb.

By October and November, the competing supply picture looks different. More vendors have launched, buyer attention is divided across more options, and the campaign that hits the market in late spring is one of many rather than one of few.

The September launch advantage is real, but it has a condition attached: preparation. A vendor who uses August well, gets the decluttering done, addresses the maintenance, books the stylist and photographer, and is genuinely ready to go by late August, can enter the September window with everything working in their favour. A vendor who has not done that work does not benefit from the timing regardless of how good the market conditions are.

The window opens in September. Whether you are ready to step through it is worth asking now.
