## Caption

The December to January window is when many inner east vendors who are planning to sell in 2032 start getting serious about preparation. They are not launching a campaign yet. They are working out what needs to be done before they can.

Preparation means different things to different people. Some vendors are focused on presentation: decluttering, repainting, freshening up the garden. Others focus on structural or maintenance issues first, knowing that a building inspection report finding something significant mid-campaign is the worst possible timing. Some go straight to a stylist and photographer, treating the whole exercise as a marketing project. Others start with the appraisal, because they want to know the price reality before they spend any money on preparation.

All of these approaches have logic. The order matters, though. Spending on styling before having a realistic price expectation, or investing in presentation before addressing a structural issue, can create a planning problem rather than solving one.

If selling your inner east home in 2032, what would you tackle first to prepare?

## Poll options

- Declutter, touch up paint, and freshen the presentation
- Address any structural or maintenance issues before going to market
- Engage a stylist and professional photographer before launch
- Get an honest appraisal and set a realistic price expectation from day one
