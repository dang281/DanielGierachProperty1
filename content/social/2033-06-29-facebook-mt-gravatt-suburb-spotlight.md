## Caption

Mt Gravatt's appeal is built on substance rather than suburb prestige. The combination of Garden City's retail and dining anchor, Griffith University's employment and student population, strong road access and a diverse housing stock creates a suburb that functions well across a wide range of buyer types. That breadth of appeal is part of what makes the market here relatively resilient.

The suburb draws buyers at different life stages in a way that few middle-ring suburbs manage. Families are attracted by the schooling options, the space available at the price point and the practical convenience of Garden City. Downsizers find the suburb's services density and its range of lower-maintenance housing options appealing as a step down from larger family homes. Investors are drawn by the dual rental market created by Griffith University and the broader family and professional rental pool. Each of these groups sustains demand from a different angle.

Griffith University's proximity means Mt Gravatt is also one of those suburbs where the employment and academic character of the institution bleeds into the residential market. Researchers, academics and professional staff often live within the suburb rather than commuting from further away, adding a segment of stable, high-quality tenants and owner-occupiers to the demographic mix.

Current medians in Mt Gravatt reflect a stable and consistent market. Well-presented properties tend to attract competitive interest, and the suburb's reputation for broad functionality continues to support its position.
