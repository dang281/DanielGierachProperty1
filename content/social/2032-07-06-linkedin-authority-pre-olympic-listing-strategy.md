## Caption

Two types of sellers are active in Brisbane's inner east right now, and they have made very different calculations.

The first group timed their campaigns to settle before the Games start on July 23. They wanted a clean finish before the city's attention moved elsewhere. Most of them made that call months ago and are now through their campaigns.

The second group is listing into July with the Games approaching. The open home numbers are lower than spring peaks. That is not a surprise and it is not a problem if you understand what it means.

The buyers who are active right now have made a deliberate decision to be in the market during a period when most people are watching sport or hosting visitors. That mindset tends to produce transactions. Tyre-kickers are not driving to open homes in July. The people walking through are genuinely interested, and the conversations at the door reflect that.

The practical implication for sellers: price accurately, present well, and be responsive. You are not working with volume. You are working with quality. A vendor who is patient, realistic on price, and has a well-presented property is in a reasonable position during this window.

Buyers in July are not doing anyone a favour. They are active because they see value in acting when competition is thinner.

Both strategies can work. The one that works for you depends on your property, your timeline, and how you want to use the next few months.
