## Caption

Two houses on the same street in Hawthorne can be $200,000 apart in value. Same suburb, same land size, same number of bedrooms.

The gap is explained by things an automated estimate cannot read: which direction the main living space faces, whether the kitchen was renovated in 2019 or 2008, whether there is a flood or character overlay, and what the actual comparable sales in the previous 90 days show for properties that are genuinely similar.

Automated valuation tools lag real market conditions by 60 to 120 days. They cannot see inside the property. They do not know whether the renovation was done well or done cheaply. They look at the street, not the house.

This matters most in the inner east where the price range within a suburb is wide and the factors that create the difference are specific and verifiable. An accurate appraisal in Norman Park or Balmoral requires someone who has walked comparable properties recently and understands the local buyer pool, not someone who has run the address through a model.

If you have been using an online estimate as a guide for what your home might sell for, the article at the link below explains what those tools measure and what they miss.
