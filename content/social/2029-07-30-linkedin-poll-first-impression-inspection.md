## Caption

You can tell a lot about how someone shops for property by what grabs their attention in the first 60 seconds of walking through. Some people clock the light immediately, how it moves through the house and which way it faces. Others are already doing mental arithmetic at the kerb before they have even opened the front door. Some need to stand in the hall and feel the floor plan before anything else registers. And some are quietly comparing what they see against the price guide. Sellers spend a lot of energy on the wrong things without understanding how buyers actually experience a home. What do you notice first when you walk through a property for sale?
