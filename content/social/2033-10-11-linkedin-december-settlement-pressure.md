## Caption

The Christmas settlement deadline is real, and the maths behind it is simple once you map it out.

A contract signed in the first week of October with a standard 30 to 45 day settlement lands in mid to late November. A contract signed in the second or third week of October settles somewhere in late November to early December. A contract signed in the first two weeks of November settles in mid to late December. A contract signed in the third week of November or later settles in January.

That timeline matters because there is a specific group of buyers in Brisbane's inner east right now whose entire decision is structured around being in their home before Christmas. They have school enrolments to confirm, removalists to book, and in some cases interstate relocations timed to a December handover. These buyers are not casually hoping for a pre-Christmas settlement. They are specifically looking for a vendor who can deliver one.

What I observe when a vendor has a genuine Christmas settlement argument is that these buyers do not negotiate as hard on price. The settlement date has its own value to them, and they will pay for certainty. A vendor who lists now and can confidently offer a November or early December settlement is selling something extra. That combination of timing and certainty is worth something.

What I also observe is that this only works if the listing actually goes live in October. A November launch does not give the same leverage. The window to use December settlement pressure as a negotiating asset is now, not next month.
