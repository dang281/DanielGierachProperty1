## Caption

Camp Hill has the kind of suburb identity that is hard to manufacture. The Village on Old Cleveland Road is one of Brisbane's most genuinely popular suburban lifestyle strips, with a concentration of cafes, restaurants, independent retail, and weekend foot traffic that draws people from surrounding suburbs as much as it serves residents.

The school catchment is one of the most significant factors in the suburb's sustained demand. Whites Hill State College serves Camp Hill alongside Norman Park, and its reputation makes it one of the most sought-after catchments in the inner east. Families who identify this catchment as a priority narrow their search to a specific geography, and Camp Hill sits squarely inside it.

The housing stock reflects a suburb where owner-occupiers stay. Character homes on established blocks, well-maintained streets, and a community feel that comes from low turnover and long-held ownership. That character is not incidental to the suburb's appeal. It is a direct result of the buyers it attracts and how long they tend to remain.

Days on market in Camp Hill run consistently below city-wide averages. ⚠️ VERIFY. When the right property comes to market, buyers who have been targeting this suburb are ready to move. They have been watching listings, they know the comparable sales, and they are not in the early stages of a search by the time they walk through an open home.

If you own in Camp Hill and are considering what the current market looks like for your property, I am happy to have that conversation.
