## Caption

Autumn in Brisbane's inner east runs roughly late February through to early May. For sellers targeting a February or March launch, the preparation timeline starts now, not in January.

Here is how the weeks actually stack up from decision to first open home. Styling and decluttering typically takes two to four weeks depending on how much needs to happen. Furniture removal, storage, and working with a stylist to stage the home all take calendar time, not just effort. Photography and floorplans take around a week once the property is ready. A pre-listing building and pest inspection, which I recommend strongly, adds another one to two weeks, particularly if anything needs to be addressed before campaign. Marketing production, Form 6 signing, and portal setup take roughly a week. Add it together and the realistic window from decision to first open home is six to ten weeks.

A seller who decides in late November or December has a comfortable runway to a strong February or March launch. The property goes to market well-presented, with known condition, and with no last-minute scramble around photography or reports. That preparation shows in the campaign. Buyers can tell when a vendor has taken the process seriously.

A seller who calls in early February expecting to be live within two weeks typically cannot hit that timeline without cutting corners. Rushed styling, rushed photography, or no pre-listing report all have consequences in how the property is received and how the negotiation unfolds.

If autumn 2033 is your target, the conversation worth having is this week, not next year.
