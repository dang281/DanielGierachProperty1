## Caption

Something shifts in the buyer pool in the first two weeks after Easter, and I have watched it happen consistently over the years in Brisbane's inner east.

During the Easter break itself, inspections quiet down. Open home attendance drops. It looks like the market is resting. But that is not quite what is happening. What is actually happening is that buyers are thinking. Families have time together, conversations happen over long weekends, and the people who have been half-committed to a purchase start to firm up their position one way or the other.

By the time the first Tuesday in May arrives, the buyers who come back to the market have usually made a decision. They are not browsing anymore. They have had the conversation with their partner, they have called the broker, and they know their number. The quality of that enquiry is different to what you see in late March.

In terms of sheer volume, post-Easter open homes are not always the busiest of the year. But the ratio of genuine buyers to casual lookers tends to be higher than at almost any other point in the calendar. When I run an open home in this window and get twelve groups through, I am not looking at twelve buyers. I might be looking at eight or nine people who are genuinely ready to act.

For sellers thinking about timing, this is a window worth understanding. The buyers showing up right now have done their homework. They are not waiting for more information. They are waiting for the right property.
