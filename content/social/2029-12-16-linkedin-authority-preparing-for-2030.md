## Caption

If you are planning to sell in March or April 2030, the preparation work starts now. Not in February. Not after the Christmas break. Now.

The four to eight weeks before a property goes to market is where a campaign is won or lost. It is not about spending money, it is about making considered decisions when you have time to make them well.

The first task is a clear-eyed declutter. Not just tidying, but removing the items that make rooms feel smaller or divert a buyer's attention away from the property itself. That process takes longer than most people expect, and it is better done over several weekends than in a rush the week before photography.

Second, a repair list. Walk through the property with a notepad and write down anything a buyer might notice or a building inspector might flag. A dripping tap, a stiff door, a worn fence panel. These are not deal-breakers on their own, but collectively they signal to a buyer that the property has not been maintained, and that perception costs money at negotiation.

Third, a styling conversation. Whether you are using a professional stylist or working with what you have, the brief needs to be set before Christmas so there is time to action it in January.

Finally, have the conversation with your agent in December, not January. Agents who are briefed early can build the right marketing strategy and prepare the campaign properly rather than working to a rushed timeline.

The vendors who do this work in December and January consistently achieve better outcomes than those who start in February.
