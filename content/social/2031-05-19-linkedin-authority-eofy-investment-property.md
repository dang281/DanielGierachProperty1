## Caption

For investors considering selling a Brisbane inner east property, the date that settlement occurs relative to 30 June is a real number worth modelling before a campaign is launched.

This is not tax advice. Every investor's situation is different, and the right person to work through the numbers is an accountant who knows the specific property and the vendor's income picture for the year. But the practical consideration is straightforward enough to be worth understanding before deciding when to launch.

A settlement that falls before 30 June places the capital gain in the current financial year. A settlement that falls on or after 1 July places it in the next. For an investor who has had a high-income year, or who has other capital gains already crystallised in this financial year, pushing settlement into the new financial year may be material. For an investor who has losses to apply, or whose income picture looks different next year, it may not matter or may even point the other way.

The timing complication for a May or June launch is that settlement dates are not entirely within a vendor's control. An auction campaign that starts in late May typically settles four to six weeks after auction day, depending on negotiated terms. That puts settlement in late June or early July, right across the boundary. A private treaty sale with unconditional terms has more flexibility to negotiate a specific settlement date.

The decision to launch in May rather than waiting until July, or to structure a campaign around a post-30 June settlement, is one that vendors who own investment properties should work through with their accountant before the campaign starts, not after contracts are signed.
