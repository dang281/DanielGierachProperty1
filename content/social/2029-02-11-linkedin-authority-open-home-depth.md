## Caption

Open home attendance numbers are one of the most direct market signals available in real time.

In Brisbane's inner east, a well-priced three to four bedroom house that opens on a Saturday morning will typically attract 20 to 40 groups in the first two weekends if the campaign is working. Below 15 groups across the first two opens is a signal worth paying attention to. Either the price needs to be reconsidered, or there is a property-specific issue that buyers are reacting to.

What I watch is not just the number but the quality of the groups. Buyers who take their time, look in the wardrobes, open the kitchen cupboards, and ask about the contract terms are buyers who are considering making an offer. Groups who walk through in four minutes and leave without questions are gathering information, not ready to commit. A strong open home has both, but the second type matters more.

For vendors, the first two weekends of an autumn campaign are the most important. Buyer enthusiasm is highest when a listing is fresh. The window of maximum competition is narrow. A correctly priced property launched with good presentation captures that competition. A property launched too high waits for buyers who may never come.

If you are heading into an open home as a buyer this autumn, how quickly you register and how thoroughly you inspect tells the agent quite a lot about where you are in your decision.
