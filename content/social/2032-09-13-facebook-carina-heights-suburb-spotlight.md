## Caption

Carina Heights sits on the ridge. That elevation is the defining feature of what the suburb offers, and buyers who understand it tend to arrive with a clear sense of what they are buying.

The suburb sits above Carina and Carindale. The streets that run along the high ground, including those off Mt Cotton Road, carry views south toward the ranges and north toward the city that are not common at this price point in Brisbane's inner south-east. These are not filtered or partial views from a balcony addition. On the right elevated blocks, the aspect is structural to the property.

Carina Heights State School gives the suburb a family-buyer anchor. The catchment demand here is consistent and drives a proportion of purchases that would not otherwise prioritise this suburb specifically.

The buyer who discovers Carina Heights often arrives from a parallel search in Camp Hill. They have been looking at elevation, aspect, land size, and price. When they find that Carina Heights delivers comparable aspect and land at a meaningful discount to Camp Hill pricing, the decision can happen quickly. That discovery is usually made by buyers who are willing to look at a suburb map and drive through streets before they dismiss the postcode.

Spring brings these buyers back to the market. The views look their best in clear September and October weather, which is also when motivated sellers tend to list.
