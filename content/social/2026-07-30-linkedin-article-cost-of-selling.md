## Caption

Selling a Brisbane home for $1.4 million doesn't mean banking $1.4 million. The total cost of selling, including agent commission, marketing, conveyancing, and preparation, typically runs to 2.5% to 4% of the sale price.

On a $1.4 million home, that's $35,000 to $56,000 in outgoings before you see your proceeds.

Agent commission is the largest cost for most vendors. In Queensland, commission is negotiated, not regulated. The typical range in metropolitan Brisbane is 2% to 3% of the final sale price. It's payable at settlement from proceeds, so no cash is needed upfront.

Marketing costs vary more than most vendors expect. A standard campaign in the inner east runs $3,000 to $6,000, covering professional photography, realestate.com.au and Domain listings, and print or social reach. A premium campaign for a higher-priced property may run higher. The right marketing spend depends on the property, not a formula.

Conveyancing, styling, and minor repairs add to the total. None of these are optional if you want a result that exceeds the base price.

What sellers often don't account for: the difference between a well-executed campaign and an average one in terms of final sale price is regularly a multiple of the total selling costs. Spending $50,000 to sell a property for $1.45 million instead of $1.35 million is a straightforward calculation.
