## Caption

Norman Park in March is as good as it gets for a vendor in this suburb. The weather is mild, the streets photograph well, and the buyer pool that has been accumulating since the start of the year is at its most active. The combination of school catchment demand, elevated land character, and the Whites Hill Reserve setting makes March campaigns in Norman Park consistently competitive.

The Norman Park State School catchment zone has a well-established reputation among Brisbane families. It draws buyers who have researched their school options and who will stretch their budget to secure a property within the zone rather than settle for something just outside it. That buyer motivation, specific and financially committed, shows up in auction results and private treaty negotiations alike. ⚠️ VERIFY

Whites Hill Reserve is the suburb's green infrastructure. The walking tracks, the elevated outlooks, and the sense of space it creates for properties on the eastern side of the suburb are not just amenity. They are a reason buyers choose Norman Park over comparable suburbs and pay more to be here.

The character home culture in Norman Park is strong. Vendors who have invested in their elevated Queenslander or post-war timber home will find buyers who appreciate that investment and are prepared to pay for it. Vendors who have maintained but not updated should be pricing accordingly and letting the land and location do the work.

March is the month. If you are ready, the market is here.
