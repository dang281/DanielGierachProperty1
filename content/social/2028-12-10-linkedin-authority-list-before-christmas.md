## Caption

The list before Christmas question comes up every December, and the honest answer is more nuanced than the advice usually is.

If you list this week and the property is properly prepared, you are targeting the buyers who are still active in December: motivated, specific, and not waiting for January. If the campaign does not find its buyer in the first two weeks of December, you will effectively be carrying the listing through Christmas and relaunching in January anyway. That is not a failure, it is just a different timeline.

If your property needs preparation work and you are thinking of rushing it to market before Christmas rather than waiting four to six weeks and presenting it well, the correct answer is almost always to wait. A rushed presentation to a motivated buyer pool in December is not better than a prepared presentation to the January and February market.

What I have seen consistently: sellers who make the decision to list in December with a properly prepared property, and price within their comparable evidence, get results. Sellers who list in December hoping urgency substitutes for preparation do not.

The question worth asking is not "can I sell before Christmas" but "is my property ready to be sold?" If the answer is yes, December can work. If the answer requires some hedging, January is the better launch.
