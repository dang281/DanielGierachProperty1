## Caption

October brings the biggest open home numbers of the year in the inner east. But not every person walking through the door is a buyer.

The genuine buyers in October are easy to identify if you know what to look for. They have been searching since July or August. They have attended between 8 and 15 inspections already. Their finance is arranged, not pending. They have a clear picture of what the right property looks like and what they are prepared to pay for it. When a property that fits their criteria hits the market, they act. They do not ask for a second or third inspection to think about it.

The browsers are also in the market in October. They are curious about what their money buys. They are at the early stages of thinking about whether to buy. They attend open homes on Sunday morning as part of a loose plan that also includes brunch. They do not transact in October. They will be back in February.

The reason pricing accuracy matters more in October than in any other month is this: the genuine buyers are ready. If a property is priced correctly for the market, those buyers compete for it and you get the result. If it is priced incorrectly, those same buyers move on to the next correctly-priced property and you miss the window. By the time the price adjusts, the best buyers are gone.

Spring is not a reason to price high. It is a reason to price right.
