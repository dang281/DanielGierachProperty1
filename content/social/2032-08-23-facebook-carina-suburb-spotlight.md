## Caption

Carina's position adjacent to Carindale and the Westfield precinct gives residents retail access that Camp Hill and Norman Park owners typically need a car and a drive to reach. For households who value that kind of proximity, it is a practical advantage that the suburb's profile does not always get credit for.

The established residential streets carry post-war homes on blocks that consistently clear 600 square metres. That land size is increasingly hard to find at Carina's price point in Brisbane's inner east, and it is one of the primary reasons buyers who arrive here from Coorparoo or Camp Hill tend to stay.

Carina State School covers a broad primary catchment. Secondary school options in the surrounding area are accessible without a significant commute for most of the suburb.

The buyers arriving in Carina in the current market have done the inner-east comparison. They know what comparable land costs in the premium suburbs. They have made a considered call that Carina's combination of block size, retail access, and price point represents better value for their household than pushing further west.

For owners in Carina's established residential streets, the product you hold, a post-war home on a 600-plus square metre block with Carindale access and a sound school catchment, is exactly what the current buyer profile is looking for.
