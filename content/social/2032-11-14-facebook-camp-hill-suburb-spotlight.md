## Caption

The eastern streets of Camp Hill, those running east of Cavendish Road toward the Whites Hill State College catchment, attract a buyer type that is quietly one of the most prepared in the inner east.

These are buyers who have usually spent most of the year researching. They have a list of streets. They know which side of Cavendish Road commands a premium and why. They have followed comparable sales through winter and spring and have an understanding of price that is not easily moved by negotiation alone. When they decide to make an offer, it is because a property has genuinely satisfied the brief they set twelve months earlier.

In November, this buyer is operating with a deadline that goes beyond the general pre-Christmas sentiment. The Whites Hill State College enrolment calendar and the start of the school year in late January create a concrete timeline. Families who need to be enrolled and settled by then are doing their maths right now. Settlement periods, inspections, finance approval: it all needs to be underway before December is very old.

The character homes on the elevated eastern streets carry Camp Hill's strongest value. The aspect, the block size, and the school proximity are the three factors these buyers evaluate. When all three align, the result is a competitive process that does not require any particular market momentum to produce a strong outcome for the seller.

If you own east of Cavendish Road, the window is open and the buyers are here.
