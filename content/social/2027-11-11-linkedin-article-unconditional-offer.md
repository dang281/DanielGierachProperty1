## Caption

Sellers in Brisbane's inner east regularly receive unconditional offers. And regularly undervalue them.

An unconditional offer is an offer with no conditions attached: no finance clause, no building and pest inspection clause, no other contingencies. If the offer is accepted, both parties are bound immediately. The buyer cannot walk away without forfeiting their deposit, typically ten percent of the purchase price.

That certainty has a dollar value that most sellers fail to price accurately.

A conditional offer at $1.3 million carries an invisible discount. During the condition period, typically two to three weeks, the seller is off the market but not certain of the outcome. The buyer may find a problem in the building report and walk away. The bank may not approve the finance at the contract price. The seller returns to the market three weeks later, now carrying days on market and the need to explain why the last buyer did not proceed.

An unconditional offer at $1.25 million eliminates that risk entirely. Whether the $50,000 difference is worth the certainty depends on how confident the seller is in the conditional buyer and the condition of the property. But the certainty of the unconditional offer is rarely given the full weight it deserves.
