## Caption

Three weeks into January and a few things are becoming clear about the buyer pool in Brisbane's inner east.

Finance-ready buyers are active. The group that pre-approved in late 2026 and paused over Christmas is back and moving. This is typically the most motivated buyer segment, because they have done the work, they know their number, and they are not browsing. They have a decision to make.

First home buyers are more cautious than this time last year. Affordability at the entry level of the inner east is the primary constraint. The suburbs where first home buyers were competitive in 2025 have moved further out of their range for many. That buyer pool has either stretched budgets, shifted suburbs, or stepped back to reassess.

Upsizers from within the inner east continue to be the most consistent buyer type for the $1.5M to $2.5M range. These are families already living in the area, selling a smaller home to buy a larger one, often motivated by school catchments or the need for a home office or additional bedroom. They are not rate-sensitive in the same way as first home buyers. They are equity-driven.

Investors are selective. The yield conversation has improved compared to two years ago, but borrowing costs mean the numbers only stack up on specific property types. Older units and townhouses near transport corridors are where I am seeing the most investor activity.

Understanding who is in the buyer pool for your property is the first thing I talk through in an appraisal. It shapes the pricing strategy, the campaign approach, and the method of sale.
