## Caption

The auction reserve is the single most important number you set during an auction campaign, and it gets less direct discussion than it should.

Many sellers arrive at auction day with a reserve that was anchored too early in the campaign, set against the agent's initial guidance rather than the buyer feedback collected through the inspection cycle.

A useful reserve is built bottom-up from three inputs: comparable sales in the suburb in the past 60 to 90 days, the genuine bidder pool that has registered through your campaign, and the lowest price you would accept if only one bidder turned up.

If your reserve sits well above what the inspection feedback supports, the auction is more likely to pass in. If it sits well below, you may sell at a price the room would have lifted past with patience. Neither is what a good campaign produces.
