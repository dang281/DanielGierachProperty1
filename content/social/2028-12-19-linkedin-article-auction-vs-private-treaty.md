## Caption

The method of sale should follow the property and the buyer pool, not the seller's preference and not what the neighbour used two months ago.

Auction works best for properties where the buyer pool is broad and competitive, and where the reserve can be set with confidence. When two or more buyers want the same property and the process forces them to compete openly, auction regularly drives a result above what private treaty would have achieved. The transparency is the mechanism.

Private treaty suits situations where the buyer pool is smaller, the property has a definable value range, or the vendor needs flexibility in timing and conditions. Most inner east properties sell by private treaty.

Expression of interest sits between the two. It is the method I see used most effectively for properties where the value is genuinely wide: a character home on a large block in a street where no direct comparable exists, or a property that will appeal differently to different buyer profiles. EOI invites buyers to put their best case forward without the public pressure of an auction room.

The mistake I see sellers make most often is choosing a method based on what worked for someone else's property or what feels more comfortable. The method that creates the most competition for your specific property and buyer profile is the method that gives you the best result.

The full article explains how each method works, which property types suit each, and the questions worth asking before you decide.
