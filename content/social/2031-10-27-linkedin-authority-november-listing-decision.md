## Caption

The October to November listing window suits a specific type of seller, and it is worth being clear about who that is.

The vendors who do well listing in late October or November, targeting a December settlement or early January settlement, share a few characteristics. They already know where they are going. They have identified their next property or have a clear plan that does not depend on a simultaneous purchase. They are not waiting to find somewhere to go before they commit to selling. That clarity removes the main friction from the transaction.

Why the window works for them: the buyer pool in November is smaller than September or October, but the quality is materially higher. The browsers who showed up in spring have dropped off. What remains is a pool of buyers who have been searching for five or six months, have attended many inspections, have their finance arranged, and are ready to commit before Christmas. These are the buyers every vendor wants. November delivers them in concentration.

For vendors who are still in the early stages of planning their next move, November is probably too soon. Rushing a campaign to catch the pre-Christmas window without having the other side of the transaction sorted creates pressure that rarely helps the outcome. Better to take the time, sort the plan, and launch when genuinely ready.

November is not the right time for every vendor. For the ones it fits, it is one of the strongest selling windows of the year.
