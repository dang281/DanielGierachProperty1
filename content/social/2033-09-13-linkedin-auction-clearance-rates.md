## Caption

Auction clearance rates are a useful but often misread indicator. A high clearance rate tells you that buyers and vendors are agreeing on price at a reasonable frequency. A low clearance rate tells you there is a gap, but it does not tell you which side is out of step with the market. Understanding what clearance rates mean requires reading them alongside volume, comparable sales data, and days on market.

In Brisbane's inner east, auction is not the dominant sale method for every property type, but the suburbs where auction is most commonly used include Hawthorne, Bulimba, Balmoral, and parts of Morningside. The clearance data from these suburbs in September gives a reasonable early read on how motivated the buyer pool is and how well-aligned vendor expectations are with current market evidence.

What the September data typically confirms in a solid market is that the vendor population is arriving with realistic expectations, the buyer pool has been building through winter, and competition at well-positioned properties is genuine. When clearance rates run above 60 percent through September, that is usually a sign that both sides of the market are reading the data accurately.

The number to watch alongside clearance rate is days on market for properties that do not clear at auction. When those properties are re-listed at adjusted prices and selling within two to three weeks, the clearance rate understates actual market health.
