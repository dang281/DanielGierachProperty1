## Caption

January is never the highest-volume auction month in Brisbane's inner east. But volume and clearance rate are different things.

What tends to happen in January is that the buyers who show up at auctions are the ones who have already done the thinking. They have been watching the market, they have their finance sorted, and they have attended enough inspections to know they want this property. You do not typically get the casually curious bidder in January. You get the buyer who came back from Christmas ready to act.

That buyer profile matters because it changes the auction room dynamic. Fewer registered bidders can still produce strong competition if those bidders are serious. In my observation, well-presented homes in good positions that come to market in January tend to face less competition from other listings than they would in March or April, while still attracting the buyers who are most motivated.

The property types that tend to perform well in this window are those that appeal to the family upsizer or the downsizer with a clear brief. These buyers plan their moves over the summer break and return to market with a decision already forming. A home that matches their criteria, presented well and priced to reflect the market, does not need high auction volume to achieve a strong result. It needs the right buyer in the room.

For vendors weighing up whether January or February makes sense as a launch window, the dynamics are worth discussing in detail. danielgierach.com
