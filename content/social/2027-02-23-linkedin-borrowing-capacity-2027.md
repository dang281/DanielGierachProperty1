## Caption

Borrowing capacity is the figure that shapes everything else for a buyer. It determines which suburbs are in range, which properties are realistic, and how much room there is to compete at auction.

The way lenders calculate it has not changed fundamentally in recent years. APRA still requires a 3% serviceability buffer above the actual lending rate. So if you are borrowing at a rate of 6%, the bank tests you at 9%. That buffer alone can reduce maximum borrowing by $80,000 to $150,000 compared to what buyers expect.

What has changed is the rate environment itself. If the RBA has moved the cash rate since the start of the year, those changes flow through to variable rates and, over time, to fixed rate products. A 25 basis point cut reduces the monthly repayment on an $800,000 loan by approximately $100 to $120. That sounds modest. The bigger effect is on the maximum loan a buyer qualifies for: it can add $20,000 to $30,000 to the upper limit.

For buyers in the inner east, this matters in specific price bands. In the $1M to $1.5M range, the difference between qualifying and not qualifying can come down to a single rate decision.

The practical action: if you have a pre-approval from more than three months ago, get it refreshed. Lending conditions and your own financial position may have moved.
