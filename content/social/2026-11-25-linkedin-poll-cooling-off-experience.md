## Caption

In Queensland, a buyer has a 5-business-day cooling-off period after signing a residential contract. Withdrawing costs 0.25% of the purchase price. On an $800,000 property, that is $2,000.

After the cooling-off period ends, withdrawing from a conditional contract requires a specific condition to have been triggered, such as finance not being approved in time, or a building and pest result that meets the threshold in the contract. Walking away for other reasons puts the deposit at risk.

Once the contract is unconditional, exit is extremely limited.

Most buyers who have been through the process once know this intuitively. First-time buyers often do not, until they are already in the middle of it.

Have you ever pulled out of a property purchase after signing a contract?

## Notes for Daniel

The 0.25% cooling-off termination fee is correct under the Property Occupations Act 2014 (Qld). The 5-business-day cooling-off period is correct for residential contracts signed in QLD. Auction contracts have no cooling-off.

Suggested follow-up comment after publishing: "The cooling-off period exists for a reason. Use it if you need it. The 0.25% fee is designed to be a genuine but manageable exit rather than a penalty."
