## Caption

Every agent has a preferred method of sale. That preference is often based on what is easiest to run, not what is best for your property.

Auction suits properties with a broad, competitive buyer pool, where competition is the mechanism that maximises price. Private treaty suits properties where the buyer pool is narrower, the price is more predictable, or where conditions and negotiation give sellers more control over the outcome. Expression of interest sits between the two and works best for unique properties where the range of what buyers will pay is genuinely wide.

None of these is universally better. The right answer depends on the property, the suburb, the current depth of buyer demand at that price point, and whether a three-week auction campaign will attract more buyers than a four-week private treaty campaign would.

In the inner east, most properties in the $1M to $1.8M range have enough buyer depth for auction to work well. Above $2M, the equation changes. The buyer pool at that level is smaller, often includes interstate and international buyers who cannot attend an auction in person, and may need more time to arrange finance than a typical three-week campaign allows.
