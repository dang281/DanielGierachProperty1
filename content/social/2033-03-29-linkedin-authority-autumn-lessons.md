## Caption

Autumn 2033 is nearly done in Brisbane's inner east. Looking back across the campaigns that performed at or above expectation, one pattern stands out: the vendors who prepared early and priced honestly outperformed those who listed late and priced optimistically, regardless of suburb.

This is not a new pattern. It holds across market cycles. What makes it worth stating again is that the post-Olympic context of 2033 brought with it a cohort of vendors who had been waiting, watching the market, expecting that a particular price point would become achievable. For some of them it did. For others, the waiting cost them the best campaign window.

The properties I saw perform best this autumn shared a practical profile: maintenance completed before the first open home, styling that made the floor plan feel liveable rather than sterile, photography taken on the right day with the right light, and a price guide that reflected recent comparable sales rather than aspirational thinking.

None of that is complicated. All of it takes time to do properly. The vendors who started the preparation conversation in January had that time. The ones who started in late February were compressing into a window that does not stretch.

For anyone already thinking about spring: the lesson from autumn 2033 is straightforward. Preparation is the variable that matters most. The calendar is secondary.
