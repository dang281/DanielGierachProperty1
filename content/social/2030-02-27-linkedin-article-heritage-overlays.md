## Caption

A heritage overlay affects what you can alter or demolish on a property. But in the inner east, where buyers actively seek character homes, it often supports rather than restricts value.

Heritage overlays in Brisbane apply to properties that have been identified as having cultural, historical, or architectural significance. They appear on the Brisbane City Plan and show up on a property's development conditions. Understanding what they mean in practice is more useful than a knee-jerk response to seeing one on a search.

What a heritage overlay typically restricts:

Demolition of the whole or significant parts of the existing structure is either prohibited or requires specific approval. If a buyer's plan is to knock down and rebuild, a heritage overlay is a material issue and needs to be identified before purchase.

Significant external alterations to the character of the building require development application. This includes changes to the roofline, facade, or materials visible from the street.

What a heritage overlay does not restrict:

Internal alterations in most cases are not affected. You can renovate the interior substantially without requiring heritage-specific approvals, provided the external character is maintained.

Additions at the rear of the property are generally possible, subject to normal BCC development rules. An extension behind the existing heritage character is a common and accepted approach in the inner east.

The value implication in the inner east is important to understand. Buyers who are seeking Queenslander, colonial, or Federation character homes are often specifically attracted to properties with heritage status. They are not deterred by the overlay; in many cases they are seeking it.
