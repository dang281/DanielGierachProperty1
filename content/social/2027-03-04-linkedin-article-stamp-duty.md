## Caption

Transfer duty in Queensland is a significant cost that many buyers do not calculate accurately until they are deep into a purchase.

On a $1,000,000 property purchased by an owner-occupier who is not a first home buyer, the transfer duty payable to the Queensland Revenue Office is approximately $38,025. That figure sits on top of the purchase price and does not add to your equity. It is a transaction cost.

First home buyers receive a concession or, in some cases, a full exemption up to the relevant threshold. Investors do not receive the same concessions as owner-occupiers.

The mistake that catches buyers is treating transfer duty as an afterthought rather than a line item in the purchase budget from the beginning. A buyer who has saved a $200,000 deposit and expects to buy a $1M property needs to account for the fact that $38,025 of that deposit disappears in transfer duty before they own anything. That reduces the cash they have available and affects their LVR calculation.

For buyers in the inner east, where properties regularly transact in the $1M to $2M range, transfer duty is a material number. It is worth calculating at the start of your search, not the end.

The Queensland Revenue Office has a transfer duty calculator on its website that gives you the exact figure for any purchase price.
