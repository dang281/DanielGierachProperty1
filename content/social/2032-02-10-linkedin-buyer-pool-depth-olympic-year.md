## Caption

There is a persistent narrative that the 2032 buyer pool in the inner east is primarily speculative. Investors chasing a short-term Games premium, interstate buyers holding for a quick resale, and offshore interest parking capital in a marketable year. Some of that is present. It is not the dominant story.

The buyers I am actually meeting at open homes are mostly owner-occupiers with genuine end-user reasons to be here. Families relocating from interstate who are using the Olympic year as a timing anchor, not a financial thesis. Locals who have been renting in the inner east for years and have decided this is the year to buy. Upsizers who have their current home under control and are now focused on what they are moving to.

For these buyers, the Olympic year is a prompt, not the reason. It gives them a defined horizon, which makes the decision to move easier to commit to. But the underlying driver is the suburb, the school catchment, the commute, and the home itself.

This distinction matters for sellers. A property positioned to the speculative buyer needs to tell a different story from one positioned to the end-user. Pitching projected capital growth to someone who just wants to live in the street is noise. Showing them comparable owner-occupier sales is what earns trust.

Understanding who is actually in the room at your open home is a core part of running an effective campaign. The number at the door matters less than who those people are.
