## Caption

January is the best time to read what is actually happening in the market, before the narrative settles.

The inner east fundamentals heading into 2030 are solid. Land supply in established suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, and Balmoral remains structurally constrained. These are not suburbs that get bigger. The blocks that exist now are the blocks that will exist in 2032, and the demand side of that equation has not weakened.

The 2032 Olympics is now two years away. That is a shift from theory to reality. Infrastructure visible across the Murarrie, Cannon Hill, and Morningside corridor is confirming what buyers in 2026 and 2027 were pricing on faith. The Gabba and Chandler precincts are not abstract anymore.

Post-holiday buyer activity is typically quiet through the first two weeks of January, then picks up meaningfully from around the 15th. The buyers who missed out in November and December are ready to act. They have done their research over the break, they know what they want to pay, and they are not browsing. They are looking for the right property to come to market.

Sellers who used the December break to prepare and are planning a late-January or early-February launch are well positioned. The buyers for their property are already active.

I am not forecasting where prices go from here. What I can say is that the precinct-level conditions I see every week continue to point to more demand than supply in the quality segments of the inner east market.

If you want to know what that means for your specific property or suburb, I am happy to have that conversation.
