## Caption

Most buyers make an initial judgement about a property within the first 60 seconds of walking through the door. After that, they may rationalise the decision in different directions, but the first impression rarely fully reverses.

What sellers sometimes miss is that the first impression is not always the kitchen or the master bedroom. It can be the smell of the house, the condition of the front garden, the natural light in the entry hallway. The things that communicate whether the property has been looked after.

When you walk through a property at an inspection, what do you notice first?

## Poll options

- The smell and feel of the space
- Natural light and the floor plan layout
- Condition and presentation (is it well maintained?)
- The kitchen, because that is where I spend my time
