## Caption

East Brisbane is among the closest of the inner east suburbs to the CBD, and in 2032, that proximity matters more than it has at almost any point in recent memory. The suburb sits between the Gabba precinct and the Kangaroo Point peninsula, with direct access to the inner city via Vulture Street and the Story Bridge.

The Gabba has been at the centre of Brisbane's Olympic venue planning since the announcement. Whatever form that transformation ultimately takes, the knock-on effect on surrounding suburbs including East Brisbane has been a sustained lift in buyer interest, particularly from investors and owner-occupiers who have taken a long view on the precinct's development.

The housing stock here is dense by inner east standards, with a mix of substantial pre-war and character homes on smaller allotments, apartment buildings along the major roads, and contemporary townhouse developments in the recent infill wave. Buyers looking for character homes with genuine inner city access, and who do not need large garden space, find East Brisbane a compelling proposition.

For families, Clem Jones Park and the access to East Brisbane State School are practical anchors. For professionals, the commute to the CBD is genuinely short: under five kilometres, accessible by cycling infrastructure that has improved significantly through the 2020s.

In a year when everything around the Gabba is under scrutiny, East Brisbane properties are well positioned for campaigns that clearly articulate the suburb's long-term urban credentials.
