## Caption

The number of properties that listed in December tells you more about the February market than December itself.

When sellers who held off listing in November and December finally come to market in late January and February, supply conditions shift. Not dramatically, but enough to matter for buyers who have been pre-approved and ready since spring.

In Brisbane's inner east, the December-to-February listing gap follows a consistent pattern. Vendors who list in the first two weeks of February tend to catch the strongest buyer pool of the year: people who have made a decision over the break, finance is organised, and the school year is still new enough that families are not yet locked into routines.

What I watch for is the quality of that stock. Well-prepared properties that come to market in the first two weeks of February consistently attract competitive bidding. The ones that arrive in March face a buyer pool that has already been through ten to fifteen inspections and is more selective.

If you are planning to sell in the first half of 2027, the preparation work starts now. Not the photography or the staging. The conversations with your solicitor, the decision about inclusions, the building and pest. That is the pre-work that decides whether your February listing is polished or rushed.

If it is useful, I am happy to walk through what a February campaign looks like in your specific suburb.
