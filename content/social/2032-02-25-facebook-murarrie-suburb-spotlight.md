## Caption

Murarrie sits at an inflection point that not enough buyers have noticed yet. The suburb borders Hemmant, Cannon Hill, and Tingalpa, and its northern edge runs along the Brisbane River in a section that has remained largely industrial and light commercial for decades. That is changing.

The precinct development that followed the Northshore Hamilton transformation and the infrastructure upgrades to the Gateway Motorway corridor have improved Murarrie's connectivity in ways that were not present 10 years ago. The Brisbane Airport is within 15 minutes without the motorway congestion that affects suburbs further west, and the suburb's position adjacent to the Murarrie Recreation Reserve gives residents one of the better green space buffers in the eastern suburbs.

The residential stock is predominantly post-war and brick, with a range of block sizes that suit both families looking for usable land and investors who understand the rental yield profile in a suburb this close to the airport employment corridor and the Gateway industrial precinct.

In 2032, Murarrie has received fresh attention as the broader Games infrastructure investment upgraded the eastern corridor. For buyers who have assessed the suburb on fundamentals and reached a positive conclusion, the autumn market provides a sensible entry window before prices in the surrounding suburbs make the comparison even more compelling.
