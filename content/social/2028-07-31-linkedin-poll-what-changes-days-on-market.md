## Caption

Days on market is one of those metrics that looks simple but is driven by a few very specific inputs. I have sold homes in under a week and I have seen well-located properties sit for two months. The difference is rarely the suburb.

When I look back at the inner east campaigns that sold quickly versus the ones that did not, a clear pattern holds. Price is the first filter buyers apply. Presentation determines whether they convert from interest to inspection. But there is a third factor that does not get enough discussion.

In your view, what most determines how quickly a home sells?

## Poll options

- Asking price relative to comparable sales
- Presentation and photography quality
- Time of year and market conditions
