## Caption

The Balmoral market does not follow the conventional seasonal rhythm in the way that higher-volume suburbs do. With a small number of transactions in any given year and a buyer pool that is concentrated, experienced, and patient, the concept of a busy season versus a quiet season applies differently here. A vendor in Balmoral who brings the right property to market in June is not necessarily disadvantaged relative to an autumn campaign, because the buyers who are specifically searching for Balmoral are not going away between seasons. They are waiting.

The riverfront premium in Balmoral is the most documented price driver in the suburb, but the premium does not stop at the river's edge. Properties on the Esplanade command the top tier, but the streets immediately behind it share enough of the river orientation, the tree canopy, and the character of the precinct to carry their own premium over equivalent housing in surrounding suburbs. Understanding that tier structure within Balmoral is important for an accurate appraisal.

The school catchment story in Balmoral connects to Balmoral State School for primary years and to the Cavendish Road and Lourdes Hill College corridor for secondary options. Families attracted to Balmoral are usually making a decision with both a primary and secondary school outcome in mind, and the suburb's school adjacency satisfies both at the high end.

The end of financial year sometimes sees buyer decisions deferred. For Balmoral, the concentration of the buyer pool means those buyers simply resume after EOFY with the same brief.
