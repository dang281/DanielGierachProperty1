## Caption

If you want to settle before Christmas, the window for launching a new campaign is essentially closed.

Here is the arithmetic: a standard inner east campaign runs three to four weeks. If you go live this week and are under contract by early December, a 30-day settlement takes you to early January. A 45-day settlement takes you to mid-January.

For most sellers this is perfectly fine. A January settlement with the funds in your account before the end of the school holidays is a clean outcome. But the sellers who specifically wanted to be financially settled before Christmas needed to have been under contract four weeks ago.

What is still very viable: launching now for a January or February settlement. The buyers who are still active in late November are motivated. They are not waiting for the new year. They want to be under contract before the summer break so they know where they stand.

A mid-November to mid-December campaign can deliver very strong results. It is not a consolation window. It is the window where the buyers who missed out on October and November properties have the clearest run at a new listing and the most motivation to commit.

If you have been sitting on a decision about listing, the question now is not whether to launch before Christmas. It is whether a January settlement suits your situation and whether the buyers who are active right now are the buyers you want.
