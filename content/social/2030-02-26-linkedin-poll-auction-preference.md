## Caption

The sale method conversation is one where seller preference and market conditions sometimes align and sometimes don't.

Auction appeals to sellers who want transparency and a defined timeline. When the buyer pool is competitive and multiple parties are genuinely interested, auction is the mechanism that best captures that competition. The result is visible, unconditional, and final.

Private treaty gives sellers more control over the negotiation timeline but relies on the agent's ability to manage multiple buyer conversations simultaneously. It works well when the buyer pool is narrower or when conditions are likely.

Expression of interest sits between the two. It creates urgency through a structured deadline but doesn't require the theatre of an auction day. For some sellers and some properties, this is the right fit.

The honest answer is: the right method depends on the property, the buyer pool, and the seller's priorities. One method is not inherently superior.

If selling your inner east home, which sale method would you choose?
