## Caption

Most Queensland buyers sign the contract first and call their solicitor second.

It is a common sequence, and it is backwards. The contract review happens before you sign, not after. By the time you hand it to a solicitor post-execution, the negotiating window has closed.

The other thing most buyers and sellers do not know: in Queensland, you do not have to use a solicitor at all. A licensed conveyancer can handle the full settlement process on a standard residential transaction, including title searches, transfer documents, and settlement coordination, for typically $800 to $1,500. A solicitor handling the same transaction typically costs $1,500 to $2,500 or more.

The practical difference comes when things get complicated. A conveyancer cannot give legal advice outside of conveyancing work and cannot represent you if a dispute ends in court. For a straightforward purchase with no unusual conditions, a conveyancer often does the job just as well.

Where a solicitor earns the extra cost: off-the-plan purchases, SMSF acquisitions, estate sales, unusual special conditions, or any transaction that could end up contested.

Understanding which one you actually need before you sign can save you money. And in some cases, it prevents a problem that costs far more.
