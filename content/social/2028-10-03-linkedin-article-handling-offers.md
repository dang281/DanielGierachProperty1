## Caption

Most sellers focus on price when an offer arrives. That is understandable. But price is only one variable in an offer, and in many cases it is not the most important one.

An unconditional offer eliminates two to three weeks of conditional risk. A buyer who is pre-approved and has already done their building and pest inspection can go unconditional today. A buyer offering more money subject to finance and a satisfactory building report is asking you to wait three weeks to find out whether the deal holds together.

In a rising market, the premium on certainty is real. In a flat market, it can be the difference between a campaign that settles cleanly and one that falls over two weeks before Christmas with no backup buyer in place.

The other variable that sellers regularly underestimate is settlement timing. A buyer willing to match your preferred settlement date is worth something. A buyer who needs twelve weeks because their own sale has not settled is asking you to carry risk that has a cost.

Rejecting a low offer outright without a counter closes the conversation entirely. Most buyers come in below their actual ceiling and expect a counter. Eight to ten per cent below asking price is the standard inner east opening position.

The full article covers how to compare offers across all the variables that matter, not just the headline number.
