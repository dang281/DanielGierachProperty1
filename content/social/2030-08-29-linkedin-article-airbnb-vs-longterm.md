## Caption

The gross revenue figure for a short-term rental is almost always higher than the equivalent long-term rent. The net figure is a different conversation.

Short-term rental platforms charge 15 to 20% of gross revenue as a service fee before you see any income. That is the starting point. On top of that, a short-term rental requires professional cleaning after every stay: typically $150 to $250 per clean depending on the property size, and you will need this done weekly or more often during peak periods. Insurance for short-term rental is a specialist product and costs more than standard landlord insurance. Maintenance is higher because of the volume of guests and the wear that comes with it. And vacancy: short-term rental in Brisbane does not run at 100% occupancy year-round. A realistic occupancy rate needs to be built into the model, not assumed away.

When you add those costs to a typical inner east property and compare the net return to a well-managed long-term tenancy with a reliable tenant and low vacancy, the gap narrows considerably. In many cases, the net difference is a few thousand dollars per year. That marginal income comes with substantially more operational complexity: managing bookings, coordinating cleaners, maintaining supplies, handling guest communications, and dealing with periods of unplanned vacancy.

For some investors and some properties, short-term rental makes good strategic sense. For many, the long-term model produces a comparable net return with significantly less involvement.
