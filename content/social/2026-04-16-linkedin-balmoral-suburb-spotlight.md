## Caption

Balmoral sits 9km east of Brisbane's CBD on the river, and it trades at a premium that reflects those fundamentals consistently.

The suburb profile is a classic high-capital-growth, lower-yield story. The buyer here is typically not a yield-chasing investor. They are an owner-occupier: usually a family, often upgrading from elsewhere in the inner east, anchored to the school catchment, the river, and the character of the streetscape. That buyer type is more rate-sensitive than an investor but also more committed once they have made the decision.

In the current environment, with the cash rate at 4.10 per cent, those buyers are taking longer to get finance approved and are moving through inspections more carefully. That does not mean they are not there. It means that properties presented well and priced accurately tend to attract the right buyer without sitting unnecessarily.

The sellers who do best in Balmoral right now are the ones who go in with a clear understanding of comparable sales, not an optimistic estimate. The suburb has a strong floor but the ceiling depends heavily on presentation and timing.
