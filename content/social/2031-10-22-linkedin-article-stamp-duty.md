## Caption

Most buyers underestimate Queensland transfer duty by $10,000 or more. That gap typically shows up at the worst possible moment: after they have made their first offer.

On a $1.1 million purchase as an owner-occupier, the transfer duty payable is approximately $43,350. Many buyers purchasing at that level have mentally budgeted $25,000 to $30,000, based on a rough recollection of a number they heard years ago. The actual figure is significantly higher and needs to be in the bank before settlement.

The duty calculation in Queensland is a progressive rate applied to the purchase price. It is not a flat percentage. That means it increases faster at higher price points than most buyers expect, and the jump between the $800K range and the $1.1 million range is meaningful.

First home buyers have access to concessions that can reduce or eliminate duty depending on the purchase price and whether the property is new or established. But many first home buyers in the inner east are purchasing at price points that sit above the full concession threshold, which means they still face a material duty obligation.

The other figure buyers frequently miss is the mortgage registration fee and transfer fee, which add further to the upfront cost at settlement.
