## Caption

Vendors often prefer a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one. Understanding why changes how you should structure your offer.

An unconditional offer removes the finance condition, the building and pest condition, and the valuation condition. Once signed, the buyer is committed.

The risk to the buyer is real: if the bank does not approve the loan or the building report reveals significant issues, you are still legally bound to complete.

In a competitive spring market, the ability to go unconditional can be the difference between securing a property and missing out to a buyer who took the risk.
