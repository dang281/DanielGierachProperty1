## Caption

Most sellers spend six months deciding whether to sell, then rush the next six weeks preparing their property. The ratio should be closer to the reverse.

The decision to sell is the simple part. Once you have made it, every day you delay preparation is a day you are losing. The campaign launch date is fixed by the preparation work, not the other way around. A property that needs four weeks of decluttering, minor repairs, and styling does not launch in a fortnight no matter how urgent the timeline feels.

What I see often in the inner east is sellers who have thought carefully about whether to sell but not about what selling actually requires. They have a price in mind, usually from an online estimate or a conversation at a dinner party. They have a vague sense of spring being better. They have not thought about the building and pest, the Form 6, the marketing budget, or what they need to do before the first photographer walks through the door.

The listing decision is actually two decisions: the first is whether to sell, the second is how to sell well. Most agents only have a conversation about the first.

If you are thinking about selling in the next six to twelve months, the time to have the second conversation is now, not the week you have decided.
