## Caption

East Brisbane sits at the city's edge in a way that few Brisbane suburbs can claim. Adjacent to The Gabba entertainment precinct and within walking distance of Kangaroo Point and South Bank via the Goodwill Bridge, it is a suburb where the city is genuinely on the doorstep rather than a drive away.

The housing mix is predominantly apartments and townhouses, which suits the buyer profile the suburb draws: professionals and downsizers who have chosen to prioritise walkability and city proximity over land. Those buyers are not in East Brisbane because they compromised on location. They are there because it was the specific outcome they were targeting.

Rental demand in East Brisbane is strong. ⚠️ VERIFY. CBD workers and university students make up a consistent portion of the tenant pool, and that demand supports investors who hold property in the suburb for yield as well as capital growth. For a well-positioned apartment or townhouse in East Brisbane, vacancy periods tend to be shorter than the Brisbane-wide average. ⚠️ VERIFY.

The walkability dimension is worth being specific about. Kangaroo Point is accessible on foot via the Goodwill Bridge, and South Bank's parklands, restaurants, and cultural precinct are a short walk or bicycle ride away. For residents who have previously driven to access those amenities from an outer suburb, the shift in daily experience is significant.
