## Caption

Sellers spend a lot of time thinking about what buyers will notice in a property. Buyers often surprise them.

The answer varies by buyer type and property. A family looking at a four-bedroom home in Camp Hill notices different things first than an investor looking at a two-bedroom unit in Bulimba. But some things are consistent.

Street appeal forms a first impression before the buyer even steps out of the car. The front fence, the garden, the condition of the facade. In the inner east, where character homes often have original timber features, this can either signal quality or flag neglect immediately.

Natural light is the most commonly cited thing buyers mention when they describe why they loved a property. Layout and light are connected. Open-plan spaces that flow toward the rear of a north-facing block tell a story in seconds.

Smell and cleanliness are things no amount of staging overcomes if they're wrong. Buyers notice. They always notice.

When you walk through a property for sale, what do you notice first?

## Poll options

- Street appeal and gardens before you even go inside
- Natural light and layout the moment you enter
- Kitchen and bathrooms
- Smell, cleanliness, and overall presentation
