## Caption

Hold period is one of the variables that most affects property outcomes in the inner east, but it rarely gets as much attention as timing or price.

A three-year hold in Hawthorne can return a solid result if the market cooperates. A ten-year hold in the same street, bought at the same price, is a different proposition entirely. Transaction costs are amortised across a longer period. The compounding effect of low vacancy and strong rental demand has more time to work. And the seller is in a position to choose their moment rather than sell because the hold period has run out.

How do you think about hold period for inner east property?
