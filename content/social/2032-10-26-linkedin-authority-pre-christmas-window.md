## Caption

There are roughly eight weeks between now and Christmas. That is a tighter window than most sellers realise when they are still deciding whether to list.

Here is the practical constraint. A buyer who wants to settle before Christmas needs to exchange contracts by late November, assuming a standard 30-day settlement. That means they need to find the property, complete due diligence, arrange finance approval, and be ready to sign somewhere around the third week of November. For a buyer working to that timeline, they need to be actively inspecting now.

The buyer pool that operates to a pre-Christmas deadline is a motivated one. These are people with a concrete reason to move: leases ending, children starting school in January, a prior sale already settled. They are not browsing. When they find a property that works, they act.

The risk for sellers who delay listing until mid-November is that they miss this cohort entirely. By the time a campaign builds momentum, those buyers have either found something else or accepted they are settling in the new year. The remaining December buyers are still genuine, but the pool is smaller and the urgency is different.

If you are planning to list this spring and the timeline is still flexible, October and early November is where the deepest motivated buyer demand sits. That is not pressure, it is just the calendar.
