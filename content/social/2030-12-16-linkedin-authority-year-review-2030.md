## Caption

2030 in Brisbane's inner east was a year where the gap between good campaigns and average ones was wider than it has been for several years.

The properties that performed well shared recognisable characteristics. They were prepared properly before launch. The price guide was supported by comparable sales, not by what the vendor hoped the market had moved to since they last checked. The agent and vendor agreed on a strategy before the first buyer walked through, rather than adjusting it in response to feedback after the first two opens.

The properties that underperformed also shared characteristics. They came to market too high, had feedback weeks before a price guide correction, and by the time the guide reflected market evidence, the buyer who would have paid the higher number had moved on to something else.

The market itself, from a demand perspective, was reasonably healthy through the year. Open home attendance was consistent. The buyer pool that emerged in spring was prepared and ready to act. The October and November auction weekends produced results in line with the depth of that buyer pool.

Where the year was more complex was in pricing expectations. Several years of strong growth created a reference point in many vendors' minds that was not always consistent with current comparable sales evidence. The vendors who adjusted to reality early in their campaign did better than those who held out.

Heading into 2031, the conditions for a well-run campaign remain intact. The 2032 Olympic countdown, continued population growth in the inner east, and a buyer pool that has rebuilt its confidence over the past 18 months all support the fundamentals. The market rewards preparation. That has not changed.
