## Caption

Norman Park in winter is a suburb in its element. The character homes on the ridge streets above the train station sit in clear winter air, the Whites Hill Reserve is at its most inviting for morning walks, and the compact village around the station has a quality that rewards the kind of routine that cool mornings create. Long-term Norman Park residents will tell you that winter is their preferred season in this suburb, and the way the streets are used during June mornings supports that.

The train line through Norman Park station is part of the suburb's fundamental transport infrastructure. For households where at least one person commutes by train, the walk to the station from the ridge streets is manageable, and the train connection to the CBD is direct. That accessibility reinforces Norman Park's position as a suburb that functions well for working households rather than solely for retirees or those with flexible schedules.

The Whites Hill State College catchment is the demand driver that prospective vendors in Norman Park need to understand precisely. The boundary is specific, and buyers who are purchasing for catchment reasons are uncompromising about it. A property that is in the catchment commands a premium over one that is outside it, even on an adjacent street, and that premium has been consistent and measurable over time.

For vendors considering a June or July campaign, the reduced competition from other Norman Park listings through winter can be meaningful for a suburb where the buyer pool is concentrated and specific.
