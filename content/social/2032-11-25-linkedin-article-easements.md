## Caption

A drainage easement along the back fence of an inner east property almost never affects its resale value. An access easement that runs through the yard is an entirely different matter. Most buyers looking at a property with an easement listed on title cannot tell which type it is without knowing exactly what to look for, and that gap in understanding affects both buying decisions and selling campaigns.

Easements appear on the title search as a notation. Without pulling the registered plan and reading the terms, the notation alone tells you almost nothing useful. Is it a drainage easement held by Council that sits quietly underground and has no effect on what you can build? Or is it an access easement in favour of a neighbouring property, giving that neighbour the legal right to cross your land? The distinction changes the conversation entirely.

In the inner east, drainage easements are common, particularly on properties backing onto older stormwater infrastructure. Most experienced buyers treat these as neutral once they understand what they actually are. Access easements require far more careful consideration because they affect how you use the land, what you can build, and potentially how future buyers perceive the property when it comes time to sell.

The article linked below covers the main types of easements found on Queensland titles, how to identify what you are actually dealing with, and the questions worth asking before going unconditional.
