## Caption

Murarrie occupies an interesting position in Brisbane's property market: close enough to the inner east to benefit from that corridor's demand, yet priced at a point that remains accessible for buyers who have found themselves priced out of neighbouring suburbs. At around eight kilometres from the CBD, it is well within the radius that Brisbane buyers increasingly consider viable for owner-occupying.

The suburb has an industrial-residential mix that reflects its evolution over time. Newer townhouse developments have brought a different demographic to the area, with younger buyers and downsizers attracted by the price point and the access the suburb provides to both the Gateway Motorway and the inner city. That connectivity is a genuine asset for residents who commute either to the CBD or to employment nodes further east and south-east.

For investors, Murarrie has offered a more accessible entry into the inner east rental market. Vacancy rates in the broader corridor have historically been low, and the suburb benefits from proximity to employment precincts that generate consistent rental demand. Current medians in Murarrie reflect its position as the more affordable end of the inner east, with pricing that contrasts with the premium suburbs immediately to the west.

The suburb is continuing to evolve as development brings new residents and amenity follows. Buyers who have been watching that trajectory and are considering a move now may be positioning ahead of continued change.
