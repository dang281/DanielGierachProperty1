## Caption

There is a version of this post I could write that makes predictions. I am not going to do that.

What I am going to do is share the signals I am actually watching as we head into 2029, because I think they are more useful than a forecast.

The Olympic 2032 infrastructure conversation has moved from announcements to construction visibility. The suburbs closest to the venue precincts and the transport upgrades are starting to see a buyer type that was not present in 2024 or 2025: the buyer who is underwriting the 2029 and 2030 market, not just the one they are buying in today. That buyer is patient, specific, and long-term in their thinking.

The supply constraint in the inner east has not resolved. The number of properties hitting the market each month continues to reflect a population of owners who are largely staying put. When something well-located comes to market, it attracts a depth of interest that the total listing numbers do not suggest.

The interest rate environment in 2029 will matter. What I am not doing is making a call on direction, because the institutions that do this for a living are not in agreement.

What I am confident about: the inner east in 2029 is a market where preparation and accurate pricing still produce better outcomes than any other variable. That has been true for the four years I have been watching it closely. I expect it to remain true.

I hope you have a good end to 2028. I look forward to the conversations in January.
