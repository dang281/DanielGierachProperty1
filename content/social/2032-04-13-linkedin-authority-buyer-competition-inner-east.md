## Caption

At open homes across the inner east over the past four to six weeks, I've been seeing a consistent pattern: more registered buyers per listing, shorter time-on-market for well-priced properties, and fewer conditional offers getting up before unconditional ones move in.

Some of that is seasonal. Autumn has historically been an active buying period in Brisbane. But Q2 2032 has an additional layer.

A portion of buyers have decided they want to be in their property before the Olympics. Whether that's because they want to be settled, or because they expect short-term rental demand to push up rental costs during the Games and want out of the rental market, the effect is the same: motivated buyers with a deadline.

There's also a supply dynamic worth noting. Listings in the inner east have not kept pace with that demand in April. When the pool of available properties is smaller and buyer motivation is higher, you get more competition per listing. That tends to show up in results.

What I want to be clear about is that this is an observation based on current conditions, not a forecast. Markets shift. The fact that competition is elevated right now does not guarantee it will remain elevated through June.

What it does mean is that sellers with well-prepared properties in good locations are in a reasonable position at this point in the year.
