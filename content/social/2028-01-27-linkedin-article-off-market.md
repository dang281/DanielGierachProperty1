## Caption

Off-market sales have become more common in Brisbane's inner east over the past few years, and sellers are often told they are getting a great deal because they avoided the stress of a campaign.

What they are usually getting is a less competitive outcome.

Off-market means one buyer, or a small number of buyers, without the pressure of an open campaign behind them. When a buyer knows there is no competing offer, their opening position shifts. When they know a property has not been publicly tested, they feel less urgency to stretch.

There are situations where off-market makes sense. A seller who genuinely cannot accommodate inspections, a property with a complex tenancy situation, a buyer who is already known and has agreed to a price at or above what a campaign would achieve. Those situations exist. They are not the majority.

The line I use with sellers is this: "We have a buyer ready to go" is a convenience offer. It is worth understanding what it means for your likely sale price before you accept it.
