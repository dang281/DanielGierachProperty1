## Caption

Two weeks into spring and the inner east is giving a clearer picture of what the post-Olympic market actually looks like rather than what was predicted.

The owner-occupier price points that dominate the Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Balmoral corridor are active. Auction clearance at well-priced homes is solid. Buyer groups at opens in that corridor are back to the kind of numbers that produce genuine competition on the day.

Properties with strong school catchment, good street appeal, and honest pricing are not sitting. The ones that are sitting are sitting for a reason that was in place before September arrived: they came to market over-priced during the Games period, and price adjustments have not yet been made.

There are two distinct market experiences right now. Properties that came to market well-prepared and priced correctly for the spring are performing cleanly. Properties that are carrying the weight of an over-optimistic Games-period launch are still working through the consequences.

Days on market tells the story more clearly than any other single measure in the inner east. A property that is well-presented, correctly priced, and in a suburb with active buyer demand should not be sitting past three weeks in September. If it is sitting past that point, the price is the conversation that needs to happen.

The honest read two weeks into spring: the market is performing. Not dramatically, not with the kind of frenzy that gets written up in weekend property sections, but with the steady buyer depth that produces good outcomes for sellers who have done the preparation.
