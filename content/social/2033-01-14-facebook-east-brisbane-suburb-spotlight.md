## Caption

East Brisbane is two kilometres from the CBD and it has always had the property values to match. The suburb sits between the Gabba precinct and the river, and the Eton Street corridor gives it a walkable, neighbourhood character that inner-city buyers consistently value.

The housing stock in East Brisbane is mixed in a way that suits a range of buyers. Older character homes, some federation-era, sit alongside converted workers' cottages, terrace-style homes, and more recent townhouse development. Owner-occupiers and investors have always competed for stock here, and that competition tends to support values in both directions of the market cycle. ⚠️ VERIFY

What has changed in recent years is the demographic character of the buyer pool. East Brisbane now draws a significant cohort of upsizing buyers from the CBD and South Brisbane, people who want more space and a house with a yard without adding thirty minutes to their commute. The Eton Street precinct and the riverfront access points give the suburb a residential quality that its proximity to the city does not diminish.

For vendors, East Brisbane in 2033 means dealing with a sophisticated buyer pool that has seen a lot of property and has clear views on what represents value. Presentation and honest pricing are the variables that separate average results from strong ones here.
