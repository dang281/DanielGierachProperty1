## Caption

Days on market is one of the most honest numbers in property.

A suburb's median price tells you what properties sold for. Days on market tells you how long they had to wait to find a buyer at that price. The two numbers together give a clearer picture of the market than either does alone.

In Brisbane's inner east right now, well-priced homes in suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Balmoral are moving in under 20 days. That's not unusual for the area. What is unusual, and worth watching, is when a property in one of these suburbs sits for 45 or 60 days.

That extended time on market almost always traces back to one of three things: price expectations that exceed what the market is willing to pay; presentation issues that the marketing photos softened; or a catchment or access issue that buyers discovered at inspection.

The properties that sell in under 14 days in the inner east are not always the best ones. They're the ones that were priced honestly, presented well, and marketed with a sense of urgency. Buyers who see a property that ticks the boxes and is priced without optimism act quickly. They've seen too many properties sit to believe a good one will wait.

If a property sits in a strong suburb, there is a reason. Finding it early is the difference between a price adjustment and a decision to sell.
