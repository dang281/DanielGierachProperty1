## Caption

Auction is not always the right method. But when the conditions are right, it is the most effective way to find the ceiling price in a competitive buyer pool.

The conditions that favour auction in the inner east: a property that is genuinely desirable to more than one buyer, a buyer pool where two or more have finance ready, and a vendor who understands that the reserve price is a floor, not the target.

Where auction underperforms: a property with a niche appeal, a suburb where comparable sales give buyers too much certainty about the ceiling, or when the vendor sets the reserve so high that the property passes in and post-auction negotiation becomes the fallback.

Spring is the season where auction works best in the inner east because it coincides with peak buyer activity. More buyers at open homes increases the probability that two of them will bid against each other. That competition is the point of the method.

Sellers sometimes ask me whether they should auction or do private treaty. The honest answer is that the right method is the one that matches the nature of your specific property and the buyer pool it will attract. For some inner east properties, auction in October generates genuine competition. For others, private treaty with expression of interest gives the vendor more control over the process.
