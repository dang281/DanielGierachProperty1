## Caption

Camp Hill does not need to be explained to buyers who have been watching the inner east for more than a cycle. It is one of the few Brisbane suburbs that sits at the top of almost every family buyer's list and stays there. The reason is straightforward: the combination of the hill streets with CBD views, the Whites Hill State College catchment overlap with Norman Park, and the Old Cleveland Road café and retail strip produces an address that is genuinely hard to replicate.

The elevation is a defining feature. Streets running up from Old Cleveland Road toward the ridge carry city views that put properties in a different conversation at inspection. Buyers who arrive at a well-presented home on one of those hill streets with a clear view toward the CBD understand immediately what they are looking at. That understanding tends to resolve price negotiations.

Supply is structurally constrained here. Camp Hill is a suburb where the owner profile skews toward long tenure. When stock does come to market, it competes against a buyer pool that has been building through the periods when nothing was available. That dynamic has supported Camp Hill's price performance through conditions that were softer for other inner east suburbs.

For owners thinking about the autumn window, early preparation is the variable that determines outcomes.
