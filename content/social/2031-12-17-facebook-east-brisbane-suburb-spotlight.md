## Caption

East Brisbane has less available land than almost any suburb in the inner east, and that scarcity is part of what keeps its property market performing. When a freestanding character home comes to market here, the buyer pool that assembles knows exactly how rare the opportunity is.

The Woolloongabba entertainment precinct has continued to develop around The Gabba, and East Brisbane residents are the closest beneficiaries. The restaurants, bars, and activity around that precinct are within walking distance for much of the suburb, and that walkability to entertainment is something that young professionals and downsizers actively seek and pay for.

East Brisbane State School anchors family demand on the local side, but the suburb's buyer profile is broader than most. Young professionals, couples, and downsizers who want an inner-city address without the apartment lifestyle make up the bulk of the owner-occupier buyer pool. The townhouse market here captures the buyers who want low maintenance, while the freestanding character home market captures those who want original quality and won't accept a compromise.

December in East Brisbane is one of the most interesting times in the market here. With limited supply coming through in the lead-up to Christmas, the buyers who are committed and financed have fewer options than usual. That supply constraint in the final weeks of the year can work strongly in favour of vendors who are ready.
