## Caption

The buyers who are actively purchasing property in November have a specific profile, and understanding them helps vendors set realistic expectations for a late-spring campaign.

November buyers in the inner east tend to be pre-approved, have been searching for at least three months, and have usually missed out on one or two properties earlier in the year. That experience makes them more decisive. When they find the right property, they act faster than they would have in August.

The second type of November buyer is the one who has just received a settlement from their own sale and needs to buy before the end of the year for tax or practical reasons. This buyer is highly motivated and typically willing to move on price to avoid carrying two properties over summer.

Neither profile is the cautious first-inspection buyer who shows up in September. November buyers are further along in the process and have already done the mental work of deciding what they want. The open home count is lower but the quality of buyers inside tends to be higher.

For vendors, this means a November campaign can work very well for the right property. The challenge is that you need to price correctly from day one. Overpriced properties that get reduced in December rarely recover the momentum they lost.

If you are considering a November campaign, a pre-market appraisal conversation is the right place to start.
