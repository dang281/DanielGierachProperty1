## Caption

One of the questions I hear from vendors who are ready to sell but hesitant about winter timing is whether a July launch will produce a result as good as waiting for spring. It is a reasonable question, and the honest answer is more nuanced than the seasonal assumption suggests.

A property going to market in the inner east in late July is not competing against the spring listing crop. That is not a consolation. It is a structural advantage if the vendor uses it correctly.

In spring, a genuine buyer in Morningside or Camp Hill might have eight, ten, or twelve comparable properties to consider across a six-week period. In late July, that same buyer might have three. Their attention and motivation is concentrated on what is available. When the right property hits the market with the right price and the right presentation, there is no competing listing pulling that buyer sideways.

The vendors who find a genuine buyer in July and negotiate a strong result are not beating the market by luck. They are selling into a window where the buyer they needed has been waiting since May for something that suits them, and competing supply has not yet arrived.

What makes this work is preparation and pricing. A winter launch that is under-prepared or over-priced does not benefit from any of this. The buyer who attends in July is decisive, but they are not forgiving of a property that is not ready or a price guide that does not reflect reality. Get both right, and a July launch can produce a result that compares favourably with what you would have achieved in October surrounded by ten other listings.

The absence of competition in winter is only an advantage if you are worth finding when someone goes looking.
