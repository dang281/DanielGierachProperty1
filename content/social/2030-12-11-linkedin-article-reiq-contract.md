## Caption

One of the most costly misunderstandings in Queensland property purchases involves the finance condition.

Most buyers understand that a finance condition gives them time to arrange their home loan. What many do not understand is that the condition lapses on the deadline date, regardless of whether the bank has provided a formal approval.

If you have a finance condition with a 14-business-day deadline and on the deadline date you have a verbal indication from your bank but no written approval, you are at a critical decision point. You can either waive the condition on time and proceed at risk, or you can request an extension from the vendor. The vendor has no obligation to grant that extension. If the extension is refused and you fail to waive or satisfy the condition by the deadline, the contract can lapse and your deposit may be at risk.

The mechanism that most buyers assume will protect them, the finance condition, only provides protection if it is managed actively. That means understanding the exact deadline, communicating regularly with your broker or bank about where the approval is in the process, and raising any risk of delay with your solicitor several days before the deadline, not on the day itself.

The other clause worth understanding is inclusions. Items present in the property at inspection but not listed in the contract are not automatically included in the sale. What stays and what goes must be written into the contract before signing.
