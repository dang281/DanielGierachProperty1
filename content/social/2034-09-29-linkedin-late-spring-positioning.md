## Caption

For sellers who have not yet listed but are still considering a 2034 campaign, the window is narrowing but it has not closed.

October is one of the more consistent months in the Brisbane inner east. Spring stock has been absorbed by buyers who were active in September, which means the properties launching in October are meeting a buyer pool that has not been exhausted by too many weekends of open homes. Buyer fatigue is a real phenomenon in spring markets, and October often benefits from having fewer properties in front of buyers who are still committed.

The preparation question is the most important one right now: can you be ready in time? A campaign launch in the second week of October means photography should be happening in the next ten days. If you are not in the preparation phase, an October launch is probably not the right target.

What October and November offer that September does not: buyers who missed out in the earlier spring surge and are now more motivated, potentially more flexible on settlement terms, and actively looking in a market with gradually decreasing competing stock.

What October and November lose compared to September: sheer volume of buyer attendance. Peak spring attendance is September. The tradeoff is fewer but more motivated buyers in October.

If you are still deciding, the clearest thing I can tell you is: make the decision this week. The timeline does not allow for much more deliberation.
