## Caption

Families with children starting high school in 2028 are making their suburb decisions right now. Not in September, not when spring listings appear. Now.

The school catchment conversation in Brisbane's inner east happens roughly 18 months before enrolment, not 18 days. Parents who want their child starting Year 7 at Balmoral State High or Whites Hill State College in early 2028 need to be living within the catchment before the enrolment deadline. That means they need to have bought, settled, and moved in. Which means the property search starts in the middle of 2027. Which means this is exactly when that buyer pool is actively looking.

I see this pattern clearly every year. Enquiry on properties within the Balmoral State High and Whites Hill State College catchments lifts noticeably in the May to July window from a specific type of buyer: organised, pre-approved, and already very clear on which streets they will consider. They are not browsing. They know what they need and they are watching for it to come to market.

For vendors with properties inside those catchments, this is worth understanding before you make a decision about campaign timing. A family-focused buyer who is operating to a school year deadline is motivated in a very specific way. They are not going to wait for spring and they are not going to offer on five properties hoping one sticks. If your property is the right one, they will act.

If you are in the inner east and you want to know whether your property sits within one of those catchments, I can tell you in about thirty seconds. Knowing that one fact changes the conversation about when to list.
