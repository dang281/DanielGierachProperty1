## Caption

The best time to sell is when your property is ready. Not when the calendar says it is spring.

This is probably the most consistent pattern I observe across the inner east, and it runs counter to how most vendors think about timing. The assumption is that spring is peak, autumn is good, winter is quiet, and December is a write-off. The reality is that a well-prepared campaign in any of those periods consistently outperforms an unprepared campaign in the supposed peak month. I see this year after year across a range of price points and property types.

The preparation phase is where most of the result is determined. Pre-sale repairs and maintenance addressed. Property styled to show at its best. Photography and floor plans done properly. Disclosure statement prepared and complete. Agent selected based on relevant comparable results rather than a lowest-fee pitch. This process takes six to ten weeks when done properly. Vendors who rush it, cutting corners on presentation or skipping styling to save a few thousand dollars, consistently leave more than those savings on the table.

In Brisbane's inner east, the gap between a well-prepared and a rushed campaign result regularly runs from $50,000 to $150,000 depending on the property's price point and the extent of the shortfall in preparation. At the lower end of the price range, that gap is a meaningful proportion of the total sale price. At the higher end, it is a number that dwarfs any saving made in the preparation phase.

The practical implication is that a vendor thinking about spring should start their preparation conversation in summer. A vendor thinking about the autumn window should start in December.
