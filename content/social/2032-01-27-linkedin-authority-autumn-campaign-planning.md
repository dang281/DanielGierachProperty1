## Caption

If you want to sell your inner east home in April or May, the preparation work starts now. Not in March. Not when you feel ready. Now.

The gap between a well-prepared and a rushed inner east campaign is consistently in the range of $50,000 to $150,000 in net proceeds, depending on the property. That is not a speculative number. It reflects the difference in buyer perception, competitive tension, and negotiation position between a home that has been methodically prepared and one that has been scrambled to market because the vendor ran out of time.

What does preparation actually involve? It varies by property, but the consistent elements are: a pre-sale building and pest inspection so there are no surprises during buyer due diligence, any maintenance or cosmetic work completed well before photography, a clear decision on method of sale, a solicitor engaged and the contract drafted, and a professional stylist or presentation consultant involved before photos are taken.

The last point is where most campaigns run short. Styling and furniture hire has lead times that most vendors do not account for. Good stylists in Brisbane are fully booked during the peak autumn season. If you want the stylist who produces results, you need to book in January for a March shoot.

In 2032, the autumn window also has an additional dimension. Buyers who want to be settled before the Olympic Games in late July are motivated in the first half of the year. The vendor who is fully prepared and on the market in March is competing for that buyer pool. The vendor who scrambles to list in May has missed the peak of that demand.

If you are thinking about selling this year, the first conversation is worth having this month.
