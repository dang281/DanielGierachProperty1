## Caption

Carina's spring market reflects the suburb's straightforward buyer proposition: more land and more house at a price point that remains accessible relative to the inner east suburbs to the west and north. The buyers who arrive at Carina open homes in September and October have typically done the comparison work. They know what Camp Hill costs, what Coorparoo costs, and they have reached Carina with a clear sense of what the extra land content and house size is worth to them at the price difference.

The Westfield Carindale anchor provides the everyday retail convenience that family buyers prioritise, and the suburb's position on the eastern corridor gives practical access to the Gateway Motorway for buyers managing commutes beyond the inner city. Those dual access points, convenient shopping within two kilometres and motorway access for regional commuting, are a consistent part of the Carina buyer value proposition.

The housing stock in Carina offers larger blocks than most inner east suburbs of comparable distance from the CBD. That land content has attracted a growing number of renovation and addition projects, reflecting owner-occupiers who have chosen the suburb for the long term and invested accordingly. Properties with recent quality renovation tend to attract buyer interest quickly when they arrive at the spring market.
