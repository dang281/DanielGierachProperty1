## Caption

Mt Gravatt carries a reputation as a functional, well-connected suburb, and that reputation is accurate. The combination of Westfield Garden City, Griffith University, and the PA Hospital precinct nearby gives it an employment and services density that most suburbs in Brisbane cannot match.

What buyers are increasingly recognising is that the residential streets west of the main Mains Road corridor, particularly those running up toward the escarpment, offer a quality of streetscape and block size that the suburb's commercial reputation does not fully advertise. These streets attract buyers who have looked at Holland Park and Camp Hill and are recalculating based on what their budget actually buys in terms of land.

Autumn is the season when Mt Gravatt performs most consistently. The broader buyer pool in March includes a significant proportion of buyers from the health and university sectors, both of which have strong local employment, and those buyers are typically well-qualified and decisive.

Properties that have been updated without being over-capitalised tend to perform well here. The buyers are practical. They value functionality, storage, and outdoor space over cosmetic renovation for its own sake.

If you are thinking about selling in Mt Gravatt this year and want to understand the current market position, I am available for appraisals through the autumn campaign window.
