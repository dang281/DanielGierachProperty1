## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits on a plateau that gives it a distinctly different feel from the flatter suburbs to its north and east. The elevated position means flood risk is not a material consideration for the vast majority of properties here, and in a market where buyers have become increasingly attentive to flood overlay mapping, that certainty has value.

The Cavendish Road State High School catchment, shared with Holland Park, is the secondary school demand driver for the area. Families who have been tracking the catchment through primary school years and are now approaching high school enrolment are the most active buyer segment in Mt Gravatt when they move. Those buyers arrive with specific criteria already resolved, which makes them decisive.

Westfield Garden City anchors the retail and commercial offer for Mt Gravatt residents in a way that works at every stage of life. It is not a destination people outgrow. The centre's scale means that services, specialty retail, and dining options expand regularly enough to remain relevant to an established residential population.

The character homes on the plateau streets represent an architecture era that is producing fewer additions to the housing stock each year. Buyers who want a pre-war or mid-century character home in a flood-free elevated position with high school catchment security and Garden City access are working with a genuinely constrained supply of options. Mt Gravatt sits at the intersection of those requirements.
