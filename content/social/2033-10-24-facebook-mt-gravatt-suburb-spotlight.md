## Caption

Mt Gravatt's Logan Road spine is one of the more useful commercial strips in Brisbane's south-east corridor. The access to daily shopping, dining, and services along that stretch means the suburb functions well for residents at a practical level without requiring them to travel far for routine needs.

The healthcare and medical precinct that has developed in and around the suburb adds an employment dimension that is not common in predominantly residential areas. For residents who work in healthcare or allied health, proximity to that employment cluster is a genuine quality-of-life factor. It also anchors demand from a professional cohort that values accessible, established suburbs.

Griffith University's Nathan campus brings education and employment infrastructure to the suburb's western edge. The combination of university employment, healthcare employment, and Logan Road commercial access means Mt Gravatt has a more diverse employment proximity picture than most Brisbane suburbs at this price point.

Owner-occupier retention is strong. The suburb has high holding rates, which reflects a resident base that is satisfied with what the address delivers over the long term. When properties do come to market, they draw buyers from families and professionals who have specifically compared Mt Gravatt to surrounding suburbs and found the access to employment, education, and daily shopping to be a more straightforward package than many alternatives offer at a lower price.
