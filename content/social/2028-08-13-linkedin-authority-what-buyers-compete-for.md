## Caption

Not all properties attract competition. Understanding which ones do, and why, is the most useful piece of market intelligence a seller can have.

In the inner east right now, competition clusters around a specific combination of factors. School catchment is still the primary filter for families, and the properties that sit inside the Whites Hill, Bulimba State School, or Cannon Hill catchments attract more qualified buyers than those just outside. Elevation and view are the second driver, particularly in Camp Hill, Norman Park, and the western Morningside ridge, where buyers are paying explicitly for what they can see from the deck.

The third factor is less discussed: block shape and rear access. An inner east block with a standard rectangular shape, good rear boundary depth, and rear lane access is genuinely scarce. Buyers who want to add a granny flat, extend the house, or keep a boat are all competing for the same small pool of properties.

What does not reliably generate competition in the current market: renovated properties that are priced for the renovation plus a margin. Buyers can tell the difference between a renovation that has been done well and one that has been staged to hide the quality of the work. The ones priced for the former and presenting as the latter sit.

Knowing which category your property falls into before you go to market is worth more than any amount of marketing.
