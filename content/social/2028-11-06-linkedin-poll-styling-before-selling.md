## Caption

Professional property styling is one of the more debated pre-sale decisions. The cost of styling a three-bedroom inner east home for a four-week campaign runs roughly $3,000 to $6,000. The argument for it is straightforward: better photography drives more enquiry, enquiry drives competition, and competition drives price.

The counterargument is also real: a well-furnished, well-presented owner-occupied home may not need replacement furniture to photograph well. The question is not whether styling works in principle, it is whether your specific property needs it.

Where I see styling make the clearest difference is in vacant properties and in homes where the owners' personal furniture is not presenting the rooms at their best. Where I see styling add less is in properties that already present exceptionally well and would be disrupted rather than improved by a full replacement.

If you were selling your Brisbane inner east home, what would your approach be to styling before the campaign?

## Poll options
