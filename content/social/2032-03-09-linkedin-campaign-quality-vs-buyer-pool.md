## Caption

Autumn is the time of year when the buyer pool is strongest. It is also the time when the gap between a well-run campaign and a poorly prepared one is most visible.

In March and April, buyers are not looking at one property. They are comparing three or four simultaneously, often in the same suburb, sometimes in the same street. When you have a highly engaged buyer pool with multiple options, presentation, photography, pricing, and the quality of the selling agent's knowledge all get tested directly against the competition.

A vendor who launches with substandard photography, a price that does not make sense against recent sales, or an agent who cannot answer basic questions about the property is not just losing ground to other listings. They are actively training the best buyers to walk past.

I see this pattern repeat every autumn. The listings that perform in the top quartile tend to share three things: they are genuinely well-presented before photography, the pricing strategy is based on real comparable sales rather than vendor expectation, and the open homes are run with the kind of detail that makes buyers feel they are dealing with someone who actually knows what they are selling.

The listings in the bottom quartile tend to blame the market. In most cases, the market is not the issue.

If you are planning a March or April campaign this year and want to understand what preparation actually looks like, I am happy to walk through it with you.
