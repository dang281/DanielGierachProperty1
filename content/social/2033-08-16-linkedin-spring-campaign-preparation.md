## Caption

The preparation work for a spring campaign in Brisbane's inner east breaks into roughly three categories, and understanding which order they happen in matters as much as understanding what they involve.

The first category is structural and condition work. Anything that affects the basic integrity or functioning of the property: roof repairs, plumbing, electrical, fencing. This goes first because trades need to be booked and their work needs to be visible and documented before open homes begin. A buyer who identifies a roof concern during a building and pest inspection is more likely to renegotiate than a buyer who sees a recent repair receipt.

The second category is presentation. Paint inside and out, landscaping, floor refinishing, anything that affects the visual first impression. This goes second because the trades from the first category sometimes affect the presentation layer, and because presentation work needs to settle and dry before photography.

The third category is styling and final preparation. Professional styling, decluttering, deep cleaning, photography. This happens last because it reflects the completed property, not the work in progress.

The vendors who separate these three phases and allow adequate time for each produce photography that sells the property before anyone walks through the front door. The vendors who collapse them into a single rushed week before launch produce photography that shows it.
