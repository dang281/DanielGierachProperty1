## Caption

Post-war character homes on larger blocks have been the quiet story of Holland Park for the past several years. Buyers who couldn't stretch to Camp Hill found that Holland Park offered similar block sizes, the same family feel, and a price differential that made the maths work.

That calculation hasn't changed, but the gap has narrowed. As Camp Hill became increasingly inaccessible for buyers in the mid-range, Holland Park absorbed that demand. Families drawn to Holland Park State School and the proximity to Griffith University are competing now with buyers who would previously have stretched further north or west but couldn't justify the premium.

The Mt Gravatt High School catchment also plays into the secondary school decision for families. Being within the catchment zone is something buyers ask about, and it adds value to the right property.

Spring in Holland Park tends to bring a mix of presentation styles. The renovated Queenslander on the good block attracts heat quickly. The original post-war brick on a flat lot finds its buyer in time, but without the urgency. Vendors who understand that distinction price better and sell faster.

On streets like Abbotsford Road and Cavendish Road, well-presented homes consistently draw strong open home attendance. Foot traffic here in spring is a good signal.
