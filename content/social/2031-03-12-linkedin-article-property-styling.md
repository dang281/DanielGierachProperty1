## Caption

Staging is primarily a photography investment. The furniture matters far less than most vendors assume, and the photography matters far more.

The logic runs like this: better photographs drive higher enquiry volume. Higher enquiry volume means more buyers registering and attending open homes. More buyers at open homes creates the competitive pressure that drives offers above appraisal. The furniture does not create that competition. The photographs do, because they determine whether a buyer who has not yet visited the property adds it to their inspection list or scrolls past.

A vacant property staged for $4,000 to $5,000 does not feel dramatically different to a buyer standing in the room. Buyers can tell the difference between furniture that belongs to the home and furniture placed for presentation. What they cannot easily compensate for, before they attend, is a set of photographs that shows an empty property with bare walls and no sense of scale or warmth. That property generates fewer inspection bookings. Fewer bookings means less competition at open homes. Less competition at open homes is where appraisals go unmet.

There is also a secondary effect that is less discussed. A well-staged property photographs in a way that holds up across all the platforms where buyers are searching: the portal listing, the social media posts, the agent's database emails. Every touchpoint a buyer encounters in the campaign is driven by the photography. The styling investment buys you better creative across the entire campaign, not just the day of the shoot.
