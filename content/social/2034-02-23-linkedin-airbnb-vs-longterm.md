## Caption

The gross revenue figure from a well-run short-term rental looks compelling on a spreadsheet. It is usually the net return, once operating costs are properly accounted for, that changes the picture.

Platform fees, professional cleaning between stays, insurance premiums calibrated for short-term occupancy, consumables, and the higher maintenance rate that comes with frequent turnovers add up. For a Brisbane inner east property operating as a short-term rental, these costs typically run between $15,000 and $25,000 per year on top of the standard ownership costs you would carry regardless of tenancy type.

When you compare the net return from a well-managed short-term operation against a well-managed long-term tenancy, the financial gap is often a few thousand dollars. The short-term property requires significantly more time, active management, and tolerance for vacancy risk. The long-term tenancy is simpler, more predictable, and places far less wear on the property.

This does not mean short-term rental is the wrong choice. There are properties and locations where it makes clear financial sense, and investors who run short-term portfolios effectively. But the decision should be made on accurate net return figures, not on headline revenue projections that treat a full calendar of bookings as the base case.

The article steps through the full comparison for a Brisbane inner east investor, including how to model the numbers for your specific property.
