## Caption

A heritage or character overlay does not freeze a property in time. The common assumption is that once a home is identified under a heritage or character overlay in Brisbane, you cannot change it. That is not accurate, and it puts buyers off properties that are entirely workable.

What the overlay protects is the external character of the building as seen from the street. Brisbane City Council requires development approval for changes that affect the building's external appearance in ways that would alter its character, such as removing the front verandah, changing the roofline, or cladding over original timber. Those changes require approval and need to be designed in keeping with the style and period of the building.

What the overlay does not restrict, in most cases, is internal renovation. A kitchen, bathroom, new flooring, internal wall removal, updated wiring, new plumbing, full internal reconfiguration: these typically proceed without heritage approval. Many of the best-renovated homes in Hawthorne, Norman Park, and Bulimba are character overlay properties that have been completely modernised inside while retaining their original facades.

The distinction matters for buyers doing their due diligence. If you see a character overlay noted on a property and you plan to renovate, the question to ask your town planner is not "can I renovate?" but "which specific works require approval, and what are the design guidelines for this property type?" The answer is almost always more permissive than buyers expect.

The article covers overlay categories in Brisbane, what triggers approval requirements, and how to check a specific property's overlay status.
