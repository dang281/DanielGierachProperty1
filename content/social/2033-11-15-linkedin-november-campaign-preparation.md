## Caption

Every spring I see two types of vendors launch in roughly the same window. One has been preparing since August. The other decided to list in September and is still working through their checklist at the first open home. The results are rarely close.

The October and November buyer pool in Brisbane's inner east includes many of the year's most motivated buyers. People with December settlement goals, families targeting school catchment enrolments, and buyers who have been through multiple campaigns earlier in the year without successfully purchasing. They are informed, they are ready, and they are comparing properties carefully. A well-prepared campaign captures that energy. An underprepared one often drives those buyers to the next property on their list.

What I mean by prepared is specific. The disclosure statement is ready before the first open home, not completed under pressure after an offer is received. Styling is done before photography is booked, not after the first inspection has already happened. Pre-sale repairs are complete so that the building and pest report does not give a buyer a reason to renegotiate. Photography is taken on a clear morning, not on the first available day regardless of weather.

Each of those details is within the vendor's control. None of them are expensive relative to the cost of a failed negotiation or a price reduction after two weeks on the market. The market does not reward the month on the calendar. It rewards the work that happened before the sign went up.

If you are planning a campaign for late this year or early next, the preparation conversation is the one worth having now.
