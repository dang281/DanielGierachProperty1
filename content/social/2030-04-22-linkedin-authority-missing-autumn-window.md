## Caption

There is a version of this conversation I have every May and June. A homeowner who was thinking about selling in autumn but held off, and now is wondering whether to push on to spring or wait.

The honest answer: for most properties in the inner east, waiting until spring is not a free option.

Here is what happens to listings that launch in June or July. Buyer numbers drop, not dramatically, but noticeably. The pool of motivated buyers who were active in March and April has largely transacted. The ones remaining are either waiting for specific properties or are not yet pre-approved. And winter, particularly in Brisbane, is associated with lower photography appeal, fewer open home numbers, and less competition between buyers.

That does not mean winter campaigns cannot work. They can. But the best results in the inner east reliably come from the March to May window, and occasionally from September and October. Everything else requires more work for a similar or lesser outcome.

If you missed March and are weighing up whether to go now or wait until spring, the calculus depends on your property. A well-positioned Bulimba house with river glimpses in the $1.4M to $1.8M range will attract buyers year-round. A two-bedroom townhouse in Carina competing with six other similar listings may be better off waiting for spring, when buyer numbers are higher and the competition thins a little.

That is a conversation worth having specifically for your property.
