## Caption

By mid-June, something predictable starts to happen at inner east open homes. The groups getting through the door are smaller in number, but a higher proportion of them arrive with pre-approval already in place.

This is the buyers-positioning-for-spring pattern. These are people who have been watching the market for three to six months, decided what they want and where they want to be, and sorted their finance before the spring campaign season arrives. They want to move before the spring supply surge because they know that more listings mean more competition from other buyers.

What this means for sellers thinking about a June or July campaign: the buyer pool is small but concentrated. You are not getting 40 groups through the first open home, but the 15 you do get are likely to include five or six who are genuinely in a position to buy. The conversion rate from first inspection to offer is higher in mid-winter than at almost any other point in the year.

For sellers thinking about spring: the buyers who are most active in June are not waiting until September. If you launch in August, you may be launching after some of those buyers have already purchased. The spring crowd they leave behind is a different group.

This is not an argument to list in June regardless of your circumstances. It is an argument to understand the buyer pool before you choose your timing.
