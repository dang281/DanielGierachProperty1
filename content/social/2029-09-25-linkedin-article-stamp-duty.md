## Caption

Most buyers underestimate their stamp duty liability by $10,000 or more. It does not add to equity, it cannot be rolled into the loan, and it has to be paid upfront at settlement. Getting this number wrong at the budgeting stage creates real problems later.

Transfer duty in Queensland is calculated on a tiered rate, not a flat percentage. On a $1 million owner-occupier purchase, the duty is approximately $38,025. On an investment property at the same price, it is higher because investor concessions do not apply at the same threshold. Buyers who estimate based on a rough percentage of the purchase price frequently land on a figure well below what they actually owe.

First home buyer concessions exist and are worth understanding clearly. The full concession applies up to $700,000. Above that, the concession tapers, and above $800,000 it does not apply at all. A first home buyer purchasing at $750,000 receives a partial concession, not the full exemption, and the specific amount depends on the exact price.

The QRO, Queensland Revenue Office, has a duty calculator that gives the precise figure for any purchase price and buyer category. Verify the exact number there before finalising your budget. Do not rely on an estimate from a comparison article or a broker's ballpark figure in a pre-approval conversation.

The article covers the full rate structure, the first home buyer thresholds, and what else needs to be in the budget alongside duty.
