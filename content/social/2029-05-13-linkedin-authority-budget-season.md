## Caption

Federal budget season tends to create a brief pause in property decision-making, particularly among buyers who are watching for any announcements that affect housing affordability, first home schemes, or negative gearing.

The pause is usually short. Most budget announcements that affect housing are flagged well in advance. By the time they are confirmed, the market has largely priced them in. The buyers and sellers who wait for certainty before acting often find that certainty arrived without them noticing.

The more relevant question for inner east buyers and sellers is not what the budget says, but what the RBA does in the months following. Fiscal policy and monetary policy work on different timelines. Budget night makes headlines. Rate decisions move mortgage payments.

For inner east sellers, the practical takeaway from budget season is this: if your campaign was performing well before the budget, it will perform well after it. If a campaign is not generating competition, the budget is not the cause and not the cure. Presentation, pricing, and timing do more work than any political announcement.

The inner east buyer pool is not waiting on Canberra. Most of them have been in the market for months and are looking for the right property, not the right headline.
