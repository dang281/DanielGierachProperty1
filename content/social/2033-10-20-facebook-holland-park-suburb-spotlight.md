## Caption

Holland Park is the kind of suburb that does not generate a lot of noise in the market because it does not need to. Turnover is low, owner-occupier retention is high, and the families who move here tend to stay. That is a quiet signal about what the suburb offers, and it is one that buyers who understand inner east property recognise.

Mount Gravatt Outlook provides a weekend destination that most inner east suburbs do not have nearby. The walking trails and elevated views from the reserve are part of what residents describe when they talk about the suburb's appeal, and it adds a recreational dimension to an established leafy address that is within 8 kilometres of the CBD.

Logan Road provides the daily retail and dining access that complements the suburb's residential character. The strip has depth without requiring Holland Park to be a high-street destination in its own right. Residents draw on it without the suburb being defined by it.

The school catchment picture adds family appeal. Seville Road State School within the suburb and proximity to Mount Gravatt State High School for secondary education creates a clear and practical pathway for families. For buyers comparing Holland Park to other suburbs in this price range, the catchment access alongside the suburb's quiet character and relative proximity to the CBD represents a combination that holds up under scrutiny.

If you own in Holland Park and are thinking about your options in the current market, I am happy to give you an honest and current assessment.
