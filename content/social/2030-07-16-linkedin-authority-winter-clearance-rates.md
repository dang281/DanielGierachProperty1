## Caption

A clearance rate figure without context is almost meaningless. This is especially true in winter, when auction volumes drop and the same percentage can tell a completely different story.

Take a hypothetical: a 62% clearance rate in July across 80 Brisbane auctions, versus a 62% clearance rate in October across 200 auctions. The ratio is identical. The market signal is not.

In October, 200 auctions means you are seeing the full range of stock. Well-priced properties, optimistically priced properties, unique properties with a narrow buyer pool, and standard stock with broad appeal. A 62% clearance rate across that spread means approximately a third of vendors misjudged the market or the timing.

In July, 80 auctions means the vendors who came to market made a deliberate decision to go in winter. Agents who advised auction in July are typically working with properties where buyer competition is already established. The clearance rate on that self-selected cohort tells you something specific: correctly priced properties with real buyer interest are selling. The cleared properties in winter are not carrying the dead weight of speculative listings.

This is why clearance rate headlines in July deserve more scrutiny than they receive. A number that sounds modest on volume can reflect a very healthy transacting market underneath.

When you are assessing market conditions, the question is not just what the clearance rate is. It is what it is measuring, and how many properties are being measured.
