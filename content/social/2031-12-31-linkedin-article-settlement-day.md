## Caption

Settlement day is often the most stressful day of the entire property transaction for both buyer and seller. Most of the stress comes from not knowing what to expect. The sequence is predictable, the steps are fixed, and understanding them in advance makes the day significantly smoother.

Here is the part most buyers and sellers do not know: settlement does not happen the moment the parties are ready. It happens when three separate parties, the buyer's lender, the seller's lender or solicitor, and the title office, have all confirmed their steps are complete and funds have moved. That coordination takes time, and on a busy settlement day, the time can extend into the afternoon even for a settlement scheduled for the morning.

The practical consequence is that key handover usually does not happen at 9am on settlement day, even if that is technically when settlement is supposed to occur. Buyers and sellers who understand this are prepared. Buyers and sellers who expect to walk into their new home at 9am sharp are often not prepared and the resulting calls to agents and solicitors asking why nothing has happened are among the most stressful of the whole transaction.

For buyers: plan not to have the removalists arriving at 9am. Plan for mid-afternoon. Have a fallback if it runs very late. For sellers: do not book a one-way flight for 10am on settlement day.
