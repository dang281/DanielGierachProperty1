## Caption

Pricing strategy is one of the most consequential decisions a seller makes, and it is the one where agent opinion and seller instinct most frequently diverge.

Pricing too high filters out the buyers most likely to compete. Pricing too low in a thin market means you need the competition to materialise, and if it doesn't, you've anchored at the wrong number. The right strategy depends on the property, the buyer pool, and the conditions at the time of launch.

In the inner east, where well-presented properties in the right suburbs attract genuine competition, the underpricing to create a bidding war strategy can work well at auction. On private treaty, it requires careful execution to avoid leaving money on the table.

The most consistently effective approach I see in practice: price at or just below the midpoint of genuine comparable sales, with a campaign designed to create competition rather than to settle.

When selling your home, which pricing approach do you think gets the best result?
