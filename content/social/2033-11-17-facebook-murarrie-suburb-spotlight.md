## Caption

Murarrie holds a specific position in the inner east that a growing number of buyers are starting to understand. It is the suburb in this part of Brisbane where detached home ownership is still accessible at a price point that has largely disappeared from the suburbs sitting closer to the river.

Block sizes in Murarrie run larger than what comparable budgets can secure in Cannon Hill, Morningside, or Norman Park. ⚠️ VERIFY. For buyers who want a house with a usable yard and room to breathe, that land dimension matters, and it becomes more apparent when they compare the alternatives at the same price.

The Gateway Motorway connection makes Murarrie practical for residents who work in areas that are not CBD-centric, and the Morningside and Cannon Hill amenity strips are within a few minutes in either direction. Murarrie does not yet have the commercial depth of its neighbours, but the daily convenience question is already answered by what sits immediately adjacent.

The suburb is transitioning from a mixed light industrial and residential character toward a more firmly residential one. First-time buyers priced out of the inner east suburbs they originally targeted, and investors looking for the next leg of the inner east story, both find Murarrie worth examining carefully.

For homeowners in Murarrie who are considering whether now is the right time to sell, the buyer interest in this suburb is worth understanding before making that decision.
