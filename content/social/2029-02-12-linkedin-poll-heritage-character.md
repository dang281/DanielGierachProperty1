## Caption

Brisbane's inner east has a high concentration of pre-war and post-war character homes, many of them under a heritage or character overlay that restricts what owners can change externally. These overlays affect what you can renovate, what you can demolish, and in some cases what colours you can paint the exterior.

For buyers, character overlays are sometimes seen as a constraint. But in practice, they are also part of what preserves the streetscape quality that makes suburbs like Hawthorne, Norman Park, and Balmoral worth living in.

Does a heritage or character overlay add value, reduce it, or make no difference to what you would pay?
