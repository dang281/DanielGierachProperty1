## Caption

East Brisbane is approximately three kilometres from the CBD, and that proximity shapes everything about the suburb's property market. Professional buyers, particularly those who value walkability to work and the inner-city lifestyle above land content, make up a dominant share of demand. The result is one of the highest entry price points in the inner east and one of the most active buyer pools per listing.

The housing mix is genuinely varied: terrace houses and narrow-frontage Queenslanders sit alongside post-war homes and modern builds. That variety means different buyer types are competing across different product categories simultaneously, which keeps the market liquid. Balmoral State School catchment brings families into the mix alongside the professional buyer base, and the school's reputation adds a layer of demand that pure walkability-focused buyers also benefit from.

Mowbray Terrace, Stanley Street, and the streets that track toward the river access points are where competition concentrates most sharply. Homes with outdoor living space, good natural light, and proximity to the Eleanor Schonell Bridge corridor tend to attract the strongest offers. The suburb's walkability score, access to Woolloongabba and Kangaroo Point, and the café density along Stanley Street are reference points buyers return to repeatedly.

East Brisbane ownership is a position most sellers leave with strong results. If you are thinking about a sale, start with a current appraisal.
