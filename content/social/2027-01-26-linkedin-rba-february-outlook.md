## Caption

The RBA's first meeting of 2027 is scheduled for February. Whatever the board decides, it will shape the borrowing capacity conversation for buyers who are active in the market right now.

Here is the practical framing that matters for inner east buyers.

If the cash rate holds, the current borrowing capacity calculations stay in place. Buyers who have a pre-approval from the past three months are working from an accurate number. No adjustment needed.

If the RBA cuts, which a number of economists are forecasting, the effect on a typical inner east purchase is real but not dramatic. On an $800,000 loan, a 25 basis point cut reduces monthly repayments by roughly $100 to $120. More meaningfully, it expands the maximum borrowing capacity of buyers who are currently sitting just below the threshold for a property they want. That brings more buyers into competition on properties in the $1M to $1.5M range.

The buyers who benefit most from a rate cut are not those already comfortably pre-approved. They are the buyers on the margin, the ones who could not quite make the numbers work at the current rate.

Worth knowing: the RBA's meeting schedule and any decisions are published on rba.gov.au. Before acting on any rate forecast you read in the press, check the primary source.

For buyers considering a February or March purchase in the inner east, now is the time to review your pre-approval and confirm it reflects what the bank will actually lend.
