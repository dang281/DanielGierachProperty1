## Caption

Seven Hills at the tail of the autumn selling season still has something to offer vendors who have not yet acted. The peak campaign window may be narrowing, but the buyer profile for this suburb is different from the broad inner east market, and that difference works in a vendor's favour even in the later weeks of autumn.

Buyers who are specifically targeting Seven Hills are looking for something they cannot find elsewhere in the inner east: larger land, a bush setting, and a connection to Whites Hill Reserve that gives the suburb a physical character no amount of urban landscaping can replicate. That specific demand does not disappear at Easter. It is there year-round, because the supply of property that meets those criteria is genuinely constrained. ⚠️ VERIFY

The acreage and near-acreage properties along the ridge lines and valley slopes here are a category of their own. They attract buyers who have made a deliberate decision that land content and setting matter more than walkability or proximity to retail. That buyer is patient but decisive. When the right property appears, they act.

For Seven Hills vendors considering whether the window has passed, the honest answer is: for this suburb's specific buyer profile, the window is longer than the broader inner east calendar suggests. The decision to sell should follow the property's readiness, not the season's headline.
