## Caption

Most property investors review their negative gearing position once a year, when the accountant asks for the figures. That is too late to make the decisions that actually matter.

The timing of settlement relative to 30 June is one of the most consequential choices an investor can make when selling, and it is almost never discussed until after the campaign is already underway. A settlement that falls before the end of the financial year means the capital gain is assessed in the current year's return. A settlement a few days later shifts it entirely into the next. Depending on the investor's income in each of those years, the difference can be significant, and not always in the direction people assume.

I have spoken with vendors who were genuinely surprised to learn this after their accountant reviewed the timing post-settlement. The conversation they needed to have happened months earlier, before they listed, not after contracts were signed.

Negative gearing strategy for inner east Brisbane investment properties is something I factor into the selling advice I give from the first conversation. When we talk about campaign timing, the financial year calendar is part of that discussion. If you have an investment property in the inner east and you are considering whether to sell before or after 30 June, the link below is a good starting point. The accountant conversation should happen before the agent conversation, and this article explains why.
