## Caption

Three observations from working the inner east market through October.

First, open home attendance is above what I typically see in September. Buyers who were watching the market in winter have moved into an active search phase, and a meaningful number of them have finance pre-approved and are ready to act on the right property.

Second, the gap between what sellers want and what buyers are willing to pay has narrowed compared to the first half of the year. That narrowing usually happens as spring progresses and vendors see transaction evidence from comparable sales in their suburb. Pricing conversations are more grounded than they were in April.

Third, the buyer profile in certain suburbs has shifted. Norman Park and Camp Hill are attracting more enquiry from buyers who have been priced out of Hawthorne and Balmoral. The ripple effect in the inner east is real and it shows up in open home attendance before it shows up in sale prices.

None of these observations come with precise data attached because the data lags the market by four to six weeks. What I am describing is what I see week to week at inspections.

The relevant question for a vendor considering a spring campaign is not what the market did last quarter. It is what the buyer pool looks like right now for your specific property type in your specific suburb.
