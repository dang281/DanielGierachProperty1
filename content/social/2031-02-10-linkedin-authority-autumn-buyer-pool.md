## Caption

The buyers who are active in the February and March window in Brisbane's inner east are not impulse purchasers. Most of them have been in the market for months before they appear at an autumn open home. They know the streets, they have seen the comparable sales, and they have a clear sense of what they need and what they are prepared to pay.

The life-stage profile of autumn buyers in the inner east is fairly consistent. Families who want to be settled in a new home before the school year builds momentum are a significant part of the pool, particularly for properties in established school catchments in Coorparoo, Bulimba, and Camp Hill. Couples upsizing from inner city apartments or townhouses, who have delayed the move until the calendar year feels settled, tend to arrive in February with finance in order and a real sense of urgency. Downsizers who have made the decision over Christmas and are now ready to act are also a feature of the February market.

What unites these buyers is that they are not testing the market. They have done that already. They come to autumn open homes with a purpose that is different from the spring inspection season, where a larger share of attendees are still forming their view of what they want.

For vendors, this is meaningful. Listing in February or March means your property is being seen by buyers who are ready to act, not buyers who are still deciding whether they want to buy at all. That concentrated motivation is part of what makes autumn one of the more reliable windows in the Brisbane selling calendar.
