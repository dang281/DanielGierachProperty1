## Caption

The conventional wisdom says spring is the time to sell. What it doesn't tell you is which part of spring. November has more buyer activity, but it also has more competing listings. By the time the market peaks in October and November, you're one of many properties asking for attention from a buyer pool that has plenty of options.

September is different. The buyers who have been watching since July are ready. They've done their research, they've attended opens throughout winter, and they've formed a clear picture of what they want and what it's worth. They're not warming up, they're deciding. At the same time, the wave of listings that will eventually crowd the market hasn't arrived yet. Supply is still catching up to demand.

The sequence that consistently produces good results in Brisbane's inner east is: preparation in August, launch in September, contract in the first two to three weeks of the campaign. Vendors who rush to be in market by late October are catching the tail of the window, not the front of it.

What I observe year after year is that the properties which do best in spring are the ones that were ready before the competition arrived, not the ones that scrambled to get on market when it was already crowded. If you started your preparation conversations in August, you're positioned exactly where you want to be right now.
