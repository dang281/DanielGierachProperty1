## Caption

March is one of Mt Gravatt's strongest months for property activity. The suburb's buyer pool is active, the comparable sales from February have started to set the market benchmarks for the season, and the families who want to be in before the school year's mid-point are focused on finding and securing property.

The Gateway Motorway access that defines Mt Gravatt's location advantage does not diminish in autumn. For buyers who work outside the inner east corridor, or who need flexibility for north-south movement, the suburb's position remains one of the better value infrastructure stories in Brisbane's south-east. ⚠️ VERIFY

The housing stock on the residential streets off the main commercial strip has benefited from sustained renovation activity. The brick homes from the 1970s and 80s that make up the bulk of Mt Gravatt's mid-century residential character have proven adaptable to modern living. Open-plan conversions, extended rear additions, and updated facades have steadily raised the suburb's presentation standard in a way that has supported price growth.

School catchment research is important for vendors in Mt Gravatt. The suburb intersects multiple catchment zones and buyers arriving from outside Brisbane's south-east often ask specifically about which school applies to which street. Having accurate catchment information available at the first open home is a basic requirement for a well-run campaign.
