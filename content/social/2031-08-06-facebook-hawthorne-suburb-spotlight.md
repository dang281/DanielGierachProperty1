## Caption

Hawthorne in August sits at exactly the right point in the selling calendar. The days are lengthening, buyer confidence is building, and spring listings have not yet flooded the market. For vendors who are thinking about presenting their property at its best, this window before the peak is worth taking seriously.

The suburb has a lot to work with. The Hawthorne Ferry terminal connects residents to the city without touching a car. Oxford Street at the Hawthorne end provides the lifestyle strip that buyers at this price point expect. Lores Bonney Park along the river gives residents access to green space and water views that are genuinely unusual this close to the CBD. And Hawthorne State School catchment supports family demand at the primary level.

The mix of character homes and newer builds means the buyer pool is broad. Character buyers will compete for original Queenslanders on good blocks, while those who prefer lower maintenance are served by the newer stock. That breadth keeps the market active across different buyer demographics simultaneously.

What Hawthorne rarely experiences is a quiet spell. The suburb's position between the ferry, Oxford Street, and the river ensures it remains on buyer shortlists throughout the year. In August, those buyers are moving from intention to action, and stock is still limited.
