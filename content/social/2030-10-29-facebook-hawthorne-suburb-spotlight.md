## Caption

Hawthorne delivers what a particular type of inner east buyer has decided they will not compromise on: Oxford Street access, river proximity, character housing on full blocks, and a neighbourhood identity that resists the blandness of density.

The streets closest to the river, particularly in the area between Hawthorne Road and the water, are among the most consistently competed-for in the inner east. The properties with direct river frontage or river views from elevated positions attract buyers who have typically been searching the inner east for six to eighteen months before finding what fits their brief.

For families, Hawthorne State School is a sought-after primary school. The suburb's proximity to the Churchie and Lourdes Hill College precincts makes it a natural choice for families with private secondary education in mind.

The Oxford Street dining strip, shared functionally with Bulimba, gives Hawthorne homeowners a weekday and weekend lifestyle that most Brisbane suburbs cannot match. Saturday morning coffee, river walking tracks, and a short ferry ride to the CBD or New Farm: that combination is genuinely rare and buyers pay for it consistently.

Days on market for well-positioned Hawthorne homes trend shorter than the inner east average. The buyer pool at the top end is experienced and decisive.
