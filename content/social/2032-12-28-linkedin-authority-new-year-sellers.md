## Caption

The conversations I have in late December are different from any other time of year. Vendors are not usually in a hurry. They are thinking. Deciding whether 2033 is the year, what preparation looks like, whether autumn is the right window or whether they move sooner.

The inner east autumn market (late February through May) has historically been the most consistent window for vendors in suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, and Camp Hill. Buyer numbers are strong, the weather supports inspections, and competition at campaign level tends to reflect genuine demand rather than seasonal noise.

But the vendors who do best in that window are not the ones who list in March. They are the ones who used January and February to prepare the property, choose the right method of sale, and understand the buyer pool they are selling into. Preparation is the variable a vendor controls. The market is not.

If you own in the inner east and you are in the thinking stage right now, the most useful conversation you can have is an honest one about what your property would achieve and what it would take to get there. That is what an appraisal is for. There is no obligation that follows.
