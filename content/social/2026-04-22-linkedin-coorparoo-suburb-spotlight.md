## Caption

Coorparoo sits 4km from Brisbane's CBD and has recorded annual house price growth of around 13 per cent, with unit values up approximately 11.5 per cent over the same period, according to current suburb data. The median house price sits around $1.64 million.

The relative value story is a genuine driver here. Buyers who have looked at Hawthorne and Bulimba at the $2M-plus price point often land in Coorparoo when they run the numbers. The suburb gives you the commute, the lifestyle amenity, and the character housing stock at a price point that serviceability can still support in the current rate environment.

That comparison dynamic creates a steady buyer pool for well-positioned Coorparoo homes. Particularly for houses. And particularly for properties in the character home category that give buyers something they cannot find in a new apartment.

The nuance for sellers right now is that Coorparoo's price growth has been stronger than many surrounding suburbs, which raises expectations. But the buyers who are looking have financing pre-arranged and are making decisions based on comparable sales, not aspirational asking prices. Accurate positioning matters.
