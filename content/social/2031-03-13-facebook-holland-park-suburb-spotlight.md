## Caption

Holland Park shares a postcode and a ridge with some of Brisbane's most consistently demanded suburbs, but its price point has not fully caught up. That differential is the story buyers are starting to pay attention to.

The suburb sits elevated above the surrounding area, with views available from the higher streets that put it in the same visual category as Mt Gravatt East and parts of Camp Hill. The housing stock is predominantly post-war character, with timber and brick homes on blocks that provide the kind of land content that inner east suburbs closer to the river no longer offer at comparable prices. Families who have done the open home circuit through Norman Park and Camp Hill and found themselves working at the top of their budget frequently discover that Holland Park provides the same school access, the same residential character, and the same quality of daily life, for a number that makes the purchase achievable.

The school catchment options, including access to Holland Park State School and the proximity to Cavendish Road State High School, give the suburb genuine credentials for the family buyer who is prioritising education infrastructure alongside liveability.

In March, buyers who have been circling the Norman Park and Camp Hill market for months and narrowing their decision arrive at Holland Park with clear intent. They have already made their comparison. They are ready to act.
