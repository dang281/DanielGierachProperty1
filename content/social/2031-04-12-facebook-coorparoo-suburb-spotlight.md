## Caption

Coorparoo has one of the most legible demand stories of any suburb in the inner east, and it comes down to a school catchment. The Coorparoo State School catchment drives buyer behaviour in this suburb with a consistency that is visible in sale prices and days on market. Families who need to be inside the boundary will pay to be there, and that dynamic operates independently of what interest rates or broader sentiment are doing in any given quarter.

Old Cleveland Road is the commercial spine that gives the suburb its daily texture. The bus connections to the CBD along that corridor are reliable and frequent, which matters to households with one car or no car. Coorparoo Square has filled out the retail offer at the upper end of the suburb, and the combination of walkable convenience and solid bus access is a practical story that resonates strongly with buyers who are weighing up suburbs across the south-east.

The housing mix in Coorparoo runs from post-war weatherboard to renovated character homes, and that spectrum means the suburb accommodates a wide budget range. A buyer entering Coorparoo at a more accessible price point is purchasing into the same school catchment as someone paying significantly more two streets away, and buyers understand that clearly.

For vendors currently considering timing, the post-Easter weeks in April have historically delivered motivated buyer activity that persists before the quieter mid-year period.
