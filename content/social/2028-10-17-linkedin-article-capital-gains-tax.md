## Caption

The main residence exemption from capital gains tax is not automatic. Most Brisbane owner-occupiers assume it applies to them without conditions, and for a straightforward case it does. But the exemption is proportional if the property was ever used to generate income.

If you rented a room through Airbnb, ran a business from a home office, or rented the whole property for a period before moving in or after moving out, a portion of any capital gain may be taxable. The calculation is based on the proportion of the ownership period during which the property was income-producing.

The six-year absence rule allows a property to retain its main residence exemption for up to six years after you vacate it, provided you have not nominated another property as your main residence during that time. It is a useful provision, but it has conditions that can catch vendors who assumed it applied when it did not.

For investment properties, settlement timing relative to 30 June determines which financial year the capital gain falls into. A settlement that crosses the financial year by a few days can shift a significant liability. That is a planning decision that needs to happen before the campaign launches, not during the negotiation.
