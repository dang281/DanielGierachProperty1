## Caption

February is when the genuine autumn buyers surface. Not browsers, not people who are "keeping an eye on things." The people who are attending open homes in February have usually made a decision about their year and are ready to act.

The profile of inner east buyers I am talking to in February 2030:

Families upgrading from a smaller home within the precinct or coming in from suburbs further out. They are motivated by school catchments, block size, and proximity. They have usually been looking for six months or more and are approaching the point where they will pay close to their limit for the right property.

Downsizers who are freeing up larger homes in suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Norman Park. They typically have no finance condition and are ready to act if the right property appears. They are among the most motivated buyers in the market.

Investors who are watching rental returns in the inner east with attention. Vacancy rates in this precinct have been low consistently, and that has not gone unnoticed by buyers who are comparing returns across Brisbane.

What these buyers have in common: they are ready, they have done their research, and they are not waiting for the market to shift. They are waiting for the right property.

February is the best time to read buyer appetite before committing to a March launch. The open home data and private inspection requests in February tell me more about what March will look like than any other indicator.

If you are planning a March listing, the time to talk about it is now.
