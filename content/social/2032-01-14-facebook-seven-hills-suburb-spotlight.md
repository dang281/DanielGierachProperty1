## Caption

Seven Hills is a suburb that rewards local knowledge. It sits between Morningside and Camp Hill, bordered by Wynnum Road to the north and the escarpment that drops toward Tingalpa Creek. The streets here are quiet, established, and predominantly owner-occupier. It does not have the profile of some of its neighbours, which is part of the appeal for buyers who have done their research.

The blocks tend to be larger than in the more commercialised parts of the inner east, and there are genuinely elevated positions along the ridge that run through the suburb's western section. City views from the higher streets are possible on larger blocks with the right orientation. Buyers who want space, privacy, and inner east access without inner east pricing look at Seven Hills seriously.

The housing stock is a mix of lowset brick, post-war character homes, and a smaller number of Queenslanders. Renovation activity has been consistent. The lack of through-traffic is a quiet feature that residents appreciate.

For vendors, Seven Hills benefits from buyers who have been looking broadly across the inner east and identified it as undervalued relative to comparable lifestyle and access. The buyer pool here tends to be considered and financially prepared.

In 2032, Seven Hills appeals to buyers who want to be in the inner east before values in higher-profile suburbs move further ahead of it.
