## Caption

Camp Hill's owner-occupier base is one of the most entrenched in the inner east. When residents do sell, it's usually because they're moving within the suburb or relocating for reasons that have nothing to do with the market. That pattern keeps supply tight in a way that structural demand alone doesn't explain.

The Whites Hill State College catchment is often the opening point of conversation with buyers. For families who've been living in Brisbane and researching carefully, it's not unusual to be told that Camp Hill is the specific target, full stop, no alternatives being considered. That level of conviction in the buyer pool is rare, and it consistently supports price outcomes.

Stones Corner on the southern edge has matured into a genuinely good food and coffee destination. The proximity to Stones Corner adds daily lifestyle value that buyers price into their decisions. A home that's walkable to the strip sits differently in buyers' minds than one that requires a drive.

Late November in Camp Hill is a good window. Buyers who want to be in before Christmas, or at least have exchanged, are making their last runs through properties. Competition is real, decisions are faster, and vendors who've prepared properly see the benefit.

The homes that command the premium are large-block Queenslanders in original or sympathetically renovated condition. Modern infill still sells but at a clear discount to the character premium.

Camp Hill median prices are consistently at the top of the inner east table. ⚠️ VERIFY current median and days on market before posting.
