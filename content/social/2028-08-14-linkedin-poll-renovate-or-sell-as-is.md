## Caption

The renovation-before-selling question comes up in almost every pre-sale appraisal conversation I have. The answer is rarely straightforward.

The evidence is reasonably consistent on full renovations: a complete kitchen or bathroom overhaul rarely earns back its full cost at sale. The buyer pays for the property's location, land size, and condition, and applies their own taste filter on top. A $50,000 kitchen renovation that the seller loves and the buyer would redo in a different style is not $50,000 of added value.

Fixing genuine defects is a different calculation. A building and pest report that flags active termite activity or structural issues will cost the seller far more than the repair bill if it stays in the report the buyer's building inspector produces mid-campaign.

Where it gets genuinely complicated is the middle category: the tired but functional kitchen, the dated bathroom that still works, the property that presents as a decade behind the market. That is where the answer depends on the specific property, the specific buyer pool, and what the comparable sales are telling you.

If you were selling your home, what would you do?
