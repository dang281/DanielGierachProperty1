## Caption

Almost all the "best time to sell" advice you'll read online is built around data from Melbourne and Sydney. The seasonal patterns in those cities, the peak listing periods, the buyer behaviour through spring and autumn, none of it maps cleanly onto Brisbane's inner east.

Here's what I've seen consistently in this specific market. Autumn, running from late February through to May, tends to be the most reliable selling window for inner east vendors. Not because spring is bad, but because the autumn buyer pool in suburbs like Hawthorne, Bulimba, Norman Park, and Camp Hill is typically more decisive and less distracted by summer activity, school holiday planning, and the general noise of the September to November period.

Spring brings volume. Autumn tends to bring quality. For vendors with a genuinely good property and enough preparation time, the autumn window has historically produced results that are hard to match in spring.

That said, the answer is never universal. Some properties suit a spring campaign. Some vendors don't have until February. Timing decisions are always a combination of the property, the price range, the buyer it's likely to attract, and when that buyer is most active.

I've written about this in detail, looking at the specific seasonal pattern for Brisbane's inner east rather than the national averages. If you're thinking about when to sell, it's worth reading before you set a campaign date.
