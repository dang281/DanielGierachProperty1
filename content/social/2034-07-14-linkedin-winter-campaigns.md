## Caption

Not every inner east property is suited to a spring campaign. A few observations from running campaigns across both seasons.

Character homes in Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Norman Park photograph better in winter light. The sharp Brisbane winter sun and cleaner skies produce images that look distinctly different from the flat haze of a November shoot. Buyers notice, even if they cannot explain why.

Suburbs with strong school catchments attract buyers who have already committed to a school, which means they are buying to a deadline. That deadline does not shift because it is July. Prep school parents looking in Camp Hill or Carina are in the market when they are in the market.

Properties where the character of the home is the selling point tend to hold their appeal year-round. A Queenslander with a wide veranda and a north-facing garden is a Queenslander regardless of the month. Buyers who want that are not waiting for more of them to appear in October.

The properties that struggle in winter are the ones that rely on outdoor entertaining, pools, and garden presentation. Those are legitimate reasons to consider timing. But they are not reasons that apply to most of the inner east stock.

If you are weighing up winter versus spring, the honest answer depends on your specific property. danielgierach.com.
