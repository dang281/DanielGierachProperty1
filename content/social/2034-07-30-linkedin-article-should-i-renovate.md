## Caption

Should you renovate before selling? It is one of the most common questions I get from sellers, and the honest answer is: it depends entirely on what you are renovating and why.

There is a useful distinction to make here. Fixing defects protects the value you already have. A leaking roof, visible damp, a broken pool pump: fixing these is not adding value, it is removing reasons for a buyer to renegotiate the price after the building and pest report comes back.

Adding value through renovation is a different calculation. A full kitchen renovation at $40,000 to $60,000 almost never earns back its full cost at sale in the inner east. Buyers want the credit for doing it themselves. They want to choose the finishes. A renovated kitchen imposes your taste; an older kitchen leaves room for theirs.

The renovations that tend to return the most: decluttering and deep cleaning, external paint if the facade is tired, garden tidying and lawn care, fresh neutral interior paint where walls are marked or dated.

These are preparation costs, not renovation costs. And preparation almost always pays for itself.
