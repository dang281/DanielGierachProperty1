## Caption

At roughly three kilometres from the CBD, East Brisbane sits closer to the city than most Brisbane suburbs that still offer genuine character housing stock. Terrace houses, Queenslanders, and modern builds share streets that are within walking distance of the river, and the Balmoral State School catchment anchors the family buyer demand that keeps this suburb highly competitive.

The buyer profile here skews toward professionals and families who want CBD proximity without the apartment trade-offs that come with living closer in. Walkability is a genuine feature of East Brisbane, not a marketing line. Buyers can and do walk to South Bank, the CBD, and the riverfront regularly, and that changes how they use and experience the suburb compared with driving-dependent alternatives further east.

Well-presented character homes and terrace houses attract the strongest buyer competition. East Brisbane has one of the higher entry price points in the inner east, which means buyers are serious and financially capable when they arrive at inspection. They are comparing this suburb directly against New Farm and Kangaroo Point, and many choose East Brisbane precisely because the character housing stock offers better value against those alternatives.

Supply here is structurally limited by the suburb's small geographic footprint, which keeps the balance of power firmly with vendors who present their properties well.

If you own in East Brisbane and are considering your options, start with a clear picture of what the market is offering right now.
