## Caption

The first quarter of 2031 in Brisbane's inner east was a solid autumn window by most measures. Buyer demand in the established house market was consistent from February onward, open home attendance was healthy at well-presented properties, and the auction clearance rate through March reflected a buyer pool that arrived with decisions already made.

What characterised the quarter was the gap between prepared and underprepared campaigns. Properties that launched with strong photography, current pricing based on comparable sales evidence, and a clear sale method from the start converted buyer interest into offers efficiently. Properties that launched with pricing ahead of the evidence sat longer, required price corrections, or passed in at auction at figures below what an earlier, better-grounded launch might have achieved.

Days on market in the inner east tracked broadly in line with recent autumn windows for properties in the sub-$2 million range. Above that price point, the buyer pool was thinner and campaigns were longer, which is consistent with what we saw in the same period in previous years. Competition at auction was most pronounced in the $1.2 million to $1.7 million range, where family buyers and upsizers were competing for a limited supply of detached properties in school catchment suburbs.

The post-Easter period will be quieter, as it always is. The winter market will have its own character. But the first quarter gave inner east vendors who were prepared a genuine opportunity, and most of those who used it well walked away with results that reflected what the market was capable of supporting.
