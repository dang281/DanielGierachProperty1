## Caption

August is a month that rewards vendors who made a deliberate decision and penalises those who drifted into it without a plan.

The vendors who do well in August are the ones who chose the timing intentionally. They looked at their property, considered the buyer pool that would be most attracted to it, prepared properly, and launched when they were ready rather than when the calendar told them to. Their campaigns tend to run cleanly because the groundwork was done in advance.

The vendors who struggle in August are often the ones who listed because they needed to rather than because they were ready. The presentation was not quite finished, the pricing was set from an optimistic position rather than from what comparable sales were showing, or the campaign launched too quickly without proper preparation. These campaigns drag. They attract the kind of enquiry that requires too much convincing.

There is still time to make August work properly if you have been considering a mid-year sale. The preparation phase for an inner east campaign typically takes four to six weeks when done well. That means the conversations worth having are happening now, not in August when the campaign is already live.

If you have a property in the inner east and you want a straight assessment of whether the timing, the pricing, and the preparation are aligned, I am happy to give you that conversation.
