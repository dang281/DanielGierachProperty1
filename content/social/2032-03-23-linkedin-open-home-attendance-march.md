## Caption

Open home attendance in late March is one of the most useful leading indicators I have. By the time CoreLogic reports a median price movement, the conditions that drove it are already six to eight weeks old. What I observe at open homes in real time tells me a great deal more about where April and May are heading.

This March, a few things stand out. Registration numbers at well-presented properties in the inner east have been consistently above the seasonal average. The proportion of buyers attending a second inspection, which is a more meaningful indicator than raw attendance, is also elevated. Second inspections mean buyers are serious, not just curious.

The other signal I watch is what happens when two or three buyers are competing on the same property. This autumn, that overlap is happening more frequently than in the same period last year. Multiple registered bidders, or a situation where a vendor receives competing offers before auction, tends to indicate that April results will be competitive.

What is also clear is that this pattern is not uniform across all property types. Detached houses on good blocks in established streets are drawing the most concentrated interest. Townhouse stock in certain corridors is slower, not because demand is absent but because supply has also increased and buyers are more selective.

The data from March open homes is a map for what April will look like. Right now, that map suggests conditions will continue to favour well-prepared vendors.
