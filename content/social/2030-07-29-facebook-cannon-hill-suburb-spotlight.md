## Caption

Cannon Hill Esplanade gives this suburb something most of Brisbane's inner east cannot offer: genuine riverside access at street level. For buyers who want to walk to the river on a Sunday morning or run along the water in the evening, Cannon Hill's esplanade is a real asset, and it shows up in the premium that well-positioned homes near the water consistently attract.

The suburb's housing mix covers Queenslanders, post-war homes, and newer builds, with an active development pipeline that reflects the underlying confidence in the area's long-term trajectory. The café culture around the Cannon Hill shopping precinct has matured considerably, and that lifestyle infrastructure matters to the owner-occupier families who form the core of the buyer pool. Access to both the CBD and the Gateway Motorway from Cannon Hill is genuinely practical, which broadens appeal to buyers with different work locations and commute priorities.

Long-term capital growth in Cannon Hill has been consistent, underpinned by that combination of river access, lifestyle amenity, and transport infrastructure. The suburb tends to attract buyers who have decided this is where they want to live for the medium to long term, which means campaigns typically involve motivated, committed purchasers rather than casual lookers.

If you own in Cannon Hill and are considering whether now is the right moment to act, a current appraisal will give you a clear and honest picture.
