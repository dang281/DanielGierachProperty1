## Caption

East Brisbane's appeal to buyers is deeply connected to its relationship with the city. Three kilometres from the CBD, residents are close enough to walk or cycle to South Bank, the Mater Hospital precinct and the cultural institutions along the river. That proximity changes how people live day-to-day, reducing car dependency and creating a lifestyle that is genuinely walkable by Brisbane standards.

The suburb's character housing stock is central to its identity. Heritage Queenslanders and worker's cottages on compact lots define the streetscape in the older sections, while some pockets have seen more recent development add a different housing type to the mix. Buyers pursuing East Brisbane are typically willing to pay for the suburb's location and character; they are not bargain-hunting. The result is a market that remains resilient and where quality homes attract competition even when broader conditions soften.

Young professionals and couples are the core buyer demographic, though families with children at nearby schools and medical workers from the Mater precinct also feature. Supply remains tight because the suburb is small, well-regarded and does not have significant development capacity to add meaningful stock. Current medians in East Brisbane sit at the upper end of the inner city range, reflecting both the scarcity premium and the lifestyle value buyers place on the location.

There is no train line, but bus connections to the CBD are frequent and reliable.
