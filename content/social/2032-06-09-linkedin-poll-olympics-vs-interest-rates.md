## Caption

Every year there is something that dominates the property conversation. In 2032 there have been two things competing for that position: the Olympics and interest rates.

The Games have been on the horizon for years, and now they are weeks away. Every seller, every buyer, every investor in Brisbane has had some version of the "what does this mean for property?" conversation. At the same time, the rate environment has been doing its own thing, affecting borrowing capacity, monthly repayments and the confidence people feel about making large financial commitments.

Both are real factors. But people tend to be moved more by one than the other, and I think that split is more interesting than either factor on its own.

Which has had the bigger impact on how you are thinking about property right now?
