## Caption

Balmoral is a suburb where the street character does most of the selling before buyers step inside a property. The large block sizes, generous setbacks, and the timber and tin character housing stock along streets like Oberon Street and Teneriffe Drive give the suburb an architectural quality that is genuinely scarce in Brisbane's inner east at any price point.

The buyer who comes to Balmoral has usually already ruled out other options. They have been through Bulimba and Hawthorne and have a clear idea of what they are prepared to pay for a specific type of home in a specific type of street. That self-selection means open homes in Balmoral tend to be attended by fewer groups than comparable suburbs, but the registration quality and follow-through rate is consistently high.

The Balmoral State School catchment and the access to the inner east's independent school belt are standard parts of the buyer conversation here. The suburb's walkability to the Bulimba and Hawthorne riverfront precincts adds to its appeal for buyers who prioritise outdoor access.

March is a productive month to campaign in Balmoral. The autumn buyer pool is at its most motivated, and properties here that are correctly positioned in the market tend to find their buyer within a three to four week campaign cycle.

If you are thinking about selling in Balmoral this autumn, the window before Easter is the right time to launch.
