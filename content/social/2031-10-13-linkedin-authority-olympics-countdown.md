## Caption

With the Brisbane 2032 Olympics less than 12 months away, the global media attention on Brisbane is at a level the city has not seen before. That attention translates into something observable at the coalface: interstate buyers who have been watching Brisbane for years are now treating the Olympic timeline as a trigger to act.

The infrastructure conversation has largely moved on. Most of the major works around the inner east are built or under construction. That disruption phase is effectively behind us for most suburbs. What is in front of us is the exposure phase, where Brisbane's inner east gets the kind of consistent international visibility that typically takes a generation to earn.

What I am actually seeing at inspections: more buyers who have done serious research before they walk through the door. They know the suburb. They know the comparable sales. They are not here to look. They are here to decide.

For inner east vendors in Q4 2031, the genuine question is a personal one. Do you want to be settled and done before the Olympic year begins, with a clean transaction behind you? Or do you want to position your property as part of the first major Olympic year market, with the attention that brings?

Neither answer is wrong. It depends entirely on where you are going next and what your timeline looks like. The Olympics is a genuine factor in the conversation. It is not, on its own, a reason to do something that does not otherwise make sense for your situation.
