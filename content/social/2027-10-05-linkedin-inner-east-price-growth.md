## Caption

By October, the spring campaign season has produced enough results to read the trend clearly. The data is no longer preliminary.

In Brisbane's inner east, the properties that have performed consistently this year share two characteristics: meaningful land content and alignment with school catchment boundaries. These are the fundamentals that underpin demand regardless of where the rate cycle sits. They were true in a rising rate environment and they remain true now.

Properties that have sat for more than four weeks have, in almost every case, had one of two problems. The first is price expectations set above the comparable range. Not dramatically above it, but far enough that buyers who have done their research walk away without making an offer. The second is a presentation issue, usually something that registers in the first ninety seconds of a walk-through and puts buyers off before they engage meaningfully with the rest of the property.

The gap between a well-prepared, correctly priced property and an equivalent property with neither of those things is not a marketing outcome. It is a process outcome. It is the result of decisions made in the weeks before the campaign launched, not during the campaign itself.

The biggest driver of above-median results in the inner east this year has been preparation quality and realistic vendor price expectations. Properties that arrived at market in good condition, priced within a defensible range, cleared quickly and without drama. The market has been more than willing to reward them.

That combination is still achievable for vendors considering listing before the spring window closes. danielgierach.com
