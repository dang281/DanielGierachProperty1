## Caption

Seven Hills sits on an elevated ridge about five kilometres from the CBD, and the position pays dividends in views that stretch toward the city skyline and breezes that make Brisbane's warmer months noticeably more comfortable. Buyers who know the suburb understand that the ridge position is not incidental; it is one of the primary reasons homes here hold their value and attract serious interest when they come to market.

The housing stock is dominated by character homes: Queenslanders and post-war homes that reflect the suburb's history of quiet residential development on the city fringe. Many have been renovated thoughtfully, and the suburb has developed a reputation for well-maintained streetscapes and a community character that attracts owner-occupiers rather than speculative buyers. Whites Hill State College is the key school catchment, and it draws families to the area who plan to stay through their children's secondary schooling years.

Demand in Seven Hills tends to be strong and supply limited, a combination that has supported pricing through different market conditions. Current medians reflect the suburb's premium attributes, with elevation and character both factored into what buyers are willing to pay. The suburb has a quieter profile than some of its neighbours, which suits buyers who want proximity to the city without the activity that comes with it.

Road access to the CBD is direct, and bus services supplement that connection.
