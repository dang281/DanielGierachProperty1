## Caption

The streets that climb above Old Cleveland Road in Camp Hill do not need to advertise themselves. The buyers who want them already know.

Camp Hill's elevation is its most tangible asset. The ridge streets, particularly around Boundary Road and Brereton Street, sit above the surrounding suburbs with views that reward on clear mornings. The character housing stock on those streets, Queenslanders and pre-war timber homes with wide verandahs, has been held tightly, with many owners staying for decades before eventually listing. When those properties come to market in autumn, they attract buyers who have often been waiting.

The Whites Hill State College catchment overlap is a significant driver for families. The school draws enrolments from Camp Hill and Norman Park, and the catchment boundary has historically added a measurable premium to properties that fall inside it. The Old Cleveland Road strip, running through the suburb's commercial spine, provides the day-to-day infrastructure: cafes, independent retailers, the kind of street that makes residents walk rather than drive.

Camp Hill shares a boundary with Norman Park to the east and Coorparoo to the west, which means buyers priced out of those suburbs frequently look here. As the autumn market opens properly through February, that demand pressure builds quickly.

Vendors in Camp Hill who prepare now and list before Easter are typically in the strongest position of the year.
