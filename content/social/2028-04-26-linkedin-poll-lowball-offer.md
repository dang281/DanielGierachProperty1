## Caption

In Brisbane's inner east, offers 8 to 10 per cent below asking price sit at the low end of what most vendors will genuinely consider. Drop further than that and you are typically not starting a negotiation, you are ending one. Vendors and their agents read lowball offers as a signal about how a buyer values the property, and that perception is hard to recover from. That said, plenty of buyers have made cheeky offers that worked out. And plenty have quietly regretted the ones that went nowhere. The experiences vary more than people expect. Have you ever made a lowball offer? What happened?
