## Caption

November buyers in Brisbane's inner east are operating under a deadline that most other buyers in the year aren't. They want to be settled before Christmas or they've decided to wait until February. There is very little middle ground.

That creates a genuine urgency window in the second and third weeks of November that I've seen play out consistently in this market. Buyers who have been attending opens since September know exactly what they want. They've refined their search, they've done their finance, and they've watched a number of properties sell without them. By mid-November, the question isn't whether to buy. It's whether the right property is available before time runs out.

Vendors who launch a well-prepared campaign in late October or the first week of November are putting their property in front of that buyer at the moment their motivation is highest. The same property listed in early December will face a different buyer pool, one that is more distracted, more likely to pause until January, and less willing to move quickly.

This isn't theory. It's what I observe running campaigns in Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, and Camp Hill in the lead-up to Christmas. The buyers at the inspections in the second week of November are not browsing. They are deciding.

If you're considering a November campaign and haven't started your preparation, the conversation about timing is the first one worth having.
