## Caption

Settlement timing relative to June 30 determines which financial year the capital gain falls into. Getting it wrong by a few days can shift significant tax liability.

This is the most time-sensitive piece of tax planning that arises in a property sale, and it is relevant right now as we sit in the final week of the financial year. A settlement on June 28 crystallises the capital gain in this financial year. A settlement on July 3 crystallises it in the next. If the gain is large, the difference in when you pay the tax, and potentially whether the income stacks with other earnings that affect your marginal rate, can be worth tens of thousands of dollars.

For investors selling a Brisbane inner east property with a significant capital gain, this is a conversation for their accountant, not their agent. But the agent needs to know it is a factor, because the settlement date in a contract is something that can be negotiated. A buyer who wants sixty-day settlement and a vendor who needs July settlement need to find common ground, and that conversation needs to happen at the offer stage, not at the contract signing.
