## Caption

The peak autumn selling window in Brisbane's inner east is shorter than most vendors expect. It runs from roughly mid-March to mid-April. That is four weeks of concentrated buyer activity before Easter disrupts open home patterns and the season starts to wind down.

A property listed in the first week of March, with four weeks of campaign ahead of it, will reach peak buyer exposure during that window. A property listed in the last week of March will spend most of its campaign competing for buyer attention that is already beginning to fragment around Easter plans.

This is not a scare tactic. It is a calendar reality that affects every autumn campaign in the inner east.

Sellers who have not yet listed in early March are not out of time, but they are running short of it. The preparation work that should have started in January needs to compress: building and pest reports, contract preparation, styling, photography, and the legal groundwork all need to happen simultaneously rather than sequentially.

What I see consistently is that vendors who rush this process to meet the calendar end up with a weaker campaign than vendors who either started earlier or made a deliberate decision to wait until spring. A compressed, underprepared campaign in a competitive suburb like Balmoral or Camp Hill is harder to recover from than most people expect.

If you are undecided and have been waiting to see how the autumn starts, the data is now in. The time to decide is this week.
