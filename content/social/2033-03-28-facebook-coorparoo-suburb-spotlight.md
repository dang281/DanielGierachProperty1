## Caption

Coorparoo in the final days of the autumn selling season is still producing solid results for vendors who have done the preparation work. The suburb's depth of demand does not evaporate in the week before Easter. What changes is that the pool is smaller, the remaining buyers are more decisive, and the properties that are still well-priced and well-presented find a buyer faster than the broader market context might suggest.

The Logan Road strip is at its best in autumn. The outdoor dining culture along this corridor thrives in the mild March and April weather, and buyers attending open homes on a Saturday morning in Coorparoo often spend time in the precinct before or after their inspection. That experience reinforces the suburb's lifestyle appeal in a way that is hard to replicate in a listing description. ⚠️ VERIFY

The federation homes in the streets closest to the Logan Road and Cavendish Road intersection have been the bellwether for the suburb's premium tier. Results in those streets set the expectation for what Coorparoo can achieve, and buyers in the broader suburb use those results as anchors when they are assessing the rest of the market.

For vendors who have properties on the market this week in Coorparoo: the buyers in the room right now are motivated, and they are running out of autumn season alongside you. That shared urgency is not a disadvantage for a vendor who is reasonably priced and well-presented.
