## Caption

By the time April arrives in Brisbane's inner east, the autumn listing wave has passed. Most of the properties that were going to come to market in February and March have already sold. The sellers who were thinking about it have mostly decided.

But the buyers are still there.

The pool of active buyers who have been through open homes for six to eight weeks without securing a property carries significant intent into April and May. These are not casual market watchers. They have finance arranged. They have been through enough properties to know what they want. They have adjusted their expectations against what the market is actually offering, not what they hoped it would offer in January.

For a property that comes to market now with good preparation and honest pricing, that pool is its audience. The competition is not between dozens of other listings. It is between one well-positioned property and the motivated buyers who have been waiting for it.

Autumn is widely understood as Brisbane's best selling window. What is less discussed: the early weeks after the main autumn wave can be a very effective time to list, precisely because the supply side has quietened and the demand side has not.

If you are weighing whether to list now or wait for spring, that is a decision worth talking through. danielgierach.com
