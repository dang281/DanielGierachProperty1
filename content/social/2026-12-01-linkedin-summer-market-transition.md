## Caption

The common assumption about December is that property buyers disappear. It is worth examining what actually happens, because the reality is more nuanced.

New listings dry up significantly in December. That much is true. The volume of properties hitting the market in the first two weeks of December is a fraction of what it was through October and November. Agencies wind down active campaigns, sellers pause plans until February, and the number of open homes drops sharply.

What does not disappear is the buyers who are still under offer, still attending campaigns already in progress, and still watching the market for something they missed in spring.

In Brisbane's inner east, December tends to benefit motivated sellers who are already on market. A vendor who listed in October and did not find the result they wanted has a smaller pool of competing listings through December. A new listing in the first two weeks of December, well-priced and well-presented, can attract genuine interest because the competition has temporarily stepped back.

The week between Christmas and New Year is genuinely quiet. The weeks leading up to December 20 are not.

If you are currently on market or considering a last-minute December campaign, the market is not closed. It is just smaller. Whether that works in your favour depends on how your property is positioned relative to what buyers in your suburb have been looking for.
