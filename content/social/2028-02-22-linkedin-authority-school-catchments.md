## Caption

School catchments in Brisbane's inner east create price differentials that can be significant, but they are not always easy to identify from a listing.

In the inner east, the catchments for schools including Bulimba State School, Coorparoo State School, Whites Hill State College, and Cavendish Road State High create concentrated demand in specific streets. A property on the right side of a catchment boundary can command a premium over an identical property on the other side. The gap depends on the school and how competitive the catchment has become.

What makes catchment pricing interesting from a seller's perspective is that it is not just about the current price. It is about who shows up to the open home. A property in a sought-after catchment draws a buyer group that includes families who have specifically identified that catchment as a requirement. That buyer group tends to be more motivated and less price-sensitive on the top end because they have already filtered out alternatives.

The practical implication: if your property is in a strong catchment, that needs to be in the marketing from day one, not mentioned as an aside. Buyers who are filtering by catchment will not find your property if it is not searchable by that feature.
