## Caption

On a $1.1 million owner-occupier purchase, transfer duty in Queensland adds approximately $43,350 to your upfront costs. Most buyers underestimate the actual number.

Transfer duty in Queensland uses a sliding scale. The rate increases as the purchase price rises, which means the duty on a $1.2 million home is not simply twelve times the duty on a $100,000 home.

Owner-occupiers who qualify for the home concession pay less than investors buying the same property. The difference on a $1 million purchase is approximately $7,000.

First home buyers purchasing a new home pay zero transfer duty with no price cap. For existing homes, a full concession applies up to $700,000 with a partial concession to $800,000.
