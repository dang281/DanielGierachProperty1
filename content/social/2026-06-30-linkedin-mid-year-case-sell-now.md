## Caption

Every year, sellers in Brisbane's inner east decide to wait for spring. Most of the time, it's not the wrong call. But it's worth understanding what you're actually waiting for.

The spring market brings more buyer activity. It also brings more competing listings. The properties that get the sharpest results in October are not necessarily the best homes. They're the homes that were priced well and marketed well in a market with genuine competition between buyers.

If you list in July and there are three comparable properties in your suburb, each buyer inquiry is more concentrated. Your open homes attract the buyers who are already in the market, already have finance, and are not casually browsing. The pool is smaller. The motivation is higher.

Spring brings more traffic. But traffic is not the same as offers.

The question worth asking: does your property benefit from being seen by 200 people at a well-attended open home, or from being seen by 40 people who are genuinely ready to buy? The answer depends on the property and the suburb.

In the inner east, I've seen both approaches work. What I rarely see work is a vendor who waits for spring, lists alongside 15 similar properties, and then gets frustrated that their home is sitting.

If you're thinking about selling in the next six months, the timing conversation is worth having now.
