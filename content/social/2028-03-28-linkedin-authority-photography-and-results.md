## Caption

The photography budget is the most consistently undervalued line item in a property campaign.

Most sellers think about photography as documentation: pictures of the rooms so buyers know what they are looking at. The best real estate photography does something different. It makes the property look like the best version of itself. Natural light captured properly, angles chosen to communicate space and flow, hero shots of the most compelling features presented in a way that stops the scroll on a listing portal.

The reason photography matters so much: the portal is where the first decision gets made. A buyer looking at 40 properties on realestate.com.au will open 10 of them and book inspections for 3. The difference between an open and a skip is almost always the lead photograph.

If your listing photography produces fewer inspection bookings, your open homes are smaller, there is less competition, and the final negotiation reflects it. If your photography produces strong early bookings, the open home momentum creates the conditions where the outcome is set early in the campaign.

Styling and photography are not cosmetic. They are infrastructure for the campaign.
