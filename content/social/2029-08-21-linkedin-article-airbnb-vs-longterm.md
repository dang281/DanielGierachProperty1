## Caption

The gross revenue from short-term rental looks attractive until you start subtracting the costs that rarely appear in the headline comparison.

Platform fees typically run 15 to 20 percent of revenue. Cleaning between each guest is a fixed cost that compounds quickly at high occupancy. Short-term rental insurance is substantially more expensive than landlord insurance on a standard tenancy. Ongoing maintenance is higher because the property is in constant use by rotating occupants who have no stake in its condition. Add vacancy, which even well-managed short-term properties carry, and the numbers look materially different.

The operational burden is the variable most investors underweight. A long-term tenancy largely runs itself once the right tenant is in place. Short-term rental is closer to running a small hospitality business. Pricing, availability, guest communication, turnover cleaning, restocking, and review management all require ongoing attention or a manager who charges for doing it on your behalf.

Net return on a well-managed inner east property in long-term tenancy often sits within a few thousand dollars of a well-run short-term rental once the full cost picture is accounted for. The question then becomes whether the additional complexity and operational involvement is worth it for that margin.

The article does the full comparison with realistic cost assumptions. Worth reading before committing to a strategy either way.
