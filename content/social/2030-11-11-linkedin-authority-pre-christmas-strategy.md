## Caption

A pre-Christmas property campaign can produce an excellent result. It can also be rushed into existence in a way that costs the vendor significantly.

The campaigns that work in November share a common structure. The preparation phase happened before October: the property was decluttered, touched up, styled or at minimum tidied. Professional photography captured the home at its best. The agent and vendor were aligned on pricing strategy before a single buyer walked through the door.

The campaigns that underperform were launched under the pressure of a deadline rather than readiness. Photos taken in two days because the vendor wanted to go live by the weekend. A price guide set at a figure the vendor hoped for rather than one supported by comparable sales. Buyers who attend and immediately sense the property has been hurried to market.

The pre-Christmas buyer is experienced. They have been attending opens for months. They can see the difference between a property that is ready and one that is not. A hesitant first open home is hard to recover from because the buyer who would have made the strongest offer often forms their view in the first 30 seconds of walking in.

If you are launching in the next two weeks, the question is not whether the property will sell. It is whether the campaign has been built to achieve the best result for your circumstances, or whether it has been built to meet a calendar deadline.
