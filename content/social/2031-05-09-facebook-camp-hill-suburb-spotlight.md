## Caption

The Old Cleveland Road commercial strip in Camp Hill is the kind of asset that a suburb does not generate artificially. It evolved because the residential population around it was dense enough, and committed enough, to sustain independent retail alongside the standard service businesses. The coffee shops, the bookshop, the specialty food retailers on that stretch are there because Camp Hill residents use them, and their presence reinforces the suburb's self-contained character.

The Whites Hill State College catchment is the structural engine beneath Camp Hill's consistent market performance. Secondary school enrolment is a decision families make years before they need to act on it, and buyers who have identified Whites Hill as their school of choice arrive at Camp Hill with a timeline and a purpose. They are not casually browsing. They have usually mapped the catchment boundary, identified the streets they prefer within it, and have a budget range they are prepared to act within.

The ridge streets above Old Cleveland Road deserve specific attention. Norman Park buyers who are drawn to the catchment and find that Camp Hill's ridge streets deliver comparable views and character architecture often make the substitution willingly once they spend time on those streets. The views north and west from the top of the hill explain a price premium within the suburb that is consistent and instructive.
