## Caption

One of the most reliable indicators I track is buyer depth at open homes. Not just the number of groups through, but how many of those groups are genuinely ready: pre-approved, have already inspected comparable properties, and are moving with purpose.

Right now, inner east buyer depth is healthy. The buyers at open homes are not casual browsers. Most have been in the market for several months, have missed on one or two properties, and understand the price range they are working within.

That matters for sellers for one reason: genuine buyer depth produces competitive offers. When two or three pre-approved buyers want the same property, the result is usually above expectations. When only one buyer is interested, even a strong property leaves money on the table.

The suburbs where I am seeing the strongest depth this season are the ones with natural limits on supply. Balmoral, Hawthorne, and Bulimba have a finite number of homes that come to market in any given quarter. When a quality property appears in those streets, the depth shows up immediately.

For sellers, the question is not just about price expectations. It is about whether the market has the depth to support those expectations. Right now, for well-prepared homes in the right suburbs, it does.
