## Caption

Vendors who list just before Easter often regret the timing. The reason is usually the same: they underestimate how compressed the window is between launch and the point at which buyer attendance starts to drop off.

Easter in 2032 falls in late April. On the surface, that seems to give a March campaign plenty of runway. The problem is that the Easter school holidays begin the Friday before Good Friday and many families start winding down their property search a week before that. Add in the fact that a proper campaign needs three to four weeks to build momentum, hold open homes, conduct second inspections, and either go to auction or negotiate a private treaty result, and the window is shorter than most vendors expect.

A campaign launched after March 24 is likely running its most critical period, the second and third open home, into the start of the Easter wind-down. That is not a reason to avoid selling in late March, but it is a reason to make sure everything is ready before the campaign launches. Photography, building reports, council searches, and the disclosure documents all need to be prepared in advance.

The vendors who perform well in this window are the ones who treat the preparation as a separate phase from the campaign itself. If you are considering a March or April listing, the conversation about preparation needs to happen now, not the week you want to go live.
