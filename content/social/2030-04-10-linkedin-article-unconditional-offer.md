## Caption

An unconditional offer in Queensland means no finance condition, no building and pest condition, and no way out except walking away and losing your deposit. It is not something to do without preparation. Done well, it is one of the most powerful tools a buyer has.

Here is what most buyers miss: sellers do not always choose the highest offer. They choose the offer with the combination of price and certainty that works best for them. If your conditional offer at $1.2M is competing against an unconditional offer at $1.17M, you might lose.

That gap, where unconditional beats conditional despite a lower price, is real. It happens regularly in the inner east, particularly with well-prepared vendors who have already bought their next property and need certainty more than they need an extra $30,000.

The path to going unconditional without unnecessary risk: pre-approval in place before you inspect, a building and pest inspection done during the marketing campaign (vendors will often allow access before offers close), and a solicitor who has reviewed the contract in advance.

None of that is complicated. It just requires preparation that most buyers leave too late.
