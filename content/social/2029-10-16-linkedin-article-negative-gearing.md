## Caption

When you sell a negatively geared investment property, the negative gearing claim stops at settlement. That's expected. What catches investors is the other half of the equation: settlement timing relative to June 30 determines which financial year the capital gain falls into.

Get that wrong by a few days and you shift a significant capital gains liability into a tax year where you may have less ability to absorb it. Get it right and you have an opportunity to plan around it. For a property in the inner east with meaningful capital growth, the tax difference between settling on June 28 and settling on July 3 can be substantial. It's not a rounding error.

The decisions worth making before you sign the contract include which financial year you want the gain to land in, whether you'll have other deductions or losses available to offset against it, whether your income position shifts between years, and how a long or short settlement period affects those answers. These are questions your accountant should be across before you commit to a campaign, not after you're already under contract.

Negative gearing also interacts with the CGT discount in ways that aren't always obvious. The relationship between holding period, discount eligibility, and the year the gain is reported all matter.
