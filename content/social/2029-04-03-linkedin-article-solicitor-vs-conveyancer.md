## Caption

Most buyers in Queensland call their solicitor after signing the contract. That sequence is backwards.

The contract is the document that determines almost everything that follows: what is included in the sale, the finance and building and pest deadlines, the settlement date, and any special conditions. Once you sign, changing it requires the vendor's agreement. Some changes are easy to get. Others are not.

A solicitor or conveyancer reviewing the contract before signing can identify conditions that are missing or onerous, inclusions that should be listed but are not, and finance deadlines that are too tight for your lender. That review takes a day. The cost of not doing it shows up later.

The distinction between a solicitor and a conveyancer in Queensland matters for certain transactions. Both can handle a standard residential purchase. A solicitor adds value when the title is complex, there are trust structures involved, or special conditions require legal negotiation. For most inner east purchases, either works. For anything unusual, the solicitor's broader legal training matters.

The most expensive conversations I have with buyers come after they have already signed without reviewing the contract. They are almost always fixable. But they are always more expensive than a pre-signing review would have been.
