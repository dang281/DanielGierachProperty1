## Caption

Most buyers call their solicitor or conveyancer after they have signed the contract. That sequence is backwards, and it costs them leverage they cannot recover.

The contract is when the terms are set. The finance deadline, the settlement date, what is included and what is excluded, the special conditions: all of these are negotiable before signing and locked in after. A solicitor or conveyancer reviewing the contract before you sign is in a position to catch a missing inclusion, flag a problematic condition, or negotiate a finance period that is realistic given current bank turnaround times.

The same review after signing is largely an exercise in understanding terms you are already bound by.

For a standard Queensland residential purchase, both a licensed conveyancer and a solicitor are qualified to handle the transaction. The practical distinction is complexity: if the title has an easement that needs interpretation, if there is a trust involved, or if the contract has been heavily negotiated and contains special conditions, a solicitor provides greater protection. For a clean freehold purchase, a licensed conveyancer covers the ground well.

What both have in common: they cannot protect you from commitments you made before you called them.
