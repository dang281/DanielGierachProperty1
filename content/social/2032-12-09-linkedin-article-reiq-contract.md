## Caption

The finance condition in a Queensland contract lapses on the deadline date, not when the bank approves the loan. Buyers who miss that date without an approved extension assume they are still protected by the condition. They are not. Missing the finance deadline without a signed extension places the buyer in breach of contract, and the deposit is at risk. This is one of the most consistently misunderstood aspects of the standard REIQ contract and the misunderstanding tends to surface at the worst possible moment.

The way it typically unfolds: a buyer receives verbal approval from their broker or lender shortly before the deadline. They assume the condition is satisfied or that formal documentation is days away. They do not request an extension. The formal approval is delayed. The deadline passes. The seller, now aware the buyer has not formally notified finance approval and has not requested an extension, is in a position to terminate.

This does not happen every day, but it happens often enough that it is worth understanding clearly before you are in contract. The protection of the finance condition exists only while the deadline is current. Once that date has passed without notification of approval or a written extension signed by both parties, the condition is gone.
