## Caption

Most buyers see "easement" on a title search and ask their solicitor what it means. Most get a short answer. Few understand the difference between the types that matter and the types that do not.

A drainage easement along the rear boundary of an inner-east property is almost always a non-issue. Council uses it to maintain drainage infrastructure. It does not prevent the landowner from using that part of the property for a garden or a fence. It does prevent permanent structures within the easement width.

An access easement running through the yard is a different situation entirely. It gives someone else the legal right to cross your land. Depending on who holds the easement and how it is worded, that could mean foot traffic from a neighbouring property, or a vehicle access route that directly affects how you use the space.

The difference between these two situations is significant. Most buyers cannot identify which type they are looking at from the title search alone.

I have written a full explanation of how easements work in Brisbane, what the most common types are in the inner east, and what to check before you sign.
