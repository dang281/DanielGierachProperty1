## Caption

Selling a property with a tenant in place is more complicated than most investors expect, and the complications tend to emerge at the worst possible time.

Under Queensland's Residential Tenancies Act, a seller is required to give 24 hours written notice before every inspection. Every open home, every private inspection, every stylist visit. That notice requirement applies regardless of how cooperative the tenant has been throughout the tenancy.

If the property has a fixed-term lease that extends past the proposed settlement date, the buyer takes over that lease. They cannot require the tenant to vacate. If the buyer is purchasing for owner-occupation, and the tenancy extends past settlement, that buyer has a problem. Matching your settlement date to the end of the lease is critical, and it needs to be confirmed before the campaign launches.

The other common issue: tenants who are cooperative in principle but difficult in practice on presentation day. A property that is tenanted has to be inspectable and presentable. Keeping that relationship working well throughout the campaign requires consistent communication from the agent.

I have written a complete guide to the practical and legal realities of selling a tenanted property in Queensland.
