## Caption

February is always the real start of the Brisbane selling year. School is back, decision-makers are at their desks, and the autumn campaign window opens. This year feels different from the moment the first open homes went live.

The buyer pool entering the inner east market in early 2032 is not the usual mix of first-look browsers and casual enquirers. Many of these buyers have been watching this market for 12 to 18 months. They have attended open homes, tracked comparable sales, and in some cases made offers that did not proceed. They understand current values at a level that serious buyers rarely do this early in the year.

That changes the dynamic for sellers considerably. You are not educating a buyer about the suburb. You are dealing with someone who may know your street better than you do.

The Olympic year adds another dimension. Brisbane 2032 begins in July, and the planning horizon for most buyers means decisions made in autumn lock in ownership well before the Games open. That is not speculation. That is calendar logic.

For sellers, this combination of a prepared buyer pool and a defined timing window is as favourable a market entry as this cycle has produced. The properties that will perform best are those that arrive well-presented, correctly priced, and with agents who understand the audience.

February campaigns that launch clean tend to clear fast. The ones that drift into March trying to find a number the market has already answered are a different story entirely.
