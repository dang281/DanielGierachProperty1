## Caption

Cannon Hill is the inner east suburb that buyers seem to discover on their second or third search area recalibration. The Cannon Hill Plaza precinct, the rail access from Cannon Hill Station, and the proximity to Morningside and Murarrie make it a suburb that works well on a practical level, and the buyer pool here reflects that.

The streets between Wynnum Road and Old Cleveland Road in the northern part of the suburb offer a mix of Queenslanders, postwar homes, and more recent infill construction. The best character stock in this area, particularly the elevated Queenslanders with city or treetop outlooks on the ridge streets, competes directly with Morningside for buyer attention and is increasingly winning that comparison on price efficiency.

The Cannon Hill Anglican College catchment is a specific draw for families and generates enquiries from buyers who may not have initially prioritised the suburb. Once a family buyer identifies the school option, Cannon Hill tends to move up their list quickly.

Autumn campaign season here mirrors the broader inner east pattern. March and early April see the most committed buyer activity, with groups who have been searching since January and are making decisions rather than gathering information.

If you own in Cannon Hill and want to understand what your property is worth in this market, an appraisal is the right starting point.
