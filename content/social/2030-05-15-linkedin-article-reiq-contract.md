## Caption

One of the most common and costly misunderstandings in Queensland property contracts involves the finance condition. Many buyers assume that if they have not heard back from their bank by the finance date, the deadline automatically extends. It does not.

Under the REIQ contract, if you do not notify the vendor that finance has been approved or request an extension before the deadline, the condition lapses and the contract continues as if you have finance. That means you are on the hook unconditionally, even if your bank has not yet confirmed approval.

Missing a finance deadline is not just a paperwork issue. It can expose you to a damages claim if you cannot settle.

The way to manage this: keep your broker and solicitor aware of the timeline from day one. Build in buffer between your expected approval date and your contract finance date. And if you are close to the wire, request an extension early, not the night before.

The other thing buyers often miss: inclusions and exclusions listed in the contract are the definitive record of what stays with the property. Light fittings are fixtures and assumed to remain unless excluded. The kitchen appliances, the dishwasher, the garden shed: if it matters to you, have it written into the contract before you sign.
