## Caption

Easements appear on more Brisbane inner east property titles than most buyers realise, and the reaction to them is often out of proportion to their actual impact.

The distinction that matters: not all easements are equal. A drainage easement along the rear or side boundary is the most common type in the inner east. It typically restricts permanent structures over the easement corridor but has no practical effect on how you use or enjoy the property. It almost never affects resale value in a meaningful way.

An access easement, where a third party has a right to cross through your property, is a different situation entirely. This affects your use of the land, your privacy, and potentially your ability to fence or build in certain areas. Buyers who do not identify this before purchase sometimes find out in a way that is difficult to resolve.

The title search will show all registered easements. Your solicitor should explain what each one means in practical terms before you exchange contracts. The location of the easement relative to the improvements on the block matters more than the existence of the easement itself.

For sellers: an easement disclosed properly is almost never a campaign-ending issue. An easement discovered by the buyer after contracts are exchanged is a different matter.

The full guide to reading easements and understanding what they mean for inner east property is at danielgierach.com/insights/easements-property-brisbane-qld.
