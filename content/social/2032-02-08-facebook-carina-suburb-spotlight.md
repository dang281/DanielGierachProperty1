## Caption

Carina is a suburb where land size has kept its relevance long after it faded in suburbs closer to the river. The housing stock through streets like Carindale Street, Broadhurst Avenue, and the blocks running north toward the Carina Heights boundary includes a generation of post-war homes on 600 to 800 square metre blocks that represent genuine development potential or renovation upside, depending on the buyer.

The opening of Westfield Carindale to the east and the Stones Corner strip to the west means Carina residents have two distinct retail and dining precincts within easy reach, which is not a trivial consideration for buyers who want suburban land without sacrificing convenience.

The buyer pool in Carina tends to be practical and value-conscious. First-home buyers who have been priced out of Camp Hill and Coorparoo have increasingly been landing here, alongside investor buyers who understand the rental yields that the land-to-house ratio in this suburb can support. That combination keeps enquiry levels consistent even when the broader market softens.

In autumn 2032, Carina is not a suburb generating the kind of headline prices that Bulimba and Hawthorne produce, but it is absorbing buyers efficiently and sitting in a price range that still gives many purchasers genuine options. For sellers with well-maintained homes on good land, the timing is solid.
