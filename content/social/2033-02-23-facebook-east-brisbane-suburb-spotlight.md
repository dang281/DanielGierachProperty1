## Caption

East Brisbane at the peak of the autumn selling season is a market worth watching closely. The suburb's position, two kilometres from the CBD and adjacent to the Gabba precinct, means that buyer competition here is not a seasonal phenomenon. It is structural. But autumn concentrates it.

The Eton Street corridor is the heart of the suburb's commercial character: independent cafes, a small number of specialty retailers, and the kind of neighbourhood-scale hospitality that buyers from denser inner-city postcodes find genuinely appealing when they are considering a move to more residential ground. The precinct is walkable from most of the suburb and that walkability is a consistent selling point in agent campaigns. ⚠️ VERIFY

The character housing stock in East Brisbane, fed by the older homes that predate Brisbane's twentieth century suburban expansion, gives the suburb a physical distinctiveness that is harder to find in more recently developed areas. Narrow lots with high street presence, deep blocks that extend to private rear gardens, and the kind of construction that rewards renovation rather than demolition.

For vendors in February 2033, the post-Olympic period has settled East Brisbane's market into something reliable. The brief period of speculation around what the Games would do to inner-city prices has resolved. What remains is a fundamentally sound location market with a consistent buyer pool. That is what good property decisions are built on.
