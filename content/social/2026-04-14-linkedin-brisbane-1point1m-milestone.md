## Caption

Brisbane's median house price crossed $1.1 million in March, according to Cotality's April 2026 Home Value Index. Annual growth of 19 per cent. The first quarter of 2026 alone added 5.1 per cent.

That is a citywide figure. In the inner east, the base is already well above it. Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Balmoral are trading in a range where the citywide median feels like a different postcode entirely.

What is driving it is not complicated. Queensland is generating more than a quarter of Australia's population growth but less than one fifth of its new dwelling supply. Demand compounds. Supply does not catch up.

The rate environment is real. Two hikes in early 2026 have reduced borrowing capacity by around $25,000 for a single person on the average full-time wage. For buyers stretching to the upper end of the inner east, that is not a rounding error.

What I see at open homes is deliberate, not frenzied. Buyers who have been pre-approved for 12 months are making decisions. Properties priced accurately are clearing. Properties priced to aspirational expectation are not.

For inner east homeowners thinking about timing: the data is solid. What matters equally is how your property is positioned within it.
