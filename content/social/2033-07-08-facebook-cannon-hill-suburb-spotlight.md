## Caption

Cannon Hill occupies a practical and consistent position in Brisbane's inner east. It is eight kilometres from the CBD, served by the Cleveland line at Cannon Hill station, and bounded by the Kmart Plaza retail precinct that provides the everyday convenience buyers weight when assessing a suburb's liveability. It is not the suburb people write articles about. It is the suburb people buy in and stay in.

The housing stock in Cannon Hill is a mix that reflects the suburb's postwar development: timber Queenslanders on moderate blocks, brick lowsets from the 1960s and 1970s, and a growing number of contemporary builds and renovations where owner-occupiers have invested in the suburb's solid bones. That diversity makes it accessible to a wide range of buyer types.

The school catchment question in Cannon Hill is specific. The suburb falls into the Norman Park State School catchment for primary and Morningside State School for secondary in parts, creating a buyer motivation among families that sustains demand across different market conditions. Parents who identify a catchment property and verify it are some of the most motivated buyers in the inner east.

Cannon Hill's price point relative to the surrounding suburbs makes it one of the more accessible entry points in the inner east corridor for buyers who want the infrastructure and convenience of the area at a price that does not require stretching to Hawthorne or Norman Park levels.
