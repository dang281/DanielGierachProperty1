## Caption

Off-market is presented to vendors as a premium. In practice, it almost always limits the result.

The mechanism is simple. Competition between buyers drives prices. Off-market means fewer buyers know the property is available, which means fewer buyers compete for it, which means the most motivated buyer in a broader pool never got the chance to make their offer.

The exception is genuine off-market demand: a situation where an agent has a pre-qualified, motivated buyer who has been looking for exactly this property type in this street, and the vendor's priority is speed and certainty rather than maximising price. That scenario exists. It is not common.

The phrase "we have a buyer ready to go" is used more often than it should be. A buyer ready to make an offer on your home at a price that suits you is valuable. A buyer ready to make an offer at whatever price they can negotiate in a market with no competition is a different thing.

Off-market can be the right answer in specific circumstances: estate sales, vendor privacy preferences, time-critical settlement needs. For most inner east vendors who want the market's full price, a properly run campaign with broad marketing exposure almost always produces a better result.
