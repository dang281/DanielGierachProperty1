## Caption

Coorparoo is where many inner-east buyers start, and where some of them stay for longer than they planned.

The Coorparoo Square precinct gives the suburb a retail and dining anchor that functions beyond a neighbourhood convenience stop. The Stones Corner end of the suburb adds a second layer of walkability for residents in the eastern section. Together they make the suburb practical in a way that buyers who are relocating from Sydney or Melbourne recognise immediately.

Coorparoo State School is a significant driver of owner-occupier demand. The families who purchase here with the school catchment as a primary factor are buying with a longer hold in mind. That orientation tends to stabilise the owner-occupier segment even in softer parts of the cycle.

The streets running south from Old Cleveland Road in the suburb's eastern section carry character homes that attract the buyer who has been priced out of Greenslopes or who has looked at Stones Corner and wants more space for the budget. These buyers arrive with a clear brief and act quickly when the right property appears.

There is also a recognised pattern in how inner-east buyers move through the market. Coorparoo tends to be the entry point. Camp Hill comes next for many. Norman Park is sometimes the destination they did not expect. That movement pattern creates consistent and renewable demand at the Coorparoo price point across multiple seasons.

Spring is when the buyer who spent winter researching comes back to act.
