## Caption

Holland Park attracts a buyer who has usually done their research in Coorparoo first. The profile is similar, the commute is comparable, and the streets east of Logan Road deliver a quieter residential character that appeals to families who want to be close to services without living on a busy corridor. The elevation on higher blocks also produces city views that add genuine value to the properties that have them.

Holland Park State School serves the local families and keeps a steady family buyer presence in the suburb. The owner-occupier base is strong and well established, and the suburb has a character home stock that has been renovated consistently over the past decade. Buyers who have been watching the market know that well-finished properties here sit in a competitive price range relative to what they would pay for the same finish in Camp Hill.

The east-of-Logan Road distinction matters in Holland Park much as the east-of-Cavendish distinction matters in Camp Hill. Buyers who know the suburb will ask which side of Logan Road a property is on early in their enquiry, and sellers should understand how that affects pricing expectations.

October is a solid window for Holland Park listings. If you are thinking about selling this spring, I can give you an honest read on where your property sits in the current buyer landscape.
