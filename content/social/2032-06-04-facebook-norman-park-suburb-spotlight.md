## Caption

Norman Park is a suburb that buyers often discover by accident, coming through on a Saturday morning to inspect something in Hawthorne or Balmoral and then realising the price point here gives them considerably more land and character for their budget. Streets like Norman Avenue, Lackey Street, and Abbotsford Road carry the feel of a suburb that has held its identity through successive market cycles, and buyers notice that.

The suburb borders the Bulimba Creek bikeway and has direct access to the Whites Hill Reserve, which is a genuine drawcard for buyers who want green space within walking distance rather than just proximity to a park strip. Chatsworth Island sits at the eastern edge and adds a geographic distinctiveness that most inner-east suburbs cannot match.

Norman Park State School has a strong local reputation and its catchment is a consistent conversation point during appraisal meetings. The nearby Lourdes Hill College on Abbotsford Road also draws families from well outside the immediate area. For buyers weighing school access alongside walkability and character housing, this suburb ticks several boxes at once.

Traditional Queenslanders on 600-square-metre-plus blocks with original hardwood floors and high ceilings consistently attract competitive buyer fields here. If you are considering selling, it is worth understanding exactly where your property sits within that demand picture.
