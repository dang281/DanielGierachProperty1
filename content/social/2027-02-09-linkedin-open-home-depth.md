## Caption

Open home attendance in the inner east has picked up materially since the Christmas break.

The groups coming through are not browsers. The buyer profile in February is generally more focused than at any other time of year. These are people who have had the holiday period to confirm their decision, who have their finance in place, and who are ready to act if the right property comes up.

A few things I am watching:

Family homes in the $1.5M to $2.2M range are drawing strong numbers. The upsizer buyer, someone selling a smaller home within the inner east to buy a larger one, remains the most consistent and motivated buyer type for this price band. School catchment is often a significant factor.

The $900K to $1.3M range is more price-sensitive. Buyers at this level are typically first home buyers or investors, both of whom are calculating the numbers more carefully given where rates have been.

Properties that present well are separating from those that do not. With buyers who have waited through January and are now serious, the presentation bar matters. Open homes where the property is genuinely well prepared are generating the most follow-up enquiries.

For sellers who have been waiting to see how February opened: it is opening well. The buyers are there. The question is whether the listings will follow.
