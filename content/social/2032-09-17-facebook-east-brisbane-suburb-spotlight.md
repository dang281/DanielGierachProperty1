## Caption

East Brisbane is one of the few suburbs in Brisbane where a buyer can walk or cycle to the CBD without making a compromise on the home they purchase.

That practical fact defines the suburb's buyer profile more than any other single factor. Buyers who arrive here are often comparing notes on commute times rather than school catchments, though East Brisbane State School draws its own segment of family buyers with a clear catchment brief.

The Mowbray Park ferry terminal provides a river crossing option that adds to the suburb's connectivity in a way that few Brisbane suburbs at this distance from the CBD can match. Access by water, road, and for many residents by foot or bike, makes East Brisbane's transport story unusually complete.

The Gabba precinct sits adjacent to the suburb. In the post-Games period, that precinct carries infrastructure investment that benefits East Brisbane's walkable catchment. The character lot fabric in the residential streets away from the main road corridors retains the Queensland character that owner-occupiers are paying a premium to secure.

The investor and owner-occupier split in East Brisbane is relevant context for sellers. The rental market here is sustained by proximity to the CBD and the universities, which means well-maintained properties attract a buyer pool that includes both groups. Spring is when the owner-occupier buyer, who has been watching through winter, comes back to act.
