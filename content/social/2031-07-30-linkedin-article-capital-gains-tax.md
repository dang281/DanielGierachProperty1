## Caption

The six-year absence rule allows a former principal residence to remain CGT-exempt for up to six years while it is rented, provided no other property is nominated as the principal residence during that period. Most vendors who rented their home for a period do not know this rule exists until they are already in the process of selling. By that point, the decisions that would have preserved the exemption have either been made correctly by accident or not made at all.

This is the post-EOFY moment when that conversation becomes urgent. Investors who sold before 30 June are now working through their CGT position. Investors who are considering a sale in the second half of the year are modelling their position before the next financial year ends. The six-year rule is the provision that most frequently changes that calculation, and it is the one most frequently overlooked.

The other CGT consideration worth understanding for inner east Brisbane properties is the 50 per cent discount on capital gains for assets held longer than 12 months. For properties that have appreciated significantly, the interplay between the discount, the absence rule, and the timing of a sale can produce meaningfully different tax outcomes depending on how the sale is structured. These are not decisions to make without speaking to an accountant, but they are decisions that require understanding the rules first.

The full article explains the main CGT provisions relevant to property sellers in Brisbane, including the principal residence exemption, the 50 per cent discount, and how the absence rule applies in practice.
