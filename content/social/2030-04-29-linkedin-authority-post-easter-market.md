## Caption

Post-Easter is always an interesting read. The school holiday pull fades, and the buyers who paused during the break re-engage. In a normal year, late April represents one of the last genuine bursts of autumn activity before the June slowdown.

What I am watching this week: properties that held their open homes through the break are now seeing faster follow-up from buyers who have had two weeks to think. That is not unusual. Serious buyers use the Easter pause to get finances in order, revisit their shortlist, and come back ready to move.

If you are a seller with a property on the market now, this week and next are important. The buyers who are going to transact in the autumn market are active right now. A motivated, pre-approved buyer in late April is different from a motivated buyer in late July. The urgency is real.

For homeowners still considering whether to list, the window is not closed but it is closing. A campaign launched in the first two weeks of May can still achieve a strong autumn result. One launched in the last week of May is heading into winter territory.

The distinction matters practically, and it matters most in suburbs like Balmoral and Hawthorne where competition between similar properties drives the final result. In those markets, being one of three strong listings is far better than being one of twelve.
