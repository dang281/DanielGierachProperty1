## Caption

Most property market commentary in Australia is written for, or from, a Sydney or Melbourne perspective. Spring selling advice is almost always produced in this context. It is not always wrong, but it does not always apply to Brisbane.

The argument for spring in Sydney and Melbourne is based on a clear seasonal dynamic: winter genuinely depresses buyer activity because outdoor inspection conditions are cold and unappealing, and spring brings a material improvement in that experience. Brisbane does not have this problem. Winter here is mild, clear, and genuinely pleasant. The seasonal logic does not translate.

What does translate to Brisbane: the psychological convention that spring is when properties list. This is real. Sellers list in spring because they believe other sellers will list in spring, and buyers have learnt to wait for spring stock because that is when most listings arrive. The convention is self-reinforcing.

But the inner east does not follow the convention cleanly. Autumn has historically been the strongest window for this market: motivated pre-approval holders who started their search in January, lower competing stock than spring, and a buyer pool that has not been diluted by December holidays and January indecision.

Spring is a good time to sell in the inner east. It is not clearly better than autumn, and the conventional advice does not consider the specifics of our market.

If you are weighing your timing, the conversation worth having is about your specific property and the current buyer pool, not the seasonal calendar.
