## Caption

Holland Park sits in a logical position between Coorparoo and Mount Gravatt, and buyers who know the inner east well understand exactly what that means. The suburb offers the same fundamentals as Camp Hill at a more accessible entry point, and the buyers arriving here have typically done the comparison deliberately. They are not settling. They are making a smart geographic choice.

The housing stock is predominantly homes built between the 1950s and 1970s, which means there is a consistent renovation market running through the suburb. Buyers see these properties as projects with genuine upside, and the most competitive open homes in Holland Park tend to be homes that combine an honest price, a good block, and clear scope to improve. That formula generates strong results consistently.

School catchments in Holland Park are solid, and the access to both Coorparoo's café strip and the Logan Road retail and dining precinct in Mount Gravatt means the lifestyle amenity is well above what the entry price point suggests. Buyers who arrive expecting less tend to leave the inspection recalibrating what they are prepared to pay.

The renovation market here has been active for long enough that Holland Park is no longer the overlooked suburb it once was. Owners who bought early in this cycle or have held for a decade have seen meaningful change in their property's value.
