## Caption

One of the most consistent mistakes vendors make before selling is confusing presentation with renovation. They are not the same thing, and they produce very different returns.

Presentation means the property looks its best at inspection and in photography. Decluttered, cleaned professionally, styled if needed, gardens tidy, minor maintenance items fixed. A fresh coat of paint on scuffed walls. Taps that do not drip. Light bulbs that work. This level of preparation almost always earns back more than it costs.

Renovation means a new kitchen, a bathroom gut and rebuild, or a structural addition. Full kitchen renovations typically cost $40,000 to $80,000 in Brisbane and almost never earn back their full cost in the sale price. Inner east buyers who can afford to pay $1.4M for a property can also afford to renovate it to their own taste. They are not paying full price for your taste.

The exception is defects. A cracked roof, a failing retaining wall, a significant pest damage issue, these are not presentation problems, they are condition problems, and they will show up in a buyer's building and pest report. Fixing genuine defects protects the existing value of the property. Ignoring them typically shows up as a price renegotiation after contract.
