## Caption

Murarrie's infrastructure position is genuinely strong. The Gateway Motorway and Logan Motorway interchange sit right on its doorstep, which means commuters heading north, south, or into the CBD all have a viable route without fighting through inner-city traffic. That access profile is what initially attracted commercial and industrial activity to this part of the inner east, and it is now drawing a different kind of buyer: residential owner-occupiers priced out of Cannon Hill and Morningside who want the same connectivity for less.

The suburb has been through a real transition over the past decade. New residential developments have appeared alongside established homes, and the buyer profile has broadened considerably. You have investors drawn to the yield story, first-home buyers looking for a foothold closer to the city than their budget would otherwise allow, and families who see the infrastructure access as a long-term asset.

For sellers, the key is understanding that Murarrie buyers are comparison shoppers. They know what Cannon Hill and Morningside are trading at, and they are making a deliberate choice to buy here. Presentation and pricing that reflects the suburb's genuine access advantages will consistently outperform.
