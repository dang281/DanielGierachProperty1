## Caption

Spring open homes have started in the inner east. A few things I am noticing from the first weeks.

Buyer attendance is up compared to the same point in winter. This is expected. What is more useful to observe is the quality of that attendance: buyers with pre-approval, accompanied by partners or parents, taking notes and asking specific questions. These are not inspection tourists. They are in the market.

The questions I am hearing most at opens: what was this property originally purchased for, has there been a building and pest, and has the vendor received any offers? These are the questions of buyers who are close to making a decision, not early-stage research questions.

Competing stock has increased since August. The properties launching in September are mostly the ones that started preparation in July and August. The sellers who waited until late August to begin are still a few weeks away from being market-ready, which means the first movers in September have the best window.

What I would tell a buyer right now: do not confuse increased stock with reduced competition. The stock that has launched in September has been well-prepared, and motivated buyers are circling it. If you find the right property, the spring market is not a reason to hesitate.
