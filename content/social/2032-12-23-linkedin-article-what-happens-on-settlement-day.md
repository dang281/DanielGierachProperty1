## Caption

Settlement day is one of the most anticipated dates in any property sale. It is also one of the least understood.

For most vendors, the actual day is quiet. Your solicitor handles the digital transfer via PEXA. You will likely find out it is done when you receive a text message. The keys are released. The funds clear. That is the transaction from the vendor's perspective.

What people do not always appreciate is that settlement day is almost entirely determined by what happens in the weeks before it. Your obligations around the pre-settlement inspection, the condition of the property at handover, the timing of your removalists relative to settlement time, and any inclusions listed in the contract all need to be resolved in advance. Arriving at settlement day with these in order means the day itself is uneventful. That is the goal.

Vendors who treat settlement as a single moment rather than the end of a process are the ones who occasionally find themselves managing disputes or delays in the final days. The article below covers the full sequence: what happens, in what order, and where the real preparation needs to happen. If you are settling before the end of the year or planning a campaign for the new year, it is worth understanding the process from both sides.
