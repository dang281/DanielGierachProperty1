## Caption

The Olympic year starts in a few days. This is not a forecast. It is a straightforward view of what I am actually observing and what I think inner east homeowners should be thinking about heading into 2032.

The Olympics will bring attention to Brisbane that no other event could generate. International media coverage, interstate relocation interest from people who have been watching Brisbane for years, and infrastructure that is already largely built. These are real. The question is what you do with them.

For vendors, the most common mistake in an Olympic year is waiting for the attention to peak before acting. By the time the Games are underway and the world's cameras are pointed at Brisbane, the buyers who were motivated by Olympic proximity have typically already transacted. The opportunity is in the months before the event, not during it.

For buyers, the question is whether a genuine long-term need to own in the inner east exists independently of the Olympic story. If it does, the conditions of early 2032 are worth moving in. If the only reason to buy is because the Olympics are happening, that is a thinner foundation for a decision.

For holders, the honest answer is that inner east property held for the long term rarely requires a specific macro event to justify it. The fundamentals of the suburb, school catchments, lifestyle, access, proximity to the river, do not change in an Olympic year.

The people who do well in 2032 will be the ones with clear personal goals. Not the ones trying to time a single macro event.
