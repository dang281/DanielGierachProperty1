## Caption

There is a clear split in the Camp Hill market that most people outside the suburb do not notice until they start looking seriously. Streets east of Cavendish Road tend to command a premium, and buyers who know the area well will search specifically within that pocket. The blocks are larger, the streetscapes are quieter, and the separation from Logan Road traffic is real.

Whites Hill State College catchment is a major factor for families moving into Camp Hill at the family-home stage. It is one of the more consistently sought-after public school catchments on the south side, and that keeps demand for four-bedroom character homes in this suburb well above what you would find in comparable areas further out.

October is typically when the Camp Hill market moves through its most active period. Spring buyers who have pre-approval sorted and a clear brief tend to move quickly when the right property appears. Character homes that present well and are priced accurately rarely sit long once they hit the market at this time of year.

If you own in Camp Hill and are weighing up whether now is the right time to sell, a no-obligation appraisal is the clearest way to get a factual answer.
