## Caption

East Brisbane's freestanding housing stock is limited in volume, and that scarcity is the key fact for sellers here. When a well-presented character house comes to market in this suburb, the buyer pool is drawing on people who've been looking across a wide range of price points and locations, and the location case for East Brisbane requires almost no explanation. They understand it.

What's been selling in East Brisbane at the strongest prices is the character housing on the residential streets, the homes where you can separate yourself from the apartment density and feel like you're in a house in a real street. Those properties compete with South Bank and New Farm buyer demand, and that cross-suburb competition has been an important driver of results.

The autumn selling window is relevant here for a specific reason. Professionals who've been waiting to buy, and who've been weighing the inner south against rental commitments, tend to make decisions in the first quarter of the year. That buyer group moves with financial discipline and good borrowing capacity.

Median house prices for freestanding homes are around $1.4M. Confirm this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before using that figure.

If you own a house in East Brisbane and are considering a March or April campaign, the position is strong. The link below has more.
