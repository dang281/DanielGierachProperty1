## Caption

Coorparoo occupies a middle ground in the inner east that suits a wide range of buyers. Close enough to the CBD to make a daily commute straightforward, large enough to offer meaningful variation in street types and price points, and well-served by both the busway and local retail, it has consistently attracted buyers who want inner-east access without paying Hawthorne or Balmoral prices.

The suburb divides into several micro-markets. The older character homes in the streets north of Old Cleveland Road tend to attract buyers who value the period architecture and the established streetscapes. The newer developments closer to the commercial strips appeal to buyers who prioritise low maintenance and proximity to cafes and services.

School catchments are a meaningful driver of demand in Coorparoo. Coorparoo State School and Coorparoo Secondary College both draw families into the suburb, and the boundary effect on pricing around those catchments is real and measurable.
