## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits at the intersection of family convenience and genuine value in Brisbane's southern middle ring. Mt Gravatt Central and Westfield Garden City between them cover most of what residents need for daily life, from grocery shopping through to medical services and large-format retail. That level of local amenity, at a price point well below the inner east, is the core proposition for buyers who have done their homework across the broader corridor.

The suburb has been through a steady period of change over the past decade. Renovators have been targeting older character homes and post-war stock, lifting the presentation standard in pockets of the suburb that used to be largely untouched. Units and newer medium-density development sit alongside those character properties, giving the suburb a broader mix than you find in some of the more tightly defined neighbouring areas.

Griffith University's Nathan campus is close enough that the suburb has always attracted some investor interest from buyers thinking about student accommodation, though the owner-occupier and family market is the main driver. South East Freeway access keeps the CBD within a manageable commute, and the motorway network opens up the broader south-east corner of Brisbane for residents who need to travel for work.

The suburb is more affordable than the inner east while still sitting close enough to tap into the same broad lifestyle corridor. If you own in Mt Gravatt and you're considering your options, I'd be glad to talk through the current market conditions with you.
