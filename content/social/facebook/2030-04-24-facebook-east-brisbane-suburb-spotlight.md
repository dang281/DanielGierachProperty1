## Caption

East Brisbane is one of the most CBD-proximate residential suburbs in Brisbane, and that proximity drives a specific buyer profile: professionals who value walkability and the ability to commute on foot or by bicycle, and investors who understand that inner-city land adjacent to the Brisbane CBD is a long-term hold.

The suburb's character stock includes a significant number of well-maintained Queenslanders and post-war homes on the elevated sections above Shafston Avenue, some of which have CBD skyline outlooks that buyers are prepared to pay a premium for. The flat sections closer to the Story Bridge corridor attract a different profile, often buyers looking for the CBD lifestyle without paying inner-city apartment prices.

What East Brisbane has benefited from in recent years is the broader transformation of the Kangaroo Point and Woolloongabba precincts, which have brought new dining, retail, and infrastructure to the immediate south. The Cross River Rail connection at Woolloongabba adds a further accessibility dimension that keeps East Brisbane on the radar for buyers who value public transport options.

The turnover rate in East Brisbane is relatively low, particularly in the character home segment. When properties do come to market, they tend to attract immediate attention from buyers who have been waiting.

For homeowners considering selling, the low listing volume in this market is an asset.
