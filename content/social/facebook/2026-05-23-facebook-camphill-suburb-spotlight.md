## Caption

Camp Hill has a median house price of around $1.76 million, with annual growth of approximately 10 per cent and a 10-year capital growth rate of 122 per cent.

That long-term number is the one worth sitting with. It is not the result of a hot cycle or a single boom year. It reflects what happens when a well-located, well-amenitied suburb with limited land release attracts consistent family demand over time. Camp Hill sits 6 to 7km from the CBD, close enough for a practical commute, with the block sizes and street character that buyers leave the inner suburbs to find.

The current market has added nuance to that story. Annual growth of around 10 per cent is still strong, but buyers are taking more time and doing more careful due diligence. The rate environment means finance approvals are taking longer. What that produces is a market where the prepared sellers are doing better than the ones who set an optimistic price and wait.

A well-presented Camp Hill home priced on comparable sales will attract genuine competition. One priced on aspiration risks sitting while the motivated buyers move on to something else.
