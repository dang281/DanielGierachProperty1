## Caption

Norman Park is built around one of Brisbane's great open spaces: Whites Hill Reserve runs through the heart of the suburb, giving it a green character that is unusual for somewhere only 6 kilometres from the CBD. Buyers who discover Norman Park consistently note that the combination of the reserve, the school catchments, and the character housing stock is difficult to find elsewhere at a comparable distance from the city.

The suburb's strongest performing streets are those backing onto or within walking distance of the reserve, and homes with established gardens tend to attract buyers who want outdoor living without sacrificing proximity to city amenity. Norman Park State School is consistently sought after, which keeps family demand stable through all market conditions.

Spring is when Norman Park listings receive the deepest buyer engagement. The suburb's reputation is well-established in the inner-east market, so new listings tend to be picked up quickly by buyers who have been monitoring the suburb and waiting for the right property.
