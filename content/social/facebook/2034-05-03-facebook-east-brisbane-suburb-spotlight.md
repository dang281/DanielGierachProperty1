## Caption

East Brisbane sits at the CBD edge of the inner east, which gives it a proximity advantage that few other suburbs in Daniel's territory can match. The distance to the city is measured in minutes by bike or foot from the right address, and that proximity is reflected in the buyer profile: professionals who want a genuine inner-city commute without the density and noise of the Fortitude Valley or New Farm fringe.

The Stones Corner retail strip is East Brisbane's main street equivalent, and it has the independent character that comes from a precinct that has evolved rather than been planned. The mix of cafes, restaurants, and specialty retail gives the suburb its own identity rather than borrowing from the adjacent suburbs.

The Mowbray Park ferry terminal provides a river commute option to the CBD that adds to the transport choice that East Brisbane residents have. The combination of bus frequency on Vulture Street, the inner-city bypass connectivity, and the ferry service means the suburb is genuinely multi-modal, which matters to buyers who have lifestyle priorities beyond the morning drive.

The housing stock includes character Queenslanders, post-war construction, and a growing volume of more recent development. The proximity to the CBD means the suburb has seen consistent investment in both renovation and new construction, which keeps the property presentation standard high.
