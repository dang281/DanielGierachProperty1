## Caption

Cannon Hill's mid-winter market is anchored by the same buyer profile that has driven the suburb's growth over the past decade: families who want inner east proximity without the inner east premium, and buyers who have been priced out of Morningside and are looking one suburb east.

The train station is the practical differentiator. Direct rail access to the CBD from Cannon Hill is an asset that sits above the lifestyle conversation for a significant subset of buyers. For dual-income professional couples who need two commute options covered, the train line is a deciding factor.

The Cannon Hill Shopping Village and the broader retail cluster near the train station give the suburb a daily-use convenience that reduces the car-dependency that characterises many of the broader outer suburban alternatives.

The housing stock quality in Cannon Hill continues to improve as renovation activity cycles through the suburb. The streets that have seen the most renovation work over the past five to eight years are now the streets generating the best comparable sales. That price evidence makes it easier to set credible price guides for properties in similar positions.

Winter open home numbers in Cannon Hill tend to be lower than autumn, but the buyers who attend in June and July are disproportionately serious. A well-priced property in this suburb will not sit in winter if the preparation has been done.
