## Caption

East Brisbane sits closer to the Brisbane CBD than almost any suburb in the inner east, and the distance is starting to price accordingly.

The suburb boundary runs to within a few kilometres of the city centre. For buyers who work in the CBD or South Bank, the practical commute options from here include walking distance for some and a short bus or cycle for most, which is a genuinely rare combination at this price point. The access story is one that holds value across market cycles because it resolves a daily problem that most other inner-east suburbs cannot.

The Gabba precinct is relevant to East Brisbane in a way that is often underdiscussed. The Brisbane 2032 Olympic and Paralympic Games infrastructure in and around the Gabba is adjacent to the suburb's western edge, and the planning changes associated with that investment are worth understanding for homeowners who are thinking about their options. East Brisbane sits in a corridor that has attracted consistent interest from buyers specifically because of its position relative to the major infrastructure commitment.

Stock levels here are structurally low. The suburb is bounded by the river and the arterial roads, which limits the supply of homes that come to market. That constrained supply profile against persistent CBD-proximity demand has supported prices through rate cycles that affected less well-positioned suburbs more significantly.

For owners who have been watching conditions in East Brisbane, now is a practical time to understand what your position looks like.
