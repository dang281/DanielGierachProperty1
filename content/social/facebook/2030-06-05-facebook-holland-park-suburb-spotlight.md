## Caption

Holland Park is one of the inner east suburbs where the winter market holds up better than the suburb's profile might suggest. The Cavendish Road State High School catchment is the primary reason: families with a specific secondary school requirement make purchasing decisions based on enrolment timing, not property season.

That catchment-driven demand keeps Holland Park's buyer pool active through June and July in a way that is noticeably different from suburbs where the buyer profile is more mixed. An agent who knows this suburb understands that the right buyer for a well-positioned Holland Park character home may be attending an open home in the middle of June because they have an enrolment deadline in January.

The housing stock in Holland Park includes a large proportion of post-war homes on 600 to 700-square-metre blocks that have seen varying degrees of renovation. The well-renovated examples set a price ceiling that justifies the land cost. The original-condition properties attract buyers who want to add value through renovation and are looking for the best possible land size within their budget.

Logan Road gives the suburb its commercial spine: a practical retail corridor that serves daily needs without the café-premium pricing of Old Cleveland Road. For buyers who prioritise function over lifestyle amenity, Holland Park delivers.

For sellers in Holland Park, a winter campaign timed around school catchment buyer demand can be more effective than waiting for spring.
