## Caption

Balmoral's market is defined by its position between the river and the escarpment, and by the character of its housing stock.

The Queenslander homes on the high ground of Balmoral with river glimpses or outlook over the surrounding suburbs are among the most distinctive properties in the inner east. They are not interchangeable with similar properties in other suburbs, which is both what makes them desirable and what makes them challenging to price. When a genuinely distinctive Balmoral home comes to market in good condition, it tends to attract a buyer pool that is prepared to pay for the irreplaceable.

The mid-market in Balmoral, the post-war brick and hardwood on a standard block, operates on stronger comparable evidence. These properties have a clear peer group in the suburb and in the neighbouring precincts of Hawthorne and Norman Park. Their prices reflect the overall inner east trajectory without the premium or the pricing ambiguity of the high-end character stock.

What Balmoral shares with Hawthorne is the Lytton Road corridor and the proximity to the river, the Balmoral Beach esplanade, and the café strips that have matured significantly in the last decade. The lifestyle offer is genuine and well understood by the buyers who seek it.

If you own in Balmoral and want a current read on the comparable evidence in your street, I am happy to walk through it with you.
