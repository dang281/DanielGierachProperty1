## Caption

Murarrie sits directly opposite Bulimba across the creek corridor, yet the land prices tell a very different story, and that gap is exactly what draws buyers here once they know where to look.

The residential pockets around Hemmant Road are quiet, well-established, and consistently undervalued relative to what comparable land costs in Cannon Hill or Morningside. Buyers who come to Murarrie are almost always coming with a specific purpose: they want more land, better value, and access to the inner east without paying inner-east prices. The Gateway Motorway access adds genuine appeal for anyone commuting north or south, making it a practical choice as much as a value one.

What makes Murarrie particularly interesting from a selling perspective is the low turnover. Properties here are held, not traded frequently. When a home does come to market, it tends to attract buyers who have been watching and waiting, and that patient demand tends to produce genuine competition rather than passive interest.

If you own property in Murarrie and have been wondering what the current market might mean for your position, it is worth having a conversation. Quietly located, well-connected, and with a buyer pool that recognises the value, Murarrie is a suburb with more going for it than its price point currently reflects.
