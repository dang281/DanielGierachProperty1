## Caption

The elevation story in Carina Heights is one that buyers who inspect in winter understand particularly well. The cross-breeze on the higher streets, the views from certain elevated blocks, and the general sense of quiet residential space above the suburban flat corridor below are features that the suburb's residents consistently rank as primary reasons they chose the area.

Carina Heights occupies a price point where family buyers find the most practical combination of block size, school access, and value in the inner east south corridor. The proximity to the Carindale Westfield hub reduces the friction of daily errands in a way that the more urban inner east suburbs do not always match. For families with children in primary school who are not yet locked into a secondary catchment, Carina Heights gives optionality.

The renovation market in Carina Heights has been active over the past several years as buyers recognise the value of the land and the scope to improve the housing stock. A well-executed renovation in the suburb adds genuine market value because the comparative sales data now includes renovated examples that establish a clear ceiling at the top end.

The winter buyer profile in Carina Heights is family-dominated and motivated by school timing. Families with a Year 5 or Year 6 child who need to be settled in a new home before the next enrolment cycle tend to make purchasing decisions in the June-August period regardless of market conditions.
