## Caption

Murarrie sits right on the doorstep of one of Brisbane's busiest employment corridors, and the buyers are noticing.

Sandwiched between Cannon Hill and Morningside, Murarrie has long offered better value per square metre than either of its neighbours. Gateway Bridge access puts the CBD within a 15-minute commute, and the Murarrie Business Park draws a steady stream of workers who would rather buy locally than battle the morning traffic.

The suburb is mid-transition. Original post-war homes sit alongside newer townhouse developments, and that mix is exactly what attracts families priced out of Morningside as well as investors chasing stronger rental yields. Lytton Road is the main artery, but the quieter residential streets off Wynne Street and Collie Street hold some of the suburb's better-presented homes.

Median house price is sitting around $1.05 million, with units offering a more accessible entry point for investors. Days on market has been tracking well below the Brisbane average, which tells you demand is consistent rather than seasonal.

If you own in Murarrie and have been wondering what the current market looks like, now is a solid time to find out. Autumn listings are limited and buyer competition stays firm when stock is tight.
