## Caption

Norman Park at the end of March is a seller's market in a very specific sense. The Whites Hill State College catchment buyers who needed to act before the school year progressed further have been in the market since January. The ones who haven't yet found something are at their most motivated point of the year. Sellers who are prepared and priced correctly are meeting those buyers right now.

What's been selling in Norman Park this autumn, and what's driven the results worth talking about, is the genuinely well-positioned character home on a large block in the streets that have the views, the quiet, and the proximity to Whites Hill Reserve. Those properties come to market rarely, and when they do the response from buyers who've been waiting is immediate.

The low turnover that defines Norman Park as a suburb means that each sale in Q1 is setting a price reference for the suburb. Sellers who understand that they're not just selling their home but also establishing a comparable for their neighbour's eventual sale tend to approach preparation with the seriousness it deserves.

Median house prices are around $1.5M. Verify this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before using it in any decision.

This is one of the last posts in the current series before the autumn window closes and winter conditions change the buyer mix. If you're a Norman Park homeowner with a decision to make, make it now.
