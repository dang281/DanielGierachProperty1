## Caption

Carina Heights does not get the same attention as Camp Hill or Morningside. For buyers who have done their research, that is exactly the point.

The suburb sits directly between Camp Hill and Carindale, with Whites Hill State College on its doorstep and bus routes into the CBD in under 30 minutes. The streets are quiet, the blocks are generous, and prices are still 10 to 15 per cent below comparable properties in the suburbs to the north.

That gap has been closing.

Entry price for a three-bedroom house is sitting around $900,000 to $950,000. Renovated properties in the streets closest to Whites Hill are regularly clearing $1.1 million. The land content here is part of the story: 600 to 700 square metre lots are still achievable at a price that has already moved past reach in Morningside or Norman Park.

The buyer pool is mainly families who have done the maths and decided the value stacks up better to the south. The Whites Hill State College catchment drives consistent demand even when other parts of the market are quieter.

If you own in Carina Heights and have been watching your neighbours sell, now is a good time to understand what your property is worth.
