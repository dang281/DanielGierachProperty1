## Caption

Morningside has one of the more consistent demand stories in the inner east. Supply has always been the constraint here. Fewer properties come to market in any given quarter than in comparable suburbs, and when they do, the competition tends to be real. That dynamic hasn't changed, and it's what gives owners in Morningside a structural advantage when they do decide to sell.

The streets north of Wynnum Road attract the strongest buyer interest, where character Queenslanders and well-renovated homes sit on good blocks with the kind of street appeal that brings multiple buyers to an open home. The housing stock across the suburb is predominantly character and post-war, with renovation being the dominant story at the top of the market. Buyers here are typically families or professionals who've been looking for a while and know what they want when they see it.

Coles at Morningside is the everyday convenience anchor, covering grocery shopping without requiring a trip further afield. The Hawthorne CityCat terminal is close enough to be a realistic commuting option for residents who work near the CBD or the river precincts. Cannon Hill Anglican College is accessible for families considering that option at the secondary level.

Prices in Morningside have tracked alongside Hawthorne at the top end of the market and Camp Hill through the middle, which tells you something about where the suburb sits in the broader inner east hierarchy. If you own here and you've been wondering what your property might achieve in the current conditions, I'd welcome the conversation.
