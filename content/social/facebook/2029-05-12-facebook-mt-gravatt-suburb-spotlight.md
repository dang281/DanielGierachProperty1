## Caption

Mt Gravatt's defining feature is the mountain itself. The lookout at the top of the reserve is the landmark, but it is the elevated residential streets on the suburb's slopes that have driven consistent buyer interest from people who understand what elevated north-facing aspects are worth in Brisbane.

The suburb sits at the junction of the inner east and the southern suburbs, which gives it a distinctive character. The housing stock is mixed: original post-war homes, mid-century brick, and a more recent wave of contemporary builds on larger elevated sites. The buyer profile reflects that variety, ranging from families looking for space to downsizers who want elevation, views, and a large garden without the intensity of the inner-ring suburbs.

The bushland reserve itself is a genuine lifestyle attribute. Walking tracks, mountain biking, and the lookout are used regularly by residents. It is the kind of suburb amenity that is difficult to put a number on but consistently comes up at open homes as a reason why buyers chose to inspect.

TAFE Queensland's Mt Gravatt campus and the Garden City Shopping Centre precinct along Logan Road add practical infrastructure to the suburb's appeal for families and renters alike.

For investors, the rental demand in Mt Gravatt has been supported by proximity to both the university precinct and the shopping and medical services along Logan Road.
