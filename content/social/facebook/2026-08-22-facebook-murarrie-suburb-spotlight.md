## Caption

Murarrie is one of those suburbs that buyers with a longer investment horizon understand better than the broader market, and the gap in understanding is closing.

The residential streets north of Murarrie Road and around Redfern Park operate at a quality level that the suburb's broader reputation has not always reflected. Well-maintained homes on proper blocks, within genuine reach of the CBD, and without the Hawthorne or Bulimba price. For buyers who can read a suburb at street level rather than postcode level, that combination has been producing returns.

The proximity to the Gateway Motorway and Brisbane Airport is a specific selling point for a buyer type that often goes unmentioned in suburb profiles. Queensland's logistics and aviation sectors employ a significant number of people who care about eliminating a complex commute. For those buyers, Murarrie's location resolves a problem that most inner-east suburbs cannot.

Investor yields in Murarrie have remained strong relative to comparable price points in adjacent suburbs. Rental demand from workers in the nearby industrial corridor and from tenants seeking inner-ring access at a lower rent than Hawthorne or Norman Park has kept vacancy tight.

Median prices have been moving through the current cycle at a rate that reflects the suburb's emerging recognition. What was genuinely underpriced three years ago is now trading closer to where comparable suburb metrics sit.
