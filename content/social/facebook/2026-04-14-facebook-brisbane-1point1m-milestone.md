## Caption

Brisbane's median house price crossed $1.1 million in March. Annual growth of 19 per cent, and the first quarter of 2026 alone added 5.1 per cent, according to Cotality's April 2026 Home Value Index.

That is the citywide figure. If you own in Bulimba, Hawthorne or Balmoral, you are already well above it.

What is driving it is not complicated. Queensland is generating more than a quarter of Australia's population growth but building less than one fifth of the new homes needed to house it. The demand keeps compounding. The supply does not keep up.

The rate environment is real too. Two hikes in early 2026 have taken a chunk out of borrowing capacity. Buyers are more deliberate now than in 2021. But the ones at open homes right now are pre-approved and ready to move. Properties priced accurately are selling. Properties with aspirational price tags are sitting.

If you own in the inner east and have been watching the market, now is a good time to get an honest number on your property.
