## Caption

Murarrie is an inner east suburb that has been quietly repricing for several years, and the buyers who discovered it early have done well.

The suburb occupies a distinctive position in the Brisbane inner east: it borders Hawthorne and Cannon Hill, sits on the Gateway Motorway interchange, and has an industrial and commercial character in its eastern sections that has historically kept residential prices below neighbouring suburbs despite genuine geographic proximity to the river and the CBD.

The residential streets in the western and northern parts of Murarrie are where the buyer story has been playing out. Post-war homes on reasonable allotments, in streets that feel quiet and suburban despite being minutes from the motorway, have attracted a buyer profile that has shifted from investor-dominant to owner-occupier-led as the suburb's residential identity has strengthened.

The comparison buyers make is simple: for the same money as a smaller property in Cannon Hill or a heavily compromised one in Morningside, Murarrie can offer more land and more house. That value gap has been closing, but it has not disappeared.
