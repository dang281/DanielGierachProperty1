## Caption

Mt Gravatt occupies an interesting position in Brisbane's inner south-east: not quite inner city, not quite middle ring, and that ambiguity has worked in its favour.

The suburb covers a range of property types across a significant elevation gradient. The hilltop sections, particularly the streets around the Summit precinct, carry genuine views across to the CBD and the Glasshouse Mountains. Those elevated properties attract buyers who are prepared to pay for outlook, and they tend to hold value in ways that flat land in the same postcode does not.

The commercial strip along Logan Road and its connection to Garden City provide daily convenience that most Brisbane suburbs at this price point do not. The trade-off has always been traffic density on the main arterials, but buyers who know Mt Gravatt treat this as background noise rather than a deterrent.

The buyer demographic is broad. Families value the Griffith University access, the private school options nearby, and the direct arterial routes south. Investors like the rental yield relative to purchase price. Downsizers see the range of property types and the manageable scale.

Mt Gravatt's story is one of steady, undramatic performance. For owners considering their options, that steady track record is the most relevant data point.
