## Caption

The RBA meets on 5 May. Two 25 basis point hikes in February and March have pushed the cash rate to 4.10 per cent. The March decision was close: five board members voted to raise, four voted to hold.

Economists are split on what happens next. Some are forecasting a third consecutive rise to 4.35 per cent. Others think the board will pause and watch how the previous hikes flow through household budgets.

For Brisbane buyers and sellers, the stakes are real. Two hikes have already reduced what buyers can borrow. A third would take another slice out of borrowing capacity across the market.

What I see at open homes across the inner east right now: buyers are still active. They are more deliberate than two years ago. They are buying on merit, not momentum. Properties priced accurately are clearing. Properties priced on optimism are sitting.

The May decision will set the tone for the back half of the year. Worth understanding what it means for your property regardless of which way the board goes.
