## Caption

Holland Park sits about 6km south of the CBD and consistently trades at a discount to Coorparoo and Camp Hill despite being directly between them geographically.

The reason is mostly streetscape and density. Holland Park has a higher proportion of post-war brick homes and less of the elevated Queenslander character that commands premium pricing in the inner east. For buyers who care more about block size and practical liveability than period architecture, that is an advantage. You get more land at a lower price point in a suburb that shares the same school catchments, the same commute times, and the same proximity to Westfield Carindale, Coorparoo and the city.

The buyer profile in Holland Park skews toward families making a deliberate value call. They have done their research, they understand why the suburb is priced where it is, and they are not paying a premium for an address. That type of buyer tends to be ready to move when they find the right property.

For sellers, the challenge is working with an accurate comparable sales set in a suburb where property type varies significantly from street to street.

If you are thinking about selling in Holland Park, I am happy to give you a current market assessment.
