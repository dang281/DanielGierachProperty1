## Caption

Carina Heights tends to attract buyers who have done the homework. It is not a suburb that generates the same volume of passing interest as Morningside or Bulimba, but the buyers who arrive at open homes here have usually already decided the suburb works for them and are looking to convert.

The elevation across much of Carina Heights is the feature that sets it apart from the flat corridor of the inner east. Some streets carry genuine elevated outlooks and a cross-breeze that is noticeably absent in lower-lying parts of the adjacent suburbs. For buyers who know Brisbane's summer climate well, this is not a trivial detail.

Land size in Carina Heights is generally strong relative to the price point. The 600-square-metre-plus blocks that once defined the inner east standard are still findable here at prices that are below the equivalent in Camp Hill or Morningside. That combination of elevation, land size, and relative affordability is what the buyer profile responds to.

Families are the core buyer segment. Proximity to the Carindale shopping corridor, access to the Gateway and Eastern Arterial roads, and the Carina Heights State School catchment all play into the practical decision-making of that buyer group.

For sellers, Carina Heights is a suburb where price precision matters more than marketing spend. Getting the guide right from the start is the most important decision in any campaign here.
