## Caption

Hawthorne has one of the most consistent premium-to-demand ratios in the inner east, and the buyers who know it understand why.

The suburb's connection to Oxford Street places it at the lifestyle centre of the inner east. The Hawthorne end of that strip has a character that draws both residents and buyers from neighbouring suburbs who are thinking about their next move. Buyers who enquire about Hawthorne have typically already decided this is the type of neighbourhood they want to be in. What they are deciding is which property.

The river-fronting properties on Hawthorne Road and the streets running east from the river represent a specific product type with deep, specific demand. That demand does not diminish significantly in softer market conditions because it is driven by buyers for whom this location is a non-negotiable. The emotional and practical case for those addresses is clear to the people who hold them and to the people who want them.

What has been notable over the past year is the rate at which properties further from the river, on the quieter residential streets east of Hawthorne Road, have closed the price gap to the premium lines. That pattern of inner-suburb value migration is well established in the inner east, and Hawthorne is at a mature stage of that process.

For homeowners in streets that were mid-tier five years ago, the equity position is now meaningfully different. Understanding what that means for your options is worth a conversation.
