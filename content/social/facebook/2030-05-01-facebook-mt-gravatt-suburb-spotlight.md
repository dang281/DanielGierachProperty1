## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits at the southern edge of the inner east corridor and has been one of the more quietly consistent performers in Brisbane's residential market over the past decade. The suburb's proximity to the Westfield Mt Gravatt hub, Griffith University, and major road corridors gives it a practical appeal that retains residents and attracts a broad buyer profile.

The elevated sections of Mt Gravatt toward the conservation reserve carry an outlook that genuinely distinguishes specific streets from the broader suburb. A property on the high side of that ridge with clear sightlines and good orientation is a fundamentally different proposition from a property in the flat centre of the suburb, even if the land sizes are similar.

The buyer profile in Mt Gravatt is one of the most diverse in the inner east catchment. Young families looking for school access and space, investors drawn by the university rental demand, and older buyers downsizing from larger properties in the outer suburbs all appear regularly at open homes.

What makes Mt Gravatt interesting for sellers right now is the supply dynamic. Properties that are well presented and correctly priced are not sitting as long as the suburb's more subdued profile might suggest. The buyers are there; they are just quieter than the Bulimba crowd.

A property-specific appraisal is the best way to understand where your home sits in the current market.
