## Caption

Mt Gravatt East sits on the elevated eastern side of the Mt Gravatt corridor, and that position gives it a different character from Mt Gravatt proper. The streets that run along the higher ground have aspect and outlook that the lower-lying addresses to the west do not, and buyers who discover the elevation advantage often find it changes how they assess the value in this part of the inner south-east.

Griffith University's Nathan campus is the employment and educational anchor for this part of Brisbane's southside. The campus generates a consistent demand from academics, administrators, and students seeking residential addresses within reasonable proximity. The hospital cluster on the Greenslopes-Princess Alexandra corridor is also accessible from Mt Gravatt East, creating a buyer profile that includes health sector workers making a suburb decision based on work commute.

The retail and dining in the area has improved consistently. The Westfield Garden City precinct is accessible without a significant drive and handles most of the shopping requirements that residents of the elevated streets would otherwise need to travel into the CBD to address. The Carindale alternative to the south adds further options.

The housing stock on the eastern streets is a mix of established post-war homes on good blocks and more recent construction. Properties with elevated outlook tend to attract buyers with specific site preferences, which can create strong competition when the right address comes to market.
