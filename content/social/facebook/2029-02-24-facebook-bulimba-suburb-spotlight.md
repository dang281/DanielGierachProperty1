## Caption

Bulimba is where the Brisbane inner east conversation often starts and frequently ends.

Oxford Street is the kind of high street that keeps a suburb self-contained in a way that most Brisbane suburbs aspire to. Coffee, restaurants, independent retail, the cinema, the ferry terminal to the city: it is all within walking distance for most Bulimba addresses. Families who move here rarely leave voluntarily.

The river boundary to the north gives Bulimba an edge that is literally and figuratively hard to replicate. Properties along the riverfront command a premium that reflects genuine scarcity. There are only so many riverfront allotments in the inner east, and the buyers who want them know exactly what they are competing for.

Away from the waterfront, the suburb still attracts strong demand for its character homes on good streets, its school options including Bulimba State School and the proximity to Lourdes Hill College and Anglican Church Grammar School, and the simple fact that it is one of the better-connected 4km-from-the-CBD suburbs in Brisbane.

Days on market for well-presented Bulimba properties have historically been among the lowest in the inner east. Buyer enquiry from both locals and interstate buyers remains consistent.

If you own in Bulimba and are thinking about what the current market means for your property, I am happy to sit down and talk through it.
