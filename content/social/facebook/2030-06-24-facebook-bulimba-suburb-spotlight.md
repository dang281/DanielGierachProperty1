## Caption

Bulimba in late June is one of the inner east suburbs where the gap between a well-prepared winter campaign and a poorly prepared one is most visible. The buyer pool for the suburb is deep and well-financed, but buyers here are sophisticated. They have usually inspected 15 to 20 properties across the inner east before arriving at an open home in Bulimba with a serious intention to buy.

What that means for sellers: presentation matters more in Bulimba than almost anywhere else in the inner east. A property that is clearly well-maintained, professionally photographed, and staged sensibly communicates immediately to a buyer pool that has seen enough properties to recognise the difference. A property that is rushed to market with mediocre photos and clutter in the living spaces loses buyer attention within the first minute of the inspection.

The river-access streets in Bulimba, those that give residents walking or cycling distance to the CityCat terminal and the riverfront park, represent the suburb's most consistently competitive segment. Properties in these streets attract buyers from across Brisbane who have specifically targeted Bulimba's riverfront lifestyle.

Oxford Street maintains its role as the suburb's social spine. The morning coffee ritual, the weekend brunch culture, and the evening restaurant trade support a daily quality of life that buyers in this price range are explicitly paying for.

For homeowners considering selling, a winter Bulimba campaign with the preparation done properly is not a compromise on the result.
