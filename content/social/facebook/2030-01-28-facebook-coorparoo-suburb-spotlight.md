## Caption

The Joseph Street café strip in Coorparoo has developed into the kind of precinct that anchors a suburb's identity. Good coffee, local dining, Saturday morning foot traffic, and a community feel that buyers and renters both respond to. It's a small thing that makes a real difference to how people experience living there day to day.

Beyond the café strip, Coorparoo is one of the inner east's most consistent family suburbs. Coorparoo State School catchment drives significant buyer interest, and the mix of character homes, post-war houses, and newer townhouses gives buyers at different price points a range of options. The $1.2M to $1.6M range is where competition is strongest, with families regularly competing on quality homes in good streets.

The suburb also attracts buyers who want the inner east lifestyle but need a bit more space than Woolloongabba or East Brisbane can offer. Blocks are generally reasonable, and the suburb's elevation in parts gives some homes good aspect.

Median house prices are around $1.5M at the time of writing. Confirm this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before using it in any property decision.
