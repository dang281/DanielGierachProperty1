## Caption

There is a suburb 8km from Brisbane's CBD where you can still buy a house for around $1.2 million. Morningside next door is sitting at $1.31 million. Cannon Hill is at $1.52 million.

Most buyers rule Murarrie out because the suburb profile says "industrial." They never drive through the residential pocket in the south-west to find out what that label actually means on the ground.

The industrial precinct in Murarrie sits on the northern edge along the riverfront and the eastern portions around Queensport Road. The residential area is a different part of the suburb entirely. Different streets, different character, different atmosphere. Buyers who have been through it know. The ones who filter it out on a search result never do.

The market data tells an interesting story. Houses in Murarrie are averaging 16 days on market. Vendor discounting is around -4.5 per cent, which means sellers are achieving close to their asking price. Annual house price growth is 4.35 per cent, and the unit market has moved at 22.6 per cent in the same period.

The gap between Murarrie and its neighbours is a perception discount, not a market one. Sellers in Murarrie are in a better position than many of them realise.
