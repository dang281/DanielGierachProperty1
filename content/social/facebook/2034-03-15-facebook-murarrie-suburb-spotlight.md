## Caption

Murarrie's location is its clearest selling point for buyers and its clearest driver of demand for vendors. The Gateway Motorway interchange means residents can reach the CBD, the airport, and the northern suburbs without navigating through inner-city traffic. For households where one or both occupants commute or travel regularly, that practical advantage has a daily dollar value.

The residential pocket of Murarrie sits adjacent to the industrial precinct and newer housing estates have continued to fill in over recent years. The result is a suburb with a range of housing types, from post-war originals through to contemporary builds, that attracts a wider buyer profile than a uniform suburb of the same age. First-home buyers who need motorway access and genuine value in the inner east, families upgrading from units in Cannon Hill or Morningside, and investors targeting rental demand from the industrial and airport corridor all find Murarrie workable. The Cannon Hill Shopping Centre and the services along Wynnum Road provide everyday amenity without requiring significant travel. The suburb lacks the restaurant strip of Morningside or the character precinct of Hawthorne, but it compensates with access and price.

Stock volumes in Murarrie are relatively modest, and buyer interest has been building as affordability pressure on the inner east has pushed buyers further along the Wynnum Road corridor.
