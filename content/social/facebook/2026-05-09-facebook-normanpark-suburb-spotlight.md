## Caption

Norman Park sits 4km from Brisbane's CBD with a median house price of around $1.7 million and annual capital growth of approximately 11.5 per cent.

The suburb draws a very specific buyer: families who want the Norman Park State School catchment, the elevated blocks with river and city views, and the established character streetscape. Around 70 per cent of households here have children. That is not coincidence. It reflects deliberate decisions by a buyer type that anchors to location fundamentals, not market cycles.

What that means for sellers is that Norman Park's demand is not simply tied to the broader Brisbane market. It is also driven by school intake timing and the genuine scarcity of well-positioned homes on elevated allotments. The river views and the CBD proximity do not change with the cash rate.

In the current environment, buyers are taking longer to move through inspections and more carefully considering comparable sales. That is a healthier market than 2021, not a worse one. Buyers who arrive at an inspection in Norman Park today have usually been searching for a while and know what they want.
