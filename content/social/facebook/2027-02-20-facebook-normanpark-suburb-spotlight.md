## Caption

Norman Park is a suburb where the secondary school catchment alone moves the market. Whites Hill State College draws families from across the inner east, and the streets within the Norman Park portion of that catchment are consistently among the most sought-after in the southern inner east.

The suburb occupies elevated terrain south of the Clem Jones Motorway, with the better streets sitting above the surrounding topography and returning city views from the upper floors of homes that have been built or renovated to take advantage of the position. Whites Hill Reserve sits at the eastern edge of the suburb, a large bushland reserve that adds a genuine lifestyle element: mountain biking tracks, bushwalking, and green space that is genuinely used by the community.

The housing stock is predominantly character Queenslanders and post-war timber homes, with a meaningful proportion of architecturally renovated properties in the $1.8M to $2.8M range. Norman Park is not a suburb where buyers find value relative to the inner east. It is a suburb where buyers are willing to pay for the catchment, the elevation, and the lifestyle.

The buyer who targets Norman Park has typically done their research. They know the streets, they know the school, and they are competing with others who have arrived at the same conclusion. Auction clearance rates in Norman Park reflect that depth.
