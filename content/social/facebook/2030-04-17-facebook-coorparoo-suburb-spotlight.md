## Caption

Coorparoo is one of the inner east suburbs where the market consistently surprises buyers who have been watching from a distance. The depth of buyer demand here, particularly for well-positioned character homes in the $1M to $1.6M range, is stronger than its profile might suggest.

The CBD proximity is the foundational driver. Coorparoo to the CBD is a 10-minute drive or a direct bus route, which keeps it firmly on the shortlist for professionals who are not prepared to trade a long commute for affordability. The suburb's café culture along Old Cleveland Road and the surrounding pockets adds a lifestyle dimension that retains residents through life stages.

School catchments in Coorparoo matter. The Coorparoo State School catchment has seen consistent buyer interest, and the access to the secondary school network in the inner east makes the suburb work for families who want to stay put as children move through school.

What sellers in Coorparoo benefit from is a buyer pool that includes both local upgraders and buyers from adjacent suburbs. Camp Hill buyers who need to move down slightly in price, Norman Park buyers looking for more space, East Brisbane buyers wanting land. That breadth of demand keeps the market relatively resilient.

The winter period in Coorparoo tends to see a modest reduction in volume but not in buyer motivation.
