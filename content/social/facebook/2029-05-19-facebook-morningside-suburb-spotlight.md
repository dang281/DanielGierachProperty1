## Caption

Morningside is one of the most consistent performers in the inner east because it offers almost everything the premium suburbs offer at a price point that a broader buyer pool can reach.

The suburb runs from the Cannon Hill boundary in the east to the edge of Hawthorne in the west, and the character shifts noticeably by street. The blocks closer to Wynnum Road and the Morningside train station attract buyers who want walkability and urban convenience. The streets further from the arterials, particularly those around Norman Street and the Morningside Park area, attract families looking for quieter residential feel without losing the suburb's proximity to everything.

The Morningside State School catchment is one of the factors that drives the family buyer pool in the suburb, and the Morningside Emporium area has become a genuine café and small business precinct that residents use daily rather than occasionally.

For sellers, Morningside is a market where preparation matters significantly. The suburb has enough listing volume at most times of year that buyers have choices. The properties that generate competition are the ones where the campaign positioning is sharp: photography that reflects the lifestyle, a price guide calibrated to comparable sales rather than aspirational outcomes, and a presentation that gives buyers confidence.

If you own in Morningside and are thinking about selling, I am happy to give you an honest view of where the market sits right now.
