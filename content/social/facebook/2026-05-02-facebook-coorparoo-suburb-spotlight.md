## Caption

Coorparoo sits 4km from Brisbane's CBD and has recorded annual house price growth of around 13 per cent. The median house price is approximately $1.64 million.

It offers something that is getting harder to find: inner-city proximity at a price point that is still below Hawthorne and Bulimba by a meaningful margin. Buyers who have been looking at the $2M-plus end of the inner east often land in Coorparoo when they run the numbers. Same commute, same lifestyle amenity, character homes, and a price that serviceability can still support.

That comparison drives a steady stream of motivated buyers to well-positioned Coorparoo properties. Houses especially. And particularly the older character homes that give buyers something they cannot find in a new apartment.

The nuance for sellers right now: Coorparoo's price growth has been stronger than many surrounding suburbs, which means buyer expectations have shifted upward. The buyers currently looking are pre-approved and working from comparable sales data, not aspirational asking prices. Accurate positioning will do more work than an optimistic price guide.

If you own in Coorparoo and want to understand what your property is worth in today's market, I am happy to give you a straight answer.
