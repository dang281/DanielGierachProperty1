## Caption

Balmoral's supply constraint is one of the most consistent features of its property market. The suburb has a finite number of homes and the buyer pool for it is always larger than the number of properties available at any given time.

The riverside streets below Wynnum Road command the most attention. Properties with river views or river access, particularly those along the Norman Creek section and the streets directly east of Bulimba, sit at the top of the inner east price range and attract buyers who have often been searching in the suburb specifically for years. When those properties come to market, the campaigns are almost always competitive.

The streets further from the river, particularly those along the ridge above Riding Road, attract a different but equally loyal buyer profile: families who want the character of the suburb, the school catchment advantages, and the access to Hawthorne and Bulimba's amenity, without the direct river premium.

Balmoral State School is an anchor for the family buyer pool in this suburb. The catchment question is one of the first things most family buyers raise when inspecting in Balmoral.

For sellers, the advantage of listing in Balmoral is the depth of the waiting buyer pool. The challenge is meeting the expectations of buyers who know the suburb intimately and are not easily impressed by anything that is not genuinely well-prepared.

If you own in Balmoral and are considering selling, reach out.
