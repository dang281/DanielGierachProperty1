## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits in the inner south corridor with motorway access, retail convenience, and a university health precinct on its doorstep, a combination that underpins consistent and varied buyer demand across property types.

Westfield Garden City is the primary retail anchor and it is a genuine day-to-day convenience driver for residents. Combine that with proximity to the South East Freeway and Logan Road, and you have a suburb that works practically for a wide range of buyers regardless of whether they commute by car or public transport. The housing stock reflects that broad appeal: original post-war homes on large blocks sit alongside modern townhouses and medium-density developments, giving buyers a real choice in what they are purchasing.

Griffith University's influence extends into the Mt Gravatt property market in a meaningful way. The university hospital, health precinct, and academic population create consistent demand in the surrounding residential streets, with buyers ranging from university staff purchasing long-term homes to professionals working in the health corridor who want to live nearby. That demand adds stability to a suburb that might otherwise be overlooked in favour of its neighbours.

Properties on the eastern ridge of Mt Gravatt with elevated positions hold a clear premium over comparable flat-street homes. Families looking for more space than Camp Hill or Coorparoo offers at a lower price point, and downsizers from larger outer-south properties wanting to be closer to health services and retail, both find what they are looking for here.
