## Caption

Norman Park is one of those suburbs where the supply constraint is structural, not cyclical. The land is built out, the character homes are protected by heritage overlays across much of the suburb, and the people who own here tend to hold for a long time. When something does come to market, the buyer pool assembles quickly.

Whites Hill Reserve is the outdoor anchor of the suburb, offering mountain biking trails and bush walking that residents in the adjacent suburbs actively use. Norman Park State School has one of the stronger community reputations in the inner east, and that catchment address is a specific draw for families choosing between this suburb and Camp Hill or Balmoral next door.

The housing stock is largely pre-war and character-era timber. Queenslanders and early 20th century workers' cottages are what most buyers are after here, and they are willing to pay for land, presentation, and position. Elevated streets above Old Cleveland Road carry a premium for aspect and views, and buyers who have done their research know which pockets to target.

The comparison with Camp Hill and Balmoral is constant. Norman Park often represents a way into this pocket of the inner east at a price point that those suburbs no longer offer. If you own in Norman Park and are thinking about selling, the current market is worth a close look. See what's been transacting at danielgierach.com/suburbs/norman-park
