## Caption

Oxford Street is one of the most reliable lifestyle draws in the inner east. The cafe strip, the restaurants, the Saturday morning rhythm of it. For buyers who want to live somewhere they actually want to spend time, Hawthorne is consistently at the top of the list.

The suburb's street-by-street variation is worth understanding if you own here. Lower streets running toward the river carry a premium that reflects the views and the proximity to the Hawthorne Ferry Terminal. Properties with any northerly river aspect hold that value firmly, because supply of genuinely river-influenced stock in Hawthorne is very limited. Character Queenslanders and post-war homes dominate the streetscape, and buyers pay for the authentic character over the renovated infill product you see in other inner east suburbs.

Turnover here is low. Families who buy in Hawthorne tend to hold because there is very little reason to leave. That low listing frequency means when a property does come to market, the buyer pool has often been waiting. Well-presented homes in good streets see genuine competition.

Hawthorne State School catchment is a consistent pull for families with primary school aged children, and that catchment demand adds a reliable floor under family home pricing.

If you own in Hawthorne and are considering selling, reach out.
