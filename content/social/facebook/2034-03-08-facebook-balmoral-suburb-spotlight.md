## Caption

Balmoral's elevated streets along Riding Road and Lytton Road offer river views on the upper sections, and those views combined with the Bulimba State School catchment and proximity to Oxford Street's village precinct make the suburb one of the most deliberately chosen addresses on Brisbane's inner east.

The housing stock is a mix of character Queenslanders, post-war homes, and more recent builds, with the elevated western pocket of the suburb carrying the highest price points. River views are not a guarantee based on suburb address alone; they depend on street and elevation, and buyers who understand the topography know exactly which streets they are targeting. [VERIFY: current median house price and comparison with Bulimba before posting.]

Buyers in Balmoral are almost exclusively owner-occupiers. Families targeting the Bulimba State School catchment and secondary proximity are the core demographic. Established couples and downsizers who want to maintain a premium lifestyle suburb address without the density of Hawthorne or the price premium of some Bulimba streets also factor into the mix.

Balmoral rarely produces a large volume of listings in any given period. The suburb is deliberately low-turnover, and when well-presented properties come to market, the buyer pool that has been watching tends to respond quickly.
