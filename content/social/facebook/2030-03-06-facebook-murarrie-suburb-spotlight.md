## Caption

Murarrie's property market works a little differently to other inner east suburbs, and sellers here benefit from understanding that difference. The buyer pool includes both investors looking at yield and development potential, and owner-occupiers who want Gateway Motorway access and a price point that's accessible. Knowing which of those buyers your property speaks to is the starting point for a well-positioned campaign.

What's been selling in Murarrie, and what's generated competition, tends to fall into a couple of distinct categories. Residential homes on full blocks in the quieter streets of the suburb, away from the industrial zoning, attract owner-occupiers who want to be close to Morningside without Morningside pricing. Land parcels with development potential attract a different buyer altogether, one who's doing sums rather than imagining their family living there.

The autumn campaign season is as relevant here as anywhere. Investor buyers who have been sitting on the sidelines tend to get more active in Q1 as they finalise their year's acquisition strategy.

Median house prices are around $1.1M. Verify this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before relying on that figure.
