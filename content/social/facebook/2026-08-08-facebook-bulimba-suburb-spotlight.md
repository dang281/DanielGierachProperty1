## Caption

Bulimba operates at a different register from most Brisbane suburbs. Buyers decide on the address before they decide on a specific property, and that changes how it trades.

Oxford Street is the most commercially recognisable strip in the inner east, and the suburb's identity is anchored around it. Good coffee, a direct CityCat connection to the CBD, Queenslander streetscapes on the blocks running back from the river, and a sense of place that buyers who grew up or spent time here find genuinely hard to walk away from. That emotional investment in the address translates into sustained demand that does not soften significantly when the broader market quietens.

The price reflects it. Bulimba has consistently traded above the inner-east median, and the composition of sales has been weighted toward larger, well-renovated homes on blocks that retain development optionality. Buyers in this suburb arrive at open homes already committed. What they are deciding is which property, not whether to buy in Bulimba.

Days on market for well-positioned properties here have consistently run below the inner-east average. That is a reliable indicator of demand depth, not a function of pricing.

For homeowners who bought before the last significant growth cycle, the equity position has moved considerably. The gap between what the market will pay now and what was paid five or eight years ago is material. That position is worth understanding.
