## Caption

Hawthorne has earned its reputation the old-fashioned way. The combination of the Hawthorne State School catchment, river access, the ferry terminal, and two good café strips on Oxford Street and Riding Road means buyers rarely need convincing about why they want to be here. The conversation is about how to get here.

Character Queenslander homes on 400 to 600sqm blocks are the primary product, and competition for them is consistent. Buyers who have been through a few unsuccessful campaigns elsewhere arrive ready to act. That is the dynamic that defines Hawthorne: motivated buyers competing for limited supply in a suburb where turnover is naturally low because people who get here tend to stay.

The buyer profile is broad. Professionals upsizing from Woolloongabba or East Brisbane are looking for the combination of walkability and neighbourhood quality that Hawthorne delivers. Relocating buyers from interstate, particularly Sydney and Melbourne, are drawn by the lifestyle offer and the price difference to comparable inner suburbs in those cities. Both groups are willing to compete.

The ferry terminal at Hawthorne gives residents a genuinely pleasant commute option that bypasses road traffic entirely. That matters to a specific buyer, and it is consistently mentioned in buyer feedback.
