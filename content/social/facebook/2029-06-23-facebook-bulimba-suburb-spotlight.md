## Caption

Bulimba is the suburb that people who have lived in the inner east tend to mention first when they think about where they would live if they could live anywhere. That kind of reputation is earned over decades and it does not move around.

The Oxford Street precinct is the most obvious reason, but the full answer is more layered. River access, the ferry terminal at Bulimba Ferry Terminal, the Colmslie Recreation Reserve, and the fabric of the surrounding residential streets combine to create a neighbourhood feel that is genuinely hard to replicate anywhere else in Brisbane.

The housing stock reflects the suburb's desirability: character Queenslanders and post-war homes on good blocks in the core residential streets, alongside a more recent wave of contemporary homes and townhouses. The premium attached to the Queenslander stock in Bulimba is real and consistent. Buyers who understand renovation and heritage are willing to pay for the character that those homes carry.

For sellers, Bulimba is a market where the premium is real but not automatic. Preparation, presentation, and campaign strategy still determine whether a result is good or exceptional. The buyer pool is strong, but the buyers are experienced and know the suburb well.

I am based in Bulimba and it is the suburb I know better than any other. If you are thinking about a sale and want an honest appraisal, I am here for that conversation.
