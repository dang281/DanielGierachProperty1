## Caption

Mt Gravatt has Logan Road, Hanlon Park, and the QIMR Berghofer Medical Research Institute, and those three things tell you a fair amount about the suburb. It is a place with practical amenity, green space, and a growing knowledge and health precinct nearby. That combination supports steady, consistent demand from owner-occupiers who are not chasing a postcode, they are chasing a suburb that works.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war, with a mix of brick and timber homes on established blocks. The suburb does not have the character home premium of Camp Hill or Hawthorne, which means buyers get genuine square metres and structure at a more accessible price point. Many homes have been extended or updated, and presentation quality across the suburb has lifted noticeably over the past decade. [VERIFY: current median house price before posting.]

Hanlon Park provides a green corridor that residents value for everyday recreation, and the Logan Road strip covers most daily food and retail needs without requiring a trip to the inner suburbs. Families targeting southside school catchments find Mt Gravatt practical, and the proximity to Griffith University and the broader health precinct around Princess Alexandra Hospital and Greenslopes makes it a logical base for workers in that corridor.

This is not a suburb with high turnover; when homes come to market, the buyer pool is typically motivated and informed.
