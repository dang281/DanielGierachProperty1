## Caption

East Brisbane's CBD proximity is its most direct value driver. At roughly two kilometres to the city centre, the suburb delivers the inner city access that many buyers have prioritised above all other criteria, at a price point that sits below the riverside suburbs that share the same proximity band. For buyers who have been watching Kangaroo Point or New Farm and finding the pricing prohibitive, East Brisbane is typically the next address they examine seriously.

The suburb's connectivity is comprehensive. The bus network provides frequent city services, the bikeway network connects to the city and the South Bank precinct, and the walk to South Bank's cultural and recreational infrastructure is practical rather than theoretical. For buyers who are weighing up CBD proximity against lifestyle access, East Brisbane delivers both from the one address.

The Mowbray Park foreshore gives East Brisbane a public river space that residents can access without needing to travel to a different suburb. The combination of river frontage at the park level and the elevated residential streets above creates a geography that buyers who have looked carefully at East Brisbane understand and value.

The housing stock in East Brisbane includes a meaningful proportion of character homes that have been well-maintained or renovated, alongside more recent construction. Properties that have been upgraded to current living standards sell strongly when they come to market.
