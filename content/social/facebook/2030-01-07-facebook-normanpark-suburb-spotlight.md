## Caption

Norman Park is the kind of suburb where many families arrive and simply don't leave. Whites Hill State College catchment is the most cited reason buyers stretch their budget to get here, and that school connection has been a consistent price driver for years. But it's not only the school. Large blocks, character Queenslanders, and direct access to Whites Hill Reserve make for a suburb that genuinely delivers on quality of life.

The buyer profile is almost exclusively families, many with children approaching school age. They tend to be well researched, willing to pay for the right home in the right street, and prepared to compete when supply is tight. And supply in Norman Park is consistently tight. The suburb has low turnover compared to its neighbours, which means well-positioned homes generate real competition when they do come to market.

Median house prices sit around $1.5M, though this figure should be checked against current CoreLogic or PropTrack data. The Camp Hill shopping strip is close by, adding day-to-day convenience alongside the lifestyle appeal.

Generational ownership is common here. Many homeowners have held for decades and are only now starting to think about what comes next.
