## Caption

Seven Hills occupies an interesting market position heading into spring: it offers the elevated outlooks and hillside character of Camp Hill at an entry point that remains genuinely competitive for the inner east. Buyers who have pushed their search budget to the limit in Camp Hill or Coorparoo find that Seven Hills delivers comparable lifestyle elements at a more achievable price.

The suburb's ridge streets have views across suburban Brisbane that buyers who discover them rarely expect. Homes on the high side of Whites Hill Road and the streets running toward Norman Park attract consistent interest from buyers who want outlook without paying for it explicitly.

Spring campaigns in Seven Hills tend to run well when sellers present accurately and price at fair market value. The buyer pool is not as deep as Hawthorne or Bulimba, which means overpricing at launch costs time more than in the suburb's larger neighbours. A well-priced home here, however, finds its buyer relatively quickly.

If you own in Seven Hills and are weighing whether this spring is the right time to sell, a current appraisal is the clearest way to understand the numbers.
