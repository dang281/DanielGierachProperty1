## Caption

Holland Park's elevation is one of its more underappreciated features. Parts of the suburb sit high enough to deliver district views, and the breezes that come with that position make a real difference to liveability in a Brisbane summer. For buyers who know the inner south well, the suburb's topography is part of what they're factoring in.

The housing stock is primarily 1950s and 60s homes on decent blocks, many of which haven't been significantly updated. That suits the renovation buyer profile that's been active across the inner south for several years. Buyers looking for a suburb where they can buy at a lower price point, invest in improvement, and hold a property in a solid location have found Holland Park to be a practical choice.

Holland Park State School and Marshall Road School catchments provide some of the school draw, and the suburb is more affordable than Camp Hill or Coorparoo while sharing reasonable proximity to both. That value gap continues to attract buyers priced out of those suburbs.

Median house prices are around $1.35M at the time of writing. Verify this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before using it in any decision.
