## Caption

Cannon Hill is one of those suburbs that keeps making sense for buyers no matter what the market is doing. The Gateway Motorway access alone makes it a practical choice for families who need to move across Brisbane without sitting in tunnel traffic, and the Cannon Hill station on the Doomben line puts the CBD within reach without a car.

The housing stock tells the story of the suburb well. Postwar character homes on decent-sized blocks sit alongside newer builds, giving buyers a choice between a renovation project and a move-in-ready house. That mix appeals to families upgrading out of smaller inner-city properties who want a proper backyard and a second bathroom without jumping to the far suburbs.

School catchment is a real driver here. Families researching the area quickly land on the local state school options and the proximity to St Oliver Plunkett Primary, which draws Catholic school buyers who might otherwise be looking further east.

The Cannon Hill Homebase precinct handles the practical side of life well enough. It is not the most glamorous shopping experience, but it means residents are not driving to Carindale for every hardware run or large format retail need.

Median house prices in Cannon Hill sit at approximately $1.05 million. ⚠️ VERIFY before posting. Land values have been climbing steadily as buyers recognise the suburb offers genuine inner-east proximity without the Camp Hill or Bulimba premium.
