## Caption

Cannon Hill is one of those inner east suburbs where supply consistently lags demand, and that dynamic has driven some of the strongest median price growth in the eastern corridor over the past several years.

The suburb's appeal is straightforward. Post-war and early brick homes on blocks between 500 and 700 square metres, close enough to the CBD and the river to matter, with a suburb feel that is quieter and more residential than Morningside or Norman Park. The Cannon Hill Angus and Taylor parklands give families genuine open space within the suburb boundary without having to drive elsewhere.

The Cannon Hill State School and the bus connections along Wynnum Road are the practical infrastructure that keep family buyers focused on this suburb year after year. School catchment conversations come up at almost every Cannon Hill open home.

For sellers, the supply constraint in Cannon Hill is a genuine advantage. When a well-prepared home comes to market here, the buyers who have been waiting months for something to appear tend to respond quickly. Campaigns that generate competition in Cannon Hill are common when the property is right and the preparation has been done.
