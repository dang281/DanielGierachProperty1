## Caption

Mt Gravatt has the kind of consistency that makes it a reliable suburb to sell in. Logan Road provides the commercial spine, Hanlon Park gives residents a genuine green space in the middle of the suburb, and QIMR Berghofer Medical Research Institute anchors a significant employment presence that brings a particular type of buyer to the area. Demand from owner-occupiers here is not trend-driven. It is steady.

The housing stock is mostly post-war brick and weatherboard, with blocks that make renovation worthwhile. Buyers who have been priced out of the inner south find Mt Gravatt a credible step up from the outer ring. The suburb has enough amenity to stand on its own without needing to borrow proximity from a trendier address.

Families are the dominant buyer group. Good school options, manageable commute times to the CBD via Logan Road, and reasonable land content relative to price are the consistent drawcards. The upgrade cycle is active, with buyers from Holland Park East and Macgregor often making Mt Gravatt their next step.
