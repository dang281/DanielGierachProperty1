## Caption

Murarrie's spring and summer market tends to attract a buyer profile that is deliberately focused. The suburb's combination of larger allotments, newer residential pockets along the river corridor, and strong employment access via the Gateway Motorway and Metroplex employment precinct appeals to buyers who prioritise practical liveability over café strip proximity.

The Murarrie riverside precinct has continued to attract buyers who want water views and proximity to the Bulimba ferry corridor without paying Bulimba prices. The newer townhouses and apartments along the river have brought a different buyer demographic into the suburb, which has expanded the overall demand pool.

Rental demand in Murarrie is consistently supported by the Gateway and airport-adjacent employment clusters. Vacancy rates have remained low across market cycles, which is a meaningful consideration for investors assessing the holding cost of a suburban investment.

For owner-occupiers in Murarrie who are thinking about a sale, the combination of strong land content, river access, and motorway connectivity is a story that is worth presenting well to buyers who may not know the suburb in detail. danielgierach.com/suburbs/murarrie is where to start that conversation.
