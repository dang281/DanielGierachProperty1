## Caption

Coorparoo sits at the junction of Cavendish Road and Old Cleveland Road, about 5km from the CBD, and the practical question buyers ask is: what do I get for the price? The answer is Cavendish Road's restaurant and cafe strip on the doorstep, Coorparoo State School for primary-aged families, secondary school options including Cavendish Road State High within the catchment, and a housing market that offers genuine variety across character homes and newer townhouses.

That variety is one of Coorparoo's structural advantages. A suburb with a single housing type tends to produce a buyer pool of similar depth. Coorparoo attracts young families looking at houses, downsizers looking at townhouses, investors looking at units, and upsizers looking at the larger character Queenslanders. When you have multiple buyer groups active at the same time, vendor conditions tend to be more competitive across different property types simultaneously. The character homes in the quieter streets off Cavendish Road are the most keenly contested. Block sizes on those streets are workable, and the combination of character and location draws buyers from Camp Hill and Norman Park who have been outpriced elsewhere.
