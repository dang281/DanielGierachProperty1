## Caption

Three kilometres from the CBD, walkable to West End and South Bank, and bookended by the Story Bridge and the Kangaroo Point Cliffs. East Brisbane doesn't need to make a case for itself. The location does that work without any help.

What makes this suburb interesting from a sales perspective is the supply picture. Lots are small, heritage overlays apply to much of the pre-war character housing stock, and genuine turnover is low. When a well-maintained Queenslander or pre-war timber home does come to market on a street like Linton or Morley, competition among buyers is real and active. Owner-occupiers dominate the buyer pool here. They are buying a lifestyle as much as a home, and they price that accordingly.

The walkability score is a genuine drawcard. Residents can reach the Kangaroo Point Cliffs for a morning run, cross the river to South Bank for dinner, and commute to the CBD without a car. That kind of daily convenience commands a premium in Brisbane and the numbers in East Brisbane reflect it consistently.
