## Caption

Carina Heights has a reputation as a suburb where buyers arrive for the price and stay for the lifestyle. It offers something genuinely scarce in Brisbane's inner east: larger land allotments at a price point below the premium suburbs, within reasonable commuting distance of the CBD.

The suburb sits on the western edge of Carindale, with proximity to the Westfield Carindale shopping centre providing retail and amenity access that buyers from more suburban outer areas expect but do not always find in established inner suburbs. That combination of inner-ring proximity and outer-ring amenity is part of what drives the suburb's consistent demand.

The housing stock is primarily post-war brick and timber homes on blocks that are generally larger than those available in the inner east suburbs closer to the river. Many have been extended or renovated, and the streets around the Carina Heights State School catchment attract particularly consistent family buyer activity.

The buyer profile is heavily family-oriented. Buyers here are typically looking for their long-term home: a property they can grow into, with space for children and outdoor living. The outdoor orientation of Brisbane property culture is well-served by the block sizes available in Carina Heights, which is a genuine selling point against higher-priced inner suburbs where 400 square metre lots are standard.
