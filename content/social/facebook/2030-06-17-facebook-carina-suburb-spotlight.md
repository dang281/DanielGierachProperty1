## Caption

Carina's winter market reflects the suburb's broader dynamic: buyers who are active in June and July here tend to be families with a specific school or access requirement, and investors who have done their numbers and identified the suburb as a sensible medium-term hold.

The Westfield Carindale effect is felt most clearly in winter. Buyers who prioritise practical daily convenience over lifestyle premium tend to be active year-round, and the Carindale retail hub makes Carina a functional choice that does not require the same seasonal motivation as a lifestyle suburb.

The housing stock in Carina continues to attract attention from buyers who are looking for the right block at the right price. The 600-square-metre-plus blocks in the better streets of Carina are increasingly valued as building costs and land scarcity make the combination of land size and price point harder to find closer to the city.

The rental market in Carina is supported by a consistent professional and family tenant profile. Properties that are well-maintained and in good condition generally achieve tenancy at competitive market rents, which supports the investor case for the suburb even when borrowing costs are higher than they have been historically.

For sellers in Carina, the mid-year period is worth taking seriously as a campaign window. The right property, correctly positioned, will find motivated buyers.
