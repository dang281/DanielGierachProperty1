## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits at a practical intersection in Brisbane's property market: close enough to the CBD to be convenient, high enough to have views, and priced at a level that remains accessible for buyers who have been priced out of the inner suburbs to the north.

The Mt Gravatt escarpment is the suburb's defining physical feature. The streets that step up toward the escarpment reward buyers with elevated blocks and northerly outlooks that are uncommon at the price point. The Mt Gravatt Conservation Reserve sits adjacent to the residential streets and gives the suburb a green buffer that adds lifestyle value without adding to property costs.

Garden City Shopping Centre at Upper Mt Gravatt provides a major retail and services hub, and the concentration of commercial activity along Kessels Road and Logan Road gives the suburb strong day-to-day convenience. For buyers who place practical amenity alongside lifestyle, Mt Gravatt ticks both boxes.

The buyer profile includes a mix of first-time upgraders, families seeking larger blocks, and investors drawn to the rental demand from Griffith University and the broader Southside health and education precinct. That diversity of demand keeps the market relatively balanced across different market conditions.

The Pacific Motorway and the South East Busway connection to the CBD complete a transport picture that makes Mt Gravatt accessible for a wide range of buyers.
