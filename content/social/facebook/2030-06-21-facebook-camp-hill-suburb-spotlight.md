## Caption

Camp Hill's winter buyer profile is one of the most predictable in the inner east: families with children in the upper primary school years who are positioning for the Whites Hill State College catchment. These buyers do not wait for spring. They have a window defined by school enrolment timelines, and if the right property appears in June or July, they will make an offer.

That pattern creates a specific dynamic for sellers in Camp Hill who are considering a winter campaign. The total number of buyer groups through the door will be smaller than in April, but the buyer pool contains a high proportion of people who are genuinely ready to transact. The seller who understands this tends to price more confidently and hold for the result rather than dropping the guide at the first sign of a quieter open home.

The streets in Camp Hill that attract the most consistent buyer attention are those north of Old Cleveland Road, particularly the blocks that offer 600 square metres or more and fall clearly within the school catchment. These streets have produced some of the suburb's strongest comparable sales over the past several years and continue to set the benchmark.

For homeowners in Camp Hill who have been considering selling but have held off waiting for "the right time," the right time in this suburb is more frequently defined by buyer motivation than by calendar.
