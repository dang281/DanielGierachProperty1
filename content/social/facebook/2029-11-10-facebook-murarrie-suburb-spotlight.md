## Caption

Murarrie is not the flashiest suburb in the inner east, and it has never pretended to be. What it offers instead is genuine accessibility, real land, and price growth that reflects what happens when buyers run out of options in the suburbs closer in.

Sitting approximately 8km from the CBD, Murarrie has direct Gateway Motorway access and is close enough to Cannon Hill for residents to use that shopping precinct without it feeling like a trip. The residential pockets around Murarrie Recreation Reserve are family-oriented, with a neighbourhood feel that is distinct from the industrial and commercial land that occupies other parts of the suburb.

The housing stock skews newer. Post-2000 houses and dual-occupancy properties make up a significant share of what sells, and that appeals to investors looking for low-maintenance yield and young families who want a ready-to-live-in home rather than a renovation project.

The transition story here is real. As buyers get pushed out of Cannon Hill, Morningside, and Carina Heights, Murarrie becomes the logical next look. That pattern of price growth driven by overflow from tighter suburbs is well established in Brisbane's inner east, and Murarrie is in the middle of it.
