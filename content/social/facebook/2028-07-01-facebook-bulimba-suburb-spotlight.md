## Caption

Bulimba is the kind of suburb that residents tend to stay in once they arrive. The Oxford Street village strip, the direct ferry access to the CBD, the river parks along Oxlade Drive, and the combination of character homes and newer townhouses give it a quality of life that is genuinely difficult to replicate elsewhere in Brisbane's inner east.

The buyer pool for Bulimba divides clearly into two groups: established families buying the character Queenslanders and larger post-war homes close to Oxford Street and the school catchments, and professionals and couples drawn to the newer townhouse and apartment stock. Both groups are consistent and financially strong, which keeps demand underpinned at almost every price point.

Bulimba State School and the proximity to Anglican Church Grammar School and Our Lady of Victories drive significant family buyer interest. Properties inside the Bulimba State School catchment boundary regularly attract premiums over those just outside it.

The river-facing streets, particularly Oxlade Drive and Riverview Terrace, trade in a separate market from the broader suburb. Well-positioned character homes there move quickly when they come to market, often drawing interstate buyer interest from buyers comparing Brisbane river values with Sydney equivalents.

Median house prices sit in the upper end of the inner east range, with quality character properties on good blocks consistently transacting well above suburb median. The stock is genuinely limited, and the waiting buyer pool runs deep.
