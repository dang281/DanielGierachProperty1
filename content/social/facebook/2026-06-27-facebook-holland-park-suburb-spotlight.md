## Caption

Holland Park is one of those suburbs where the lifestyle case is easy to make and the price data backs it up.

Eight kilometres from the CBD, on the ridge that connects the inner south to the Whites Hill Reserve bushland, it offers the kind of block sizes that have largely disappeared from suburbs closer to the city. Post-war homes on 600 to 700 square metre lots are common, and the buyer profile reflects that: families who want space, schools within walking distance, and the lifestyle access that Whites Hill and Toohey Forest provide.

The suburb sits in the catchment for Holland Park State High School and within reach of several strong primary options. For families, those school catchments drive genuine premiums. A home on the right side of the catchment boundary commands more interest at open homes than an almost identical property two streets over.

Rental demand has been strong, with rents moving sharply over the past year as supply stays constrained. For owners considering whether to stay or sell, the current market conditions favour vendors who are ready to present well and price honestly.

If you're thinking about selling in Holland Park, a conversation about current comparable sales takes about 30 minutes.
