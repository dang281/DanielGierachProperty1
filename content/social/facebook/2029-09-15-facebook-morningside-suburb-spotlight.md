## Caption

Morningside runs along Wynnum Road with a post-war and Queenslander character that holds its integrity better than most suburbs at a comparable distance from the CBD. The streets north of Wynnum Road, in particular, carry that distinct character precinct quality that buyers who have been in the inner east for any length of time recognise immediately.

Bulimba and Cannon Hill are both adjacent, and the buyer profile here includes consistent upgrade enquiry from both directions. Families who have grown into the suburb from Cannon Hill and buyers stepping back from Bulimba prices both find Morningside sits in a position that makes sense. The Gateway Motorway access adds practical connectivity for those who need it, without the suburb feeling like it is defined by it.

The Lores Bonney Riverwalk along the river boundary is a quiet local asset, Morningside State School and St Oliver Plunkett both service the catchment, and the ongoing redevelopment activity around the former Morningside Raceway site is gradually adding a new residential dimension to the suburb's eastern edge.

For sellers, Morningside offers a depth of buyer enquiry that comes from its position between stronger-performing neighbours. That adjacency works in your favour when the right property comes to market.
