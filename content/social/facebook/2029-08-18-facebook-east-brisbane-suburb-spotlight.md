## Caption

East Brisbane is one of the closest suburbs to the CBD with a residential street character that still feels like a neighbourhood. The Kangaroo Point cliffs and the river sit on the eastern boundary, the Gabba precinct is on the western edge, and the Citycat and ferry network connects the suburb to the rest of the city without a car.

The property mix here spans original character homes on tight blocks, apartments, and townhouses, with the owner-occupier and investor demand running side by side. South Brisbane and Woolloongabba are walkable, which puts residents within range of the dining, retail, and entertainment precincts that buyers in this price range consider essential. The Olympic 2032 infrastructure activity in the adjacent Woolloongabba precinct has reinforced that urban energy.

For families in the suburb's residential pockets, Coorparoo State School catchment provides the school pathway. Character homes in the quieter streets away from main roads hold their value well and attract owner-occupiers who want inner-city proximity without apartment living.

The urban character of East Brisbane is not for every buyer, but for those it suits, there is nowhere else in the inner east that gives you this level of CBD proximity with genuine street presence. That specificity of buyer appeal is a strength at selling time.
