## Caption

Cannon Hill sits at the edge of where inner east becomes inner southeast, and that positioning gives it a practical appeal that a number of surrounding suburbs cannot match. The Cannon Hill Plaza retail precinct, the Cannon Hill Anglican College catchment, and direct access to the Inner City Bypass make it attractive for buyers who want the inner-east lifestyle at a more competitive entry price than Bulimba or Hawthorne.

The suburb has historically attracted buyers relocating from outer suburbs who want to reduce commute times without paying Morningside or Hawthorne prices. That positioning has held even as values across the inner east have grown, because the relative difference in price point has remained. Investors also find Cannon Hill attractive: the rental pool from nearby commercial and retail employment keeps vacancy rates consistent.

Spring is typically when Cannon Hill sees its deepest buyer activity. Open homes that attract multiple groups are common in October, and sellers who are well-prepared going into spring tend to benefit from that competition.

A current appraisal puts the numbers on the table before you decide whether spring is the right time to move.
