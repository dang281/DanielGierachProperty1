## Caption

Seven Hills does not always appear at the top of inner east shortlists, but buyers who know it well tend to stay there. The suburb offers what is increasingly rare in the inner east: reasonably sized blocks, strong elevation in parts with genuine views, and a price point that still makes sense relative to the coastal corridor to the east.

The topography of Seven Hills is its most underappreciated characteristic. The eastern ridgeline gives some streets a Brisbane-city-lights outlook that buyers in neighbouring suburbs cannot access at any price. Properties with that outlook and a good floorplan attract a specific buyer who is not browsing broadly; they have identified the street and they are waiting for the right property.

The access story is also strong. Lytton Road connects Seven Hills east and west, and the suburb sits within reasonable reach of the Gateway Motorway, making it attractive for buyers who need flexibility in their commute direction.

The buyer pool here is a mix of families attracted by the land size and relative affordability compared to Morningside and Carina, investors looking for long-term capital growth in an underserved suburb, and buyers from the eastern suburbs who want to stay close to family and lifestyle connections without paying Balmoral prices.

Seven Hills is a suburb that rewards buyers who are willing to look past the headlines and do the street-by-street work.
