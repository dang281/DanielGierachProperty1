## Caption

Oxford Street is the public-facing version of Bulimba, and it earns its reputation. The cafes, restaurants, and independent businesses along this strip create the kind of weekday and weekend rhythm that residents build their lives around. The CityCat and ferry service to the CBD adds a commute option that most Brisbane suburbs cannot offer. But the suburb's market appeal goes deeper than lifestyle assets on a list.

Bulimba State School is among the most sought-after state primary catchments in the inner east, and its presence anchors a significant share of the family buyer pool. Federation and Californian bungalow character homes dominate the older housing stock, and in the streets closest to the river, the land values reflect a premium that has been consistent for well over a decade. Owner-occupiers control this market. Investors are present but they are not setting the price. The buyers who pay the highest prices here are doing so because they want to live there and plan to stay.

The supply picture is tight. Bulimba does not turn over at a high rate, and when well-maintained homes on full blocks come to market in streets like Devon, Denman, or Stratford, the buyer competition reflects that scarcity.

If you own in Bulimba and are weighing up whether now is the right time to sell, the market data tells a consistent story. See recent sales at danielgierach.com/suburbs/bulimba
