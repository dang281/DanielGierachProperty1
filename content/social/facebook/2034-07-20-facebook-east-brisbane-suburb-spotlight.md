## Caption

East Brisbane is the closest of the inner east suburbs to the CBD, and the market here reflects that proximity. The suburb is compact, the housing stock is dense by inner east standards, and buyers come here with a specific brief: walkable to the city, access to Kangaroo Point amenity and the Woolloongabba precinct, and a character home or solid post-war property within a manageable budget.

The Stanley Street and Mowbray Terrace corridor provides the suburb's lifestyle anchor. The Gabba is a five-minute walk. The CBD is a similar distance. For buyers who are reducing their commute or moving from an inner-city apartment into a house, East Brisbane is usually the first suburb they look at seriously.

The supply in East Brisbane is genuinely constrained. The suburb is largely built out, which means when properties come to market they meet a buyer pool that has been waiting. Days on market tend to be shorter here than most inner east suburbs when properties are well-presented and priced at market.
