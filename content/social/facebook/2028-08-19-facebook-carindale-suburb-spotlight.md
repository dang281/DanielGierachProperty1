## Caption

Carindale anchors the eastern end of Brisbane's inner east market. It sits around the Westfield Carindale centre, which provides a retail and amenity hub that draws buyers from across the eastern suburbs and gives the suburb an accessibility and convenience profile that many inner-ring suburbs cannot match.

The housing stock is predominantly brick, with a mix of post-war homes and more recent construction. The suburb has large allotments by inner east standards, and the streets around Carindale's school catchments, particularly those close to Citipointe Christian College and Glenala State High School, attract consistent family demand.

Buyers in Carindale are primarily families who have come from outer eastern suburbs looking for a step up in property quality without moving to the higher-priced inner suburbs. They understand the commute trade-off, they value the larger blocks, and they are drawn to the community and school infrastructure that makes Carindale work as a long-term family base.

The suburb is also popular with buyers who want the Westfield convenience for day-to-day living. For households with children, the combination of a major retail centre, multiple school options, and larger-than-average land sizes provides practical advantages that command consistent demand.

Days on market for well-presented Carindale homes are competitive. The suburb's broad buyer appeal keeps enquiry volumes healthy through most market conditions.
