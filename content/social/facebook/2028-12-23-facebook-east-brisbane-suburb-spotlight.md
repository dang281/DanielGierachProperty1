## Caption

East Brisbane is one of the few inner east suburbs where the buyer who wants to walk to South Bank, the Gabba, or the CBD fringe has a genuine option in a house rather than an apartment.

The suburb sits less than three kilometres from the CBD on the south bank of the river, and its residential character is distinct: a mix of elevated post-war and older character homes, many on compact blocks that reflect the inner-urban density of the precinct, alongside a growing number of townhouse developments in the lower-lying areas. The buyer who comes to East Brisbane is usually choosing proximity over land size.

The streets on the higher ground, particularly those with a city skyline outlook or river glimpses, consistently deliver the suburb's strongest results. These properties attract a buyer profile that is specifically inner-urban in its orientation: professionals and couples who want to walk or cycle to work, the cultural precinct, and the Woolloongabba dining strip.

The Gabba redevelopment for the 2032 Olympics has added a conversation to East Brisbane's property market that did not exist five years ago. The precinct transformation, and the upgraded transport and pedestrian connections that come with it, are relevant to buyers who are thinking about what the suburb looks like in five years as well as what it looks like today.
