## Caption

Camp Hill's position in the inner east market is well understood by buyers who have been watching for any length of time: a suburb where the school catchment premium is real, the block sizes are genuine, and the character home stock consistently attracts competitive buyer interest.

The streets that fall inside the Whites Hill State College catchment boundary are among the most closely watched in the inner east. Families who have done the research on that school and understand what it means for their child's education are prepared to compete on price in a way that moves final results above the comparable sales average.

The winter period in Camp Hill follows a predictable pattern. Volume drops from the autumn peak, but the buyers who are active in May and June are disproportionately motivated by the school catchment. For those buyers, timing is less flexible than it might be for a buyer without a schooling deadline driving their decision.

Old Cleveland Road gives Camp Hill its commercial spine: cafes, restaurants, and local retail that support a daily lifestyle without requiring a car for every errand. The suburb's elevation provides cross-breezes and quiet residential streets that buyers describe as one of its most underrated features.

For sellers in Camp Hill, a well-prepared winter campaign in a suburb with limited supply can generate results that rival autumn.
