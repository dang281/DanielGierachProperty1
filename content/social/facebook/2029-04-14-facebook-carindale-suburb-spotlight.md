## Caption

Carindale is one of those suburbs that consistently surprises buyers who have only heard the retail centre reference and not looked at the residential streets.

The housing stock is predominantly 1980s to 1990s brick construction on generous blocks, with a strong representation of the classic Queensland low-set design. For buyers looking for established homes on land larger than 600 square metres with minimal compromise on presentation, Carindale offers that without the premium attached to the closer-in postcodes.

The suburb's eastern position relative to the CBD means it sits in a different price bracket to equivalent properties in Camp Hill or Coorparoo. That gap has attracted buyers priced out of those suburbs who are willing to add 10 to 15 minutes to the commute in exchange for more land and a quieter street environment.

The freeway access at Creek Road and the Westfield Carindale precinct give the suburb a practical self-sufficiency that families specifically seek out. Most of what a family needs on a weekly basis is within a short drive.

The buyer pool in Carindale is predominantly upgrader families and investors looking for long-hold residential positions with strong rental demand. Days on market for well-presented properties has remained steady because the buyer pool, while not enormous, is consistent.
