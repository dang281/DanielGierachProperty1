## Caption

Morningside's spring and autumn seasons consistently produce strong results, and this February the conditions look familiar. Buyer demand is steady, stock levels are modest, and the profile of buyers who want this suburb is clear: professionals and families who've been watching the inner east market long enough to know that Morningside offers something genuinely different at a price point below Bulimba and Balmoral.

What's selling here right now is the product that always sells: well-positioned Queenslanders with character retained, extensions done properly, and land content that gives families room. The closer to New Farm prices that Morningside has shifted, the more those characteristics matter in separating the top-end results from the middle-of-the-road ones.

Supply remains the controlling factor. Morningside doesn't produce many listings at any given time, and the homes that are well-prepared and campaign-ready tend to attract multiple buyers within the first two weeks on market. That's a seller's dynamic, and it's held up through different rate environments.

Median house prices are around $1.55M, though confirm current figures with CoreLogic or PropTrack before relying on that.

If you own in Morningside and are considering the autumn window, getting an appraisal done now gives you time to prepare properly rather than rushing a campaign.
