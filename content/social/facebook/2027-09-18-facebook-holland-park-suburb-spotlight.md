## Caption

Holland Park is a suburb where the land goes further than it does in most comparable parts of the inner south, and that practical advantage is what quietly drives its buyer appeal.

Sitting between Coorparoo and Tarragindi, the suburb is predominantly post-war housing stock: low-set brick homes from the 1950s and 1960s on generous blocks, many of which have been renovated or are well-suited for it. Buyers who come here are often comparing what Holland Park offers against what the same budget buys in Camp Hill or Coorparoo, and they find they get more land, more space, and a quieter street character without sacrificing inner-south access.

Holland Park State High School is a large secondary school in the suburb and its presence shapes the buyer demographic noticeably. Families with school-age children are a consistent part of the demand picture, and the suburb's proximity to both the M1 and Pacific Motorway corridors adds practical appeal for buyers who commute regularly. Bus services along Logan Road and Holland Road provide reasonable frequency to the CBD for those who prefer public transport.

The renovation potential across much of the housing stock is genuine. Original homes on good blocks with north-facing aspects are the type of property that attracts multiple parties at auction, particularly buyers who want to create something rather than pay a premium for someone else's finished product.
