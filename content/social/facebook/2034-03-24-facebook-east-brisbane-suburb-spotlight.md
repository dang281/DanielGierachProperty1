## Caption

East Brisbane is 3km from the CBD, but the more relevant measure for most buyers is what they can do without getting in a car. The Story Bridge puts the northern suburbs in reach on foot or by bike. South Bank is a short walk along the river. New Farm and the Merthyr Road village are accessible without a commute. For buyers who value that kind of proximity, East Brisbane has a compelling case that does not depend on any single amenity.

The housing stock is predominantly heritage character homes, workers cottages, and pre-war Queenslanders on compact lots. These properties are not interchangeable with post-war brick homes on larger suburban blocks, and buyers who want them are not typically comparison-shopping against Carindale or Holland Park. They are comparing East Brisbane to Kangaroo Point, New Farm, and South Brisbane on price and character, and making a location-specific decision. [VERIFY: current median house price and comparison with Kangaroo Point and New Farm before posting.]

Supply in East Brisbane is structurally constrained. The suburb is fully established, with minimal capacity for new housing. When properties come to market, they draw buyers who have been watching and who understand that waiting for a better option in the same suburb may mean waiting a long time.

Owner-occupier intent is very high. These are homes that people buy and occupy, not renovate and flip or hold for yield. That shapes both the buyer motivation and the quality of competition at sale.
