## Caption

Carina Heights occupies a position in the inner east that its neighbours are starting to price it out of. Sitting between Carina and Camp Hill, the suburb shares much of the same amenity as Camp Hill while carrying a noticeably different price tag. For buyers who've been chasing Camp Hill and finding the competition too intense or the prices too high, Carina Heights is where many of them end up.

The blocks here tend to be larger than what you find closer to the city. The housing stock is predominantly 1960s and 70s brick homes, the kind of properties that appeal to buyers with renovation budgets who want to add value rather than pay for someone else's renovation. That buyer profile has grown in the past few years as the renovation market across the inner east has matured.

Median house prices are around $1.3M at the time of writing, though you should verify this with current CoreLogic or PropTrack data. The gap between Carina Heights and Camp Hill is one of the more consistent value observations in the inner east, and it continues to attract attention from buyers who know the area well.

Supply is modest. The suburb doesn't produce a large number of listings, which means when a good home on a proper block comes up, it tends to draw a focused buyer pool.
