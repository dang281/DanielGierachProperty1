## Caption

Camp Hill consistently ranks among the most competitive markets in Brisbane's inner east for one reason: the buyers who want it have usually already lived nearby and know exactly what they are looking for.

The suburb's elevation gives a large proportion of its housing stock elevated north-facing aspects with views across the city or toward Moreton Bay. The streets north of Old Cleveland Road, particularly those around Whites Hill Avenue and the eastern ridgeline, are the most sought-after in a suburb where almost every street is considered desirable.

The Camp Hill Marketplace precinct on Wynnum Road functions as a genuine neighbourhood hub. The coffee, dining, and retail options there are used daily rather than occasionally by locals, and it is consistently mentioned by buyers as part of the reason they chose to inspect in Camp Hill rather than a comparable suburb.

For families, the catchment access to Coorparoo Secondary College and the local primary school network drives a consistent buyer pool. School catchment due diligence is part of almost every Camp Hill purchase conversation I have.

The supply dynamics in Camp Hill are tight. The suburb does not produce a large volume of listings relative to the number of buyers watching it. When a quality property does come to market, the competition can be significant.
