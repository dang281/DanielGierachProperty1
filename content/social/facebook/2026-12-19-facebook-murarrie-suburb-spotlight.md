## Caption

Murarrie is the suburb in Brisbane's inner east that is most clearly mid-transition, and that transition creates a specific opportunity for buyers and a useful question for current owners.

The suburb sits between Cannon Hill and Hemmant, with Murarrie Road as its spine and a broad mix of older residential housing alongside the industrial and commercial land that has historically defined its western edge. That industrial boundary has been contracting. Rezoning and redevelopment activity has moved progressively east, and the residential character of the suburb has strengthened as a result.

The practical access story here is significant. The Murarrie station on the Cleveland rail line puts residents within 20 minutes of the CBD. The access to the Gateway Motorway and the Airport Link tunnel is as good as anywhere in the inner ring. For buyers who travel for work or have dual-direction commute requirements, Murarrie's location solves a problem that more fashionable suburbs cannot.

The buyer profile is evolving. It used to be primarily investors, drawn by yield relative to price. It is increasingly owner-occupiers who have identified the suburb's trajectory and have a view on where it finishes rather than just where it is.

For owners who have been in Murarrie for some years, the gap between what they paid and what comparable properties are now achieving is one that is worth understanding precisely.
