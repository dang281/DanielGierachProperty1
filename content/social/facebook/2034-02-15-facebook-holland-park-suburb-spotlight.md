## Caption

Holland Park sits along Ipswich Road with bus links to the CBD and walkable access to the Cavendish Road State High catchment, and that combination has kept it on the radar for family buyers for years. It is a suburb that delivers more than its price tag suggests when you look at what is actually on offer.

The housing stock is mainly post-war homes, a proportion of which have been extended or updated over the decades. Block sizes are workable for families, and the suburb is compact enough that daily errands on Ipswich Road or at the nearby Garden City are straightforward. Buyers who have been priced out of Camp Hill or Greenslopes regularly find their way here and discover the value gap is real. [VERIFY: current median house price compared with Camp Hill before posting.]

Who buys in Holland Park? Predominantly families and couples upgrading from units or smaller homes in nearby suburbs. The Cavendish Road State High catchment is a specific driver: buyers who want secondary school access in the southside catchment treat a Holland Park address as practical and cost-effective. Owner-occupier intent is high; this is not a suburb that turns over quickly.

Properties that have had any level of renovation or extension present well and attract strong interest. Unrenovated homes on good blocks also draw attention from buyers who want to add value.
