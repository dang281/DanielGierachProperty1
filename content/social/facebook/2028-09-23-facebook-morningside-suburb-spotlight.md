## Caption

Morningside is one of Brisbane's most internally varied suburbs, and understanding which part of Morningside a property sits in is more important than knowing the suburb name alone.

The western ridgeline streets, particularly those running above Wynnum Road toward the Camp Hill boundary, offer elevated positions with city views and premium pricing to match. These streets attract buyers who want the Camp Hill lifestyle at a step below the Camp Hill price. The flat sections east of Wynnum Road toward Cannon Hill are a different market: larger blocks, more space, and lower prices, appealing to buyers who prioritise land over views.

Morningside State School is the primary family buyer driver for the western section of the suburb. The Morningside train station and the cycling connection along the bikeway network into the CBD are consistent draws for commuter-focused buyers.

The commercial strip on Wynnum Road at the Morningside end has evolved significantly. Cafe and hospitality options have improved, and the suburb now has the village feel it previously lacked. That lifestyle upgrade has supported buyer demand in a way that is not yet fully reflected in the price premium relative to more established strips like Bulimba's Oxford Street.

For sellers on the western ridge, the comparison is Camp Hill and Norman Park. For sellers in the flatter eastern section, the comparison is Cannon Hill and Murarrie. Both segments have their buyer, but they are different buyers.
