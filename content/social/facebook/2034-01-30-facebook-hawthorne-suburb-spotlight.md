## Caption

Hawthorne has one of the highest owner-occupier rates in Brisbane's inner east, and that single data point tells you most of what you need to know about the market. People who buy here plan to stay. They are not flipping, not renting it out, and not treating it as a stepping-stone. They are buying a suburb they intend to live in for a long time. That buyer psychology produces a market where quality is consistently rewarded and where price floors are well-supported.

The Oxford Street (Bulimba end) strip and the Hawthorne ferry terminal both sit within easy reach for residents. Pre-war and post-war character homes make up the bulk of the housing stock, and the school catchment options, including access to the Bulimba State School zone for some streets, are a consistent factor in buyer decisions. The suburb is genuinely compact, which contributes to that tightly-held supply dynamic.

When a home comes to market in Hawthorne, the buyer pool is typically made up of people who know the suburb well and have been looking for a while. Buyers shopping here are not easily redirected to Balmoral or Morningside as alternatives. They have specifically identified Hawthorne and are prepared to pay accordingly.

If you own in Hawthorne and are thinking about your options, the data on comparable sales is worth reviewing carefully before you make any decisions. Start at danielgierach.com/suburbs/hawthorne
