## Caption

Seven Hills sits above Enoggera Creek on the western edge of the inner east, and it regularly surprises buyers who know it only from a map.

The topography works in sellers' favour. The elevated blocks on the ridge streets offer views back toward the city that are rare at this price point, and the scale of the homes reflects decades of owner-occupier investment. Most of the stock here has been lived in and cared for rather than managed as an investment, and that shows in the condition of what comes to market.

The buyer profile runs from young professionals trading out of Coorparoo or Camp Hill, to established families drawn by the St Peters Lutheran College catchment. That school consistently pulls buyers into Seven Hills who would otherwise be looking further east at a meaningfully higher price. The school access story is one of the most reliable demand anchors in any suburb, and Seven Hills has it.

Stock turnover here tends to be low. People who buy in Seven Hills typically stay. When a quality property does come to market, the pool of buyers who have been watching is considerably larger than the number of homes listed at any given time. That imbalance produces competitive conditions even when broader market sentiment is softer.

If you own in Seven Hills and have been watching conditions, the current market is worth understanding before you decide on timing.
