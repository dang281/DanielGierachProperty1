## Caption

Mt Gravatt is one of those suburbs that people keep discovering when they benchmark it against the inner east.

It sits around 9km south of the CBD, which puts it just outside the postcodes most buyers target first. What they find when they look is a suburb with strong transport links via the South East Busway, proximity to Griffith University and the major hospitals precinct, and housing stock that ranges from original post-war homes on generous blocks to renovated properties that compete well against Camp Hill at a lower entry price.

The suburb's commercial spine along Logan Road serves the local community without dominating the residential character. The streets behind the main road are quiet and well-established.

Buyer demand in Mt Gravatt tends to be driven by practicality: people who want proximity to employment corridors and infrastructure without paying inner-suburb prices. That buyer group is consistent and motivated, which is what keeps the market relatively active through the year.

For sellers, Mt Gravatt offers a broader comparable sales pool than some surrounding suburbs, which makes accurate pricing more straightforward.
