## Caption

Mt Gravatt East has a locational advantage that buyers keep coming back to: Greenslopes Private Hospital to the north, Westfield Carindale to the south-east, and Logan Road running through the middle with a restaurant strip that has become one of the more distinctive dining precincts in Brisbane's south side. The Korean community that has shaped Logan Road over decades is embedded in the suburb's identity in a way that adds genuine character.

The housing stock skews older, which means blocks tend to be a decent size, and that is exactly what buyers shopping in this price range are after. A well-positioned home on a good allotment in Mt Gravatt East gives buyers a lot more land than the same spend would in Greenslopes or Coorparoo, and canny buyers know that.

The buyer profile is broad. Healthcare workers at Greenslopes are a consistent source of demand, given the short commute. Families looking for value relative to the inner suburbs are another steady group. Investors also watch this market, though owner-occupiers tend to set the pricing ceiling.
