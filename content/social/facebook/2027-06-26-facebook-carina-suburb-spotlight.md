## Caption

Carina sits between Carindale and Morningside in a way that makes it one of the more sensible conversations for buyers who want inner east access without inner east prices. The suburb is established, family-oriented, and well serviced, and its blocks tend to be larger than what you find closer to the city, which opens up a different kind of conversation for buyers who are thinking longer term.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war brick homes alongside some character renovations, reflecting the gradual lift in buyer expectations that has moved through this part of Brisbane over the past decade. Cavendish Road provides bus access to the city and connects the suburb to Westfield Carindale for daily shopping and errands. That combination of public transport and retail convenience at a price below Morningside and Camp Hill is what positions Carina as a realistic entry point to the broader inner east market.

Carindale State School and Holland Park State High serve the catchment at primary and secondary level, which keeps the family buyer pool consistently engaged. The suburb's blocks are also worth noting in the context of Brisbane's evolving planning rules: the new zoning framework has created genuine development potential on certain larger sites, and buyers who understand that dynamic have been factoring it into their thinking.

For owners who've been holding for a while, the current market conditions and the increased buyer interest from people priced out of Morningside make for a useful combination. If you're thinking about selling in Carina, I'd be happy to run through the numbers with you.
