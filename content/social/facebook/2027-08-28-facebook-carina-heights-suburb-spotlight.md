## Caption

Carina Heights offers something that has become genuinely scarce in Brisbane's inner south-east: family homes on generous blocks, at price points that are still more accessible than Coorparoo or Camp Hill, with Whites Hill Reserve practically on the doorstep.

The reserve is a significant lifestyle asset. It is one of Brisbane's larger suburban parkland reserves, and for families with children it is a genuine drawcard rather than a background feature. The suburb sits between Carina and Carindale, quietly positioned and well-served by local parks and the surrounding creek corridors, with the kind of indoor-outdoor space that families are specifically looking for when they have outgrown their first home.

Whites Hill State College is the other major driver of buyer interest in Carina Heights. The catchment is one of the most consistently sought-after in the inner east, and properties within the boundary attract multiple buyers and shorter days on market as a result. It is not unusual to see buyers who have been actively monitoring this suburb for months, waiting specifically for a catchment property to appear.

Turnover in Carina Heights is low. Owners hold here, which means the supply of quality stock coming to market in any given year is limited. That scarcity, combined with steady family buyer demand, means well-positioned properties perform strongly when they do appear.
