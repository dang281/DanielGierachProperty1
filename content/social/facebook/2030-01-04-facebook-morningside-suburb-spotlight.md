## Caption

Morningside has changed considerably over the past decade. The workers cottages and Queenslanders that once defined the suburb have been renovated, extended, and in many cases substantially rebuilt. The result is a suburb that carries character and lifestyle appeal in equal measure, with buyers from New Farm and Hawthorne looking across regularly.

The buyer profile here tends to be professionals and families who want inner east access without committing to Bulimba or Balmoral pricing. River proximity in parts of the suburb adds another layer of appeal, and the connection to the Oxford Street precinct via Hawthorne keeps lifestyle options close.

Median house prices are around $1.55M based on recent data, though you should verify this against CoreLogic or PropTrack before using it in any decision. Competition at the top of that range has been consistent, particularly for Queenslanders with land on the high side of the street.

Supply has remained tight. Morningside doesn't turn over quickly, and when good homes do come to market they tend to attract a wide buyer pool quickly. That dynamic has kept prices firm through different market conditions.

If you own in Morningside and are thinking about 2030, it's worth having a current read on your property's position before the market gets busy in February and March.
