## Caption

Cannon Hill is in the middle of a slow but visible transformation that buyers with a medium-term horizon are noticing.

The suburb sits on Old Cleveland Road between Morningside and Carina, with a mix of original post-war homes, older unit stock, and a growing number of renovated and newly built properties that reflect the suburb's improving trajectory. A decade ago, Cannon Hill was one of the more affordable inner-east suburbs for families. That story is changing, though there is still meaningful value relative to the prestige suburbs to the north.

Cannon Hill Anglican College is one of the significant drawcards that sets the suburb apart. Families who want access to that school without paying Bulimba or Hawthorne prices will find Cannon Hill's residential streets a practical alternative. The school's presence creates a sustained buyer pool that supports property values in a way that many neighbouring suburbs do not have.

The suburb's proximity to the Cannon Hill shopping precinct and the Cannon Hill railway station gives it practical connectivity that pure residential suburbs often lack. For buyers who commute by train, the station access is a genuine asset.

Investors have historically been attracted to Cannon Hill's older unit stock for yield, but it is the house market where the more interesting story is playing out. Original homes on good-sized blocks in the streets closest to the school corridor are where the strongest demand concentrates.
