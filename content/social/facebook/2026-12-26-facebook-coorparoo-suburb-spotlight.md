## Caption

Coorparoo is where Brisbane's inner south and inner east overlap, and the result is a suburb that has consistently outperformed its price history.

The suburb's commercial spine along Old Cleveland Road runs for long enough to cover most daily needs within walking distance. The Coorparoo Square development changed the retail gravity of the area when it opened, and the broader precinct around it continues to see steady activity. For buyers who place significant weight on walkability and the ability to run daily errands without a car, Coorparoo competes with suburbs that are considerably more expensive.

The residential streets fanning out from the ridge line are where Coorparoo's character lives. The older Queenslander stock on elevated blocks, the larger post-war brick homes, and the more recent infill townhouses all exist in proximity in a way that creates a genuinely mixed streetscape. Buyers who know what they are looking for can find value in streets that less experienced buyers overlook.

The school access is broad. Archbishop Duhig College, Coorparoo Secondary College, and the range of primary schools within reach give families options at different price points and values.

For owners watching what has settled in Coorparoo over the last 12 months, the direction has been clear.
