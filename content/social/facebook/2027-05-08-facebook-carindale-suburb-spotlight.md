## Caption

Carindale is built around a different set of priorities to the inner east suburbs that tend to dominate Brisbane property conversations, and for the families who choose it, that's precisely the point. Westfield Carindale is one of Brisbane's largest shopping centres and sits effectively at the suburb's centre of gravity, giving residents access to retail, dining, and services that most suburban locations can't match. For households that rely on that kind of daily convenience, it's a significant quality of life factor.

The housing stock reflects the era in which most of the suburb was developed. Brick and tile homes from the 1980s and 1990s are the dominant type, sitting on blocks that are noticeably larger than what you find in the inner east. For families who've been squeezed into smaller footprints closer to the city, the combination of indoor and outdoor space that Carindale offers at its price point is often what tips the decision.

Gateway Motorway and Old Cleveland Road both run through or near the suburb, making Carindale a practical base for buyers who travel for work or need to move between Brisbane's different business corridors. That accessibility is a consistent selling point for dual-income households with different commuting directions.

School infrastructure is solid, with options at both primary and secondary level serving the family market that forms the core of demand here.

If you own in Carindale and you're thinking about your next move, I'd be happy to talk through what's been happening in the market.
