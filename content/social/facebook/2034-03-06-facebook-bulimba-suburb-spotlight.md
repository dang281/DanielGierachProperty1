## Caption

Oxford Street in Bulimba functions as a genuine village precinct: restaurants, cafes, independent retailers, and a ferry terminal that connects residents to the CBD without a car. That combination is what Bulimba owners mean when they describe the suburb as one they never plan to leave, and it is also what drives competition among buyers when properties do come to market.

Bulimba State School is a consistent drawcard for families, and the broader riverside lifestyle, with the Bulimba Riverside Park and ferry access as daily touchpoints, is genuinely distinctive. The supply constraint is real: the suburb is bordered by the Brisbane River on two sides, Hawthorne on one, and Morningside on another, and the lot count reflects that geography. New supply is minimal. What comes to market tends to be owner-occupied homes that have been held for years. [VERIFY: current median house price and average hold period before posting.]

The buyer profile is weighted toward established owner-occupiers: families with children at or approaching school age, professionals who want riverside access and CBD proximity, and downsizers who are unwilling to trade the lifestyle for a more affordable address. Price sensitivity at the top end of the market is real, but so is the conviction of buyers who have already decided they want Bulimba specifically.

Properties that are well-presented and correctly priced on Oxford Street or the quieter residential streets off it do not require extended campaigns.
