## Caption

Carina Heights sits in a position that buyers keep discovering. It is quieter than the suburbs immediately to its north and west, the blocks are decent, and the price gap with Camp Hill has been widening. For a buyer who wants the inner-east family lifestyle without the Camp Hill price, it has become a logical destination.

The Cavendish Road strip is accessible from the northern end of the suburb, which means residents are not cut off from the café and retail options that Camp Hill buyers use daily. The Westfield Carindale precinct is a short drive east, covering the large-format retail and dining needs that a local strip cannot. That dual access to two different commercial precincts is a practical advantage that buyers recognise.

The housing stock has renovation potential across a meaningful portion of the suburb. Postwar and 1960s to 1970s homes on good blocks are the target for buyers who want to add value rather than pay for someone else's work. The school catchment area around Nursery Road draws in families who are making deliberate decisions about which side of the school boundary they land on.

As price gaps with Camp Hill have grown, Carina Heights has picked up overflow buyer interest that previously would not have looked this far east. That pattern tends to compress over time.
