## Caption

East Brisbane is the inner east suburb closest to the city boundary, and that proximity is both its most obvious asset and its most consistently underestimated one.

The suburb sits immediately east of the Story Bridge, with some streets offering a two-kilometre commute to the CBD. For buyers who work in the city and want to walk or cycle to work, East Brisbane offers options that no other inner east suburb can match. The Main Street cafe strip and the connection to Kangaroo Point's dining and recreational infrastructure give the suburb an urban texture that few Brisbane neighbourhoods have achieved.

The housing stock is the most mixed of any inner east suburb: original Queenslanders on narrow lots in the heritage-protected sections, newer townhouses and small-lot dwellings on subdivided blocks, and a scattering of larger character homes where the blocks have been retained intact. The heritage overlay applies to a significant portion of the suburb, which limits subdivision in those areas and has helped maintain the streetscape quality.

The buyer pool is diverse. Owner-occupiers who want CBD proximity and urban lifestyle sit alongside investors who understand that the combination of strong rental demand and constrained housing supply produces reliable yields.

Supply in East Brisbane is genuinely limited. The suburb has a fixed geographic boundary, no room for outward growth, and a heritage overlay that limits significant new stock. When quality properties appear, they do not wait.
