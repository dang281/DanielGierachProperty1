## Caption

Murarrie carries an industrial history that's shaped how it's been perceived, but the residential component of the suburb tells a different story. Gateway Motorway access, proximity to Morningside, and pricing that sits below most of its inner east neighbours have made it a consistent target for investors and entry-level buyers who want an eastern Brisbane address without eastern Brisbane prices.

The suburb has genuine development interest. Land here is looked at differently to land in the established residential streets of Hawthorne or Balmoral, partly because of the mix of zoning and partly because the suburb itself is still evolving. For buyers who understand what that means, it represents a different kind of opportunity.

Owner-occupiers here tend to be practical buyers. First home buyers who want to get into the market, investors seeking yield on a property they can hold long-term, and buyers who prioritise access to the motorway network over lifestyle proximity.

Median house prices are around $1.1M at the time of writing. Verify this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before making decisions based on it.

If you own in Murarrie, the suburb's position between an established inner east and a changing land use context is worth understanding. The link below gives you a starting point.
