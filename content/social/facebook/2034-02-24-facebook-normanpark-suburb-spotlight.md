## Caption

Norman Park is bordered by Camp Hill, Balmoral, and Hawthorne, and that geography is not coincidental to its appeal. Whites Hill Reserve runs along the suburb's southern and eastern boundary, providing bushland access that residents use daily for walking and cycling. Norman Park State School sits in catchment for a suburb that consistently attracts families who have done their research on the southside.

The housing stock is dominated by character Queenslanders, and the streets off Lytton Road and Norman Avenue carry a consistency of presentation that reflects how long-term and invested the ownership base is. Buyers targeting Norman Park are typically looking for the character suburb lifestyle at a price point that sits below Hawthorne and Balmoral, with the same quality of streetscape and the same green space access. [VERIFY: current median house price and comparison with Hawthorne and Balmoral before posting.]

Owner-occupier demand here is high and buyer intent tends to be specific. People arrive at Norman Park after looking at Camp Hill, Balmoral, and Morningside and making a deliberate choice, not because they settled for it, but because the combination of park access, school, and character housing at the price point made sense. Turnover is low.

Properties in Norman Park rarely need long to attract offers. Presentation matters, but the suburb's fundamentals do a significant amount of the work.
