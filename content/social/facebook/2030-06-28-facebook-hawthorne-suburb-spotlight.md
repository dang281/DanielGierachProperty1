## Caption

Hawthorne ends the first half of 2030 in a position that reflects the suburb's fundamental market characteristics: solid demand, limited supply, and a buyer pool that is consistently better-informed and more motivated than in the broader Brisbane market.

The buyers attending open homes in Hawthorne in late June are not browsing. They have worked out the suburb over months, narrowed their search to specific streets or property types, and are attending because the property is genuinely close to what they want. The conversion rate from open home attendance to serious inquiry in a suburb like Hawthorne in winter is meaningfully higher than the comparable rate in a more supply-heavy market.

The Glen Road café strip is one of those lifestyle details that buyers mention unprompted when they describe why they chose Hawthorne. It is not dramatic, and it is not comparable to Oxford Street in scale, but it functions as a genuine daily-use social infrastructure for people who work from home or have flexible working arrangements. In a suburb where that buyer profile is prominent, the café strip matters.

For homeowners in Hawthorne who have been thinking about selling, the latter half of the year brings the spring campaign period where supply typically increases and competition between listings is higher. The case for a late June or July campaign, with thin supply and motivated buyers, is worth understanding before committing to spring.
