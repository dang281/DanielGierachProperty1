## Caption

CoreLogic data puts Brisbane's median dwelling value at around $1.08 million at the end of February 2026, following annual growth of 17.3 per cent. That growth happened while interest rates climbed.

What is driving it is not one thing. Queensland is absorbing more than 25,000 new residents from interstate and overseas each year while building nowhere near enough new homes to house them. Demand keeps compounding. Supply keeps falling short.

Brisbane house and unit forecasts for the next 12 months vary widely across the major forecasters. KPMG is forecasting 10.9 per cent growth for Brisbane houses. NAB is more conservative at around 4.6 per cent. SQM Research sees 10 to 15 per cent possible under favourable conditions.

The differences come down to how each firm weighs the supply shortage against the rate environment. The RBA lifted the cash rate to 4.10 per cent in March, its second consecutive hike, and serviceability at that rate is genuinely tighter for buyers.

For inner east homeowners, both sides of that picture matter. Strong demand and limited supply underpin values. The rate environment shapes who can actually buy. Understanding both is what good selling strategy requires.
