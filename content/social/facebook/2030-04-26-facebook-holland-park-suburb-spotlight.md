## Caption

Holland Park sits at the price point where value and location intersect most clearly in the inner east. For buyers who have been looking at Coorparoo and Camp Hill but need more space for the budget, Holland Park regularly delivers that combination.

The suburb's housing stock is predominantly 1950s to 1970s character and brick homes on large blocks, often 600 to 800 square metres. That land content is what the buyer profile responds to, particularly families who want renovation potential and space for children without paying the land premiums of the river-corridor suburbs.

The Cavendish Road State High School catchment is the most significant factor in the secondary market in Holland Park. Properties that fall within that catchment consistently achieve a price premium relative to comparable properties outside it, and families who are serious about the school are willing to compete on price to secure access.

Logan Road along the Holland Park corridor provides practical retail, dining, and cafe access without the premium that attaches to the Oxford Street or Stanley Street precincts. For buyers who want liveable day-to-day access and are less driven by lifestyle amenity, Holland Park delivers.

The winter market in Holland Park tends to be measured but not absent. Motivated buyers, particularly those with school catchment requirements and a specific timeline, continue to transact through June and July.
