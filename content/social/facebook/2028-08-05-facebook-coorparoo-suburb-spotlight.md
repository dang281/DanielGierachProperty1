## Caption

Coorparoo sits at the southern edge of Brisbane's inner east, close enough to the CBD to attract buyers who want the inner ring access without the top-end inner east price point, and with a character and community that has developed over decades of family ownership.

The suburb's housing stock is genuinely varied. The streets north of Old Cleveland Road toward the Coorparoo ridgeline attract the most consistent demand, with post-war character homes on elevated blocks offering city glimpses and a quieter residential feel. The flatter sections closer to the train line and Logan Road offer better value for buyers who prioritise transport access over elevation.

Coorparoo station and the train line to the CBD make the suburb accessible without a car, which broadens the buyer pool to include buyers who want walkability as well as those who drive. Logan Road is the commercial spine, with a range of cafes, restaurants, and retail that has improved significantly over the past decade.

The buyer profile is predominantly young families and couples who are either buying their first house or making their first meaningful upgrade. They are budget-conscious but understand what they are getting for their money, which means they respond well to honest pricing and move quickly on properties that represent clear value.

Median house prices in Coorparoo represent one of the more accessible entry points into the inner east property market, and the suburb regularly features in first-home-buyer and upgrader searches.
