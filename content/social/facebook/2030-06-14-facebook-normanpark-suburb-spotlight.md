## Caption

Norman Park's market has a quality that most of its residents instinctively understand and most outside observers underestimate: the buyers who want to live in this suburb are not looking at alternatives. They have identified Norman Park specifically, often because of the reserve, the school catchment, the character of the streets, or a combination of all three.

That specificity of buyer demand means that when a property appears in Norman Park, the buyer pool that responds is narrower than in a suburb with broader appeal, but it is more motivated. Buyers who have been watching the suburb for months or years do not need to be convinced that the suburb works for them. They need to be convinced that the property is worth the price.

Whites Hill State College continues to anchor secondary school demand in the suburb and across the catchment boundary into Camp Hill. For families who have already decided on that school, buying in Norman Park is a logical choice because it places them solidly within the catchment without the higher price attached to some Camp Hill streets.

The Whites Hill Reserve walking network is a daily-use asset rather than an occasional destination. Residents who use the reserve regularly describe it as one of the primary reasons they have not moved despite having the equity to upgrade.

For sellers in Norman Park, a property that is well-prepared and correctly priced will find its buyer quickly. The patience in this market is not in waiting for a buyer; it is in finding the right one.
