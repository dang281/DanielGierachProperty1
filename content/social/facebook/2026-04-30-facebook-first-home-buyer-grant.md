## Caption

Queensland's $30,000 first home owner grant drops back to $15,000 for contracts signed after 30 June 2026. That is a $15,000 reduction, now less than ten weeks away.

The grant applies to new homes valued under $750,000. In Brisbane's current market, that mostly means buyers are looking at outer suburbs, new estates, or off-the-plan apartments. The inner east sits well above that price cap.

Stack the grant on top of the federal deposit guarantee scheme and stamp duty concessions, and the total government support available to an eligible first home buyer can run to well over $100,000 in equivalent value before 30 June.

That sounds substantial. The harder reality is that Brisbane's median dwelling value is now above $1 million, and even with all available assistance, a significant deposit and borrowing capacity at current rates are still the main constraints for most first-time buyers.

If you know someone thinking about getting into the market before the grant drops, the window is shorter than it feels. Worth having a conversation sooner rather than later.

If you are looking for a new build in the inner east corridor, I am happy to point you in the right direction.
