## Caption

Mt Gravatt East sits about 8km south of the CBD and tends to attract buyers who have worked their way through the inner-east shortlist and want a suburb that delivers on the fundamentals without the price tag of Camp Hill or Coorparoo.

The elevation is one of the suburb's genuine advantages. Houses on the ridge streets get city views and the feel of a semi-rural setting while sitting a 15-minute drive from the CBD. The suburb is predominantly owner-occupied, with a mix of original post-war homes on larger blocks and renovated character properties that have followed the same trajectory as the inner east more broadly.

The eastern boundary of Mt Gravatt East borders Carindale, which keeps buyer flow from both directions. Families upgrading from the outer southern suburbs discover it at the same time as inner-east buyers looking for a larger block at a lower entry point.

For sellers, the practical question is how to price a suburb where the range between an original home on a good block and a renovated property in the same street is significant.
