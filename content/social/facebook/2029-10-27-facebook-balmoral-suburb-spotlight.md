## Caption

Balmoral sits in a useful position in the inner east, bounded by Hawthorne to the north and Morningside to the south, with the river accessible and the Bulimba lifestyle within walking distance. It is the suburb where buyers who love the Hawthorne and Bulimba character find that the price of entry is a little lower, and that gap has kept demand here consistent.

The character housing stock is the real asset. Entry-level Queenslanders on good-sized blocks are what buyers come for, and competition for that product is genuine. There is enough renovation potential in the housing stock to attract the buyer who wants to add value, and enough already-done examples to satisfy those who want to move straight in.

Lores Bonney Riverwalk along the river frontage is one of the better morning walk and cycle routes in this part of Brisbane. Tingalpa Creek reserve adds to the green space, and both contribute to the suburb having a more settled, established feel than its relatively modest price point might suggest.

School catchment is a practical consideration for family buyers. Balmoral State School draws in families who might otherwise be stretching toward Hawthorne, and the proximity to Oxford Street means the café and restaurant lifestyle is accessible without living directly on it.
