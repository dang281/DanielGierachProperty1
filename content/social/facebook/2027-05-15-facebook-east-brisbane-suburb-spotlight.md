## Caption

East Brisbane is one of those suburbs where the numbers and the lifestyle don't quite line up in the way you'd expect. At two to three kilometres from the CBD, it sits closer to the city than most buyers realise, yet it consistently prices below inner suburbs that offer less in the way of access and amenity. For professionals, young couples, and investors who want genuine CBD proximity without paying Fortitude Valley prices, that gap is worth paying attention to.

The suburb is compact and walkable in a way that few Brisbane locations manage. The Kangaroo Point cliffs and the Riverwalk are accessible on foot or by bike, giving residents a connection to the river that most of Brisbane has to drive to. Woolloongabba is directly adjacent, which brings the South East Freeway, bus access, and the GABBA within reach. Bus services through East Brisbane itself are frequent enough that a car becomes optional rather than essential for daily life.

The housing stock is a mix of character Queenslanders on the higher streets, townhouses, and apartments that reflect the suburb's increasing density over recent years. The Queenslanders tend to be most in demand, particularly the ones on elevated blocks where you get some separation from the denser pockets below. Infill development has been steady, which has changed the feel of parts of the suburb, but the character streets remain intact.
