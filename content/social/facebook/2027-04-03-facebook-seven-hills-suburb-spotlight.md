## Caption

Seven Hills sits on a ridge between Norman Park and Camp Hill, and that geography is pretty much the whole story. The suburb's elevated streets catch city skyline views that buyers in the adjacent postcodes pay a premium for, yet Seven Hills consistently prices below both neighbours. For families and owner-occupiers who want that inner east lifestyle without stretching to Camp Hill's top end, it's one of the more sensible conversations to have right now.

The housing stock is a mix of original Queenslanders, post-war homes, and renovated properties that reflect the broader character renovation trend running through the inner east. Streets along the ridgeline are the most in demand, particularly where the city skyline opens up to the west. Buyers who know this suburb tend to move quickly when something decent comes to market.

Whites Hill Reserve is a genuine lifestyle asset here. The reserve backs onto the suburb and gives families access to walking trails and green space that would cost considerably more to live near in other parts of Brisbane. Camp Hill Marketplace is a short drive or bus ride away for daily shopping, dining, and coffee. Bus access along Old Cleveland Road connects residents to the CBD without relying on a car for every trip.

The school catchment for Whites Hill State College at secondary level is a consistent drawcard for families approaching that stage of planning.

If you own in Seven Hills and you're thinking about what your property is worth, I'd be glad to have that conversation.
