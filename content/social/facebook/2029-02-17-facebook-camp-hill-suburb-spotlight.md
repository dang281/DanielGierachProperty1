## Caption

Camp Hill is one of those inner east suburbs where buyers arrive knowing exactly what they want and often end up staying for twenty years.

The ridgeline that runs through the heart of Camp Hill gives many properties elevated positions with views west toward the city. Those positions are well understood by buyers who have been looking in the area, and the competition for well-presented homes with a city outlook tends to be immediate in any market.

The suburb's school catchment covers Camp Hill State Infants and Primary School, and the broader area gives access to the secondary school options that families in this part of the inner east prioritise. That catchment drives demand from a particular buyer profile, families with young children who want to be settled before school transitions, and that demographic is consistent regardless of broader market conditions.

The housing mix includes both the original post-war timber homes that define the inner east streetscape and more recent full rebuilds. The blocks on the western side of the suburb, where the elevation and views are strongest, attract the most competitive buyer interest.

Turnover in Camp Hill is low. When a well-positioned property does come to market, the buyer pool is ready.

If you are thinking about what your Camp Hill home could achieve, I am happy to have that conversation.
