## Caption

Holland Park is a suburb that rewards buyers who look carefully at the streets rather than the postcode. The suburb sits between Mount Gravatt and Greenslopes, and its character varies meaningfully across the street grid: the elevated streets to the east have aspect and views that justify a premium over the flatter western streets, while the proximity to the Ipswich Road corridor creates variation in noise and access that buyers who know the suburb navigate carefully.

The Holland Park West rail station and the SE Busway access at nearby Upper Mount Gravatt give residents reliable public transport options that the surrounding suburban addresses do not match. For buyers who are prioritising commute options alongside the value proposition that Holland Park delivers, the transport access adds to the suburb's appeal.

The main strip along Logan Road handles Holland Park's everyday retail and dining requirements with a practical mix of local businesses and chain options that have established themselves in a corridor with strong resident and passing traffic. The strip has improved consistently and now offers the regular weekly needs without requiring a trip to a major shopping centre for most households.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war, with brick and Queenslander construction on established blocks that give buyers the land content that inner-south suburbs at similar price points often cannot match. Renovation activity is consistent, which keeps the street presentation standard high across most of the suburb.
