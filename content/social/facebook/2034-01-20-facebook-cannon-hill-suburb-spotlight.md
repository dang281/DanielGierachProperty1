## Caption

Cannon Hill has genuine transport infrastructure behind it, and that matters in a city where commute times drive a significant share of buyer decisions. Cannon Hill train station on the Cleveland line puts the CBD within a 20-minute commute, and bus routes along Wynnum Road extend the reach further. Combined with the Cannon Hill Shopping Centre for day-to-day convenience, the suburb is set up practically in a way that a lot of its neighbours are not.

Cannon Hill State School is a well-regarded local primary, and that catchment is a consistent factor for families choosing between this suburb and Morningside or Murarrie to the north. Post-war character homes dominate the stock. They are the right product for a buyer group that is weighted towards first-home buyers and young families who want a character home with the option to renovate incrementally.

The price point has made Cannon Hill one of the more active entry markets in the inner east over the past several years. Buyers who cannot stretch to Hawthorne or Bulimba land here, and many of them stay. That turnover pattern means the suburb sees consistent sales activity, and sellers who price correctly move quickly.
