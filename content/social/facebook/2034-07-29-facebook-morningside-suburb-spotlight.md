## Caption

Morningside has an identity that has solidified over the past decade: a suburb with genuine character, strong proximity to the city, and a buyer base that is loyal to it. Once buyers land in Morningside, they tend to stay in the suburb through subsequent moves.

The Wynnum Road and Morningside Avenue corridors provide the suburb's commercial character. The housing stock is predominantly pre and post-war, with a mix of Queenslanders, timber workers' cottages, and brick homes that offer a range of entry points across the market.

The eastern end of the suburb, particularly streets running off Norman Avenue and toward Seven Hills, tends to offer more space and quieter residential character. The western streets near the railway and the busway corridor attract buyers who are prioritising commute convenience.

Morningside's days on market tend to sit below the inner east average. When a well-presented property hits the market here, the buyer pool is both large and motivated.
