## Caption

Norman Park's position between the river corridor and Whites Hill Reserve gives the suburb a green amenity profile that is difficult to replicate in the inner east at comparable price points. The reserve is accessible on foot from most Norman Park streets, and its scale means that families who use it regularly do not feel the park saturation that smaller reserves in denser suburbs create. The combination of the river edge to the north and the reserve to the south creates an outdoor amenity frame that the suburb sits inside rather than adjacent to.

The Brisbane State High School catchment drives a specific and persistent buyer segment in Norman Park. Families who have made the decision that Brisbane State High is their preferred secondary school option for their children narrow their address search to the catchment boundary and do not consider properties outside it. That search behaviour creates reliable demand for well-located Norman Park properties regardless of the broader market's temperature.

The Lores Bonney Riverwalk access from Norman Park gives residents an off-road active transport corridor along the river bank. Buyers who are prioritising active lifestyle infrastructure place real value on that connection, and it is a feature that the Norman Park address includes without an additional premium.

Properties in Norman Park that present well and are within the Brisbane State High catchment attract buyer pools that are both larger and more motivated than properties at similar price points in suburbs without the catchment advantage.
