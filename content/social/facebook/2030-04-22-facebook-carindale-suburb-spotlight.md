## Caption

Carindale's market is built around one consistent theme: families who want space, good schools, and access to the Westfield shopping hub, without paying the premium attached to the river-corridor suburbs to the north and west.

The suburb's housing stock is predominantly 1980s and 1990s brick construction on large blocks, which gives buyers entry to genuine land holdings at prices that are below the inner east median in many cases. That land component is increasingly understood by buyers who have been watching the inner east long enough to see how the character home suburbs have appreciated on the back of their land content.

The renovation opportunity in Carindale is significant. A well-located 700-square-metre block with a liveable but dated house is a realistic entry point for buyers who are prepared to renovate progressively, and the suburb's price gradient means that improvement is genuinely rewarded at resale.

School access is a significant driver. The Carindale area falls within reach of several well-regarded state and private school options, and the family buyer profile that dominates the suburb is well aware of the options and what they cost to access.

The outer reaches of Carindale can feel more suburban than inner east, but the central pockets around the Carindale Road corridor punch above their weight for lifestyle access.
