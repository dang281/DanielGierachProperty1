## Caption

Carina Heights sits on elevated ground east of Carina. The topography gives ridge-line properties an elevated position and views that buyers in neighbouring suburbs spend considerably more to access.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war brick and fibro on standard Brisbane allotments. The renovation wave that swept through Coorparoo and Camp Hill has been working its way east, and Carina Heights is mid-transition, with a growing share of completed projects coming to market.

Relative to its price point, Carina Heights offers allotment sizes, elevation, and access to Belmont Hills Reserve and the Cavendish Road strip without paying the premium that suburbs to the west command.

Families with school-age children have become a consistent presence, drawn by value and schooling options in the Carindale-Carina catchment.

For Carina Heights homeowners considering a sale, it is worth understanding what the current buyer pool looks like. The appetite for well-maintained, correctly priced homes here has been stronger than many vendors expect.
