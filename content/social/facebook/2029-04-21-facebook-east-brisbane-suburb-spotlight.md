## Caption

East Brisbane sits closer to the CBD than almost any other inner east suburb and that proximity is exactly what drives its buyer profile.

The suburb is predominantly terrace houses, post-war workers cottages, and low-rise units, with limited detached housing stock relative to the demand for it. When a freestanding home on a usable block does come to market in East Brisbane, the competition reflects how rarely it happens.

Mowbray Park and the Norman Creek bikeway corridor give the suburb genuine lifestyle appeal beyond the CBD convenience argument. The buyers here are not just buying proximity: they are buying the combination of walkability, neighbourhood feel, and urban access that is hard to replicate anywhere further east.

For investors, East Brisbane has historically offered solid rental demand given the proximity to the PA Hospital, the Gabba precinct, and the South Bank institutions. That demand base has remained consistent.

The key consideration for buyers is the mix of residential and commercial zoning across parts of the suburb. Understanding what you can and cannot build on a given site requires checking the relevant overlay maps before inspecting. It is not a complication that affects most standard residential purchases, but it is worth knowing before making assumptions about what a block can accommodate.

If you own in East Brisbane and want to understand what the market looks like right now, I am happy to give you a straightforward appraisal.
