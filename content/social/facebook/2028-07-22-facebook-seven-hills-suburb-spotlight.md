## Caption

Seven Hills is one of Brisbane's inner east suburbs that consistently rewards buyers who understand what they are actually buying: elevated land, sweeping views, and a quiet residential character within ten kilometres of the CBD.

The suburb sits on the ridge between Camp Hill and Coorparoo, with the best streets offering city views that are genuinely rare at the price point. Properties facing east toward the bay or north toward the CBD regularly attract premiums from buyers who have been watching the suburb for some time and understand the view is not easily replicated elsewhere in the inner east.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war brick and timber on larger-than-average blocks for the area. Many of these homes have been held for a generation and come to market only occasionally, which keeps supply genuinely constrained. When they do appear, the combination of the land size, the elevation, and the view creates competition that supports strong results.

The buyer pool for Seven Hills is mostly families and established couples upgrading within the inner east. They are typically looking for a long-term home rather than a property to flip, which means motivated and financially capable buyers who understand the suburb's value.

Seven Hills is not a suburb that shouts about itself. The buyers who know it, know it well.
