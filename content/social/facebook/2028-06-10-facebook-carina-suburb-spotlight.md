## Caption

Carina offers something that is genuinely harder to find as Brisbane prices continue to move: a quiet family suburb with good schooling, decent block sizes, and real access to the city, at a price point that still makes sense for families doing the numbers.

Carina State School is well regarded and draws families from the surrounding area who could technically afford a more expensive postcode but choose Carina for the community feel and the straightforward commute. Old Cleveland Road provides easy bus access into the CBD, and Westfield Carindale is minutes away for everything else.

The housing stock is a practical mix of three and four-bedroom brick homes from the 1970s and 1980s alongside a growing number of renovated properties. Buyers who want a solid home in a stable suburb without the renovation project tend to target Carina for exactly this reason.

Compared to Cannon Hill and Morningside to the north, Carina still offers a meaningful value gap. That gap attracts buyers who understand the inner-east market but are working within a budget that those suburbs no longer accommodate.

Median house price sits around $1.05 million. Rental demand is consistent, supported by proximity to the PA Hospital precinct and the broader south-east employment corridor.

Winter listings in Carina tend to be limited, which puts sellers in a strong position when stock is tight and buyer enquiry stays active.
