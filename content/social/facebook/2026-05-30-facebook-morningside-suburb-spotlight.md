## Caption

Morningside has a median house price of around $1.31 million, with annual house price growth of approximately 10 per cent and unit values up roughly 14 to 17 per cent over the same period.

The suburb draws two distinct buyer types, which is one of the things that makes it healthy for sellers. Families and owner-occupiers who want the flat, character-home lifestyle at a price point below Bulimba and Hawthorne. And investors drawn by unit rental yields and the suburb's longer-term gentrification trajectory. That breadth of demand means a well-presented property is not dependent on a single buyer type showing up.

Days on market for houses is currently around 22 days. That is a competitive market, not a slow one.

There is also an infrastructure tailwind worth noting. The Cannon Hill Shopping Centre redevelopment will transform the precinct with new retail and dining, adding long-term supply to the eastern corridor that Morningside sits within.

For homeowners thinking about timing, the fundamentals here are solid. Whether now is the right moment for you specifically depends on your individual circumstances.
