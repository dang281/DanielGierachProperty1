## Caption

Seven Hills is one of the inner east suburbs where the value proposition and the actual liveability are not yet fully reflected in the price. Buyers who discover Seven Hills through a thorough search of the inner east, rather than arriving from the headline suburbs, often find that the land content, the elevation, and the residential character deliver what they were looking for at a price they did not expect to find this close to the CBD.

The suburb's elevation gives it something rare in the flat southern suburbs of the inner east: natural views and cross-ventilation that reduce the reliance on air conditioning that most inner Brisbane homes require through summer. Streets that run along the ridgeline have aspect that buyers who find it rarely want to leave.

The Murarrie Road connection provides direct access to the Gateway Motorway corridor, which matters to buyers who are considering commute logistics across multiple potential employment centres rather than just the CBD. The airport precinct and the logistics employment base at Murarrie are both accessible in under 15 minutes.

The housing stock retains the character that comes from a suburb that has not experienced the degree of infill development that has changed neighbouring suburbs. Larger blocks on elevated streets represent a combination that is increasingly uncommon in the inner east at accessible price points.

For vendors in Seven Hills, the buyer who knows this suburb is decisive when the right property appears.
