## Caption

Westfield Carindale is probably the most recognised feature of the suburb, and it shapes the lifestyle here in a practical way. Shopping, dining, cinema, and services all within a few minutes of home is a genuine draw for families who want that kind of convenience built into their daily routine. But the suburb offers more than retail proximity.

Carindale's blocks are generally larger than what you find in the inner east, and the housing stock tends to be well-maintained family homes from the 1980s and 90s. Families who want space, good school access, and a suburb that functions well for day-to-day life find Carindale straightforward to choose. It doesn't carry the character of Hawthorne or the café culture of Bulimba, but it delivers reliably on the practical elements that families with school-aged children tend to prioritise.

Median house prices are around $1.2M at the time of writing. Confirm current figures via CoreLogic or PropTrack before making any decisions based on that number. The consistency of family demand here tends to provide a reasonable floor on prices through varying market conditions.

Investor interest exists but is quieter than in some neighbouring suburbs. The primary buyer profile is owner-occupying families.
