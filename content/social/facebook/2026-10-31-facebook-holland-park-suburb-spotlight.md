## Caption

Holland Park sits in the middle of a conversation Brisbane buyers have been having for several years, about where the value boundary between the inner ring and the middle ring actually is, and whether the gap has permanently closed.

The suburb is far enough from the CBD that it spent a long time being described as affordable relative to Norman Park or Camp Hill. That descriptor is now largely redundant. The price gap between Holland Park and its inner-east neighbours has compressed in a way that reflects genuine buyer conviction rather than overflow demand.

The practical profile of the suburb holds up well. The Ipswich Road and Cavendish Road corridors give efficient bus access to the CBD. The Toohey Forest Park is a substantial green asset on the suburb's southern boundary, providing a nature access point that buyers with children and active lifestyles consistently identify as a genuine lifestyle benefit rather than a map feature.

The housing stock covers a broad range. Post-war character homes on 600-plus square metre blocks coexist with more recent infill development, giving buyers at different price points and renovation appetites genuine options within the same suburb boundary.

The buyers I see in Holland Park are not here because they missed out somewhere else. They have chosen the suburb for what it specifically offers.
