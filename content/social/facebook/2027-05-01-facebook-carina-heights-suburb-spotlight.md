## Caption

Carina Heights tends to fly under the radar in conversations about Brisbane's inner east, but the buyers who know it keep coming back. The suburb sits on elevated ground, and the western-facing streets on the higher sections carry genuine city skyline views that are difficult to find at this price point elsewhere in the corridor. That combination of outlook and affordability is what draws families who've done their research.

The housing stock is a mix of original post-war homes and renovated properties. Blocks tend to be a reasonable size by Brisbane standards, giving owners room to extend, renovate, or simply enjoy a backyard without being hemmed in by neighbouring walls. The suburb is quieter than Carina proper, with fewer through roads funnelling traffic across residential streets, which is something families with younger children tend to value.

Carindale State School and Holland Park State High cover the catchment at primary and secondary level respectively, which appeals to the family buyers who make up the bulk of demand here. Westfield Carindale is a short drive away, meaning daily shopping, dining, and services are all well within reach without requiring a trip into the inner city. Gateway Motorway access also makes Carina Heights practical for households where commuting further afield is part of daily life.

The suburb holds its value consistently despite being less prominent than some neighbours. If you own here and you're thinking about what your property could achieve in the current market, I'd be glad to have a conversation.
