## Caption

Cannon Hill is one of the inner east suburbs that rewards buyers who look past the name and into the numbers.

The suburb sits on an elevated ridge between Morningside and Murarrie, with creek corridor green space to the north and rail access that puts the CBD inside 20 minutes. For a suburb at its price point, that combination of elevation, outlook, and infrastructure access is not common.

The Cannon Hill Anglican College is a central factor in the buyer profile. Families who have identified the school as their preferred secondary option and are working back from it to find a property within the catchment represent a segment of the market that is price-resilient in a way that more generic demand is not. Schools create floors.

The Cannon Hill Shopping Centre and the immediate surrounding retail strip give residents the practical daily convenience that holds appeal for owner-occupiers who value walkability. The proximity to the Gateway Motorway arterial is useful for buyers with work commitments that require road access south.

What I see in Cannon Hill is a suburb where the well-presented family home on a flat site continues to perform well. The properties that take longer to move are the ones at the upper end of their range asking for recognition they haven't fully earned yet.
