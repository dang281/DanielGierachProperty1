## Caption

Bulimba is the suburb the rest of the inner east measures itself against, and the comparison almost always ends in Bulimba's favour.

Oxford Street is the most complete village retail strip on Brisbane's inner east. The combination of the ferry connection to the CBD, the riverside parkland, the established cafe and restaurant precinct, and the walkable residential streets behind it produces a lifestyle proposition that buyers are willing to pay a meaningful premium for. The premium is not irrational. It reflects real, daily utility.

The river frontage is the obvious headline. But the streets that do not face the river are often the more interesting value proposition. Properties on the elevated blocks behind the river and backing onto the leafier residential streets combine Bulimba's amenity access with the privacy and land content that riverside exposure sometimes sacrifices.

The buyer demographic at the upper end of Bulimba's market is specific: families who have chosen the suburb for the school access, the Hawthorne and Morningside Aquatic Centre proximity, and the ferry commute, and who are not going to settle for something that does not meet their brief. That creates a competitive environment for well-positioned properties and a patient wait for anything that is not.

For Bulimba owners, the market's depth is the most relevant indicator heading into 2027.
