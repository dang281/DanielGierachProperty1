## Caption

Camp Hill's elevated position gives it something most inner east suburbs do not have: a view corridor that buyers pay attention to. Streets that run along the ridge line offer aspects across the inner south and south-east that are genuinely rare at the inner east price point. Buyers who have found a Camp Hill address with good aspect and open outlook tend to hold on to it for a long time.

The suburb's proximity to Westfield Carindale handles the retail and dining requirements without Camp Hill itself being dominated by commercial activity. The streets are predominantly residential, quiet, and tree-lined. That combination, suburban calm with direct access to major retail, is one that resonates with the family buyer profile that drives Camp Hill demand.

School access is a factor in buying decisions here. East Brisbane State School and the Anglican schools corridor both have relevance to Camp Hill buyers depending on the education approach families are pursuing. Buyers who are specifically researching primary school catchment options are identifying Camp Hill as a viable address for multiple schooling preferences.

The property stock is a mix of post-war character homes and the elevated, architect-designed homes that take advantage of the ridge views. Well-presented properties on the right streets attract active buyer pools and the suburb's absorption rate has been consistent through the autumn market.
