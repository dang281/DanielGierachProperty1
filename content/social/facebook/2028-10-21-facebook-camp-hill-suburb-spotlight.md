## Caption

Camp Hill sits on the ridge line between the Bulimba Creek and Whites Hill Reserve, which is a location advantage that shows up consistently in the suburb's price performance.

The streets that run east from Old Cleveland Road toward Whites Hill tend to produce the strongest results in the suburb. The elevated positions, the outlook over the reserve, and the proximity to the Whites Hill walking tracks make this one of the more pleasant precincts in the inner east for owner-occupiers. The buyer who moves to Camp Hill from a flat inner-east suburb and discovers the ridge-line aspect often does not leave.

The suburb has a particularly strong family demographic built around the catchment for Camp Hill State School and the private school corridor on Old Cleveland Road. Families coming into Camp Hill from outside Brisbane frequently identify the school options as a primary driver of the decision.

The housing stock is mixed: original post-war homes on large blocks alongside well-executed renovations and new builds. The renovated property on a good block in the right street consistently commands a premium that reflects both the renovation quality and the replacement cost in the current building environment.
