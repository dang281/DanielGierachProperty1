## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits at the junction of several of Brisbane's key south-side corridors, which makes it one of the better-connected suburbs in the inner east for buyers who commute.

The suburb straddles the Mt Gravatt shopping precinct along Logan Road, with residential streets spreading east and west from the commercial spine. The housing is a mix: some original post-war homes on larger blocks, newer townhouse and unit developments closer to the commercial areas, and a cluster of architecturally renovated homes in the elevated streets that trade at the top of the suburb's price range.

Mt Gravatt's buyer profile is among the most diverse of any inner-east suburb. First home buyers and investors are drawn to the unit and townhouse stock closer to the arterial roads, where yield is stronger and entry prices are below the broader inner-east average. Family buyers target the elevated residential streets for the space, the access to Griffith University's Mt Gravatt Campus, and the proximity to Westfield Garden City.

The commercial strip on Logan Road is a practical asset for buyers with families: grocery options, medical services, and dining within walking distance. It is not the lifestyle destination that Oxford Street in Bulimba is, but it serves a different buyer need.

For sellers, Mt Gravatt's broad buyer base means a well-run campaign should generate enquiry from multiple buyer types simultaneously.
