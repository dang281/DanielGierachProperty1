## Caption

Mt Gravatt East is a suburb that punches above its profile. The name does not carry the same immediate recognition as Bulimba or Norman Park, but the price trajectory and the buyer depth tell a more complete story.

The suburb sits on elevated terrain with pockets that return city views, backing onto the Mt Gravatt Conservation Reserve to the west. That green buffer gives a number of streets a bush-edge feel that is rare at this distance from the CBD. The housing stock is predominantly 1960s to 1980s brick and timber, with a significant portion of the suburb having been renovated or rebuilt over the past decade.

The buyer profile is broad: families who want space at a value price point, investors attracted by the proximity to Griffith University and the consistent rental demand from students and professionals, and buyers who have been pushed south by price growth in Camp Hill and Coorparoo.

Griffith University Nathan Campus is within close proximity, which creates a consistent tenant pool that keeps rental vacancy rates in Mt Gravatt East below the broader Brisbane average in most market conditions.

Days on market in Mt Gravatt East has tightened over recent years as buyers who cannot afford the northern inner-east suburbs recalibrate their search. Properties in the streets closest to the conservation reserve tend to be the most competitive.
