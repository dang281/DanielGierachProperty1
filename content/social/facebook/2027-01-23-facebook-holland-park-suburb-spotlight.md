## Caption

Holland Park is an elevated suburb with a clear advantage that does not always make the property listings: city views from the upper streets that most suburban suburbs at this price point simply cannot offer.

Sitting south of Camp Hill and west of Coorparoo, Holland Park is predominantly 1950s to 1970s housing, with a mix of original and renovated homes on blocks that are generally larger than what you find in the inner suburbs to the north. The elevation means that a well-positioned property in Holland Park can return views toward the CBD that add genuine buyer appeal and, in the right market, a meaningful premium.

The buyer profile is split between families seeking more space than the prestige inner-east offers at their budget, and buyers who genuinely want Holland Park for its own merits: the Cavendish Road school corridor, the proximity to Greenslopes Private Hospital for medical professionals, and a suburb that is established and quiet without being remote.

Cavendish Road State High School is one of the most consistently sought state secondary catchments in the southern inner east, and properties within that catchment carry a demonstrable premium compared to equivalent homes just outside the boundary.

The turnover rate in Holland Park is relatively low. Homeowners here tend to stay. When a good property in the right street comes to market, buyers who have been watching act quickly.
