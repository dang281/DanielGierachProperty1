## Caption

Carindale does something that few suburbs in Brisbane's inner east can match: it gives buyers a generous block at a price point that is still accessible when the suburbs closer to the city are not. The post-1980 housing stock on 600 to 800sqm blocks is exactly what family buyers are after when they have children, a dog, and a need for a functional outdoor space that is not a token 4x4 patch of grass.

Westfield Carindale is the central node of daily life here. It is one of the larger shopping centres in Brisbane's south side, and having it within the suburb removes the need for a major retail drive. Carindale State School is well regarded locally, and the proximity to Citipointe Christian College gives the suburb appeal across Catholic and independent school buyer segments.

The buyer profile is firmly family-oriented. First-home buyers who have outgrown units, families upsizing from townhouses, and even downsizers who want a single-level home with low-maintenance yard all compete for stock here. Investor interest has been steady as rental yields hold firm in a suburb where demand from working families with children is structural rather than speculative.

Carindale's entry price relative to Coorparoo or Camp Hill is the defining commercial argument, and buyers increasingly know it.
