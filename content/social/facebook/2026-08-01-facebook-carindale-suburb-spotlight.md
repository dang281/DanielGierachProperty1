## Caption

Carindale is where Brisbane's inner-east price gradient runs out, and that is increasingly working in homeowners' favour.

The suburb sits at the eastern edge of the established inner ring, anchored by Westfield Carindale and within reach of the school triangle that draws families from across the south-east. Citipointe Christian College and Redeemer Lutheran College consistently pull buyers into this suburb who would otherwise be priced into the inner suburbs at significantly higher entry points.

What distinguishes Carindale from comparable addresses in Camp Hill or Coorparoo is block size. The lots here are notably larger, which creates a structural advantage for owners. More land means more renovation ceiling, more development optionality, and more appeal to buyers who have been watching the inner east and decided they want a proper yard without paying a Balmoral price.

Demand in Carindale has been sustained by a steady pipeline of buyers moving east from Coorparoo and Holland Park who see the value gap and act. That migration pattern is not new, but it has been more pronounced as prices in the closer-in suburbs have moved up.

Days on market for correctly priced properties have remained short. The buyers watching this suburb are prepared and tend to move when the right home comes up.

If you own in Carindale and have been thinking about what the current market means for your position, the conversation is straightforward.
