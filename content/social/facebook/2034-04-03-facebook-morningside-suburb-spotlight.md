## Caption

Morningside sits at the practical centre of the inner east. It is close enough to Bulimba and Hawthorne to benefit from the lifestyle reputation of the Oxford Street precinct without carrying the full premium of a riverfront address. For buyers who want inner east access at a more considered price point, Morningside delivers on most of the criteria that make the surrounding suburbs desirable.

The suburb's appeal is grounded in practicality. Morningside Station provides direct rail access to the CBD. The Wynnum Road strip has the everyday retail and dining that families rely on. And the housing stock, a mix of post-war character homes and well-maintained cottages on reasonable blocks, gives buyers who want land without a seven-figure premium a realistic path.

The buyer profile here skews toward families in the early to mid stages of their property journey. School catchment decisions play a role, with access to Morningside State School and proximity to the Anglican schools corridor being factors that buyers research specifically before choosing an address. Properties north of Wynnum Road, with easier access to the river suburbs and walkability to the café strip, consistently command a premium over the broader suburb median.

For vendors in Morningside, the autumn market has been active. Well-presented family homes in the sub-$1.5 million range have been attracting multiple buyers at open homes.
