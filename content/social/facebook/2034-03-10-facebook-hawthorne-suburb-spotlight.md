## Caption

Hawthorne is one of Brisbane's most tightly held premium character suburbs, and the data reflects that consistently. The Oxford Street (Bulimba end) ferry terminal gives residents direct CBD access by water, and the compact grid of character Queenslanders between Hawthorne and Bulimba represents the kind of housing stock that simply does not come to market often. When it does, the buyer pool is typically experienced, motivated, and financially ready.

The suburb sits between Bulimba to the west and Morningside to the east, with the Brisbane River to the north, a geography that constrains supply as effectively as any planning instrument could. There is no meaningful infill capacity, which means the lot count is stable and owner turnover drives whatever stock appears. [VERIFY: current median house price before posting.]

Buyers in Hawthorne are predominantly owner-occupiers at the premium end: established professionals, long-term Brisbane residents who have built equity and are trading up, and buyers relocating from interstate or internationally who have specifically researched the inner east. The suburb does not produce large volumes of listings, and a well-presented character home on the right street here draws a concentrated buyer pool at the top of the market.

Presentation, styling, and campaign strategy matter at this level. Properties that are correctly prepared tend to reflect that in their results.
