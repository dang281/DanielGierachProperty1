## Caption

Seven Hills rarely appears on the radar, and that is precisely why it holds its value so consistently. Sitting above Enoggera Creek Reserve on streets like Hillside Road and Seven Hills Road, it is one of Brisbane's smallest suburbs by lot count, which means genuine scarcity rather than manufactured urgency.

The housing stock leans heavily toward pre-war and post-war Queenslanders, many on elevated blocks with city views from the upper storeys. These are not homes that come up often. When they do, buyers already watching the suburb move quickly, because they know the next comparable listing could be months away.

Who buys here? Mostly owner-occupiers who value quiet residential streets, proximity to Enoggera Creek bushland, and the convenience of being minutes from Ashgrove and The Gap's shops and cafes. Families appreciate the access to schools including Ashgrove State School and Marist College Ashgrove. Professionals value the commute, with the CBD reachable in under 20 minutes via Waterworks Road.

Median house prices in Seven Hills sit above the Brisbane-wide median, reflecting the limited supply and elevated lifestyle. [VERIFY: current median and recent sales data before posting.]
