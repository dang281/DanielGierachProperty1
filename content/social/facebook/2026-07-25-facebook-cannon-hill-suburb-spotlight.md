## Caption

Cannon Hill is the kind of suburb that surprises buyers who arrive without much expectation and leave wanting to make an offer.

The architecture along the ridgeline streets north of Wynnum Road has an underappreciated character. Queenslander-style homes and well-preserved post-war renovations on proper-sized blocks, with sightlines that justify the address. The suburb is genuinely walkable to Cannon Hill Plaza and has direct access to the Cannon Hill CityCat terminal at Colmslie, which is what makes it compelling for buyers who want the inner-east lifestyle without the Bulimba or Hawthorne price.

The CityCat connection to the CBD, South Bank, and the university campuses is one of the suburb's most underrated selling points. It means the suburb functions as a genuine transport alternative for buyers who factor commute quality into their decision.

Cannon Hill has been trading at a median in the mid to high $1 million range, depending on the street and the quality of the home, and prices have held through the current cycle without the volatility seen in some of the more speculative outer suburbs.

For homeowners who have been watching the Bulimba and Hawthorne market from across the suburb boundary, the value gap has consistently worked in their favour. What that means now is worth understanding.
