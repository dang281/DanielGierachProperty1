## Caption

The autumn selling window in Mt Gravatt typically closes by mid-April when school holidays arrive and buyer activity pauses. That makes the next few weeks, March through to the end of April, the most productive period of Q1 for sellers in this suburb. Homeowners who've been thinking about a campaign and haven't yet started preparation are at the point where a decision needs to be made.

What's been selling in Mt Gravatt this quarter, and what's produced the best results, is consistent with the suburb's broader demand story. Elevated homes with district views have been the product generating the most buyer attention. The Cavendish Road State High School catchment remains a meaningful price driver for homes in the relevant streets, and Westfield Garden City access continues to be part of the lifestyle conversation for buyers with families.

The supply side in Mt Gravatt has been reasonably controlled. Sellers who've priced to current data rather than peak-market expectations have found their campaigns moving well. Those who've overshot have sat.

Median house prices are around $1.25M. Verify via CoreLogic or PropTrack before relying on that figure.

If you're a Mt Gravatt homeowner and are weighing up whether the remaining autumn window works for you, now is the time to get an appraisal and make that call with current information.
