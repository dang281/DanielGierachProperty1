## Caption

Coorparoo has been changing faster than most inner-east suburbs, and the buyer profile is shifting with it.

The commercial strip along Old Cleveland Road is a reasonable indicator of what is happening at a residential level. The quality of cafes and dining has moved up noticeably over the past few years, which is typically a leading signal of residential demand consolidating. When buyers start choosing a suburb partly for its lifestyle offer and not just its proximity to the CBD, the composition of demand becomes more durable and the prices that follow tend to hold through softer cycles.

The train line through Coorparoo provides direct access to the CBD in well under 20 minutes, which is a genuinely rare feature in a suburb at this price point. Most of the inner east relies on bus or ferry access. Coorparoo's rail connection puts it in a smaller subset of suburbs that work for buyers who need reliability and speed in their commute, and that subset commands a premium.

The residential mix spans heritage character homes on the ridge streets through to post-war brick and a growing proportion of renovated or rebuilt product. That diversity attracts a broader buyer cohort than more uniform suburbs, which in turn produces more competitive auction conditions.

The value gap between Coorparoo and Camp Hill and Holland Park has narrowed considerably over the past three years. Homeowners who bought ahead of that narrowing are in a strong position.
