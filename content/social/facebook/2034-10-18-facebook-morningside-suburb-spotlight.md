## Caption

Morningside's evolution over the past decade has followed a familiar inner east pattern: the arrival of younger buyers and renovators who saw value, the gradual transformation of the housing stock through renovation and development, and the eventual repricing of the suburb to reflect its improved amenity and central position.

The suburb now sits at a price point that attracts buyers who have been priced out of Hawthorne and Bulimba but want to remain in the inner east corridor with river access nearby. The character homes in the streets behind Thynne Road attract particular attention, offering pre-war architecture at prices that are still meaningful value relative to the suburbs immediately to the north.

Morningside's school catchments, including Morningside State School, are a consistent draw for families. The proximity to Bulimba and Hawthorne retail, the Wynnum Road bikeway, and public transport to the CBD all support the lifestyle case for buyers who do their homework on the suburb.
