## Caption

Carina Heights sits elevated above the surrounding suburbs with a quiet residential character that families who live here are often reluctant to leave. It is a suburb where people buy and hold, which is precisely what makes it worth understanding as a seller.

The homes are predominantly 1970s and 1980s family builds, many of them on good-sized blocks with renovation potential that has not yet been fully realised. Buyers looking for a project with bones, space, and a liveable suburb while the work happens are a consistent part of the enquiry pool here. The elevated position on many streets delivers aspect that buyers paying equivalent money in flatter suburbs simply cannot access.

Westfield Carindale is ten minutes away for everyday retail, and the Belmont Hills and Chandler sporting facilities give active families options that close inner east suburbs cannot offer. For families arriving from further out who want to move closer to the city without paying Morningside or Camp Hill prices, Carina Heights consistently lands on the shortlist.

Listing frequency here is low. When properties in good streets with sound bones come to market, the buyer pool is ready. That limited supply dynamic is something sellers here can rely on.

If you own in Carina Heights and are considering selling, reach out.
