## Caption

Hawthorne sits at the top of many buyers' lists in Brisbane's inner east, and the reasons are layered in a way that makes the suburb's premium durable rather than speculative.

The Oxford Street strip, shared with Bulimba, is one factor. But what sets Hawthorne apart from its neighbour is the quieter, more residential streets behind Oxford Street, the proximity to the river without the prices of the front-row Oxlade Drive market, and the school catchment access to Balmoral State High School, which is a genuine drawcard for families with secondary school-age children.

The character housing stock is varied, from original Queenslanders on large blocks to more recent townhouse infill in the lower streets. The Queenslanders attract owner-occupiers looking for a long-term family home. The townhouses attract a different buyer pool: professionals, downsizers, and buyers who want the Hawthorne address without the maintenance of a large character home.

Days on market for well-priced Hawthorne properties have historically been short. The suburb draws a deep buyer pool from across Brisbane, and it consistently attracts interstate buyers comparing the value proposition with equivalent Sydney and Melbourne inner suburbs.

For sellers in Hawthorne, the combination of limited stock and consistent demand creates a strong campaign environment when the property is priced correctly and presented well.
