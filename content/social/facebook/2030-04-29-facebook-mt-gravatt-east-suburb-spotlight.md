## Caption

Mt Gravatt East is a suburb that rewards patient observation. It does not have the headline profile of Bulimba or Hawthorne, but for buyers who have done the research, it offers strong land content, elevation, and access at prices that have historically lagged the inner east premium.

The elevated sections of Mt Gravatt East, particularly the streets running off Newnham Road toward the Mt Gravatt Conservation Reserve, carry views and a suburban quietness that buyers from the flat inner east consistently find appealing once they start looking. The proximity to the conservation area gives properties on those streets a green outlook that cannot be built out.

Griffith University at Nathan brings a consistent rental demand to the suburb that supports investor appeal, while the broader family buyer profile responds to the school access, the Westfield Mt Gravatt proximity, and the general sense of a suburb that has been improving progressively over the past decade.

Housing stock here varies considerably. The older 1970s and 1980s brick homes on large blocks are what investors and renovators target. The established character homes in better condition attract owner-occupier families who want to settle and stay.

For sellers considering autumn campaigns, the period from April to May has historically been when the best competition concentrates in this part of the inner east.
