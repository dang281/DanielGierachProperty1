## Caption

Coorparoo sits in a strong position in the inner-east market: close enough to the CBD to attract professionals who want a short commute, large enough in lot size to appeal to families, and served by a retail and café strip on Old Cleveland Road that has continued to improve year on year.

The suburb has benefited from the Cross River Rail network, with Coorparoo Station putting the city within easy reach without a car. That infrastructure connection has attracted a younger buyer demographic who might otherwise have looked at West End or South Brisbane, and it has supported price performance in the streets closest to the station.

Character homes in Coorparoo range from intact Queenslanders to well-renovated post-war houses. The best results consistently come from homes with indoor-outdoor flow and air conditioning, the two features buyers in this market prioritise most.

If your Coorparoo home has been in the family for a while and you're curious what it's worth today, a current appraisal takes about an hour and costs nothing.
