## Caption

Balmoral sits on the Brisbane River, 9km east of the CBD. It has consistently traded at a premium, and the reason is simple: it is the kind of suburb where once people arrive, they tend to stay.

The typical buyer here is an owner-occupier, often upgrading from elsewhere in the inner east. Families anchored to the school catchment, the river, and the character of the streetscape. They are not speculative buyers. They have made a lifestyle decision, and they are committed to it.

That commitment means the buyer pool in Balmoral is more rate-sensitive than an investor-heavy suburb would be, but also more decisive once they have made up their mind. In the current environment, with the cash rate at 4.10 per cent, finance takes longer and buyers are doing more due diligence. But they are still there.

The sellers who do best in Balmoral right now go in with an honest understanding of comparable sales, not an optimistic figure. The suburb has a strong floor. The ceiling depends on preparation, presentation, and accurate pricing.
