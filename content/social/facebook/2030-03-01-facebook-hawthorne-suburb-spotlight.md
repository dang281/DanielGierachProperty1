## Caption

March is when Hawthorne's market typically finds its stride. The buyers who've been watching since the start of the year are active, pre-approvals are in place, and the competition for quality character homes in this suburb is real and consistent. Sellers who are ready to go in March are meeting the market at a good point.

What's been selling in Hawthorne, and what's driven the strongest results, is relatively consistent: character homes that have been extended or renovated to a high standard, on blocks that give families genuine space, within easy reach of Oxford Street. The upsizer buyer from Bulimba who wants more land is a consistent force in this market, and they arrive knowing the suburb well.

Hawthorne's supply constraints are structural. The suburb is established, the housing stock doesn't turn over often, and the number of listings in any given quarter is low relative to buyer demand. That dynamic has kept competition alive through rate rises and market cooling periods that softened results elsewhere.

Median house prices are around $1.85M. Verify this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before relying on it.

If you're a Hawthorne homeowner considering a March or April campaign, preparation needs to start this week. The window is real and it closes by the time the school holidays arrive in mid-April.
