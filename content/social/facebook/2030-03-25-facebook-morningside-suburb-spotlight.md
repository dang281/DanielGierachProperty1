## Caption

Late March is when Morningside's autumn market tends to deliver its sharpest results. The buyers who've been active since February have refined their criteria and are making offers. At the same time, the number of quality listings in this suburb never fully meets demand, so sellers who arrive on market now are competing against very little.

The product that's been selling well in Morningside this quarter is the Queenslander on a full block in the streets that sit north of the railway line and give easy access to the inner east lifestyle. Those homes carry the character, the land, and the position that motivates the suburb's primary buyer. When they're well-presented, they generate the buyer competition that produces strong prices.

The character homes that have been renovated with care, rather than stripped back and modernised without sensitivity, have been the clear outperformers. Buyers in Morningside are discerning. They know what a good renovation looks like here, and they're willing to pay for it. They're also quick to adjust their offer when they can see a property hasn't been presented to its potential.

Median house prices are around $1.55M. Verify this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before relying on it.

If you own in Morningside and are thinking about whether there's still time to launch a campaign before the autumn window closes, the answer for most sellers is yes, but that window is weeks not months.
