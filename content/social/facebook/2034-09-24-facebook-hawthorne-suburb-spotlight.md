## Caption

Hawthorne in spring is as active as any suburb in Brisbane's inner east. Buyers who spent the winter researching the suburb, attending opens and missing out on the limited winter stock, come into September and October with clear intent and pre-approval in hand. The supply-demand balance through this period consistently favours sellers who are well-presented and well-priced.

The suburb's strengths are well established: two ferry terminals, the Oxford Street lifestyle corridor shared with Bulimba, Queenslander streetscapes that are among the most intact in the inner east, and access to several of Brisbane's most sought-after private and state schools. Those fundamentals do not change with the season, but spring's additional buyer depth means they translate into stronger results.

Homes within walking distance of the Hawthorne and Mowbray Park ferry terminals attract particular interest from buyers who commute to the CBD. Properties with elevated river glimpses, north-facing aspects, and renovated but not over-capitalised interiors tend to move within the first three weeks of spring campaigns.

If you own in Hawthorne and want a current read on the market before listing this spring, a no-obligation appraisal is the right first step.
