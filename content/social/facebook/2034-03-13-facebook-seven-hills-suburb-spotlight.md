## Caption

Seven Hills does not produce many sales each year, and that is the central fact any owner here needs to understand. With a small total lot count and an ownership base that tends to hold long-term, the market is thin by definition. That scarcity cuts both ways: comparable sales data can be limited, but when a good property does come to market, competition from buyers who have been waiting is real and concentrated.

The streets along Hillside Road and Seven Hills Road sit at elevation above Enoggera Creek Reserve, and the bush outlook combined with city views from the upper storeys of some homes gives the suburb a character that is genuinely difficult to replicate within the same distance from the CBD. The reserve itself provides direct walking track access, which is a daily feature of life for residents who value it. [VERIFY: current median house price and recent comparable sales before posting.]

Pre-war and post-war Queenslanders make up the bulk of the housing stock, and many have been carefully maintained or selectively updated. Buyers who find their way to Seven Hills are typically well-informed about what they are looking for: character, elevation, green space, and a suburb small enough that they are unlikely to have regular neighbours come to market.

If you have owned in Seven Hills for some time and are thinking about what a sale might achieve in this market, a current appraisal gives you the grounding to make that decision properly. danielgierach.com/suburbs/seven-hills
