## Caption

Carina Heights sits between Carina and Carindale in a pocket of Brisbane's inner east that tends to get overlooked in favour of its neighbours. That is the opportunity for buyers who look closely enough.

The suburb is primarily post-war and 1970s housing stock on generous blocks. Compared to Coorparoo or Camp Hill at a similar distance from the CBD, the entry price has historically been lower, which is exactly what attracts the buyer type who dominates enquiry here: families upgrading from a unit or smaller home who want land without stretching to the prestige suburbs.

School catchments are a significant driver. Whites Hill State College, one of the most sought-after state secondary school catchments in the inner east, draws direct from Carina Heights. Families who want the catchment and cannot afford the premium prices in Seven Hills or Camp Hill often find their value proposition in Carina Heights.

The streets closest to the Carindale border tend to achieve the strongest results, partly due to block size and partly due to buyer familiarity with Carindale's established reputation.

Median days on market in Carina Heights has generally tracked below the broader Brisbane average during peak seasons, which reflects genuine buyer depth in the suburb rather than aspirational pricing.

For homeowners in Carina Heights who are thinking about a 2027 sale, the suburb is in a stronger position than many in the area realise.
