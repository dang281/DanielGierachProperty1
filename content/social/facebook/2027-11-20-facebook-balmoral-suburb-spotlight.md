## Caption

Balmoral is a suburb that buyers discover and rarely leave. The streets that step down toward the river produce the kind of morning light and cross-breeze that cannot be engineered into a new build. Once you have lived here, most other parts of Brisbane feel like a compromise.

The housing stock is predominantly pre-war and post-war Queensland homes, many renovated to a high standard over the past fifteen years. New builds are relatively rare and tend to attract scrutiny from buyers who specifically seek the suburb's character.

Balmoral State School is a consistent demand driver. Properties within the catchment attract a specific buyer profile: families who have done thorough research on inner east schooling and understand the difference in outcomes between catchments. That demand has proven stable across different market conditions.

Balmoral sits between Hawthorne and Morningside, with the Lytton Road strip, ferry access, and Story Bridge proximity making the CBD straightforward without the drive.

For homeowners considering selling, well-presented properties at any price point tend to attract serious buyers. The buyer depth here is genuine.
