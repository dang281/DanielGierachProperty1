## Caption

Coorparoo has been quietly repositioning for the better part of a decade. What was once a stepping-stone suburb has developed its own character and a buyer base that is increasingly loyal.

The Old Cleveland Road retail corridor has improved substantially, with markets, cafes, and independent retailers that have given the suburb a genuine local economy.

Housing stock is diverse: Queenslanders and post-war homes on ridge streets with city views, medium-density townhouses along the corridor, and larger estates at the top end. That diversity means Coorparoo serves a wider buyer pool than most inner east suburbs.

Coorparoo State School is a consistent catchment driver, and properties within walking distance of the school tend to attract a focused buyer group that has done its research and is not easily deterred by market conditions.

Five kilometres from the CBD, Coorparoo sits between Greenslopes and Camp Hill in the inner south-east corridor. For homeowners considering a sale, buyer appetite here has strengthened materially over recent years.
