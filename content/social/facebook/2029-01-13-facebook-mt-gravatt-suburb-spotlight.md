## Caption

Mt Gravatt is one of those suburbs where buyers arrive with a clear picture of what they want and leave with a contract.

The draw is practical: affordable entry into the inner south with access to the Pacific Motorway, direct buses to the CBD, Garden City shopping, and the QEII hospital precinct. For professionals and families alike, the value equation is hard to find elsewhere within 12 kilometres of the city.

The housing stock is a mix of original post-war homes, extensively renovated character properties, and newer townhouses built to meet demand from the hospital workforce. The original homes on larger allotments attract owner-occupiers who want to renovate or build. The units and townhouses appeal to investors who understand the yield that comes with consistent healthcare-sector rental demand.

Mt Gravatt does not generate the same weekend supplement press as Bulimba or Hawthorne, but it does generate steady buyer enquiry from people who know the suburb well and have been watching it for a while. Those are often the most decisive buyers.

If you own in Mt Gravatt and want to understand what the current market looks like for your street, I am happy to have that conversation.
