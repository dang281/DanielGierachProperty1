## Caption

Mt Gravatt East sits at a useful intersection of value and convenience. Greenslopes Private Hospital is a short drive north, Garden City is close for major retail, and the Logan Road dining and restaurant strip in nearby Mt Gravatt provides weekend options without needing to cross the river. It is a suburb that functions well on a daily basis, and that is consistently what buyers tell you matters.

The housing stock here skews toward post-war and 1960s-era homes, with a reasonable proportion of blocks that can accommodate extensions or granny flat additions. For buyers who want to add value over time, or who need dual-occupancy capacity for extended family, Mt Gravatt East has been one of the more practical choices in the southside inner ring. [VERIFY: current median house price before posting.]

The buyer profile is mixed. First-home buyers who need to be within reach of the inner south but cannot stretch to Coorparoo or Greenslopes find Mt Gravatt East workable. Families look at the suburb for block size and school access. Investors target properties near public transport corridors with granny flat potential. That breadth of demand keeps competition across different price points reasonably active.

Proximity to Griffith University (Nathan campus) and the Princess Alexandra Hospital precinct also draws buyers whose work or study is anchored on that side of the city.
