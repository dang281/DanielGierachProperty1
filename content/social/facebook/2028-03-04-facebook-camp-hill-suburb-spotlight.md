## Caption

Camp Hill consistently produces some of the most competitive sale outcomes in Brisbane's inner east, and the reasons have not changed much over the past decade.

The suburb sits 6 to 7km from the CBD on a ridge that provides city views from the elevated streets. Block sizes are generous by inner-suburban standards. The character streetscape is predominantly Queenslander and pre-war timber homes on large flat blocks, which is precisely what the family buyer demographic in this price range is searching for.

What Camp Hill offers sellers is a buyer pool that is both broad and motivated. Families who have identified the suburb as their target are not casual browsers. They understand the market, they know the comparable sales, and when a well-presented property appears at the right price, they act quickly.

The practical advice I give sellers in Camp Hill is consistent: preparation matters more here than in most suburbs because the buyer expectations are high. A property that is genuinely ready to go and accurately priced will attract competition. One that needs obvious work at an optimistic price will not.

The autumn window running from now through May is historically the strongest period for Camp Hill campaigns.

If you are thinking about selling in Camp Hill, I am happy to walk through what the current market looks like for your property.
