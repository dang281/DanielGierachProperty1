## Caption

The autumn campaign period suits Cannon Hill well. The suburb's streetscape, with its mature trees and post-war homes on decent blocks, presents at its best in the softer February and March light. Buyers who are active right now after a slow January are looking across a range of inner east suburbs, and Cannon Hill's price point relative to Morningside and Bulimba is a consistent point of difference.

What's been selling in Cannon Hill is the product that has reliable buyer depth: post-war homes on full blocks in the streets that connect well to the motorway and the shopping centre, with enough land to give owner-occupiers expansion options. Rental yield has kept investor interest alive, and with vacancy rates low, the numbers have held up for those buyers.

Supply has remained measured. Cannon Hill isn't a high-volume listing suburb, which works for sellers who are prepared and priced correctly. The homes that sit on market are generally the ones that have overestimated their price or underestimated their preparation.

Median house prices are around $1.15M. Verify this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before relying on it.

If you own in Cannon Hill and are thinking about selling this autumn, the buyer pool is there. The question is timing and preparation.
