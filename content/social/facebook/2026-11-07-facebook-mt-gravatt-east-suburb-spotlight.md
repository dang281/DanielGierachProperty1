## Caption

Mt Gravatt East is the suburb where the value case and the lifestyle case point to the same conclusion.

It sits between the elevated green belt of Whites Hill Regional Park to the east and the commercial corridors of Mt Gravatt to the west. The park is not incidental to the suburb. It shapes the character of the eastern streets in a way that is hard to replicate closer to the city. Residents on streets backing the reserve get a rear-property outlook that belongs to a different price bracket.

The buyer profile here has shifted over the past few years. The original appeal was affordability relative to Camp Hill and Coorparoo. That gap has narrowed, and in some price ranges it has effectively closed. What now draws buyers to Mt Gravatt East is the school catchment, in particular the Whites Hill State College zone, and the sheer amount of property they get for the price relative to suburbs with more recognised names.

The land component of properties in the suburb is a genuine differentiator. Lots in the flatter sections of Mt Gravatt East that have development potential are attracting a different category of buyer than the owner-occupier market that has historically dominated here.

If you own in Mt Gravatt East and have been watching conditions, the current demand profile is worth understanding in specific terms rather than general ones.
