## Caption

Camp Hill's identity is built on something that most suburb profiles miss: the people who buy here tend to stay.

The turnover rate in Camp Hill is consistently lower than comparable inner-south suburbs, and that says something about what buyers are looking for when they choose it. The streets around Robertson Road and Halford Street are predominantly owner-occupied, well-maintained, and sit on blocks that have become increasingly scarce in the inner east. When that stock does come to market, it moves quickly.

The buyer profile is almost exclusively owner-occupiers. Families are drawn by school access, primarily Whites Hill State College, and by the suburb's position relative to the services on Old Cleveland Road without being directly on the arterial. The lifestyle offer is quiet residential with real amenity close by, which is a combination that has become harder to find at this price point in Brisbane's south-east.

One observation that comes up consistently from buyers in Camp Hill is the sense that the suburb is complete. It is not a transitional address for people on their way somewhere else. That stability has a price, and it shows in the fact that well-positioned homes attract multiple registered bidders at auction even when the broader market is quiet.

Spring is historically one of the stronger selling windows in Camp Hill, as families time their moves around school cycles. If you are considering your options, understanding the current conditions before that window opens is worth doing now.
