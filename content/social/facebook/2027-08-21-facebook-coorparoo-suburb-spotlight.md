## Caption

Coorparoo is one of the inner east's most connected suburbs, sitting on direct bus and train corridors into the CBD with a frequency and convenience that most comparable suburbs simply cannot match.

That transport access is not the only reason buyers come here, but it is consistently one of the first things they mention. The suburb itself spans a wide range, from entry-level units along the Old Cleveland Road strip to large, elevated family homes on the northern and western blocks, giving buyers at different price points a genuine foothold in the inner east. The variety is something neighbouring Camp Hill cannot offer in the same way.

Coorparoo State School is a strong and consistently popular catchment, and properties within its boundary tend to command a premium over otherwise comparable homes outside it. For family buyers, that catchment line is not a minor consideration. It actively shapes where they search and what they are prepared to pay.

The Coorparoo Square retail precinct has added amenity to the main strip, bringing more foot traffic and day-to-day convenience to a suburb that was already well-served. Character homes from the 1930s through to the 1960s sit alongside well-renovated properties and newer builds, giving the streetscape more texture than many suburbs at similar price points.
