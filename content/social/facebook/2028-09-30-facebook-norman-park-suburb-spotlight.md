## Caption

Norman Park is one of those inner east suburbs where the residents are fiercely loyal and the buyers who arrive from outside the suburb quickly understand why.

Whites Hill Reserve is the centrepiece. The park sits in the middle of the suburb and provides green space, walking trails, and a community focal point that is genuinely uncommon for a suburb of Norman Park's size and density. Streets that back onto or face the reserve regularly command premiums that reflect the rarity of that outlook.

The housing stock is a mix of original Queenslanders and post-war timber homes, many of them substantially extended and renovated over recent decades. The blocks are typically solid, and the elevated streets toward the ridge offer partial city views that add meaningful value above the lower Norman Park streets.

Norman Park State School is a strong community anchor, and the proximity to the Whites Hill State College catchment is the dominant buyer driver for families with secondary school-age children. The school factor is not marginal here. It is the primary reason many families choose Norman Park over cheaper adjacent suburbs.

The Norman Park train station provides direct access to the CBD and links the suburb to the broader inner east transport network. For buyers who want to combine walkability, school access, and a strong residential community in one package, Norman Park consistently delivers.

Median house prices reflect the school catchment premium clearly. The streets closest to Whites Hill State College transact at the upper end of the suburb range.
