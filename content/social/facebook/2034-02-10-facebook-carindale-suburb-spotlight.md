## Caption

Carindale is built around a logic that works for families: Westfield Carindale for everyday shopping, Logan Road for dining and services, Gateway and South East Motorway access for commuting north or south, and quiet residential streets in between. That combination has sustained consistent demand here for decades.

The housing stock spans post-war originals through to 1990s brick and tile homes on solid suburban blocks. Many of the original homes have been extended or renovated, and there is a meaningful supply of properties that offer four bedrooms, a double garage, and backyard space at a price point that the inner suburbs cannot match. That is the proposition Carindale delivers, and it resonates with families trading up from smaller homes or relocating from interstate. [VERIFY: current median house price before posting.]

Buyers in Carindale tend to be family-focused owner-occupiers. The suburb is not a high-turnover market; people come here and stay for the school years. When properties do come to market, they draw interest from buyers who have been watching the suburb for some time, particularly those targeting the eastern suburbs corridor with motorway access to both the CBD and the Gold Coast.

School catchment access to Carindale State School and proximity to a range of secondary options adds to the family appeal.
