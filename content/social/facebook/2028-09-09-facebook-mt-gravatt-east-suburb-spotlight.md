## Caption

Mt Gravatt East is one of the more underappreciated suburbs in Brisbane's inner east market, and experienced buyers tend to know it before the broader market catches up.

The suburb offers an elevation advantage that is rare at its price point. The ridge running through the centre of Mt Gravatt East gives many properties views that buyers in the premium inner suburbs pay significantly more for. The views look north toward the city and east toward the bay, and the best streets offer that outlook from both the living areas and the outdoor spaces.

The Gateway Motorway and Pacific Motorway access make Mt Gravatt East practical for buyers who work across Brisbane's south or who travel to the Gold Coast regularly. That transport connectivity gives the suburb a catchment of buyers who would otherwise look further afield, and it supports rental demand for investors.

The housing stock is mostly post-war brick, with a proportion of homes that have been extended and updated. The blocks are generally solid, and the prices represent genuine value relative to what comparable land and views cost in the premium inner east suburbs.

Griffith University's Nathan campus and the Macgregor State School catchment are both draws for specific buyer segments: international students and families seeking Queensland's public school academic pathways respectively.
