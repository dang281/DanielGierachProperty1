## Caption

SQM Research puts Brisbane's residential vacancy rate at 0.6 per cent as of February 2026. The national average is 1.1 per cent. Brisbane has been the tightest capital city rental market for most of the past two years.

A vacancy rate below 1 per cent means very few investment properties sit empty. For landlords, that means consistent rental income and upward pressure on rents. Brisbane gross rental yields in the inner east are sitting around 3.5 to 4 per cent for houses, with units pushing higher.

The counter-argument is straightforward. The RBA cash rate is at 4.10 per cent after two consecutive hikes in early 2026. The interest bill on an investment loan at current rates is significantly larger than it was in 2021, and serviceability buffers make it harder to borrow the same amount.

SQM Research expects vacancy to stay below 1.5 per cent through 2026 as population growth continues to outpace new housing supply in south-east Queensland.

Whether the numbers stack up for an investment property depends heavily on your individual borrowing position and the specific property. Happy to walk through the inner east investment picture with you.
