## Caption

Seven Hills is priced well below Hawthorne and Bulimba. For buyers who discover it, that gap rarely makes sense once they have driven the streets.

The suburb sits 5km east of the CBD on an elevated ridge, with one of Brisbane's larger inner-city bushland reserves on its boundary. Cavendish Road State High School catchment. Established blocks with room. City views from the ridge streets. On the fundamentals that drive inner-east pricing, Seven Hills scores well above its current market position.

The reason for the discount is mostly about profile. Hawthorne and Bulimba have visible café strips and active lifestyle anchors. Seven Hills is quieter. Its character comes from the elevation, the reserve, and the streetscape rather than foot traffic. Harder to market broadly, which means it tends to be underrepresented in the buyer awareness that drives competition.

That also means lower transaction volume: around 47 houses traded in Seven Hills in the past 12 months. For sellers, that is a double-edged reality. The buyer pool is self-selecting and motivated. But the comparable sales base is shallow, which means accurate pricing matters more here than in a high-volume suburb. Overpricing does not work. Accurate pricing with strong presentation does.

If you are thinking about selling in Seven Hills, I am happy to walk through what the numbers look like.
