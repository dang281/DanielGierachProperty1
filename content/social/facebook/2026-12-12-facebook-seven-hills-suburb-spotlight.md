## Caption

Seven Hills sits just far enough from the inner east's main arterials to feel removed from them, but close enough to everything that matters to make that distance irrelevant.

The suburb's defining feature is its hillside position south-west of Coorparoo. The elevated blocks deliver the kind of views across Brisbane that buyers in flat suburbs spend significantly more money trying to buy. Properties on the upper streets have outlook as a genuine asset, not just a marketing note.

The buyer profile has shifted in Seven Hills over the past few years. It used to attract the buyer who had been priced out of Coorparoo. It now attracts buyers who have actively chosen it for the elevation, the established trees, the larger blocks relative to most inner-east suburbs at comparable prices, and the proximity to the Toohey Forest Park trail network.

The school options are strong. The Brisbane State High School catchment covers parts of Seven Hills, and that alone draws a buyer type who will not be negotiated significantly below their price ceiling.

For a suburb that spent years being defined by what it was next to rather than what it was, Seven Hills has established its own identity. The demand it attracts now is not overflow from adjacent suburbs. It is intentional.
