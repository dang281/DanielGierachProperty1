## Caption

Oxford Street does not need much introduction to anyone who has spent time in Brisbane's inner east. The stretch of cafes, restaurants, and independent retailers between Bulimba Creek and the river ferry terminal is one of the few genuinely walkable lifestyle strips in Brisbane. That is the drawcard for a lot of buyers, and it is what keeps Bulimba prices where they are.

The ferry terminal at the eastern end of Oxford Street connects directly to the city and to the north side, which matters for professionals who want to live somewhere with a real neighbourhood feel but still need to be in the CBD regularly. The river frontage and heritage-listed streetscapes give parts of the suburb a character that newer suburbs simply cannot replicate.

The buyer profile is consistent: professionals, dual-income couples, and families who are prepared to trade land size for proximity and amenity. A renovated Queenslander on a 400sqm block here holds its value differently to a similar home in the suburbs further east, because the lifestyle infrastructure around it is already built.

Competition in Bulimba sits between character homes that have been well renovated and smaller lots where newer builds have replaced the original housing stock. Both sell well when presented correctly.

Median house price in Bulimba is approximately $1.7 million, with typical days on market around 25 to 35 days. ⚠️ VERIFY both figures before posting.
