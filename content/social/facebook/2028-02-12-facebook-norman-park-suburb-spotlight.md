## Caption

Norman Park is a suburb that rewards buyers who discover it before it is the most obvious answer on their shortlist.

It sits 5km south-east of the CBD along the river, with elevated ridge streets, a bus corridor into the city, and a character streetscape dominated by well-maintained Queenslanders and period brick homes. The suburb has its own low-key village retail strip, a quiet community feel, and a catchment that includes several well-regarded state schools.

The buyer profile is predominantly owner-occupiers: families and couples who have done a genuine comparison of the inner east and concluded that Norman Park delivers the most consistent combination of character, liveability, and location at a price point below Hawthorne and Bulimba.

For sellers, Norman Park tends to produce campaigns with a self-selecting buyer pool. The people who come through have usually been looking in the suburb deliberately rather than landing there by process of elimination. That motivation level changes how they engage with the property and how quickly they act.

If you own in Norman Park and have been thinking about what your home might be worth in the current market, I am happy to give you an honest, research-based answer.
