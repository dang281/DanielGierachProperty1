## Caption

At 3km from the CBD, East Brisbane has the kind of walkability score that most Brisbane suburbs cannot approach. Residents walk to South Bank, cross the Story Bridge to the CBD, or take a short trip to New Farm and West End without needing a car. For buyers who want inner-city access without an inner-city address, East Brisbane has been the answer for a long time.

The character homes here, predominantly heritage Queenslanders and workers cottages on compact lots, are the reason the suburb holds its value so stubbornly. There is very little vacant land and no capacity for broad infill, which means supply stays tight. Owners in East Brisbane are typically long-term, and when a well-maintained home does come to market, buyer competition tends to be strong and fast-moving. The buyer profile is predominantly owner-occupiers: professionals, couples, and smaller families who prioritise proximity and walkability above block size. Heritage character is a genuine drawcard. Buyers who come to East Brisbane are not comparing it to Carindale on square metres; they are comparing it to New Farm and Kangaroo Point on lifestyle and price.

Stock in East Brisbane rarely sits unsold for long. The combination of location, character, and genuine supply constraint produces consistently competitive conditions at the top end.
