## Caption

Balmoral punches above its size in almost every conversation about Brisbane's inner east property market.

The suburb is defined by its elevated terrain, the timber character homes that line the ridges above the river, and a level of streetscape consistency that is rare within 7 kilometres of the Brisbane CBD. Buyers who discover Balmoral for the first time often come having looked first at Hawthorne or Bulimba and then realising the same quality of street and character, with fewer listings and a slightly different buyer profile.

The Balmoral State School catchment is a significant driver of demand from families. The beach, while not exactly a beach, is a practical and genuinely used local amenity. The ferry access from the Bulimba Wharf, within cycling distance, gives Balmoral residents a CBD commute option that most comparable suburbs cannot offer.

What makes a Balmoral campaign interesting is the buyer competition. The suburb does not produce enough listings in any given quarter to satisfy the number of qualified buyers who are watching it. When a well-maintained home on a good street comes to market, especially one with views or an elevated position, the response is immediate.

If you own in Balmoral and have been thinking about what your property might achieve in the current market, I am happy to give you an honest assessment.
