## Caption

Mt Gravatt East occupies an elevated position with Wishart Road and Cavendish Road forming its main corridors, and Westfield Garden City a short drive in either direction. It is a suburb that works practically for families and has done so for a long time.

The post-war family home stock here sits on blocks that compare well to equivalent land sizes in Coorparoo or Camp Hill, often at a lower entry point. That value differential is what draws buyers who have been looking at the closer inner east and found the price gap difficult to bridge. Griffith University's Mt Gravatt campus adds a quiet employment and academic anchor nearby, and the South East Freeway access is a genuine asset for buyers who commute.

School options are solid across both the state and private sectors, with several private school bus corridors servicing the suburb. The buyer profile is predominantly families and long-term owner-occupiers, and the dual-occupancy potential on some larger blocks adds a secondary buyer type that broadens competition.

For sellers, the story in Mt Gravatt East is consistent buyer demand with a price point that remains accessible enough to maintain depth. When well-presented properties in good streets come to market, the enquiry reflects that depth clearly.

If you own in Mt Gravatt East and are considering selling, reach out.
