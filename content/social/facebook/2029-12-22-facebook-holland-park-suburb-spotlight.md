## Caption

Holland Park does not get the same attention as Camp Hill or Seven Hills, but buyers who do their homework find the fundamentals here are solid. The southern edge of the inner east, positioned between the inner city and the southeast motorway, means the suburb works practically for people who need access in multiple directions.

The postwar housing stock is what draws the renovation buyer. Original 1950s and 1960s homes on decent blocks represent the kind of project that an experienced renovator or owner-builder can work with, and the suburb's price point relative to Camp Hill or Seven Hills gives that buyer room to move. Buyers who cannot stretch to those suburbs without compromising on block size or home quality often find Holland Park makes more sense.

Holland Park State School and Holland Park State High School are both well-regarded and represent a genuine catchment advantage. For families choosing a suburb with the school decision in mind, having both a primary and secondary school option locally reduces one of the practical complications of the inner east schooling landscape.

The proximity to Westfield Garden City via Mount Gravatt covers retail and dining needs, and the transit corridors along Logan Road and Cavendish Road are seeing gradual density increases that reflect the suburb's long-term trajectory.
