## Caption

Coorparoo's commercial strip along Old Cleveland Road gives the suburb a practical amenity base that residents use daily rather than weekly. The cafes, independent retail, and services along the strip create the walkable high street character that buyers from the inner north and inner west specifically seek when they are relocating to the inner east. For buyers who are making a first move into the inner east, Coorparoo often wins on the combination of recognisable suburb character and price accessibility.

The suburb's proximity to the CBD, at roughly four kilometres, is significant for buyers who want inner city access without inner city pricing. The bus network along Old Cleveland Road provides a frequent service into the city, and the cycling infrastructure in the area has improved the active transport options for buyers who want to commute without a car.

The school catchment dynamics in Coorparoo draw from both the state and private sector. The combination of Coorparoo State School, Coorparoo Secondary College, and proximity to several independent schools creates a school access profile that accommodates a range of family priorities. Buyers who are making an address decision based on school access find Coorparoo's options comprehensive.

Winter buyer activity in Coorparoo is sustained by the suburb's consistent demand profile. The suburb does not depend on spring to generate buyer interest.
