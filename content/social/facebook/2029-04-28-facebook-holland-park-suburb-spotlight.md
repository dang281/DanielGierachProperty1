## Caption

Holland Park delivers something the suburbs closer to the river have largely run out of: room to move without paying premium prices for it.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war and early brick homes, many of them still in original or partially renovated condition, sitting on blocks between 500 and 700 square metres. The renovation opportunity is part of what drives buyer interest here. Holland Park West and the streets north of Logan Road attract buyers who want character and space in a suburb they can grow into over the next decade rather than one they are priced out of in three years.

The suburb's elevation gives many blocks genuine views across the southern suburbs. It is an underrated attribute that buyers from outside the area sometimes discover for the first time at inspection and immediately adjust their expectations accordingly.

Wellers Hill State School is the catchment anchor for parts of Holland Park, and school catchment questions come up consistently in buyer conversations about this suburb and its surrounds.

Practical access matters here too. The Logan Road corridor connects Holland Park to the CBD via Coorparoo efficiently, and the distance to the inner east amenities around Camp Hill and Carindale is manageable by car or bike.
