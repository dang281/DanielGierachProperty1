## Caption

Murarrie doesn't always make it onto the inner east shortlist, but the buyers who do their research keep coming back to it. The suburb sits at the gateway to Morningside, borders the river, and has direct access to the Gateway Motorway, which makes it genuinely practical for households where one or both partners work outside the inner city ring. That combination of location and price point is harder to find than it looks on a map.

The residential pockets here are tightly held. A lot of Murarrie's housing stock is post-war and brick, with a growing number of newer townhouse developments filling in around the edges. It's not a suburb where turnover is especially high, which means when a good property does come to market, buyers tend to notice. The riverside sections, particularly where Murarrie meets the river near Bulimba, carry their own appeal for buyers who want water proximity without paying Hawthorne prices.

Median prices sit below adjoining Morningside and Cannon Hill, which positions Murarrie as one of the more realistic entry points to this stretch of the inner east. For buyers who have been circling Morningside or Cannon Hill without quite getting there, Murarrie often becomes the suburb that makes financial sense without requiring compromise on liveability or commute.

If you own in Murarrie and you're considering your options in the current market, I'd be happy to walk you through what's been selling and what your property might be worth.
