## Caption

Carina Heights occupies a position in the inner east market that is worth understanding precisely: it delivers most of Carindale's advantages at a price point that reflects its location rather than its amenity.

The suburb is bounded by the Carindale shopping precinct to the east, Whites Hill Reserve to the north, and the Camp Hill residential precinct to the west. For the buyer who wants proximity to major retail, good public and private school options, and access to the Eastern Freeway corridor, Carina Heights is a consistent entry point to the inner east without the premium of the suburbs immediately to the west.

The housing stock in Carina Heights is predominantly post-war and 1970s construction, with a mix of original homes and well-executed renovations. The elevated streets on the western edge of the suburb, those closest to the Whites Hill Reserve boundary, attract the strongest prices and the most consistent buyer competition. These properties offer the reserve outlook and the school catchment combination that defined buyers in this precinct actively seek.

Supply is generally moderate. The suburb turns over at a pace that keeps enough comparable evidence in the market to support confident appraisals but does not dilute buyer competition when good properties arrive.

If you own in Carina Heights and want to understand where your property sits in the current market, I am happy to have that conversation.
