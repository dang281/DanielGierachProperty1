## Caption

Camp Hill has a school catchment story that repeats across nearly every buyer conversation I have in the suburb. Camp Hill State School is one of the most consistently oversubscribed primary schools in the inner east, and the streets within its catchment are priced accordingly.

The suburb sits on elevated terrain between Coorparoo and Carina Heights, with streets that roll across ridgelines and return city views from the better-positioned homes. The housing stock is a genuine mix of original Queenslanders in various states of renovation, architect-designed rebuilds, and post-war brick homes that represent the renovation opportunity for buyers who cannot afford the turnkey price points.

The buyer profile in Camp Hill is almost entirely owner-occupying families. It is not a suburb with a strong investor market. The people who buy here are buying to live here, and they are making a considered decision about the school catchment, the elevation, and the suburb's established residential character.

The Camp Hill shopping precinct on Old Cleveland Road provides the everyday convenience that complements the residential streets. A growing number of cafes and independent businesses have improved the lifestyle amenity over the past five years.

For sellers in Camp Hill, the autumn window is the most competitive period. The family buyer who is active in March is the buyer who drives results in this suburb.
