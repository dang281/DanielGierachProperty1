## Caption

Norman Park is the quiet achiever of Brisbane's inner east.

It does not have the Oxford Street café strip of Bulimba or the river ferry access of Hawthorne, but it does have Whites Hill Reserve running along its eastern boundary, some of the best-preserved Queenslander streetscapes in the inner east, and a school catchment that includes Norman Park State School and is within reach of the Anglican Church Grammar School.

The buyer who chooses Norman Park over Bulimba or Hawthorne is typically making a deliberate trade: a larger block, more house for the price, and streets that feel quieter without being far from anything. The suburb is five minutes from Oxford Street and ten minutes from the CBD by car. The families who move here tend to stay.

That stickiness shows in the data. Turnover in Norman Park is lower than in some of the higher-profile inner east suburbs, and when a property does come to market, especially a well-presented home on a corner block or ridge position, the buyer competition tends to be immediate and focused.

If you own in Norman Park and want to understand what the current buyer demand looks like for your address, I am happy to sit down and give you a straight picture.
