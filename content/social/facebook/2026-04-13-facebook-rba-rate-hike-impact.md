## Caption

The RBA raised the cash rate to 4.10 per cent in March, a 25 basis point hike that divided the board, five members voting to raise and four voting to hold.

For Brisbane property, the picture is more nuanced than the headline suggests.

Listings are down around 26 per cent year-on-year according to CoreLogic. Days on market in Brisbane is sitting at just 17 days. Buyers who have finance approved are still competing for well-positioned properties. The rate environment is real, but it has not broken demand in Brisbane's inner east.

What the hike does change: buyers at the edge of their borrowing capacity are being pushed back. The buyer pool is smaller and more deliberate than it was at the peak. That is not a reason for sellers to panic. It is a reason to price accurately and present your property well.

The next RBA decision is May 5. Between now and then, the market keeps moving.

If you are trying to work out what this means for your specific property in the inner east, I am happy to have that conversation.
