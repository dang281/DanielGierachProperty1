## Caption

Mt Gravatt East is picking up attention from buyers who've been looking at Camp Hill and Coorparoo and found themselves priced just outside what they're comfortable stretching to. The suburb shares a border with both, offers a similar mix of post-war brick homes and character renovations, and sits at a price point that still makes the numbers work for families entering the inner east bracket.

Cavendish Road is the main artery through the suburb and runs bus services that connect residents to the city without requiring a car for the commute. Westfield Carindale is close enough via Cavendish Road that daily shopping and errands are covered without much effort. Some of the elevated streets in the suburb carry views toward the coast that are worth factoring in when you're looking at comparable properties in the area.

The school catchment includes Coorparoo Secondary College and Mansfield State High at the secondary level, which appeals to families with older children. The suburb is quieter than Coorparoo in terms of through traffic, which tends to suit owner-occupiers who want the access without the noise.

Buyers moving toward Mt Gravatt East are typically doing so because Camp Hill and Coorparoo have moved beyond what they can justify. That buyer pool has been building steadily, which tends to push prices in one direction over time. If you own in Mt Gravatt East and you're wondering what your property might achieve, I'd be happy to walk through the recent data with you.
