## Caption

Carina Heights sits on elevated ground between Carina and Carindale, and that elevation does more than provide views. It shapes the character of the suburb in a way that sets it apart from the flat land immediately surrounding it.

The streets running along the ridge lines carry a different quality of property to the surrounding suburbs at comparable prices. Blocks tend to be larger. The established tree canopy is thicker. The outlook from many sites extends across Brisbane's south-east in a way that buyers who discover the suburb for the first time often did not expect.

The practical case is also strong. The Westfield Carindale complex is within a short drive, giving residents access to retail and services that most inner-ring suburbs do not have immediately adjacent. The arterial road access to the CBD and to the M1 motorway south is efficient without the suburb itself sitting on a main road.

The buyer profile is weighted toward families who have looked at Coorparoo and Camp Hill, found the price ceiling limiting, and discovered that Carina Heights delivers land content, elevation, and presentation quality that the more recognisable suburbs have progressively stopped offering at accessible prices.

For owners in Carina Heights, spring through to year-end tends to be the period when buyer activity from that profile is at its highest.
