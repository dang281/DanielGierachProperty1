## Caption

Norman Park has one of the most loyal buyer pools in Brisbane's inner east. The families who want this suburb want it specifically, not as a compromise for Hawthorne or Balmoral.

The elevated streets north of Whites Hill Reserve are the most sought-after pockets. Blocks on those streets that look back toward the city or across the reserve have consistently produced results above suburb median prices because the combination of elevation, outlook, and quiet residential character is genuinely rare in a suburb this close to the CBD.

Whites Hill Reserve is the suburb's centrepiece: 70 hectares of bushland reserve with walking tracks, picnic facilities, and genuine natural buffer from the surrounding residential density. It is the kind of infrastructure that is impossible to build from scratch and impossible to replicate once it is there. Buyers who prioritise liveability in a dense urban context find Norman Park for that reason.

Clem Jones College and the school catchment boundaries for the state school system attract a significant portion of the family buyer pool that inspects here. The catchment question comes up at almost every Norman Park open home I run.

Properties in Norman Park tend to move with limited time on market when they are well-prepared and correctly priced. When they are not, they sit, because this buyer pool is patient and knows the suburb well enough to wait for value.
