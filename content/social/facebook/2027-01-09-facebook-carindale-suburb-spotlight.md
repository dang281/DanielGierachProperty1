## Caption

Carindale is one of the few suburbs in Brisbane's inner east where you can still find a four-bedroom home on a 700-square-metre block at a price that makes sense for a family buyer.

The suburb is anchored by Westfield Carindale, which provides retail convenience that matters to families and gives the suburb a clear identity. The streets north and west of the shopping centre are predominantly 1970s to 1990s housing on larger blocks, and that is where most of the family buyer activity concentrates.

The buyer profile in Carindale skews strongly toward owner-occupying families, often with school-aged children. The suburb sits within the Carindale State School catchment for primary, with secondary students typically accessing Belmont State High School or Cannon Hill Anglican College depending on the street.

What makes Carindale interesting for sellers is that the suburb's price growth story has been quieter than the prestige inner east, meaning there is still headroom relative to equivalent suburbs closer to the CBD. Buyers who have been priced out of Camp Hill or Norman Park often land in Carindale and find they get meaningfully more land and space for a similar budget.

For homeowners in Carindale who have been watching the market, 2027's autumn window is worth considering seriously.
