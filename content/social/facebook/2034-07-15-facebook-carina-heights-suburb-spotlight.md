## Caption

Carina Heights sits on the ridge between Carindale and Carina, and the elevation translates in two ways: better views from elevated blocks and a quieter residential character than the flat streets below.

The housing stock in Carina Heights is predominantly post-war and early renovation era, which means buyers often find solid bones at prices that reflect the suburb's lower profile relative to Camp Hill or Coorparoo. The trade-off is walking distance to retail, which is limited compared to the main-road suburbs. The Carindale Shopping Centre nearby partially compensates.

The buyer profile here is practical and value-focused. Families and owner-occupiers who have done the suburb research and understand that the price-per-square-metre advantage over adjacent suburbs is real. The infrastructure around Carina Heights, including school catchments, public transport corridors, and proximity to the Eastern Busway, is stronger than the suburb's market price currently reflects.
