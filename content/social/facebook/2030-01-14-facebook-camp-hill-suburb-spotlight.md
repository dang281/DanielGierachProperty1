## Caption

Camp Hill is one of those suburbs where the demand story hasn't changed in years, because the fundamentals haven't changed. Whites Hill State College catchment shared with Norman Park, the Martha Street retail strip for coffee and day-to-day errands, heritage and character overlay protecting the streetscape in parts, and a tight supply of well-positioned homes on the elevated side. That combination keeps prices firm and buyer competition consistent.

The buyer profile is almost entirely families. Many are upsizing from Coorparoo or Carina, arriving with a clear idea of what they want and prepared to move quickly when the right property appears. They know Camp Hill, they've been watching it, and when something good comes up, they act.

Median house prices are sitting around $1.65M, though you should check current figures via CoreLogic or PropTrack before using that number in any decision. The best-performing homes are those with character features intact, a full land component, and position on the high side with some aspect.

Supply remains the defining feature of this market. Camp Hill doesn't produce many listings, and when it does, the homes that are well-presented and properly positioned sell quickly. Days on market for quality stock is short.
