## Caption

Murarrie is the inner east suburb that has been on the move for longer than most people realise. It sits directly between Cannon Hill and Bulimba, with access to the Gateway Motorway that makes it genuinely attractive for buyers who need to travel across Brisbane's north or south.

The suburb underwent a significant demographic shift as buyers priced out of Bulimba and Hawthorne moved east in search of comparable land sizes at lower entry points. What they found was a suburb with good housing stock, reasonable proximity to the river and inner east amenity, and a price point that still offered value relative to its immediate neighbours.

The housing mix is predominantly post-war brick and timber on standard residential blocks, with the western end of the suburb closest to Cannon Hill and Bulimba commanding the highest prices. The eastern end toward the Gateway is more industrial-adjacent and attracts a different buyer profile.

The Cannon Hill Anglican College catchment has become an increasingly important factor for families considering Murarrie, as it provides access to a well-regarded private school at a lower purchase price than equivalent Bulimba addresses.

Days on market for correctly priced Murarrie properties have been shortening as buyer demand from adjacent suburbs has increased. The suburb is no longer the afterthought it was a decade ago.
