## Caption

Carina sits alongside Carindale and Carina Heights and is often the first of the three that buyers in this part of the inner east encounter. The suburb has a consistent residential character: post-war and early-renovation homes on practical block sizes, proximity to Westfield Carindale, and a price point that has historically sat slightly below the more prominent inner east names.

The buyer pool in Carina includes a wide range. First-home buyers stretching into the inner east find Carina more accessible than Camp Hill or Norman Park. Investors assess it for its rental demand from the Griffith University and hospital precincts. Families with school-age children research the local catchments before looking at anything else.

The streets running toward the ridgeline and those adjacent to local parklands tend to attract more interest. Properties that are well-presented and positioned correctly tend to generate a higher volume of enquiry than owners often expect.
