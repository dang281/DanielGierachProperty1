## Caption

Morningside sits on the boundary of two different property conversations.

North of Wynnum Road, on the river-facing slopes toward Hawthorne and Bulimba, it competes directly with the most sought-after streets in the inner east. The views from those ridgeline positions attract buyers who would otherwise be looking at Hawthorne, and the price gap between the two suburbs has been narrowing for several years.

South of Wynnum Road, the suburb shifts in character: more post-war housing, larger land parcels, and a buyer pool that is strongly owner-occupier and family-focused. The proximity to Morningside Plaza, the State School and Anglican Church Grammar School, and the Cannon Hill retail strip gives this part of the suburb its own identity and its own repeat buyer demand.

What unites both parts of Morningside is access. Wynnum Road is one of the more direct routes to the CBD, and the ferry at Bulimba is within cycling distance for the northern part of the suburb. That convenience is priced in, and buyers who understand the inner east know it.
