## Caption

Two to three kilometres from the Brisbane CBD, East Brisbane offers inner-city proximity that most suburbs in this price range simply cannot deliver.

The suburb sits adjacent to the Gabba, which continues to attract significant development activity in the lead-up to the 2032 Olympics, and that activity has added a layer of medium-term interest to a suburb that already had strong underlying fundamentals. The western pocket near the Gabba is more urban and mixed-use in character, while the eastern streets bordering Woolloongabba and Kangaroo Point are quieter and more residential. The two ends of the suburb have a different feel, and buyers often have a clear preference for one over the other.

Units and townhouses make up a meaningful share of the housing stock here, more so than in many other inner east suburbs. Freestanding homes on decent-sized blocks are the sought-after minority in East Brisbane, and when they do appear, they tend to attract buyers who have been watching specifically for that type of property. The buyer profile is broad: CBD professionals who want a short commute, buyers who looked at New Farm or Teneriffe first and found East Brisbane a more realistic entry point, and investors who see consistent rental demand in a high-employment corridor.
