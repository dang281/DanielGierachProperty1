## Caption

East Brisbane's property market is driven by one thing more than any other: proximity to the CBD. At under two kilometres from the city, it offers the kind of access that buyers who prioritise their commute are willing to pay a premium for. The catchment includes workers at Roma Street, the precinct around South Bank and Woolloongabba, and professionals who want to walk or cycle to work.

The suburb is compact, with limited new supply, and the buyer pool consistently includes people who have been outbid on properties here before and return when the next one becomes available. That repeat buyer dynamic drives strong competition for well-presented properties in any season.

Heritage-listed and pre-war homes in East Brisbane command a meaningful premium over more recently built properties. The character of the built form is the product, and buyers who value it are willing to bid accordingly.

For owners who have held in East Brisbane for several years, the capital growth record is one of the stronger in the inner east. If you are considering what a sale might look like, danielgierach.com/suburbs/east-brisbane is the starting point.
