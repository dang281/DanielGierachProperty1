## Caption

Mt Gravatt East sits at a price point that is drawing genuine buyer interest from multiple directions, and that breadth of demand is worth understanding if you own here. First-home buyers who need inner south access but cannot stretch to Greenslopes or Coorparoo find the suburb workable. Families who need more space than the inner suburbs offer at a comparable price come here for the block sizes. And investors who want proximity to the Princess Alexandra Hospital and Griffith University employment corridors target the rental yield potential in the suburb.

Greenslopes Private Hospital is a short drive north, Garden City covers major retail to the south, and the Logan Road restaurant strip in nearby Mt Gravatt provides weekend dining without crossing the river. Day-to-day life in Mt Gravatt East functions on its own terms; you do not need the CBD to run your week. [VERIFY: current median house price before posting.]

The housing stock spans post-war originals through to 1960s brick homes, many of which carry genuine block sizes that the suburb's geography has protected from aggressive subdivision. Properties with dual-occupancy potential or granny flat capacity attract a specific buyer who understands the zoning provisions and is prepared to act when the right lot appears.

Vendors who have owned in Mt Gravatt East for a decade or more have seen material capital growth, and the current buyer pool supports solid campaign results for well-presented properties.
