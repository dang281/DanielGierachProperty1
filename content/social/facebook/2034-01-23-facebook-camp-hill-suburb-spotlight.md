## Caption

Camp Hill sits in the Cavendish Road State High School catchment, and that one fact shapes a significant portion of the suburb's buyer pool. Families who have identified that school as their target plan their property search around it, and the catchment boundary is one of the more carefully watched lines in Brisbane's inner east. Whites Hill State College adds a second strong public school option for primary families, which makes the suburb genuinely well-served by the public system at both levels.

The Greenway strip and The Depot precinct on Old Cleveland Road give Camp Hill a local retail and dining character that residents value. These are not destination venues for the rest of Brisbane but they keep the suburb self-contained in a way that residents appreciate on a weekday morning. You do not need to travel far for a good coffee or a local meal.

The housing mix ranges from character homes on decent blocks to new builds and quality renovations. Buyers here are typically upgraders: professionals and families who have outgrown a smaller home or a closer-in suburb, and who have chosen Camp Hill specifically, not as a compromise. The active renovation market means the suburb's presentation level has lifted materially over the past decade, and that filters into pricing.
