## Caption

Bulimba is one of the few inner-east suburbs where buyers will stretch their budget to be here rather than compromise to a nearby alternative.

The draw is Oxford Street. The cafe strip, the independent retailers, the ferry terminal, and the community feel that comes from a suburb that knows it is somewhere worth being. Buyers who have lived in Bulimba and then moved elsewhere for price reasons regularly come back when their circumstances allow.

The housing stock spans a wide range: original Queenslanders on elevated riverfront-adjacent streets, post-war timber homes in the residential middle, and newer townhouse and unit developments closer to the Oxford Street corridor. The spread means there are entry points at different price levels, though even the entry point in Bulimba sits above most inner-east neighbours.

Bulimba State School catchment is a consistent driver of family buyer competition in the suburb's residential streets. The school's reputation adds a floor to demand that holds even in softer market conditions.

The Bulimba Ferry connects the suburb to the New Farm and East Brisbane side of the river, which matters for buyers who commute or who value that direct link to the inner north. In the Brisbane property context, river access in a suburb adds tangible buyer appeal that translates to price.

For sellers, Bulimba is a suburb where a well-run campaign on a good property should generate genuine competition. The buyer pool is deep and motivated.
