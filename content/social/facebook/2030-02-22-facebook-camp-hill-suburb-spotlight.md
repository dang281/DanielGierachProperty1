## Caption

Camp Hill's autumn selling season is one of the most reliable in the inner east. The Whites Hill State College catchment buyers who've spent summer on the sidelines are back with purpose in February and March, and the supply of quality homes in this suburb never fully satisfies them. That imbalance is what makes Camp Hill a consistently strong market for sellers who prepare properly.

What's selling here, and for the best results, is specific. Homes on the high side with good aspect, character features that have been preserved or sympathetically restored, and land content that gives families genuine options. The premium within the suburb for the best streets and the best positions has held firm. The gap between an average Camp Hill home and an exceptional one has, if anything, widened over time.

For sellers, preparation matters more in Camp Hill than in many suburbs. Buyers here are discerning and have usually looked at a lot of property before they find the one they want. Presentation, styling, and a campaign that reflects the quality of the home are what separates a good result from a great one.

Median house prices are around $1.65M. Verify this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before making decisions based on it.

If you're thinking about selling in Camp Hill this autumn, the conversation should start now, before the peak of the season.
