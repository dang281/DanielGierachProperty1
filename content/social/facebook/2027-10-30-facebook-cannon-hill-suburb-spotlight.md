## Caption

Cannon Hill has its own train station on the Cleveland line and a residential character built around post-war timber homes and genuine street-level community, and those two things together explain why owners here tend to stay for a long time.

The commute into the CBD by train is short and direct, and that reliability of access is one of the most consistently cited reasons buyers target Cannon Hill specifically. For families, the public transport option adds to a suburb that is already strong on school appeal. Cannon Hill Anglican College is a significant private school within the suburb itself, and its presence draws families who want a specific school option and want to be close to it. That school catchment pull narrows the buyer pool in one sense, but it deepens the commitment of the buyers who are looking, and those buyers tend to be well-prepared and decisive when the right property appears.

The housing stock is a mix of renovated Queenslanders and low-set brick homes on good-sized blocks, and both types attract genuine demand. The industrial corridor on the suburb's northern boundary is well-separated from the residential streets and has minimal impact on the liveability of those streets day-to-day. It is not a factor that buyers raise, because the residential precinct simply does not feel it.

Annual stock is limited. Cannon Hill's owner-occupier retention rate is high, and the pool of active buyers typically exceeds available supply.
