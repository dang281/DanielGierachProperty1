## Caption

Carina's market has a particular dynamic right now. The buyers arriving from Camp Hill and Coorparoo have done the maths and they're not settling for Carina because they couldn't afford somewhere else. Many of them genuinely prefer what Carina offers at the price: a proper block, a project home with scope, and enough budget left over to renovate on their own terms.

That buyer profile shift is showing up in what sells and for how much. Full-block original homes in the better streets are generating competition from buyers who know exactly what they want to do with the property. The townhouse and newer stock moves differently, but the original 1960s and 70s brick homes on genuine land are the ones commanding the most attention.

Supply has been modest. Carina doesn't produce a high volume of listings, and sellers who present well and price to current market conditions rather than their own expectations are the ones getting the results. Days on market for well-prepared homes has been reasonable.

Median house prices are around $1.1M. Verify this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before using it in any decision.

If you own an original home in Carina with good land content, the current buyer pool is stronger than it's been in some time. The link below has more on the suburb's market position.
