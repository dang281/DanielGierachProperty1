## Caption

Norman Park in winter is a different open home experience from Norman Park in autumn. The foot traffic is lower but the buyers who attend are almost uniformly serious. The browsing buyer who attends five open homes in a Saturday to get a feel for the market is not the buyer who drives to Norman Park in June. The June buyer has already done the research, has their finance sorted, and is assessing whether this is the property they have been looking for.

For vendors in Norman Park who are considering whether to list now or wait for spring, that buyer profile is worth understanding. A motivated winter buyer with certainty in their finance position is a better counterparty than an uncertain spring buyer who is competing with high emotion but low financial clarity.

Norman Park's enduring appeal is its combination of Whites Hill Reserve access, consistent residential character, and school proximity. Whites Hill Reserve is the kind of amenity that does not diminish over time. It requires no maintenance, it is accessible on foot from most Norman Park streets, and it provides the green infrastructure that families with children specifically look for when they are making a 10-year address decision.

The Brisbane State High School catchment creates a buyer pool in Norman Park that is specifically motivated by the school access and will pay for it. Those buyers are active year-round, not just in spring.
