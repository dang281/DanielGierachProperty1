## Caption

Carina sits between Camp Hill and Carindale in Brisbane's inner south-east, offering a mix of original post-war homes and more recent renovations on block sizes that give buyers genuine options for how they live in and around the home.

The suburb is well-served by bus routes along Creek Road and Old Cleveland Road toward the CBD, making it a practical choice for buyers who want inner-south-east access without paying Camp Hill prices for it. The housing stock spans a range: some streets are predominantly original homes waiting for their next chapter, while others have seen a steady run of renovations and infill development that has lifted the overall quality of the precinct. Buyers at different price points and with different plans for the property both find a foothold in Carina.

Whites Hill Reserve is accessible from the suburb's eastern side, bringing bushland and creek corridor recreational space to what is otherwise a largely residential setting. For families, that proximity to green space without needing to drive to reach it is a practical lifestyle advantage. School access is also a consideration: Carina State School and parts of the Whites Hill State College catchment are both accessible depending on the specific street, which means family buyers do their homework on catchment boundaries before making offers.

Investor interest in the suburb is driven by solid rental demand from workers and students in the south-east corridor, adding a layer of buyer depth beyond the owner-occupier market.
