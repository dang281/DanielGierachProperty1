## Caption

Norman Park has one of the most consistent profiles of any inner east suburb. Buyers who choose Norman Park know exactly what they are buying: established residential streets, Whites Hill Reserve on the eastern boundary, good school access, and a quiet character that the surrounding suburbs trade away for more commercial activity.

Whites Hill Reserve is the kind of amenity that does not appear in the weekly property headlines but consistently influences what buyers will pay for the address. Walking tracks, open space, and genuine green infrastructure in a suburb 6 kilometres from the CBD represents a combination that is difficult to find at any price point in the inner east.

Brisbane State High School catchment access is a specific buying driver for families who have researched school options carefully. Properties within the catchment boundary attract a buyer pool that is both motivated and informed. They have done the research and they understand the value of the address.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war character homes on established blocks with good tree cover. Large development sites are rare and land content is valued. The suburb's owner-occupier base is stable and long-term, which means listings are not frequent and competition for quality properties is consistently strong when they come to market.

For vendors in Norman Park, the preparation window before winter is the time to have the campaign conversation.
