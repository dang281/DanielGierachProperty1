## Caption

Coorparoo is the most price-diverse suburb in the inner east, which makes it genuinely useful for a wide range of buyers but also means that understanding what your property is competing against requires more precision than in a more uniform suburb.

The suburb spans from the railway corridor along the Stones Corner boundary to the elevated streets above Old Cleveland Road, and that geographical range corresponds to a meaningful price range. A well-positioned family home on a larger block in the upper part of Coorparoo is in a different market from a townhouse near the commercial strip on Cavendish Road. Treating the suburb as a single market produces inaccurate expectations for both buyers and vendors.

What the whole suburb shares is strong transport connectivity. Coorparoo Station provides direct rail access to the CBD. The bus network connects to the Cultural Centre terminus. For buyers who are prioritising CBD commute options, Coorparoo delivers multiple public transport alternatives that fewer inner east suburbs can match.

Cavendish Road State High School is the school proximity factor that drives a meaningful portion of the buyer pool, particularly for families researching selective entry high school options alongside catchment access. Streets within the Cavendish Road SHS catchment are specifically sought after by families who have researched the school's academic profile.
