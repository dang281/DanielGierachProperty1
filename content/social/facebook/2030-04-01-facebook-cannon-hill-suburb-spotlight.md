## Caption

Cannon Hill sits at a useful intersection for inner east buyers: close enough to the CBD to be genuinely convenient, affordable enough relative to Hawthorne and Bulimba to attract buyers who have been priced out of those suburbs but want the same river-corridor lifestyle.

The buyer profile here is consistent. Young families looking for a three or four-bedroom house on a decent block, couples upgrading from an apartment, and investors attracted by the suburb's historical stability and the ongoing strength of rental demand close to the Cannon Hill Coles precinct and the train line.

What makes Cannon Hill work for sellers right now is the school catchment dynamic. Properties that fall inside the Cannon Hill State School catchment attract a slightly different buyer pool from those that do not, and in some price brackets that differentiation is visible in the result.

The suburb has seen ongoing renovation activity over the past decade, which has lifted the quality of the housing stock considerably. A well-presented character home in the suburb commands genuine buyer attention, particularly from buyers who have lost out on similar properties in Morningside or Carina.

Days on market for well-priced Cannon Hill properties are competitive with the broader inner east average. The market here does not collapse in winter; it just quiets slightly.

If you are thinking about selling in Cannon Hill, the autumn window is worth taking seriously.
