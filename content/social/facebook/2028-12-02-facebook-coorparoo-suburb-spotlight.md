## Caption

Coorparoo is the inner east suburb that the broader Brisbane market thinks of as a growth suburb, but that locals know as an established community with deep roots in the area.

The streets that run north from Old Cleveland Road toward the Coorparoo Junction precinct have tracked some of the strongest price growth in the suburb over the past few years, driven by the combination of walkability to the café and retail strip, the catchment for Coorparoo State School, and the availability of both original and renovated stock at prices that still represent genuine value relative to Norman Park and Balmoral.

The buyer profile in Coorparoo is broad but has a dominant cohort: the younger family or professional couple upgrading from a unit or townhouse who wants a house on land within the inner east and within budget. This buyer understands the market, has usually been looking for 6 to 12 months, and moves decisively when the right property comes to market.

The Junction precinct redevelopment and the connectivity to the South-East Busway have made Coorparoo one of the more efficient inner east transport nodes. That connectivity is increasingly valued by buyers who weigh commute time as a real cost.
