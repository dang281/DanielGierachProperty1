## Caption

Mt Gravatt East punches above its profile, and the buyers who know it tend to move quickly when stock comes up.

The proximity to Griffith University's Nathan campus and the Princess Alexandra Hospital makes this suburb a genuine target for academics, medical professionals, and the support workforce that orbits both institutions. That creates a dual market: owner-occupiers who want a settled family suburb close to work, and investors who know the rental demand is structural rather than speculative.

Westfield Garden City is within easy reach along Logan Road, covering major retail and dining needs without requiring a trip into the CBD. The suburb itself is quiet, with streets that slope gently toward the escarpment and a tree canopy that keeps it feeling more like a hill suburb than a city fringe postcode.

The housing mix leans toward four-bedroom family homes on decent blocks, with a growing number of townhouse developments filling in the gaps for lower-maintenance buyers. Garden Road and Newnham Road see consistent activity at auction, with competition between families and investors keeping clearance rates solid.

Median house price is sitting around $1.05 million. Rental yields remain above the Brisbane average, which keeps investor enquiry consistent even when owner-occupier demand softens.
