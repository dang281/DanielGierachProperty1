## Caption

Coorparoo's winter market is one of the more active in the inner east for the price bracket it represents. The suburb draws buyers from adjacent Camp Hill, Norman Park, and East Brisbane who are looking for more space within a familiar lifestyle corridor, and those buyers do not follow the same seasonal rhythms as the broader market.

The suburb's internal geography matters for sellers. Coorparoo north of Old Cleveland Road has a character and walkability that is meaningfully different from the southern sections, and that distinction is reflected in how buyers search and what they are prepared to pay. Properties in the northern pockets of Coorparoo, within walking distance of the Old Cleveland Road café strip, attract a premium that properties in the deeper south of the suburb do not.

School access in Coorparoo remains a consistent driver. The Coorparoo State School catchment, combined with access to the secondary school network across the inner east, means the suburb works for families across multiple schooling stages without requiring a move as children age.

The character home market in Coorparoo is well-supplied relative to some adjacent suburbs. Buyers have choice, which means presentation and pricing strategy matter more here than in a market with near-zero supply. A well-prepared, correctly priced Coorparoo property will outperform a more expensive, under-prepared one every time.
