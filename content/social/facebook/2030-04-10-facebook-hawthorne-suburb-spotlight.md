## Caption

Hawthorne is the inner east suburb that appears most often on the shortlist of buyers who have outgrown their first purchase and are ready to commit to a long-term home. The combination of Wynnum Road access, proximity to Bulimba's Oxford Street, ferry access, and one of Brisbane's more underrated café strips on Glen Road makes it genuinely liveable across life stages.

The character of the suburb's housing stock is largely consistent: Queenslanders, post-war weatherboards, and a growing number of architecturally renovated homes that have set a quality benchmark for the market. Properties that have been renovated well but not over-capitalised consistently achieve strong results because buyers can see the investment clearly.

The school catchment story in Hawthorne is straightforward: proximity to the Balmoral State High catchment and strong feeder primary options make it a logical suburb for families who are planning ahead. That buyer segment anchors the market even in quieter periods.

What Hawthorne does not do well is sit still for long when it is priced correctly. Properties in the $1.4M to $2.2M range that are presented well, priced credibly, and marketed with enough campaign time for buyers to register tend to generate genuine competition. The properties that sit are usually the ones where vendor expectation and market feedback have not aligned from the start.
