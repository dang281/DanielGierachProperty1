## Caption

Coorparoo has changed materially over the past decade, and the buyers arriving now are responding to a suburb that has earned a different kind of attention.

The Coorparoo Square development and the Cross River Rail station at Woolloongabba, just over the suburb boundary, have together shifted the calculus for buyers who want inner-city access without inner-city prices. The connection to the CBD by train is direct and fast. The retail and dining options along Old Cleveland Road have broadened. The suburb has the density and walkability that younger owner-occupier buyers are now specifically seeking in the inner east.

The housing stock is a genuine mix: original post-war homes on flat blocks in the quieter residential streets, newer townhouses and mid-density developments along the main corridors, and a growing number of fully renovated character homes that attract buyers who want the suburb's convenience with a finished product they can move straight into.

What is consistent across buyer types is the value comparison with Bulimba and Hawthorne. Coorparoo offers more access and convenience than its prices currently reflect relative to those suburbs. That gap has been narrowing, and buyers who understand the fundamentals are aware of it.
