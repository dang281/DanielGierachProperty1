## Caption

Morningside occupies a price band in the inner east that is difficult to replicate elsewhere: the tree-lined character streets of Hawthorne, a short train ride from the CBD, at a price point that remains more accessible than Bulimba or Hawthorne on the one side and comparable to Cannon Hill on the other.

For buyers who looked at Hawthorne and found it beyond reach, Morningside is often where the search ends. The suburb delivers the same broad appeal: character Queenslanders on good blocks, a genuine residential feel, proximity to Bulimba Creek and the parks and walkways that run alongside it, without the premium that Hawthorne's tightest streets demand. The Cannon Hill train line puts the CBD within a short commute, and that direct rail access is a consistent factor in why professionals and families both end up here.

Morningside State School holds a strong reputation and drives genuine catchment demand from family buyers. The Wynnum Road and Thynne Road corridors provide the main arteries through the suburb, connecting residents to Bulimba Creek, the broader inner east, and the motorway network beyond. Townhouse and medium-density development along those main road corridors gives the suburb some diversity of housing type without affecting the character of the quieter residential streets behind them.

Buyers here tend to be young families, professionals stepping up from units, or buyers who want inner east access without paying the top of the market to get it.
