## Caption

Carina doesn't carry the same profile as Camp Hill or Coorparoo, and that gap in perception is precisely what's drawing buyers to it now. Families who've done the numbers and found themselves $200,000 short of their target suburb are landing in Carina and discovering that the compromises are fewer than expected.

The suburb has solid bones. A mix of 1960s and 70s brick homes on decent blocks, straightforward access to Westfield Carindale, and reasonable proximity to the inner east without the inner east price tag. Median house prices are around $1.1M at the time of writing, though you should verify that figure against current CoreLogic or PropTrack data.

The buyer profile has shifted in recent years. It's not just investors and first home buyers anymore. Families priced out of Camp Hill and Coorparoo are arriving with renovation budgets and a longer-term view. That's adding a layer of competition in the $1M to $1.2M range that wasn't there a few years ago.

Townhouse development has increased supply in some pockets, so not all Carina property is equal. Original homes on full blocks in the better streets are the product that's drawing the most attention.

If you own in Carina and want to understand where your property sits in the current market, start here.
