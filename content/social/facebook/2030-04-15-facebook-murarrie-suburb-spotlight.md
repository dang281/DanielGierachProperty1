## Caption

Murarrie sits at an interesting junction in the inner east market. It is close enough to the employment precincts at Murarrie and the Gateway corridor to attract buyers who prioritise commute efficiency, and it is adjacent to the Morningside-Cannon Hill corridor where prices have risen consistently over the past decade.

The suburb's recent trajectory has been driven partly by buyers priced out of Morningside and Cannon Hill who have recognised that Murarrie offers comparable land sizes and access with a price point that still allows for some upside. That recognition has been growing for several years, and it is reflected in the activity pattern in the sales market.

The housing stock is more varied than the adjacent suburbs: a mix of character homes, 1970s and 1980s brick houses, and newer townhouse developments. The older stock on larger blocks continues to attract the most interest, particularly where the renovation has been done well or the land size allows for further development.

Families and investors make up the predominant buyer profile. School access via Murarrie State School and proximity to the Cannon Hill Shopping Village add practical lifestyle credentials that make Murarrie more than just a price-driven choice.

For homeowners in Murarrie considering selling, the autumn period has historically delivered strong buyer competition in this price bracket.
