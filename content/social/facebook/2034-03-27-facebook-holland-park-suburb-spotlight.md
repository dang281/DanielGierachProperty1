## Caption

Holland Park is a suburb that tends to appear in buyer searches once Camp Hill and Greenslopes have been reviewed and found to be out of reach on budget. What those buyers discover is that the gap between Holland Park and its more expensive neighbours is not a lifestyle gap; it is largely a price gap, and that is what drives consistent demand in the suburb.

The Ipswich Road bus corridor is a genuine commuting asset, connecting residents to the CBD without a car and keeping Holland Park on the shortlist for buyers who prioritise public transport. The Cavendish Road State High catchment applies to Holland Park addresses, which is a specific and deliberate driver for families who have done their research. That school access at a Holland Park price point, rather than a Camp Hill price point, is a genuine advantage vendors can communicate in a campaign. [VERIFY: catchment boundaries and current median house price before posting.]

The housing stock is predominantly post-war, with a number of homes that have been extended or renovated over the decades. Unrenovated properties on good blocks also attract attention from buyers who want to add value. The suburb is not high-turnover, which means buyers who want a specific street or configuration may wait some time before the right property appears.

For vendors, a low-turnover market means less competition from comparable listings when you do come to market.
