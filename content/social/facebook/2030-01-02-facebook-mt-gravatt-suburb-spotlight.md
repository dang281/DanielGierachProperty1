## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits higher than most of the inner east, and that elevation does a lot of work. District views, cooler breezes in summer, and blocks with genuine size are qualities that are genuinely hard to find this close to the city. Buyers priced out of Coorparoo and Camp Hill are looking here in growing numbers, and the value comparison holds up well.

The suburb draws a particular kind of buyer: families who want land, school access, and proximity to retail without paying inner-city prices. Cavendish Road State High School is the catchment anchor, and Westfield Garden City a few minutes away covers the practical side. The housing stock leans toward postwar and 1970s-80s brick and tile, with plenty of scope for renovation or extension on the better blocks.

Median house prices are sitting around $1.25M at the time of writing, though this figure moves, and you should check current CoreLogic or PropTrack data before making decisions based on it.

Competition is consistent on well-positioned homes with a view or a generous block. Properties that tick those boxes rarely sit long once they hit the market.
