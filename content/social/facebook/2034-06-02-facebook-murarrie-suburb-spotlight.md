## Caption

Murarrie's position on the Gateway Motorway corridor gives it a transport flexibility that most comparable inner east suburbs do not have. Access to the airport, the Gold Coast, and the Sunshine Coast from Murarrie is direct without requiring a motorway merge at peak hour. For buyers whose work or lifestyle involves regular travel, that access is a practical daily advantage rather than an occasional convenience.

The Murarrie Recreation Reserve is the suburb's primary open space asset and it serves a broad function. The walking paths, sports fields, and creek corridor provide the kind of active outdoor amenity that families with primary school age children prioritise. The reserve's scale means it does not feel crowded in the way that smaller pocket parks in denser inner east suburbs can.

The suburb's housing stock is a mix of post-war character homes and more recent construction, with the character housing concentrated in the established residential streets away from the industrial edge. The transition in character across the suburb means that buyers who understand the street-level geography of Murarrie can identify good value relative to what comparable land content costs in adjacent suburbs.

Murarrie's pricing sits below Seven Hills and Coorparoo for comparable house and land configurations, which creates a value entry point for buyers who are committed to the inner east location and are working to a budget.
