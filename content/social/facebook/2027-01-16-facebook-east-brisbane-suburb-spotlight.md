## Caption

East Brisbane is a suburb that most Brisbane residents drive through rather than stop in. That has made it one of the better-kept secrets in the inner east, though the market has been catching up steadily.

The suburb sits just three kilometres from the CBD, bordered by Kangaroo Point to the west and Coorparoo to the south. The housing stock is a mix of original Queenslanders, character workers' cottages, and post-war renovations on compact inner-city blocks. It is not a suburb of large yards. It is a suburb of location, walkability, and genuine proximity to the city that few other inner-east suburbs can match.

The buyer profile is distinctive: professionals in their 30s and 40s who want to be close to the CBD without paying the premium of New Farm or Fortitude Valley, and who place high value on cycling infrastructure, the Mowbray Park foreshore, and easy access to the Story Bridge. East Brisbane delivers all of that at a price point that remains below the river suburbs to the north.

For investors, the rental demand in East Brisbane is consistent and strong. Proximity to the city, the Gabba precinct, and public transport makes it a natural choice for young professionals.

Sales in East Brisbane are watched closely by buyers who know the suburb well. When a good property comes to market, competition follows quickly.
