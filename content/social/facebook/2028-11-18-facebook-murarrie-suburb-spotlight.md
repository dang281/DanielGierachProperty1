## Caption

Murarrie is the inner east suburb that most of the market has underpriced for longer than it deserved.

The suburb sits directly between Cannon Hill and Hemmant, with Tingalpa Creek forming the eastern boundary and the Gateway Motorway providing direct freeway access in both directions. The residential precinct is relatively compact and genuinely owner-occupied, which means supply is structurally constrained and the buyers competing when something does come to market are competing from a thin pool of alternatives.

The lifestyle narrative around Murarrie has strengthened as the eastern corridor infrastructure has improved. The proximity to the Cannon Hill Shopping Centre, the Eastern Busway at Cannon Hill, and the airport precinct employment base makes Murarrie efficient for a buyer profile that values access over status address.

The housing stock in Murarrie's residential streets is predominantly established homes on generous land. The blocks are typically larger than comparable inner east suburbs at the same price point, which has attracted a specific buyer: the family or investor who wants land within 12 kilometres of the CBD without the Morningside or Hawthorne premium.

When a well-presented Murarrie home on a genuine land holding reaches the market, the buyer response is typically faster than the suburb's lower profile would suggest.
