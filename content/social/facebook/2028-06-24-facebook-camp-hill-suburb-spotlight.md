## Caption

Camp Hill has earned its reputation as one of Brisbane's most desirable family suburbs, and the queue of buyers waiting for stock to come to market is the proof.

The elevated position gives many homes sweeping city views, and the character housing stock, predominantly Queenslanders and post-war timber homes, gives the suburb a visual identity that newer estates simply cannot replicate. Norton Street is the local heart: coffee, breakfast, a bottle shop, and the kind of weekend foot traffic that tells you a suburb has genuine community depth.

The Whites Hill State College catchment is the school story for Camp Hill, and it is a significant one. Families from across Brisbane's inner east actively target the catchment boundaries when buying, which keeps demand for Camp Hill addresses elevated and consistent regardless of broader market conditions.

Buyers here are mostly families upgrading from inner-city townhouses or smaller homes in Coorparoo and Greenslopes. They are looking for space, character, and a suburb where they can settle for the long term. That buyer profile tends to be financially strong and confident at auction.

Median house price sits around $1.55 million, with well-presented character homes on elevated blocks regularly transacting above $1.8 million. Stock is tight, and when quality listings come to market, they do not wait around.

If you are weighing up whether now is the right time to sell in Camp Hill, the current market has a clear answer.
