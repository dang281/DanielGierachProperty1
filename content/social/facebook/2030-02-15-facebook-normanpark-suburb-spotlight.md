## Caption

Norman Park in February means the Whites Hill State College catchment buyers are already active. School enrolment timelines create a real urgency for families who want to secure an address in the catchment, and that urgency translates directly into buyer behaviour at this time of year. Sellers who list in the autumn window are meeting those buyers at their most motivated.

What's been selling in Norman Park is what always sells here: character Queenslanders with genuine land content in the streets that buyers know by name. The suburb's topography means that some streets are materially more desirable than others, and buyers who've done their research know exactly which ones they want.

The supply side is the defining feature of this market. Norman Park has genuinely low turnover. Many owners have held for fifteen or twenty years, and there are streets where listings appear once in a decade. That scarcity supports prices and means that when a well-positioned home does become available, the waiting buyer pool responds immediately.

Median house prices are around $1.5M. Verify this against current CoreLogic or PropTrack data before using it in any property decision.

If you're a Norman Park homeowner who has been holding for a long time and thinking that this might be the year, the autumn campaign window from late February through April is historically one of the strongest.
