## Caption

Holland Park sits elevated above the surrounding suburbs, and the view from both the streets and the market data tells the same story: this is a suburb with consistent appeal.

The leafy canopy along streets like Cavendish Road East and Abbotsleigh Street gives Holland Park a character that buyers coming from denser inner-city postcodes genuinely appreciate. Whites Hill Reserve is on the doorstep, providing walking tracks and open space that are hard to put a dollar figure on but absolutely show up in buyer demand.

Greenslopes Private Hospital is close by, which matters for the medical and allied health professionals who make up a solid portion of the suburb's owner-occupier base. For families, the mix of state and private schooling options nearby, combined with the quieter street environment, continues to draw upgraders from the inner suburbs.

The housing stock is predominantly 1960s and 1970s originals alongside an increasing number of renovated properties. That split creates opportunity at both ends: buyers who want a project can still find one, and buyers who want a finished home are not short of options either.

Median house price is sitting around $1.25 million. Well-presented homes on elevated blocks with views have been achieving strong results above that figure.

Winter is when Holland Park's liveability really sells itself. If you have been thinking about timing your sale, now is a good moment to have the conversation.
