## Caption

Balmoral sits alongside Bulimba in the inner east and shares the Bulimba State School catchment, but it has developed its own identity over time. Riding Road and Lytton Road both carry cafe precincts that give the suburb a neighbourhood feel without the Oxford Street foot traffic. The elevated streets on the western and northern edges catch river views that buyers in the market for a premium home have been paying attention to for years.

The housing stock is almost entirely character-era. Queenslanders and timber bungalows dominate, and they sit on allotments that give renovation scope or subdivision potential on the right block. This is a suburb where buyers have done serious research before they come to inspections. They know the streets, the school, the flood mapping, and the comparable sales. The ones who make offers are committed.

Balmoral is tightly held. Turnover is low, and that keeps supply constrained. When a home comes to market in a good street such as Raven, Eldernell Terrace, or portions of Lytton Road, the buyer pool often includes people who have been watching and waiting. That dynamic supports pricing for sellers who are well-prepared and not in a rush to drop their expectations.

If you own in Balmoral and have been considering selling, timing and preparation are both worth understanding in detail. See suburb sales data at danielgierach.com/suburbs/balmoral
