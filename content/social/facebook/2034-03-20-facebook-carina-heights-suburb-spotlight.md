## Caption

The appeal of Carina Heights is quietly specific. It is not a suburb that generates headlines, but the buyers who target it have usually made a deliberate calculation: they want the block sizes and the residential quiet of Carina Heights, they want to be as close to Camp Hill's school catchments as possible, and they want to pay less than Camp Hill's median to get there. That is a rational position, and the buyer demand it generates is consistent.

The housing stock across Carina Heights leans post-war and 1970s-era, with genuine block sizes that have not been subdivided to the degree seen in inner suburbs closer to the CBD. For families who need backyard space, a spot for a pool, or room for an extension, the blocks here deliver. The streets are quiet and residential without the busyness that comes with a main road thoroughfare running through the suburb. [VERIFY: current median house price before posting.]

The school catchment picture in Carina Heights is worth understanding at the street level. Depending on where the boundary falls, properties can access Whites Hill State College or Cavendish Road State High catchments. Buyers who know this do their research at the street level before inspecting, and vendors who understand which catchment applies to their property can communicate that clearly in the campaign.
