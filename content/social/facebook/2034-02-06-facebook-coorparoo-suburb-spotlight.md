## Caption

Coorparoo's Cavendish Road strip does a lot of work. Between the cafes, restaurants, and everyday retail along that stretch, residents genuinely do not need to leave the suburb for most of what they want on a weekend. That amenity is a consistent driver in buyer conversations, and it shows in how properties perform at sale.

The suburb sits on the junction of Cavendish Road and Old Cleveland Road, about 5km from the CBD, and it draws a broad buyer profile as a result. Young families are here for Coorparoo State School and the proximity to secondary options including Cavendish Road State High. Upsizers are here for the character homes, particularly the older Queenslanders that still represent a significant share of the housing stock. Downsizers and investors look at the newer townhouses, which have filled in a number of the original larger blocks over the past two decades.

The mix of stock means Coorparoo serves multiple buyer groups simultaneously, which gives vendors a wider pool to work with than a single-profile suburb. [VERIFY: current median house and unit prices before posting.]

Days on market for well-presented character homes in Coorparoo have remained low relative to the broader Brisbane market. Properties in tighter streets off Cavendish Road tend to attract the strongest competition.
