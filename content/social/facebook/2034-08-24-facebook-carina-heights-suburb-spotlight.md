## Caption

Carina Heights offers something increasingly difficult to find in Brisbane's inner east: large blocks, quiet residential streets, and a price point that still represents genuine value compared to the suburbs immediately to the north. That combination continues to attract families who have been priced out of Camp Hill or Coorparoo and want more space without compromising on access.

The suburb's elevated position delivers outlook across the wider Brisbane basin, and homes with views to the city or the Taylor Range have consistently achieved strong results. The Carina Heights State School catchment and proximity to Westfield Carindale round out the appeal for families weighing up where to settle for the medium to long term.

Winter has tended to quiet the market here, but the buyers who stay active in this season are typically the most committed. Less competition from other sellers and more attention from serious buyers is a combination that works for well-presented homes.
