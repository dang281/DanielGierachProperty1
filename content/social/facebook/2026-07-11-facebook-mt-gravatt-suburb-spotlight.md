## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits about 10 kilometres from the CBD, but it operates like a suburb that has worked out exactly what it is and isn't trying to be anything else.

The commercial strip on Logan Road anchors the suburb on the eastern side. The residential streets to the west are a mix of post-war homes on larger blocks and more recent builds. For buyers who want a suburb with genuine infrastructure, schools, transport, and a functioning local retail environment, Mt Gravatt delivers all of it without the price premium that comes with Coorparoo or Camp Hill.

The price growth story here has been one of the stronger ones in the broader inner south corridor over the past year. Buyers who were outbid in Camp Hill and Morningside three years ago moved to Mt Gravatt East and Mt Gravatt. That demand has now been absorbed into the local median, but the underlying fundamentals remain.

Days on market for well-priced houses in Mt Gravatt have been running at under 20 days. That's the kind of turnover that reflects a suburb with genuine, ongoing buyer demand rather than episodic interest.

If you own in Mt Gravatt and you've watched the suburb's profile rise over the past few years, now is a reasonable time to understand what your property is worth in today's market.
