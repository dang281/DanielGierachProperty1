## Caption

Carina has some of the better block sizes in Brisbane's inner east, and that is a detail that matters more now than it did ten years ago. As the inner suburbs have been subdivided and consolidated, Carina's larger lots, particularly in the pockets off Old Cleveland Road and toward the Cavendish Road boundary, stand out for buyers who want backyard space and room to build or extend.

The Carina Leagues Club is a genuine local anchor, and Cavendish Road access means the suburb connects easily to both the Camp Hill dining precinct and the Coorparoo Junction. Daily life in Carina functions without requiring a trip to the CBD or inner ring, which appeals to the owner-occupier families who make up the majority of the buyer base here.

Upgrader demand from Carina is also a consistent pattern: buyers who have outgrown a unit or townhouse in the eastern suburbs look at Carina for a first freestanding home with a yard. The price point relative to Camp Hill and Coorparoo is a specific driver, and buyers who understand that geography realise the suburb delivers similar access at a meaningful discount.
