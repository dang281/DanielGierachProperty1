## Caption

Cannon Hill's property market has been shaped by two consistent forces over the past decade: buyers moving east from Morningside and buyers moving west from Seven Hills and Murarrie. Both groups are attracted by the suburb's position, its price relative to the river-corridor suburbs, and the genuine community feel that concentrates around the Cannon Hill Shopping Village.

The train station is a practical asset that keeps Cannon Hill on the shortlist for buyers who are serious about commute options. Direct rail access to the CBD puts the suburb in a different conversation from the road-only suburbs in the inner east south.

The housing stock in Cannon Hill is mixed in a way that creates opportunity at multiple price points. Well-renovated character homes attract the highest buyer competition. Original condition properties on 600-square-metre-plus blocks attract renovators and investors. The newer townhouse stock in the suburb's flatter sections serves the entry-level buyer and the downsizer segment.

What winter looks like in Cannon Hill: the volume of open home registrations drops, but the buyers who attend tend to be further along in the decision-making process. Offers in winter Cannon Hill campaigns often move faster than in spring, because the buyers have been watching the market for longer.

For sellers, the winter period in Cannon Hill is genuinely worth considering as a campaign window if the preparation is right.
