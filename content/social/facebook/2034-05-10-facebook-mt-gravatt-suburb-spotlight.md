## Caption

Mt Gravatt is a suburb that delivers consistent fundamentals without requiring buyers to accept a premium for lifestyle cachet they may or may not value. Logan Road handles the majority of everyday retail and hospitality needs. Hanlon Park provides green space, walking tracks, and sporting facilities. The motorway access from this part of Brisbane's southside is among the most direct of any inner south-east suburb for buyers who regularly need to travel across multiple employment centres.

The QIMR Berghofer Medical Research Institute anchors the knowledge and health precinct that is developing in this corridor and generating demand from well-qualified residents who are choosing proximity to a growing employment cluster. The Princess Alexandra Hospital complex, accessible via the motorway corridor, adds to the health sector employment base that contributes to the Mt Gravatt buyer profile.

The owner-occupier base is stable and long-term. Vendors in Mt Gravatt are typically selling after extended periods of ownership, which means the properties that come to market often have deferred maintenance that informed buyers factor into their offers. Vendors who address presentation issues before campaign launch are consistently achieving better results than those who price to condition.

The buyer pool includes families trading up within the southside, interstate buyers researching access and school options, and investor activity on properties near key transport routes. Demand is consistent and the suburb's practical fundamentals sustain it through different market conditions.
