## Caption

Carina Heights sits in the sweet spot between the freeway convenience of Carindale and the café culture of Coorparoo, which is why the buyer profile here skews toward growing families who want space without sacrificing access.

The streets south of Broadrick Road attract buyers specifically for the block sizes: 600 to 900 square metres is common, and the relatively low density compared to the closer-in suburbs makes it genuinely liveable for families with young children. Whites Hill State College is the anchor school catchment for much of the suburb, and that draws buyers who have done their research.

Properties here tend to present as post-war or early brick homes on good land, with a consistent market for buyers who want to renovate rather than knock down and rebuild. The renovation-focused buyer pool means that properties which present well and show the potential clearly tend to generate stronger competition than those that have been left without maintenance.

The trade-off is proximity. Carina Heights is further from the CBD than the Bulimba to Hawthorne corridor, and buyers who prioritise river access or rapid CBD commutes often shortlist elsewhere first. But for families who are prioritising school catchment, land size, and the lifestyle feel of a quieter suburb, Carina Heights consistently delivers.
