## Caption

Morningside sits between Bulimba and Cannon Hill and consistently attracts two distinct buyer types, which is one of the things that makes it a healthy market for sellers.

Families and owner-occupiers are drawn to the flat, character-home streets at a price point below Bulimba and Hawthorne. Investors are attracted by unit rental yields and the suburb's longer-term trajectory. That breadth of demand means a well-presented property is not dependent on a single buyer type showing up.

The suburb's days on market have historically been competitive, reflecting the fact that motivated buyers are not waiting for a price reduction. They are watching new listings and moving when the right property appears.

There is also an infrastructure tailwind in this part of the city. The Cannon Hill precinct has seen ongoing retail and dining investment that adds to the eastern corridor that Morningside sits within. The Cross River Rail and its downstream effects on Brisbane's south-east connectivity have also benefited suburbs along this part of the eastern bus corridor.

For homeowners in Morningside considering timing, the fundamentals here have been solid across multiple market cycles.
