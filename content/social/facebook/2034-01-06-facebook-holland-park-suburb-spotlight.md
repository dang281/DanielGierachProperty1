## Caption

Holland Park sits in the middle ring of Brisbane's south side, and it has built a consistent following among buyers who want genuine value without retreating to the outer suburbs. The Ipswich Road corridor gives you quick access to the CBD and the inner south, and you are 10 minutes from Westfield Carindale in the other direction.

The housing stock is honest and diverse. Post-war Queenslanders and 1970s to 1980s brick homes on decent allotments make up the bulk of what trades here. Buyers coming from closer-in suburbs often find the block sizes compelling, particularly when they are comparing what the same budget gets them in Coorparoo or Greenslopes. Cavendish Road State High School is the catchment drawcard for families. It consistently draws buyers who have specifically chosen an address to qualify, and that demand is not going anywhere.

The upgrade market is active in Holland Park. First-home buyers who bought in surrounding suburbs are ready to move up, and Holland Park is where many of them land. That steady pipeline of motivated buyers keeps the market from going quiet for long, even when broader conditions are softer.
