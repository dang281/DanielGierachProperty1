## Caption

Carina Heights occupies a position in the inner east that buyers increasingly recognise as genuinely undervalued relative to its immediate neighbours. The suburb shares the Westfield Carindale access with Carina and Carindale, the school catchment infrastructure with Camp Hill and Holland Park, and the elevated residential character with the ridgeline suburbs to the north, but it prices below most of them for comparable house and land configurations.

The elevated streets in Carina Heights provide the aspect and morning sun access that flat land suburbs do not deliver. For buyers who are comparing across several inner east options and finding that the better-known suburb names come with a price premium that the underlying amenity does not justify, Carina Heights often emerges as the rational alternative.

The suburb's owner-occupier character is strong. Properties in Carina Heights are typically held for substantial periods before they come to market, which means the housing stock has been maintained by families who have lived in it rather than by investors managing for rental return. That ownership pattern shows in the presentation quality of the properties that are listed.

The winter buyer who is looking at Carina Heights has usually already done the suburb comparison work. They are not browsing. They have arrived at Carina Heights through a deliberate process of elimination and they know what they are looking for.
