## Caption

Carindale's market position is built on a foundation that inner east buyers who have been watching the suburb for years understand well: it is the largest retail precinct in Brisbane's inner east, and the residential streets that surround it are some of the most consistently family-oriented in the corridor.

The suburb sits at the eastern edge of the inner east, approximately 12 kilometres from the CBD, and its relationship with the Carindale Shopping Centre and the surrounding commercial precinct defines its character as a suburb where amenity comes first. For the family who values retail convenience, private school proximity, and a residential streetscape of predominantly post-war and brick construction homes, Carindale delivers a practical combination that does not require a premium address.

The housing stock in Carindale skews toward the larger family home on a genuine land holding. Properties in the streets north and east of the shopping centre, those closest to the Carindale State School and the private school catchment zones along Old Cleveland Road, achieve the highest prices in the suburb and attract competition from buyers who have done the school catchment research.

The Carindale market is not fashionable. It does not attract the buyer who is buying a lifestyle narrative. It attracts the buyer who has found the numbers work and the location makes sense, and that buyer is often very ready to move when the right property arrives.
