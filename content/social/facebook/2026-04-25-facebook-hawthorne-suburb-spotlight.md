## Caption

Hawthorne sits on the Brisbane River, 7km from the CBD, with a median house price of around $2.1 million and annual capital growth of approximately 8 per cent.

It is one of the inner east's most deliberately chosen suburbs. Buyers here are not stumbling into it. They have decided they want the Oxford Street precinct, the CityCat commute, and the river proximity. That intentional buyer profile is one of the things that keeps a floor under Hawthorne values even when other parts of the market soften.

The current rate environment at 4.10 per cent has made buyers more careful. Finance takes longer to arrange, and inspection numbers are more measured than in 2021. But the pool of buyers specifically targeting Hawthorne is still there. They have a very clear picture of what they want, and when the right property is priced accurately, they move.

For Hawthorne homeowners thinking about selling: presentation and positioning matter more than they did in the peak years. The suburb's fundamentals are strong, but the buyers who are looking now are comparing your property directly against recent sales.

If you would like an honest read on what your property is worth in the current market, I am happy to have that conversation.
