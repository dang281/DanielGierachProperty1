## Caption

Carindale sits about 10km south-east of the CBD, and it tends to be priced below Camp Hill and Coorparoo despite sharing many of the same fundamentals: established blocks, Cavendish Road State High School catchment, and easy access to the inner east.

The difference is perception. Carindale is associated with Westfield, which puts some buyers off. They filter for a suburb identity built around cafe strips rather than convenience infrastructure. What they miss is that the residential streets in Carindale are largely quiet, character-home areas with nothing in common with the commercial precinct.

For sellers, this perception discount is worth understanding. It means the suburb is not overvalued relative to its neighbours, which keeps genuine buyers competing without the inflated pricing pressure that some inner-east suburbs carry. Properties here tend to attract families who have done their research rather than buyers chasing a postcode.

If you own in Carindale and have been thinking about what your property is worth in the current market, I am happy to give you a clear and honest answer.
