## Caption

Carina is a suburb where the block sizes still make sense for a family with a budget. At a time when 400-square-metre inner-east sites are commanding premium prices, Carina continues to offer 600 to 700-square-metre blocks at a price point that remains accessible for buyers who want land without pushing to the outer suburbs.

The suburb's location is genuinely practical: immediate access to Old Cleveland Road and Gateway Motorway for commuters, proximity to Westfield Carindale for retail, and Carina State School as a well-regarded local primary option.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war brick and timber, reflecting the suburb's development period. A significant proportion of homes in Carina have been renovated over the past decade, and the variance between a renovated and unrenovated home of similar specifications can be significant. That gap is where buyers looking to add value find their opportunity.

The buyer profile is family-focused, with a secondary layer of investor interest driven by the suburb's consistently strong rental demand and yields that are more competitive than the prestige inner-east suburbs. Tenants are drawn by the suburb's location and the access to the Gateway corridor for employment in the port and industrial precincts.

For sellers in Carina, the autumn window is worth capturing. The family buyer who is active in March and April is motivated and finance-ready.
