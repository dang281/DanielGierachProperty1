## Caption

Carina sits at a particular point on the inner east affordability scale that keeps attracting a specific buyer: the household upgrading from a unit or townhouse in Coorparoo or Camp Hill who wants a house on land within reach of the Carindale retail strip and the Eastern Busway corridor.

That buyer has been consistent through the market cycles of the past few years. They are looking for the original weatherboard or brick home on a decent block, with enough land to justify the upgrade from medium density. They are not buying Carina for the character architecture. They are buying it for the land, the school catchments, and the proximity to amenity that more expensive suburbs share without the price premium.

The streets north of Creek Road have historically attracted better prices than those further east, reflecting the school catchment boundary and the proximity to the Whites Hill corridor. Properties on large, flat blocks in these streets continue to attract multiple buyers when they come to market.

Supply in Carina is generally tight. The suburb does not turn over quickly. When something on a genuine land holding hits the market, it tends to attract buyers who have been watching for months.

If you own in Carina and want to understand what the current market looks like for your property, I am happy to walk through the comparable evidence.
