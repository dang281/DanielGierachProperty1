## Caption

Morningside has one of the highest concentrations of auction activity in the inner east, and the clearance rates have been telling.

The suburb sits in the middle of the inner east value corridor, positioned between the premium Hawthorne and Balmoral addresses to the north and the emerging value of Cannon Hill and Murarrie to the south. That positioning creates a depth of buyer demand that is structural rather than cyclical. Buyers in Morningside have typically done their research and arrive at open homes with a clear understanding of what the suburb is worth.

What is worth noting in the current market is the composition of stock and how location within the suburb affects price. The heritage-character streets on the elevated blocks closest to Morningside's central zone have held price better than the lower-lying residential pockets closer to the train line. Within a single suburb, a 500-metre difference in position can produce a material difference in outcome.

Family buyers here are largely driven by school catchment access to Norman Park State School and Morningside State School, both of which pull buyers from a wider geographic radius than the suburb boundary would suggest.

For homeowners who bought before 2021, the equity position in Morningside has moved considerably. Understanding what that looks like against current conditions, and what timing means for your specific property, is worth a practical conversation.
