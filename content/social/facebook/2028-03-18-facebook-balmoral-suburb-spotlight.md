## Caption

Balmoral sits directly on the river between Hawthorne and Wynnum Road, and it has a buyer profile unlike most inner-east suburbs.

The suburb is known for its elevated riverside position, large established blocks, and a concentration of substantial homes that rarely come to market. Transaction volume in Balmoral is genuinely low compared to neighbouring suburbs, which means comparable sales data is thin and pricing requires more nuanced judgment than a simple suburb median suggests.

For sellers, low volume is a double-edged reality. There is less competing stock, which can work in your favour if you are well-prepared and the market is active. But the buyer pool that is specifically looking in Balmoral is smaller than in higher-volume suburbs. A property that sits for three or four weeks without an offer in Balmoral signals a pricing issue more clearly than the same timeline would in Camp Hill or Coorparoo.

The buyer who buys in Balmoral has usually looked at Hawthorne and Norman Park and made a deliberate decision to pay for the specific combination of elevation, position, and quiet that Balmoral delivers. That buyer is almost always financially ready and emotionally committed to the suburb.

If you own in Balmoral and have been thinking about timing a sale, I am happy to give you an honest assessment of what the current market looks like for your property.
