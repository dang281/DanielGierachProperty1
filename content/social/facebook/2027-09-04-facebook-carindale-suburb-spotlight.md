## Caption

Carindale gives families space, privacy, and one of Brisbane's largest retail centres at the end of the street, a combination that is harder to find as you move closer to the inner city.

Westfield Carindale is a genuine lifestyle anchor for the suburb. Day-to-day convenience is built into the fabric of living here in a way that residents often only fully appreciate when they consider suburbs without it. But the retail centre is only part of the picture. The housing stock, predominantly post-1970s construction on generous blocks, suits buyers who want room to renovate over time, space for a pool or outdoor entertaining area, and a sense of separation between their home and their neighbours.

Carindale borders the Carindale Valley and creek corridor, which gives the suburb a greener, more open feel than inner suburbs at comparable price points. The streets are quieter, the blocks are larger, and the overall liveability has a different character to the denser pockets of the inner east.

The buyer profile here tends toward established families, often with older children, who have the budget for a larger and more private property and are prioritising space and practicality. Public transport is primarily bus-based, which suits buyers who rely on car access and are not chasing train-line proximity.
