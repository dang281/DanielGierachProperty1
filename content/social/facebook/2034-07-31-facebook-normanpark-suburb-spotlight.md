## Caption

Norman Park is where the elevation, the park, and the character home stock combine to create a suburb that resists being described quickly. It sits between Hawthorne and Carina, commands ridge views in its upper streets, and has Whites Hill Reserve as the suburb's green anchor.

The housing stock ranges from very large Queenslanders on wide blocks in the elevated streets to post-war brick and modest timber homes toward the suburb's flat perimeter. Buyers who invest the time to understand Norman Park's internal geography usually find that the difference in price between the top and bottom of the suburb is substantial.

The school catchment, Norman Park State School, is a consistent driver of family buyer demand and contributes to the suburb's lower rate of turnover. Owners here tend to stay.

For the right buyer, particularly those looking for a character home with a outlook and within reach of the Morningside retail strip and the Hawthorne ferry, Norman Park is one of the inner east's most rewarding decisions.
