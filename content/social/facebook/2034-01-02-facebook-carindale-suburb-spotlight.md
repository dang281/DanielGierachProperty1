## Caption

Carindale sits at the intersection of two motorways and one of Brisbane's largest retail precincts, and that combination keeps demand solid year after year. Families relocating to the eastern suburbs consistently shortlist Carindale for the block sizes, the Westfield proximity, and the straightforward access to the Gateway and Logan motorways when work or travel pulls them south or north.

The housing stock here is a genuine mix. Post-war homes on wide allotments sit alongside more recent builds, and buyers are often weighing the land component just as heavily as the house itself. State school options in the area attract young families who want good public education without paying the premium that comes with a Cavendish Road High catchment address.

Sales tend to be driven by upsizers from nearby Mount Gravatt or Carina who know this corridor well, alongside interstate buyers who have done their research and want space within reach of the CBD. Homes that are well-presented and honestly priced move cleanly. Buyers here are practical and do their numbers.

If you own in Carindale and are thinking about your next move, the current market is worth understanding before you decide anything. See what's been happening locally at danielgierach.com/suburbs/carindale
