## Caption

The elevated terrain across much of Mt Gravatt East produces something that buyers in Brisbane's inner south consistently seek and rarely find at this price point: genuine cross-breezes, views, and a sense of separation from neighbouring homes that flat suburban streets cannot replicate.

Properties on the higher ground here benefit from natural ventilation and aspect in a way that makes Brisbane's subtropical climate far more liveable. That physical characteristic of the suburb is a genuine selling point, and buyers who have lived on elevated blocks tend to specifically look for that again when they move. The mix of housing stock is broad, from post-war homes and 1970s and 1980s construction through to more recently renovated properties across a range of block sizes, which gives buyers at different budget levels a realistic entry point.

Griffith University's Nathan campus borders the western edge of the suburb and creates consistent demand from academic staff and postgraduate students seeking to own or rent nearby. That university-driven demand adds a layer of depth to buyer interest that purely residential suburbs of similar size do not have.

Day-to-day convenience is well-covered: Westfield Garden City is close by, and Greenbank and Mimosa parks give families practical green space in the immediate area. The Macgregor and Nathan road corridors provide solid access to the CBD and south-east Brisbane more broadly.
