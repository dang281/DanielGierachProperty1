## Caption

Camp Hill has two of the most sought-after school catchments on Brisbane's southside in Cavendish Road State High and Whites Hill State College, and that fact shapes the suburb's buyer profile more than almost any other single factor. Families do not stumble into Camp Hill; they target it, often after a careful review of catchment boundaries and available stock.

The Depot precinct and the Greenway development have added genuine dining and retail amenity over the past decade, and the result is a suburb that no longer requires a trip to Cavendish Road or Oxford Street for a good meal or a weekend coffee. That shift has only reinforced owner-occupier demand. The housing stock is a mix of character Queenslanders, post-war homes that have been extended and renovated, and newer builds, giving the suburb a range of entry points without a uniform look. Properties in Camp Hill that sit within the Cavendish Road State High or Whites Hill State College catchment zones and present well draw multiple-buyer competition consistently. The market here is not passive; buyers who want this suburb know their boundaries and act when the right property comes up.

Turnover is low relative to demand, which means well-prepared vendors are in a strong position when they do bring a property to market.
