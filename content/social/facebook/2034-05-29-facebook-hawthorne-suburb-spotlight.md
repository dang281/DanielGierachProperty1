## Caption

Hawthorne's location between Bulimba and Balmoral places it at the centre of the inner east river corridor without the price premium that the Bulimba Oxford Street address commands. For buyers who want river proximity and a walkable village strip without paying for the address recognition that Bulimba carries, Hawthorne is the logical alternative and the buyer pool knows it.

The Hawthorne Ferry terminal connects directly to the City, and the service runs frequently enough to be a reliable commute option rather than an occasional leisure trip. For buyers who are calculating their daily travel costs and time, the ferry removes the parking and traffic variables that make CBD commuting from further suburbs unpredictable.

The Lores Bonney Riverwalk access from Hawthorne is a genuine lifestyle asset. The off-road path along the river bank connects Hawthorne to Bulimba in one direction and to Norman Park and beyond in the other. Buyers who are prioritising an active lifestyle and want that infrastructure from their front door put Hawthorne on their shortlist specifically.

The suburb's stock of character homes on established blocks creates a renovation opportunity profile that attracts buyers who want to buy at the suburb's floor price and improve from there. Those buyers are present in winter.
