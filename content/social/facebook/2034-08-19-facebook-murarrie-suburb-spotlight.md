## Caption

Murarrie's transformation over the past decade has reshaped the conversation about what inner-east Brisbane looks like. Newer residential pockets have attracted buyers who want more space than Bulimba or Hawthorne can offer at the same budget, while still being within a short drive of the Oxford Street amenity strip and Brisbane Airport.

The suburb's access to Wynnum Road and the Gateway Motorway corridor makes it attractive for buyers with commitments on both sides of the city. Families with children in state schools appreciate the Murarrie State School catchment, and the suburb's relatively low-density character means blocks remain generous.

Investor interest has also been steady given Murarrie's proximity to large employment nodes at the Gateway precinct and Metroplex. Rental demand has been consistent, keeping vacancy rates low relative to the broader Brisbane market.

If you own in Murarrie and want a realistic picture of what a sale would return in the current market, a no-obligation appraisal is a good first conversation.
