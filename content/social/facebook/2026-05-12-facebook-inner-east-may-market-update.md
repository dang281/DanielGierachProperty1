## Caption

The Brisbane inner east is posting 17 days on market as a median. That figure tells you more about the current buyer pool than any single auction result.

Seventeen days is fast. For context, a balanced market typically sits around 40 to 45 days. At 17 days, well-priced properties in Balmoral, Camp Hill, Norman Park, and Morningside are turning over before most buyers even get a second look. The sellers who are performing are those who prepared properly and priced on evidence rather than aspiration.

The rental vacancy rate in Brisbane held at 1.6 per cent in March 2026. Inner east pockets are running below 1 per cent in many streets. That sustained undersupply is keeping investor demand active even as borrowing costs have risen. Investors who held through the rate cycle are seeing yield recovery.

What this means if you are thinking about selling: the window is open. Stock is constrained, buyer demand is genuine, and the competition dynamic that drives results is present. That will not be true indefinitely. Autumn markets in Brisbane historically wind down through May and into June as buyer urgency fades ahead of winter.
