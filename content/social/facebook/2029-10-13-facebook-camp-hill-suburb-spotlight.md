## Caption

The blocks on the western and northern ridges of Camp Hill earn a premium for a reason. The city skyline is right there from the back deck. That view, combined with the character housing stock and the school catchment options, is why Camp Hill consistently holds its value when other suburbs soften.

The suburb is predominantly Queenslanders and postwar character homes. Buyers come here knowing they will likely be renovating, and they are fine with that. The bones of the housing stock are good and the blocks are often larger than what you find in Hawthorne or Bulimba at a comparable price.

Whites Hill Reserve is genuinely one of the better green spaces in the inner east. It is used daily by locals for trail running, mountain biking, and dog walking, and it adds a quality of life element that does not show up in the numbers but absolutely drives buyer decisions.

School catchment is a significant factor. Camp Hill State School draws families early in their suburb search, and proximity to Citipointe Christian College keeps another buyer segment firmly focused on this pocket. The Cavendish Road shopping strip on the eastern side of the suburb handles daily life well.

Median house price in Camp Hill is approximately $1.55 million. ⚠️ VERIFY before posting. Prestige downsizers wanting a level block in a quiet street are competing alongside upsizing families, which keeps supply tight.
