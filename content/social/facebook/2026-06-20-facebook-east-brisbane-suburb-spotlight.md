## Caption

East Brisbane sits 3 kilometres from the CBD, yet it has retained the character of a suburb that genuinely hasn't been overdeveloped.

The streets between Vulture Street and Wellington Road are some of the most sought-after in Brisbane's inner south. Character homes on decent-sized blocks, proximity to the Gabba and East Brisbane State School, and walkable access to the Coorparoo and Woolloongabba retail strips. For buyers who want river access at Mowbray Park, it's one of only a handful of inner-east suburbs that delivers it.

The house market here is small by volume and closely held. There were fewer than 90 house sales in the past 12 months across the entire suburb, which means comparable sales are limited and pricing requires more careful analysis than in higher-turnover suburbs. When a well-presented character home comes to market with honest pricing and a strong marketing campaign, the buyer pool is ready. Days on market for well-positioned homes is typically under 30 days.

With 2032 Olympic infrastructure reshaping the Woolloongabba and Gabba corridor over the coming years, East Brisbane's position becomes more prominent rather than less.

If you own here and you're considering your options, the conversation is worth having now.
