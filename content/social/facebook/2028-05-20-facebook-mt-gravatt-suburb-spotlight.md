## Caption

Mt Gravatt has a reputation as Brisbane's south-side gateway, and for good reason. The suburb is one of the more self-contained in the city, with retail, schooling, dining, and open space all within comfortable reach.

Mount Gravatt Conservation Reserve is the suburb's natural centrepiece. The bushland trails and elevated lookouts give residents something most city-fringe suburbs cannot offer, and that lifestyle quality shows up in long-term property performance. Families who move into Mt Gravatt tend to stay, which means turnover is measured and stock is rarely oversupplied.

The Garden City shopping precinct on the Upper Mount Gravatt border covers the major retail and services base, while Logan Road provides a commercial spine with cafes, local services, and easy access north toward the CBD or south toward the motorway network.

Buyers in Mt Gravatt range from families seeking space and schooling to investors targeting the strong tenant pool generated by Griffith University and the broader south-side hospital precinct. Both groups compete for the same limited housing stock, which keeps auction clearance rates healthy.

Median house price is sitting around $1.15 million. Well-located homes on the higher ground, with views toward the reserve or the city skyline, have been regularly clearing above that benchmark.
