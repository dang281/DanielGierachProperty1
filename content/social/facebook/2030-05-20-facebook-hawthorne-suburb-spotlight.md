## Caption

Hawthorne's position in the inner east has strengthened over the past decade not because the suburb changed dramatically but because the buyer profile that comes here increasingly understands what they are getting and is prepared to pay for it accordingly.

The access points are multiple: Wynnum Road, the Morningside train station a short walk away, ferry access at the Hawthorne wharf, and the quick drive to Oxford Street in Bulimba. For buyers who value optionality in how they get around Brisbane, Hawthorne sits at a genuinely convenient junction.

Glen Road is the suburb's own commercial strip: smaller and quieter than Oxford Street but with a local café culture that residents describe as one of the suburb's most appealing day-to-day features. The morning traffic at the better cafes on Glen Road reflects a community of people who chose the suburb deliberately.

The character home stock in Hawthorne is largely intact. The Queenslanders and weatherboard homes that define the streetscapes have been subject to renovation cycles over multiple decades, and the current quality of the renovated stock is high enough that buyers can often occupy them without major capital works.

For sellers in Hawthorne, winter campaigns in this suburb are worth serious consideration. The buyer pool is motivated, the suburb is well-known to searchers, and thin supply in the cooler months is a real advantage.
