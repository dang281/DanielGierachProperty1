## Caption

Cannon Hill station puts the CBD within easy commuting range without the inner-city price tag, and that arithmetic is not lost on the buyers who consistently look here. The suburb also has its own shopping centre for everyday needs, post-war character homes on established blocks, and a location that connects naturally to both the Wynnum Road and Lytton Road corridors.

The housing stock is primarily post-war, with a mix of original and updated homes. Cannon Hill has not been subject to the degree of infill and consolidation that characterises some neighbouring suburbs, so block sizes have remained relatively intact and homes feel properly established rather than squeezed in. That appeals to first-home buyers and young families who want a proper yard and a suburb that functions on a human scale. [VERIFY: current median house price before posting.]

First-home buyers and young family upgraders make up a significant share of the buyer profile in Cannon Hill. The combination of train access, a suburb shopping centre, and a price point below Morningside and Hawthorne makes it a logical step-up suburb for buyers coming out of units in the inner east. Investor interest is also present, given the rental demand from workers who need Gateway Motorway or train access.

Properties in Cannon Hill that present cleanly and are priced correctly tend to move without extended campaigns.
