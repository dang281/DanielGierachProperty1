## Caption

East Brisbane is the kind of suburb that people discover when they are priced out of New Farm and find that the fundamentals are almost identical at a meaningfully lower entry price.

It sits between the CBD and Woolloongabba, 2 to 3km from the city, with river access, easy cross-city connectivity, and the character streetscape that defines Brisbane's inner east. The suburb benefited directly from the Gabba precinct activity and Cross River Rail development in the years leading up to the 2032 Games corridor build-out.

The buyer profile here skews toward professional couples and young families who want walkability and character without the New Farm premium. That demand base tends to be specific and motivated, which is what produces competitive conditions at the well-presented end of the market.

For sellers in East Brisbane, the typical challenge is not finding a buyer. It is pricing accurately in a suburb where comparable sales can vary significantly based on aspect, renovation quality, and block position.
