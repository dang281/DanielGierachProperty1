## Caption

Oxford Street in Hawthorne is one of the better-known high streets in Brisbane's inner east, and it is one of the main reasons buyers target the suburb once they have decided the inner east is where they want to be.

But Oxford Street is the easy part of the Hawthorne story to tell. The more interesting part is what happens when you walk two or three streets back from it.

The streets running off the riverfront and up the gentle ridges above Oxford Street are where Hawthorne produces its best results. Character homes on elevated positions, with views toward the river or the CBD, in streets with a consistency of architecture that reflects how long the suburb has been regarded as one of Brisbane's most desirable. Those properties do not come to market often, and when they do, the buyer competition is focused and quick.

Away from the ridgeline, Hawthorne still offers straightforward value for families who want walkable access to Oxford Street, good school options, and the ferry terminal at Bulimba Wharf within cycling distance. The suburb's geography keeps it self-contained in a way that few comparable suburbs can manage.
