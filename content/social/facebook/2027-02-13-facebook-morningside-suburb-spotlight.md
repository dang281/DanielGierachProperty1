## Caption

Morningside is a suburb with two distinct property markets, and which side of Wynnum Road you are on tells most of the story.

North of Wynnum Road, the streets slope toward the river and the properties are character Queenslanders on elevated blocks with aspects that, in the right streets, return glimpses of the river corridor. This is where Morningside commands its strongest prices and where competition at auction is most consistent. The buyer profile is overwhelmingly owner-occupying families and couples who want the character and proximity of the inner east without paying the full Bulimba or Hawthorne premium.

South of Wynnum Road, the housing is more varied: post-war brick homes, smaller Queenslanders, and a growing number of townhouses and newer infill development. This part of Morningside is where the first home buyer and investor buyer competes, and it offers a more accessible entry into the inner east.

The suburb's lifestyle draw is genuine. Morningside is walkable to a growing cafe and dining strip, has good bus connections to the CBD, and sits in close proximity to the Bulimba Ferry and the Hawthorne restaurants. Cannon Hill Anglican College and Morningside State School are both accessible from within the suburb.

The streets between Lytton Road and the river tend to be where sellers achieve results that surprise even the most optimistic agents.
