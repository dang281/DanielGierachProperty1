## Caption

Norman Park has a consistent identity in the inner east market: a character home suburb with genuine lifestyle credentials, held by owners who typically stay for a long time and sell when life changes demand it rather than for investment reasons alone.

Whites Hill Reserve is the defining open space. The walking tracks through the reserve, the proximity to the Clem Jones Centre, and the generally elevated and quiet character of the residential streets give Norman Park a liveability score that regular buyers in the inner east place near the top of their list.

The housing stock is predominantly character homes: Queenslanders, post-war weatherboards, and the occasional postwar brick property on larger blocks. The renovation quality varies, which creates both the price spread and the opportunity. A well-renovated character home in a good street commands significant buyer attention. A home in original condition on the same block offers a renovation prospect that builder-buyers and developers respond to.

The school catchment story is important. Whites Hill State College sits at the centre of the secondary catchment logic for this suburb, and it has been a consistent driver of buyer demand from families with children approaching high school age.

Norman Park is a suburb where selling at the right time and with the right campaign structure consistently achieves above-expectation results.
