## Caption

Norman Park has one of the most consistent buyer profiles in Brisbane's inner east. The typical purchaser is a family that has outgrown an apartment or inner-suburb townhouse and wants a house with a yard in a suburb that still feels genuinely liveable. Whites Hill Reserve, the bike paths to the CBD, and the access to the Coorparoo and Carindale retail catchments all feature prominently in what buyers say they value.

The suburb's price range sits above its immediate neighbours to the east but below Hawthorne and Balmoral to the north, which creates a specific positioning for sellers. Buyers who have been active in those suburbs and have not found what they need are frequently drawn to Norman Park as the next consideration. That movement of buyers across suburb boundaries is one of the more reliable drivers of demand in Norman Park open homes.

Character homes in the central streets of Norman Park, particularly those with city or treetop views, consistently achieve strong results when they are well-presented and correctly priced. The buyer competition for the right property in the right street remains meaningful.
