## Caption

Mt Gravatt East offers something that becomes harder to find the closer you get to the CBD: elevation, district views, and decent land. For buyers who prioritise block size and school catchment over the shortest possible commute, this suburb keeps coming up as the answer.

The housing stock is a mix of postwar homes and 1970s to 1980s brick and tile on blocks that are generally larger than what you find in the inner east suburbs at a comparable price. That land component is what renovation buyers are paying for. A well-executed renovation on a good block here with views can produce a result that justifies the work, and there is enough unrenovated stock remaining to give buyers that opportunity.

The school catchment is a genuine driver of buyer interest. Seton College and Padua College are within reach for Catholic school families, and Cavendish Road State High School is one of the inner east's well-regarded state secondary options. Families doing suburb research with high school in mind tend to land on this pocket of the inner south-east.

Westfield Garden City is the practical retail anchor, accessible without a long drive. As inner east buyers have been priced out of Coorparoo, Holland Park, and Camp Hill, Mt Gravatt East has picked up consistent interest from families who want the land and the school proximity and are comfortable being a little further from the city.

Median house price in Mt Gravatt East is approximately $1.2 million. ⚠️ VERIFY before posting.
