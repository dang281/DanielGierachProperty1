## Caption

Coorparoo has a lot going for it that doesn't always get talked about in the same breath as the suburb's more prominent inner east neighbours. Train access from Coorparoo station on the Cleveland line puts the CBD within a straightforward commute, and the bus network fills in the gaps for residents who aren't close to the station. That kind of transport infrastructure is increasingly hard to find at Coorparoo's price point.

The housing stock ranges from character homes and Queenslanders through to post-war housing and a growing number of infill townhouses. The streets south of Cavendish Road tend to be quieter and sit on more elevated ground, which draws buyers who want a sense of separation from the main traffic corridors while still being close to everything. Cavendish Road itself is the main commercial spine, with daily shopping and food options concentrated along that stretch.

Coorparoo State School and Coorparoo Secondary College anchor the school catchment appeal, which is a consistent pull for families at the primary and secondary level alike. The suburb also benefits from proximity to Greenslopes and Holland Park, giving residents access to retail and services without travelling far.

For buyers entering the inner east, Coorparoo offers one of the more realistic starting points without requiring significant compromise on amenity or access. That dynamic keeps demand steady even when other parts of the market soften.
