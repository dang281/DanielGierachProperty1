## Caption

Carindale sits at the price point where the inner east corridor begins to offer genuine family-scale land at values that are increasingly hard to find in the suburbs closer to the river. The combination of large blocks, practical retail access, and a well-established school network has made the suburb a consistent performer for families who are making their second or third property purchase and prioritising space over proximity.

The Westfield Carindale hub is an underrated lifestyle asset. For families with young children, the convenience of having a full-service shopping centre within a five-minute drive reduces the weekly logistics burden considerably. The opening of restaurants and casual dining options within and around the centre over the past several years has improved Carindale's evening lifestyle credentials.

The housing stock in the core Carindale residential streets is predominantly 1980s to 2000s construction on 600 to 800-square-metre blocks. The renovation potential in this stock is significant for buyers who are prepared to invest time and capital. The land values alone in Carindale make the arithmetic work in a way that it does not in suburbs where the land price is much higher.

For investors, the rental demand in Carindale is anchored by the family segment and is relatively stable through market cycles. Properties that are well-maintained and appropriately priced tend not to sit vacant for long.
