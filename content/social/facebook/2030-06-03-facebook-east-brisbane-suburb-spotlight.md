## Caption

East Brisbane's market dynamic is driven by a buyer profile that is unlike most other inner east suburbs. The suburb attracts a high proportion of buyers who are prioritising CBD walkability above almost all other considerations. For that buyer, East Brisbane is one of a small number of residential suburbs in Brisbane where the lifestyle they want is achievable without an apartment.

The character home stock in East Brisbane is concentrated on the elevated streets above the flat corridor. Properties with a genuine CBD or Story Bridge outlook are a different asset class from the standard East Brisbane residential house, and buyers who have identified those streets and know what they want will pay to access them.

The Cross River Rail connection at Woolloongabba, a short walk from parts of East Brisbane, has added a layer of connectivity that is reshaping how buyers think about the suburb's accessibility. The transformation of the Woolloongabba precinct around the Olympic Legacy infrastructure has made the broader corridor significantly more appealing.

Winter in East Brisbane does not dramatically change the buyer profile. The buyers who want to live within walking distance of the CBD are not seasonal. They are looking year-round and respond to properties that meet their criteria when they appear.

For sellers, the tightly held character of the suburb and the consistent depth of demand makes the decision about timing somewhat less critical than in suburbs where buyer pools are more seasonal.
