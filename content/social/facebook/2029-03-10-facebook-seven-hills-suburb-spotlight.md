## Caption

Seven Hills is one of the least-publicised suburbs in Brisbane's inner east, which is exactly why the buyers who know it value it as much as they do.

The suburb sits between Mt Gravatt East to the south and Coorparoo to the north, with direct access to the M3 motorway and a quiet, residential character that stands in contrast to the busier arterial corridors surrounding it. For families who want to be close to everything without living on a main road, the combination of location and streetscape is difficult to find at the price points Seven Hills offers.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war detached homes on well-sized blocks, with a mix of original properties and renovated homes that reflect the suburb's growing owner-occupier base. The school options in the surrounding area, particularly within easy reach of Seton College and closer private school options, make the suburb an active target for family buyers who have done the research.

Seven Hills does not appear in the same weekend media coverage as Bulimba or Hawthorne, which often means buyers arrive from research rather than fashion. Those buyers tend to know what they want and be ready to commit when the right property comes up.
