## Caption

Cannon Hill sits at the eastern gateway of Brisbane's inner suburbs, and the families who buy here tend to know exactly why they chose it over the alternatives.

The suburb is quiet by inner-east standards. Wide streets, good tree cover, and a block size that still accommodates a proper backyard make it a natural target for families moving up from townhouse living in the inner city. Cannon Hill Anglican College is the suburb's highest-profile schooling drawcard, and it consistently pulls buyer enquiry from families who are specifically targeting the catchment.

The commute story is straightforward: the Gateway Motorway is accessible for those heading south, and bus services along Creek Road and Wynnum Road connect to the CBD for those who prefer public transport. The Cannon Hill shopping precinct covers day-to-day needs, and both Westfield Carindale and Westfield Chermside are within a practical drive.

Housing stock is mostly three and four-bedroom post-war homes, with a meaningful number of renovated Queenslanders sprinkled through the better streets. Buyers looking for a project still find them here, and the competition at the finished end of the market reflects steady demand rather than seasonal spikes.

Median house price sits around $1.2 million, which positions Cannon Hill as better value than the Morningside median while offering comparable lifestyle fundamentals.
