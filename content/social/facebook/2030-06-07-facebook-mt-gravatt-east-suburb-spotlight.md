## Caption

Mt Gravatt East in winter is a suburb where patient buyers find value that the broader market has not yet fully reflected. The combination of elevated blocks, conservation reserve proximity, and a price point below the inner east premium makes it worth inspecting for buyers who are doing their own research rather than relying on what is popular in their peer group.

The Mt Gravatt Conservation Reserve is a practical lifestyle asset that is undervalued in how the suburb is discussed. Walking and cycling access to the reserve is available from a significant proportion of the residential streets, and the sense of green space within an inner-south Brisbane address is increasingly sought after by buyers who want lifestyle without the inner east price.

The Griffith University Nathan campus proximity means that rental demand in the suburb is supported by a consistent student and academic tenant base in addition to the family owner-occupier segment. For investors considering the broader inner south corridor, that dual demand base reduces vacancy risk.

The housing stock in Mt Gravatt East includes examples at every stage of the renovation cycle, which means buyers at different budget levels can find a property that meets their criteria. The best-value positions tend to be the older brick homes on larger blocks in the elevated sections of the suburb, where land value and outlook combine.
