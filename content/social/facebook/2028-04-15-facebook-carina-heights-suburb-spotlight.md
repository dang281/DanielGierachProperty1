## Caption

Carina Heights does not have the profile of some of its east Brisbane neighbours, but that is exactly why the buyers who find it tend to stay.

Sitting just west of Carina and a short drive from Westfield Carindale, Carina Heights offers the kind of quiet residential streets that families actively seek out. The block sizes are generous by Brisbane standards, and the housing mix, mostly post-war homes alongside a growing number of renovated Queenslanders, gives buyers options at different price points.

Bus connections to the CBD run along Creek Road and Old Cleveland Road, making the suburb practical for commuters who do not want to drive every day. For families, the proximity to Westfield Carindale covers most day-to-day shopping and dining needs without the chaos of an inner-city postcode.

The median house price sits around $1.0 million, which places it below Carina and well below Cannon Hill. That gap is the selling point for buyers stretching into the eastern suburbs for the first time.

Sellers in Carina Heights benefit from that affordability positioning. Stock tends to be limited in this pocket, which keeps competition among buyers steady through autumn and into winter.
