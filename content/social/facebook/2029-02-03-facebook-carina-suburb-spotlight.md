## Caption

Carina's value proposition in Brisbane's inner east has always been straightforward: more house for the money, with less compromise on location than most buyers expect.

The suburb sits between Carindale to the east and Coorparoo to the west, close enough to both Westfield Carindale and the Coorparoo Square shopping precinct to make daily life convenient without depending on either. Families come for the school options in the area and the generous block sizes that are increasingly difficult to find within 10 kilometres of the CBD.

What defines the Carina buyer is a preference for practical value over prestige. They are often coming from a rental, or upsizing from a unit elsewhere in the inner east, and they want space, a yard, and access to the city without paying Bulimba prices. The properties that attract the most competition are the original post-war homes on larger allotments, particularly any with dual living potential or a flat block that suits a family extension.

Carina does not generate the same social media attention as some of its neighbours, but it consistently produces strong results for vendors who price correctly and present their properties well.
