## Caption

Seven Hills is the inner-east suburb that consistently surprises buyers who drive through it for the first time.

The name does not carry the recognition of Hawthorne or Bulimba. The suburb profile in search results does not capture what the streets actually look like. What buyers find when they get there: an elevated ridge 5km from the CBD, a large bushland reserve on the suburb's boundary, city views from the high streets, and a quiet, established residential character that has nothing in common with the profile people expect.

The buyer pool that discovers Seven Hills is self-selecting. They have done a genuine comparison of the inner east, looked beyond the obvious addresses, and concluded that the combination of location, elevation, and the Cavendish Road State High School catchment represents value relative to what adjacent suburbs command.

Transaction volume in Seven Hills is lower than most inner-east suburbs, which means the comparable sales pool is shallow. For sellers, that is a double-edged reality. The scarcity keeps genuine buyers focused. But overpricing in a low-volume suburb leaves a listing sitting, which is visible to everyone watching.

If you own in Seven Hills and want to understand what your property is worth in the current autumn market, I am happy to give you an honest, research-based answer.
