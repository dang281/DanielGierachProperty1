## Caption

Bulimba in winter is a different open home experience from Bulimba in autumn. The volume drops, but the quality of buyer increases. The buyers who make the effort to attend an open home in May or June in Bulimba have almost always already decided the suburb is where they want to be. They are inspecting to confirm the property, not to browse the suburb.

That profile matters for sellers. A Bulimba campaign in the cooler months will typically attract fewer total registrations than the same property launched in March. But the conversion rate from inspection to offer tends to be stronger, because the uncommitted browsers who swell spring and autumn numbers are not part of the mix.

Oxford Street is the community anchor. The morning rhythm of the coffee strip, the Friday evening restaurant trade, and the weekend farmers market activity gives Bulimba a genuinely active local life that residents describe as the primary reason they stay. That lifestyle premium is real and it is reflected in property results.

The river precinct and the CityCat ferry service add a CBD connectivity option that most inner east suburbs cannot match. For buyers who want to commute by water, Bulimba is one of a small number of residential suburbs where that genuinely works.

The tightly held nature of the suburb means that supply in winter is thin, and thin supply with motivated buyers is a combination sellers can work with.
