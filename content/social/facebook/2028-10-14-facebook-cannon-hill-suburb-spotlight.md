## Caption

Cannon Hill gets less attention than its immediate neighbours but consistently delivers strong results for vendors who know how to position it.

The suburb sits between Morningside and Murarrie, with the Cannon Hill Shopping Centre providing the day-to-day retail anchor and the Eastern Busway making the CBD commute genuinely practical. For the buyer who is priced out of Morningside but wants the same fundamentals, Cannon Hill is consistently where they land.

The housing stock in Cannon Hill is predominantly the original post-war and 1960s brick and hardwood construction that defines the broader inner east. The renovated homes in the streets around the shopping precinct and those backing onto the Bulimba Creek floodplain edges tend to achieve the upper range of results. Properties presenting with outdated kitchens and bathrooms but on good land still attract strong competition from buyers who understand what a full renovation delivers in the current market.

Days on market in Cannon Hill tends to run lean for well-priced properties. The suburb has a reliable buyer pool driven by affordability relative to Morningside and by genuine liveability credentials that first-time and repeat inner east buyers recognise.
