## Caption

At approximately 5km from the CBD, Coorparoo offers inner-east proximity without the price tag that comes with Hawthorne or Camp Hill. That gap is the reason the suburb consistently attracts a wide buyer pool, from first-home buyers stretching into a house to investors who understand what rental demand looks like this close to the city.

Coorparoo Square has changed how residents interact with the suburb's commercial core. The supermarket, food and beverage tenancies, and service retail make it a proper local hub, and the precinct has added foot traffic and a sense of activity to that part of the suburb. Transport is practical: multiple bus routes service the suburb, and the proximity to the Eastern Busway gives residents bus rapid transit access that is faster than the average inner-suburb commute.

The housing stock is genuinely mixed. Queenslanders, interwar homes, and a significant number of units across multiple price points mean the suburb works for different buyer profiles at the same time. That breadth of stock is part of what keeps turnover healthy.

Coorparoo Secondary College catchment draws family buyers, particularly those who have been tracking inner-east suburbs and recognise that the school catchment here is solid without carrying the premium of the most sought-after state schools.
