## Caption

Balmoral's river frontage and park system create a combination of public amenity that is genuinely rare in the inner east. The Balmoral foreshore, the parkland along Lytton Road, and the access to the river trail network give residents a daily outdoor environment that has no equivalent at this proximity to the CBD. For buyers who have been watching Balmoral from a distance while considering Hawthorne or Bulimba, the foreshore access is often the deciding factor.

The housing character in Balmoral ranges from restored Queenslanders on elevated blocks to more contemporary infill on the flatter streets closer to the water. The consistency of land content and the relative restraint on subdivision compared to some neighbouring suburbs means that the street character has held up well. Buyers who are sensitive to streetscape quality respond to that, and it sustains values across the market cycle.

The proximity to Bulimba State School and the broader Seven Hills precinct gives Balmoral families access to school infrastructure that does not require a long morning drive. For a two-income household with children in primary school, that morning logistics advantage is worth quantifying.

Winter in Balmoral is not a deterrent to buyers who have specifically chosen this suburb for the river and park access. The outdoor environment does not close in June.
