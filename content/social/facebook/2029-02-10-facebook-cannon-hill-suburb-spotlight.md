## Caption

Cannon Hill sits between Morningside and Carina in a part of Brisbane's inner east that has seen sustained buyer interest over the past several years.

The suburb has a practical identity. Cannon Hill Shopping Centre provides everyday convenience, and the Cleveland rail line running through the northern edge of the suburb gives it public transport access that many comparable inner east suburbs lack. Families buying here are often making a deliberate choice based on the combination of school options, rail access, and the ability to find detached housing at a price point below Hawthorne and Bulimba without feeling like they have left the inner east.

The housing stock in Cannon Hill is predominantly post-war detached homes, with a mix of original properties and extensively renovated character homes. The blocks tend to be reasonable in size by inner east standards, and many have been developed with second dwellings or studios, which adds flexibility for buyers with specific requirements.

Buyer demand in Cannon Hill has been consistent. The suburb does not have the trophy street profile of some of its neighbours, but the value it offers for families looking to enter or upsize within the inner east has kept enquiry steady regardless of the broader market cycle.
