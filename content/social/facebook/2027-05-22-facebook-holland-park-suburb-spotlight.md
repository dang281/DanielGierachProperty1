## Caption

Holland Park sits between Camp Hill and Coorparoo in a way that captures some of the best qualities of both without quite matching their price tags. The suburb is well established, predominantly character homes on elevated ground, and it has the kind of local amenity that families tend to prioritise: solid schools, parks, good bus access, and a low-fuss daily routine.

The housing stock leans toward Queenslanders and post-war weatherboards, many of them renovated to a standard that reflects how seriously the area's owner-occupier base takes their properties. The elevated streets are the most sought-after, offering views and breezes that make a meaningful difference to liveability through a Brisbane summer. For buyers who've been looking at Hawthorne or Camp Hill and found themselves priced just outside reach, Holland Park is often where the search lands.

Holland Park State School and Holland Park State High both serve the suburb's catchment, which is a consistent factor in keeping family demand steady. Logan Road runs through the area and provides direct bus access to the city, reducing the reliance on a car for the commute. Parks are well distributed through the suburb, and the Holland Park West shopping strip covers day-to-day retail without requiring a trip further afield.

The suburb doesn't have river access, but for many families that's a fair exchange for what Holland Park offers at its price point. If you own here and you're thinking about selling, I'd welcome a conversation about what your property might be worth.
