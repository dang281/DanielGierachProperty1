## Caption

Mt Gravatt East punches above its weight on livability relative to its price point, which is why it keeps appearing on shortlists for families who have been priced out of Camp Hill or Coorparoo.

The suburb's main advantage is land. Block sizes in the 500 to 800 square metre range on quiet residential streets give families the outdoor space that the smaller inner-ring suburbs can no longer offer consistently. Griffith University's Nathan campus is within easy distance, which provides a reliable rental demand base for the investor market.

The Kessels Road and Mains Road corridors define the suburb's practical boundaries and give it strong access north to the inner east and south to the Gateway network. Buyers who need to travel regularly in multiple directions find the positioning genuinely useful.

The housing mix is predominantly post-war brick and weatherboard, with a good proportion of homes that have been updated in the past decade and a still-significant number that represent genuine renovation opportunity. The renovation buyer pool here tends to be financially grounded: people who know what a renovation costs and want the equity upside without paying for someone else's recent work.

Mt Gravatt East is a suburb where first and second home buyers can often find better value per square metre than the headlines suggest. That gap tends to close over time.
