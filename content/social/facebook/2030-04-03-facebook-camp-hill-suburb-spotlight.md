## Caption

Camp Hill is one of the inner east suburbs where the buyer profile has shifted most noticeably over the past decade. What was once predominantly a first-home-buyer suburb has become a genuine destination for families upgrading from apartments and townhouses in Coorparoo and Morningside.

The reason is Whites Hill State College. For families with children approaching high school age, the catchment boundary in Camp Hill has become a genuine factor in property decisions. Streets that fall inside the catchment carry a premium that is consistently reflected in comparable sale analysis.

The housing stock in Camp Hill is predominantly character homes on 600-square-metre-plus blocks, which gives buyers the combination of land and renovation potential that drives demand in this part of the inner east. The result is a suburb where the gap between a well-prepared property and a poorly presented one is often more than $100,000 at the final result.

Access to Whites Hill Reserve, proximity to Old Cleveland Road shopping and cafes, and good bus routes to the CBD make Camp Hill genuinely liveable for a wide range of buyers, not just those drawn by the school catchment.

For sellers, the autumn period tends to deliver strong competition in Camp Hill, particularly for properties in the $1.2M to $1.7M range.
