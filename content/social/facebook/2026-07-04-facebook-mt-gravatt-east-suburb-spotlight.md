## Caption

Mt Gravatt East has been one of the quieter success stories in Brisbane's inner south-east, and it's becoming less quiet.

The suburb sits between the shopping and commercial density of Westfield Mt Gravatt and the residential streets that climb up towards Whites Hill Reserve. The buyer profile has shifted over the past three years from predominantly families trading up from further east, to include a growing number of buyers priced out of Coorparoo, Camp Hill, and Morningside who see the value gap and act.

The appeal is a combination that doesn't require much explaining: blocks that aren't postage stamp sizes, a mix of renovated post-war homes and newer builds, strong secondary school access through Seton College and Mt Gravatt State High, and Westfield at the bottom of the hill.

Price growth in the suburb has run at roughly 10% annually over the past year, and the pace of that growth is unlikely to slow while demand from displaced inner-east buyers remains active.

For owners who bought five or more years ago, the equity position has moved considerably. If you've been thinking about whether to use it or take it, the current conditions are worth understanding before you decide.
