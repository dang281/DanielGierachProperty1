## Caption

March is historically one of Coorparoo's most productive months for sellers. The suburb's family buyer pool is active, school catchment urgency is high for families who've been watching the market, and the number of quality listings is never as high as demand. That combination tends to produce good results for sellers who are prepared.

What's been selling in Coorparoo, and drawing the most serious buyers, is the character housing stock on full blocks in the streets that sit above the valley floor. The Coorparoo State School catchment drives a meaningful portion of demand, but the Joseph Street café culture and the suburb's general accessibility also attract buyers who aren't exclusively driven by the school. That breadth of buyer appeal is what gives Coorparoo its market depth.

The $1.2M to $1.6M price range is where competition is most intense. Buyers in this bracket are typically well-prepared and have been looking for some time. When a home that meets their criteria appears, they don't wait.

Median house prices are around $1.5M. Check CoreLogic or PropTrack for current data before relying on that figure.

If you're a Coorparoo homeowner thinking about March or April, the preparation window is tight. The link below has what you need to understand the current market position.
