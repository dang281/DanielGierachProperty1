## Caption

At approximately 2km from the CBD, East Brisbane is one of the closest residential suburbs to the city centre on Brisbane's inner east side. That proximity has always driven demand, but the transformation of the Gabba precinct in the lead-up to the 2032 Olympics has added a new layer to the conversation around what this location will look like in the years ahead.

The suburb has a mix of older character homes and a substantial unit stock that services the strong rental demand from CBD workers, hospital staff from the nearby PA and Mater precincts, and students. That rental demand gives investors a reliable income story in a suburb where vacancy is tight.

Woolloongabba is a short walk west and has gone through its own commercial transformation, with the Stanley Street and Logan Road strips now offering restaurant, bar, and café options that have changed the neighbourhood's day-to-day feel. The Kangaroo Point Cliffs are accessible by foot across the Story Bridge, which matters to the fitness and outdoor lifestyle buyer.

Public transport is well covered. Buses along Main Street and Stanley Street connect to the city and to the inner south efficiently, and the suburb's flat topography makes cycling a genuine option for daily commuters.
