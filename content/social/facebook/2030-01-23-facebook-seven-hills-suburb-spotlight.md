## Caption

Seven Hills doesn't advertise itself loudly, which is why buyers who discover it tend to feel like they've found something the broader market hasn't fully accounted for. The suburb sits between Morningside and Cannon Hill, sharing their proximity to the inner east and the M3, yet its pricing has historically sat below both.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war, with a mix of original homes and those that have been updated or extended. Some blocks carry meaningful views given the elevated position, and land content is generally solid. For buyers who want an inner east address without an inner east price, Seven Hills is the kind of suburb worth looking at seriously.

The buyer profile has been shifting. Investors who were priced out of Morningside have been active here for some time. More recently, owner-occupiers who want a project home and a suburb with room to grow have been looking closely at what's available.

Median house prices are around $1.2M at the time of writing, though you should confirm this via CoreLogic or PropTrack before relying on it. As inner east affordability tightens, the gap between Seven Hills and its neighbours is likely to continue attracting attention.
