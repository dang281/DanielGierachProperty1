## Caption

February is when Mt Gravatt's property market starts picking up pace. The school year is underway, families have settled into routines, and buyers who missed out on something over summer are back looking with fresh urgency. For sellers who are thinking about an autumn campaign, the preparation window is right now.

What's been selling in Mt Gravatt is fairly clear: homes with genuine elevation, a view to the south or east, and blocks with enough size to justify the land component. The Cavendish Road State High School catchment continues to underpin demand from families specifically targeting that schooling option. Buyers in this category tend to be well-qualified and motivated.

Supply in Mt Gravatt has been measured. It's not a suburb that floods the market with listings, which works in favour of sellers who present well and price with the current data rather than aspirational figures from two years ago.

Median house prices are around $1.25M, though you should check current CoreLogic or PropTrack figures before making decisions based on that. The gap between Mt Gravatt and inner suburbs like Coorparoo remains meaningful, which continues to drive buyers outward from those suburbs.

If you're considering selling in Mt Gravatt this autumn, the time to have the conversation is before everyone else is trying to schedule their campaign at the same time.
