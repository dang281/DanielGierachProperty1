## Caption

Coorparoo's transport options along Old Cleveland Road make it one of the most connected suburbs in the inner east for buyers who don't want to drive. Frequent buses and the general accessibility of the corridor mean that the suburb works for people who are deliberate about their car use, and that buyer type has been growing in Brisbane.

The food scene developing along the strip has been a genuine improvement over the past several years. It's not Oxford Street, but there's enough there now that residents have real options without leaving the suburb. For buyers who want inner east liveability without the inner east premium, Coorparoo has been one of the clearest answers.

Coorparoo State School is well regarded and anchors family demand in the suburb. Greenslopes Hospital's proximity to the north adds the healthcare professional buyer segment that consistently features in the surrounding suburbs.

The housing variety in Coorparoo is broader than most people expect. Character Queenslanders, 1970s brick, and newer infill all exist within a few streets of each other. That variety means buyers with different budgets and different use cases can all find something here, and the market benefits from that breadth.

December in Coorparoo sees buyers who want to settle before the new year or position for a Q1 move-in. Properties ready to present well benefit from reduced competition at this point in the year.
