## Caption

Camp Hill is one of the most consistently demanded suburbs in the inner east. The combination of Whites Hill State College catchment coverage, elevated land with city views on the streets west of Old Cleveland Road, and a café and restaurant strip on Old Cleveland Road that punches above its size makes this suburb an easy answer to a lot of buyers' briefs.

The streets around Churchill Street and Jubilee Terrace are the ones that generate the strongest auction competition. Buyers who have attended enough open homes in Camp Hill know what a Jubilee Terrace position means for the outlook and the result at auction.

Camp Hill State Infants and Primary School is a strong primary option. For secondary, Whites Hill State College covers most of the suburb and is the school that drives premium pricing on the western side of Old Cleveland Road.

What makes Camp Hill particularly interesting in the current market is the balance between supply and demand. It is a suburb where stock does not sit for long when it is priced correctly. The buyers who have been searching for three to six months in Camp Hill are thorough. They have attended every open home and know the comparable sales better than most agents. When a property lands at the right price, the offer follows quickly.

For Camp Hill homeowners, spring and early summer continues to be the most competitive buyer period.
