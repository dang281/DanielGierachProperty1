## Caption

Mt Gravatt's spring story starts in August. The suburb's tree-lined streets and character homes photograph beautifully when the light improves and gardens begin to recover from winter, and that presentation advantage is very real in a market where first impressions online determine which properties receive inspections.

The case for Mt Gravatt in the current environment is anchored in the school catchment and the land. Cavendish Road State High is consistently one of the more sought-after state secondary catchments in the inner south-east, and families who have done their research understand that securing an address in zone is a long-term investment in school access. That buyer conviction creates a seller's market for well-positioned properties in the catchment, particularly as spring buyer numbers increase.

The plateau position of Mt Gravatt gives the suburb a character that is meaningfully different from the flat streets of surrounding areas. Owners talk about the suburb in terms of how it feels to live there, and that lived quality is not easily replicated. Buyers who inspect properties here and compare with suburbs at a similar price point frequently comment on the amenity of the streets themselves.

For vendors who have been watching and waiting, late August is the last comfortable window before spring volume makes every campaign harder to differentiate. Getting into the market now is a considered strategic move.
