## Caption

June results in the inner east are a useful leading indicator for spring. The buyers who transact in winter are the active core of the market, the ones who are not waiting for a better season. What they pay and what they pass on tells us where the genuine buyer floor sits.

Properties that cleared in June at strong prices are telling us the buyer pool has depth. Buyers are transacting in a quieter market rather than waiting. That willingness suggests the spring market will have genuine competition when the listing supply arrives.

Properties that sat in June without resolution are telling a different story. If they sat because of price, the spring buyer pool will face the same resistance. If they sat because of presentation, that is a solvable problem before spring. The vendors who use June and July to resolve presentation issues rather than hoping the spring volume papers over them are the ones who launch September campaigns into genuine competition.

The other signal to watch is what comes to market in July. Vendors who launch winter campaigns in Brisbane are typically either highly motivated or testing the market. The ones testing the market will pull back and relaunch in spring. The ones who are genuinely motivated will resolve. That pattern sets up the opening weeks of spring with a combination of motivated buyers and vendors who have been through a winter campaign and understand what the market is actually willing to pay.
