## Caption

Every December I find myself going back over the year and looking at what actually happened versus what I expected in January. 2033 had a few patterns worth noting.

The autumn window, from late February through to May, was the strongest selling period of the year. This is not unusual for Brisbane's inner east. It is a consistent historical pattern that I observe again and again, and 2033 confirmed it. Vendors who launched well-prepared campaigns in that window generally achieved strong results. The ones who launched in June or later into the winter period had to work harder for the same outcome.

School catchment suburbs continued to perform with a premium that went beyond what the broader market would suggest. The premium was not just about price growth. It was about days on market and the depth of competition at open homes. Properties within the most sought-after catchments attracted more committed buyers faster. That gap between catchment and non-catchment streets in the same suburb remained stubbornly wide.

The preparation variable continued to be the most consistent differentiator I observed across the year. Properties that went to market well presented, styled, and with strong photography consistently outperformed comparable properties that did not, regardless of the month or market conditions. This is not a new observation, but 2033 reinforced it across a wider range of price points than I have seen before.

Looking toward 2034, the post-Olympics infrastructure legacy continues to support long-term inner east demand. The investment in transport connectivity and public amenity that was built around the Games does not go away, and its effect on desirability is still working through the market.

It has been a solid year. Happy to talk through what 2034 might look like for your property.
