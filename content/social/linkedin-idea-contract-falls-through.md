## Caption

A property going back to market after a contract falls through is not a failure. It is a situation that needs to be handled correctly.

The first question is why it fell through. A buyer who exits due to a finance condition failing had a lending problem, not a property problem. A buyer who exits after a building and pest report found something significant is telling you something you need to address, either through pricing or remediation. Understanding the reason determines what happens next.

The way the property returns to market matters. A vendor who goes back publicly immediately with no change in price or presentation is signalling that nothing has changed. Buyers who passed on the property before will pass again. A vendor who takes two weeks to address whatever the contract revealed, adjusts the presentation or the price as needed, and relaunches with a clear reason why now is a better opportunity is positioned differently.

Days on market accumulates even when a property is under contract. A property that has been on the market for 90 days total, including a period under contract that fell through, needs a strong reason for the next buyer to proceed with confidence.

The practical answer is transparency. Buyers who understand exactly what happened and why a property is available again, combined with a price that reflects the current situation, tend to transact. Buyers who feel they are being kept in the dark do not.
