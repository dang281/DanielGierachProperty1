## Caption

Ten days until the Opening Ceremony. Here is what inner east open homes actually look like right now.

Attendance is down from spring peaks. That is expected and it is consistent with how July has tracked in previous years, before you even factor in the Games.

What is different this year is the composition of the people coming through. The usual weekend browsers are not showing up. The people walking through properties right now have made a deliberate choice to be in the market during a period when most of Brisbane's attention is focused elsewhere. That is a meaningful signal.

I have noticed the conversations at open homes are more substantive. People are asking specific questions about the property, the suburb, and the comparable sales. They are not just getting a feel for the area. They are genuinely assessing.

Buyers who are active in the ten days before and during the Games window tend to have clear criteria, access to finance, and a real motivation to transact. That mindset produces offers.

For sellers, this is not the environment to test the market with an optimistic price. It is the environment to price accurately, present the property at its best, and be responsive when a motivated buyer wants to have a conversation.

The open home numbers will look different in September. The buyer quality right now is as good as it gets.
