## Caption

December in Brisbane's inner east is a specific type of property market and it is worth understanding clearly rather than assuming it mirrors the rest of the year.

Listing volumes are low. Most sellers who wanted to be on market in 2032 are already listed or have already transacted. The pool of properties available to buyers has thinned considerably from the October peak. That is one part of the picture.

The other part is who is still buying. The buyers active in December have a clear reason to be there. They are not browsing on a Saturday morning out of general curiosity. Downsizers who have made their decision and need to act before Christmas. Professionals relocating to Brisbane for a January start date who cannot wait until February. Buyers who inspected properties through spring, missed out two or three times, and are determined not to carry that into the new year. These are people who are ready to make a decision when the right property and the right price are in front of them.

What that creates, in practice, is a market where transactions tend to be cleaner. Fewer low-ball offers from buyers who are not serious. Less time spent on drawn-out negotiations with people who were never committed. When a December buyer makes an offer, they have usually thought it through.

For a seller with a well-presented property and honest pricing, December is not a penalty box. The inquiry is lower in volume. The buyers who show up are self-selected for seriousness, and that changes the texture of the campaign in a way that is worth appreciating.
