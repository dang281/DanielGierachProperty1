## Caption

Summer open homes in Brisbane's inner east run differently from spring ones, and not always in the way vendors expect.

The number of buyers walking through is lower. But the nature of the buyers who do attend often changes in a useful way. Summer browsers, the people who go to opens out of mild curiosity with no firm timeline, have largely stepped away from the market until February. The people attending opens in January are usually there because they have a specific reason to be there.

For a vendor with a property that has been on the market since October, a continued summer open home schedule can still produce the right buyer. The decision to take the property off the market and re-list in February should be based on a genuine assessment of the property's positioning, not the reduced attendance numbers of December and January.

For properties entering the market in January, the key is honest pricing relative to what the spring market delivered. The summer buyer knows that the spring competition has faded. If a vendor carries over a price that was appropriate for a multiple-bidder October auction but is not supported by the current buyer pool, the summer market will make that clear quickly.

The inner east in January also benefits from one specific buyer profile: the interstate relocator who has committed to a move and needs to be settled before February or March. This buyer is motivated, prepared, and often willing to move quickly. A property that fits their brief exactly can achieve a strong result in January in a way that many vendors who have pulled their property for the summer never get to experience.
