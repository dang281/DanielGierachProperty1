## Caption

In Queensland, when a contract becomes unconditional, both parties are legally committed to proceed to settlement. There is no exit without financial consequences.

Most buyers know that "unconditional" sounds serious. But what it actually means in practice, and how it differs from simply having a signed contract with conditions attached, is something that does not always get explained clearly before the contract is signed.

Getting this wrong costs money. Sometimes a lot of it.

Which of these best describes what "unconditional" means in a property contract?
