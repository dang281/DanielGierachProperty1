## Caption

Mt Gravatt's role as a retail and services hub for Brisbane's south-east gives it an economic weight that most suburban postcodes do not have. Garden City draws shoppers and employment from a wide catchment, and the flow-on effect for residential property in the immediate area is real: convenience, local employment and amenity all contribute to a suburb that is more self-sufficient than many at a similar distance from the CBD.

Griffith University's presence adds a dimension that benefits both the rental market and the broader suburban economy. Academic and professional staff, as well as students requiring off-campus housing, contribute to a rental demand base that investors find useful when assessing long-term viability. Owner-occupiers benefit from the employment nearby as well, particularly those working in the health, education or research sectors.

The housing mix in Mt Gravatt includes post-war homes, newer detached houses and a growing townhouse and medium-density segment that has developed as the suburb has become more attractive to a broader range of buyers. Downsizers feature prominently in the owner-occupier market, often transitioning from larger family homes in the surrounding area while staying close to the services and social networks they value. Current medians reflect a stable market with broad buyer appeal.

Bus connections to the CBD are the primary public transport option, supplemented by strong road access.
