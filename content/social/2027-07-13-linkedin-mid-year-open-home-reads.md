## Caption

The quality of the buyer at an inner east open home tells you more about where the market is than the attendance number does.

A well-attended open home in July with fifty people through the door is not necessarily a good signal if the majority are early-stage browsers who have not yet spoken to a lender. A quiet Saturday with eight serious, pre-approved buyers who have already seen the comparable sales is a better position for the vendor than it might appear.

What I am seeing in the inner east right now is a buyer pool that is self-selecting. The people attending open homes in July are not casual. They are typically three to six months into their search, they have been finance-ready for at least that long, and they have a clear view of what they will pay for the right property. They are not going to be talked into a property that does not fit their criteria, but when the right one comes up, they act.

The properties attracting genuine competitive interest share something in common: they are priced honestly and presented without apology. The vendor has done the preparation work, the pricing is grounded in what comparable properties have actually sold for, and the marketing does not oversell what the property is.

If you are thinking about selling in the second half of 2027, the buyer pool is there. The question is whether your property gives them a reason to act.
