## Caption

If you are thinking about selling your home and the question is whether to go to market now or wait until autumn, here is the honest framework.

Listing in October means your campaign runs through late October and November. Settlement lands in late November or December. If you are downsizing, this gives you time to buy or rent over summer at your own pace. If you are upsizing, it means you need to have your next property sorted before Christmas or negotiate a longer settlement.

Listing in early November means your campaign runs through most of November. Settlement in mid-December or early January. Buyer numbers at open homes start to thin in the last week of November, which can mean fewer competing buyers and slightly longer campaign times. It still works, but you are operating with less runway.

Listing in late November is a different calculation entirely. You are effectively marketing to a reduced buyer pool heading into Christmas, with settlement likely in January or February. That is not necessarily bad: some buyers are highly motivated in late November specifically because competition is lower. But it changes the dynamic.

Most vendors who tell me they want to list in spring actually mean they want to be under contract by the end of November. That window is shorter than people expect. The preparation phase alone takes four to six weeks.

If you are still in the research phase, now is the right time to have that conversation.
