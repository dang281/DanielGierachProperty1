## Caption

Selling season debate is one of the most persistent conversations in Brisbane property.

Autumn (February to May) is Brisbane's most reliable window in the inner east. Buyer motivation is high, the weather is comfortable for inspections, and properties present well without the summer heat or the winter grey. Competition from other sellers exists but is manageable.

Spring (September to November) brings more buyer activity but also more listings. In a precinct like the inner east, where stock is always relatively tight, spring can still produce strong results. But you're competing with more sellers simultaneously.

Winter is underrated by many sellers but can suit the right property. There are fewer listings, and the buyers who are inspecting in June or July tend to be serious.

The honest answer: timing matters less than preparation and pricing. But if you're asking which window I'd choose for a well-presented inner east family home, the answer is autumn.

If you were selling your home in Brisbane's inner east, which season would you choose?
