## Caption

Seven Hills is one of those suburbs where the land content tells most of the story. Buyers who are comparing it with Coorparoo at a similar price point often find they are getting noticeably more land here, and that difference matters when you are planning for a family or thinking long term about what the site is worth.

The elevation is another factor that separates Seven Hills from nearby options. Views from the higher streets are a genuine draw, and homes that capture city or district outlooks attract a specific type of buyer who has already ruled out the flatter alternatives. Seven Hills State School adds a public schooling option that families in the catchment value, and the suburb has a character home stock that has not been as heavily turned over as some surrounding areas.

Spring suits Seven Hills well. The leafy character of the streets improves in October and the views read better on photography than they do in the grey months. Buyers who have been circling Coorparoo and Camp Hill without finding the right block often end up here when they start looking at land content more carefully.

If you are thinking about selling in Seven Hills, the combination of land, elevation, and relative value compared to neighbouring suburbs is worth understanding before you set your price expectations.
