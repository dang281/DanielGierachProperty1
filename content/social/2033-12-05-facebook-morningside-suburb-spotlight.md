## Caption

Morningside is a suburb with two distinct characters, and understanding which side of the Wynnum Road divide a property sits on is one of the first things informed buyers consider. North of Wynnum Road, the streets toward Colmslie Beach Reserve and the river attract a buyer profile that is chasing the lifestyle access that comes with that northern fringe location. South of Wynnum Road, the character is more residential, the price points are different, and the buyer profile shifts accordingly.

Colmslie Beach Reserve on the northern edge gives Morningside a river and parkland amenity that most buyers at this price range would not expect. For families who value outdoor space and proximity to the water, it is a genuine point of difference and one that I find consistently noted by buyers attending open homes in the northern streets.

Morningside State School anchors family demand within the suburb's catchment boundary, and Cannon Hill Anglican College is close enough to be relevant for families considering independent school options as well. Both are consistent conversation topics at open homes.

The buyer profile I see most often in Morningside has typically come from Camp Hill, Coorparoo, or Carina. They have reached their ceiling in those suburbs and are looking for the next step in their property trajectory. They know Morningside well, they have usually been looking for several months, and when the right property presents itself they are ready to act.

If you own in Morningside and want a current market appraisal, I am happy to talk.
