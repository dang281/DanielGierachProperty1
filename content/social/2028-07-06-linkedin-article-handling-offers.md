## Caption

Most sellers receive their first offer and immediately want to counter at the full asking price. That instinct, while understandable, often costs more than it gains.

An offer is not a final position. It is the opening of a negotiation. How you respond in the first 24 hours sets the tone for everything that follows.

Two things are worth understanding before you counter. First: price is only one variable. An unconditional offer at $20,000 below asking eliminates two to three weeks of finance and building-and-pest risk. A conditional offer $20,000 above asking can still fall over if the buyer cannot get finance approved in time. Second: rejecting an offer outright closes the conversation. A counter, even a modest one, keeps the buyer at the table.

In the inner east, buyers typically open 8 to 10 per cent below the asking price. That is not disrespect. It is a negotiating position. The sellers who handle this well are the ones who go into it knowing their number before any offer arrives, rather than working out their position under pressure after one lands.

The full breakdown of how to think through an offer, including what to do when you have multiple offers at once, is at the link below.
