## Caption

The Form 6 is the agency appointment agreement that a seller signs when they engage a real estate agent in Queensland. Most sellers sign it without reading it carefully.

What it contains: the commission rate, the marketing costs the seller is agreeing to pay, the appointment period (how long the agent has exclusive rights to sell the property), and any rebates the agent receives from third-party vendors including photographers, copywriters, and advertising portals.

The rebate clause is the part most sellers do not notice. Queensland law requires that any rebate or financial benefit the agent receives from a third-party supplier connected to your sale must be disclosed in the Form 6. If it is not disclosed, the agent is not entitled to receive it.

What sellers often do not know: there is a 5-business-day cooling-off period after signing a Form 6 under the Property Occupations Act 2014 (Qld). This is a statutory right. If you sign and change your mind within five business days, you can exit the appointment without penalty.

This does not mean every Form 6 is problematic. Most agents operate straightforwardly. But understanding what you are signing before you sign it is the baseline.
