## Caption

One of the most commonly misunderstood parts of a Queensland residential property contract is the cooling-off period, and what sellers can and cannot do about it.

Sellers cannot waive it.

The five-business-day cooling-off period after contract signing is a statutory buyer right under Queensland law. The seller has no mechanism to remove or shorten it regardless of how motivated the buyer appears or how clean the contract looks. Sellers who accept an offer and assume settlement is a formality often get caught by this.

What sellers can control is the other risk points: the finance condition window, the building and pest condition window, and whether the contract inclusions are documented clearly enough that there is no dispute at pre-settlement inspection.

The one situation where there is no cooling-off period is an auction contract. A buyer who signs a contract at auction, or immediately after auction, is bound from signature. Auction results carry no cooling-off risk for the seller.

For buyers, the cooling-off exit costs 0.25% of the purchase price, not zero. Walking away from a $1.2 million contract within the cooling-off period costs the buyer $3,000. It is not a free exit.
