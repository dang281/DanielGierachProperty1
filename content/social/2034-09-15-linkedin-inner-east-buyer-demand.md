## Caption

Not all inner east suburbs are operating in the same buyer pool this spring. A few observations from what I am seeing at opens and from conversations with buyers:

The school catchment suburbs continue to attract the most motivated buyers. Camp Hill, Carina, and Norman Park draw buyers with a specific school on their list, and those buyers have a deadline that does not shift with the season. When a property hits the market in their target catchment at the right price, they move quickly.

The riverside character suburbs, Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Balmoral, are attracting a buyer profile that skews toward owner-occupiers upgrading within the inner east. These buyers typically have equity from a previous inner east sale, are less rate-sensitive than first-home buyers, and are buying a lifestyle as much as a property. Spring is their preferred season.

The ridge suburbs, Carina Heights, Mt Gravatt East, and Carindale, have seen strong interest from buyers moving out of smaller properties and looking for more space without leaving the inner east. Land size matters more in these suburbs, and properties with usable rear yards are showing higher enquiry rates than compact blocks.

The buyers who are missing right now: first-home buyers in the $700K to $900K range are fewer than a year ago. Affordability compression in the inner east has pushed more of this group toward outer suburbs.
