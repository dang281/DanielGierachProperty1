## Caption

Most vendors think of property styling as an in-person presentation decision. It is not, primarily. Property styling is a photography investment, and understanding that changes how you think about whether it is worth the cost.

The sequence is direct: better photographs drive higher enquiry volume online. More enquiry creates more competition at the open home. More competition moves the price. The photographs are the first contact most buyers have with your property, and on most listing platforms, the difference between a scroll-past and a click-through comes down entirely to the quality of those images. The in-person styling experience matters, but the photographs are what get buyers through the door in the first place.

The return on styling is not guaranteed and it is not uniform across all properties. A prestige home on a premium street, where every room has a story to tell, benefits enormously from professional styling because there is material to work with. A modest investment property where the primary buyer is comparing rental yield does not need the same investment.

The middle ground, the family home or townhouse where the target buyer is a purchaser-occupier, is where styling has the most consistent return. These buyers are making an emotional decision alongside a financial one, and a property that photographs beautifully and presents well at the open home gives them permission to pay at the upper end of their capacity.

The article below covers how to assess whether styling is worth the investment for your specific property, and what to look for when selecting a property stylist.
