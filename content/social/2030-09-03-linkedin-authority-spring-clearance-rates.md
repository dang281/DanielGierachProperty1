## Caption

A clearance rate is not a number. It is a ratio. And like any ratio, the denominator matters as much as the numerator.

When clearance rates are reported in the mid-60s during winter, it often reflects a thin auction schedule: 40 or 50 properties going to market across a city in a single weekend. When the same rate appears in September with 200 auctions scheduled, the market is saying something meaningfully different.

Volume is the context that most reports leave out.

If clearance rates are tracking above 65% as we enter October, and auction volumes are rising week on week, that combination tells vendors something specific: buyers are not just bidding, they are competing. The pool of active buyers is large enough to absorb higher listing numbers without clearance rates softening. That is a fundamentally stronger signal than a high clearance rate on a quiet winter program.

For inner east vendors deciding whether to launch now or wait for October, this distinction matters. A September campaign into a strong, high-volume clearance environment typically produces competition at auction. A campaign that waits and enters a market where volumes have peaked but clearance rates have begun to slip faces a different dynamic entirely.

Read clearance data with context. Ask what the volume is. Ask whether it is rising or falling. Ask how the inner east compares to the broader Brisbane number. The single headline figure rarely tells you what you need to know before making a $1 million plus decision.
