## Caption

Late December and early January is not a time for launching a new property campaign. It is something else, and it is worth understanding what that something else actually is.

The visible market in this period is thin. There are not many new listings. Open home numbers are low. The portals look quiet. But below the surface, this period has its own activity that is often more useful for buyers than any other time of year.

Price adjustments happen quietly in this window. Vendors who did not transact during November or December, who are carrying a listing into the new year, often use the Christmas break to have a serious conversation with their agent about where the price needs to be. Those properties come back to market in January at a more realistic position than they left.

Off-market activity picks up. Buyers who are serious and well-financed contact agents directly during this period, knowing that the usual open home route is not running. Agents with strong vendor relationships can facilitate conversations that do not make it onto the portals at all.

For buyers with a clear idea of what they want and the ability to move quickly, late December and early January is a period worth being active. Competition from other buyers is minimal. Vendors who are still on market are more motivated than they were in October.

For vendors who have a genuine reason to sell quickly, the same logic applies. This period is not a dead zone. It is just a different kind of market.
