## Caption

Most of the advice about the best time to sell property is built around Melbourne and Sydney data. In those markets, spring is demonstrably the strongest selling season. The data from Brisbane's inner east tells a more nuanced story.

Autumn, specifically late February through May, is consistently the strongest window for Brisbane's inner east. The buyer pool that arrives after the summer break is motivated, the listing supply has not yet peaked, and the conditions that drive competition, active buyers and limited quality stock, are aligned.

Spring in Brisbane produces buyer volume but it also produces listing volume. The buyer-to-listing ratio does not consistently favour vendors the way the conventional spring wisdom suggests. And the inner east market, with its concentration of quality property and committed local buyers, does not follow the seasonal pattern of the broader Brisbane or national market.

The variable that matters more than season in the inner east is preparation. A well-prepared property launched in any month of the year outperforms an unprepared one launched in the supposed peak season. The difference between a vendor who spent four weeks preparing and one who listed the weekend after they decided to sell is regularly more than any seasonal advantage.
