## Caption

East Brisbane's spring market attracts buyers who want inner city proximity and are willing to pay for it. The suburb sits within 3 kilometres of the CBD and is bordered by the river on two sides, a geographical position that is not replicable elsewhere in the inner east. Buyers who arrive at East Brisbane open homes in September have typically already worked through the inner city suburb options and reached East Brisbane as their answer to the city proximity question.

The Mowbray Park and Norman Creek parkland connections give East Brisbane a recreational amenity that its density might not suggest. Properties within walking distance of the riverfront paths produce a lifestyle case that buyers from more distant suburbs often find genuinely compelling: inner city distance with river access at the end of the street.

The café and retail strip along Wellington Road and the surrounding streets gives East Brisbane residents a local amenity base that reduces the need to drive to larger precincts for daily requirements. That walkability within the suburb complements the city access and adds to the case that buyers make for East Brisbane when explaining their purchase to others.

Spring is when East Brisbane produces its most active buyer competition of the year. Correctly priced, well-presented properties consistently achieve strong outcomes in September and October.
