## Caption

Two rate cuts in two months.

The RBA has now brought the cash rate to 4.10%, and the big four banks have passed both cuts through to variable mortgage holders. Borrowing capacity on a $700,000 loan has increased by roughly $30,000 to $40,000 compared with six months ago.

That is not a small number. It is enough to push a buyer from a townhouse in Carina into a house in Camp Hill, or from the fringe of Morningside into the streets closer to the river.

What I am seeing at open homes right now: more traffic, more pre-approved buyers following through, and multiple-offer situations returning on properties priced correctly.

Sellers who have been waiting for conditions to improve are running out of reasons to wait. Demand is real, stock is still tight, and the buyers who sat on the sideline for the past 12 months are now moving.

One caution: rate cuts do not fix a property that is overpriced or poorly presented. Motivated buyers in this market are more discerning, not less.

If you are thinking about selling this year and want to know what your property is worth right now, I am happy to walk through it.
