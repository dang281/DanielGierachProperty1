## Caption

The streets of Hawthorne that run down toward the river end at Lores Bonney Riverside Park, and that conclusion to the streetscape is one of the features long-term residents identify when they describe what they would miss about living here. The park is accessible, flat, and used throughout the year. In the cooler months of May and June, morning walks along the river become a routine rather than an aspiration, and that kind of daily-use amenity is harder to put a price on than a school catchment but equally real.

Hawthorne's position as a middle ground between Balmoral's old-money reserve and Morningside's more accessible entry point gives it a breadth of buyer enquiry that benefits vendors. The suburb is not priced out of reach for buyers who are stretching, and it is not considered a consolation prize for buyers who could not afford Balmoral. It occupies its own distinct position in the market, and buyers who are specifically targeting Hawthorne are not typically the buyers who failed to get into another suburb.

The mix of character homes and newer builds in Hawthorne means that vendors with well-renovated character properties are competing on quality rather than on location alone. A Queenslander on a good lot in a Hawthorne street that has river glimpses from the upper level is a compelling asset in any season.

Mid-May tends to mark the point where winter buyer behaviour becomes established. The buyers who remain active in May tend to be motivated and ready to transact.
