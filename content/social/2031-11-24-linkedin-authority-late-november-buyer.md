## Caption

The buyer attending open homes in late November is a materially different person to the buyer who was attending open homes in September.

In September, the buyer pool is wide. It includes people who have just started looking, people who are not sure if they want to buy or rent, people who are deciding between the inner east and other suburbs, and people attending because it is something to do on a Sunday. Genuine buyers exist in that pool but they are mixed in with a lot of noise.

By late November, the noise has gone. The casual inspectors dropped out weeks ago. What remains is a concentrated group of buyers who have been searching since at least June. Many of them have made offers that were not accepted. They know exactly what comparable properties have sold for. They have spoken to their broker and confirmed their capacity. They are looking for the right property and they want to move.

This buyer profile matters for vendors because it changes the campaign dynamic. You do not need to educate a late November buyer about value. They already know. What they need is a property that meets their criteria, priced where the market actually sits. When a property that fits walks through their criteria, they act quickly. They are not going to take three weeks to decide.

For the right vendor, this is the most efficient buyer pool of the year. Less volume, more intent. The conversion rate from inspection to offer for this buyer profile is high. The question is whether your property is ready to meet it.
