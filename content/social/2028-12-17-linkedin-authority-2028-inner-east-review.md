## Caption

2028 in the inner east has been a year of consolidation and selective strength, which is a different story to the broad run the market had in 2026 and 2027.

The pattern I have observed through the year: well-located properties on good land in the core inner east suburbs have continued to find competitive buyer pools and solid results. Properties that required significant work, carried presentation issues, or were priced above their comparable evidence have sat, sometimes for months, before either selling at a corrected price or being withdrawn.

The two-speed nature of this is important to understand. When someone says the Brisbane market is strong, they are usually talking about one end of it. When someone says it is soft, they are usually talking about the other. Both are true simultaneously. The properties doing well are doing well for specific reasons: location, land size, renovation quality, school catchment.

The buyer pool has been consistent in one way: buyers are more informed than they were three years ago. They have done the comparable sales research. They know what things have sold for. They are not easily moved by a high asking price if the evidence does not support it.

What I am watching heading into 2029: the Olympic infrastructure timeline will become more visible in the new year. Properties in the suburbs closest to the announced venue precincts and transport upgrades are already attracting a specific buyer who is thinking beyond the immediate sale. That is a buyer worth understanding.
