## Caption

Oxford Street is the reference point that most buyers use when they start researching Bulimba. The dining, cafe, boutique retail, and weekend-lifestyle quality of that strip is genuinely one of Brisbane's best suburban precincts, and it is the reason many buyers put Bulimba at the top of their list before they have looked at a single listing.

The Bulimba Riverside reserve and the City Cat ferry terminal add a water access and commuting option that very few Brisbane suburbs can match. Being able to commute to the CBD by ferry without a transfer, or to walk to the river for weekends, is the kind of lifestyle combination that buyers are willing to pay a premium to access.

Bulimba State School is one of the most sought-after primary school catchments in the inner east. For families, the combination of that catchment and the Oxford Street lifestyle makes Bulimba the first choice rather than a fallback. These buyers have usually been looking with intent for several months, understand the comparable sales, and are prepared to move when the right property comes to market.

The trade-off in Bulimba is land size. Buyers who accept that trade have prioritised location and lifestyle access above space. The consistent demand from buyers who have made exactly that assessment keeps competition strong across most market conditions.
