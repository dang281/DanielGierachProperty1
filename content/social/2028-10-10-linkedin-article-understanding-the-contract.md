## Caption

Most buyers in Queensland sign the contract first and call their solicitor second. That sequence is common, but it creates a risk that most buyers do not realise until they are already in the conditional period.

The finance condition deadline is a hard deadline. If you miss it, the condition lapses. That is true whether your bank has approved your loan or not. If your lender is slow and you fail to request an extension before the deadline, you can find yourself in default on a contract you thought was still running.

The same applies to the building and pest condition. The clock starts from the contract date, not from when you found a suitable inspector. Booking an inspection three days after signing and finding your preferred inspector is unavailable until after the deadline is a problem that is entirely avoidable.

Inclusions and chattels need to be listed in the contract before signing. Light fittings are fixtures and stay unless specifically excluded. If the sellers plan to take the outdoor blinds, the garden shed, or the dishwasher, that needs to be in the contract.

Reviewing the contract before signing takes about twenty minutes with a solicitor. Finding a problem two weeks into the conditional period costs significantly more to navigate.

The full article explains each condition, what the deadlines mean, and what to check before you put pen to paper.
