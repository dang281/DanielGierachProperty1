## Caption

The Cross River Rail changed travel times for a number of Brisbane suburbs significantly. New stations and upgraded corridors affect commute times, and commute times affect property demand.

In the inner east, suburbs within walking distance of rail access consistently attract a buyer premium. Not because buyers are explicitly paying for the train. Because proximity to a train station expands the pool of buyers who want to live there, and a larger buyer pool supports values.

The same principle will apply to Olympic infrastructure investment in the lead-up to 2032. Venues, transport upgrades, and urban improvements that concentrate in specific corridors will affect demand in those areas over time.

Whether or not buyers consciously factor this into a purchase decision, the market prices it in.

When choosing where to buy, how much does access to public transport influence your decision?
