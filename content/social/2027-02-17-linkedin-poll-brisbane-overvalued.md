## Caption

Brisbane's median house price has grown significantly over the past four years. Affordability relative to incomes is stretched compared to historical norms. At the same time, the fundamentals that underpin demand remain intact: population growth, infrastructure investment, relative affordability compared to Sydney and Melbourne, and an Olympic Games in 2032 that is drawing long-term investor attention.

The overvaluation debate is active. Some economists argue prices have run ahead of fundamentals and that a correction is overdue. Others point to the structural supply shortage and sustained migration as reasons the market will hold.

This is not a question with a clean answer. It depends on the suburb, the price point, and your time horizon.

Do you think Brisbane residential property is currently overvalued?
