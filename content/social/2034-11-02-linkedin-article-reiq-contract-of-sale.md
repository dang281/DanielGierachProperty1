## Caption

The inclusions schedule is the section of the REIQ contract that causes the most disputes after settlement. Most buyers and sellers do not read it carefully enough.

Fixtures are assumed to be included unless specifically excluded. Chattels are assumed to be excluded unless specifically included. The line between them is frequently misunderstood.

The finance condition has a specific deadline date. If you have not received formal approval and the deadline passes, your contract may lapse. Do not assume your bank will always meet the date.

A solicitor or conveyancer review before signing should take thirty to forty-five minutes. That investment routinely prevents disputes that cost thousands to resolve after settlement.
