## Caption

Every October, a portion of vendors in Brisbane's inner east decide to wait. They think: the market will still be there in February, and we will have more time to prepare. What they do not think about is that every other vendor who feels the same way will be launching at the same time.

The February market is contested on both sides. Buyers who missed out before Christmas are back. But so are all the vendors who sat out the spring window. Listings that would have faced very little competition in October are now competing with everything else that came to market in late January and February. The dynamic shifts, and the urgency that characterised the October buyer pool is gone.

What I observe with vendors who list in October is that they are often surprised by how quickly the serious buyers appear. October open homes attract people who have been looking since August, who have finance ready, and who are working to a settlement timeline. There are fewer properties competing for that buyer's attention. The result is that October campaigns, when well prepared and priced correctly, consistently perform at or above February results, even though the common assumption is that spring is always stronger.

The window to list, campaign, and settle before Christmas closes in mid-November. After that, the pre-Christmas settlement argument disappears and the February supply dynamic becomes the reality. Vendors who understand this act in October. The ones who do not find out in February.
