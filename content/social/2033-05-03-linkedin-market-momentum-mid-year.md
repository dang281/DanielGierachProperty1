## Caption

May is the month where real market momentum separates from seasonal noise. The buyers who are still active at this point in the year are not casual. They have been looking since at least February. They have missed out on properties. They know what a good price for a good home looks like, and they are ready to pay it when the property earns it.

In the inner east this year, the buyer pools for well-presented houses in Hawthorne, Bulimba, and Morningside are still strong through May. The enquiry is qualified. The open home attendance is not the numbers you see in spring, but the ratio of genuine buyers to browsers is higher. That shifts the negotiation dynamic in a vendor's favour when the property is right.

The risk for May vendors is a different one. Coming to market with stale or weak photography, with a property that hasn't been presented to its ceiling, or with a price guide that isn't anchored to recent comparable sales. May buyers in the inner east have seen enough by now to know the difference. They will not chase a property that is asking them to do the vendor's job for them.

What is working right now: clean, styled, accurately priced houses with good natural light and functional family layouts. What is not working: renovations that were started and not finished, properties with unresolved compliance issues, and anything priced on peak 2032 numbers rather than current sales evidence.

Momentum is real. But it is earned by the property, not just assumed from the market.
