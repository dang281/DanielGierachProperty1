## Caption

Sellers will often accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one. That sounds counterintuitive until you understand what a conditional offer actually costs.

A standard Queensland contract with a finance condition and a building and pest condition creates a two to three week window where the deal can fall apart. The finance condition lapses if the buyer's lender cannot approve in time. The building and pest condition gives the buyer the right to withdraw if the inspector finds material issues. During that window, the property is effectively off the market. Other buyers move on. If the deal falls apart at day fourteen, the seller goes back to the beginning.

An unconditional offer eliminates that window entirely. The contract is binding from signing. The buyer has done their financing and inspections in advance, or they are willing to accept the risk of not doing so. Either way, the seller knows the deal is done.

The practical implication for buyers who want to compete strongly: finance pre-approval and an independent building inspection before you make an offer turns you from a conditional buyer into someone a seller can choose confidently, even at a price slightly below the highest conditional offer in the room.

The full article explains how unconditional offers work in Queensland, what due diligence buyers should complete beforehand, and when the strategy makes sense.
