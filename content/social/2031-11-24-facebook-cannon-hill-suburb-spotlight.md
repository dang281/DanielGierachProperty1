## Caption

Cannon Hill Rowing Club gives this suburb something genuinely unusual for the inner east: organised river access that residents and families can actually use. The river frontage doesn't translate directly into property prices for most streets in the suburb, but the lifestyle connection to the water matters to a certain buyer, and they actively seek it out.

The Cannon Hill Anglican College draw is consistent and well understood. Families relocating to Brisbane from interstate often have the college on their list before they've even started looking at suburbs. That means the buyer pool here includes a higher proportion of motivated, research-led buyers who've already made decisions before they walk in the door.

November brings the pre-Christmas window, and Cannon Hill typically sees good buyer activity at this time. Families who want to be settled in a new home before the school year restarts are often making their final decisions in November and early December. That creates a window where vendor timing and buyer urgency overlap well.

Character homes in good condition on the streets closest to the Anglican College and in the area around the shopping centre move with relatively short days on market. Properties that require significant renovation or sit on main road exposure take longer and tend to attract a different buyer profile at a different price.
