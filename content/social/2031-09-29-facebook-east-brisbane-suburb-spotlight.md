## Caption

East Brisbane closes Q3 as one of the inner east's most active markets. The suburb's proximity to the CBD and South Bank has kept buyer attention high throughout spring, and the mix of owner-occupier and investor demand has produced a deeper buyer field than most comparable suburbs can generate. That depth shows in results.

The spring season in East Brisbane is particularly meaningful for character home owners. Queenslanders and post-war homes in the suburb photograph beautifully against the warmer September light, and the Gabba precinct framing the suburb's western edge adds an urban energy that resonates with the buyer demographic who chooses inner-city addresses over outer ring convenience. That buyer values proximity above almost everything else, and East Brisbane delivers it in a way that few addresses can.

The flood-aware buyer has done their research by the time they arrive at a spring inspection in East Brisbane. They know which properties sit above the relevant flood levels, and they have factored that into their budget. For owners of flood-free properties, spring is the season where that research-backed confidence produces the most competitive buyer fields of the year.

As Q3 closes and Q4 opens, the East Brisbane market shows no signs of the fatigue that affects some outer suburbs late in the spring season. Demand from CBD workers, investors, and lifestyle buyers remains layered and active.
