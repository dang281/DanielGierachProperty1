## Caption

Spring in the inner east is at its peak right now, and the energy on the ground reflects it.

Character homes with decent land along the Bulimba to Balmoral corridor are drawing genuine competition. When a property in that stretch presents well, is priced honestly, and has the lot size buyers want, it is not sitting long. The buyers who missed out through the quieter winter period are still active, and in many cases they are moving faster and with more conviction than they were six months ago.

The school catchment properties in Camp Hill and Coorparoo are a separate conversation. Families who want to be enrolled for the 2033 school year are already thinking about where they need to be by February. That urgency is real, and it is showing up in the way buyers engage at opens and in how quickly they follow up.

What is sitting? Properties where the condition does not match the price expectation, and older renovations that look tired against what buyers can now do themselves for a known cost. Buyers are more discerning about presentation than they were two years ago.

The pre-Christmas dynamic is the other factor shaping November. Buyers who want settlement before Christmas need contracts executed this month. A standard 30-day settlement from the third week of November gets you to mid-December. That window is closing. The buyers who are serious about a year-end settlement are making decisions now, not in a fortnight.

If you are watching the market or thinking about timing a campaign, this is what November looks like from where I sit.
