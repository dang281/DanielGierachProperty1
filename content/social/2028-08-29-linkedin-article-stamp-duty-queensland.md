## Caption

On a $1 million owner-occupier purchase in Queensland, transfer duty adds approximately $38,025. Most buyers underestimate that number by $10,000 or more when they are calculating their deposit and borrowing requirement.

The underestimate matters because it affects the loan-to-value ratio calculation. If the stamp duty comes out of savings rather than being borrowed, a buyer who planned on a 20 per cent deposit may find themselves at 18 per cent after the duty is paid, which pushes them into lenders mortgage insurance territory. That is an outcome that a straightforward calculation before the offer stage would have prevented.

The tiered rate structure is the source of most confusion. Transfer duty in Queensland is not a flat percentage applied to the purchase price. It is calculated on tiered bands, and the effective rate increases as the purchase price rises. The first home buyer concession reduces the liability significantly on purchases up to $700,000, but above that threshold the concession tapers and then disappears.

The practical step is to use the Queensland Revenue Office calculator on qro.qld.gov.au before you make an offer, not after. The number takes about thirty seconds to verify.

The full article walks through the current rate schedule, the first home buyer concession, and what to factor into your deposit calculation.
