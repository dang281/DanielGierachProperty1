## Caption

February in the Brisbane inner east played out largely as the early open home numbers suggested it would. Buyer attendance was solid from the first weekend, offer conversations moved quickly on well-presented properties, and the vendors who had their preparation complete before the month started were the ones with the strongest outcomes.

The properties that sat longer shared a recognisable pattern: pricing set above where the buyer pool was operating, or presentation issues that gave buyers reasons to hold back rather than commit. Neither of these is a market problem. Both are fixable at the campaign level.

The transition into March is the moment the season shifts. February gives vendors a cleaner run with less listing competition. March delivers more buyer volume but also more properties to compete against. Vendors launching in March need stronger differentiation: better presentation, sharper pricing, more considered marketing. The rising tide of buyer numbers does not automatically lift every campaign.

Vendors who launched in February and are now in the offer or under-contract phase have had a productive month. For those considering a March launch, the preparation window is now. Waiting until the first week of March to start the pre-sale work means losing the early weeks of the peak period.

March in the inner east tends to be the highest-volume month of the year. The foundations for a strong result in that environment are set now.
