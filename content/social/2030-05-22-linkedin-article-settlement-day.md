## Caption

Settlement day has a reputation for being stressful and complicated. The reality for most Queensland property transactions is that it is a quiet day, handled almost entirely digitally, and the vendor's role is to have done the preparation in the preceding weeks.

PEXA (the Property Exchange Australia platform) handles the transfer of title and funds electronically. The settlement parties confirm the transaction online, the funds move, the title transfers, and you get a message from your solicitor telling you it is done. In most cases, you are not in a room handing over keys. The buyer accesses the property, and you get your proceeds.

What vendors actually need to focus on is the period before settlement. The pre-settlement inspection, which happens in the final few days, is where problems can emerge. If the property is not in the same condition as when the contract was signed, if something has been damaged or removed, if inclusions that were listed in the contract are no longer there, that is when a settled day can turn into a disputed one.

The practical preparation: do your own walkthrough 48 to 72 hours before settlement. Confirm all inclusions are present, the property is clean, and nothing has been damaged since handover. That is the version of settlement day preparation that actually matters.
