## Caption

Cannon Hill sits at the meeting point of several key corridors in the inner east: easy access to Murarrie and the Gateway, proximity to the Cannon Hill Anglican College precinct, and a direct train line to the CBD via the Cannon Hill station. That combination of connectivity is one of the suburb's key selling points for buyers assessing the inner east.

The housing stock in Cannon Hill is predominantly postwar character homes, many on blocks of 600 square metres or more. The streets on the higher ground east of the railway line deliver better outlooks and the kind of natural light that makes a difference at open homes. Buyers who have done their research know which side of the hill they want to be on.

Cannon Hill Anglican College is the school that generates the most buyer conversation in the suburb. As a private school rather than a catchment-based state school, it does not affect pricing in the same way as state school zones, but its presence shapes the family buyer profile of the suburb.

Days on market in Cannon Hill through 2030 have been consistent with the broader inner east, sitting in the mid-to-high twenties. Properties on the elevated streets with character intact have moved more quickly than those on the lower-lying sections.

For Cannon Hill homeowners thinking about spring or early summer, the buyer pool is active now.
