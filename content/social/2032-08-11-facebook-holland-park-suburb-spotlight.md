## Caption

Holland Park West borders Annerley and Greenslopes, and buyers who come close to the Echlin Street corridor or the streets around Musgrave Park but do not find what they need often end up in the western sections of the suburb. That is not a fallback position. It is frequently where the best value in the suburb sits.

The Holland Park Bowls Club on Cavendish Road anchors one of the suburb's more established social and community nodes, and the Cavendish Road corridor itself carries good access north toward Greenslopes and the inner south. For buyers who work on the south side or need both city and southern corridor access, Holland Park's positioning off that corridor is genuinely convenient.

Holland Park State High School is one of the larger secondary schools serving the broader area and is a consistent catchment consideration for families buying in the suburb with secondary-age children.

Renovation potential is one of the suburb's more dependable stories. Post-war homes on blocks that regularly exceed 600 square metres give buyers who want to build equity through work rather than purchase price a reliable starting point. The value is in the land and the structure. The upside is in what you do with them.

For owners in Holland Park who have been sitting on a larger block and watching the surrounding suburbs appreciate, the time to understand your position is before others tell you what it is.
