## Caption

Norman Park State School is one of the most sought-after primary catchments in the inner east, and that single fact drives more buying decisions in this suburb than almost anything else. Families plan years ahead to get into this catchment. When a home inside it comes to market, the competition is real.

Whites Hill Reserve borders the suburb and it defines the lifestyle here in a way that no shopping precinct can. Walking tracks, open space, and the feeling of genuine bushland within minutes of the CBD. Streets backing onto or near the reserve consistently achieve premiums. The single bus route into the CBD is a practical bonus that buyers with city jobs mention frequently.

The buyer profile in Norman Park skews strongly toward families, both those upgrading from smaller homes and those arriving from outer suburbs with equity to spend. Character homes in original condition or sympathetically renovated hold the most heat. Modern infill townhouses exist in pockets but they are not what this market is built on.

The supply here is naturally constrained because turnover among long-term owner-occupiers is low. When a good home comes up on streets like Maryvale Street or Norman Avenue, it tends not to sit.
