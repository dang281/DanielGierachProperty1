## Caption

Norman Park is the suburb that buyers tend to identify after a thorough search of the inner east, not at the beginning of one.

The buyer who purchases in Norman Park has usually been looking for six to twelve months. They have worked through Coorparoo, Camp Hill, and sometimes Morningside. They have a brief that includes elevation, character homes, land, and a school catchment. Norman Park is the suburb that, for a specific buyer type, delivers all of those things together in a way that no single alternative entirely matches.

Whites Hill Reserve gives the suburb a green space amenity that is genuinely large and genuinely accessible from the residential streets. This is not a small park at the end of a street. It is a significant bushland reserve that defines the suburb's eastern character and gives residents a place to be outside that most inner-east suburbs cannot offer.

Norman Park State School and East Brisbane State School both draw buyer demand in the suburb's western and eastern sections respectively. The dual school catchment influence means that the family buyer segment here is broader than in suburbs anchored by a single school.

September and October are when Norman Park's spring listing pool expands. The buyer who has been researching for months and identified Norman Park as the answer to their search is active right now. They are writing offers and ready to move when the right property appears.

If you own in Norman Park and have been thinking about timing, this is the window.
