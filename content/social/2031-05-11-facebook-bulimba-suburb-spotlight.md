## Caption

The detached housing supply in Bulimba is tight in a way that is worth articulating clearly for vendors who are weighing up their timing. The suburb is bounded. It cannot grow outward, the character stock cannot be replaced like-for-like once it is demolished, and the number of sales in any given twelve-month period is low relative to the number of buyers who have Bulimba on their shortlist. That supply constraint is a durable feature of the suburb's market, not a temporary condition.

Upsizer demand is consistently strong in Bulimba. Families who have been living in a townhouse or a smaller home in Morningside, Cannon Hill, or Coorparoo, and who have reached the stage where they need a proper backyard, a second living space, and proximity to a good primary school, tend to arrive at Bulimba as their preferred destination. The Bulimba State School catchment resolves the school question for those families, and Oxford Street resolves the lifestyle question.

The heritage character architecture on streets like Riverview Terrace and the streets running north from the river is a feature that attracts buyers who have specifically rejected the newer suburbs of the south-east. Those buyers are not price-sensitive in the conventional sense. They are quality-sensitive, and they will pay to secure the right house in the right street.

The winter market in Bulimba does not collapse the way it does in suburbs without structural demand drivers. The catchment-and-lifestyle buyer is not seasonal.
