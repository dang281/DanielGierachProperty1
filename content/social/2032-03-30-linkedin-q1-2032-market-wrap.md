## Caption

Q1 2032 is nearly done. Here is an honest account of what I observed in the inner east over the first quarter of the Olympic year.

Buyer activity in January was slower than February, as it usually is, but it picked up faster than in Q1 2031. By the time February opened homes were running, the buyer registration numbers were already telling a clear story: the Olympic year context was accelerating decision timelines for a proportion of the market. Some of those buyers were rational about it. Some were not, but the effect on competition at open homes was real regardless of the underlying reasoning.

March was strong. The detached house market in the established inner east streets performed consistently, with well-prepared campaigns attracting multiple parties in the bidding or offer phase. The townhouse and boutique apartment market was more mixed, with results depending heavily on the specific property, the body corporate position, and the pricing strategy.

The clearance rate at auctions in the inner east suburbs I cover held above the historical average for Q1. Days on market for properties that were correctly priced from day one was shorter than the same period in 2031.

What I am not doing here is extrapolating from Q1 into predictions for the rest of the year. The second half of 2032 will be different from the first half, for obvious reasons. What Q1 tells me is that the buyer pool entered the year with genuine intent, and the results reflected that.
