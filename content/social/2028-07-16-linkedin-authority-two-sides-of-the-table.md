## Caption

I spend part of every week in conversations with sellers and a different part in conversations with buyers. The gap between what each side believes about the other is consistently larger than it should be.

Sellers believe buyers have more money than they reveal. They think the early offer is always lowball and the buyer is holding back. They expect competition to develop in the final days of a campaign and are surprised when it does not.

Buyers believe sellers are more flexible than they present. They think the asking price is a starting point and the agent is managing expectations on behalf of a vendor who will take less. They are often wrong about this on well-priced properties in the inner east.

The result is two groups talking past each other through a process that should be straightforward. The seller holds out for a number the market has already told them is above the buyer pool. The buyer holds back, waiting for the seller to blink. The property sits.

The best outcomes I see come from sellers who have a clear, evidence-based price and buyers who have done their comparable sales research. When both sides of the table are working from the same data, the gap closes quickly.

The agent's job is to close the gap faster, not to build leverage for either party.
