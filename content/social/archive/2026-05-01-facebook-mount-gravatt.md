## Caption

Mount Gravatt has had a significant 12 months in the property market, with median house prices rising approximately 20% over the past year to sit at around $1,305,000. That pace of growth is notable for a suburb of this size and profile.

Located 8km south of the Brisbane CBD, Mount Gravatt sits along the ridge that gives the suburb its elevated character. The Westfield Mount Gravatt shopping centre provides major retail amenity, and the suburb is well-served by the South-East Busway at Eight Mile Plains and the south-east freeway corridor. Griffith University's Nathan campus borders the suburb, which keeps a consistent pool of buyers and renters connected to the area.

The mix of character homes on the elevated streets and more standard residential stock across the broader suburb means pricing varies significantly by location. The upper end of the market tends to cluster around properties with outlooks or larger land sizes.
