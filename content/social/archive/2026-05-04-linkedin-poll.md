## Caption

SQM Research puts Brisbane's residential vacancy rate at 0.6 per cent as of February 2026. The national average sits at 1.1 per cent. Brisbane has been the tightest capital city rental market for most of the past two years.

A vacancy rate below 1 per cent generally signals a landlord's market: low vacancy, upward pressure on rents, and strong yield for existing investors. Brisbane gross rental yields have been climbing as a result, with houses in the inner east now averaging around 3.5 to 4 per cent and units pushing higher.

The counter-argument: the RBA cash rate is at 4.10 per cent after two consecutive hikes in early 2026. Serviceability on an investment loan at current rates is significantly tighter than it was in 2021. Negative gearing still applies, but the interest bill is real.

SQM Research expects vacancy to remain below 1.5 per cent through 2026 as population growth continues to outpace new housing supply.

With Brisbane's rental market as tight as it is, is now a good time to buy an investment property?
