## Caption

Woolloongabba is in the middle of one of the most significant infrastructure investments in Queensland's history, and the property market is already pricing it in.

The median house price in Woolloongabba is around $1,400,000, up approximately 15.7% over the past 12 months. The suburb sits 2km from the Brisbane CBD, and the Cross River Rail project has delivered a Woolloongabba station that provides direct underground connections to the CBD, Roma Street, and Fortitude Valley. The Gabba precinct is also earmarked for major redevelopment as part of the 2032 Olympic Games program, which adds a longer-term infrastructure story that buyers are factoring in now.

The suburb has a mix of character homes on the residential streets running off Logan Road and Ipswich Road, and a growing number of apartment developments closer to the Gabba precinct. Buyers who purchased before the infrastructure announcement have seen strong capital positions develop quickly.

For homeowners in Woolloongabba, the combination of transport infrastructure, Olympic precinct redevelopment, and inner-city proximity is unusual. An appraisal will give you a current read on where that story translates into value for your specific property.
