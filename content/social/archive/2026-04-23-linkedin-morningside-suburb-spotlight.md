## Caption

Morningside has a median house price of around $1.31 million, with annual house price growth of approximately 10 per cent and unit values up roughly 14 to 17 per cent over the same period, according to current suburb data.

What makes Morningside interesting from an agency perspective is the buyer profile. It draws two distinct groups: families and owner-occupiers who specifically want the flat, character-home lifestyle at a price point below Bulimba and Hawthorne; and investors attracted by unit rental yields and the suburb's gentrification trajectory. That breadth of demand is healthy for sellers because it means a well-presented property is not reliant on a single buyer type.

Days on market for houses is sitting around 22 days. That reflects a competitive market, not a slow one.

The longer-term story is also worth noting. The Cannon Hill Shopping Centre redevelopment, set to transform the precinct with new retail and dining, adds infrastructure-backed tailwinds to the eastern corridor that Morningside sits within.

For homeowners thinking about timing, the fundamentals here are solid. The question is whether your individual circumstances make now the right moment.
