## Caption

Kangaroo Point has a median unit price of around $800,000 with annual unit capital growth of approximately 14 per cent, according to current suburb data. The median house price sits around $1.27 million.

The suburb's appeal is straightforward: it is a few minutes from the CBD by bridge or ferry, has the cliffs, the river, and city views, and offers the inner-city lifestyle without being in the middle of a commercial precinct. That combination is consistent enough that demand does not significantly drop off when broader market conditions tighten.

The house market here is limited — much of Kangaroo Point's stock is apartments and units. The house buyers who do look here are typically purchasing something distinct and are less price-sensitive than the typical unit buyer. For unit sellers, the active buyer pool skews toward professionals and investors, with rental yields sitting around 3.9 per cent.

One thing worth noting for sellers: the 2032 Brisbane Olympics infrastructure pipeline has Kangaroo Point in close proximity to Woolloongabba and the wider inner-south precinct, which several analysts have identified as a longer-term growth catalyst for the riverside corridor.
