## Caption

Queensland's $30,000 first home owner grant is set to drop back to $15,000 for contracts signed after 30 June 2026. That is a $15,000 cliff edge, now less than ten weeks away.

The grant applies to new homes valued under $750,000, which in Brisbane's current market means buyers are largely looking at outer suburbs, new estates, or apartments off the plan. The Queensland Government has confirmed the extension ends on 30 June and the $15,000 base rate resumes from 1 July.

Stack on top of that the federal deposit guarantee scheme and stamp duty concessions, and the total government support available to an eligible first home buyer can run to well over $100,000 in equivalent value before 30 June, depending on what and where they buy.

But with Brisbane's median dwelling value sitting above $1 million, the question is whether government grants meaningfully move the needle for buyers trying to get into the market, or whether the bigger issue is something else entirely.

What is the biggest barrier for first home buyers in Brisbane right now?
