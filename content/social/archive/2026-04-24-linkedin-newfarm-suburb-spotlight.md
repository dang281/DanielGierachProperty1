## Caption

New Farm sits 2km from the Brisbane CBD, on the river, with New Farm Park, the Brisbane Powerhouse, and some of the city's most established cafe and restaurant culture. Unit values have recorded annual capital growth of approximately 10 per cent, and the prestige end of the house market commands prices well above the city average.

The buyer in New Farm is a specific type. Professionals and downsizers who have prioritised lifestyle proximity over land size. They are not searching broadly across inner Brisbane. They have decided on New Farm and they are waiting for the right property to come up. When it does, competition tends to be meaningful.

That concentration of motivated, lifestyle-focused buyers creates a different dynamic for sellers than in a broader market suburb. Presentation and fit-out matter more here than almost anywhere else in the inner east. The buyers know what the suburb commands and they are comparing your home to others in the same street and precinct.

In the current rate environment, those buyers are also more considered. But their fundamentals are typically strong: professional incomes, significant equity from prior property, and a long-term commitment to the location.
