## Caption

Seven Hills is one of the most tightly held addresses in Brisbane's inner east.

Around 47 houses traded in the suburb over the past 12 months. That is a fraction of the turnover you see in comparable inner-east suburbs. Buyers who want Seven Hills know there is rarely much to choose from, and that shapes how they behave when something does come up.

Low turnover is not a sign of a quiet market. It is a sign of a loyal one. When motivated buyers have limited choices, the properties that do appear get more attention, more inspections, and more competitive interest.

Then there is the elevation. The ridge streets sit above the flat of Carina and Morningside, with open views across to the city that those suburbs simply cannot replicate. That is an aspect premium that does not fully show up in aggregated suburb data. And at a price point well below Hawthorne and Bulimba, it is a position buyers in that range pay close attention to.

If you own in Seven Hills and have been thinking about what the current market looks like for your property, I am happy to walk through it with you.
