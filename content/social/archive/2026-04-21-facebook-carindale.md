## Caption

Carindale has built a strong buyer market around what families actually want from a suburb, and the recent growth figures show it.

Around 11km east of the Brisbane CBD, Carindale's median house price has reached approximately $1,580,000, up around 13.7% over the past 12 months. The suburb is anchored by Westfield Carindale, one of Queensland's largest shopping centres, which provides genuine daily convenience that many comparable suburbs can't match. Larger blocks, good freeway access, and the Gateway Motorway connection make Carindale practical for families who commute across the city or to the airport precinct.

What drives buyer interest here is the combination of space and amenity. Carindale tends to offer more land for the price compared to Camp Hill or Coorparoo, and buyers who prioritise that trade-off are finding competition is real.

For homeowners, the 13.7% growth over the past year is not a small number. If you're considering whether now is the right time to act on that equity, a conversation about current market conditions is a good place to start.
