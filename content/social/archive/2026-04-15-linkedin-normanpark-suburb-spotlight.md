## Caption

Norman Park sits 4km from Brisbane's CBD with a median house price of around $1.7 million and annual capital growth of approximately 11.5 per cent, according to current suburb data.

The demographic is heavily family-oriented. Around 70 per cent of households have children, with parents typically aged 35 to 64. The suburb catchment for Norman Park State School is tight, which creates sustained demand from a specific buyer type who specifically want into that catchment.

What that means for sellers is that demand in Norman Park is not just correlated to the broader Brisbane market. It is also driven by school intake timing, family formation, and the limited supply of elevated allotments with river and city views.

The current rate environment adds complexity to any sale decision. Buyers are more deliberate now than in 2021. But the fundamentals that drive Norman Park's demand are structural, not cyclical. Location, school catchment, and views do not change with the cash rate.
