## Caption

Teneriffe unit values have recorded annual capital growth of approximately 17 per cent, with a median unit price sitting around $1.055 million, according to current suburb data. The median house price is around $1.57 million.

The suburb is dominated by the converted woolstore buildings along the riverfront, which is what defines its character and its buyer type. The demographic is mostly professionals aged 20 to 39: childless couples, early-career high earners, and downsizers who want the urban lifestyle without a maintenance-heavy home. Days on market is around 24 days for units, which reflects active demand.

What drives Teneriffe's premium is scarcity. The woolstore stock is finite. You cannot replicate it, and it does not come to market often. When it does, buyers who have been watching the market for 12 or 18 months tend to move quickly.

The counterpoint: at the $1M-plus price point for units, serviceability at 4.10 per cent is a real constraint for buyers who are not cash-heavy. The buyers who remain active here are typically equity-rich from prior property or have high professional incomes. That is a narrower pool than three years ago, but a motivated one.

For Teneriffe owners thinking about selling, positioning and timing matter more than they do in a broad market. Happy to talk through what that looks like for your specific property.
