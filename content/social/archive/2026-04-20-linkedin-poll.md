## Caption

CoreLogic data puts Brisbane's median dwelling value at around $1.08 million at the end of February 2026, following annual growth of 17.3 per cent over the past 12 months.

That growth happened while interest rates climbed. The RBA lifted the cash rate to 4.10 per cent in March, its second consecutive hike, citing inflation picking up in the second half of 2025. Major bank economists are now split: NAB forecasts around 4.6 per cent price growth for Brisbane over the next year, while SQM Research sees 10 to 15 per cent possible under favourable conditions. KPMG is forecasting 10.9 per cent growth for houses.

The bull case: chronic supply shortage, 25,000-plus people migrating to south-east Queensland each year, and Olympic infrastructure driving long-run demand. The bear case: serviceability pressure at 4.10 per cent, affordability constraints at the $1M-plus price point, and the possibility of more rate hikes before the end of 2026.

Where do you think Brisbane prices are heading over the next 12 months?
