## Caption

Holland Park has spent the past several years quietly becoming one of Brisbane's more competitive family suburb markets, and the numbers now reflect what locals already knew.

The median house price in Holland Park is approximately $1,612,000, up around 8.5% over the past 12 months. The suburb sits 7km south of the CBD, with Logan Road providing a direct connection to the city and the South-East Freeway access point at Stones Corner close by. Holland Park State High School is a major drawcard for families, and the mix of character Queenslanders and postwar homes on established blocks gives buyers genuine choice.

What distinguishes Holland Park from some of its neighbours is consistency. It doesn't have the sharp peaks of some inner-east suburbs, but it also doesn't have the volatility. Buyers treat it as a suburb to hold, which is part of why supply stays tight.
