## Caption

Having walked through hundreds of properties alongside buyers over the years, one pattern stands out consistently: what people say they look for and what they actually respond to emotionally are often not the same thing. Buyers will tell you they want a functional floor plan and good storage. Then they walk into a room filled with natural light and their whole posture changes. Others say they focus on the kitchen, but the thing that kills the deal is a poor connection between the living area and the outdoor space. The declared priorities and the felt experience frequently diverge. I am genuinely curious about this audience. What do you notice first when you walk through a property?
