## Caption

Most buyers walk into an auction knowing their limit. Fewer have actually done the groundwork that makes that limit mean something.

Before you register to bid, five things are worth doing.

Check the flood overlay. Brisbane City Council publishes flood maps for free. A property sitting in a Q100 zone can add 40% to annual insurance premiums or make the property uninsurable entirely. It takes five minutes to check, and it changes the picture.

Read the contract before the auction, not after. The headline price gets the attention, but special conditions are where money quietly disappears. If a clause reads oddly, ask your solicitor. That question costs nothing.

Get fully pre-approved, not pre-qualified. Pre-qualification is a lender estimating what you might borrow. Pre-approval is a written commitment. Bidding on an unconditional contract with the wrong one is how deposits get lost.

Inspect twice: once on your own, once with a building inspector. The first walk-through is emotional. The second is forensic. A $600 inspection report has saved buyers in my experience from spending hundreds of thousands more than a property was worth.

Write your ceiling down before you walk in. Auction rooms are designed to push past your number. Having it on paper, not just in your head, is how you hold the line.
