## Caption

Coorparoo sits six kilometres from the CBD and has developed into one of Brisbane's most consistently active family property markets. The suburb's school catchments, particularly Coorparoo State School, are a central reason families target the area and why owner-occupier demand tends to hold firm regardless of broader market conditions. Buyers with children approaching school age often make the decision to buy in Coorparoo specifically to secure the catchment.

The café strip along Old Cleveland Road gives the suburb a genuine local identity. Independent cafes, specialty food stores and everyday retail mean residents can get through a weekend without needing to drive far, which is a quality of life detail that buyers note and value. The suburb has a lived-in, community feel that distinguishes it from more transient areas closer to the city.

Housing stock in Coorparoo ranges from original post-war brick homes and Queenslanders through to renovated character homes and infill townhouse developments. That range means there are entry points for buyers at different stages and budgets. Current medians in Coorparoo reflect the suburb's strong family demand, with well-presented homes on good blocks consistently achieving solid results.

Bus services along Old Cleveland Road connect residents to the CBD and to the broader south-east, and the suburb's central position makes most of Brisbane's key destinations accessible within a reasonable drive.
