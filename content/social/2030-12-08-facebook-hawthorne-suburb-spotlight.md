## Caption

Hawthorne's Oxford Street and the foreshore walking track are the two assets that define the suburb for buyers who are discovering it for the first time. For buyers who know it well, those things are assumed. What brings them back to the suburb specifically is the combination of character housing on full blocks and a neighbourhood scale that feels managed rather than sprawling.

The residential streets between Oxford Street and the river, in the area bounded roughly by Hawthorne Road and the waterfront, are where the suburb's top auction results consistently come from. River proximity is the primary driver, but the character of the homes on those streets, many with high ceilings, verandas, and original Queenslander detailing, is what converts interest into competition at auction.

Hawthorne State School has a strong reputation and consistent waiting lists at enrolment time, which tells its own story about the family demand in the suburb. The school's walking catchment means that residents on the streets within easy walking distance are in a position that parents value practically, not just in principle.

December in Hawthorne brings fewer open homes, but the buyers still active are serious. For vendors with a property entering the market in December, the reduced competition from other listings can work in their favour if the property is positioned correctly.
