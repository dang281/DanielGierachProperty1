## Caption

Blocks above the Norman Creek corridor in Morningside are some of the most defensible properties in the inner east. Flood risk assessment, position above the creek line, and the elevation premium combine to make the buyer calculation clear: pay more for the elevated block, or accept the tradeoff on a flat one.

That distinction matters because it explains a lot of the price spread within Morningside. The difference between a well-positioned elevated Queenslander and a flat post-war home near the creek corridor can be substantial, and buyers in this market understand why.

Morningside State School serves the local families, but the suburb draws more than just school-focused buyers. Young professionals who want inner east proximity and some character in the streetscape are increasingly prominent in the buyer pool. The cafe activity along the Wynnum Road strip brings the lifestyle credentials that this demographic expects.

The suburb also sits close enough to the city to be genuinely commutable without a car. That public transport piece matters to a growing segment of buyers who are making deliberate choices about car dependence.

In November, the spring market in Morningside runs hot. Well-positioned character homes that have been maintained well move with clear buyer competition. The properties that sit are those where position, condition, and price aren't aligning.
