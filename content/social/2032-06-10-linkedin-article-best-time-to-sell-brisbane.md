## Caption

Almost every answer to "when is the best time to sell?" is incomplete, because it leaves out the most important variable: your specific situation.

The seasonal argument for spring is real but overstated. Yes, more buyers are active in spring. More sellers are also active in spring, which means more competition for attention and, often, more comparable properties establishing the price ceiling for yours. The counter-argument for winter, that buyer-to-seller ratios can actually be more favourable for a well-presented home in a slower market, is underappreciated.

In Brisbane, the seasonal pattern is also less pronounced than in Melbourne or Sydney. The climate means buyer activity does not compress into a narrow spring window the same way. Motivated buyers in Brisbane are active year-round.

What matters more than the calendar month is the rate environment at the time, the quality and quantity of comparable stock in your area when you list, your own timeline and carrying costs, and how well your property is prepared. A well-prepared property in the right market conditions in July will outperform a poorly prepared one in a busy spring market.

In 2032 the Olympic period adds another layer to this calculation. I have written a detailed guide covering how to think about all of these factors together, including the Brisbane-specific seasonal data and what the Olympic calendar means for the rest of this year.
