## Caption

September is Brisbane's strongest selling month for residential property. The market has more active buyers, more energy at open homes, and historically a higher volume of above-appraisal results than any other month.

The sellers who achieve those results in September are not the ones who called their agent in early September. They're the ones who had the conversation in late July.

Getting a home genuinely ready to launch in September requires five to eight weeks of preparation. Styling decisions, any cosmetic work, photography, marketing copy, listing strategy, and the timing of the first open home all need to be coordinated. Running that process in a hurry produces a weaker result than running it properly.

The other reason July matters: the buyers who are ready to move in September are already inspecting properties in August. If your launch is late, you miss the buyers who have been in the market for three months and are ready to commit.

If you own a home in the inner east and you're thinking about selling before the end of the year, July is the right time to start the conversation, not to lock in the campaign date, but to understand what preparation is needed, what your likely price range looks like, and whether September or October makes more sense for your specific property.

That conversation costs nothing and takes about an hour.
