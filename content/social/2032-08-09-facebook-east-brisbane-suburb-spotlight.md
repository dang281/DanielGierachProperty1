## Caption

East Brisbane is the inner-east suburb with the most direct CBD access of the group, and that geography shapes everything about who buys here and why.

The eastern boundary sits against Woolloongabba, which means the Gabba precinct and the South East Busway are immediately adjacent. The Mowbray Park ferry provides river access without needing a car. Buyers who want to walk to work or cycle along the riverside parks have a shorter commute from East Brisbane than from almost anywhere in the inner east.

East Brisbane State School covers the primary catchment for family buyers. The secondary option at Woolloongabba and the wider school network beyond sit within practical reach.

The housing stock reflects the suburb's dual character. Character Queenslanders on traditional inner-city lots serve the owner-occupier segment, where buyers who want to live close to the CBD make up the discretionary tier that sets the suburb's price ceiling. Contemporary townhouses and units serve a significant rental and investor segment that the suburb's CBD proximity naturally attracts.

For investors, the proximity to hospitals, universities, and the city employment core creates a rental demand story that does not rely on a single anchor.

For owner-occupiers, East Brisbane offers a city address on a residential street, and that combination stays in demand across different market cycles.
