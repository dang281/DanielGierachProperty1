## Caption

The 60 days before a property goes to market are where most campaigns are won or lost.

In that window, the sellers who do the best are the ones who separate the tasks into two categories: things that protect existing value, and things that add value. The first category is essential. The second is where people spend money they will not recover.

Fixing a leaking roof protects value. A full bathroom renovation does not typically earn back its cost. Re-painting the exterior and interior in neutral tones protects value. A new kitchen rarely does. Clearing clutter and professionally staging a property protects and often adds value, because the photography is better and enquiry is higher.

The conversation I have with sellers before campaign launch is about where to spend and where to stop. Not every property needs work. Some need nothing. A few need targeted investment in specific areas. The decision should be based on comparable sales and likely buyer expectations, not on what the seller feels needs updating.

If you are planning to list in autumn 2028 and want to have that conversation now, I am happy to walk through your property and give you a clear list.
