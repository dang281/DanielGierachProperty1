## Caption

A seller will often accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one. Understanding why makes you a better negotiator on both sides of the transaction.

An unconditional offer in Queensland means the buyer has removed both the finance condition and the building and pest condition. There are no remaining exit points in the contract other than the standard cooling-off period. The deal is effectively done. For a seller who has already found their next property, or who simply wants certainty, that is worth a material discount on price.

The typical conditional offer carries two to three weeks of live risk. Finance approval can be delayed or declined. A building and pest report can produce findings that the buyer uses to renegotiate, sometimes significantly. Sellers know this, and the uncertainty is priced in when they are comparing offers.

Buyers who want to compete unconditionally are not taking a blind risk. Pre-approval with a lender who has assessed the property type and location removes most of the finance uncertainty. An independent building and pest inspection commissioned before making the offer removes the other variable. It takes preparation, but it is achievable, and in a competitive inner east market it is increasingly the difference between winning and missing out.

The article explains what unconditional means in practice, how to get there as a buyer, and what the risk window actually looks like when you eliminate those conditions for a seller.
