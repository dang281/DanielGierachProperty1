## Caption

A price guide and a reserve price are not the same figure, and treating them as interchangeable is one of the more common misunderstandings buyers bring to auction campaigns in Queensland.

The price guide is set before the campaign begins. In Queensland, agents are required to provide a price guide that reflects a reasonable expectation of the likely sale range, based on comparable sales evidence at the time of listing. It is an estimate, made before any buyer feedback has been received, and before the market has had the chance to respond to the property.

The reserve is different. It is the minimum price the vendor will accept on auction day, and it is confirmed on the morning of the auction, not weeks earlier. By that point, the agent has four weeks of buyer feedback, open home attendance data, and registered bidder interest to inform the conversation with the vendor about where to set it. The reserve is a decision made with far more information than was available when the guide was written.

What this means in practice is that the reserve can sit above the guide if the campaign has generated strong interest and the evidence supports it. It can also sit below the top of the guide if the buyer pool has been thinner than expected. Neither outcome means the original guide was wrong. It reflects the fact that guides are estimates made at a point in time and the reserve is a decision made at the end of the process.

Sophisticated inner east buyers understand this. They watch the guide, assess the property against it, and arrive at their own view of value before auction day. The buyers who do that work are the ones who bid with confidence rather than hesitation.
