## Caption

Morningside has one of the most consistently active auction markets in the inner east, and it has held that position across multiple market cycles. The suburb sits adjacent to the Cannon Hill Plaza, the riverfront walk, and good school catchments, and buyers who want inner east credentials without paying Bulimba or Hawthorne prices continue to arrive in numbers that exceed available supply.

The buyer profile is primarily families and established professionals who have done detailed research on the inner east and identified Morningside as the suburb where the combination of character housing stock, access, and lifestyle value is most compelling for their budget. They are informed, they are financially ready, and they move decisively at well-run auctions. Competition at inspection for character Queenslanders on good streets is a reliable feature of the market here.

Post-war homes with solid bones and renovation potential attract strong interest from a separate cohort of buyers who are willing to invest time and money to achieve the finish they want. The renovation trajectory of Morningside over the past decade has raised the bar on what buyers expect, which in turn has lifted the ceiling on what well-presented properties can achieve.

Supply has stayed tight relative to demand, which means owners who time their campaigns well and present their properties properly tend to achieve results above their initial expectations.

If you own in Morningside and want to know where your property sits in today's market, start here.
