## Caption

The 2032 Brisbane Olympics are five years away. That timing sits in an interesting position for property buyers and sellers. The infrastructure is underway, the economic activity is real, and the corridor that benefits most from both includes Brisbane's inner east.

Cross River Rail opened in 2025, and the commute time reduction from inner east suburbs to the CBD has been a consistent factor in buyer conversations since. It shortened a trip that previously required a connection and extended it meaningfully. Buyers, particularly those relocating from Sydney and Melbourne where rail access is a baseline expectation, factor it into their decisions.

The Woolloongabba precinct, with the venues and athlete village development in and around it, has brought sustained construction and economic activity to the inner east corridor. That activity generates employment, it draws workers who need accommodation nearby, and it keeps the precinct prominent in interstate and international property conversations in a way that a suburb without that narrative does not benefit from.

What I have observed in campaigns over the past 18 months is a noticeably broader buyer pool for properties with good access to Cross River Rail stations and the Woolloongabba corridor. Buyers who might previously have looked at a different part of Brisbane are considering the inner east because the infrastructure story makes sense to them on a long time horizon.

The market has not fully priced in completion. Five years is close enough for the infrastructure to be real and visible, and far enough that the uplift from the event itself is still ahead.
