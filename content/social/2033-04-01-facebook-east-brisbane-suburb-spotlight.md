## Caption

East Brisbane sits three kilometres from the city in a position that most inner east buyers cannot replicate for the price. It is close enough to walk to South Bank, close enough to access Mater and PA Hospitals, and positioned on the river side of the peninsula in a way that makes it genuinely different from suburbs a kilometre or two further out.

The buyer profile here skews toward professionals who want proximity without paying Fortitude Valley or New Farm prices. Young families are a growing part of the market too, drawn by the character home stock and the sense that the suburb still has room to move. Tight supply helps. East Brisbane does not turn over quickly, and when homes do come to market, demand tends to be concentrated.

The character terrace and Queenslander homes along the eastern streets attract buyers who understand the difference between a cosmetically updated property and one that has been properly renovated. Investors hold here for the yield and the long-run appreciation that inner-ring suburban land has historically delivered in Brisbane.

As we move into the cooler months, autumn campaigns in East Brisbane are typically the most active part of the year. Listings that present well in April and May rarely sit long.
