## Caption

Enough of March has played out in the inner east to share an honest read.

Well-prepared properties that launched in the first three weeks of March with competitive pricing achieved strong results. The buyer pool was deep, inspection numbers were solid, and when competition existed between genuine buyers, it showed in the final price. These are not exceptional outcomes. They are what a well-run campaign in the right window produces.

The pattern I see across campaigns that performed well: the sellers had done the preparation work. Building reports were done. Styling was considered and brief-matched to the buyer pool. Pricing was set based on comparable sales rather than aspiration. Photography was done properly. The campaigns that underperformed typically had at least one of those elements compromised.

For sellers who held off and are now considering spring: the buyer pool you will enter in September and October is different from the one that was active in March. Not worse necessarily, but different. Spring brings more listings, which gives buyers more choice. The buyers who were highly motivated in February and March have already bought something.

If you are planning to sell in spring, the preparation conversation is worth starting now rather than in August. The sellers who perform in October planned in June.

For sellers who are still on the market from March campaigns: February through late March is the peak. If the market has given you clear feedback through inspection numbers and offer activity, that feedback is worth acting on before the pool thins further.

The honest question for sellers currently on market: is the strategy working, or does something need to change?
