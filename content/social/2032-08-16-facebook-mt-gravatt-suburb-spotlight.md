## Caption

Mt Gravatt is not a suburb that positions itself on lifestyle. It positions itself on practicality, and that is a stronger argument than it might initially appear.

Griffith University's Nathan campus places Mt Gravatt in a consistent rental demand corridor. Student and professional rental demand from the university, combined with the permanent employment base at Princess Alexandra Hospital nearby, means the investor market here is not relying on population growth alone to maintain its case.

Westfield Garden City sits immediately to the suburb's east. Mains Road retail extends the precinct northward. Residents in Mt Gravatt have access to one of the better retail and dining precincts in South East Queensland without commuting to find it.

Families targeting the Mt Gravatt State High School catchment drive a steady owner-occupier segment in the mid-price range. Post-war brick and tile homes on substantial blocks give that buyer profile what it is looking for: school access, land, and a practical home that suits family living rather than a renovation project.

The value proposition here is clear. Less lifestyle premium than the river corridor, more space than the premium inner-east suburbs, solid infrastructure all round. For owners in Mt Gravatt, that proposition appeals to multiple buyer types simultaneously.
