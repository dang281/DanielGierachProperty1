## Caption

Bulimba consistently records some of the lowest days on market of any inner east Brisbane suburb, and the competition at open homes on Wyandra Street and Edinburgh Castle Road tells you exactly why. Buyers want in, and very few owners are selling.

The buyer profile here is broad but quality-driven. Families chasing Bulimba State School's catchment make up a large portion of the enquiry pool, alongside professionals who want the Oxford Street café strip, the City Cat terminal, and a sub-20-minute commute to the CBD. That combination keeps pressure on prices even when listings are lean.

What sells fastest are well-presented character Queenslanders and post-war homes on generous blocks. Buyers compete hard for anything with a renovation upside, particularly on the quieter streets north of Oxford Street. Newer builds attract a separate cohort of buyers who want low-maintenance living without leaving the suburb.

Supply in Bulimba has stayed tight for years. When a good property is priced honestly and presented well, it rarely lingers. Owners who have been considering a move tend to get the best outcomes by acting before the spring wave brings more competition to the buyer pool.

If you own in Bulimba and want to understand what your property is worth in the current market, start here.
