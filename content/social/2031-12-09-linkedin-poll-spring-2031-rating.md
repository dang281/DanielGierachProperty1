## Caption

Spring 2031 is wrapping up. The open home season from September through November has wound down, the pre-Christmas campaign window is closing, and it is worth taking a direct look at how the season actually played out.

There has been a lot of commentary throughout the spring about Olympic-year momentum, interstate buyer interest, and the state of competition at open homes. Some of that matched what I saw at inspections. Some of the narrative ran ahead of the actual activity. The real measure is what buyers and sellers experienced on the ground.

This is a market where different suburbs and different price points often tell genuinely different stories, even within the same spring. The $900K to $1.3M range in Hawthorne can be a very different experience to the $1.8M-plus range in Bulimba in the same month.

Looking back at spring 2031, how would you rate buyer activity in Brisbane's inner east?

## Poll options

- Exceptionally strong, one of the better spring markets in recent years
- Solid, consistent with a normal spring pattern
- Mixed, very suburb and price-point dependent
- Softer than I expected given the Olympic year approach
