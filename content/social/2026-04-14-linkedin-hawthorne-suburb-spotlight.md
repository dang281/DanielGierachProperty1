## Caption

Hawthorne has a median house price of around $2.1 million, with annual capital growth of roughly 8 per cent according to current suburb data. That places it among the higher-value pockets in Brisbane's inner east.

What keeps the floor under Hawthorne is consistent buyer demand from a narrow demographic: mostly families and professionals who specifically want river proximity, the Oxford Street precinct, and a CityCat commute. It is a deliberate choice suburb. Buyers do not stumble into it.

That matters for sellers because it limits the wild swings you see in more speculative markets. The buyers here are anchored to the lifestyle, not just the market cycle.

The challenge for homeowners considering a sale right now is the same one everyone faces: the cash rate is at 4.10 per cent and serviceability is tighter than it was two years ago. The buyer pool is still there, but competition has shifted from raw volume to quality. Presentation and positioning count more than they did in 2021.
