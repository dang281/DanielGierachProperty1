## Caption

Carina sits at the eastern edge of Brisbane's inner east and delivers the fundamentals that family buyers prioritise, without the price premium of the river-adjacent suburbs. The suburb's appeal is consistently practical: large block sizes by inner east standards, a housing stock that skews toward post-war brick lowsets and Queenslanders, and a position that balances easy road access to the CBD with proximity to the retail and dining anchors of Westfield Carindale.

The buyer profile in Carina is predominantly families and upgraders who have done the comparative analysis on the surrounding suburbs and arrived at Carina as the suburb that delivers the most square metres of land for the budget. That buyer is research-led and understands comparable sales evidence. They are not buying on suburb prestige. They are buying on value relative to neighbouring suburbs at a similar price point.

Carina's school catchment access to Carina State School and proximity to Mount Gravatt State High School sustains a portion of the family buyer demand that persists regardless of broader market conditions. The practical lifestyle picture, Westfield Carindale within two kilometres, good road access to the Gateway Motorway for airport and southern suburbs commuters, and the suburban spaciousness of the housing stock, makes the suburb attractive to a range of buyer types.
