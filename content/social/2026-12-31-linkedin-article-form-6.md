## Caption

Most sellers sign the Form 6 at their kitchen table while the agent is still packing up their bag. Almost none of them read it first.

The Form 6 is Queensland's agency agreement. It sets out what the agent is authorised to do on your behalf, the commission structure, the marketing spend, the duration of the appointment, and the terms under which you or the agent can end the arrangement. It is a legally binding contract, not a formality.

The thing most sellers miss: under the Property Occupations Act 2014 (Qld), you have a five-business-day cooling-off right after signing the Form 6. That window is there precisely because sellers often sign under time pressure. You do not have to proceed if something changes or if you want to reconsider. Most agents will not volunteer this information unprompted.

The other common oversight is the rebate clause. If an agent receives a benefit from a third party, such as a referring fee from a particular conveyancer or building inspector, that must be disclosed in the Form 6. Worth reading.

Before you sign, ask for a few minutes. Read the duration clause, the commission rate, and what happens at the end of the appointment period. Those three sections determine most of the practical outcomes.
