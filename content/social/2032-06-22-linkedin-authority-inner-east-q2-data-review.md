## Caption

We are a week from the end of Q2. Before the noise of the Games takes over, it is worth pausing on what the inner east market has actually done in the first half of 2032.

Days on market is one of the numbers I watch most closely. It tells you more about buyer conviction than median price does, because it reflects how long it takes for a buyer to commit, not just what they paid when they did. Through Q2, well-priced properties in the Bulimba, Hawthorne and Balmoral corridor have been clearing within campaign timelines for the most part. The listings that have sat have generally had a pricing story that did not match the market, not a demand problem.

Auction clearance rates have held firm through the quarter. Buyer registrations have been consistent at well-run campaigns. The interstate buyer component has remained active, particularly for family homes with good school access.

What the data does not yet show is what happens in Q3. The Games, the post-Games period, and the rate environment in the second half of the year will all feed into that. I will be watching the Q3 numbers closely and sharing what I see.

If you are an investor, a seller planning for spring, or a buyer sitting on the sidelines through the Games period, Q2 is useful context for what you step back into.
