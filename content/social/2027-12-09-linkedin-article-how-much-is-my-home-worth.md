## Caption

Two houses on the same street in Hawthorne can be $200,000 apart in price. Not because one is dramatically larger, but because one catches the morning sun, sits on a slightly higher allotment, and faces the park. The other backs onto a fence and has a carport where the second garage used to be.

Automated estimates cannot see any of that. The property valuation tools you find online are pulling comparable sales from the past 60 to 120 days and applying a formula. They do not know your aspect, your renovation quality, your flood risk overlay, or the fact that the street behind yours is considerably noisier than it used to be. They also do not know which buyers are active right now and what they are prepared to pay.

The most accurate indication of what your property is worth is a current market appraisal done by an agent who has been through the door and who is actively selling comparable properties in the area. Not because agents are always right, but because they have access to contract prices that are not yet reflected in public sold data, and they know what the buyers who missed out on recent sales are still prepared to pay.

The automated estimate is a reasonable starting point. It should not be the basis for your selling price strategy.
