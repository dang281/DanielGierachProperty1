## Caption

End of financial year is a more relevant milestone for inner east property owners than most people realise, particularly for investors and anyone considering a sale in the next six months.

For investors, the period around 30 June affects which financial year a capital gain falls into. A settlement that happens on 1 July rather than 28 June can shift a significant tax liability by twelve months. It does not change the total amount owing, but it changes the timing, which affects cash flow planning and the decision about whether to hold another year or proceed.

For vendors who are already mid-campaign or preparing to list, the EOFY question is worth raising with your accountant before you set a settlement date. In most cases, the difference is a few weeks of negotiation on the settlement period. It costs nothing to model it; it can cost a great deal to miss it.

For buyers acquiring an investment property, June and July can also be a productive period. Motivated vendors who need to settle before or after 30 June for tax reasons are sometimes willing to accommodate flexible settlement terms. That flexibility has value.

None of this is financial advice. It is a flag that the end of June is worth a conversation with your accountant, not just your real estate agent.
