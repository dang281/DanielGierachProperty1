## Caption

Preparation matters more than season. A well-prepared inner east campaign launched in May regularly outperforms a rushed one launched in October. The gap between the two outcomes regularly sits between $50,000 and $150,000.

That figure is not a claim about winter being a better time to sell than spring. It is a claim about preparation being more important than timing. Spring does produce more buyer activity and higher listing volumes. The additional competition from other properties in spring often offsets the benefit of more buyers being in the market. A vendor who waits until October and launches underprepared, because they believed the season would do the work, regularly achieves a worse result than one who launched in May with everything done properly.

The pre-campaign preparation phase typically runs four to eight weeks. It covers pricing based on current comparable sales, presentation work on the property, photography, and marketing strategy. That phase is where the result is largely determined. Vendors who compress it, or skip parts of it because a spring launch date is approaching, are making the single decision most likely to affect their outcome more than any seasonal factor.

Pricing accuracy is part of this. A vendor who launches in spring with pricing based on last spring's comparable sales, rather than the current market, is not benefiting from the season. The market they are entering is the one that exists in October 2031, and comparable sales from twelve months earlier may not reflect current buyer behaviour or current lending conditions.

The article makes the case for investing in preparation and explains what that phase involves and what it typically costs.
