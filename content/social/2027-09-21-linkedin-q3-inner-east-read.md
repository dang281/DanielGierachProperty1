Q3 2027 in Brisbane's inner east is wrapping up. Here is what the open home floor and the auction results have actually shown.

Winter was quieter for new listings, as it typically is. But buyer depth held. The buyers who came through in July and August were not casual browsers. They were motivated, finance-ready, and in most cases had been watching specific streets for months. That made for a genuinely competitive environment despite the lower volume of new stock.

September has brought more listings and more buyer traffic. The net effect on auction results has been broadly stable. More competition among properties has been offset by a larger pool of buyers entering the market for the spring. Clearance rates have not shifted dramatically in either direction.

Properties with strong land content, a good school catchment, or a recent renovation of substance have continued to draw multiple registered bidders. That has been consistent across Q3. The fundamentals buyers value in this corridor have not changed.

The friction point that has held some campaigns back is the gap between vendor price expectations and what the market will actually pay. When that gap is wide, properties sit. Four weeks on market in the inner east is not disqualifying, but it raises questions for subsequent buyers about what others saw that they did not.

The campaigns that performed strongest in Q3 were the ones that launched with vendor expectations calibrated to the real market from day one. Not the ones with the biggest marketing spend. Preparation and honest pricing, done early, drove the outcomes.
