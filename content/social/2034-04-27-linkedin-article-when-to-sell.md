## Caption

The honest answer to when to sell is that preparation matters more than season. That is not what most vendors want to hear when they are asking about timing, but it is what the evidence consistently supports.

The gap between a well-prepared inner east campaign and a rushed one is regularly $50,000 to $150,000 on properties in the $1.2 million to $2 million range. That gap is not created by market conditions. It is created by the presentation, photography, pricing strategy, and buyer pool management that determine how many people compete for the property and how far they are prepared to go.

A well-prepared campaign launched in July often outperforms an unprepared one launched in September when the spring buyer rush arrives. The spring buyer pool is larger, but so is the supply of competing listings. The prepared vendor does better than the unprepared one regardless of season.

The seasonal timing conversation is worth having. Autumn and spring do have genuine differences in buyer volume and competition dynamics. But the variable the vendor actually controls is preparation, and that is the one that consistently makes the largest difference to the result.
