## Caption

Seven Hills in spring shows the suburb at its natural best. The elevated position means the streets benefit from the longer days and clear September skies in a way that flat suburbs simply do not. Views that are present year-round become part of the daily visual experience in a more immediate way when the light is good, and buyers inspecting in September understand what they are purchasing.

The renovation opportunity in Seven Hills has a specific spring dimension. Buyers who have spent winter planning and budgeting for a project arrive in September ready to commit. They have usually been watching the suburb for months, and they know the price range well enough to recognise genuine value when it presents. For vendors of properties with clear renovation potential, that buyer readiness translates directly into confident offers.

Large lots in Seven Hills also attract buyers thinking about future development potential. The dual-income property buyer and the knockdown-rebuild buyer both operate in this suburb, and they bring a calculation to their offers that is more about the land than the existing structure. That buyer type is active in spring and tends to transact efficiently once they have made their land assessment.

Seven Hills is still underappreciated in the context of its motorway access. The Gateway and Pacific are major practical assets, and not every buyer has fully priced that into their comparison with similar suburbs.
