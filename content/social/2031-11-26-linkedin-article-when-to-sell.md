## Caption

The best time to sell is when you are ready. That sounds obvious. In practice, most vendors spend more time thinking about market conditions than about their own preparation, and that calculus is backwards.

A well-prepared July campaign regularly outperforms an unprepared October campaign. The spring market is busy and buyer numbers are high, but a property that is not presentation-ready, has not had a pre-campaign building inspection, and launches with a price that does not reflect comparable sales will underperform regardless of what month it is listed. The buyer pool in October can see through a campaign that has not been properly set up.

The variable a vendor actually controls is preparation: the quality of the photographs, the state of the presentation, the pre-sale maintenance, the accuracy of the price guide, the inclusions list, the contract ready to go before the first open home. These are the decisions that determine the outcome of a campaign. Market conditions are the backdrop. Preparation is the difference.

There are situations where timing does matter. A vendor who can choose between autumn and spring, all else being equal, should factor in the seasonal patterns specific to their suburb and price point. But "all else being equal" is rare. Most vendors have a property they need to get ready, a timeline shaped by personal circumstances, and a market that will be whatever it is.

The question is not when. The question is how ready.
