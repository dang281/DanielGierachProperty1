## Caption

There is a buyer type that only concentrates in winter, and they are among the most motivated buyers in any market.

They have typically been looking for four to six months. They know the inner east well enough to have an opinion on almost every street. They have finance pre-approved and they have had it updated at least once because they have been ready for long enough that the approval lapsed. They have lost a property, possibly more than one, and that experience has recalibrated what they are willing to pay and how quickly they are prepared to move.

When these buyers find the right property in July, the campaign tends to move fast. They are not attending open homes to get a feel for the market. They are attending because they believe the property might be the one. If it is, they will make that clear.

This is the buyer pool that properties launching in July and August are primarily competing for. It is a smaller pool than spring, but the quality of motivation is different. A buyer who has been searching since January and still has not found their property is not in the same frame of mind as a buyer who started looking three weeks ago.

If you have been watching the market from the sideline and wondering whether July is the right time, the answer depends on your property. For a lot of inner east homes, the buyers are already out there and looking.
