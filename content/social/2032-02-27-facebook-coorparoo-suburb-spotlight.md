## Caption

Coorparoo is the suburb where the inner east buyer arrives when they have made up their mind. The Coorparoo Secondary College catchment is one of the most reliably tracked school-zone premiums in the inner east, and the suburb's position on the Old Cleveland Road corridor, with its established retail, dining, and transport infrastructure, means buyers are not making a lifestyle compromise to access it.

The housing stock is a broad mix: elevated Queenslanders on the ridgeline streets off Old Cleveland Road, post-war brick and timber across the body of the suburb, and a growing number of well-executed townhouse and unit projects that have absorbed buyers who want the address without the detached home maintenance load. Lorimer Street, Ipswich Road, and the elevated streets running south toward Camp Hill capture some of the suburb's best character housing and its longest-standing owner-occupier base.

The Coorparoo Secondary College catchment is the specific factor that separates buyer competition for homes here from comparable properties just outside the boundary. Buyers who have assessed private school fees versus the school zone premium have regularly concluded that the address pays for itself over a secondary schooling period.

In autumn 2032, Coorparoo is as active as it has been at any point in this cycle. The suburb's identity is clear, the buyer pool is consistent, and correctly priced properties are not taking long to find a buyer.
