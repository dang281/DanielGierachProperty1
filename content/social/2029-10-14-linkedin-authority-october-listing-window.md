## Caption

Every spring I watch the same pattern play out. Vendors who launch in the last two weeks of October run their first open homes at peak buyer engagement, build genuine competition through November, and reach settlement before Christmas. Vendors who wait until mid-November run campaigns that bump directly into the December calendar, with buyers distracted and negotiating leverage shifting.

The difference in outcomes is not always dramatic. But it is consistent enough that when a vendor asks me whether to launch now or wait, my honest answer is: waiting past the first week of November is a timing cost, not a safety margin.

Here is why. The spring buyer pool is not one undifferentiated group. There are buyers who are ready now, who have been watching since August, who have finance in place, and who will act on the right property in October. There is a second wave who come back to the market in autumn after deciding not to buy in spring. Neither group is reliably available in mid-December. Listing in that window means you're relying on buyers who are under Christmas deadline pressure, which can work but changes the dynamic.

The vendors I work with who get the cleanest results in spring are the ones who made the preparation call in September and were ready to launch before the October long weekend. That decision window is now. If you're still thinking about it, the question worth asking is what exactly you're waiting for.
