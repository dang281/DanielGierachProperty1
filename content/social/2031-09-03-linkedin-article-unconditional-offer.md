## Caption

In a competitive spring market, a buyer who can go unconditional has genuine negotiating power. Not necessarily on price downward, but on terms: shorter settlement periods, access before settlement, flexibility on conditions that a conditional buyer simply cannot offer. Vendors in a competitive spring campaign often value those terms enough to accept an unconditional buyer's offer over a higher conditional one. The certainty of an unconditional exchange is worth something real, and the most experienced buyers understand that and use it deliberately.

What enables a buyer to go unconditional is the preparation that happens before the campaign opens. Finance must be formally approved, not just pre-approved. A building and pest inspection must have been completed on the specific property. The buyer must have legal advice on the contract and be satisfied with the title. These are not steps that can be compressed into the 48 hours of a competitive offer process. They require lead time.

Buyers who arrive at a spring campaign having done the pre-work are in a fundamentally different position to buyers who are still subject to standard conditions. They can act in a timeframe that conditional buyers cannot match. In a strong spring market, that speed is often the deciding factor between a buyer who secures a property and one who makes a competitive offer and still misses out.

Understanding what unconditional actually means in Queensland and what is required to be in a position to offer it is the subject of the full article.
