## Caption

Two years ago, the opening of Cross River Rail was described as a transformational moment for Brisbane's inner suburbs. The question worth asking now is: what has actually changed?

In the inner east, the answer is nuanced. Suburbs with direct access to the new stations have seen an increase in buyer enquiry from groups who previously ruled them out based on commute time. Cannon Hill, Coorparoo, and the streets within walking distance of the Woolloongabba and Dutton Park precinct have attracted a different buyer profile to five years ago. People who work in the CBD but wanted a larger block and a full house are now looking at the inner east where they once looked at the inner north or west.

What has not changed is the premium that the streets further from rail infrastructure carry for buyers who do not commute daily. For families where the school catchment is the primary driver, Cross River Rail is a secondary consideration. Camp Hill and Carina Heights still trade on school zones and block size.

The net effect has been a broadening of the buyer pool rather than a rerating of any single suburb. More buyers competing for the same number of properties is not a separate condition from the price growth the inner east has recorded over the past three years. They are the same condition.

If you are selling or considering selling in the inner east, the relevant question is not which infrastructure project is coming. It is what the buyer who wants your specific property looks like today. That buyer pool has changed.
