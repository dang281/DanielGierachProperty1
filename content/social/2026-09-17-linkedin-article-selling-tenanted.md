## Caption

Here is the part that catches most Queensland landlords mid-campaign: you need 24 hours written notice before every single inspection when selling a tenanted property. Not an ongoing open invitation when the listing goes live. Each open home, each private inspection, each agent visit requires its own compliant written notice. When you're running weekly opens across a four-week campaign, that's a significant administrative obligation, and missing a step gives tenants grounds to refuse entry.

Tenants who are treated well and given proper notice almost always cooperate. They understand the situation and in most cases they'd prefer a smooth sale to a dragged-out campaign with recurring conflict. Tenants who feel their home is being used as a thoroughfare without proper consideration exercise their legal rights, and they're entitled to. The landlord who expects seamless campaign access without doing the compliance work consistently runs into problems.

The more serious scenario is a fixed-term lease that extends past the intended settlement date. A tenant in a valid fixed-term lease cannot be required to vacate simply because a sale has occurred. If vacant possession is a condition of the contract, and the lease end date doesn't align, you have a problem that should have been identified before the property was listed, not after a buyer is under contract.

The article at danielgierach.com/insights/selling-tenanted-property-queensland covers the full set of notice requirements, how to manage the relationship with tenants through a campaign, and how to handle the vacant possession question before it becomes an issue.
