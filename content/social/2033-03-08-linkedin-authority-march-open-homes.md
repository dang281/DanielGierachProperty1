## Caption

Three weeks into the peak autumn season, the picture across Brisbane's inner east is clearer than it was in February.

Buyer groups at well-priced properties in Morningside, Norman Park and Cannon Hill are solid. ⚠️ VERIFY Not the frenetic numbers of a 2021-style surge, but consistent, qualified groups with genuine intent. The buyers I am speaking with at open homes have done their research. They know the comparable sales. They are not guessing at what a property is worth.

What is drawing the crowds: a well-maintained character home with a functional floor plan, good natural light, and a yard that works for a family. What is not: a renovated interior in a poorly maintained street, or a property presented beautifully online but showing its age in person. The gap between digital expectations and physical reality costs campaigns significantly.

Offer speed is another signal worth tracking. Properties that receive offers within ten days of the first open home are being priced and presented at or below where the market is willing to pay. Those that pass three open homes before receiving a first offer are usually sitting above market, or they have an accessibility or presentation issue that the campaign has not addressed.

March is the moment the autumn season earns its reputation. The weeks ahead will tell us whether 2033 delivers for sellers who prepared well. Based on what I am seeing at open homes, it should.
