## Caption

Most advice about when to sell your home was written about Melbourne and Sydney.

The spring surge, the pre-Christmas rush, the quiet January market: all of that reflects southeastern capitals where spring genuinely does produce the year's strongest selling conditions. It does not describe Brisbane's inner east.

Brisbane's most consistent selling window for houses in Hawthorne, Norman Park, Camp Hill, and Morningside runs from late February through to mid-May. Autumn, not spring. The buyer pool that matters in these suburbs, families with school-age children making deliberate moves, tends to be most active in the first half of the year. By the time spring arrives, many of those buyers have already found something.

The second point is one most agents do not make clearly enough: preparation matters more than season. A well-prepared property launched in a quieter period will almost always outperform a poorly prepared property launched at peak season. The work done in the weeks before photographs are taken is often where the campaign result is decided.

If you are trying to work out the right time to sell your inner east property, the article at the link below gives a more accurate Brisbane-specific framework than the generic spring-selling advice you will find elsewhere.
