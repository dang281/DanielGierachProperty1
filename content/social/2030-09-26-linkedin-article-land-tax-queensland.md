## Caption

Queensland land tax is not calculated per property. It is calculated on the combined unimproved land value of all your taxable Queensland landholdings. This is the detail that catches inner east investors off guard, often when the assessment arrives and the number is larger than they expected.

An investor with one inner east property might sit comfortably below the threshold. Add a second property, even a modest one, and the combined land value of both can push them over. The assessment reflects that total, not each property in isolation. Inner east land values have been rising for several years, which means investors who were comfortably below the threshold two or three years ago may have crossed it without buying anything new.

The mechanics work like this: the Queensland Valuer-General assesses the unimproved value of your land each year. Your primary residence is generally exempt. All other Queensland landholdings are aggregated. If the combined total exceeds the individual threshold, land tax applies to the amount above it. The rates increase in tiers as the combined value rises.

If you own two or more Queensland properties and you have not reviewed your land tax position recently, it is worth doing before the next assessment cycle. The Queensland Revenue Office publishes the current thresholds and rate schedule at qro.qld.gov.au. The full context for inner east investors is at danielgierach.com/insights/land-tax-queensland-investment-property.
