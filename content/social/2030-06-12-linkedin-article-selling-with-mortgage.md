## Caption

When a homeowner is working out how much they will walk away with after a sale, the instinctive calculation is sale price minus loan balance. That number is not your net equity. It is the starting point for a calculation that has several more steps.

The costs that reduce net proceeds: agent commission (typically 2% to 2.5% of the sale price in Queensland), conveyancing and legal fees, mortgage discharge fees, and marketing costs. On a $1.5M sale, these costs together are often $40,000 to $55,000. Some sellers are genuinely surprised by this number when they reach settlement.

Fixed-rate loans add an additional layer. If your mortgage has a fixed-rate period that is still running at the time of sale, the lender will calculate a break cost. Depending on how rates have moved since your loan was established, this can range from negligible to several thousand dollars. The only way to know is to ask your lender directly, before you commit to a settlement date.

The practical step: before you sign a contract on your next property and lock in your purchase price, confirm your actual net proceeds from the sale with your solicitor and accountant. The calculation is straightforward, but it needs to be done specifically for your loan and costs, not estimated from general numbers.
