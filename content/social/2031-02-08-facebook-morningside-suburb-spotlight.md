## Caption

Morningside has a buyer profile that covers more ground than most inner east suburbs. Families drawn by the school catchment options and the elevated streets above Wynnum Road. Downsizers who have been in the suburb for twenty years and want to stay close to the community they know. Younger buyers who want the Wynnum Road café strip, the Morningside Station access to the CBD, and a character home they can work on over time. A suburb that attracts this range of buyers consistently is one that does not depend on a single market segment to sustain its performance.

The elevated streets above Wynnum Road are the address within the address. Views across the bay corridor from these sections are genuine and persistent. The character housing stock on these streets, particularly the Queenslanders that have been sympathetically updated rather than heavily renovated, holds value through cycles in a way that presentation-only properties do not.

Morningside Station puts the CBD within a practical train commute. The Wynnum Road strip, from coffee in the morning through to dinner, means residents can cover most of their day-to-day needs without leaving the suburb's immediate orbit.

Supply remains healthy without being oversupplied. Properties are moving at a pace that reflects active buyer demand rather than a market searching for its level. For owners considering the autumn 2031 window, Morningside is well positioned.
