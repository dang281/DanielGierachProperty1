## Caption

January 2034 in Brisbane's inner east played out broadly in line with what the start of the year typically produces: thin listing volume, motivated buyers, and a market that moved faster than its quiet appearance suggested.

Buyer enquiry levels through the first fortnight came back strongly. Inspection attendance across the properties we were running was telling: the buyers showing up were not window-shoppers. They were people who had made decisions over the summer break and returned with finance confirmed and a clear brief. That kind of buyer is not common at this time of year in high volumes, but when the listing pool is limited, you do not need high volumes to produce competition.

The properties that performed best in January tended to share a common characteristic: they were properly prepared before they launched. Vendors who used December and early January to complete their preparation work rather than delay it had a cleaner campaign than those who rushed to market without that lead time.

What January signals for February is a market that is still supply-constrained relative to buyer demand. The autumn listing volume has not arrived yet. For vendors who are ready to launch in February, the buyer pool that built through January is waiting. For vendors still in preparation, the window narrows as March approaches and the broader market adds listings.

The month confirmed what good campaigns in the inner east tend to require at any time of year: the right preparation, the right timing, and an honest read on the property's position in the market. danielgierach.com
