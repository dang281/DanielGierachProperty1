## Caption

A seller will often accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one. Most buyers find this confusing until they understand the risk window they are asking a vendor to sit inside.

In Queensland, a conditional contract, typically subject to finance and building and pest, creates a period of uncertainty that can run two to three weeks. During that window the vendor has accepted the offer and taken the property off market, but the deal can still fall over. If the buyer's finance is declined, or the building report surfaces something the buyer uses as an exit, the vendor is back at day one. In a market where momentum matters, starting again two weeks in is costly.

An unconditional offer eliminates that risk entirely. The buyer has pre-approved finance, has done or waived the building and pest, and is committing without a way out. For a vendor who wants certainty, particularly one who has already purchased their next home or has a fixed settlement obligation, the certainty of unconditional is worth real money. Often fifty thousand dollars or more on a typical inner east transaction.

For buyers, understanding this is a genuine competitive advantage. If you have your finance fully approved and you're comfortable proceeding without conditions on a property you've already inspected thoroughly, you are in a position that most buyers in the market are not in. That is not a small thing.

I've explained the mechanics of unconditional contracts in Queensland in full detail, including what you're actually giving up and when it makes sense.
