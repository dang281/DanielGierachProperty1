## Caption

Norman Park has a geography that sets it apart from most Brisbane suburbs at a similar distance from the city. The suburb occupies a peninsular position, bounded by the Brisbane River to the north and east and by the South East Freeway to the south. That containment means the streets are quiet, through-traffic is minimal, and the suburb has developed a calm, leafy character that residents actively protect.

The housing stock leans toward premium character homes: Queenslanders, worker's cottages and post-war homes that have been renovated to a high standard. Original homes still exist for buyers prepared to invest in a project, but the market increasingly rewards the well-finished product. Current medians in Norman Park sit at the upper end of the inner east range, reflecting the lifestyle premium that the suburb's geography and housing quality command.

Mowbray Park offers river access and the CityCat ferry terminal there connects Norman Park to the CBD and South Bank without a car. It is a feature that buyers from the ferry-commuter demographic place real value on. Walkable streets, river proximity and quality schooling options in the surrounding area combine to make Norman Park particularly appealing to professional families and empty nesters who prioritise environment over convenience retail.
