## Caption

Most of the "best time to sell" advice circulating in Australia is built around Sydney and Melbourne data. If you live in Brisbane's inner east, that advice does not apply to your market.

Brisbane's inner east does not follow the standard southern-states seasonal pattern. Autumn, specifically late February through to May, is consistently the strongest selling window for this part of the city. The reasons are practical: the weather is settled, the evenings are still long enough for after-work inspections, and buyers are in a focused mindset after the distraction of school holidays.

Spring in Brisbane is hotter, more humid, and brings significantly more listing competition. More listings means more choice for buyers, which generally means more negotiating room. Sellers who launch in spring are not walking into a less competitive market. They are walking into a more crowded one.

The other variable that most timing conversations overlook is preparation. A well-prepared campaign launched in July or August will regularly outperform an unprepared October campaign. The market does not reward the season. It rewards the property that presents well, is priced accurately, and is backed by a clear strategy.

If you are deciding when to sell in Brisbane's inner east, the full picture is on the site.
