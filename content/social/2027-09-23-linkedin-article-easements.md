**Caption:**

Not all easements on a Brisbane property title are created equal. The word "easement" appears in a title search and most buyers do not know whether they are looking at something minor or something that meaningfully affects how they can use the land.

A drainage easement running along the rear fence line is common in inner east Brisbane. Council or a utility authority has the right to access that strip to maintain infrastructure. In most cases it affects a narrow band at the back of the block and has no practical impact on how the owner uses the property. It rarely moves the price.

An access easement running through the back yard, giving a neighbouring property or a third party the right to pass through, is a materially different situation. It can affect fencing, landscaping, and what you can build. The value impact depends entirely on the terms of the easement and where it runs.

Most buyers see the word on a title search, flag it with their solicitor, and either understand it correctly or make a decision based on incomplete information. The article I have linked covers the types of easements you are most likely to see on inner east Brisbane titles, what each type actually allows, and how to read the registered plan to understand its footprint.

Understanding what you are looking at before you bid at auction or sign a contract gives you the right basis for your decision, rather than reacting to a term that sounds more alarming than it is.
