## Caption

Net equity is not sale price minus loan balance. Most vendors work out this is not the right calculation only during the campaign, which is not the best time to discover a shortfall.

The costs of selling are real and they are significant. Agent commission, conveyancing, marketing, styling, and any pre-sale repairs typically run 2.5 to 4% of the sale price. On a $1.6M property that is $40,000 to $64,000 before any proceeds are calculated. These costs come out before the loan is paid out. A vendor who has not modelled this correctly can find that the net proceeds are materially lower than they expected, particularly if the gap between the loan balance and the sale price is not large.

The fixed-rate break cost is another figure that vendors with fixed-rate loans need to confirm before the campaign launches, not after an offer is accepted. Once the settlement period is running, the options narrow considerably. Break costs can add thousands of dollars to the payout figure and they fluctuate with interest rate movements. The only way to know the actual figure is to ask the lender for a written quote, and the right time to do that is before the marketing agreement is signed.

For vendors with offset accounts or redraw facilities, the payout figure is not always what appears on the internet banking screen. Lenders calculate payouts differently depending on the structure of the account and the timing of the settlement. Understanding how your lender applies this calculation before the campaign starts means the net proceeds figure is accurate, not an estimate.
