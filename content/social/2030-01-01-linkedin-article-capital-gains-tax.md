## Caption

Most Brisbane vendors assume the main residence exemption covers them completely. It doesn't always.

If your home was rented at any point after 20 August 1996, partial CGT liability applies for the period it was not your principal place of residence. This catches a lot of inner east owners who rented their home for a year or two before moving in, or who moved out temporarily while renovating or working interstate.

The maths matters. The ATO apportions CGT based on the proportion of time the property was not your main residence. On a well-held inner east home that has grown significantly in value, even a short rental period can create a meaningful tax bill that no one planned for.

A few things sellers routinely overlook:

The six-year rule lets you treat a rented property as your main residence for up to six years after you move out, provided you don't claim another property as your main residence at the same time. This can eliminate or reduce CGT if applied correctly.

Capital improvements add to your cost base, which reduces your gain. Keeping records of renovations over the hold period is worth doing from day one.

Depreciation claimed as an investment deduction reduces your cost base, which increases your CGT liability when you sell.

None of this is advice. It is the kind of question your accountant should be answering before you sign a contract, not after.
