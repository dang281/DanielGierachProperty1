## Caption

"We have a buyer ready to go" is one of the most common things sellers hear before a campaign launches. Sometimes it is a genuine opportunity. More often, it is a way to close a deal quickly, usually at a price that suits the buyer more than the vendor.

Off-market transactions have a place in the market. For sellers with genuine privacy concerns, for unusual properties where the buyer pool is genuinely narrow, or for vendors who prioritise speed and certainty over price optimisation, an off-market sale can make sense. These situations exist.

They are not the norm.

In most cases, limiting a property to one buyer or a small pool of buyers limits the mechanism that creates competition. And competition is what drives the gap between a market price and an above-market result. Removing competition almost always affects price, and in Brisbane's inner east, that gap on a $1.2M to $1.8M property can be significant.

The question for any seller who receives an off-market approach is: why is this buyer coming to me instead of waiting for the campaign? The answer to that question usually tells you everything you need to know.

The full analysis of when off-market works and when a full campaign outperforms it is at danielgierach.com/insights/off-market-property-sales-brisbane.
