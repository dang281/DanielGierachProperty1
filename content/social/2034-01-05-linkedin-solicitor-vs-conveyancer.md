## Caption

Most buyers sign the contract before they speak to a solicitor or conveyancer. It is one of the most common sequences in Queensland property, and it is the wrong way around.

Before you sign, you have leverage. The inclusions, the conditions, the settlement period, the risk clauses: all of these are negotiable. A solicitor reviewing the contract at that point can identify missing inclusions, flag risky conditions, and recommend amendments while there is still room to make them. That negotiating window is real and it is available to every buyer before the pen goes down.

After you sign, most of that leverage disappears. The contract terms are set. Your solicitor is no longer reviewing to protect you from what is in there. They are helping you manage it.

The difference between getting legal advice before signing and after signing is not about the quality of the advice. It is about what the advice can actually achieve. The same issue spotted before signing can be fixed. Spotted after, it becomes something to manage.

Knowing whether you need a solicitor or a conveyancer for your transaction is a separate question worth understanding before you engage anyone. The answer depends on the complexity of what you are buying.

The full article covers both questions: when to engage, and what the difference actually means for your purchase.
