## Caption

If you have been considering listing before the Games open, the window is closing. Not in a vague, "things slow down eventually" way. In a specific, calendar-driven way.

The Games open 26 July. A campaign that launches in the first week of June, runs for four weeks, and allows 30 days for settlement clears just before that date, with almost no margin. A campaign that launches after the first week of June does not clear comfortably, and you are either settling during the Games or asking your buyer to settle into a period of genuine logistical noise across the city.

That matters less for some properties than others. But for anything in the inner east where your likely buyer is a family relocating or an owner-occupier who needs to coordinate a move, settlement during the Games is a complexity they would rather avoid. And buyers who would rather avoid a complexity sometimes walk away from it.

The last three weeks of May are genuinely the final window for sellers who want to run a full campaign and settle cleanly before July. After that, the options are shorter campaigns with less market exposure, or listing into the post-Games period.

Neither of those is necessarily wrong. But they are different decisions with different trade-offs. If you want to talk through which one makes sense for your situation, reach me at danielgierach.com
