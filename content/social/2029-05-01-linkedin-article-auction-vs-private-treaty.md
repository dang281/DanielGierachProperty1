## Caption

Method of sale is one of the most important campaign decisions a vendor makes. It is also one of the most misunderstood, because the standard advice is to choose based on what neighbours did, or what the agent prefers, rather than what the property and buyer pool actually call for.

Auction creates urgency and transparency. When the buyer pool is competitive and multiple registered bidders are present, auction extracts the market's full price. When the buyer pool is thin or price-sensitive, a passed-in auction starts the negotiation at a disadvantage.

Private treaty is the default for most inner east sales. It allows buyers to purchase with conditions, which broadens the pool to include buyers who need finance approval or building and pest inspection before committing. A conditional offer carries more risk than an unconditional one, but it is also a larger pool of potential buyers.

Expression of interest suits properties where the buyer's idea of value varies significantly, either because the property is unusual, the land has multiple use scenarios, or the buyer pool spans different demographics and price brackets. EOI captures that variance. Private treaty does not.

The decision is not about agent preference. It is about where the buyer pool sits and how the market is likely to respond to each approach. That conversation should happen before the campaign strategy is set.
