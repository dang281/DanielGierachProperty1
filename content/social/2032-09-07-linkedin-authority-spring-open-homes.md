## Caption

It is the first full week of September and the difference from July and August is clear at open homes across Brisbane's inner east.

The buyers who were watching from the sidelines through the Games period are now making decisions. Groups are larger. Questions are more specific. The conversations happening at open homes are the kind that turn into offers, not the casual looking you get mid-campaign when buyers have not yet committed to a direction.

Days on market for well-priced properties is tightening. Properties that came to market with honest pricing and good presentation are not sitting.

What is also noticeable is the quality of buyers rather than just the numbers. Owner-occupiers who held off listing or buying through the July and August period are now active with purpose. They have been watching comparable sales, they know the corridor, and they are not wasting opens on properties they would not buy.

Sellers who used the Games period to prepare, refresh, and get their presentation right are launching into this. That preparation is showing up in the quality of early enquiry.

The sellers who skipped preparation and came to market during the Olympics hoping to catch a reduced buyer pool on urgency are the ones still sitting with days on market ticking up.

Spring in Brisbane's inner east rewards preparation over timing. That observation is not new, but it is very visible right now.
