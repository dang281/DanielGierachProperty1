## Caption

The gap between a good spring campaign and an average one in the inner east is almost always preparation, and preparation is almost always a July problem.

Vendors who start in July for a September or October launch have six to eight weeks. That is enough time to get three quotes for the kitchen tap that has needed replacing for two years, book the painter before they fill their schedule, make a genuine decision about styling rather than accepting whatever is available, and talk through the price strategy before the first open home locks in expectations.

Vendors who start in mid-September are doing all of that in two weeks. The trades who would have done a better job are already booked. The stylist is working with whatever furniture is left in the property because the good pieces are committed elsewhere. The price conversation happens at the first open home rather than before it.

The photography decision illustrates this clearly. A property photographed after the styling is complete, the garden is presented, and the maintenance items are resolved produces images that hold up through a four-week campaign. A property photographed before those decisions are made goes to market carrying whatever state it was in when the photographer arrived.

Same property. Different preparation timeline. Materially different first impression for every buyer who sees it online.

Spring listings in the inner east will open in late August and September. The ones that go smoothly are being organised now.

If you are considering a spring campaign, a conversation in July is the right starting point. danielgierach.com
