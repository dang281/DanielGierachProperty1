## Caption

Most vendors think about staging as something buyers experience when they walk through the door. The more accurate way to think about it is as a photography investment. The photographs are what generate enquiry. Enquiry is what fills your open homes. Open home attendance is what creates competition. Competition is what moves price.

Staging works backwards from that logic. A well-staged property photographs better than a lived-in one, even if both are clean and well-presented. It's not about disguising anything. It's about helping buyers see the space at its best before they've set foot inside, at a point in the process when they're choosing which opens to attend out of dozens of options.

For a three-bedroom inner east home on a four-week campaign, professional staging typically costs somewhere between three thousand and six thousand dollars. Whether that investment makes sense depends on the gap between your property's current presentation and how it would photograph with professional furniture and styling. In some cases it's obvious. In others, it's worth a direct conversation about what the return on that spend looks like in your specific market and price bracket.

There are also alternatives to full staging, including partial staging, furniture hire, and styling consults where you use your own items differently. The article below covers the full range of options, typical costs, what to look for when engaging a stylist, and the question of when staging is genuinely worth it versus when it isn't.
