## Caption

Mt Gravatt East is the suburb that tends to be discovered by buyers who have spent several months trying to afford Camp Hill or Coorparoo and realise the value proposition three kilometres further south is considerably stronger.

The streets between Newnham Road and Cavendish Road in Mt Gravatt East deliver the block sizes, the established tree cover, and the school catchment access that inner east buyers prioritise, at a price point that still offers genuine value relative to suburbs closer to the river. Mansfield State High is a significant driver for families with secondary school-aged children in this pocket.

The character housing stock on the elevated streets gives views across the southern corridor in some positions, and the topography produces the kind of natural light in living areas that photographs well and presents strongly at open homes.

Days on market in Mt Gravatt East through 2030 have been consistent with the broader inner east, sitting in the twenties for well-positioned homes. Properties that have struggled are those where the price expectation has not moved with the evidence from comparable sales. The buyers here are systematic in their research.

For homeowners in the elevated streets of Mt Gravatt East, the spring season through October and November typically produces the deepest buyer pool of the year.
