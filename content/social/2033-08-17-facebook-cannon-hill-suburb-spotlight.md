## Caption

Cannon Hill's spring market is a good time to observe the suburb's practical buyer profile in action. Buyers who are not pursuing a specific lifestyle statement, who are pursuing access, school catchment access, retail convenience, and an inner east position that does not require stretching the budget, tend to do their most active searching in spring when the listing pool gives them options to compare.

The Cannon Hill Kmart Plaza precinct provides a level of retail services density that residents find consistently useful in the way that larger but less convenient centres cannot replicate. The ability to resolve most everyday household requirements within a short drive of home is a feature that shows up in buyer feedback at open homes repeatedly.

The Cleveland line train at Cannon Hill station connects residents directly to the CBD in under 15 minutes during off-peak, and the walk from the Cannon Hill station to properties in the residential streets north of Creek Road is modest enough to be genuinely practical for a daily commuter. That combination of rail access and the retail precinct is part of what sustains buyer demand across seasons.

The spring preparation advice for Cannon Hill vendors is the same as for the broader inner east: the buyers at September open homes have done their research. Presentation and pricing need to match what the comparable sales evidence says the property is worth.
