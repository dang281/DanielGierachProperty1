## Caption

September buyers are different from October buyers, and vendors who understand this list their properties accordingly.

The buyer inspecting in September has typically been searching since late July or August. They have done their research, they know the market, and they are not inspecting out of general curiosity. They are inspecting because they need to buy, and they need to settle before the year ends. Upgrading families are the most common profile: they want to be in a new home before the school year rolls around again. Investors looking to add before the market tightens further are also active early. And a significant share of September buyers are relocating from interstate or overseas, working to a firm arrival date.

What they have in common is that they have already made their decision to buy. They just have not found the right property yet.

This has direct implications for campaign strategy. A September listing does not need to spend three weeks building profile awareness in the buyer pool. The awareness is already there. What it needs is presentation that justifies the decision a committed buyer is ready to make. That means photography, styling and pricing that signal quality without inviting negotiation on the fundamentals.

The September buyer who walks through your home on day one and says nothing is often writing an offer by day four. The campaign that reads them correctly is the one that comes to market clean, priced to the evidence, and ready to move at the speed they are moving.
