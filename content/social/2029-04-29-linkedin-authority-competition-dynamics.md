## Caption

The difference between a competitive result and an average one in Brisbane's inner east almost never comes down to the property itself. It comes down to whether the campaign generated genuine competition between buyers.

Competition does not happen by accident. It is the product of preparation, timing, and positioning. A well-presented property that launches at the right point in the campaign season, with a clear price guide that attracts rather than deters, will draw multiple qualified buyers. When two or more of those buyers want the same property, the result takes care of itself.

The reverse is equally true. A property that launches with visible deferred maintenance, an aggressive price guide that prices out the most motivated buyer segment, or poor campaign photography will reduce its own buyer pool before the first open home. A pool of one is not a negotiation. It is a take-it-or-leave-it conversation.

Most of what determines whether an inner east campaign produces competition is decided in the four to six weeks before the property is listed. The presentation decisions, the pricing conversation, the photography brief. By the time the first open home happens, the result is largely set.

The question worth asking before listing: does this campaign give the buyer pool a reason to compete?
