## Caption

Murarrie's residential pocket is doing something interesting. As the inner east suburbs around it have become harder to enter at any price, the value case for Murarrie has been strengthening consistently, and buyers who've been watching it have started moving.

The Gateway Motorway access is a genuine practical advantage for buyers who commute east or south. The Trade Coast employment hub nearby creates a natural rental demand base that investors have been aware of for years. The residential streets are separated from the industrial precinct, and the distinction matters more than the address sometimes suggests.

The buyer profile coming into Murarrie now includes two distinct groups. Investors who see rental yield and capital growth potential as surrounding suburbs push median prices higher. And owner-occupiers, often younger buyers, who want to be in the inner east address range without spending the premium that Morningside or Cannon Hill requires. Both groups are currently active.

Character homes that have been maintained well sell with less friction than the market average. Properties in need of work still find buyers, but the investor profile there tends to negotiate hard on price given carrying costs.
