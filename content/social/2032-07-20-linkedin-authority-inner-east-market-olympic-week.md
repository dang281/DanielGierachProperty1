## Caption

Three days until the Opening Ceremony. Here is an honest read on where the inner east market sits in Olympic week.

The city is genuinely distracted. Open home attendance reflects that. Properties that were well-priced and well-presented before the Games are still generating enquiry and some are transacting. Properties that came to market overpriced are sitting.

The pattern is consistent with what you would expect: the Games have not created new market dynamics, they have amplified the ones that already existed. Accurate pricing still sells. Overpricing still waits.

Days on market are longer for properties that needed a price correction mid-campaign. That is not an Olympic effect, it is a pricing effect that the quieter period has made more visible.

What is worth noting about the transactions that are happening right now: the buyers committing this week are getting access they would not get in September. Fewer competing buyers, more time with the agent and the vendor, and in some cases a vendor who is genuinely motivated to transact before the distraction peaks.

Sellers who are patient and have presented their property well are finding those buyers. The volume is lower. The quality of the buyer conversation is higher.

September will bring depth back to the market. The question for each seller is whether waiting for that depth gives them a better outcome than the access they have to motivated buyers right now.
