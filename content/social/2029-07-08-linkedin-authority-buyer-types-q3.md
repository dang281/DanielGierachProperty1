## Caption

The buyer pool in Brisbane's inner east is not the same in July as it is in March. The makeup shifts meaningfully through the year, and understanding who is actively buying in Q3 tells you a great deal about how to run a campaign, price a property, and manage expectations.

Right now, the dominant group at inner east open homes is upgraders. Most of them have either already sold their previous home or are under contract with a finance or building and pest condition ticking down. They are not speculating. They have equity confirmed and a settlement date in mind. These buyers move fast when the property is right, and they are not afraid of a competitive offer environment.

The second group is investors who have done their end-of-financial-year review and are reallocating capital. July brings them into the market with a clearer picture of their tax position and borrowing capacity than they had in June. They tend to focus on established houses in flood-free positions with strong rental histories, and they will pay up for certainty.

The third group is families aiming to be settled before the new school year. For suburbs in strong state school catchments, this creates a genuinely urgent timeline. A buyer who needs to be in a catchment zone by the end of January does not have the luxury of watching and waiting through spring.

Knowing this, sellers who are considering a July or August campaign are not entering a slow market. They are entering a market with a concentrated, motivated, ready-to-act buyer pool. That combination produces results.
