## Caption

December buyers in Brisbane's inner east are not the people who spent October attending 15 opens on a Saturday morning. That crowd has either bought, paused, or shifted their attention to January. The person inspecting a property in December is someone different.

They are often relocating for work, with a start date in February that makes January settlement a genuine need rather than a preference. They may be a buyer who missed out on the right property in spring and has recalibrated. Or they are someone who has recently sold and is under time pressure to commit to their next home before the new year.

What these buyers share is motivation. They are not browsing. They have a reason to move, they are pre-approved, and they are not waiting for more options. The December buyer pool is smaller, but the intent-to-purchase rate is notably higher than most other months.

For vendors, this changes how a campaign should be framed. A December listing does not need to attract 80 groups through the door. It needs to reach the right 10. That means targeted digital reach, a clear property narrative, and an agent who knows how to identify and communicate with buyer categories that are active right now.

The December market in the inner east is not a write-off. It is just a different conversation.
