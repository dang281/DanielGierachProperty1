## Caption

Bulimba is one of Brisbane's most consistently sought-after inner suburbs, and the reasons are not difficult to understand. Oxford Street gives the suburb a genuine village feel, with independent cafes, restaurants and specialty shops creating a street life that residents value deeply and that buyers factor into their purchasing decisions. At five kilometres from the CBD, the combination of urban amenity and residential character is genuinely difficult to find.

Waterfront access along the river is a defining feature for properties near the foreshore, and the CityCat ferry at Oxford Street provides a scenic and practical commute into the city. Bulimba State School is among the more sought-after primary catchments in Brisbane's inner east, which drives strong owner-occupier demand from families planning ahead for their children's schooling.

Current medians in Bulimba sit at the premium end of the inner east market. The suburb does not offer bargains, and buyers understand that. What they are paying for is a combination of lifestyle, school catchment, community quality and river proximity that comparable suburbs do not replicate. Premium character homes on good blocks attract the highest prices, particularly those within walking distance of Oxford Street.

Turnover is moderate; many owners stay for a long time. When quality stock does come to the market, it draws strong interest from buyers who have been watching and waiting.

If you own in Bulimba and are considering whether now is the right time to sell, I would welcome the conversation.
