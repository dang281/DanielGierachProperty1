## Caption

The Cannon Hill Esplanade is one of the inner east's quieter secrets: a river-access strip that puts morning walks along the Brisbane River within reach of a suburb that also connects directly to the Gateway Motorway. That combination of lifestyle and logistics is rare, and it is a consistent part of why buyers target Cannon Hill specifically rather than settling for it.

Morningside State School catchment draws families reliably, and the café culture developing around the Cannon Hill shopping precinct has added a day-to-day livability that the suburb lacked a decade ago. The mix of housing stock is broad: character Queenslanders sit alongside solid post-war homes and newer builds, which means buyers at different price points and with different renovation appetites can all find something viable.

The development pipeline in Cannon Hill is active. That brings risk for some buyers but opportunity for sellers, as new supply often validates and lifts surrounding land values over time. Established homes on good blocks in established streets have held their appeal precisely because they offer something new builds cannot replicate.
