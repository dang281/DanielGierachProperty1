## Caption

Mt Gravatt has the kind of positioning that infrastructure planners dream about: Gateway Motorway access to the north and south, a substantial commercial and retail strip along the main road, and family residential streets running off both sides. For buyers who need access to the motorway network, the suburb's location is genuinely hard to match at comparable price points.

The residential character in Mt Gravatt is family-oriented and practical. The housing stock is predominantly from the 1970s through to the 90s, with a solid proportion of brick homes that have aged well and respond strongly to sensible renovation investment. Elevated blocks in parts of the suburb offer views and cross-ventilation. ⚠️ VERIFY

The school catchment story in Mt Gravatt is a practical one. Several catchment zones intersect the suburb, and buyers with school-age children typically research these carefully before shortlisting. For vendors, understanding which catchment your property falls in, and whether that catchment is actively sought by buyers, matters for how you position the property.

What I observe consistently in Mt Gravatt is that well-maintained properties at sensible price points move quickly. The suburb attracts motivated buyers who have done their research on comparable sales and are ready to act when the right property appears.

If your property has been well looked after, 2033 is a reasonable time to understand what the market is prepared to pay for it.
