## Caption

Most people asking when to sell a property are really asking about the calendar. Spring is popular. Autumn is solid. Winter is written off. December barely gets a mention.

The problem with this framing is that it puts all the focus on market timing and almost none on preparation, and preparation is the variable that actually matters.

In Brisbane's inner east, the difference in outcome between a well-prepared campaign and a rushed one is regularly in the range of $50,000 to $150,000. That gap is not driven by which month the property launches. It is driven by whether the presentation was right, whether the marketing was targeted correctly, and whether the agent had enough time to build the campaign before day one.

A vendor who is ready in July and lists in August will almost always outperform a vendor who waits until September hoping for spring lift but does not have the property or the campaign in shape when they go to market.

The season matters at the margin. Preparation is the main event.

I have written a full article on this topic, covering what "ready to sell" actually means, how to build a preparation timeline, and why the vendors who move when they are prepared, rather than when the calendar says so, tend to achieve the best results.
