## Caption

The December property market in Brisbane's inner east is smaller than October and November, but it is not the market most people imagine when they say "nothing sells in December."

The buyers who are active in early to mid-December are among the most committed buyers of the year. They have been searching since September. They know what properties are worth. They have their finance in order. They have not stepped away from the search because the calendar year is ending, because for them the timing matters: a property settled in January means moving in January, which for interstate families means being in before the school year starts.

The December buyer is also often the upgrade buyer who has been watching one specific suburb and one specific property type and has not yet found the right one. When a property hits the market in December that matches, they respond.

What the December market does not have is the casual browsing buyer. The window shoppers who added themselves to mailing lists in spring and attended a dozen open homes without real intent are not here in December.

For sellers who have been sitting on the decision: the window is genuinely short now. A campaign launched in the first two weeks of December can still deliver a strong result. A campaign launched in the third week of December is effectively running into the Christmas break and relaunching in January.
