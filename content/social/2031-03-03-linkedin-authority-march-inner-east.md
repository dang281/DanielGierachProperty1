## Caption

March is the most concentrated month in the Brisbane inner east selling calendar. Buyer numbers are at their autumn peak. Pre-approvals that were issued in December and January are approaching their expiry dates, which adds urgency to buyer decision-making. The Easter break is visible on the horizon, and buyers who want to be settled before mid-year are aware that their window is narrowing.

What characterises a good March auction or offer process in the inner east is genuine competition between qualified buyers. That competition does not appear by accident. It is the product of a campaign that launched early enough to build a buyer list across two or three open homes before the property goes to contract or under the hammer. The buyers who compete hardest on a March auction day are usually the ones who inspected in week one, came back in week two, and arrived at auction having already decided this was the one. Their urgency is real because they have been through the process before.

For vendors, converting March buyer activity into an offer requires two things to be in order. The pricing conversation has to be resolved, meaning the vendor has to be aligned with their agent on a number that reflects the current evidence, not a ceiling they read from a listing portal. And the disclosure documents have to be ready: a serious buyer in March will want to have their solicitor review the contract quickly, and delays at that point lose buyers who have other options.

March is not the only time to sell in the inner east. But it is the time when prepared vendors tend to see the best concentration of ready buyers.
