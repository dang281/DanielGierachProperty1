## Caption

The most common property mistake I see buyers make is not the price they pay. It is the sequence in which they engage their legal representative.

Most buyers find a property, fall in love with it, sign the contract, and then call a solicitor to "check it over." By the time the solicitor is reading the document, the buyer has already agreed to every clause in it.

In Queensland, a solicitor or conveyancer should be your first call, not your second. Before you make an offer. Certainly before you sign.

The practical difference matters. A solicitor reviewing a contract before signing will pick up missing inclusions that seemed obvious but were never written down. They will identify finance condition deadlines that are shorter than a lender needs to formally approve. They will spot special conditions that shift risk from vendor to buyer in ways most people would not notice without legal training.

The distinction between a solicitor and a conveyancer also matters in Queensland. For straightforward purchases, either can handle the transaction well. For anything more complex, a solicitor's broader legal training is worth the difference in cost.
