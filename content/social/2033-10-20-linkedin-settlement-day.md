## Caption

Most of the anxiety vendors have about settlement day is about not knowing what to expect. What actually happens is straightforward.

In Queensland, settlement is handled digitally via PEXA. The vendor does not attend. There is no dramatic handover moment, no signatures exchanged across a table. For most vendors, the first confirmation they get is a text message from their solicitor saying it is done. After months of preparation, the actual settlement is usually anticlimactic in the best possible way.

The preparation in the weeks before settlement is where vendors genuinely need to focus. The pre-settlement inspection is the buyer's final check that the property is in the same condition as when the contract was signed. This is not a formality. I have seen sales go to the wire over inclusions that were listed in the contract but were not present at inspection, over damage that occurred during the move-out, and over a condition issue that was minor but was not there at contract signing. The buyer has the right to address these things before funds are released, and exercising that right can delay or complicate what should be a clean finish.

The most common settlement complications are physical, not financial. Removalist damage. A light fitting listed as an inclusion that is missing. A gate that was working fine six weeks ago and is not now. Getting these details right is entirely within the vendor's control, and doing so makes settlement day exactly what it should be: uneventful.
