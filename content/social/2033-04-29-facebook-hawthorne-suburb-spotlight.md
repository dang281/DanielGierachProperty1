## Caption

Hawthorne is one of the inner east's most tightly held suburbs, and that characteristic is not accidental. At four kilometres from the CBD, residents enjoy a position that combines genuine proximity to the city with a residential streetscape that feels insulated from the urban intensity of the inner core. The combination is rare, and buyers who understand Brisbane's geography pursue Hawthorne properties with corresponding determination.

The housing stock is dominated by character homes on generous blocks, a legacy of the suburb's early development that has been carefully maintained through consistent renovation and reinvestment. Original homes in good condition attract strong interest, as do fully renovated properties that have preserved period features while delivering contemporary amenity. Buyers are typically professional couples, established families and downsizers with the means to compete at this price point.

Current medians in Hawthorne sit at the premium tier for the inner east. The suburb does not offer entry-level buying, and long-term owners in Hawthorne will often find that even modest properties have appreciated substantially over their period of ownership.

Proximity to Bulimba's Oxford Street and to New Farm's café culture means residents have access to two of Brisbane's most active food and retail precincts. Bus services connect the suburb to the CBD, and cycling infrastructure along the river corridor is a practical option for the commute.
