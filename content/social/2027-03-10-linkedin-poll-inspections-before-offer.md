## Caption

Buying a property involves a research period that looks very different for different buyers.

Some buyers attend dozens of open homes over six to twelve months before they find the right property and feel confident enough to make an offer. Others make an offer on the second property they inspect. Neither approach is inherently right or wrong. What matters is whether you have enough information to make a confident decision at the price you are offering.

In the inner east, the buyers who tend to do best at auction are the ones who have attended a significant number of open homes before they bid. They understand what properties are actually selling for versus what they are listed at. They know which streets carry a premium and which do not. That knowledge converts into conviction on the day.

How many properties did you inspect before making your first offer?
