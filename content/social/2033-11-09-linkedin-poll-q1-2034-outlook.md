## Caption

The first quarter of the year in Brisbane's inner east historically carries pent-up demand from buyers who held back through December and January. The combination of the established infrastructure investment legacy from the 2032 Olympics period, continued strong migration patterns into South East Queensland, and buyer groups who have been searching through 2033 creates a Q1 that is rarely quiet. The question is whether that demand is matched by adequate supply. More listings in Q1 means buyers have more options and less urgency. Fewer listings means the motivated buyers from 2033 are competing for whatever comes to market. Both scenarios are possible, and which one plays out will depend partly on how many vendors decide to wait versus how many list early. What is your read on where the inner east is heading in Q1 2034?
