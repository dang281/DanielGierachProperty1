## Caption

Mt Gravatt's appeal rests on a combination of anchors that produce broad and consistent buyer demand. Garden City's retail and dining footprint is one of Brisbane's most complete, and its position within the suburb gives residents the everyday convenience that buyers with families weight heavily. Griffith University's employment and student population creates a sustained rental market alongside the owner-occupier market, and the suburb's position on the escarpment delivers a spaciousness that inner-city suburbs cannot match.

The buyer profile in Mt Gravatt reflects that breadth of appeal. Families are drawn by the schooling options, the practical convenience, and the block sizes. Investors pursue the dual rental market. Downsizers find the suburb's mix of apartment and house options practical as they look for lower-maintenance alternatives to larger family homes. Each group sustains demand from a different angle, which is part of what gives Mt Gravatt its market resilience.

The spring market in Mt Gravatt tends to bring the full spread of these buyer profiles to open homes, creating the competitive conditions that well-prepared and accurately priced properties benefit from. Properties that arrive underprepared or overpriced meet a buyer pool that knows the comparable sales evidence well enough to walk away.
