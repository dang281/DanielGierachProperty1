## Caption

Most vendors find out their property has settled when they receive a text message from their solicitor.

Settlement day in Queensland is handled digitally through the PEXA platform. The vendor does not attend. The buyer does not attend. The solicitors on both sides complete the transfer electronically, the bank releases the funds, and the title changes hands.

What this means in practice is that settlement day is rarely the day things go wrong. The problems that arise on settlement day are almost always caused by something that should have been identified and resolved in the weeks before.

The pre-settlement inspection reveals that an inclusion listed in the contract is missing. A dishwasher the buyer assumed was included is gone. A fence that was in good condition at contract signing has been damaged during the move-out. The mortgage discharge is delayed because the lender was not notified immediately when the offer was accepted.

Each of these is manageable if it comes up with a week to spare. It is significantly harder to manage on settlement morning when both solicitors are trying to complete the transaction by 4pm.

For vendors preparing to settle, the article at the link below walks through the complete process and the preparation steps that make settlement day straightforward rather than stressful.
