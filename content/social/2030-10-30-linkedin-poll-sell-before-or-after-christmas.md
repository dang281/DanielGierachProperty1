## Caption

One of the most consistent questions I get from inner east homeowners in October and November is whether to launch before Christmas or wait for the February market.

Both have genuine arguments in their favour. Pre-Christmas means motivated buyers with a hard settlement deadline. February means a fresh buyer pool, post-holiday clarity, and a full autumn season ahead. The right answer depends on the property, the vendor's timeline, and how prepared the home is right now.

It is genuinely a question of trade-offs rather than a single right answer. Curious what people's instinct is.

If you were selling your inner east home, which would you choose?
