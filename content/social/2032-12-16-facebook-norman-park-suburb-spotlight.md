## Caption

Norman Park buyers are a patient group. Many of them have been watching the suburb for six months or more before they find what they are looking for, and that careful searching reflects something real about the area: it is specific, it is sought-after, and the right properties do not appear on a schedule.

Whites Hill Reserve gives the suburb a green boundary that sets it apart from more hemmed-in inner-east postcodes. The dual school catchments are well known among the buyers circling here, and that knowledge shapes how seriously they engage when a property finally comes to market. There is rarely a casual offer in Norman Park.

December marks the point where buyers who have been waiting all year make a calculation. Summer is here, the new year is approaching, and autumn 2033 is closer than it feels. Some of the most motivated purchasers in this market are the ones who arrive at an open home in mid-December, because they have already decided they are done waiting. For owners thinking about early-year timing, listing before the January pause means your property is the one those buyers see.

If Norman Park is where you plan to sell in 2033, starting the conversation now is the most useful thing you can do before the holidays.
