## Caption

Most vendors think a property campaign starts when the listing goes live. The agents who get the best results know it starts four to eight weeks before that.

The preparation phase is where the price is made. Styling decisions, photography scheduling, building and pest reports for vendor disclosure, Form 6 review, method of sale discussions, comparable sales analysis, all of this happens before a single buyer walks through the door. Vendors who rush this phase and launch before they are ready usually pay for it in the result.

Photography is the most underestimated item. In the inner east, the majority of buyer research happens online before anyone attends an open home. The photographs are the campaign's first impression. Properties with strong photography attract more enquiry, more attendees, and more competition at open homes. Better competition produces better results.

The timeline that works is roughly this: four to six weeks of preparation, photography and listing material finalised, launch on a Wednesday or Thursday so the first weekend has full online exposure, two or three open homes over the first two weekends while buyer interest is highest, then offer and negotiation.

The first two weekends are critical. Buyer interest peaks at launch and does not come back at the same level. A campaign that has not generated offers by weekend three is usually dealing with a preparation or pricing problem that should have been solved before launch.
