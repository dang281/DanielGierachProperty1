## Caption

Most vendors think a property campaign starts when the listing goes live. The agents who get the best results know it starts four to six weeks before that.

Photography is the most underestimated decision in a campaign. In the inner east, the majority of buyer research happens online before anyone attends an open home. The photographs are the campaign's first impression. Properties with strong photography attract more enquiry, more attendees, and more competition at open homes. Better competition produces better results. This is not a minor effect.

Buyers in active suburbs have been watching specific streets and specific floor plans for months before a property appears on realestate.com.au. An agent working that patch actively knows which buyers are primed for a particular property before it is listed. That intelligence shapes the launch price, the method of sale, and the timing. It is not available when a vendor signs and rushes to photographs the next week.

The first two weekends are the most valuable in any campaign. Buyer interest peaks at launch and does not return at the same level. A property that reaches the market under-prepared does not get a second first impression. The correction, when it comes, costs more than the preparation would have.

That is the conversation to have before you pick a launch date.
