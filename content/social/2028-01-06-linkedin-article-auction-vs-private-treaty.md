## Caption

The method of sale is one of the first decisions a seller makes, and most get it wrong by defaulting to what their neighbour did or what the agent prefers.

Auction creates unconditional competition on a fixed date. It works well when there are multiple buyers who cannot agree on price without bidding against each other. It fails when the buyer pool is thin or the property appeals to a very specific type of purchaser.

Private treaty gives buyers conditions and time to arrange finance and inspections. It works well when the right buyer needs reassurance to commit. It can stall if the price guide is off and the first few weeks of enquiry go quiet.

Expression of interest is less common but effective for unique properties where the range of buyer values is genuinely wide.

The question to ask before choosing is not "what did the last three houses in this street use?" The question is: who is the likely buyer for this property, and what does that buyer need to commit?
