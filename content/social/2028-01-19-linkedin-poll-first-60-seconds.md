## Caption

Buyers often describe knowing within the first minute of walking through a property whether it has a chance. The paperwork, the comparable sales, the due diligence all comes later. But the emotional decision usually happens fast.

As someone who spends a lot of time at open homes, I find the first 60 seconds tells me a lot about what a buyer is going to do.

I am curious what others notice first.

When you walk through a property for the first time, what do you decide in the first 60 seconds?

Daniel Gierach
