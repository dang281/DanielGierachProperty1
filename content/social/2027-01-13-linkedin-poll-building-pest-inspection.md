## Caption

The building and pest clause is standard in Queensland contracts. Most buyers use it. Some waive it in competitive markets to make their offer more attractive.

A clear building and pest report takes roughly three to five business days and costs $400 to $700. What you find can change the deal significantly: active termite damage, structural movement, drainage issues that are not visible at an open home.

The argument for waiving it: in a multiple-offer situation, an unconditional offer carries real weight with a seller. The argument against: you are taking on unknown risk without knowing the full picture of what you are buying.

In the inner east, older character homes in Hawthorne, Bulimba, and Norman Park can carry surprises that a good inspector will identify. New builds or recently completed renovations carry a different risk profile.

Would you ever waive the building and pest inspection to secure a property?
