## Caption

Most disputes on settlement day trace back to the pre-settlement inspection, not the contract.

Buyers are entitled to one inspection before settlement to confirm the property is in the same condition as when contracts were exchanged. That is a statutory right, not a courtesy. And sellers who treat it as a formality sometimes find themselves in a last-minute dispute at the worst possible moment.

The most common problems: removalist damage that happened after the contract date, inclusions that were removed without agreement, and garden or pool maintenance that was let go during the settlement period.

The fix is straightforward. Walk through the property yourself two to three days before settlement with a phone and take photos. Note what was included in the contract and confirm it is still there. If the removalists are coming, do a second walkthrough after they leave.

A buyer who raises a legitimate defect before settlement has leverage. The same defect raised on settlement day, when the vendor has already moved out, is far messier and more expensive to resolve.

The full guide for sellers on what to expect and how to prepare is at danielgierach.com/insights/pre-settlement-inspection-seller-guide-qld.
