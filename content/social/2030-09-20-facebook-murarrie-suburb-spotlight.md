## Caption

Murarrie has undergone a meaningful transition over the past decade. What was primarily an industrial and logistics suburb has seen a growing residential component take shape, driven by buyers priced out of Cannon Hill and Morningside who recognise the infrastructure position of the suburb. The Gateway Motorway and Logan Motorway interchange puts virtually any corner of Brisbane within a manageable drive, and that access story is not going away.

New residential developments have added volume to the suburb, but the established home stock on dedicated residential streets is where the most motivated buyers are looking. These buyers want the inner east address and the access without the character-home price tag, and Murarrie delivers that combination. Families and professionals who need motorway access for work, particularly those who split commutes between the CBD and industrial corridors, have been consistently active here.

The suburb's transition is still underway, which means sellers who act while the development tailwind is present have a market that is paying close attention. Accurately priced, well-presented homes have moved quickly here in recent selling seasons, and the supply side remains manageable.

If you own in Murarrie and have been considering a sale, the suburb's access and value proposition are drawing buyers in numbers that the listing supply has not always kept up with.
