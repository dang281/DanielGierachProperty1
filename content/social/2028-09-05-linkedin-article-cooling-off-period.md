## Caption

The cooling off period in Queensland is a buyer right. It cannot be waived by the seller, it cannot be negotiated away in the contract, and it does not apply at all to contracts formed at auction.

That last point is the one that catches buyers off guard. At an auction, the contract is unconditional from the fall of the hammer. There is no finance condition, no building and pest condition, and no cooling off period. The buyer is committed the moment they sign. Which is why completing a building inspection before auction day and having finance pre-approval in place is not a nice-to-have for auction buyers, it is essential.

For private treaty purchases, the cooling off period runs for five business days from the date the buyer receives the signed contract. To exit during that period costs the buyer 0.25 per cent of the purchase price. That is $2,500 on a $1 million purchase. Not zero, but significantly less than the alternative of proceeding with a property you have decided you no longer want.

What sellers sometimes misunderstand is that the cooling off period cannot be used as a negotiating tool. A seller who tells a buyer they will accept the offer only if the buyer waives cooling off is asking for something the law does not permit the buyer to give.
