## Caption

ANZAC Day creates a natural break in the autumn campaign calendar that most sellers do not plan around.

The long weekend draws buyers away from open homes. Volume drops, families travel, and the property market effectively pauses for a few days. Vendors who launch their campaign in the week before ANZAC Day often do so with reduced open home traffic at the critical first-weekend stage, when first impressions matter most.

The better approach is to launch after the long weekend, not before. Buyers return refreshed and, in many cases, more decisive. The first open home after ANZAC Day regularly outperforms the last open home before it.

There is a related consideration for vendors already mid-campaign. If your first open home was before the long weekend and you saw lighter numbers than expected, do not read too much into it. Week two is the one to watch.

The rhythm of the inner east autumn campaign season has two natural peaks: the weeks before Easter and the weeks in May before the school holiday disruptions start. Fitting a campaign into those peaks, rather than around them, consistently produces better results.

If you are planning to list this autumn, the May window is still open. There is time to prepare properly rather than rush to catch the end of April.
