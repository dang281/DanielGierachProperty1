## Caption

Carina offers something that is increasingly rare in Brisbane's inner east: land. Blocks that give families room to extend, room for a pool, room to grow into the property rather than out of it within five years. When you compare what that land costs in Carina against comparable sizes in Camp Hill or Norman Park, the gap is still meaningful.

The suburb sits in a practical position. Westfield Carindale is minutes away for day-to-day convenience. The Belmont Hills corridor to the east provides topographic variety and some of the larger elevated sections. Creek frontage appears in parts of the suburb, adding a natural boundary and green amenity that is hard to replicate. School catchments here connect into the inner east network and families buying in Carina are often making a deliberate choice: they want the inner east address, the land size, and the Carindale shopping access in one package.

The buyer profile in Carina is broad. Young families buying their second home, upgraders who started in Murarrie or Cannon Hill, and investors watching the corridor from Carina through to Chandler. Demand has been consistent precisely because the suburb delivers value relative to its neighbours without compromising on the fundamentals.

If you own in Carina and want to understand where the market sits heading into 2031, the conversation is worth having now.
