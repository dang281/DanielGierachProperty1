## Caption

East Brisbane sits roughly three kilometres from the CBD, and that proximity shapes everything about the suburb's market. Buyers here are overwhelmingly professionals who want to walk or cycle to work, reach the city in under ten minutes by car, or simply live close to the cultural and dining infrastructure that concentrates in and around the inner ring. Walkability scores in East Brisbane are among the highest of any suburb on this side of the river.

The housing mix is genuinely diverse: terrace houses on narrow lots, elevated Queenslanders, and modern builds that have appeared as the suburb has densified. Balmoral State School is the primary drawcard for families, and its catchment adds a consistent family buyer layer on top of the professional market. Entry prices sit at the higher end of the inner east range, which reflects the suburb's genuine scarcity of supply relative to the depth of demand.

Stock volumes in East Brisbane have historically been low. Owners tend to hold for extended periods, and that means when a well-presented property does come to market, it arrives in front of a buyer pool that has often been waiting for something to appear. That supply-demand tension typically produces competitive campaign conditions.

If you own in East Brisbane and want to understand what the current market looks like for your property type, I am glad to have that conversation.
