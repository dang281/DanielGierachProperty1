## Caption

Mt Gravatt sits on a plateau that gives it two things Brisbane families consistently seek: elevation above the flood plain and space. The blocks here are generous, and the suburb has maintained a character feel with tree-lined streets that many closer-in suburbs have lost to infill development.

The Cavendish Road State High School catchment covers parts of Mt Gravatt, and that school zone remains one of the most reliable demand signals in the inner south-east. Families who do their homework on state secondary schools frequently end up in this corridor, and they are prepared to pay for the right address.

Garden City is the retail anchor for daily life in Mt Gravatt, and it handles the job well. The convenience of having a major centre close by without the suburb itself being dominated by commercial activity strikes a balance that residents appreciate and buyers notice.

What Mt Gravatt does particularly well is hold its position across market cycles. It is not the suburb that makes dramatic headlines, but owners here have consistently seen their property values supported by genuine underlying demand. That combination of space, schooling, and flood-free land is not going out of fashion.
