## Caption

With three years to go until the Brisbane 2032 Olympics, the infrastructure picture for the inner east is becoming clearer.

The Cross River Rail network is operational and has already changed access patterns for suburbs like Woolloongabba and the broader inner east corridor. The venue precincts around the Gabba and Chandler have been progressively upgraded. What started as a planning exercise is now a visible, physical reality for people buying and selling in this part of the city.

The impact on property values is uneven. Suburbs within easy access of upgraded transport corridors and venues have seen the infrastructure premium already priced in. For buyers expecting a surge in the final twelve months before the Games, the historical pattern from other Olympic cities suggests the growth typically front-runs the event, not follows it.

What the 2032 build-out has done more reliably is cement Brisbane's position as an infrastructure-serious city. The investment in transport, stadiums, and public space across the inner east has been substantial. For long-hold inner east owners, that is a foundation that does not disappear after the closing ceremony.

The more interesting question now is what happens to prices in the decade after 2032. The cities that have benefited most from Olympic investment are the ones that had strong underlying demand before the Games started. Brisbane clearly qualifies.
