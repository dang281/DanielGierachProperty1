## Caption

Mid-autumn is when Coorparoo shows its best side. The humidity of summer has passed, the light is soft, and the streets off Logan Road have a comfortable suburban quality that photographs well and presents better. It is also the period when the suburb's established buyer pool is most active, and the competition between them for well-presented properties tends to produce the year's strongest auction results.

The federation home character that defines parts of Coorparoo is genuinely rare in Brisbane's inner east. These homes, built during the late nineteenth and early twentieth century, have a solidity and a street presence that newer construction cannot replicate. Buyers who are specifically looking for this housing type are prepared to pay for it, and in Coorparoo they find a concentration of it that is not matched in many other inner east suburbs. ⚠️ VERIFY

The Stones Corner precinct at the eastern edge of the suburb adds a dining and bar culture that has attracted a younger owner-occupier demographic to complement the longer-established family buyer base. This broadening of the buyer pool over recent years has been good for vendor outcomes, as more buyer types competing for the same stock creates more competition.

For a Coorparoo vendor approaching this autumn with a well-prepared property, the market conditions are as favourable as they have been in recent years. The preparation work done in January makes itself felt in March.
