## Caption

Seven Hills does not compete on the same terms as the character-home precincts of Bulimba and Hawthorne, and it is stronger for not trying to. The suburb's value proposition is land: large lots on elevated terrain, views where the orientation allows it, and quiet streets that deliver a suburban quality of life that the denser inner precincts simply cannot offer at any price.

The buyers who arrive at Seven Hills have typically worked through a process of elimination rather than a process of aspiration. They have considered the closer-in suburbs, concluded that the lot sizes do not meet their requirements for the price, and identified Seven Hills as the suburb where the land arithmetic works. That buyer is usually well-informed, has done a substantive amount of research, and tends to know the suburb's streets better than buyers who are browsing casually.

Renovation potential is a theme that recurs in Seven Hills buyer conversations. The elevated positions, particularly on properties where the existing building does not yet capture the available view, represent an opportunity to add value through a well-planned addition that a flat block in a lower suburb cannot replicate. Buyers with a renovation lens will find that Seven Hills rewards a careful survey of what is possible on the lot.

In winter, the suburb's elevated position makes the cool mornings notable. Properties with north-facing living areas make the most of the winter sun, and buyers who have lived in elevated Brisbane suburbs know to look for that orientation.
