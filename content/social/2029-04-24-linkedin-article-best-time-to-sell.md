## Caption

Almost every piece of mainstream property advice about when to sell is built around Melbourne and Sydney. Spring in the southern states is the peak campaign season because the cold winter genuinely suppresses buyer activity. Brisbane does not have that winter.

The practical implication is that autumn, specifically the period from late February to early May, is consistently the strongest campaign window for Brisbane's inner east. Buyer motivation is high, the competitive pool is intact, and the post-summer sense of purpose drives decision-making in a way that September openings do not always replicate.

Spring in the inner east is still a viable campaign window. But vendors who wait for September because they have heard it is peak season are often surprised to find that the buyer pool they were hoping for arrived and moved on in April.

The other variable that rarely gets discussed is preparation. A well-prepared home launched in autumn almost always outperforms a rushed campaign launched in spring. Season affects the buyer pool. Preparation affects the result within whatever buyer pool is available. Preparation matters more.

If you are sitting on a property you are considering selling and you are waiting for spring, it is worth asking whether that advice was written with Brisbane in mind.
