## Caption

Most Brisbane properties in the inner east have at least one easement on the title. Most owners don't know about it until they list.

An easement is a registered right allowing someone else to use part of your land for a specific purpose. Drainage easements, utility corridors for Energex or Unity Water, access strips for neighbouring lots: these appear on titles across Bulimba, Hawthorne, Camp Hill, and much of the inner east because the area was subdivided and serviced over many decades.

The practical reality: a drainage easement running along your back fence has almost no impact on a sale. Buyers in Brisbane see these routinely. Their solicitors review the title, note the easement, and move on.

The more significant question is when an easement runs through the usable part of the yard, restricts where you can build, or involves a neighbour's formal right to cross your property. Those need to be disclosed correctly and understood before going to market.

The rule in Queensland is simple: disclose what's on the title, and disclose any informal access arrangements you know about even if they're not registered. Your conveyancer handles the formal disclosure. Your job is to give them the full picture.

I've put together a plain-English guide to the types of easements common on Brisbane inner-east properties, what they mean for the sale process, and how buyers typically respond.
