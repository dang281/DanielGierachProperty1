## Caption

One detail about the Queensland cooling off period that most buyers and many sellers do not know until it becomes relevant: exiting within the five business days costs money.

The cooling off period is a statutory buyer right. The vendor cannot waive it in a private treaty contract. The buyer has five business days from the day after the contract is signed to exit without the seller's consent.

But it is not free. The exit fee is 0.25 per cent of the purchase price. On a $1.3 million purchase, that is $3,250.

Most people assume exiting during the cooling off period means walking away with no consequences. The five business days exist, the right to exit exists, but the fee is real and is deducted from the deposit before it is returned.

The other point most sellers do not realise: they cannot remove the cooling off period as a negotiating tool. A seller who wants certainty that a buyer will not exit in the first week after signing cannot get that through a private treaty contract. The statutory right exists regardless of what the contract says.

Auction contracts are different. A property sold at auction has no cooling off period at all. The buyer who signs at the fall of the hammer is unconditional from that moment.

Understanding which situation applies, and what the cost implications are, is the kind of thing worth knowing before the contract is signed rather than after.
