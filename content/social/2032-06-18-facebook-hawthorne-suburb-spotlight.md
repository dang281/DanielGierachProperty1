## Caption

Hawthorne runs along the river between Bulimba and Balmoral, and its compact geography is a significant part of its appeal. The suburb has a short main street on Hawthorne Road with a cinema, cafe strip, and wine bar that give it a self-contained quality that buyers from larger, less cohesive suburbs tend to find appealing when they first encounter it. That village scale, sitting between the river and the character housing on the ridge, shapes what buyers are actually competing for.

Streets like Amy Street, Wylie Street, and the river-end reaches of Hawthorne Road attract particular attention from buyers who have been watching the suburb and waiting for the right property to come to market. Pre-war and interwar Queenslanders with original detailing are the most contested property type, and well-presented examples on good-sized blocks regularly attract buyer fields that exceed vendor expectations.

The Hawthorne riverfront park and ferry terminal give residents direct access to the river and an alternative CBD route, which buyers factor into liveability assessments alongside the suburb's walkable amenity. Hawthorne State School serves the local family demographic, and the proximity to Anglican Church Grammar School at East Brisbane adds a private school dimension that influences buyer interest from further afield.
