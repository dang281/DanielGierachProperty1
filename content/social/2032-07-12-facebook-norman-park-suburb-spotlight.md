## Caption

Buyers who arrive in Norman Park have usually been in the inner east for a while. They have come through Camp Hill or Morningside with equity behind them, and they have arrived at a specific brief: a character Queenslander, a veranda, and a block large enough to genuinely work with.

Norman Park delivers that brief more consistently than almost anywhere else in the inner east.

Whites Hill Reserve anchors the suburb's eastern boundary, over 100 hectares of bushland that backs directly onto residential streets. That kind of green space access does not exist at this address point elsewhere in the inner east, and buyers who know the suburb understand what they are getting.

The housing stock skews heavily toward character Queenslanders, many of them elevated, on blocks that suit modern living. The Camp Hill retail strip on Old Cleveland Road is the suburb's western anchor for daily retail and hospitality. The school catchments for Norman Park State School and East Brisbane State School are regularly cited as a primary deciding factor for family buyers comparing Norman Park against Morningside or Coorparoo.

The premium over those suburbs reflects the elevation, the character stock, the reserve, and the school story. For owners in Norman Park, that premium is your position.
