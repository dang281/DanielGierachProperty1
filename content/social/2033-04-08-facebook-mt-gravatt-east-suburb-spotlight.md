## Caption

Mt Gravatt East offers something that is increasingly rare in Brisbane's inner and middle rings: genuine value for money within ten kilometres of the CBD. The suburb draws both families and investors who recognise that the price gap between here and suburbs closer to the city is not always justified by lifestyle outcomes alone.

Cavendish Road is the suburb's main commercial spine, with everyday retail, cafes and services all accessible without needing to travel far. The TAFE Queensland campus nearby supports a rental market that appeals to investors, while the mix of original and updated homes gives buyers at different price points a realistic entry path.

Families are drawn by the school options in the area and the straightforward access to Garden City at Mt Gravatt, a short drive away. The housing stock runs from unrenovated post-war homes with development potential through to modernised family residences ready to move into. Current medians in Mt Gravatt East tend to sit at a meaningful discount to comparable suburbs closer to the city, which is precisely what motivates buyers who have been watching the inner east market carefully.

Bus links connect the suburb to the CBD and surrounding centres. The streetscape is functional rather than boutique, but that practicality is part of the appeal for buyers focused on space and value over café-strip prestige.
