## Caption

August open homes are quieter than October open homes. That is not a problem. It is actually useful information.

The buyers who turn up in winter are not browsing. There is no crowd to get lost in, no sense of occasion or urgency created by a packed open home. A buyer in August is there because they need to be. They have done their research, they know the suburb, and they are close to a decision. That kind of motivation is not something you can manufacture by waiting for spring.

When multiple genuine buyers are attending the same property in August, that is a signal worth paying attention to. It does not mean the market is running hot in a headline sense. It means the pool of active, committed buyers is deep enough to generate real competition even without the spring crowd.

I see this pattern regularly in Brisbane's inner east. The properties that sell well in August are not selling in spite of the season. They are selling because the vendors and their agent read the buyer pool correctly and recognised that motivated buyers in winter are worth more than a bigger crowd in October who are still weighing up their options.

If you are sitting on a property and waiting for September because you assume that is when buyers appear, the buyers worth selling to may already be looking. That is the part of the market that does not make it into the weekend property sections.
