## Caption

Not everything in the inner east is selling equally well right now, and vendors deserve to know which side of that line their property sits on before they commit to a campaign.

What is selling: four-bedroom character homes in Hawthorne and Bulimba that have had functional updates to the kitchen and bathroom without losing the original features. These are moving quickly when priced correctly, often within three weeks of listing. The buyers are predominantly families upgrading from three-bedroom homes in the same or adjacent suburbs. They are equity-rich and have done the research.

Also selling well: smaller, well-located houses in Camp Hill and Coorparoo in the $1.2 to $1.7 million range that give first-generation buyers a foothold in the inner east. Competition at this price point has been consistent.

What is sitting: three-bedroom units in older complexes without car parking upgrades or recent body corporate work. Also sitting: anything presented as a renovation opportunity but priced as if the renovation has been done. The market is not currently paying a premium for potential.

The suburb breakdown matters because the conversations at kitchen tables in Morningside and Balmoral are different to those in Coorparoo. A blanket view of the inner east market doesn't help anyone make a good decision.

If you want to know specifically where your home fits in the current buyer demand picture, that is a conversation worth having directly.
