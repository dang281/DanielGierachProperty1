## Caption

Most advice about when to sell property in Australia is written for Melbourne and Sydney. In those markets, October is peak season: the gardens are at their best, auction clearance rates are high, and the pre-Christmas buyer pool is deep and motivated. The advice makes sense for those markets. It does not map directly onto Brisbane's inner east.

In the inner east, the autumn window from late February through to May is the most consistent and historically reliable selling period of the year. Spring is active and produces strong results. Autumn produces more of them, more reliably, and with less competition from other listings.

The reasons are practical. Brisbane's climate in autumn is genuinely pleasant rather than unpredictably hot, which affects both property presentation and buyer comfort during inspections. The autumn buyer pool includes buyers who have resolved their summer plans, have children settled into the new school year, and are ready to make a major decision. It also includes buyers who missed out on properties in the autumn window from the previous year and have been ready to move since. That accumulated demand is a real and measurable factor in autumn clearance rates.

Spring in the inner east works. October campaigns produce genuine results. But vendors who are deciding between a late October listing and holding to February should understand that they are not waiting for a worse selling window. In the inner east specifically, they may be waiting for a better one.
