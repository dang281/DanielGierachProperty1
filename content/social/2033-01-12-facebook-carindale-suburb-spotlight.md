## Caption

Carindale is built around Westfield Carindale, one of Brisbane's largest retail centres, and that anchors both the convenience and the community identity of the suburb. Families who move here know what they are choosing: good retail access, newer housing stock, and a quieter residential character that sits at the eastern fringe of the inner belt.

The housing in Carindale skews newer than most of the inner east. Brick homes from the 1980s and 90s, some more recent construction, and a mix of house types that suits the family demographic that has consistently chosen the suburb over the past two decades. Block sizes tend to be more generous than in closer-in suburbs, which is increasingly relevant as families look for functional outdoor space. ⚠️ VERIFY

The bushland surrounds give parts of Carindale a different feel from the more densely suburban pockets further west. The eastern edge of the suburb backs onto reserves and creek corridors that are valued by residents and that add a genuine green character to the streetscape in those pockets.

For vendors in 2033, the buyer profile in Carindale is typically family-led, practical, and focused on the school catchments and access to services. These buyers are doing their homework and they know the comparable sales. Pricing correctly and presenting well are the two things that consistently make the difference here.
