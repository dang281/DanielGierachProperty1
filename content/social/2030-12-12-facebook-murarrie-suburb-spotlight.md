## Caption

Murarrie's residential market in December enters a period of lower supply and a more targeted buyer pool, which historically works in favour of well-positioned homes on the elevated streets. The suburb's year-round advantage, Gateway Motorway access and large block availability, does not diminish in December.

The Murarrie industrial and commercial precinct to the north is the element that most first-time visitors to the suburb process and then file away. The residential streets on the high ground, approaching the Tingalpa Creek boundary and along Kianawah Road, have no meaningful interface with the commercial precinct. Buyers who attend opens in these streets find a quiet, elevated suburb with the city visible on the horizon and little suburban intrusion.

For buyers commuting to the airport or the Gold Coast corridor, Murarrie's direct Gateway access removes one of the friction points that affects daily life for people in comparable suburbs. The absence of the school-run traffic conflicts and the residential quiet of the higher streets are factors buyers who have lived here consistently raise when asked what they value about the suburb.

The Cannon Hill Anglican College, one suburb to the west, gives Murarrie families access to a prominent private school without the premium residential address that living immediately adjacent to the school would require.

For Murarrie homeowners on the elevated streets, December is a lower-volume but not inactive month.
