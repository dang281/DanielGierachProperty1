## Caption

Seasonal selling advice gets repeated so often that it starts to sound like fact. Spring is the best time. Avoid December and January. Winter is slow. Wait for the autumn window.

Some of that has a basis in data. Open home numbers in June and July are lower than in September and October. The Christmas and January period does see reduced buyer activity. These are real patterns.

But they are patterns in volume, not necessarily in quality or outcome. A well-presented, correctly-priced property in a strong inner east suburb attracts genuine buyers in most months of the year. The buyer who needs to purchase in July because of a lease expiry, a job relocation, or a family change is still a buyer. They are motivated and they are in the market regardless of what the calendar says.

The question is whether the conventional wisdom about timing is the primary variable for vendors, or whether preparation and pricing sit above it.

Is there such a thing as a bad time to sell a well-presented inner east property?

## Poll options
