## Caption

The buyers who are still active in November are not the ones browsing casually. By this point in the year, they have been looking since August or September. They know exactly what they want, they have their finance sorted, and they have a hard deadline: settled before Christmas.

That combination of motivation and urgency does not appear in a quiet autumn campaign. It is specific to this window.

Brisbane's inner east typically sees its most efficient auction clearance in the four weeks between late October and late November. Properties with realistic pricing and a well-prepared presentation are converting quickly because the buyer doing the inspecting has no time to play games. They are ready to buy.

The vendors who understand this tend to list in late October or early November with a four to five week campaign in mind, targeting a December settlement. The ones who hold off until February are not wrong, autumn is a strong market too. But they are trading a motivated, time-pressured buyer pool for a fresh one that is still forming its view of the market.

If your property is ready or close to ready, November is not a consolation prize for missing spring. It is its own window with its own advantages.
