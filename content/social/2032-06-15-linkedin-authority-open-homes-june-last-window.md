## Caption

The open homes I am running in June are telling me something useful about where buyer motivation sits right now.

Attendance is one indicator. Buyer quality is another. A well-attended open home with mostly curious neighbours and speculative first-timers is a different signal to a smaller group of pre-qualified, pre-approved buyers who have been searching for six months and are ready to act. In June 2032, the latter is more common than usual among the buyers coming through my properties.

The people attending opens in the inner east this month have typically made a decision: they want to be settled before the Games or they have accepted they will buy after them and are using this period for research. The first group is motivated and time-conscious. The second group is patient but attentive. Neither is a passive, just-looking open home browser.

For sellers, this is a meaningful distinction. If you are in a campaign right now, the buyers walking through your property in June are more likely to be serious than your average spring open home crowd. That is worth factoring into how you present, how you price, and how quickly you respond to follow-up enquiry.

For buyers, it is worth noting that the competition in June has not dropped away. It has concentrated.
