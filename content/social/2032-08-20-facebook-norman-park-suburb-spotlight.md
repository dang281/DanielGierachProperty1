## Caption

Whites Hill Reserve is one of the most underappreciated assets attached to any inner-east suburb. Over 100 hectares of bushland that backs directly onto residential streets, with walking and mountain bike trails accessible from your front gate. That kind of green space access does not exist at this address in most of Brisbane, and buyers who have walked the reserve understand what it means for the way you live day to day.

Norman Park's housing stock does the rest. Elevated Queenslanders, many of them original or partially renovated, on blocks that suit the way families actually use outdoor space. The suburb's terrain means most properties carry aspect, and many carry views.

Buyers who arrive in Norman Park have done their work. They have been through Morningside and Coorparoo. They have considered Camp Hill. They have arrived at Norman Park on merit: character, elevation, Whites Hill, a specific school catchment, and the Camp Hill retail strip as the daily lifestyle anchor.

Norman Park State School and East Brisbane State School are the catchment anchors for family buyers, and they are consistently cited as deciding factors for buyers comparing Norman Park against its neighbours.

The price premium over Morningside and Coorparoo reflects every one of those things. For owners in Norman Park, those factors are not incidental. They are the substance of your property's position in the market.
