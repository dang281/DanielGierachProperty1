## Caption

When people ask me about the state of the buyer pool in the inner east, I usually answer with what I am seeing at open homes rather than what any report is saying. Reports tell you what happened six weeks ago. Open homes tell you what is happening now.

Three or four genuine buyers at an inspection, by which I mean people who are pre-approved, have done multiple inspections in the area, and are asking questions that indicate a real intent to buy, is a competitive situation. You do not need ten registered bidders or twenty groups through an open home to have a strong result. You need the right two or three people at the right time.

One or two genuine buyers at an inspection does not mean a thin market. It means you need a different strategy: patient negotiation, correct pricing from the start, and an agent who can work that smaller pool to find the real ceiling. A soft open home that is poorly managed can leave money on the table even when the buyer is there.

The depth question that I watch closely is how quickly a well-priced property in a given suburb attracts a second and third interested party. That timeline varies significantly right now depending on price point and location within the inner east. Certain streets and property types are seeing multiple parties within the first week. Others are sitting for three to four weeks before a competitive situation develops. Knowing which category your property falls into before you list changes how you approach pricing, timing, and negotiation.

That is a conversation worth having before the board goes up, not after.
