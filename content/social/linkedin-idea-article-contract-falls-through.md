## Caption

A property going back to market after a contract falls through is more common than most vendors expect, and understanding how to handle it determines what happens next.

Contracts fall through for a limited set of reasons. A buyer fails their finance condition because their lender does not approve the loan at the agreed price or amount. A building and pest report reveals something material that the buyer and vendor cannot agree on. A buyer changes their circumstances, a job loss, a relationship change, a competing property, and exits under a condition they are entitled to use. Each of these has a different implication for the vendor.

A finance fall-through is a buyer problem, not a property problem. The vendor returns to market with the same property in the same condition. Transparency about what happened and why is useful: buyers who approach a property knowing it fell through for finance reasons are not deterred. Buyers who feel they are being kept in the dark are.

A building and pest fall-through requires a different response. If a buyer exited because the report revealed a material defect, the vendor needs to decide whether to remediate it, adjust the price, or disclose it clearly to the next buyer. Continuing to market a property without addressing a known issue creates legal exposure.
