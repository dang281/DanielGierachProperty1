## Caption

Open homes across the inner east have been running hot this spring. Registered buyer numbers are up across the Bulimba, Morningside and Coorparoo corridors, and competition is clustering most intensely in the $900K to $1.4M bracket where upsizers and investors are chasing the same properties.

What is behind it? There are several plausible answers, and I am genuinely curious which one the people in my network weigh most heavily. Rate cuts change borrowing capacity. Population growth creates underlying demand. Tight listing volumes create scarcity. And the 2032 Olympic timeline is now close enough to feel real for a certain type of buyer. Vote below and I will share what I am seeing from the floor.
