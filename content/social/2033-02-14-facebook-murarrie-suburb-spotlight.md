## Caption

Murarrie does not have the inner east profile of its neighbours, and that has kept it accessible at a price point that is narrowing as buyers priced out of Morningside and Cannon Hill extend their search radius. The residential pockets west of the Gateway Motorway represent genuine value for buyers who are willing to assess the suburb on its own terms rather than through the lens of what it is not.

Gateway Motorway access cuts in both directions. For buyers who need north-south movement, or who work in the port or logistics corridor, Murarrie's location is practical in a way that more sought-after suburbs cannot offer. The trade-off has always been the proximity to industrial uses, but the residential pockets, particularly those closer to the Morningside boundary, have a character that is more purely suburban than the suburb's broader reputation suggests. ⚠️ VERIFY

The housing mix in Murarrie runs from original post-war homes to infill townhouses that have appeared as development activity has followed buyer interest. The infill tends to be better quality than in similar transition suburbs further from the inner east, because the buyer pool demanding it is more discerning.

For vendors in February 2033, the autumn season is active and the buyer attention that has been building in Murarrie over the past several years is translating into genuine inquiry. If you have been sitting on an original home in the better pockets of Murarrie, this year's autumn season is worth a genuine conversation.
