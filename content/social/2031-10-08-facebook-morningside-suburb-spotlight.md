## Caption

The cafe strip along Wynnum Road just north of Morningside is where you run into the same people every Saturday morning. It's that kind of suburb. The community feel here is genuine, not manufactured, and buyers who've lived here for a few years rarely leave without a fight.

Morningside sits between Cannon Hill and East Brisbane, with Norman Creek running through the corridor. Elevated blocks above the creek line are among the most contested properties in the suburb. The river proximity on the eastern edge matters for pricing, and buyers who understand that will pay for it. Morningside State School is the local primary, and the suburb pulls a mix of young families and professionals who want inner east location without the Camp Hill price tag.

Character homes on the higher ground sell quickly. The market is particularly competitive for well-presented Queenslanders with original features intact. The detached post-war brick homes on flat blocks take longer and tend to attract investors and first buyers rather than the upsizer profile. That's a meaningful difference when you're thinking about presentation and buyer targeting.
