## Caption

A few observations from 2027 in Brisbane's inner east, for anyone planning the next move.

The suburbs that performed most consistently were the ones with genuine owner-occupier demand: Balmoral, Norman Park, Camp Hill, Hawthorne. These are not suburbs that attract speculative investor activity. They attract buyers who want to live there, who research the catchment, who know the streets. That kind of demand is not easily shaken by rate movements or media sentiment.

The properties that underperformed were almost uniformly those that overestimated buyer appetite for presentation compromise. Buyers in 2027 were not forgiving of deferred maintenance, cluttered presentations, or price guides that did not reflect reality. The gap between a well-prepared property and an average one remained significant throughout the year.

Timing mattered less than preparation. Vendors who spent six to eight weeks getting their property ready before listing consistently out-performed those who rushed to market. This was true in spring, autumn, and the quieter December window.

Looking into 2028: the fundamentals that drove the inner east through 2027, genuine undersupply of quality stock, continued population growth in Brisbane's inner east, strong owner-occupier demand in school catchment suburbs, remain intact. The first quarter of 2028 is likely to be active.

If you are thinking about 2028, now is the right time to start that conversation.
