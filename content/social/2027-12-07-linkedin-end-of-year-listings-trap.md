## Caption

Not every December listing is a good idea. There is a specific trap that catches vendors at this time of year, and it is worth being direct about.

The trap is listing too late with an unrealistic price. A property that enters the market in the second week of December with a four-week campaign at an aspirational price is not going to close before Christmas. It will pass through Christmas and into January carrying a "days on market" count that buyers use as a negotiating tool. A property that has been sitting for six weeks in January is a different negotiation than one that launched in January.

December listings work when three conditions are met: the property is genuinely ready to present, the price reflects where the market is today, and the campaign is designed for a tight window rather than a slow burn.

Properties that tick all three of those boxes can and do sell efficiently in December. The ones that are listed out of deadline pressure, with an aspirational price and preparation that is not quite finished, are doing themselves a disservice.

The honest advice at this point in the year is: if you can be ready by this week and you can price realistically, proceed. If your preparation needs another three weeks or your price expectations are based on the peak of spring, wait until February and do it properly.
