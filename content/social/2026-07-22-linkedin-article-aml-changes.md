## Caption

Yesterday's poll asked how familiar people are with the new AML rules for property. The votes split roughly between "never heard of it" and "heard of it, not sure how it affects me". That is worth addressing before the 1 July commencement.

From 1 July, real estate agents become reporting entities under AUSTRAC. In plain language, every Brisbane property transaction will now involve a verified ID check, a beneficial ownership disclosure if the property sits in a trust, company or SMSF, and, in narrow cases, a report to the regulator.

For most buyers and sellers, the experience will feel similar to opening a bank account. A short digital ID check at listing or contract stage, a few extra forms, and the contract moves on. There is no new tax, no government fee on the transaction, and settlement timing is unaffected.

The operational lift sits on the agency side. Every reporting entity now needs a documented AML/CTF program, customer due diligence on every party, suspicious matter reporting, and seven years of records.

At Ray White Bulimba we have partnered with AML HUB, a specialist in real estate compliance, so the workflow runs cleanly in the background. The compliance is rigorous. The sale experience stays the same.

The full breakdown of what changes for sellers, what changes for buyers, and the practical checklist before 1 July is in the article. Link in the comments.
