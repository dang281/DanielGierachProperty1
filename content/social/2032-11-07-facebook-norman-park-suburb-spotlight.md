## Caption

The Norman Park buyer who has been searching for six to twelve months arrives at a particular point in November with a clear focus. They have seen what the spring market had to offer. They have written a few offers, possibly missed a property or two, and they are now working to a real deadline: settled before Christmas.

That buyer is among the most motivated in the inner east right now. They know the suburb. They know the streets. They know which school catchment they are targeting between Norman Park State School and East Brisbane State School. When the right property appears, the decision process is short.

Norman Park homes on the elevated streets between the school and Whites Hill Reserve are the ones that tend to attract this buyer most directly. The street character here, full-size blocks, Queenslander and character home stock, proximity to the reserve, is a combination the buyer has been specifically waiting for. These properties do not come to market in a rush in November. They represent a small proportion of listings, and the shortage of available stock is exactly what creates the competitive environment that benefits sellers who do list.

Whites Hill Reserve itself is part of the suburb's appeal across every season, but in November, when the weather is warm and the days are long, the reserve's accessibility reads clearly at an open house inspection and at a first walk-through.

If you own in Norman Park and have been thinking about selling, the pre-Christmas buyer is here now.
