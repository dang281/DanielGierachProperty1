## Caption

School catchment boundaries have a measurable effect on property values in Brisbane's inner east, and the effect shows up clearly in comparable sales analysis. Properties within the Whites Hill State College, Coorparoo State School, and Bulimba State School catchments consistently attract a premium that is visible in the data, and that premium concentrates in families with primary-age children who are making their buying decision partly on the basis of which school their child attends.

The effect of the catchment boundary on comparable sales is one of the more striking things to explain to a vendor preparing for a campaign. A property on one side of a boundary street can have a meaningfully different buyer pool, and therefore a different price ceiling, than a property on the other side that looks almost identical from the kerb. This is not theoretical. It shows up in auction results and in the types of enquiries agents receive.

For vendors inside a sought-after catchment, that premium needs to be reflected in the pricing conversation and in the campaign marketing. The buyers who will pay the most for that property are buyers who have already researched which streets fall within the catchment. They know where the boundary sits. Confirming it clearly in marketing materials removes one of the questions that otherwise requires a follow-up call.

For buyers targeting a specific catchment, the most useful thing to do is verify the boundary directly with the school or the Department of Education before making a decision, rather than relying on listing portal catchment maps, which can be out of date.
