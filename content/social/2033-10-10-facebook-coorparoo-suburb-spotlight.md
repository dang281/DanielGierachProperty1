## Caption

At 4 kilometres from Brisbane CBD, Coorparoo sits in a position that makes it genuinely competitive across a broad range of buyer types. The proximity to the city is one draw, but it is the combination of school catchments, retail infrastructure, and diverse housing stock that explains why Coorparoo sustains buyer interest across market cycles.

The school catchment picture here is strong. Coorparoo State School and Coorparoo Secondary College both draw families specifically into the suburb, and for buyers with primary and secondary school aged children, having both stages covered within the one catchment removes a layer of complexity from the decision. That is a meaningful simplification for families who would otherwise be managing school boundaries across multiple suburbs.

Coorparoo Square has added real depth to the suburb's retail and dining offering. It functions as a local centre with genuine range, and its presence has made the suburb more convenient for daily life in ways that are now reflected in how buyers talk about it when they compare it to neighbouring postcodes.

The housing stock is genuinely diverse. Heritage Queenslanders sit alongside new townhouses and apartment buildings, which means the suburb attracts buyers at very different price points and with very different briefs. Families chasing land and a character home, downsizers looking for walkability and city proximity, and buyers entering the market through more affordable apartment stock are all present in Coorparoo's buyer pool simultaneously.
