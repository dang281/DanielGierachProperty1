## Caption

Cannon Hill's rail connection to the Brisbane CBD is one of the suburb's most consistently valued attributes, and it separates it from a number of neighbouring suburbs that rely entirely on road transport. For buyers who commute to the city or who want the option of train access without a transfer, direct rail is a deciding factor in choosing Cannon Hill over an otherwise comparable suburb.

Cannon Hill Plaza functions as the suburb's local commercial hub, providing daily convenience retail and services within easy reach of most residential streets. It is functional rather than glamorous, but residents value the accessibility it provides.

The proximity to the Morningside Racecourse mixed-use development precinct is a factor that more buyers are factoring into their thinking about Cannon Hill's medium-term trajectory. That precinct represents a meaningful injection of residential, retail, and lifestyle development into the area, and the suburb's proximity to it is something buyers with an eye on long-term value are noting.

Cannon Hill State School anchors family demand within its catchment, and the buyer profile I see most often is families who have compared Cannon Hill with Bulimba and Morningside and concluded that the relative value at this price point, combined with equivalent train access, makes Cannon Hill the better decision for their budget. They are typically informed buyers who have done the suburb comparison work and arrived at Cannon Hill with clear intent.
