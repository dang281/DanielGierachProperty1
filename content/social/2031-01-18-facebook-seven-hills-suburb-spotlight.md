## Caption

Seven Hills does not feature in many Brisbane property conversations, and that gap between its quality and its profile is exactly the point. The suburb sits elevated between Coorparoo and Moorooka, with ridge streets that carry panoramic views toward the city and the ranges. Yeronga and Annerley are close. The fundamentals of the location, proximity to the CBD, access to the inner south and inner east corridors, and the elevation that Brisbane buyers consistently pay for elsewhere, are all present here.

The entry price sits meaningfully below Camp Hill and Norman Park for comparable land and housing type. That differential is not explained by any deficiency in the suburb. It reflects the lower profile that Seven Hills maintains relative to its neighbours, and for buyers who have found it, that is a feature rather than a problem.

The housing stock includes a mix of character homes and post-war builds. The ridge streets give the suburb its identity. Views from the upper sections are genuine, the kind that buyers from Coorparoo or Annerley notice when they cross the boundary for the first time and realise they have been underweighting this address in their search.

Demand here has been building. Not loudly. But the buyers who have found Seven Hills tend not to leave it off their list once they have inspected.

For owners in Seven Hills, the 2031 market will reward early preparation.
