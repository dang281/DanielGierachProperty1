## Caption

With Brisbane 2032 now less than three years out, the Olympic conversation has changed. It is no longer hypothetical infrastructure planning. The work is visible. The timelines are confirmed. And at open homes across the inner east, the questions buyers are asking have shifted noticeably.

A year ago, buyers would mention the Olympics as background context, almost as an afterthought. Now it comes up in a specific way: proximity to the Olympic corridor, access to venues, ease of getting around a city that will be under sustained transport investment until 2032 and beyond. The buyers asking these questions are not speculators chasing a short-term price bump. They are buyers who plan to hold for a decade and want to understand the long-term liveability picture.

What I observe is that inner east suburbs are benefiting from the combination of proximity to the CBD, existing infrastructure, and the improvements that are being built around them. Buyers know that the areas that hold value through a major infrastructure cycle are the ones with genuine fundamentals, not just event adjacency. Bulimba, Hawthorne, Balmoral, and Norman Park sit in that category.

For sellers, the Olympic timeline is a legitimate part of the campaign narrative. Not as hype, but as context. Buyers are already asking about it. A seller who understands that context and can speak to it clearly is better positioned than one who dismisses it.

The 2032 Games will come and go. The transport links, the upgraded public spaces, and the attention Brisbane receives between now and then will not.
