## Caption

The decision about whether to launch a property campaign in December or wait until February is one of the most common conversations I have with inner east homeowners in late November.

The case for December is real but narrow. If the property is ready, the price guide is supported by current evidence, and the vendor is comfortable with a campaign running through early December, there is a buyer pool available that is motivated and has been in the market for most of the year. December 1 to 14 is the practical window. After that, buyer attention starts to fragment with Christmas travel, work commitments, and the general wind-down of the year.

The case for February is often stronger than people expect. The autumn selling season, which runs from late February through May, is Brisbane's most consistent window for inner east property. The buyer pool that emerges in February is often fresh, having made decisions over the summer about where they want to live and what they can afford. Stock supply in late February is typically lower than in spring, which can benefit vendors who arrive early in the season.

Where vendors get this wrong is treating December as a consolation prize for missing the October and November window. A December campaign entered into reluctantly, with a property that is not quite ready, achieves neither the pre-Christmas buyer urgency nor the February fresh-start advantage.

The right answer depends on how ready the property is and how motivated the vendor is. More at danielgierach.com.
