## Caption

The summer property market in Brisbane's inner east operates differently from the rest of the year, but not in the ways most people assume.

What changes: open home attendance drops. The curious buyer, the window shopper, and the early-research buyer all take a break over Christmas and January. What remains is the committed buyer: the one who has been looking for six months, knows their budget and their non-negotiables, and will purchase when the right property appears.

What does not change: the fundamental drivers of price. Two motivated buyers competing for the same property in December produce the same outcome as two motivated buyers competing in September. The mechanics of supply and demand do not take a Christmas holiday.

What this means for sellers: a summer campaign with correctly priced property and good presentation attracts the committed buyer pool. It will not attract the wide early-spring crowd, but that crowd rarely drives the final price anyway. The buyer who has been searching since July and missed out twice is far more likely to stretch their budget than the buyer who started inspecting in October.

For buyers, summer is one of the better times to purchase in the inner east specifically because competition is lower. That creates opportunity for the patient buyer who does not need to rush.

The market does not stop in December. It selects for different participants.
