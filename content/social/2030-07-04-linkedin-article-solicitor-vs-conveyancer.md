## Caption

Most buyers call their solicitor after they have already signed the contract. That sequence is backwards, and it costs people money.

A Queensland property contract is a legal document with conditions, deadlines, and inclusions that are not always obvious on first read. The finance clause deadline, the building and pest inspection window, what is included in the sale and what is not: these are not details to sort out after you have already committed. They are the things that determine whether the deal you think you are signing is the deal you are actually signing.

The difference between a solicitor and a conveyancer matters here too. A licensed conveyancer handles the settlement process efficiently and at lower cost. A solicitor can do the same work but can also advise you on the legal substance of the contract itself, negotiate amended terms, and flag special conditions that carry genuine risk. For a straightforward purchase of a standard property with no complications, a conveyancer often serves buyers well. For anything involving complex conditions, a deceased estate, unusual access arrangements, or a development site, the ability to get legal advice on the contract itself is worth having.

The question is not which one is better. The question is which one you need for the property you are buying, and whether you have engaged them before or after you sign.
