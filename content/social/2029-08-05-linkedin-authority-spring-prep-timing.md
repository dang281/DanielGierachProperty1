## Caption

The vendors who achieve the strongest spring results are rarely the ones who called their agent in September. They are the ones who started the conversation in August.

This is not about padding timelines unnecessarily. It is about what actually has to happen before a property is ready to launch. A tradesperson needs to be booked, which in Brisbane's inner east in late August and early September means competing with every other vendor who waited too long. A stylist needs to assess the property, write a brief, source furniture, and schedule the install. A photographer needs a date that works around the styling, the weather, and the agent's launch timing. None of these things happen in three days.

The vendors who call me in September and want to be live in two weeks almost always have to compromise somewhere. The photography gets rushed. The styling is incomplete. The first open home happens before the property is truly ready. That first weekend matters more than people realise. The buyers who attend in the opening week of a campaign are often the most prepared and motivated buyers you will see. Presenting anything less than your best presentation to the best buyers in the market is a poor trade.

August preparation does not mean an August launch. It means a controlled, well-executed campaign that is ready to open on the first weekend of spring with clean photography, a styled property, and a marketing plan that has had time to build momentum.

The difference between a well-prepared spring campaign and a rushed one shows up in the result.
