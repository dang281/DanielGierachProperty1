## Caption

A campaign that has been on the market for four weeks without serious interest is rarely fixed by waiting another month for the right buyer to walk in.

The buyers who would have responded to the property at its current price have largely seen the listing. The portals send saved-search alerts the moment a property goes live. Genuinely engaged buyers move quickly, inspect, and either offer or decide it is not for them. Two to four weeks of open homes is the window where that filtering happens.

If the campaign passes that window without an offer that respects asking, the question is rarely whether the property is wrong. It is whether the price is wrong for the buyer pool that has seen it.

I built a tool that helps you work through the decision rationally rather than emotionally. It looks at where you sit relative to comparable sales, the feedback collected so far, and what changes in the buyer pool a price adjustment would unlock.
