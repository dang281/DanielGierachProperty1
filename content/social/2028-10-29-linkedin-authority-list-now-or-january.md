## Caption

Every year I have the same conversation with sellers who are weighing up October and November against waiting for the new year.

The case for listing now: the buyer pool that is active in late October and early November is one of the most motivated pools of the year. Interstate relocators have firm January school timelines. Owner-occupiers who missed out on properties earlier in spring have real urgency. The competition from other sellers, while building, has not yet peaked.

The case for January: there is always a reason to wait, and in a rising market the reasons compound. But January does not deliver fewer sellers, it just delivers a different seller mix. Every other homeowner who chose to wait rather than launch in October or November is also launching in January. The pool of competing properties grows, the buyer pool from spring has either bought or moved on, and the preparation advantage of launching into a warm market is gone.

What I observe consistently: sellers who launch well-prepared properties in late October or early November, priced within their comparable evidence, tend to get clean campaigns and clean results. The pre-Christmas window is shorter than sellers expect, but it is more effective than its reputation suggests.

If you are thinking about January, the question worth asking is: what does January give you that November does not?
