## Caption

Balmoral is one of Brisbane's inner east suburbs where the housing stock itself does most of the selling. Character homes and Queenslanders dominate the streetscapes here, and the buyers who come looking for them are not browsing broadly. They have decided they want this precinct specifically, and they are prepared to wait for the right property.

Wynnum Road forms the northern boundary, and it does more than mark the edge of the suburb. It creates a clear separation from Morningside, both geographically and in price point. Buyers on both sides of that boundary know the difference and price accordingly.

The catchment picture adds to the appeal for families. Balmoral State School and Balmoral State High School are both well regarded, and being zoned for both in the one suburb removes the secondary school transition complexity that buyers in neighbouring suburbs have to think through. That combination is worth something to families actively comparing options across the inner east.

What I notice when I work with buyers in Balmoral is that the question at open homes is not about the suburb anymore. They have already answered that. The conversation is about whether this particular property is the one they have been looking for. For a well-presented character home in a street they know, the comparison is not to what else is currently listed. It is to how many months of searching brought them here.

If you are thinking about what your Balmoral home is worth in the current market, I am happy to give you an honest assessment.
