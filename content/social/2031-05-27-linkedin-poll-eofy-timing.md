## Caption

For investors with Queensland property, the settlement date relative to 30 June determines which financial year a capital gain falls into. Settling before 30 June books the gain this year. Settling after 1 July delays it by 12 months. On a significant inner east property transaction, that timing difference can carry a meaningful tax consequence. Owner-occupiers are generally less affected, given the main residence exemption. But for investors managing a portfolio, EOFY is a real factor in both the timing of a sale and the urgency of a purchase. Does the end of financial year shape your property decisions?
