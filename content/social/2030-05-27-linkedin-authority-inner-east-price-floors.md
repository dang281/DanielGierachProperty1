## Caption

In quieter periods, the question I get most often from homeowners is whether they should be concerned about where values are sitting. The honest answer for the inner east: the structural supports for this market have not changed, even when transaction volumes slow.

What underpins inner east values consistently: school catchment quality, proximity to the CBD via multiple routes, genuine land scarcity in established suburbs, and a buyer profile that is largely local equity-rich upgraders rather than highly leveraged first-home buyers. That last point matters more than most commentators acknowledge. A market held up by people who have owned their properties for five to ten years and are buying within a suburb they already know is very different from a market held up by high-LVR borrowing.

The suburbs that show the most resilience in quiet periods are Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, and the eastern edge of Camp Hill. These are markets where the properties that come up are genuinely wanted and the buyer pool, even in winter, includes people who have been watching specific streets for months.

The properties that soften in quiet periods are the ones where supply is higher and the buyer profile is less predictable: some pockets of Carindale, the outer reaches of Mt Gravatt. Not collapsing, just slower.

Understanding which segment you are in matters for timing and pricing strategy. Happy to talk through where your property sits.
