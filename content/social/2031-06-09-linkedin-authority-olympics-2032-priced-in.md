## Caption

With the Brisbane 2032 Games just over a year away, the question of how much Olympic demand is already reflected in inner east property prices is one that buyers and vendors are asking more directly.

The honest answer is that it depends on which part of the question you are asking.

The infrastructure effect is measurable. Cross River Rail changed commute times for parts of the inner east in a way that is not speculative. It is real, it happened, and buyers with a workplace in the CBD or the inner north have been factoring it into their suburb decisions since the line became operational. Road upgrades that reduced travel times around key inner east arterials have had a similar, if less dramatic, effect. That part of the Olympic preparation has already been capitalised into property values to some meaningful degree because the infrastructure itself is already in use.

The speculative Olympic premium is harder to isolate. The expectation that international visitors, media presence, and global exposure will create lasting demand uplift for Brisbane property is widely held. Whether it is already priced in, partially priced in, or still ahead of us is genuinely difficult to answer, and anyone who gives you a precise figure is working from assumption rather than evidence.

The complicating factor is that inner east Brisbane was already experiencing structural demand pressure from population growth and genuine undersupply of well-located detached housing before the Olympics became part of the conversation. Separating the Games-related premium from the underlying supply and demand dynamic is not straightforward even for professionals who watch this market closely.

What is reasonable to say is that the suburbs closest to venues and transport infrastructure have attracted attention from investors and buyers who are explicitly citing the Games as a factor. Whether that attention has moved prices above where underlying fundamentals would have placed them is a question worth asking honestly rather than assuming.
