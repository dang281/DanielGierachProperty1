## Caption

Morningside has changed significantly over the past decade, and the pace of that change is reflected in the gap between what long-term owners paid for their homes and what buyers are now willing to offer. The suburb sits between Hawthorne and Cannon Hill along the Wynnum Road corridor, with the Morningside train station on the Cleveland line providing a direct and frequent service into the Brisbane CBD.

The commercial strip along Wynnum Road at Morningside has developed into one of the inner east's better food and hospitality precincts, with a concentration of cafes, restaurants, and small bars that have drawn a younger professional demographic to the suburb over the past several years. That demographic now competes alongside established families and downsizers in what has become a genuinely contested market at open homes.

Morningside State School is the primary catchment for much of the suburb, and the relatively modest school roll compared to some of the larger inner-east primaries gives it a community feel that parents mention as a factor in their decision to buy in the area. The suburb also sits within easy reach of the Norman Park and Balmoral waterfront for weekend recreation.
