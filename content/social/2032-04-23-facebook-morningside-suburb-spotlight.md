## Caption

Morningside has spent the better part of a decade closing the gap with its more prominent neighbours, and the results now speak clearly. The suburb sits between Hawthorne and Cannon Hill, and buyers who enter either of those markets and find themselves outbid tend to reset their expectations and look at Morningside with fresh attention. When they do, they find a suburb with genuine character and strong owner-occupier roots.

Wynnum Road and Thynne Road form the main commercial arteries, with the Morningside Central precinct providing supermarket and specialty retail access without requiring a drive to a major shopping centre. The Morningside train station on the Cleveland line delivers a reliable CBD commute, and that public transport access has long been a point of difference for the suburb relative to car-dependent alternatives at the same price point.

Streets like Lytton Road, Primrose Street and the quieter pockets behind the station precinct house a mix of postwar homes and renovated Queenslanders that attract buyers in their late thirties and forties who are buying for the long term. Those buyers tend to compete hard at auction because they have usually been searching for some time and recognise the right property when they see it.

The suburb's proximity to the Gateway Motorway also makes it relevant for buyers with commitments in both inner-city and bayside locations, a profile that is more common in Brisbane's east than it might appear. If you own in Morningside and are considering the timing of a sale, the details are at danielgierach.com/suburbs/morningside.
