## Caption

Morningside sits between the Wynnum Road and Lytton Road corridors, and its character reflects that duality: a suburb that is genuinely connected to the city but that has held on to a residential quality that heavier traffic corridors often erode. The pockets off the main roads are quieter than the suburb's main arteries suggest.

Morningside State School has long been a draw for family buyers, and the school's reputation for community engagement adds to the suburb's appeal for buyers with young children. The mix of character homes and post-war housing gives the suburb a diversity of stock that means buyers at different budgets can find something that fits. ⚠️ VERIFY

The Wynnum Road commercial strip provides everyday convenience: supermarkets, cafes, and service retail that residents do not need to drive far from home to access. For buyers making the comparison between Morningside and neighbouring Norman Park or Hawthorne, the gap in median values tends to narrow with each year as the suburb's profile rises.

The renovation activity in Morningside over the past decade has been significant. Streets that had original-condition post-war homes ten years ago now feature a mix of well-renovated properties that have reset what buyers expect and what vendors can realistically achieve.

For anyone who has owned in Morningside for a decade or more, what you paid and what the suburb has moved to may be further apart than you expect.
