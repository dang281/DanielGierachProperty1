## Caption

Every year, somewhere around late October, I hear the same thing from vendors across Hawthorne, Bulimba, Norman Park, and Camp Hill: "We'll wait until autumn." Sometimes that's the right call. Often it isn't.

The argument for autumn is sound in principle. The market is consistent, buyer motivation is high, and the post-summer energy in Brisbane's inner east translates into strong open home attendance. I don't disagree with any of that.

But here's what gets lost in that conversation. The buyer who turns up to a Hawthorne open in the first week of November has typically been attending inspections since September. They've seen what's available. They know the market. They've been through finance. And increasingly, they're asking themselves whether they want to be waiting past Christmas or whether they'd prefer to have a settlement behind them and be thinking about the new year from inside a property they've chosen.

That buyer is not waiting for summer to end. They're ready now, and they're looking for the right home to act on.

A well-prepared November campaign captures that specific buyer at a moment when competition on the supply side is easing. The number of new listings tends to thin out in late November as vendors pull back for summer. If your property is well-presented and correctly priced, a smaller competing pool works in your favour.

The vendors who do best in November aren't the ones who listed because they had to. They're the ones who recognised the window and prepared for it properly.

If you're weighing the timing of a campaign, I'm happy to talk through what the current buyer pool looks like for your specific property.
