## Caption

Norman Park has one of the more consistent value propositions in Brisbane's inner east. Whites Hill State College is the primary engine of buyer demand, and the school's reputation has meant that homes in the catchment zone consistently attract family buyers willing to pay a meaningful premium over comparable properties outside the boundary.

The streets between Old Cleveland Road and Whites Hill Reserve give the suburb a character and quietness that is hard to replicate in denser inner east suburbs. Large fig trees on the residential streets, generous block sizes, and a predominantly freestanding housing stock create a suburb that appeals to a buyer who has actively chosen to move away from development pressure.

Norman Park State School is a second drawcard for families with younger children, and the combination of the two schools in the one suburb is a genuine differentiator.

The busway access from Old Cleveland Road gives the suburb strong CBD connectivity. The walk or short bus to the Carindale or Westfield shopping centres is a practical lifestyle feature buyers note.

Days on market in Norman Park have been competitive through 2030, particularly for well-positioned character homes in the school catchment. Properties above the median price point have taken a little longer to find their buyer, but demand has remained.
