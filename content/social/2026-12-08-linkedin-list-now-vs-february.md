## Caption

The question I get asked most often this time of year: should I list now or wait until February?

There is a genuine case for each. Here is how I think about it.

The case for listing before Christmas: buyers who are currently active in the inner east have been in the market for months. They are not browsing. A well-prepared property launching before mid-December hits a small but highly motivated pool. Stock is thin, which reduces competition. If your property and your life circumstances are ready, there is no structural reason to wait.

The case for waiting until February: the first two to three weeks of February bring the sharpest jump in buyer activity of any period in Brisbane's year. Families who resolved over Christmas to move in 2027 are back in the market. Interstate buyers who visited in January are making decisions. If your property needs more preparation time, or if you are not ready to manage the process through December, February is a strong window.

What I tell sellers: the question is not which window is better in the abstract. It is which window you are genuinely ready for. A rushed December campaign often underperforms a well-prepared February one. A property that is ready now should not sit idle until February.

The honest version of this question requires knowing your property, your timeline, and what you are trying to achieve.
