## Caption

There is a difference between genuine buyer competition and the perception of it. Both exist in the inner east right now, and understanding which is which matters for how vendors approach offer negotiations.

Genuine competition is what happens when two or more buyers with pre-approval, clear motivation, and no significant conditions are both interested in the same property. In that situation, the process almost always produces a result above the vendor's initial expectation. The buyers know each other is there. The timeline compresses. Decisions get made.

Perceived competition is what an agent creates when there is buyer interest but not necessarily multiple active offers. Structured deadlines, offer-by dates, and transparent buyer count information are tools agents use to signal that a window is closing. These tools work, but only when the underlying buyer activity is genuine. Experienced buyers recognise the difference quickly.

What I am observing mid-February: well-presented properties in the $1.1m to $1.8m range in Morningside, Norman Park, and Camp Hill are generating the conditions for genuine competition. The buyer pool is active enough that a strong property, priced and presented correctly, is drawing multiple conversations within the first ten days of a campaign.

Where I see single-buyer dynamics emerge is on properties with presentation issues that the vendor has not addressed, or where the price guide is creating a gap between vendor expectation and market reality.
