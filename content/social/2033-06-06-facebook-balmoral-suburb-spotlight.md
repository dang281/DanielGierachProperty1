## Caption

Balmoral's appeal is in many ways about what it is not. It is not a through-road suburb, not a high-density precinct, and not a suburb with the foot traffic and activity that come with a commercial strip. What it offers instead is quietness, character and a residential environment that sits adjacent to Bulimba's lifestyle assets without sharing Bulimba's level of activity. For buyers who want both quality and calm, Balmoral is a rational destination.

The suburb sits below the Lytton Road ridge at around six kilometres from the CBD, with streets that feel sheltered and residential in a way that is increasingly difficult to find at this distance from the city. The housing stock is predominantly character homes on generous blocks, reflecting a period of development that prized space and quality of construction over density. Many have been renovated to a high standard; others retain original details that attract buyers with a preference for the character rather than the renovation.

Proximity to Bulimba's Oxford Street means residents can access one of Brisbane's better café and dining strips without a long drive. The CityCat ferry at Oxford Street extends that convenience to a riverfront commute option. These two elements, the proximity to Bulimba and the quiet of Balmoral's own streets, are the key arguments the suburb makes to buyers at this price point.
