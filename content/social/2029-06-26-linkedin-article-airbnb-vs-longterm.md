## Caption

Short-term rental looks attractive on gross revenue. The net comparison with a well-managed long-term tenancy is usually much closer than the headline numbers suggest.

Platform fees on short-term rental platforms run between 15 and 20 percent of gross revenue. Then add professional cleaning between stays, higher insurance premiums for short-term rental properties, increased maintenance and furnishing replacement cycles, and the cost of vacancies during shoulder periods. That adds up to $15,000 to $25,000 per year in extra costs for a typical inner east property, depending on occupancy and how the property is managed.

A well-leased inner east investment property with a reliable long-term tenant sitting at market rent has low vacancy, predictable costs, and none of the operational overhead of short-term rental management. The net return often sits within a few thousand dollars annually of an actively managed short-term rental, without the complexity.

There is also a body corporate dimension. Many inner east unit complexes have by-laws that restrict or prohibit short-term rental. Checking the by-laws before committing to a short-term rental strategy is not optional.

For investors assessing a Brisbane inner east property, the question is not which platform pays more per night. It is which strategy produces the best net return after accounting for every real cost.
