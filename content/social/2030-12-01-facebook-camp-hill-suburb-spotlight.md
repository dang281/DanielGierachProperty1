## Caption

Camp Hill's position in the inner east market is anchored by one thing above almost everything else: Whites Hill State College. The school's reputation in the Brisbane secondary school conversation is strong, and the consequence for properties inside the catchment boundary is a consistent price premium relative to near-identical homes just outside it.

The streets west of Old Cleveland Road in Camp Hill, on the ridge that delivers city views toward the CBD, are the suburb's top performers. The Jubilee Terrace and Churchill Street precinct in particular has produced some of the inner east's strongest auction results in years where buyer competition is at its most active.

Camp Hill State Infants and Primary School services younger children in the suburb, and the combination of a strong state primary and a well-regarded state secondary in the one suburb is a family lifestyle proposition that is genuinely rare in Brisbane.

The Old Cleveland Road café strip from Stones Corner through to Camp Hill gives residents a weekend dining and social scene that draws them out of the suburb as often as it keeps them in it. That energy and walkability is mentioned consistently at open homes by buyers arriving from suburbs that lack it.

For Camp Hill homeowners considering a pre-Christmas or early February campaign, December is the month to have the preparation conversation.
