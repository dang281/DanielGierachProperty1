## Caption

Vendor discounting is a number worth paying attention to in any suburb you are buying or selling in.

It measures the average gap between the first listed price and the eventual sale price. A suburb averaging minus 2% vendor discounting means sellers are getting within 2% of their initial guide, which is a tight and competitive market. A suburb at minus 6% or minus 7% means buyers are regularly negotiating sellers down significantly, which signals either ambitious pricing or softer buyer demand.

What the number does not tell you is which of those two things is driving it. A suburb with high vendor discounting can still produce strong individual outcomes for well-priced, well-presented properties. The average includes every listing, including the ones that were overpriced from day one.

The more useful question for sellers is not what the suburb averages. It is what the comparable sales for properties like yours actually achieved, and what the market is telling you about your specific property at your specific price point.

Discounting data is available through CoreLogic and SQM Research. It is worth looking at before you agree to a price guide, not after your first open home.
