## Caption

One of the most debated questions in inner east Brisbane property is how long you actually need to hold to see meaningful capital growth. Shorter hold periods can still produce solid outcomes, particularly when you buy well and the market cooperates. But the compounding effect of land value in established suburbs over seven to ten years is a different conversation entirely. Buyers with a long horizon tend to worry less about timing and more about asset quality. Those with a shorter window need the market to do more of the work. The right answer depends on circumstance, but instincts on this vary a lot. How long would you want to hold before selling in Brisbane's inner east?
