## Caption

Most buyers in Queensland sign the contract first and call their solicitor second. That sequence is backwards.

By the time you make that call, the conditions, deadlines, deposit timing, and inclusions are already locked in. Your solicitor can explain what you have agreed to. They cannot change it.

The contract review is where a solicitor can actually protect you. The standard REIQ contract used in Queensland has specific clauses around finance conditions, building and pest deadlines, and the consequences of default. Each of those elements can be negotiated before signing. Once you sign, they are fixed.

This matters most for a few specific situations. If you are buying with a longer settlement period, conditional on the sale of another property, or purchasing a property with a tenant in place, there are clauses that need to be reviewed before you commit. None of those situations are unusual, but each has details that affect your position if something goes wrong.

The other common question is whether to use a solicitor or a conveyancer. In Queensland, licensed conveyancers can handle straightforward residential transactions. A solicitor can also advise on related matters, like tax implications, trust structures, or disputes. For most standard purchases, either option works. For anything with complexity, a solicitor is the safer choice.

The article covers what to look for in the contract before you sign, and how to choose between your options.
