## Caption

Pre-spring is the moment Carina Heights owners should pay attention to. The suburb's family buyer base starts making real decisions in August, before spring listings create a more crowded field. Buyers who are buying for Cannon Hill Anglican College or for the quieter, larger-lot lifestyle of Carina Heights have often been in the market since mid-year, and by August they are ready to act.

The elevated positions in Carina Heights offer something that purely flat suburbs cannot: natural light, cross ventilation, and in some streets, genuine views. These are selling points that photograph well and that buyers feel immediately on inspection. As days lengthen through August and the light improves, these properties present at their seasonal best.

Block sizes here give owners renovation optionality that is worth assessing in the current market. Extensions, granny flats, and pool additions have added meaningful value to Carina Heights properties over the past decade, and the remaining developable space on many blocks means those additions remain practical for new owners. Buyers who understand that upside are prepared to pay for it.

The relative quiet of August in Carina Heights compared to Cannon Hill and Carindale means less buyer distraction. Your property is not competing with fifteen others for the same buyer's attention. That focus has value.
