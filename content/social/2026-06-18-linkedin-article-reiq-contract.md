## Caption

Most buyers sign the REIQ contract before they've spoken to a solicitor. By the time they call for advice, they're already legally bound.

The standard Queensland contract for houses and residential land is not complicated, but it has several clauses that catch buyers off guard if they read them for the first time after signing.

The cooling-off period is one. Private treaty buyers in Queensland have five business days to withdraw, but exercising it is not free. You forfeit 0.25% of the purchase price as a termination penalty. On a $1.2 million property, that's $3,000. The cooling off exists as a safety net, not as a substitute for reading the contract first.

The finance condition is another. If you allow the timeframe to pass without giving formal written notice, the condition lapses and the contract becomes unconditional by default. A text to the agent does not count. Your solicitor sends formal written notice, and the timing is strictly observed.

A contract review by a solicitor before you sign typically costs $150 to $300. On a property worth $900,000 to $2 million, it is not a corner worth cutting.

I've written a plain-English guide to the key clauses: what the front page actually records, how conditions work, what the deposit means, and when special conditions matter.
