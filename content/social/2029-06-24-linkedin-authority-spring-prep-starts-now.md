## Caption

If you are planning to list in spring, the preparation window is open now.

The vendors who achieve the best spring results in Brisbane's inner east are not the ones who call me in late August with a target to be on market by mid-September. They are the ones who start the conversation in June, give the preparation phase the time it deserves, and launch when the property is genuinely ready rather than rushed.

What happens in those twelve weeks matters. Paint, repairs, and maintenance that need tradespeople require booking lead times. Professional photography scheduled when the garden looks its best rather than whatever week the property is technically ready. Styling sourced and placed early so there is time to adjust before the campaign begins.

The practical cost of rushing a spring campaign is rarely visible on the surface. The property still goes to market. The photos still get taken. The listing still goes live. What changes is whether the campaign captured the full potential of the property or settled for 90 per cent of it. On a $1.2 million home, that 10 per cent is $120,000.

The spring season in Brisbane's inner east typically runs from mid-September to late November. To be on market in September with a well-prepared campaign, the conversation needs to start in June or July.
