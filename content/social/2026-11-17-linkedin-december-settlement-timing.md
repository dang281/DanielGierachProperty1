## Caption

If you accept an offer this week and negotiate a 30-day settlement, you are settling in the third week of December. If you go to 45 days, that is early January. Both are achievable, but they require different logistics.

A few things sellers often do not think about until they are already under contract.

Conveyancers and solicitors operate on reduced capacity between Christmas and New Year. Most close entirely for a period of between five and ten business days. This affects your ability to respond to requisitions, execute transfer documents, and coordinate with your bank for discharge of mortgage. A settlement scheduled during that window needs to be planned well in advance or moved to the first or second week of January.

Banks are also slower. Loan discharge requests submitted in late November typically settle in December without issue. Requests submitted in the second or third week of December can face delays depending on the lender and their workload.

The practical takeaway: if you want a December settlement, aim for contracts exchanged before the end of November. If that window closes, a January settlement booked for the second week of that month is often the cleaner outcome than a scramble in the last week of December.

None of this should stop you from listing. It just means your conveyancer needs to be briefed on your timeline from day one.
