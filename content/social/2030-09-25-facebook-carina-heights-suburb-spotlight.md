## Caption

Carina Heights is a suburb where the block sizes do the talking. The predominant housing stock is 1960s to 1980s homes on blocks that run noticeably wider and deeper than what buyers find at comparable price points in Coorparoo or Camp Hill. For families who want usable outdoor space, the comparison is straightforward: Carina Heights delivers more land per dollar than most inner east alternatives.

The buyer profile here is primarily families making a deliberate trade: they accept a quieter café culture in exchange for more space, better value, and school catchments that are still strong. Access to both the Old Cleveland Road corridor and the Gateway Motorway means commuting options are genuine, not compromised. Buyers who have been through the Camp Hill and Coorparoo markets and found them beyond reach are arriving in Carina Heights with clear intent.

The renovation market is active. A steady stream of buyers is purchasing homes with the explicit plan to improve them, and those improved properties are setting new reference points for the suburb. That cycle tends to compound: better presented homes lift suburb perception, which draws a broader and more competitive buyer pool over time.

If you own in Carina Heights and have been thinking about selling, the value story here is clear and buyers are paying attention.
