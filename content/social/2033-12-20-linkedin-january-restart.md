## Caption

The first three weeks of January are the quietest weeks of the year in Brisbane property. Open homes are sparse, buyers are still in holiday mode, and the market has not yet found its rhythm. If you launch your campaign in that window, you are selling into a thin and distracted buyer pool.

The real restart happens in the last week of January. That is when buyers who spent December thinking seriously about their search come back into the market with intent. They have been browsing listings over the break, talking to brokers, working out what they can afford, and narrowing their brief. By late January they are ready to act.

The vendors who capture that buyer demand are the ones who are already on the market when it returns. Their disclosure statement is done. Styling is booked. Photography is complete. The agent is appointed and the campaign is live. They prepared in December.

The vendors who start their preparation conversation after New Year typically launch in mid-February. That puts them four to six weeks behind the vendors who started in December. In a market where the autumn window, late February through to May, is historically the strongest selling period in Brisbane's inner east, losing the first four to six weeks of that window is a material cost.

The most valuable timing decision a 2034 vendor can make is not about which month to list. It is about when to start the preparation conversation. That decision, made in December rather than January, is worth more than the calendar date on the listing agreement.

If a 2034 sale is on your radar, the right time to talk is now, not after the break.
