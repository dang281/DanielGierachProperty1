## Caption

The spring selling peak in Brisbane's inner east is not a single weekend. It is roughly a ten-week window that opens in late September and closes when the December pre-Christmas pause arrives. That window is now open.

What characterises this period is the concentration of motivated buyers. People who have spent the cooler months doing research, attending opens, and getting finance pre-approved are now ready to act. The psychology shifts from "we are looking" to "we want to be settled before Christmas." That urgency, combined with elevated auction clearance rates and a healthy volume of stock, creates the conditions where well-run campaigns achieve strong results.

The inner east specifically benefits from two buyer cohorts that become more active in spring. First, families wanting to be in their new home before the school year locks in. Second, downsizers who have waited for a cleaner run of weekends to make their own decision. Both cohorts are in the market now.

The properties that perform best in this window share a common trait: they are brought to market at a price supported by recent evidence, not a number the vendor hoped was possible six months ago. The spring buyer is informed. They have seen enough comparable sales to know what is real.

If a spring campaign is something you have been considering, the preparation phase matters more than the launch date. More at danielgierach.com.
