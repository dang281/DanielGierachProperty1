## Caption

When buyers look at a strata title property, the first number they focus on is the body corporate levy. It is almost the wrong number.

The levy tells you what you are paying quarterly. The sinking fund balance tells you whether the building has been maintained properly or whether it is running on deferred maintenance and the next special levy is a matter of when, not if.

A building with a healthy sinking fund and a clear maintenance schedule is a different asset to one with the same levy amount and a sinking fund that has not been adequately funded for years. Those two properties can look identical on a listings search. They are not the same purchase.

Body corporate levies for a small townhouse complex typically run from $2,000 to $4,000 per year. For a mid-rise building with shared facilities, a pool, lifts, and common area maintenance, the range moves to $6,000 to $14,000 or more per year. The levy alone does not tell you whether that cost is reasonable for the asset.

The other thing buyers miss is the AGM minutes and committee meeting minutes from the past two to three years. Disputes, unresolved maintenance items, and planned capital works are all documented there. A competent solicitor will review the disclosure documents, but understanding what you are reading before you get that report back is worthwhile.
