## Caption

The buyer pool in May is different from the buyer pool in March. The volume is lower, the urgency is different, and the motivation is more varied. Understanding that shift is useful for both vendors and buyers who are active right now.

The buyers remaining in the market in May fall broadly into three groups. The first is genuinely motivated buyers who were unable to secure a property during the autumn peak, either through competition or bad luck. These buyers are often frustrated and willing to move decisively when the right property appears. The second is patient buyers who prefer the quieter winter market, have no hard deadline, and are looking for opportunities that the spring rush will reduce. The third is buyers whose circumstances have created a specific timeline, a job change, a family event, a settlement date.

For vendors, the implication is that the campaign needs to work harder to find and attract buyers. The open home foot traffic will be lower than it was in March. The buyers who do attend are typically more serious, but fewer of them arrive without the campaign actively drawing them in.

The marketing effort and the price strategy both need to reflect this. A May campaign cannot rely on organic foot traffic to build competition. It needs to generate it.
