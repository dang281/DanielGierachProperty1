## Caption

Long-term suburb performance in the inner east comes down to four things: school catchment quality, access to infrastructure, the ratio of land value to total property price, and the kind of lifestyle the suburb supports for the buyers who will pay a premium to live there.

The inner east has historically outperformed the broader Brisbane market on these metrics. But within the inner east, there are real differences between corridors. Some have benefited from infrastructure investment. Some carry stronger school catchment premiums. Some are earlier in their gentrification cycle with more upside still to run. Which corridor do you think has the strongest decade ahead?
