## Caption

The sellers who achieve the best results in Brisbane's inner east autumn campaign window are almost never the ones who started preparing in February.

They started in January.

Here is what the timeline actually looks like for a property that goes to market in late March or April.

Week one to two: honest conversation about the property, comparable sales, and what the market will support. That conversation takes an hour and shapes everything that follows.

Week three to four: any minor maintenance or repairs worth doing before photographs. Not a renovation, not a full repaint, but the things a buyer will notice and factor into their offer.

Week five to six: stylist briefed, photographer booked. This is also when the disclosure statement is prepared, which catches title issues early rather than mid-campaign.

Week seven: photographs taken, listing goes live. Open homes start the following Saturday.

From that first conversation to the first open home is six to eight weeks. That is not long when you are coordinating contractors, stylists, and solicitors around a real life. The vendors who treat it as a four-week process end up rushing things that should not be rushed.

If you are thinking about autumn 2029 and want to understand what the preparation process actually looks like for your property, I am happy to start that conversation now.
