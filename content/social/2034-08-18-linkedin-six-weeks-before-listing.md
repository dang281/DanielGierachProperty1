## Caption

Six weeks before a campaign launch is the period where most of the outcome gets decided.

What actually happens in those six weeks, and what commonly gets skipped:

The declutter. This is the preparation task with the highest return and the one that vendors most underestimate in terms of time. Getting a family home campaign-ready typically involves removing 30 to 40 per cent of the visible furniture and personal items. Most vendors think it will take a weekend. It takes three.

The building checklist. Not a full pre-listing inspection, but a walk-through with fresh eyes: leaking taps, broken fixtures, door handles that stick, window seals that have failed, any items a buyer's building inspector is likely to note. These are negotiation points waiting to happen. Address them before the campaign, not after.

The photography day. This is the fixed point that everything else works backwards from. Lock in the photography date six weeks out and use it as the deadline for everything that precedes it.

What commonly gets skipped: the garden. Vendors who do not start the garden six weeks out arrive at the photography day with a lawn that has not recovered from whatever happened to it in winter.

The campaign is a single point in time. The preparation is where it is won or lost.
