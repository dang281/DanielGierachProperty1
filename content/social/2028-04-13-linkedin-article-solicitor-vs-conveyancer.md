## Caption

Most buyers call their solicitor after signing the contract. By then, it is too late to fix the things that could have been caught beforehand.

Missing inclusions, problematic special conditions, finance deadline risks, unclear chattels, these are the items a good solicitor can identify and address before you put pen to paper. Once the contract is signed, your options narrow significantly. The solicitor becomes a processor rather than an adviser.

This is the part of the solicitor vs conveyancer conversation that rarely gets discussed. The question is not just which professional you use. It is when you bring them in.

Both solicitors and conveyancers can handle a standard residential purchase in Queensland competently. The distinction matters most when the transaction has complexity attached to it. A deceased estate, a property held in trust, a boundary dispute, a contract with unusual special conditions, these are situations where a conveyancer's scope of practice has limits that a solicitor's does not.

The cost difference between a solicitor and a conveyancer is genuinely small. On a $900,000 purchase, the gap between the two is unlikely to exceed $500 to $800. What a missed contract issue can cost, in lost inclusions, a failed finance clause, or a dispute at settlement, is multiples of that.
