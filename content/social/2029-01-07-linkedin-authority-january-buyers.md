## Caption

January gets called a slow month. The volume is lower, which is true. But the buyers who are active in the first two weeks of January are not casual.

They have been watching the inner east for months. Some have been outbid on three or four properties through spring. They come in with pre-approval confirmed, building and pest contact ready, and a clear ceiling. These are the buyers most vendors want to find.

What I track in January is the quality of enquiry, not the quantity. In Hawthorne, Balmoral, and Norman Park the January pool tends to be owner-occupier dominant: families working around school-year transitions, professionals moving for work, and upsizers who have already sold and are ready to commit.

The autumn campaign window opens properly in late February. Vendors who list in March and April are selling into the strongest buyer depth of the year in Brisbane's inner east. But the preparation for that window starts now, in January, not in February when everyone else starts thinking about it.

If you are planning to list before Easter, the time to start the conversation is in the next four to six weeks. Not because of any urgency on my end, but because the vendors who achieve the best results in autumn are the ones who had time to prepare properly.

If that is you, I am happy to have a straight conversation about what your property could achieve.
