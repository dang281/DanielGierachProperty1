## Caption

The thing most buyers get wrong about stamp duty in Queensland is leaving it out of their budget until the last minute. By the time they are in contract and asking how much it is, the calculation has already affected their position.

Stamp duty, formally called transfer duty in Queensland, is calculated on the dutiable value of the property. For an owner-occupier buying a $1.5 million home in the inner east, the figure is substantial. First home buyers have access to concessions that can reduce or eliminate the liability, but those concessions have conditions attached that not everyone qualifies for, and assuming you qualify before you have checked is a common and expensive mistake.

The other issue is timing. Stamp duty is payable at settlement, which means buyers who have not accounted for it in their cash flow can find themselves stretching to complete a purchase they thought they had covered. For buyers relying on a bridging structure between a purchase and a sale, the sequencing of when duty is payable matters even more.

I am not a solicitor or a financial adviser, and the specifics of your situation should be worked through with someone qualified. But understanding the framework before you are in contract is part of being a prepared buyer. I have written a detailed breakdown of how transfer duty works in Queensland, including the current rate schedule, the concessions available, and the questions worth asking your solicitor before you sign.
