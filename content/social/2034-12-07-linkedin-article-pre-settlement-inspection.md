## Caption

The buyer is entitled to one pre-settlement inspection. The property must be in the same condition as at the time of contract. This is where disputes most often arise.

Damage that occurred after the contract was signed, including during moving, is the vendor's responsibility to rectify or compensate for before settlement.

Inclusions listed in the contract must be present at the pre-settlement inspection. A light fitting that was there at contract signing but removed before settlement is a breach.

If the buyer identifies an issue at the pre-settlement inspection, they can delay settlement until it is resolved. Vendors who address potential issues before the inspection avoid that delay.
