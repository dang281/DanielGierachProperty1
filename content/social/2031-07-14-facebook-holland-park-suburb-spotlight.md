## Caption

Holland Park has a structural advantage that buyers in Brisbane increasingly prioritise: elevation with no significant flood risk. In a city where flood overlay affects large portions of the inner suburbs, owning on the high ground of Holland Park gives homeowners something genuinely valuable, not just in liveability terms but in how lenders and insurers treat the property.

Cavendish Road State High School catchment is the other major demand driver. It is one of the most sought-after state secondary school zones in the inner east, and families will plan entire purchasing decisions around it. That school-driven demand creates a reliable base of motivated buyers who are not particularly price-sensitive when the right property presents itself.

The blocks in Holland Park run larger than the inner-east average, and Old Cleveland Road connects the suburb to the city efficiently. For buyers who want space, a quality school zone, and the reassurance of flood-free land, Holland Park checks boxes that are hard to tick together this close to the CBD.

If you have owned in Holland Park for several years, the combination of land content and school catchment premium is likely working in your favour. A current appraisal will give you a precise picture of where the market places your property today.
