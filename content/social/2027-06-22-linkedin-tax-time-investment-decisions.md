## Caption

The end of the Australian financial year is a genuine decision point for property investors, not just an administrative date. For vendors with investment properties, June is the month where the timing of a sale can have real consequences that persist long after settlement.

When someone calls me in June about selling an investment property, the first conversation is not about price or marketing. It is about settlement timing relative to 30 June. A capital gain that settles before the end of the financial year is assessed in this year's return. A settlement that falls after 1 July belongs to the next. Depending on the vendor's income position in each of those years, the difference in tax treatment can be meaningful. Sometimes it is better to settle before 30 June. Sometimes it is considerably better to wait. That determination is not mine to make; it belongs to the accountant. But it needs to be made before the campaign goes live, not after contracts are exchanged.

The same logic applies to depreciation schedules. If an investment property has been held with a quantity surveyor's depreciation report in place, the year the sale falls into affects how remaining depreciable value is treated. That is another variable worth understanding before the campaign design is finalised.

My strong advice for anyone with an inner east investment property who is thinking about selling near the end of the financial year: have the accountant conversation first. Bring your tentative timeline and ask specifically about settlement timing. Then call me. The order of those conversations matters.
