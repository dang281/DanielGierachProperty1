## Caption

Land tax in Queensland is calculated on the combined unimproved value of all taxable land you hold in the state, not on a property-by-property basis.

This distinction matters for investors who own multiple Queensland properties and have been watching inner east land values rise. The land value threshold at which land tax applies is not assessed per property. It is assessed across your total Queensland portfolio. An investor who owns two inner east properties with rising unimproved land values can cross the land tax threshold without any single property being above it individually.

The primary residence is exempt. But any investment property, whether a house in Coorparoo, a unit in Morningside, or a townhouse in Carina Heights, contributes its unimproved land value to the aggregate. As inner east land values have increased steadily over the past several years, more investors are being assessed for land tax for the first time without having planned for it.

The assessment arrives in late October or November for the preceding financial year. The time to model your land tax position is not when the assessment arrives, but when you are deciding whether to acquire another property or hold an existing one.
