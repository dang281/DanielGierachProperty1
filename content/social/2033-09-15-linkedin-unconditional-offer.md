## Caption

An unconditional offer is a contract to purchase a property without any conditions attached. No finance condition, no building and pest inspection clause, no due diligence period. The buyer is committing to the purchase at the agreed price regardless of what a bank assessment or building inspection might reveal after the contract is signed.

That sounds like a significant risk for the buyer, and it is. But unconditional offers regularly win in competitive inner east markets precisely because they eliminate the two to three week risk window that sellers absorb with conditional contracts. During a conditional period, the deal can fall over. The seller cannot properly market the property to other buyers. They sit and wait.

An unconditional offer removes all of that risk from the seller's position. For the right price, many sellers will accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one, because the certainty of the unconditional outcome is worth the price difference.

In a competitive spring market, buyers who are prepared to offer unconditionally have a meaningful structural advantage. Those buyers almost always have pre-approved finance and have either had the property inspected prior to offering, or have made a considered decision that the risk profile is acceptable. That preparation is the precondition for offering unconditionally responsibly.

The article covers when unconditional offers make sense for buyers, how sellers view them, and the due diligence steps that responsible buyers complete before removing conditions.
