## Caption

Most buyers receive their building and pest report, skip to the summary page, read the overall risk rating, and make a decision based on that single line.

The summary page is an overview. The active defects section is where the contract-affecting information lives.

There is a meaningful difference between a report that flags active termite activity and one that notes general maintenance items. Both can appear under a moderate or even high risk summary rating. The practical implication of each is completely different. Active termite damage affects the structural integrity of the home. A leaking tap does not.

The other misunderstanding is around timber pest activity. Reports often note "conducive conditions for termite activity," meaning the environment is right for termites to move in. That is not the same as confirmed active termite presence. Both warrant attention but in very different ways and at very different costs.

Before making a decision based on a building and pest report, read the full active defects section. Look at the photographs. If there is anything in the structural or timber pest categories you do not understand, the inspector who wrote the report is available by phone and will walk you through their findings. That call takes fifteen minutes and can change how you interpret a document that cost you $500.
