## Caption

Stamp duty is the cost that disappears from a buyer's mental budget until the moment they need it. On a $1.1 million purchase in Queensland, transfer duty adds approximately $43,350. That is a number most buyers who have not run the calculation before making an offer are surprised by, and surprise at that stage of a transaction is not useful.

Buyers who are searching in July are often at the point of finalising their borrowing capacity and working out what they can actually offer. This is exactly when the transfer duty calculation belongs in the conversation, not after a purchase price is agreed and a contract is signed. A buyer who has $1.1 million in borrowing capacity and $43,350 in transfer duty sitting outside that figure needs to know that before inspecting properties, not after. The total cost of purchase is what matters for borrowing and budgeting, not the purchase price alone.

There are also concessions worth understanding. Owner-occupiers and first home buyers may be eligible for partial or full concessions depending on the purchase price and property type. The thresholds and eligibility criteria have changed over time, so checking the current position before assuming a concession applies is the correct approach.

Queensland uses a tiered rate structure, which means the duty payable is not a flat percentage. The full article includes the current rate bands and a worked example at several price points common in the inner east market.
