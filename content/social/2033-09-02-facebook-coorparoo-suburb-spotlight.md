## Caption

Coorparoo's spring market is consistently one of the highest-activity periods in the inner east. The suburb draws a buyer pool that covers a wide demographic range: first-time buyers working up to the Coorparoo price point, families upgrading from smaller properties in the eastern suburbs, and downsizers selling from the larger inner east suburbs who want walkability without sacrificing a city proximity. All three groups tend to be most active in September and October.

Cavendish Road is one of the more functional commercial strips in the inner east. The combination of supermarkets, independent cafes, and specialty retail within walking distance of the residential streets gives Coorparoo a daily convenience that buyers consistently rate highly. That walkability to everyday amenity is a feature that shows up in buyer feedback at open homes repeatedly, particularly from buyers comparing Coorparoo to more car-dependent suburbs at similar price points.

The Coorparoo train station provides direct access to the CBD in under 20 minutes. For buyers making a household decision that includes a daily commute, that rail access combined with Cavendish Road walkability creates a practical combination that is genuinely difficult to replicate elsewhere in the inner east at the same price.

Spring is when Coorparoo vendor campaigns attract their widest buyer pool. Properties that launch in September consistently achieve stronger competition than those that wait until October.
