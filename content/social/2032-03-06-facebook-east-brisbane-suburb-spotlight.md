## Caption

East Brisbane is one of the most location-efficient suburbs in the inner east. The proximity to the Gabba, the Captain Cook Bridge, and the South East Freeway means buyers who prioritise commute time put it near the top of their list, and that demand has been consistent regardless of broader market cycles.

The streets between Mowbray Terrace and Shafston Avenue capture a mix of classic Queenslanders and postwar homes, many of which have been renovated over the past two decades. The suburban grain is tight here, lots are smaller than Balmoral or Norman Park, but the walkability and city access trade-off is what buyers are paying for.

In March, the suburb performs well precisely because the buyer pool is skewed toward professionals with city-focused lifestyles. Autumn campaign season in East Brisbane tends to see fewer tire-kickers and more buyers who have done their research and are comparing two or three properties simultaneously.

The strata market in East Brisbane, townhouses and boutique complexes along Lytton Road and nearby streets, is active at this time of year too. Investors and owner-occupiers are both in the mix for well-located two and three bedroom stock.

If you own in East Brisbane and want a current appraisal, the autumn window is the right time to get that picture.
