## Caption

The Closing Ceremony was two nights ago. The Games are done.

The pre-Games commentary on property was heavy. Price surge predictions, demand wave forecasts, talk of a post-Games correction. The reality, at least from what I have seen on the ground in Brisbane's inner east, appears considerably more measured.

The market during the Games period was quieter than a typical July, and July is already the quietest month of the inner east selling calendar. Open home volumes were down. Buyer enquiry was lower in quantity but focused in quality. Nothing unusual happened to prices in either direction.

That does not mean the Olympics had no effect on the market. The infrastructure investment, the city confidence, and the international attention that came with the Games all matter over time. But the idea that the Olympics would produce a dramatic and immediate price movement, up or down, was always more speculation than analysis.

What happens now is the more interesting question.

Buyers who held back during the Games period are returning to the market. Sellers who deferred their campaigns are making decisions about spring timing. The post-Games window, from now through to October, is likely to be a busier and more competitive market than what we saw through July and early August.

If you have been waiting, the city is open again.
