## Caption

Camp Hill is consistently one of the most sought-after addresses in Brisbane's inner east, and the reasons are straightforward. Wynnum Road's café and dining strip gives the suburb a walkable main street that residents actually use, not just one that looks good in a listing photo. Camp Hill State Infants and Primary School and Whites Hill State College together cover the full primary and secondary range, which means families can often spend their entire school years without leaving the suburb's catchment zone. That school continuity drives a particular type of committed, long-term buyer.

The housing mix of renovated Queenslanders and post-war homes on elevated blocks has an enduring appeal. These are the kinds of properties that photograph well, feel immediately liveable, and hold their value across different market conditions. The renovation market remains active, with buyers willing to pay for quality work and prepared to outcompete each other for well-presented stock.

Days on market in Camp Hill sits at the lower end of the inner east range, and the buyer competition for quality listings is consistent. Sellers who prepare properly, price based on current evidence, and run a structured campaign tend to achieve results that reflect the suburb's genuine underlying demand.

If you own in Camp Hill and are thinking about your next move, the current market conditions are worth understanding in detail.
