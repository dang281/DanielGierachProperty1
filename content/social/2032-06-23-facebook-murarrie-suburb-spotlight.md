## Caption

Murarrie sits at the eastern edge of the inner east cluster, bordered by the Gateway Motorway to the east and the Bulimba Creek corridor to the south, and its market position has shifted noticeably over the past decade as buyers priced out of Bulimba and Cannon Hill have moved their search further along the creek. The suburb carries a mix of residential housing and light industrial zoning, and the residential streets closest to the creek and the Cannon Hill boundary are where the strongest buyer interest concentrates.

Hubert Street and the streets running off Lytton Road in the residential precinct offer buyers access to the Bulimba Creek bikeway, which links south to Carina and north toward the river. That active transport connection has become a consistent part of the buyer conversation in Murarrie and has changed how some buyers think about the suburb relative to its better-known neighbours.

The Cannon Hill Anglican College sits just across the boundary, and buyers with school-age children factor catchment access into their decision-making. Gateway Motorway access makes Murarrie a practical choice for households that travel to the south side, the airport, or the Gold Coast regularly, and that commuting logic attracts a buyer demographic that prioritises access and value over postcode prestige.

If you own in Murarrie and want to understand how the market is reading your property, start at danielgierach.com/suburbs/murarrie.
