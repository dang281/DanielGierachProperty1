## Caption

Styling is primarily a photography investment. That is the frame that changes how vendors think about it and why the return is real.

The photography is what drives enquiry. Enquiry drives competition. Competition is what moves the price. By the time a buyer is standing in a styled home at an open house, the decision to attend was made from the photographs online. If the photography didn't generate the enquiry, the open home doesn't happen. The styled furniture and the carefully chosen linen are doing their most important work three weeks before the first open home, in a shoot that takes a few hours.

This matters because vendors often weigh up styling costs against the question of whether buyers can see past it. That is the wrong question. The buyer who doesn't enquire because the online photos looked average never gets to open home at all. You can't negotiate with a buyer you never attracted.

In the inner east, where properties in Coorparoo, Camp Hill, and Hawthorne are often competing with four or five comparable listings at any given time, the quality of the visual presentation is frequently the variable that determines which open home a buyer prioritises. There are only so many Saturdays in a campaign. Getting into the shortlist on week one is where the price is made.

The article covers what a stylist actually does, what it costs in the Brisbane market, and how to assess whether it is the right call for your property type and price point.
