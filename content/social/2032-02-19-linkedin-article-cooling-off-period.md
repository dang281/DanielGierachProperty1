## Caption

The cooling off period in Queensland property transactions is one of the most misunderstood buyer rights in the process. Buyers assume it is a negotiation variable. It is not. The vendor cannot waive it, cannot shorten it, and cannot trade it away as a term of the contract. It is a statutory buyer right, and it exists regardless of what either party would prefer.

The standard period is 5 business days from the date of contract. During that window, a buyer can terminate the contract for any reason. But the exit is not free. The cost is 0.25% of the purchase price. On a $1.5 million property, that is $3,750. Most buyers assume walking away during cooling off costs nothing. That assumption is incorrect.

The other point buyers consistently miss is that auction contracts have no cooling off period at all. None. A buyer who signs at auction is immediately bound. There is no 5-business-day protection, no termination right, and no financial penalty because there is no exit. That is why due diligence before auction is not optional.

Understanding these two conditions, how the cooling off period actually works and where it does not exist, changes how a buyer should approach the period between signing a contract and the expiry of that protection.

The full article covers timelines, costs, when and how to exercise the right to terminate, and what buyers should be doing during the cooling off window.
