## Caption

Balmoral Beach is not the Sunshine Coast. It is a river beach, shaded by figs, sitting at the base of the ridge that defines this suburb's character. Families use it in summer. Walkers use the path along the river year-round. Buyers who discover it tend to revise their budget upward before they make an offer on anything on the streets above.

The ridge in Balmoral is the address. Streets running along and below it carry river views from Tingal Road through to the elevated sections closer to Hawthorne. The housing stock here is some of the most intact character housing in the inner east. Wide blocks, timber homes, established trees, and a suburb boundary that has not been disrupted by major arterial roads.

The buyer profile in Balmoral divides into two consistent groups. Families who want the school catchment access, the Bulimba proximity, and the character home to renovate or hold. And downsizers who have lived in the inner east for decades and want to consolidate into something on the ridge that they will not need to leave. Both groups are competitive. Both groups are patient but decisive when the right property arrives.

Stock turnover in Balmoral is low relative to demand. When a home on one of the ridge streets with an unobstructed river view does come to market, it does not stay there.
