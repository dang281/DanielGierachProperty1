## Caption

Carina sits between two stronger-branded neighbours, Carindale to the east and Cannon Hill to the west, and benefits from both. Buyers who find Cannon Hill beyond their budget frequently look at Carina as the logical next step, and the suburb offers genuine family appeal at that more accessible price point.

Carina State School underpins the family market, and bus access to the CBD makes the suburb a practical choice for commuters who do not want or cannot access train connectivity directly. The housing stock is a mix of newer builds and character homes, giving buyers options across different renovation appetites and price levels.

The blocks here are workable for families. Not the large acreage style of Carindale, but enough to have a usable backyard without demanding a major time commitment to maintenance. For buyers in their thirties and forties raising children, that practical balance is often exactly what they are looking for.

Carina is not a suburb that generates dramatic market cycles, and that steadiness is underappreciated. It attracts buyers who are making a practical, considered decision rather than speculating on lifestyle branding. Those buyers stay, and that long-term owner-occupier presence supports values over time.
