## Caption

Carina Heights offers something increasingly scarce in Brisbane's inner east: quiet residential streets with block sizes that allow families to actually use their outdoor space. The suburb sits between Coorparoo and Camp Hill in terms of geography, but has historically traded at a lower price point than both, which is precisely why it has been attracting more serious buyer attention over recent years.

The housing stock is mostly 1960s to 1980s homes, a generation of solid construction on generous lots. Some have been renovated, many have not, and that renovation scope is a real drawcard for buyers who want to add their own stamp rather than pay someone else's finished-product premium. School catchments are solid, and access to both the Coorparoo café culture and the Camp Hill dining strip on Wynnum Road means residents do not have to travel far for the lifestyle amenity that buyers in this bracket expect.

Buyers choosing Carina Heights are typically making a deliberate calculation: more space per dollar than the more established inner east names, with access to the same lifestyle infrastructure. For sellers, that calculation is worth understanding. The buyers who arrive at your door have done their homework and they know what comparable homes in Coorparoo and Camp Hill have sold for.

A current appraisal will show you exactly where your home sits in that context.
