## Caption

Holland Park's block sizes are a feature that does not always get the attention it deserves in market commentary focused on price per square metre. The allotments in Holland Park are larger on average than those in many comparable inner-south-east suburbs, and that extra land translates directly into side setbacks, backyard depth, and the ability to add a pool, a granny flat, or a substantial extension without the lot coverage constraints that tighter suburban blocks impose.

For families who are buying and planning simultaneously, the difference between a lot that can accommodate a future extension and one that cannot is not abstract. It determines whether the house they are buying is the house they will be living in in ten years, or whether they will be moving again. Holland Park's blocks tend to resolve that question positively.

The Cavendish Road State High School catchment that covers Holland Park is one of the most sought-after secondary catchments in the south-east corridor. The families who buy into it are not making a casual purchasing decision. They have mapped the boundary, assessed the suburb against their lifestyle requirements, and arrived at Holland Park with a clear brief. Vendors who understand this buyer profile will set their campaign up to meet that buyer where they are rather than broadcasting broadly.

Winter is a period when Holland Park listing volumes are lower, but the school-catchment buyer does not hibernate. For vendors with a secondary school deadline approaching, the timing of a winter listing can be appropriate rather than disadvantageous.
