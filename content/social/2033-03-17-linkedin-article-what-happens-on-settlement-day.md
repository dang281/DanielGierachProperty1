## Caption

Most Queensland vendors imagine settlement day as a high-stakes moment where something could go wrong. In practice, for a standard residential transaction, it is one of the quietest days of the entire process.

Since the adoption of PEXA (Property Exchange Australia), the majority of Queensland residential settlements are handled electronically. The solicitor for the vendor and the solicitor for the purchaser coordinate through the platform. The title transfers. The funds move. Most vendors find out it is done via a text message from their solicitor saying "settled."

That is it. No exchanged keys across a table. No signing of documents on the day. No physical presence required from either party in most transactions. The work happened weeks earlier, during the contract period, when your solicitor prepared the transfer documentation.

What can create complications on settlement day: a balance discrepancy between what was agreed and what the buyer's bank has ready, a title issue that was not resolved during the pre-settlement period, or a pre-settlement inspection dispute that has not been resolved before the settlement time arrives. These are not common, but they are the things worth understanding so that if one arises, you are not caught off guard.

The anxiety most vendors experience about settlement day is almost entirely about the unknown. The article at the link below explains what actually happens, step by step.
