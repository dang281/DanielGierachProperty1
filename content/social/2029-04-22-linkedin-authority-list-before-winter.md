## Caption

The most consistent piece of advice I give inner east vendors at this time of year is simple: if you are thinking about listing, do it now rather than after the school holidays.

Winter in Brisbane is mild compared to the southern states, so the season argument is different here. The issue is not temperature. It is buyer fatigue and listing volume. By late June, buyer pools that have been active since February are thinning. Some have bought. Others have stepped back to reassess. The buyers who remain are often pickier, more patient, and less likely to compete aggressively.

May tends to be the strongest month of the inner east autumn campaign season. Supply is still tighter than the spring peak, buyer motivation is high, and the competition dynamic works in the seller's favour.

There is also a practical consideration around settlement. A late May or early June contract typically settles in July or August. For sellers who need to time their own purchase around settlement, that window aligns well with the spring buying period in most years.

The vendors who get the best autumn results are not the ones who waited to see how the market would play out. They are the ones who prepared early, launched at the right time, and let the buyer pool do the work.

The May window is narrowing. It is still open.
