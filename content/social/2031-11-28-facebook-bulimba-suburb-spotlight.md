## Caption

The houses closest to the river in Bulimba occupy a tier of the market that operates largely by its own rules. Frontage, views, and the ability to see the city from your deck are a combination that attracts buyers who aren't constrained by the typical budget conversation. Those properties don't come up often, and when they do, the inquiry volume reflects it.

For the majority of Bulimba, the story is Oxford Street and the lifestyle that comes with it. The suburb's identity is built around those two blocks of restaurants, bars, and weekend activity. Bulimba State School grounds the family buyer market. The Bulimba Ferry to Teneriffe gives residents a commute option that remains genuinely appealing to professionals who like the idea of arriving in Teneriffe without getting in a car.

Late November here sees buyers who want to be in the suburb before summer. The warm weather suits Bulimba's outdoor character, and buyers who've been watching the market often make their move in this window. That creates a concentrated period of activity where well-presented homes attract strong attendance and fast decisions.

The homes that sit here are those where the price expectation has moved ahead of where the market actually is. Buyers in Bulimba are experienced and well-researched. Ambition without evidence doesn't work here.
