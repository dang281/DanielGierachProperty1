## Caption

The Property Law Act 2023 introduced mandatory seller disclosure in Queensland. Failure to disclose before contract can give the buyer the right to terminate at any time before settlement.

The Seller Disclosure Statement must be provided to the buyer before any contract is signed. It covers the title, encumbrances, rates, and any known material facts affecting the property.

Material facts include known structural defects, flooding history, and neighbourhood disputes that could affect the buyer's decision. The definition is deliberately broad.

Getting the disclosure right before the campaign launches protects the vendor from a buyer using a disclosure failure as grounds to exit a contract they have changed their mind about.
