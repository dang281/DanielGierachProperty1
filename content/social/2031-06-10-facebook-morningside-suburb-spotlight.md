## Caption

Morningside is a suburb where the story being told by the housing stock has changed significantly over the past decade. The character homes on streets like Apollo Road and the surrounding residential grid were, for a long time, the province of buyers who tolerated imperfect presentation because the price made the proposition workable. The properties that come to market now in those streets have been renovated to a standard that reflects what Morningside buyers expect and what they are prepared to pay for.

That shift in renovation standard has compressed the discount that Morningside previously offered relative to Hawthorne and Bulimba. The gap between the three suburbs in price terms still exists, but it is narrower than it was, and the lifestyle infrastructure gap has also narrowed as the suburb's cafe and restaurant offer has deepened over the same period.

The Cross River Rail connection at Morningside station is the transport anchor for residents who commute to the CBD or through it to the inner north. The reliability and frequency of that service changes the daily experience of living a few kilometres further from the city than Hawthorne, and for buyers who are using public transport daily, the train connection removes the primary objection to Morningside's distance.

Winter in Morningside sees the weekend open home schedule thin somewhat, but the buyers who remain active are not casual. The combination of Cross River Rail access, the character home streets, and the price position relative to the river suburbs creates a buyer type that is motivated and well-informed.
