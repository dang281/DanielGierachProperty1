## Caption

Balmoral is the quiet end of the corridor, and that quietness is exactly what a specific buyer is paying for.

The Bulimba-Hawthorne-Balmoral stretch is one of the most consistent stretches of residential demand in Brisbane's inner east. Buyers move along it like a progression. They arrive in Bulimba, they stay in Hawthorne, and then some of them land in Balmoral when they want more land, a quieter street, and a home that sits on its block rather than butting up against the next one.

The streets that run closest to the river, particularly along Balmoral Avenue and the elevated blocks above Wynnum Road, carry the suburb's strongest land value. These are held properties. When they do come to market, they attract buyers who have been waiting for them.

Balmoral State School anchors the family buyer segment. Parents who have already worked out their school catchment before they write an offer represent a significant proportion of the suburb's owner-occupier demand.

The buyers arriving here from Hawthorne typically have equity and a brief: larger land, more privacy, or a specific lifestyle that the corridor's more active retail precincts do not offer. The absence of a main-street retail strip is a feature for this buyer, not a drawback.

Spring brings these buyers back to the market after winter. If you own in Balmoral and have been considering your options, this is when the right buyer is looking.
