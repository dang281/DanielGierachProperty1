## Caption

The finance condition deadline in a Queensland contract lapses whether or not your bank has approved your loan. Most buyers do not know this until it is too late.

Under the standard REIQ contract of sale, the finance condition has a specific due date. If that date passes without formal approval and without a written extension agreed to by both parties, the condition lapses. The contract does not simply pause. The vendor has grounds to terminate. This is not a technicality that only affects distracted buyers. It catches organised, well-resourced buyers who assumed their broker's confidence was the same thing as unconditional approval.

The fix is straightforward but it requires attention during the campaign. If your finance approval is taking longer than expected, your solicitor needs to request an extension from the vendor's solicitor before the deadline, not after. Once the deadline passes, you are no longer in a position to request anything. You are in a position to receive a termination notice.

There is a separate exit path: the cooling-off period. But this is worth understanding clearly. The cooling-off period in Queensland is five business days from contract formation, and exercising it costs 0.25% of the purchase price. On a $900,000 purchase, that is $2,250. It is not a free escape route.

Queensland contract law rewards buyers who read the contract before signing it. The conditions, the deadlines, and the cost of exercising each exit are all spelled out. The document is not designed to trap buyers. It is designed to be read.
