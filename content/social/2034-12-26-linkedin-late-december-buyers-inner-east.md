## Caption

Property does not stop in the week between Christmas and New Year. A small but consistent group of buyers is actively searching and sometimes purchasing during this period, and understanding who they are helps vendors decide whether to list and helps buyers understand their competition.

The late December buyer in the inner east is usually one of three types. The first is the buyer who has recently settled on their own sale and needs to purchase before the end of the financial year for accounting reasons. This buyer is motivated, has cash or confirmed finance, and is working to a genuine deadline.

The second type is the buyer who has been searching all year, has missed out multiple times, and is determined to use the quieter period to find and secure the right property without competition. This buyer has done their research and is ready to act.

The third type is the relocating buyer: someone moving to Brisbane from interstate or from overseas, who is here over the Christmas break and needs to make a decision before returning. This buyer is often operating without the same depth of market knowledge and can move quickly when a property meets their criteria.

None of these buyer types need the widest possible pool of competing properties to make a decision. They need one property that fits.

If you have a property on the market or are considering listing, the late December period is quieter but not inactive.
