## Caption

Brisbane's inner east spring campaign window opens properly in mid-September. That is six weeks away. And six weeks sounds like a lot of time until you account for what actually needs to happen before a property is ready to go to market.

Styling takes time. Furniture needs to be sourced or repositioned. Minor repairs, a fresh coat of paint, and any landscaping work all need to be booked and completed before a photographer sets foot in the door. Good property photographers, particularly the ones who do twilight shoots and floor plans, are booked out weeks in advance in spring. You cannot rush that sequence.

The sellers who achieve the strongest results in September and October made their decisions in July or August. They had time to prepare properly, photograph in ideal conditions, and launch with a clean, well-presented campaign. They did not walk into open homes feeling like they were catching up.

The sellers who start thinking about spring in September are already behind. They are rushing trades, accepting photographer availability that does not suit them, and launching into a market where other vendors have had weeks to prepare. The campaign is often shorter before the pre-Christmas slowdown cuts the selling window off.

Four to six weeks of preparation is not padding. It is the minimum for a campaign that represents your property well. If you are considering selling this spring, the decision needs to be made now, not in a month.
