## Caption

Most spring property advice is written for Melbourne and Sydney. The problem is that a significant part of Brisbane's seller market reads it and acts on it as though it applies here.

The advice says spring is when buyers come out, stock is tight in winter, and listing in September or October maximises your buyer pool. For Melbourne, where there is a genuine weather-driven suppression of buyer activity in winter, this is broadly true. For Brisbane, it is only partly true and partly misleading.

Brisbane buyers do not hibernate in winter. The inner east open home calendar in June and July is not dramatically thinner than September. What changes in spring is that more sellers list, which means more competition for the buyer pool you already had. A well-presented inner east home listed in late July with almost no competing stock may outperform the same property listed in September in a crowded field.

The autumn window, from late February to May, is historically the most consistent period for strong inner east results. The weather is still comfortable, buyers who missed the pre-Christmas market are motivated, and the competing stock levels are lower than spring.

Spring is a legitimate selling window in Brisbane. But it is not automatically superior to the alternatives. The best time to sell your property is when it is prepared well enough to compete, in a market where you have the least qualified competition.
