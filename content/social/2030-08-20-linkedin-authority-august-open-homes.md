## Caption

The people walking through open homes in August are doing their research. They are not typically ready to make an offer on day one of the next campaign they inspect, but they are building a reference library that they will use when they are ready.

A buyer who attends three or four open homes every weekend through August has a clear mental picture of what $1.8 million buys in Bulimba compared to Hawthorne. They know which floor plans work and which ones feel compromised. They know which streets have noise issues and which are quiet. They have a view on which properties were styled to compensate for layout problems rather than to showcase genuine quality. They will carry that knowledge into September and October when they are ready to sign.

What this means for a vendor launching in late September is straightforward: the committed buyers who will write your offer have already been through your comparable competition. If those properties were presented better, priced more accurately, or marketed more clearly than yours, your buyer has a reference point that works against you.

At every open home in August, I am paying attention to what buyers say and what they do not say. The questions they ask most often, the details they notice, the features they photograph on their phones. That observation informs how I advise vendors on presentation priorities, on which maintenance items are worth spending on, and on how to position a property against the competition that has already been seen.

If you are preparing for spring, the August open home circuit is not just useful for buyers. It is useful for vendors who want honest, current intelligence about the market they are entering.
