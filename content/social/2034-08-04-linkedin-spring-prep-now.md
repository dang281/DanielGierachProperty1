## Caption

If you are planning a spring campaign, the decisions you make in August determine the outcome more than the decisions you make in September.

The timeline looks like this. You need four to eight weeks of preparation before a campaign: decluttering, repairs, painting, garden work, styling appointments, photography. If you plan to be live in early October, your preparation needs to start now.

The agents who are consistently busy in October are the ones who spent August having conversations with vendors, not the ones who started those conversations in late September.

What preparation actually covers: a thorough declutter so the home photographs well without personal items as distractions, any outstanding maintenance items that a building and pest inspector would flag, fresh external and internal paint where the condition has deteriorated, garden work that can be done progressively over six weeks rather than in a last-minute rush.

What preparation does not cover: expensive renovations that will not return their cost, complete refurnishing, or anything a buyer expects to do themselves.

The window between now and late September is the preparation window. The sellers who use it are the ones who approach spring with options. The ones who do not are the ones calling agents in panic in the first week of September.
