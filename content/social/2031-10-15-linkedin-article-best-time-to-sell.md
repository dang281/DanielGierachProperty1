## Caption

Almost all the advice you will read about the best time to sell is written using Melbourne and Sydney data. Brisbane's inner east has its own seasonal pattern, and it does not follow the same script.

The strongest selling window for the inner east is autumn, specifically late February through to the end of May. Not spring. The buyer pool at that time of year tends to be more focused, less browsing, and more willing to make decisions quickly. Competition from other sellers is often lower than in the spring peak, which means well-prepared vendors face less noise in the market.

Spring in Brisbane is busy. Open home numbers are higher. But higher foot traffic does not automatically mean better outcomes. It means more browsers alongside the genuine buyers, more competition from other listings, and more noise in the campaign.

The detail that overrides both seasonal arguments is this: preparation is the variable the vendor controls. A well-prepared campaign launched in July regularly outperforms an underprepared campaign launched in the middle of October's peak spring market. The buyers who need to find a property will buy in any month. What determines whether they choose yours is how well it presents, how accurately it is priced, and how cleanly the campaign is run.

Season is a factor. It is not the deciding factor.
