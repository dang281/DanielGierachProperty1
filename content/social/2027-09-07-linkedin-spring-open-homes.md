**Caption:**

The first two weekends of spring open homes in Brisbane's inner east are some of the most telling of the year. Not because of what sells, but because of who shows up.

September open home traffic spikes after the quieter winter months. Numbers at the door go up. That is real. But a meaningful portion of that traffic is curiosity, people who are six to twelve months away from buying, browsing to calibrate their sense of the market. The serious spring buyers look different. They have been watching since July and August, they have finance in order, and they come in with specific questions.

The distinction matters if you are selling. More bodies through the door does not automatically mean more competitive offers. What you are looking for is the proportion of those visitors who are genuinely ready to act.

For sellers launching in September, there is a real trade-off to consider. The buyer pool is larger than in August, and that is an advantage. But the listing competition is also higher. More properties come to market in September than any other month. You are not launching into clear air; you are launching into a field.

That is not a reason to avoid September. A well-prepared property with a realistic vendor mindset, launched into a market with genuine buyer depth, is a strong position. The goal is not to be first to market in spring. The goal is to be ready when the right buyers are there.
