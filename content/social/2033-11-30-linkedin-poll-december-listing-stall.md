## Caption

Every year there are homeowners who spend October and November genuinely considering listing their property, and then decide at the last moment to wait until the new year. Some of those decisions are completely rational. The property is not ready, the vendor is not ready, or the plan for what comes next is not yet settled. But others reflect a hesitation that, on reflection, could have been resolved differently. A belief that buyers disappear at Christmas when the evidence says motivated buyers are still very much present. A vague preference to wait for a better market that may or may not materialise in Q1. Understanding what is actually driving the stall helps both agents and homeowners have a more useful conversation about timing rather than a default one. What do you think is most likely to stop a homeowner from listing in December?

## Poll options

- Not enough time to properly prepare the property before Christmas
- A belief that buyers will be focused on Christmas rather than property
- A preference to see what Q1 2034 looks like before committing
- The need to have a clear plan for where to buy before listing
