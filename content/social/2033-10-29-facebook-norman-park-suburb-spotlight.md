## Caption

Whites Hill State College catchment is one of the most sought-after school zones in Brisbane's inner east, and Norman Park shares it with Camp Hill. That shared catchment is a significant part of why buyers are prepared to pay above-median prices for properties in Norman Park, sometimes substantially above, specifically to secure a home within the zone.

The price premium on Norman Park properties that are clearly within the catchment boundary compared to identical homes just outside it is observable and consistent. I see it in comparable sales analysis regularly. Two properties of similar size, similar condition, similar street quality, with a catchment boundary between them, will not achieve the same price. Buyers who are purchasing specifically for the school access know exactly where the boundary falls, and they price that knowledge into their offers.

Whites Hill Reserve adds a second dimension to the suburb's appeal. The reserve's walking trails and open space access from the suburb's eastern edge provides the kind of recreational amenity that residents use regularly. For families with children, the combination of a sought-after school zone and accessible parkland on the doorstep is a straightforward quality-of-life argument.

The character home stock reinforces the suburb's feel. Norman Park has a strong heritage presence in its streetscapes, and buyers who are drawn to that kind of established, settled character find the suburb delivers it authentically.

If you own in Norman Park and want to understand what your property is worth in the current market, I am happy to give you a clear and current assessment.
