## Caption

Murarrie is the suburb that most inner east buyers walk past on the way to somewhere else, and that habit has kept it at an entry price point that its location does not quite justify.

The suburb borders Cannon Hill to the south and Morningside to the northwest. The Bulimba Ferry Terminal at the northern edge of the area provides a river crossing that puts Bulimba and its Oxford Street strip within a short ride. By road, Wynnum Road and the Gateway Motorway connections make Murarrie one of the more practically located suburbs in the inner east corridor for buyers who need access in multiple directions.

The residential fabric of Murarrie has been changing. The industrial-residential boundary that once defined the suburb's identity has been shifting as infill development fills former light industrial sites with contemporary housing. That transition creates a mixed market: established character homes on the older residential streets sitting alongside newer townhouse stock. For buyers entering the inner east for the first time, or investors looking for yield within a reasonable distance of the CBD, Murarrie presents a case that the more prominent suburbs cannot match on price.

The autumn market brings buyers who have been priced out of Cannon Hill and Morningside. They arrive knowing Murarrie is the next move, and they are ready to act.
