## Caption

Queensland land tax catches more inner east investors off guard each year, and the mechanism is one that most people do not fully understand until they receive their first assessment.

Land tax is calculated not on what you paid for the property, not on its market value, but on its unimproved land value as assessed by the state government each year. And it is assessed on the combined unimproved land value across all your Queensland holdings, excluding your principal place of residence.

The inner east dynamic: land values in suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, Balmoral, and Morningside have increased significantly over the past decade. Investors who bought in these suburbs several years ago may have crossed the land tax threshold without any change to their portfolio. The land value has moved; they have not.

The threshold is not per property. It is combined across all taxable Queensland land. An investor with two inner east properties at current land values is almost certain to be above it.

This matters at the point of a potential sale too. If you are an investor considering selling, land tax obligations for the assessment year in which you sell are still payable, and the accountant conversation about selling costs needs to include them.

The full guide, including how land tax interacts with selling decisions, is at danielgierach.com/insights/land-tax-queensland-investment-property.
