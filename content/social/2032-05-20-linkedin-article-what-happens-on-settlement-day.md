## Caption

Most sellers know settlement is the finish line. Far fewer know what actually happens on the day, and that gap in understanding causes unnecessary anxiety and, sometimes, real problems.

Settlement is not a ceremony. There is no handshake over a table in a lawyer's office while funds transfer. In Queensland it is largely an electronic process handled by solicitors and conveyancers using the PEXA platform. The parties rarely see each other. The seller does not hand over the keys at a specific dramatic moment. And yet things can still go wrong, and when they do, knowing what is happening and why is the difference between managing a problem calmly and panicking.

The most common issues on settlement day are not the dramatic ones. They are things like a bank being slow to release funds, a final reading not being lodged on time, or a buyer's solicitor identifying a last-minute query on the title. These are usually resolvable. But they go better when the seller has chosen a competent conveyancer and knows enough about the process to ask the right questions rather than just wait.

I have written a detailed guide to what settlement day actually looks like in Queensland: the timeline, the sequence, what you need to have done in advance, and what happens if something does not go to plan. If you are selling this year, it is worth reading before you get there.
