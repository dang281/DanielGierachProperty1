## Caption

East Brisbane's proximity to the CBD is measured in kilometres, but the experience of living here makes the distance feel more marginal than a map suggests. The walk to South Bank via Vulture Street, the short ride to the Eagle Street precinct, and the access to the Gabba's surrounding hospitality strip are not weekend activities for East Brisbane residents. They are the fabric of daily life, and buyers who have made the transition from outer suburbs to East Brisbane report that the change in how they use the city is more significant than they anticipated.

The investor segment of the East Brisbane market has its own logic. Rental demand in the suburb is sustained by the CBD employment base, the South Bank education and cultural precinct, and the consistent draw of the Gabba catchment. Investors who understand what drives tenants to this suburb, rather than to a lower-cost alternative further out, tend to hold their East Brisbane assets through market cycles rather than trading out of them when sentiment shifts.

The flood overlay consideration is most relevant for the lower-lying streets near the river and along drainage corridors. Properties that sit above the overlay boundary command a premium that has become more pronounced as buyer awareness of flood risk has increased. Vendors in elevated parts of the suburb should understand that their flood-free status is a marketing asset, not a technical footnote.

As May closes out and winter settles, East Brisbane's rental market typically tightens, which reinforces the investment case for the suburb heading into the second half of the year.
