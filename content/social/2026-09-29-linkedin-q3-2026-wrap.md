## Caption

Three months of opens, appraisals, negotiations, and settlements in Brisbane's inner east gives you a picture that no data report fully captures. Here's what I've observed across the July to September quarter.

The buyer profile in suburbs like Hawthorne, Camp Hill, Norman Park, and Morningside this quarter has been dominated by owner-occupier upgraders and families relocating from interstate. Interstate buyers in particular have arrived well-researched and motivated. They're not browsing. They've typically been watching the market for several months before they arrive in Brisbane, they have a clear budget, and they move quickly when the right property appears.

Competition at opens on well-prepared properties remained strong through the quarter. Properties that launched with good presentation, correct pricing, and no obvious defects were generating multiple genuine buyer conversations within the first two weeks. The ones that struggled were the ones that either overpriced the launch or hadn't addressed the obvious presentation issues.

Land and character are what buyers in this corridor consistently pay a premium for. A good land component in a desirable street, or a genuine Queenslander in good condition, continues to attract the depth of interest that drives competitive outcomes. Renovated modern interiors in the right location also performed well.

What the quarter reinforced: the fundamentals haven't changed. Preparation, positioning, and correct pricing still determine outcomes more than market conditions do.
