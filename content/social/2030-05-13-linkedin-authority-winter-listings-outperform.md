## Caption

There is a version of spring property advice that gets repeated every August and September: "wait until spring, the market picks up." For some sellers and some properties, that is the right call. For others, it is not.

Here is what actually happens in the inner east when spring arrives. Supply increases significantly. Buyers who were waiting for the market to come back have more properties to choose from. The competition between buyers that drove strong autumn results is diluted by a larger number of listings. Your property is one of fifteen rather than one of six.

A well-prepared winter campaign, on the other hand, is working against a much smaller pool of competing listings. The buyers who are active in June and July are serious buyers. They are not casual open-home browsers. They are people with a genuine need to move: growing families, people relocating for work, investors repositioning. Those buyers are motivated, and they have fewer alternatives.

The caveat is presentation. Winter photography requires more care and often benefits from staging that maximises natural light. An unprepared property in winter is a harder campaign than an unprepared property in spring. But a well-prepared winter listing with the right price strategy, against thin supply, is a genuinely strong position.

If you are thinking about spring, it is worth asking: what would a well-run June campaign actually look like for your property?
