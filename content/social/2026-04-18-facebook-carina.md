## Caption

Carina sits 8km east of the Brisbane CBD and has quietly become one of the more competitive buying markets in Brisbane's inner east.

The suburb's median house price is around $1,162,000, which continues to attract buyers who want established character and proximity to the city without paying Morningside or Camp Hill prices. Carina Village provides walkable retail and cafe access, and the suburb is well-served by bus connections along Old Cleveland Road.

What's changed over the last few years is the buyer profile. Carina is no longer just a starting point for first home buyers stepping up from units. Upsizing families and downsizers from the outer suburbs are competing for the same blocks. That broadening of demand is part of why the market here has held firm even during periods of broader uncertainty.

For homeowners in Carina, the question worth asking is whether your current property still fits your life, or whether the equity you've built could be working harder somewhere else.
