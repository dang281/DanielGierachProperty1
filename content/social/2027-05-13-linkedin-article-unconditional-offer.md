## Caption

A seller will often accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one. Understanding why is one of the most useful things a buyer in Brisbane can know.

A conditional offer typically includes a finance clause, a building and pest inspection clause, or both. These conditions give the buyer a window, usually two to three weeks, during which they can exit the contract if the condition is not met. From the seller's perspective, that window is a period of real uncertainty. The property is effectively off the market, they cannot accept other offers, and the deal can still collapse. A conditional offer at $1.1 million is not $1.1 million in the seller's hand. It is a probable $1.1 million, subject to conditions that have a real chance of not being satisfied.

An unconditional offer eliminates that risk window entirely. The buyer has already completed their due diligence, arranged their finance, and is committing to the purchase without an exit clause. For the seller, that is a qualitatively different offer. It is certain. And certainty has a value that shows up in the price a seller is willing to accept.

For buyers who are finance-ready and have done their homework on a property, knowing when to go unconditional is a competitive position most do not use. The article below explains what an unconditional offer means in Queensland, what you need to have in place before making one, and when it is and is not the right move.
