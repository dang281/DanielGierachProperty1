## Caption

East Brisbane sits just three kilometres from the CBD, and that proximity shapes everything about who lives here and why they stay. The suburb attracts young professionals and couples who want inner-city convenience without sacrificing character. Streets lined with heritage Queenslanders, worker's cottages and post-war homes give the area a texture that newer precincts simply cannot replicate.

Demand in East Brisbane is consistently tight. Stock levels tend to be low, partly because the suburb is compact and partly because owners hold on. When properties do hit the market, they draw competitive interest from buyers priced out of New Farm and Teneriffe who still want walkable access to South Bank, the Mater Hospital and the cultural corridor along the river.

Current medians sit in the mid-to-upper range for the inner east, reflecting both the land scarcity and the lifestyle premium buyers are willing to pay. There is no train line through East Brisbane, but express bus services connect residents to the CBD quickly, and cycling infrastructure has improved considerably in recent years.

For families, the proximity to South Brisbane schooling options and the walkability to Mater and QEII precincts is a genuine drawcard. For investors, low vacancy rates and strong tenant demand make the suburb a reliable performer.
