## Caption

Every year, homeowners in Brisbane ask some version of the same question: should we wait for spring?

The spring selling season is a concept that originated in Melbourne and Sydney. In those cities, the weather in October and November genuinely improves presentation. Gardens look better. Natural light is stronger. Buyers emerge from winter.

Brisbane does not have that problem. Brisbane's version of the spring uplift is autumn.

From late February through to the end of April, the inner east consistently produces its strongest clearance rates and highest buyer attendance at open homes. The reasons are practical. The humidity has dropped. School terms are settled. Buyers who paused over Christmas have returned with decisions made and finance in place. And critically, autumn supply in the inner east tends to be lower than spring supply, which means less competition between sellers.

By contrast, the period from October to December in Brisbane sees more listings and, in some years, slightly softer conditions as the year-end approaches and buyers start thinking about Christmas rather than contracts.

This does not mean spring is a bad time to sell in Brisbane. It means that autumn, not spring, is the period where conditions most consistently favour sellers.

If you are watching the calendar and thinking about when to list, February is not too early. It is the right time.
