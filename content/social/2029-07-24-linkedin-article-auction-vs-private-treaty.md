## Caption

The method of sale should follow the property and the buyer pool. When it follows the seller's preference, or whatever the neighbour used, sellers often leave money on the table or extend their campaign unnecessarily.

Auction does specific things well. It creates transparency, forces buyers to compete in real time, and produces unconditional contracts on the day. For a property with broad appeal and multiple genuinely interested buyers, that competition is where the price gets made. The four-week campaign also creates urgency in a way that private treaty rarely replicates.

Private treaty gives sellers more control over the negotiation. It suits conditional buyers, properties with a narrower or more defined buyer profile, and situations where the seller needs flexibility on timing. It is not a lesser method, it is a different one, and it works well when the conditions suit it.

EOI, expression of interest, is worth understanding separately. It tends to suit unique or high-value properties where pricing is genuinely difficult to establish and where the buyer pool is smaller and more considered. It is not the right default for every premium home.

The decision is a strategic one that should be made with clear data on comparable sales, buyer enquiry patterns, and the specific characteristics of the property. The article breaks down when each method works, when it does not, and what questions to ask before committing.
