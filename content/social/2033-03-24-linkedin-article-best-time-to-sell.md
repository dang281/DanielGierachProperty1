## Caption

The dominant narrative in Australian property media about when to sell is built around the spring season. List in September, be on market through October and November, take advantage of the warmer weather and buyer enthusiasm that follows winter. It is reasonable advice for Melbourne and Sydney, where the spring season consistently delivers.

For Brisbane's inner east, it is the wrong framework.

The inner east autumn window, running from late February through to the end of April, is the most consistent selling season in this market. The reasons are practical: Brisbane's climate means winter does not suppress buyer activity the way it does further south, but autumn's mild temperatures, lower humidity and stable weather are genuinely better for open homes and photography than the humid summer months. The school year is established, families are making location decisions, and the post-Christmas clarity about what the year holds has settled in.

The spring season in Brisbane's inner east is real and active, but it is shorter and more variable than the autumn equivalent. And every seller who waits for spring is competing with a cohort of other vendors who have also waited.

The most honest answer to "when should I sell?" is: when the property is ready, when you are personally ready, and when the marketing can be executed well. Preparation matters more than the season.
