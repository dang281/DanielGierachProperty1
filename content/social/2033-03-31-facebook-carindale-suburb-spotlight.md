## Caption

The last day of Q1 2033 and the final suburb spotlight of the quarter lands in Carindale, a suburb that has had a steady and purposeful autumn season. The family buyer profile that drives this market has been active across the quarter, and the properties that have sold here in the first three months of 2033 tell a consistent story about what this suburb's buyers value and what they are prepared to pay.

Carindale's newer housing stock is an asset in the current market. Buyers who are comparing Carindale with older character suburbs like Coorparoo or Camp Hill are making a conscious trade: they are choosing condition and newer construction over patina and character, and they are making that choice for practical reasons. Less maintenance, better insulation, more functional floor plans out of the box. For a family moving to Brisbane from interstate, this is often an easy choice. ⚠️ VERIFY

The suburb's bushland edges, which back onto creek corridors and green reserves to the east and south, have supported values in those pockets consistently. Buyers who prioritise privacy and outlook have found that Carindale delivers both at a price point that rewards the search.

As the autumn season closes, Carindale vendors who are still preparing to list should not panic. The post-Easter market in this suburb tends to remain active through May as family buyers with mid-year settlement intentions continue their search.

The preparation conversation is still worth having today.
