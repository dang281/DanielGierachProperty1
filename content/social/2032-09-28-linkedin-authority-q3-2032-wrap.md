## Caption

Q3 2032 is almost done. It was the quarter that had the Brisbane Olympics running through the middle of it, and the market performed more normally than many predicted.

The pre-Games speculation was heavy. There were credible voices on both sides: some suggesting that the global attention and pre-Games preparation spend would accelerate the inner east market, others that buyer activity would freeze entirely while the city was in host mode.

What actually happened was measured.

July and August were quieter than a typical midyear period. Open home attendance pulled back. Listing volumes dropped as sellers chose to wait rather than compete for a reduced buyer pool. The vendors who transacted in this window did so cleanly where their pricing was honest, and held on where it was not.

September was a genuine spring rebound. Buyers who had been watching from the sidelines through the Games period re-entered with purpose. Well-priced properties in the Bulimba to Balmoral corridor, in Camp Hill and Norman Park, and in Coorparoo and Carina found good competition. Days on market for well-prepared properties tightened noticeably from the August pace.

The sellers who stayed patient and kept their properties well-presented transacted cleanly. The sellers who rushed to market during the Games hoping to find less competition found less competition and fewer buyers. The outcomes reflected that.

Looking back at Q3 overall: a quarter that absorbed an Olympic Games and came out the other side with its fundamentals intact. That is not a bad result.
