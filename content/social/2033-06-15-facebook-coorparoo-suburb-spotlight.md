## Caption

Coorparoo continues to attract consistent buyer interest for a combination of reasons that have held up well over time. The suburb is six kilometres from the CBD with good road and bus access, a café and retail strip that gives it a local identity, and school catchments that families pursue deliberately. That combination is not accidental; it is the product of the suburb having the right characteristics in the right proportions for the family buyer demographic.

Old Cleveland Road through Coorparoo has developed into a legitimate café and dining destination. Independent operators, specialty food stores and everyday services give the strip a texture that encourages residents to spend time in the suburb rather than always heading elsewhere. Buyers who value that kind of local commercial identity factor it into their assessments, and suburbs with active strips tend to hold value better than those without.

Coorparoo State School is the anchor of the catchment argument, and it draws families from outside the suburb who make the buying decision specifically to secure enrolment. That school-driven demand is structural and provides a reliable base of buyer interest regardless of what the broader market is doing at any given point. Current medians in Coorparoo reflect the combined effect of location, catchment and lifestyle access.

The housing mix includes character homes, post-war brick and timber, and infill development, giving buyers at different price points a genuine opportunity to enter.
