## Caption

The most common misunderstanding I see from buyers in Queensland is this: they believe the finance condition stays open until the bank makes a decision. It does not. The finance condition lapses on the deadline date, regardless of whether the bank has approved the loan or not.

If your finance date is 5pm on Thursday and the bank has not yet issued formal approval, you have two options: request an extension from the vendor before the deadline, or let the condition lapse. If you let it lapse without a formal extension, you are now unconditional. You own the property whether the bank approves the loan or not.

This happens more often than it should. Buyers assume their solicitor or broker is managing the deadline. Brokers assume the solicitor is monitoring it. In a busy settlement period, things fall through the gaps.

The REIQ Contract of Sale is the document that governs nearly every residential property transaction in Queensland. Most buyers sign it without reading it. Understanding the finance clause, the building and pest clause, and the settlement provisions before you make an offer is not optional if you want to protect yourself.

The other area where buyers are routinely caught out is the settlement date. It is not the same as the possession date in all cases. Knowing the difference matters on the day.
