## Caption

Q1 2033 is not quite finished, but the shape of it is clear enough to draw some conclusions.

The inner east market across January, February and March delivered what the evidence suggested it would: a steady, qualified buyer pool, reasonable days on market for well-prepared properties, and some price softness in the segments where vendor expectations had not adjusted to where the market actually sat after 2032. ⚠️ VERIFY

What sold: character homes with functional floor plans and genuine outdoor space, in school catchments buyers were willing to pay for. Whites Hill State College catchment in Norman Park, Balmoral State School, and Coorparoo Secondary College zones all held buyer appetite strongly. ⚠️ VERIFY

What did not sell, or sold below expectation: properties priced against the top of the comparable range without the presentation to justify it, and properties that arrived on market without the preparation work done. The market in 2033 is not forgiving of this combination.

The decision point for sellers who did not list in Q1 is now arriving: list before Easter and capture the remaining autumn window, or hold until spring with a clear-eyed view of what the post-winter market looks like.

Neither choice is wrong. The wrong choice is making it without understanding the current data.
