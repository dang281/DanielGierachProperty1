## Caption

Every year the same pattern plays out. The sellers who use the December break to think seriously about preparation are the ones who perform in February and March. The ones who start thinking about it in late January are already behind.

Preparation is not complicated, but it takes time. Here is what it actually looks like in practice.

A declutter takes longer than anyone expects. If a property has been lived in for five, ten, or fifteen years, the volume of decisions required before styling can begin is significant. Starting in January is not early.

A building and pest report before listing is worth doing before you price the property, not after you accept an offer. Defects discovered mid-contract create renegotiation points. Knowing them in advance gives you options.

Styling quotes take a week to organise and the right stylist books up quickly heading into autumn. If you want the campaign to launch in March, the styling booking needs to happen now.

A pricing conversation with an agent is most useful when it is separate from the urgency of a campaign launch. Do it now, when there is time to have a proper discussion, not in the week before photography.

The buyers who missed out in November and December have not gone away. They took a break, they recalibrated their expectations over the summer, and they are back and ready. The January-fresh listing that is well prepared and well presented goes to a pool of buyers who have been waiting for months.

If you are thinking about autumn, the conversation to have is now, not March.
