## Caption

Hawthorne is one of a small number of inner east suburbs where the lifestyle offering and the property market have reinforced each other over a long enough period that the relationship is now self-sustaining. The suburb's position on the river bend, its access to the Oxford Street precinct shared with Bulimba, and its housing stock of character homes and renovated Queenslanders on generous lots have produced a buyer pool that is consistent, well-resourced, and deliberate in its search.

The market in Hawthorne is not driven by bargain-seeking buyers. The buyers who pursue Hawthorne have identified the suburb as their target location after a process of comparison that has led them here. They understand the price range. They have watched the comparable sales. When they inspect a property that meets their criteria, they move with intent.

The school catchment to Balmoral State High School is a well-known demand driver in parts of Hawthorne, and the access to the NewFarm and Hawthorne Cinemas, the Powerhouse precinct, and the river walks that run through the suburb's parkland add a cultural dimension to the lifestyle case that some buyers weight significantly.

The winter market in Hawthorne typically maintains activity from the committed buyer pool even as broader listing volumes compress. Properties that are well positioned and well priced do not wait long regardless of the month.
