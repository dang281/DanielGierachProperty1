## Caption

There is a version of buyer negotiation that most people focus on, and it is the wrong one. The conversation about price dominates, while the conversation about conditions is treated as a formality. In a competitive market, that framing is expensive.

Sellers will regularly accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one. This is not because sellers are irrational. It is because a finance condition and a building and pest condition create a 2 to 3 week window where the deal can fall apart. For a seller who has already had one contract collapse, that window represents real risk. An unconditional buyer eliminates it entirely.

The power this gives a prepared buyer is significant and mostly underused. A buyer who has pre-arranged finance, who has done a pre-purchase building and pest inspection on the property or a similar one nearby, and who is willing to go unconditional, is presenting a fundamentally different proposition to the seller. That buyer can often secure the property at a price a conditional buyer could not match even by bidding higher.

Most buyers do not understand this. They focus on the offer number and treat the conditions as standard. Understanding how sellers actually weigh their options changes the strategy entirely.

The full article covers what unconditional means in Queensland, when going unconditional is sensible, and what preparation is required before you can do it safely.
