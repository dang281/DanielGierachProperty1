## Caption

Mt Gravatt heading into Q4 is a suburb where the infrastructure story and the buyer profile align in a way that creates consistent activity through the spring selling season.

Westfield Garden City gives the suburb a major retail anchor that buyers who are comparing lifestyle options against the inner east take seriously. The practical convenience is real: everyday shopping, dining, and services within the suburb means fewer reasons to travel for the day-to-day.

Mt Gravatt State High School is a secondary catchment anchor. The families who purchase here with the school in mind are making a long-term decision, which creates demand at the family home price point regardless of where the broader market sits.

Griffith University's Nathan campus is a different driver. The investor buyer who understands rental demand in proximity to a major university has a rational basis for being in Mt Gravatt that does not depend on sentiment or suburb narrative. That segment adds a second buyer type to the pool, which broadens competition on well-positioned properties.

Spring in Mt Gravatt typically brings more listings than the autumn market. That increased listing volume gives buyers more options, but it also brings more buyers to the suburb. The net effect for sellers who present well and price accurately is a larger, more competitive buyer pool than the winter market provides.

If you own in Mt Gravatt and are thinking about Q4, the spring window is the right time to start the conversation.
