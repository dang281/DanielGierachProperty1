## Caption

Most buyers in Queensland sign the contract first and call their solicitor second. That sequence is backwards.

In Queensland, a property contract is binding from the moment both parties sign. The cooling-off period gives buyers five business days to exit, but there are costs attached and conditions that apply. By the time a solicitor reviews the contract, the buyer has already committed to its terms, including any clauses, special conditions, or inclusions that may not be in their interest.

The distinction between a solicitor and a conveyancer matters here. A licensed conveyancer can handle the settlement process competently and is typically less expensive. But a solicitor can provide legal advice, identify risks in the contract, and act if a dispute arises. For a property over $1 million with complex conditions, the difference in cost is rarely the most important factor.

A few things worth understanding before you sign:

Special conditions in Queensland contracts can favour either party significantly. They are not standard. A solicitor can identify conditions that expose a buyer to risk before the contract becomes binding.

Inclusions and exclusions are another area where buyers are regularly surprised after signing. What the agent describes verbally and what the contract specifies are not always the same.

The REIQ contract is the standard Queensland residential contract, but standard does not mean simple. There are obligations on both sides that run through to settlement.

Getting your solicitor briefed before you inspect a property costs nothing. Having them review a contract before you sign costs very little. Both are worth doing.
