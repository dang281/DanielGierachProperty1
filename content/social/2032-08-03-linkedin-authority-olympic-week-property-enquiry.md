## Caption

Eleven days into the Games, and the pattern at open homes is worth talking about honestly.

Attendance is down on what you would expect for a comparable period in a normal year. That is not a concern in itself. Brisbane is hosting the Olympics. People have other things going on on weekends right now.

What is more interesting is the quality of the enquiry from the buyers who did come through. The casual, browsing behaviour that characterises a typical spring or autumn open home was largely absent. The people who showed up were there with a specific purpose. They had done their research, they had finance in order, and they were asking transactional questions.

That is a different buyer profile to what agents typically see in a high-volume open home weekend.

For sellers who are currently on the market: your audience is smaller but more serious. Presentation, pricing, and your agent's follow-up process matter more right now than they would in a crowded spring market where you can rely on volume to create competition.

For buyers who want to transact before September: the window between now and the end of the Games is one of the less competitive periods the inner east has seen in some time. Fewer buyers in the room means less pressure on price.

The city returns to normal after the Closing Ceremony on August 8. The buyers who held back during the Games will come back. If you want to move before that happens, the next two weeks are worth paying attention to.
