## Caption

A drainage easement along the rear boundary of a Brisbane property rarely affects the sale price in any material way. An access easement through the yard to a neighbouring property is a completely different situation.

Most buyers cannot tell which type they are looking at from the title search alone, and the description in the contract often does not make it clear. What matters is not the existence of an easement, it is the nature of the easement: who benefits from it, what they are permitted to do on or over the property, and whether it restricts how the owner can use that part of the land.

Drainage and sewerage easements are common in the inner east, particularly on older properties with infrastructure running through the back section. They typically mean you cannot build over that area, but otherwise the land is fully usable. An access easement that gives a neighbour the right to drive across your rear yard to reach their own property is a materially different encumbrance, and one that buyers should understand before they sign.

The title search shows all registered easements. A solicitor who has been engaged before signing can identify the practical implications of each one in about twenty minutes.

The full article explains the main easement types you encounter in Brisbane's inner east, how to identify them, and what questions to ask.
