## Caption

"Off-market" gets pitched to vendors fairly often as a VIP option: a quiet, low-fuss sale to a pre-qualified buyer without the open homes, the photography, and the weeks of campaign preparation.

Sometimes it works well. A ready buyer at the right price, a vendor who values discretion or speed, and a property that would have attracted genuine competition regardless. In those cases, off-market can make sense.

But the trade-off is almost always price. A full campaign creates competition, and competition is what pushes prices above the first offer. Without it, you're often leaving money on the table, even if the transaction feels cleaner.

What's your read on this?
