## Caption

Brisbane winters are mild by most measures, but they change the way a property presents in ways that sellers and agents do not always account for.

The biggest factor is light. In winter, the sun sits lower in the sky and moves through a narrower arc across the day. A north-facing living room that floods with light in an afternoon inspection in October will behave very differently at 10am in June. The light is cooler, the angle is lower, and rooms that feel generous in summer can feel smaller and darker than they actually are. This is not a defect in the property. It is a predictable seasonal reality that good preparation accounts for.

The simplest adjustment I recommend to sellers in the inner east is to shift open homes to the afternoon wherever the layout allows it. A 1pm or 2pm inspection in June or July gives north-facing living areas their best chance at natural light. For properties that face east or west, the timing question is worth thinking through carefully with your agent rather than defaulting to the standard Saturday morning slot.

Gardens are also past their autumn peak by May. Lawns can look tired, flowering plants have often finished, and the lush green that buyers respond to in spring is not reliably there. A tidy, maintained garden still presents well. But a garden that was left to manage itself through summer may need a session of attention before the first open home.

The sellers who do well in winter are not the ones who wait for better conditions. They are the ones who adjust their preparation and their timing to suit the season they are actually in.
