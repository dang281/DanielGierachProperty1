## Caption

Seven Hills offers more land per block than almost anywhere else in the inner east. In a market where Camp Hill and Norman Park have compressed to the point where finding a 600sqm block is a genuine achievement, Seven Hills regularly delivers it, and that's the story that gets buyers through the door.

Whites Hill Reserve access is a key lifestyle draw. Seven Hills State School services the local families. The suburb sits on the western edge of the inner east cluster, and for buyers who've been priced out of Camp Hill or Norman Park, Seven Hills is where the space conversation starts again. That buyer profile tends to be families with children or buyers who want garden space and aren't prepared to compromise on it.

The streets are quiet. Blocks are generous. The green space ratio per household is above anything you'd find closer to the city. Those qualities attract buyers who know exactly what they want and are prepared to wait for the right property.

What sells fastest here is the well-presented home on a large, private block, particularly where the garden has been maintained. Homes that need full renovation on overgrown blocks take more time and require buyers with a clear project mindset.
