## Caption

Most buyers in Brisbane's inner east underestimate how long their search will actually take before they make an offer.

What tends to happen is this: someone starts watching the market casually, then gradually becomes more deliberate about attending open homes, then narrows their search to specific streets or property types. By the time they make an offer, they have often been watching for six months or more. The offer looks quick from the outside but it is backed by months of market reading.

Understanding your own search timeline is useful. It tells you how close you actually are, and it helps calibrate your expectations about what you still need to see before you feel ready to act.

When did your property search actually start?
