## Caption

Mt Gravatt is one of the better-serviced suburban addresses on Brisbane's south east, with the Logan Road café and dining strip forming a genuine activity spine through the area. The combination of local retail, restaurant options, and Griffith University's presence gives the suburb a rhythm that is distinct from the quieter residential pockets surrounding it. That energy attracts buyers who want amenity and connection, not just a house on a street.

The buyer base in Mt Gravatt covers a wider range than many inner east suburbs. Apartment buyers, house buyers, investors, and owner-occupiers are all active in the market here, which means the suburb rarely experiences the concentrated pressure of one buyer type. That spread of demand tends to produce more consistent price performance across market cycles.

Griffith University's proximity keeps a steady rotation of professional and academic buyers looking in the suburb, while the Logan Road strip draws residents from surrounding areas who eventually decide to live where they already spend their weekends. Both dynamics sustain healthy enquiry levels on new listings.

Long-term homeowners in Mt Gravatt have benefited from a strong capital growth record, and the infrastructure investment across the south east corridor has reinforced the suburb's connectivity story for buyers who need options in multiple directions.
