## Caption

When someone contacts me for a property appraisal, what they say they want and what they actually want are usually two different things.

On the surface: a price range. Underneath that: confirmation that the decision they have already started making is a reasonable one. By the time most people pick up the phone, they have been thinking about this for six months to two years. The appraisal is not the beginning of the decision. It is the first external input into a process that has been running internally for a long time.

What I hear most often in appraisal conversations in February 2034: "We have been watching for a while and we think the time might be right." That sentence tells me the vendor is ready to move, has already formed a rough price view, and wants to test it against a professional opinion. They are not starting from zero.

The typical timeline from first appraisal conversation to active listing in the inner east is six to twelve weeks for vendors who are in the preparation phase, and as short as two to three weeks for vendors who have already done the work. That gap is almost entirely driven by how much pre-sale preparation is already done.

What compresses the timeline: vendors who have a clear financial goal, a specific reason for selling, and maintenance and presentation work that is largely complete. What extends it: vendors who are still working out whether they want to sell, or who have significant preparation work ahead of them.

The appraisal conversation is the right starting point regardless of where you are in that process. It tells you what is realistic before the preparation budget is committed.
