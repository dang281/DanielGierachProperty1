## Caption

Q2 in Brisbane's inner east has its own character, and it is different enough from Q1 to be worth naming clearly.

The autumn window through February and March delivered results for vendors who were prepared. Buyer demand was consistent in the established house market, and the auction clearance rate through March reflected a buyer pool that arrived with decisions already made. That window closed, as it always does, around Easter.

The post-Easter period confirmed what it usually confirms. The buyer pool was smaller and more decisive. The browsing buyer who was weighing up multiple suburbs through February was largely absent. The buyers who remained active in mid to late April had specific requirements and were not conducting exploratory inspections. Properties that had carried pricing from the earlier part of the season without adjustment found the post-Easter pool less forgiving than the March pool had been.

The transition into May brought a different dynamic. The competition for buyer attention at the listing level reduced as spring-motivated vendors stepped back from campaigns. Properties that launched in May with current pricing and strong presentation had less noise around them than they would have faced in March. That is not the same thing as less demand, but it does mean the inquiry that arrived was more targeted.

As the market moves into June, the buyer pool reflects what late autumn produces in this part of Brisbane: fewer browsers, more buyers with genuine timelines, and less patience for campaigns that are not properly set up from the start. The vendors who will perform well in this period are the ones who launched clean and priced their properties with honesty about what the current evidence supports.
