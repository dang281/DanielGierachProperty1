## Caption

The late January shift in Brisbane's inner east is subtle but consistent. The post-holiday quiet starts to give way to genuine campaign preparation, and the vendors who will launch in February and March are already in motion.

What that preparation actually involves is worth understanding before you set your own timeline.

If you are starting from a house that is lived-in and needs some work before it presents well, six to eight weeks of preparation is realistic. That accounts for painting, minor repairs, landscaping, styling quotes, photography, and the staging of a campaign strategy with your agent. It does not account for delays with tradespeople, which are common in January when bookings are still backed up from the pre-Christmas rush.

If you are starting from a property that is already in strong order, four weeks can be sufficient. But four weeks from a late January decision still puts you at a late February or early March launch, which is well inside the autumn window.

The vendors who achieve the best results in the autumn campaign season are generally not the ones who listed in March because they decided to sell in March. They are the ones who made the decision in January, used February to prepare properly, and launched with a property that was genuinely ready and a campaign that was well-structured.

If an autumn launch is on your radar, the time to have the planning conversation is now, not when the campaign is already underway. danielgierach.com
