## Caption

Holland Park's preparation for spring begins now. Vendors who will present the strongest campaigns in September are the ones doing their groundwork in August: getting appraisals, speaking with agents, understanding the buyer pool, and making decisions about timing with actual information rather than assumptions.

The suburb's fundamental appeal does not change with the season. Elevation, flood-free position, larger blocks, and the Cavendish Road State High catchment remain the pillars of demand. But spring does bring the highest volume of active buyers to the market, and Holland Park historically sees its strongest sale prices when that buyer depth is at its peak.

The land content in Holland Park is worth examining carefully in the current market. Brisbane's broader trend toward larger household sizes, multigenerational living, and home office requirements has pushed block size back into buyer consideration in a way that was less prominent five years ago. Holland Park's blocks, generally larger than the inner-east average, align well with that shift.

For buyers in the Cavendish Road State High catchment, the school year creates its own timeline. Families buying to secure a Year 8 enrolment work backward from the school intake date, and those buyers are often closing their search in September and October. August is when they get serious.
