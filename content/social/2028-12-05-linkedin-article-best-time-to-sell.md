## Caption

Almost every piece of advice about the best time to sell a property in Australia is built around Melbourne and Sydney. Spring in those cities brings a genuine surge in buyer activity because winter genuinely suppresses it. The advice travelled north and became the conventional wisdom for Brisbane too.

Brisbane's inner east does not follow the same seasonal logic.

The autumn window in Brisbane, from late February through to May, is consistently the strongest selling period for inner east properties. The reasons are structural rather than seasonal: the weather is excellent, the school year is running, families who want to move by mid-year are motivated, and the buyer pool from the previous spring who did not find what they were looking for has carried urgency into the new year.

Spring in Brisbane is also a functional window. October and November deliver good results. But they deliver them in competition with significantly more stock than autumn does.

What the article challenges is the assumption that spring is automatically the best time to sell. In the inner east, a well-prepared property launched in autumn into a motivated and less competitive market often outperforms an equivalent spring campaign.

The best time to sell is when you are genuinely ready. But if you have flexibility, understanding which window has historically performed better for your property type and suburb is worth knowing.
