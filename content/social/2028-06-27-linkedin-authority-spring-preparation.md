## Caption

The sellers who run the strongest spring campaigns in Brisbane's inner east are not the ones who start preparing in August. By August, you are already late.

The difference is not dramatic in any single week. It accumulates. A seller who starts the preparation process in late June has time to get the photography right. They can complete any minor repairs without rushing trades. They can go through the staging brief properly, make adjustments, and have the property genuinely ready when it goes live. They can have the pricing conversation without the pressure of a deadline forcing an early decision. The Form 6 is signed and the campaign is structured before September starts.

A seller who starts in August is doing all of that in a compressed window while the spring market is already warming up around them. Photography is booked when the photographer is already stretched. Staging is assembled under time pressure. The campaign launches in late September or October rather than early September. That is a materially different window.

The first two weeks of September in Brisbane's inner east are among the most active of the entire year. Buyers who have been watching the market through winter and waiting for spring are primed. They have been researching, attending the occasional open home, and waiting for the supply side to open up. A well-prepared property that launches in the first week of September walks into that pool at full strength.

A property that launches in the first week of October is not walking into the same market. The early spring buyers have often already committed elsewhere by then.

If spring is your window, now is when the work starts. danielgierach.com
