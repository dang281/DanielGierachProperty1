## Caption

Oxford Street is the most legible measure of what Bulimba is worth to the people who live here. It is not a commuter strip that residents tolerate on the way to somewhere else. It is the reason buyers specifically narrow their search to this suburb, and it explains a significant portion of the price premium Bulimba commands over comparable character home suburbs further from the river.

The supply dynamics are worth understanding clearly. Bulimba is a tight precinct. The housing stock is largely detached character homes on modest lots, and the rate at which those homes change hands is low. When a property on Baines Street or Carrongarra Street comes to market, buyers who have been watching for months move quickly. Demand is not concentrated in one buyer type. Upsizers from inner-north suburbs, families transitioning from Morningside, and owners right-sizing after decades in the street all compete for the same limited pool of stock.

The ferry terminal on Bulimba Reach is genuinely useful infrastructure, not just a lifestyle talking point. For households with one partner working near Eagle Street or QUT Gardens Point, the cross-river connection changes the daily arithmetic of suburban living in a way that proximity to a motorway does not replicate.

The Bulimba State School catchment remains a consistent draw for families with primary-school-age children, and school-catchment-driven buyers are among the most motivated in any market, regardless of prevailing conditions.
