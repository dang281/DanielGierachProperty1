## Caption

The southern streets of Camp Hill are one of the most consistent markets for character Queenslanders in Brisbane's inner east, and they have been that way across multiple market cycles.

Stanley Road, Harcourt Street, and the parallel streets running toward Coorparoo's northern boundary are where the suburb's most competed-for stock sits. These are streets that buyers who know Camp Hill target specifically, and the reason is straightforward: a well-maintained Queenslander on 600 to 700 square metres in this pocket of the suburb satisfies a brief that is difficult to match anywhere else in the inner east at the same price.

Camp Hill State School is the factor that converts interest into motivation for a significant segment of the buyer pool. Proximity to the catchment is regularly cited as the decisive factor for buyers in the $1.4 million to $2 million range who are choosing between Camp Hill and comparable suburbs. At enrolment time, that motivation sharpens.

The interstate buyer presence in Camp Hill has been growing. Buyers arriving from Sydney and Melbourne often arrive with a specific brief, character, land, school, lifestyle precinct, reasonable CBD access. Camp Hill consistently satisfies that brief.

For owners in the southern streets, what you hold is precisely what the current market is looking for.
