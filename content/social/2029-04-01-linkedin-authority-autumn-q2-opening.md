## Caption

The autumn campaign window is open. What I am seeing at inner east open homes right now tells a clearer story than any headline figure.

Buyer numbers at well-presented properties are solid. The drop-off happens fast though: homes that need work, or are priced ahead of comparable sales, are sitting. The market has not become forgiving on presentation or pricing just because autumn is traditionally active.

The suburbs where competition is sharpest are the ones with the tightest supply. Cannon Hill, Camp Hill, and Norman Park are all running low on quality stock relative to registered buyer interest. When a well-prepared home hits those markets, the result is usually a short campaign.

Coorparoo and Morningside are producing more listings, which gives buyers more choice and reduces urgency. Sellers in those suburbs need to work harder on presentation and campaign positioning than they did twelve months ago.

The practical implication for anyone considering listing this autumn: preparation time is not a luxury. The gap between a well-prepared and a rushed inner east campaign regularly shows up in the final result by $50,000 to $100,000 or more.

If you are thinking about the autumn window, the time to start is now, not after Easter.
