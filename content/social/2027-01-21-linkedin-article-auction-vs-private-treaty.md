## Caption

One of the first decisions a seller makes with their agent is method of sale: auction, private treaty, or expression of interest. Most homeowners have a gut instinct about which they prefer. That instinct is not always wrong, but it is worth stress-testing against what the property and the market actually call for.

Auction works best when buyer depth is strong, the property is genuinely hard to value (character home, unusual configuration, large land component), and the seller wants unconditional certainty on the day. The competitive environment of an auction can push a result beyond what a private treaty negotiation would achieve.

Private treaty gives the buyer time to arrange finance and due diligence, which expands the buyer pool to include people who are not yet in a position to go unconditional. It suits properties where the buyer pool is narrower and the deal requires time to work through.

Expression of interest is less common but useful for higher-price-point properties where there may be three to five serious buyers and the seller wants the transparency of a structured offer process without the public pressure of an auction room.

The right choice depends on the property, the suburb, the price point, and what is selling well in that segment right now. It is one of the most important conversations to have before a campaign launches.
