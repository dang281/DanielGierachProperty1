## Caption

Camp Hill is one of the inner east suburbs where understanding the school catchment boundary matters more than almost any other single factor when assessing the market. Properties within the Whites Hill State College catchment, which draws from parts of Camp Hill, consistently attract a buyer pool that is more motivated and more price-competitive than properties on the catchment boundary or outside it. That differential shows up in comparable sales evidence in ways that are measurable and consistent.

The suburb is positioned on a ridge that provides elevated outlooks across the inner east. Streets like Martha Terrace and Berrigan Street are known by buyers who have researched the area carefully, and they attract interest accordingly. The lifestyle mix of small cafes and independent retailers on Samuel Street and Old Cleveland Road gives Camp Hill a neighbourhood character that has grown into a genuine dining and lifestyle precinct over the past decade.

The buyer profile in Camp Hill is skewed toward families making long-term decisions about where their children will be schooled. That buyer tends to be patient, research-led, and prepared to pay correctly for the right property in the right catchment position. They are not impulse buyers. They know what they want and they know what the comparable sales say it costs.

If you own in Camp Hill and want to understand what your property is worth in the current market, I am glad to have that conversation.
