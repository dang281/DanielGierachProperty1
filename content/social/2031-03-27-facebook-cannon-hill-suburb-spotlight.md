## Caption

Cannon Hill is the kind of suburb that performs without requiring a narrative. The numbers come in consistently across market cycles because the fundamentals that support them, school access, transport, elevated housing stock, proximity to Morningside, do not change with sentiment.

The elevated streets in the suburb's western sections carry views that buyers in Morningside and Coorparoo pay a premium to access. In Cannon Hill, those views arrive at a price point that still offers genuine value relative to the inner east corridor average. Cannon Hill Anglican College and Cannon Hill State School provide the school access that family buyers require, without the catchment boundary premium that drives prices at Whites Hill State College or Coorparoo State School.

The train station access at Cannon Hill connects residents to the CBD and broader network in a way that removes the daily car dependency that affects comparable suburbs without rail. Combined with the proximity to Wynnum Road and the Creek Road arterials, Cannon Hill offers a commute flexibility that broadens its buyer appeal beyond the catchment-specific family buyer.

Late March is the final push of the autumn market before Easter interrupts the selling momentum that February and March have built. Vendors who have their properties listed and campaigns underway before Easter will complete the autumn selling cycle in its strongest window. Those who delay will be listing into a quieter post-Easter market.
