## Caption

Hawthorne occupies a position in the inner east that gives it genuine advantages over its more commercially prominent neighbours. The riverfront along Hawthorne Road and the streets stepping down from Lytton Road to the water hold some of the finest residential real estate in Brisbane, and they do it without the foot traffic of Oxford Street one suburb north.

The suburb's identity is built around the Hawthorne Cinema, the cafe strip on Hawthorne Road, and a residential character that runs heavily to substantial post-war homes, well-executed renovations, and a number of significant architect-designed builds that have been completed over the last two decades. The blocks on the northern end of the suburb, overlooking the river toward New Farm and Teneriffe, carry views that are difficult to price out of the inner east market entirely.

The Balmoral State School and Bulimba State School catchments both edge into parts of Hawthorne, which adds school-zone competition to the already motivated buyer profile this suburb attracts. Families who have identified Hawthorne as their destination tend to be well-researched and financially prepared.

In the Olympic year, Hawthorne's proximity to the broader inner east precinct without the premium of Bulimba frontage is attracting buyers who want substance over profile. For sellers approaching autumn 2032, the market here is as considered as it has been in this cycle.
