## Caption

Seven Hills is a suburb that rewards buyers who look at a map before they commit to a postcode.

The name does not carry the same recognition as Stones Corner or Camp Hill, but the position on the map tells a different story. Seven Hills sits directly between those two markets, with Boundary Road connecting it to the Coorparoo catchment to the north and the Old Cleveland Road corridor to the east. The Seven Hills shopping centre provides the day-to-day practical anchor that buyers expect before they commit.

The residential streets here are post-war character homes on genuinely generous lots. These are not the subdivided parcels that have become common across the inner south. The land size is real, and buyers who know what they are measuring quickly notice it.

The buyer profile is consistent across spring and autumn cycles. Camp Hill buyers who want more land for the money arrive here when they have exhausted the Camp Hill options. Coorparoo buyers who want a quieter street arrive here when the Coorparoo competition becomes too dense. Both buyer types share an orientation toward the inner east and a willingness to look slightly past the obvious postcode.

Low listing volume is both an advantage and a challenge in Seven Hills. When well-presented homes appear, they attract the buyers who have been watching and waiting. That buyer is active in spring.

If you own in Seven Hills and have been weighing up your options, the spring window is open now.
