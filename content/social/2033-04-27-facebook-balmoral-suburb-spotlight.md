## Caption

Balmoral occupies a quiet, premium position in Brisbane's inner east, approximately six kilometres from the CBD. The suburb sits below the Lytton Road cliff line, with streets that feel notably residential and unhurried. It is the kind of suburb where people arrive and stay, which keeps stock levels low and keeps demand from buyers who know the area consistently active.

The character home is the defining housing type in Balmoral. Queenslanders and post-war homes on generous blocks are the norm, and many have been renovated to a high standard while retaining their original architectural lines. That combination of space, character and quality finish is what the Balmoral buyer is typically seeking. Properties in poor condition are less common, and when they appear, they attract buyers willing to invest in the work required.

Proximity to Bulimba is a significant asset. Oxford Street's cafes, restaurants and village atmosphere are within easy reach, as is the CityCat ferry terminal. Buyers who want Bulimba's lifestyle but prefer quieter streets and slightly larger blocks often find Balmoral to be the answer. Current medians reflect that premium positioning, with the suburb consistently performing at the upper end of the inner east range.

Public transport relies on buses, but road access to the CBD and surrounding suburbs is straightforward.
