## Caption

East Brisbane sits in a position very few suburbs can match. The CBD is minutes away, South Bank is accessible via Vulture Street, and the Gabba precinct anchors the suburb's identity in a way that keeps it on buyers' shortlists year after year.

The housing mix here is genuinely diverse. Character homes on traditional blocks attract owner-occupiers who want proximity to the city without apartment living. The investor market is also active, driven by strong rental demand from people working in the CBD and inner south who want to walk or cycle to work. That dual demand from two distinct buyer types creates a market that rarely goes cold.

Flood overlay applies to parts of East Brisbane, and informed buyers price that into their decisions. Properties on higher ground, or with a clear flood-free certificate, command a premium precisely because that distinction matters to both owner-occupiers and lenders.

Winter is not a quiet season here the way it is in more lifestyle-driven suburbs. Proximity buyers are active all year, and the reduced listing volume in July means genuine competition for well-presented stock. If your property falls on the right side of that flood distinction, the conditions in winter can work in your favour.
