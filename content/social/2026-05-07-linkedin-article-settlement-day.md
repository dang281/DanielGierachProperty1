## Caption

Most vendors spend weeks imagining settlement day as a high-stakes event. In practice, for the majority of Queensland sellers, the day passes quietly and almost entirely without their direct involvement.

Here is why. Residential property settlements in Queensland now happen through PEXA, an electronic settlement platform. Your solicitor logs into the system, lodges the transfer documents, and authorises the transfer of funds. The buyer's solicitor does the same. Ownership transfers electronically, the Queensland Titles Registry records the change, and the purchase price lands in your bank account. No one stands in a room handing over a cheque.

What does require your attention is the lead-up. The week before settlement, your solicitor needs you to sign discharge authorities, confirm your bank account details, and respond to any last-minute requests quickly. Delays on the vendor side in the lead-up period are among the most common reasons settlement gets pushed back.

There is one part of the day that does involve you directly: key handover. Once your solicitor confirms settlement is complete, your agent releases the keys to the buyer. You should have the property vacated and cleaned on the morning of settlement day. Every inclusion listed in the contract should be present. Every exclusion should be gone.

The final inspection happens one to two business days before settlement. How you leave the property at that inspection determines whether the day runs smoothly or generates last-minute negotiations.

I've written a full guide to what actually happens at every stage, including what to do if something goes wrong.
