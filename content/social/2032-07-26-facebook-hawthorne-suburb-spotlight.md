## Caption

Hawthorne is one of the tightest supply markets in Brisbane's inner east. Properties in the premium streets come to market rarely, and when they do, they are absorbed by a buyer pool that has been waiting longer than the typical inner-east buyer.

The suburb's identity is shaped by the Hawthorne Cinema on Riding Road, the character shopping strip along Hawthorne Street, and the density of original pre-war Queenslanders in the eastern residential streets. The northern boundary bleeds into Bulimba's Oxford Street precinct, which means residents access that lifestyle without the Bulimba address premium.

Lourdes Hill College sits within the suburb boundary and is a significant driver for the family buyer segment. The riverfront streets carry a premium that reflects their scarcity: there are not many of them, and buyers who want them know it.

Wybelenna Street and the elevated streets around it offer city views that have drawn buyers who want the character home and the outlook in the same package. That combination is not easily found elsewhere at this distance from the CBD.

For owners in Hawthorne who have been holding and watching, the scarcity of comparable stock in your suburb is working in your favour. When quality homes here are properly presented and well-managed, the buyer competition reflects the supply constraints.
