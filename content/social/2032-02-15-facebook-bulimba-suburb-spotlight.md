## Caption

Bulimba is the reference point for the inner east. When buyers describe what they are looking for, they often end up describing Bulimba, even if they name a different suburb. The Oxford Street dining and retail strip, the river frontage and ferry access, the Bulimba State School catchment, and the density of high-quality renovation in the surrounding residential streets have made it the suburb that sets expectations for the whole corridor.

The housing mix in Bulimba runs from entry-level pre-war cottages on small lots in the back streets to substantial Queenslander restorations and executive new builds on the riverfront. That range means the suburb absorbs multiple buyer profiles simultaneously, which keeps liquidity strong even when specific price points in the broader market soften.

In the Olympic year, Bulimba's proximity to the inner east venue precinct and its established international profile as part of the riverside district have put it on the radar of buyers who would not historically have prioritised Brisbane at all. The buyer from interstate or overseas who wants an inner-city character address with riverside amenity often lands here after a short search.

For sellers in Bulimba, the spring and autumn markets are the natural campaign windows, but autumn 2032 has an additional dimension. The buyer pool is deeper than a typical February because the Olympic calendar is concentrating decisions.
