## Caption

Not all easements affect a property the same way, and most buyers cannot tell the difference from the title search description alone.

A drainage easement along a rear boundary is the most common type in Brisbane's inner east. It typically means a council or utility has the right to maintain underground infrastructure that runs through that strip of land. In most cases it does not affect what you can build, what you can plant, or how you use the yard. It rarely affects resale.

An access easement is a different matter. If a neighbour or a utility company has a registered right to cross through your property, that right travels with the title. Every future owner is bound by it. You need to understand what access means, who has it, when they can exercise it, and what restrictions apply to what you can build near that easement corridor.

Buyers who read the words "easement" on a title search and assume they know what it means without reading the registered terms carefully are taking a risk. The type matters. The location matters. What the easement holder is actually entitled to do matters.

This is not a conversation to have at the coffee table after the open home. It is a solicitor conversation before you sign the contract, while you can still ask questions, seek clarification, and decide whether the terms are acceptable.
