## Caption

The cooling-off period in Queensland is 5 business days. A solicitor who reviews the contract before signing can identify problems while the buyer still has room to renegotiate or walk away. A solicitor reviewing after signing can only advise on what the buyer has already agreed to. That difference is not a technicality. It is the difference between renegotiating a problematic clause and being legally bound by it.

This matters most in July, when buyers move quickly. Winter buyer pools are smaller, but the buyers who are active in July tend to be motivated and decisive. That decisiveness is a strength in a negotiation, but it becomes a liability when speed leads someone to sign a contract before having the right professional look at it. The cooling-off period exists as a safety net, but it is a much more useful tool in the hands of a solicitor who is engaged before signing rather than after.

The distinction between a solicitor and a conveyancer is also worth understanding clearly. A conveyancer is licensed to handle the transfer of title. A solicitor can do that and also advise on the broader contractual and legal implications of what a buyer is signing. For a straightforward purchase, the difference may be minor. For a contract with unusual conditions, a complex title, or an unfamiliar special condition inserted by the vendor's solicitor, a solicitor is the right professional for the job.

The full article walks through both options and explains when each is appropriate for Queensland buyers.
