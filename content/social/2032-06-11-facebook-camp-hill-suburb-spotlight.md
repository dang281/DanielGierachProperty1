## Caption

Camp Hill sits on one of the more pronounced ridgelines in Brisbane's inner east, and that elevation is a defining feature of how the suburb sells. Properties on the high side of streets like Boundary Road, Old Cleveland Road, and Martha Street regularly attract buyer attention for their city view potential, and buyers will pay a meaningful premium for the aspect when it is realised well.

The suburb has a tight-knit village feel along Old Cleveland Road, where a cluster of cafes, a butcher, and independent retailers have maintained a consistent local following. That strip gives Camp Hill a daily rhythm that residents value and that buyers new to the area often comment on during open inspections. It is the kind of street-level amenity that is difficult to quantify but easy to understand once you have walked it on a weekday morning.

Camp Hill State School draws families into the catchment deliberately, and proximity to Villanova College on Old Cleveland Road adds another layer of private school access that influences buyer behaviour from Coorparoo through to Morningside. The suburb skews toward larger post-war homes, and properties that have been well maintained without over-renovation tend to attract broad buyer interest.
