## Caption

Three months of 2027 are nearly done. Here is what the inner east has shown us so far.

January opened with more buyer intent than expected. The usual post-Christmas lull was shorter than prior years. Finance-ready buyers came back early and moved quickly on properties that were priced correctly.

February confirmed the pattern. Open home numbers were strong across Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, and Camp Hill. The family buyer, typically someone upgrading from a smaller inner east home, has been the most consistent and competitive buyer type through the quarter. The $1.5M to $2.2M range has generated the most active competition.

March delivered what autumn in the inner east typically does. The pre-Easter window saw strong clearance rates, limited listings relative to buyer depth, and results that rewarded sellers who prepared properly and priced accurately from the first weekend.

What has not changed: the supply side. Listings in the inner east remain below where they would need to be to satisfy buyer demand. That imbalance continues to be the dominant force holding values. It is not rate-driven sentiment. It is a structural shortage of the right type of property in the right locations.

Q2 starts next week. The post-Easter market typically finds its footing again in May. For sellers who missed the autumn window, there is still time to position well for the second half of the year.
