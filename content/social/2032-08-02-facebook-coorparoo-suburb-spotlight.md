## Caption

Coorparoo is one of Brisbane's most established inner-east suburbs, and it functions as both a destination and a stepping stone in the same market.

Buyers arrive from Greenslopes and Stones Corner looking to upsize into a family home. They move on to Camp Hill and Norman Park when they have the equity to make that step. In between those two movements, Coorparoo is the suburb they spend years in, and those are usually good years.

Coorparoo Square shopping centre anchors the suburb's core for daily retail and dining. The Stones Corner precinct at the northern edge extends the lifestyle offer. The streets running south off Old Cleveland Road toward the Camp Hill boundary carry elevated terrain and the kind of older character stock that family buyers price their whole search around.

Coorparoo State School is one of the most consistently sought-after primary catchments in the inner east. It is cited as a deciding factor at every price point in the suburb, from entry-level units to full family homes on large blocks.

The housing stock includes pre-war and post-war character homes alongside more recent townhouse and unit development, giving the suburb's market breadth that supports both owner-occupier and investor buyer activity.

For owners in Coorparoo, the suburb's depth of buyer demand is a consistent advantage.
