Inner east auction season is underway, and for buyers who have not yet bid at a Queensland auction, there are a few things worth getting straight before you register.

In Queensland, an auction contract is unconditional the moment the hammer falls. There is no finance clause, no building and pest clause, no cooling-off period. If you are the successful bidder, you are buying the property. That is not a technicality; it is the fundamental condition of the method.

Finance pre-approval before the auction is the entry requirement, not a nice-to-have. Talk to your broker or bank and confirm exactly what you are approved to borrow before you register to bid.

Building and pest inspections need to be done before auction day, not after. Vendors are required to provide reasonable access before the auction. Book the inspection during the campaign, not the week of the auction.

Know your maximum bid number before you walk through the gates. Decide it with your broker and stick to it. The atmosphere of a competitive auction floor is not the right environment to revise your ceiling upward.

Brisbane's inner east spring auctions typically attract three to six registered bidders. You are rarely the only serious buyer in the room.

The buyers I see perform best at auction are the ones who have attended two or three auctions as observers before they bid. They know the pace, they know the auctioneer's rhythm, and they are not processing the format at the same time as they are making financial decisions.
