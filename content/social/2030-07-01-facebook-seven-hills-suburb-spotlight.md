## Caption

Seven Hills sits quietly between Morningside and Murarrie, and that positioning is exactly why buyer interest has been building here. It carries the same Gateway Motorway access profile as its better-known neighbours, without the same price premium attached to the address. For families, the Morningside State School catchment is a genuine draw, and it is one of the primary reasons owner-occupiers are choosing to put down roots in this pocket rather than continuing to compete in Morningside itself.

The housing stock is a mix of post-war homes and newer builds on modest blocks, which opens the suburb to a broad buyer pool. Renovators come looking for value and space. Young families come for the school zone. Investors come because the fundamentals are sound and the price point still makes sense relative to neighbouring suburbs.

Stock availability in Seven Hills has historically been tighter than in adjacent areas, which works in favour of sellers. When well-presented homes come to market here, they tend to attract competition from buyers who have been watching the area for some time and are ready to move quickly.

If you own in Seven Hills and have been thinking about what your home is worth in the current market, the answer may surprise you.
