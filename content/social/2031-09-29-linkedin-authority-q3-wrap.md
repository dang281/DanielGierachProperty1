## Caption

Q3 2031 is wrapping up, and it is worth putting down what the quarter actually looked like across the inner east rather than waiting for someone else to summarise it.

July opened the quarter with the characteristic winter profile: a smaller buyer pool than autumn, but a more decisive one. The buyers who attended open homes through July were not browsers. They had been searching since at least May and in many cases longer. The properties that launched in July with accurate pricing and honest presentation found those buyers quickly. The properties that launched at optimistic prices sat and waited, and several adjusted their guides heading into August.

August was the transition month. The pre-spring dynamic that I have written about in previous posts played out consistently: buyer motivation remained high while competing supply stayed constrained. Vendors who used August to complete their preparation and launch before the spring crowd arrived generally fared well. The open home quality in August, in terms of buyer seriousness and readiness to act, was as strong as any point in the year.

September opened the spring window. Buyer numbers built from late August and the energy at opens was noticeably different from the winter pattern. More groups, more variety of buyer type, and a stronger sense that competition among buyers was building. The listings that launched into September with complete preparation and realistic pricing benefited from that energy directly.

The dividing line across the quarter was preparation. Campaigns that had done the work, on pricing accuracy, property condition, and presentation, transacted well across all three months. Campaigns that cut corners or launched ahead of schedule struggled regardless of which month they went live.

Q4 starts tomorrow. The spring window is open and the question is which vendors are ready to use it.
