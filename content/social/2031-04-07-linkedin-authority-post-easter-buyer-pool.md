## Caption

The buyer pool that re-emerges after Easter is smaller than the one that was active through February and March, and that is actually useful information for vendors who understand what it means.

Buyers who are still searching in mid-April have typically already resolved the big questions. They know which suburbs they will buy in, what price they can spend, and what property type they need. The browsing phase is over. These buyers are not comparing Morningside to Hawthorne to see which one they prefer. They are waiting for the right property in a suburb they have already committed to. That is a materially different buyer from the one who was inspecting broadly in February.

For vendors who are currently in campaign and approaching the end of their fourth or fifth week, the post-Easter period is worth reading carefully. If genuine interest exists in your property, the buyers who remain active right now will act. If the inquiry has dried up, the Easter break is unlikely to reset it. The more useful question is whether the campaign has the right pricing and presentation to convert the buyers who are genuinely in the market.

For vendors considering a late autumn launch, mid-April can work, but the window is shorter than it appears. The buyer pool begins to thin again as May progresses toward the middle of the month, and the properties that perform best in this period are those that launch cleanly, with current pricing and strong presentation, without needing time to find their feet in the market.

The post-Easter window is not a second spring. It is a different market, with different buyers, and it rewards preparation more than timing.
