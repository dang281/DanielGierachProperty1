## Caption

The number most vendors focus on when they start thinking about selling is the sale price. The number that matters more at the end of a campaign is the net equity after mortgage discharge, selling costs, and any applicable break costs. Those are not always the same conversation.

Selling costs in the inner east typically run between 2.5 and 4 percent of the sale price. On a $1.4 million property, that is between $35,000 and $56,000 in agent fees, marketing, conveyancing, and related costs. That figure does not appear in the headline sale price but it comes directly out of the vendor's proceeds.

Fixed-rate mortgages carry an additional calculation. If a vendor is inside a fixed-rate period and the current rate is below the market rate when they took the loan, the lender calculates a break cost based on the interest differential for the remaining fixed term. That number can be substantial and should be confirmed with the lender before a campaign launches, not after contracts are exchanged.

The article covers the full cost picture for selling with a mortgage, how to calculate your net equity position accurately before making a campaign decision, and the questions to ask your lender and your agent before you commit.
