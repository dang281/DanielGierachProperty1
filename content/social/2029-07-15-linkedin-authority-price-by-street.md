## Caption

Two properties, same suburb, same land size, similar build. One sells for $200,000 more than the other. People assume the agent made an error, or that one buyer overpaid. Usually, neither is true.

Price variance within a single suburb is one of the most consistent patterns in Brisbane's inner east, and it is driven by specific, identifiable factors. Street position matters. A street that flows through to a main road carries a different buyer profile than a quiet cul-de-sac two blocks over. Aspect matters: a north-facing rear in Bulimba or Balmoral sells in a different bracket than a south-facing block of the same dimensions.

Flood overlay is the most significant of all. Within the same street, one side can sit outside the flood area while the other carries an overlay that directly affects insurance, lending, and the pool of buyers willing to compete. Buyers doing their research understand this. Sellers sometimes do not, which creates genuine surprises at appraisal time.

School catchment creates a third layer. The premium attached to state school catchments in the inner east is not theoretical. Buyers will pay materially more for a property inside a catchment boundary, and that premium is most acute in the months before school enrolment deadlines.

Distance to amenity rounds it out. A property within 400 metres of a good cafe strip or train station competes with a different buyer pool than one that requires a car for every errand, even if both are technically in the same suburb.

Knowing which factors apply to your property is not just interesting. It shapes how a campaign is framed, who it is shown to, and what the result looks like.
