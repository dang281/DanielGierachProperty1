## Caption

Norman Park sits on a ridge above the inner east with a view of the CBD and a housing stock that buyers keep coming back for. The streets climbing above Old Cleveland Road, the proximity to Whites Hill Reserve, and the Whites Hill State College catchment have made this one of Brisbane's most consistently sought-after family addresses.

The character of the suburb is specific. Timber and tin homes on elevated blocks, generous yards, and streets that feel settled. The proximity to Camp Hill and Coorparoo gives buyers a range of shopping, dining, and school options without needing to leave the inner east corridor. Hawthorne and Bulimba are close enough to be considered when comparing, and that comparison consistently works in Norman Park's favour for buyers prioritising land size and the school catchment.

Supply has remained tight. Owners here tend to stay. When a well-maintained character home comes to market on one of the ridge streets with views toward the city, the buyer pool is ready. Competition at that end of the market is not manufactured. It is structural: there are more buyers prepared to pay for this address than there are homes available at any given time.

For owners thinking about the 2031 selling window, February through May is Brisbane's most reliable inner east period. The buyers who will be active in that window are making their plans now.
