## Caption

Mt Gravatt attracts three distinct buyer types, and the suburb's consistent market performance comes precisely from that diversity. When one buyer type steps back, another is already in the queue.

The first is the owner-occupier family, drawn by the suburb's elevated position, its school catchment options, and the access to Westfield Garden City. These buyers are looking for a long-term home, and they tend to make considered decisions rather than reactive ones. They understand what Mt Gravatt offers and they arrive at open homes prepared.

The second is the investor. The combination of Griffith University and the Princess Alexandra Hospital to the northwest creates a permanent rental demand base that is largely insulated from the broader property cycle. A well-located rental property in Mt Gravatt is rarely vacant for long, and investors who understand the suburb's rental market price that stability into their purchase decisions.

The third is the downsizer. Mt Gravatt's proximity to medical facilities, its practical access, and the quality of its housing stock at the lower-maintenance end of the scale attract buyers who are trading space for convenience. The suburb's elevation means properties retain quality outlooks without requiring the land area that generates maintenance work.

In March, all three buyer types are active. Vendors who understand which type their property appeals to most directly, and who price and present accordingly, extract the best results the autumn market can offer.
