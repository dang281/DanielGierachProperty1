## Caption

By the time mid-April arrives, the main autumn listing wave has passed. The properties that came to market in February and March have either sold or stalled. The sellers who were thinking about it have mostly made their call.

But the buyers are still there.

A buyer who started searching in February and is still active in April is a materially different kind of buyer than the one who picks up a brochure at a spring open home out of mild curiosity. They have finance arranged. They have been through enough properties to understand what the market is actually offering. They have lost one or two properties they wanted. That experience sharpens decision-making considerably.

What this means for a seller considering whether to list now is straightforward. A property coming to market in mid-April is not walking into a crowded field. It is walking into a pool of motivated buyers with fewer properties competing for their attention. The buyers who have been searching for six or eight weeks are not browsing. They are ready.

The mid-autumn window does not get as much attention as the February or March rush. Sellers hear that autumn is a good time to list and assume they need to move in the first weeks of the season. The reality is that the quieter weeks after the main wave can be just as effective, and in some cases more so, precisely because the supply side has thinned out before the demand side has.

If you are weighing up whether to move now or hold until spring, that is worth a conversation. danielgierach.com
