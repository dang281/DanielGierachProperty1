## Caption

The mountain itself is one of Brisbane's underrated assets, and residents of Mt Gravatt live with it as part of their daily backdrop. Mt Gravatt Conservation Reserve runs through the suburb and offers walking trails and views that buyers increasingly factor into their lifestyle calculus when comparing suburbs at a similar price point. The fact that significant green space is protected adjacent to the residential area is a durable feature that will not be developed away.

Mt Gravatt sits about 10 kilometres south of the CBD and is anchored commercially by the activity along Logan Road and the concentration of business and education infrastructure around the Griffith University precinct. The suburb draws a broad buyer demographic: families attracted by Mt Gravatt State School, professionals working at Griffith or the surrounding medical and business precincts, and downsizers who want to stay within an active neighbourhood.

Properties on the elevated sections of the suburb, particularly those with aspect toward the reserve or with glimpses toward the city, tend to sell with a premium that surprises owners who have not kept close tabs on recent transactions. The land on and around the mountain ridge holds value that the flat sections of comparable suburbs cannot match.
