## Caption

Mt Gravatt has an anchor that few Brisbane suburbs can match: the mountain itself. The forested reserve at the suburb's heart gives residents access to walking tracks and lookout points that deliver genuine city and hinterland views, and properties on streets that border or overlook the reserve, including portions of Nathan Road and the upper sections of Newnham Road, carry that positioning in their price history.

Beyond the reserve, the suburb functions as a practical family base. Mt Gravatt State High School is one of the area's well-regarded secondary options, and proximity to Griffith University's Nathan campus makes the suburb relevant for academic households as well. That dual-demographic appeal, families and university-adjacent professionals, contributes to a buyer pool that is broader than a single suburb's typical reach.

The Garden City precinct at the suburb's commercial edge provides the retail infrastructure that supports everyday living, and the combination of Logan Road and the SE Freeway interchange makes most of metropolitan Brisbane accessible within a reasonable drive. For buyers working in multiple locations or managing a commute that changes across the week, that connectivity matters.

Character and postwar homes on the inner streets of Mt Gravatt have attracted growing interest from buyers who have been priced out of suburbs closer to the river. The value proposition is straightforward: land, liveability and a mountain reserve on the doorstep at a price point below the inner ring. If you own in Mt Gravatt and are assessing your options, the current position is at danielgierach.com/suburbs/mt-gravatt.
