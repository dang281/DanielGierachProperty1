## Caption

A contract falling through is not the end of a campaign. How you handle the next two weeks is.

Start with the diagnosis. A buyer exiting on finance had a lender problem, not a property problem. A buyer exiting on a building and pest report found something real, and the market will find it too if you do not address it. The reason determines what you do next.

A vendor who throws the listing straight back up publicly, same photos, same price, is telling the market nothing has changed. The buyers who passed before will pass again. A vendor who takes two weeks, addresses what the inspection revealed, adjusts price or presentation where it is needed, and relaunches with a specific reason the new campaign is stronger, lands in a different conversation.

Days on market accumulates even when the property is under contract. A property heading back to market on day 60 needs a clear story for the buyers looking at day 61.

The rule of thumb: transparency beats silence. Buyers who know exactly what happened and can price it in will transact. Buyers who sense information is being hidden will not.
