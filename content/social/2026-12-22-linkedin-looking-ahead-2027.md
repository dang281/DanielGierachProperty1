## Caption

As the year closes, a few things I will be watching closely in Brisbane's inner east in 2027.

The supply story. Listings in the inner east have run below five-year averages for most of 2026. If that continues into the first quarter of 2027, it will remain the dominant factor supporting prices rather than anything driven by interest rates or sentiment. What changes this picture is if a wave of sellers who have been waiting decide to move in the autumn window. That is the scenario I watch for in February and March.

The rate decision cycle. The RBA's meeting schedule and any shift in the cash rate will affect borrowing capacity directly. The buyers I work with are finance-ready at current rates. A cut, if one comes, expands the buyer pool. A hold maintains the status quo. Either scenario is workable.

The school catchment premium. In 2026, properties within the Balmoral State High, Cannon Hill Anglican College, and Whites Hill State College catchments continued to attract a demonstrable premium. I expect that to persist in 2027. Families with a two to three year planning horizon are making decisions now.

The Olympic infrastructure timeline. As 2032 gets closer and project milestones become visible, the inner east suburbs that sit near planned venues and transport upgrades tend to attract attention from investors with a longer hold strategy.
