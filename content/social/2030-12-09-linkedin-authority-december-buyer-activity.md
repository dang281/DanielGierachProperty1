## Caption

The buyers still active in December are a specific group, and understanding them is useful for anyone with a property still on the market or considering a late launch.

By December, the buyers who had a loose interest in the market have largely stepped away until February. The buyers who remain are serious for one of a small number of reasons.

The first group is the motivated upgrader or relocator with a specific deadline. A job start date, a school enrolment locked in, a lease ending in January. These buyers cannot wait for February. They are willing to act in December if the property is right.

The second group is the investor with a financial year objective. End-of-year timing matters for tax purposes for some investors, and December contracts can serve specific structuring needs.

The third group is the buyer who has missed out at multiple auctions through spring and is determined not to let another suitable property pass. These buyers are often the most decisive in December because their frustration has converted to motivation.

For a vendor with a property still on the market in December, the practical response is to ensure the property remains well-presented and accessible for inspections, and to resist the temptation to withdraw and re-list in February without a genuine reason to do so. A buyer exists for most well-priced inner east properties in December. The window is narrower, but the buyer quality tends to be high.
