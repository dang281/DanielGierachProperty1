## Caption

November open home attendance in Brisbane's inner east typically peaks in the first two weekends of the month. By the third weekend, the buyer who has been searching since August either has a property or has decided to wait until February. That pattern is relatively consistent year to year.

What this means for vendors launching in November is worth understanding clearly.

A property entering the market on November 1 will see its strongest open home attendance in the first two weekends. After the third weekend, buyer numbers at open homes tend to thin, not because the property is priced wrong or presented poorly, but because the buyer pool is self-selecting down to those still available and uncommitted.

This does not make a late November launch a bad decision. It makes it a different decision. A property launching in the final week of November is effectively targeting a specific buyer: one who is either still active after an extended search, or one who enters the market fresh with a particular brief. That buyer can still be the right buyer. But the broad competitive pool that characterised October is narrower by late November.

The practical implication for vendors: if you are going to list in November, earlier in the month gives you more of the concentrated buyer activity. If you cannot be ready until late November, that is not a reason not to proceed, but it is worth adjusting the campaign strategy accordingly rather than applying the same approach you would use in October.
