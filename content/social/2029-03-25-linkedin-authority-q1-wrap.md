## Caption

Three months into 2029, here is an honest read on what the Brisbane inner east property market has looked like.

January and February confirmed what the end of 2028 suggested: buyer depth in the inner east remains strong for well-priced properties in the right suburbs. The family buyer pool, owner-occupiers making planned moves in Hawthorne, Balmoral, Norman Park, and Camp Hill, has been active and willing to move quickly on properties that meet their criteria.

Days on market for correctly priced detached houses in these suburbs has stayed low. Properties that launched at or below the comparable sales evidence attracted offers in the first two weekends. Properties that launched above it accumulated days and then had to negotiate from a weaker position.

The autumn campaign window is now in its final and historically strongest phase. The four to five weekends between now and mid-May are where buyers who have been qualifying their options since January tend to make their moves. The competition in this window is real, particularly in the $1.2 million to $1.8 million range where the buyer pool for inner east houses is deepest.

For vendors who have not launched yet: the window is open but it is not indefinite. May campaigns can still produce strong results but they require a buyer who has not yet found what they are looking for, and that pool shrinks as the season progresses.

For vendors who have already completed campaigns this quarter: the results from well-prepared properties have been solid. The work done in preparation made the difference.
