## Caption

March is when the Brisbane inner east autumn campaign window reaches its most competitive point.

The buyer pool that built through February is now active and in negotiation on properties across the market. Pre-approvals have been refreshed. Building and pest contacts have been called. Families who need to be settled before May, either for school transitions or because they have already committed elsewhere, are not browsing anymore.

What this means for vendors is that a property that launches in March, correctly priced and well presented, enters the market at the point of highest buyer readiness. The first open home weekend will typically produce the strongest attendance of the campaign. If the pricing is right, the offers follow from there.

What it means for buyers is that March is not the time to take a relaxed approach. Properties that fit the brief tend to attract multiple interested parties quickly. The buyers who win in this window are the ones who have done their preparation before the first open home, not after it.

The autumn window historically runs through to mid-May before buyer depth begins to soften as winter approaches. Six weeks remain in the strongest part of the window. For vendors deciding whether to launch now or wait for spring, the market answer in Brisbane's inner east is usually: now.
