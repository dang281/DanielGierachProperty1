## Caption

Most advice about spring selling is written for Melbourne and Sydney. In those markets, spring peaks in October and fades into Christmas. Brisbane's inner east does not follow the same rhythm.

In the suburbs I work, the market runs hot from late October well into November. Buyer enquiry does not fall off a cliff at the start of November. What changes is the composition of the buyer pool. The casual looker has dropped out. What remains is the qualified, motivated buyer who has been searching for three months and is running out of time to settle before the year ends.

This is not a smaller buyer pool. It is a more efficient one. Open home numbers might be slightly lower, but the ratio of buyers who make offers to buyers who attend is considerably higher.

For sellers, mid-November is not the trailing edge of spring. It is the start of a different kind of market: fewer tyre-kickers, clearer conversations, faster decisions.

Properties that launch this week with realistic price expectations and a tight campaign have every reason to be under contract before December. That is the opportunity that gets overlooked when people apply standard spring-selling timelines to a Brisbane market that runs on a different calendar.
