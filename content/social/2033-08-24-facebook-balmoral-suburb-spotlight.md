## Caption

Balmoral in spring returns the suburb to the period where its character housing market is at its most competitive. The buyers who pursue Balmoral's pre-war and interwar Queenslanders through September and October are buyers who have been searching for a specific type of property for months or longer. When it arrives, they engage quickly.

The Balmoral Esplanade and the river access along Lores Bonney Park make the eastern section of the suburb distinctive within the inner east. Streets that run down toward the river with elevated outlooks from the ridge, including parts of Belmont Road and the streets around it, produce the kind of comparable sales evidence that makes Balmoral one of the more compelling price stories in the corridor.

The proximity to Hawthorne's Oxford Street dining precinct gives Balmoral residents lifestyle access to a precinct that is genuinely excellent without requiring them to pay Hawthorne's price premium for that proximity. That adjacency is part of what the Balmoral buyer understands intuitively and prices into their offer when they find the right property.

The heritage overlay that applies to street-facing character on many Balmoral properties is well understood by the buyers who pursue this suburb. They have already resolved that concern and are not filtering out on it.
