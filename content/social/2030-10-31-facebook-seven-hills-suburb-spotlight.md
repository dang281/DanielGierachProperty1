## Caption

Seven Hills is a suburb that sits quietly alongside some of the more frequently discussed inner east names, but its land values and the quality of its housing stock tell a consistent story. The suburb's elevated position gives views across the southern and eastern Brisbane corridor, and the streets closest to the reservoirs and parklands offer a natural environment that is hard to find at this distance from the CBD.

The buyer profile in Seven Hills includes families from Coorparoo and Camp Hill who have been priced out of their first preference but find Seven Hills delivers the block size, the school access, and the greenery they were targeting. The suburb straddles several primary school catchments, and buyers with children run through the options carefully.

Development pressure in Seven Hills has been measured. The suburb's topography limits the appeal of townhouse development on the steeper streets, which has preserved more of the original freestanding housing character than some neighbouring suburbs. That relative scarcity of medium-density stock is a factor in the sustained interest in house-on-land product here.

The access to Coorparoo's retail and dining precinct, just over the hill, gives Seven Hills residents the lifestyle of a suburb with a more active commercial strip without the density that goes with it.

For homeowners in Seven Hills, spring through October and November brings the inner east buyer pool to its annual peak.
