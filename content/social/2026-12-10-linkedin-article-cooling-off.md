## Caption

In Queensland, a buyer who signs a residential contract has five business days to withdraw. The cost is 0.25% of the purchase price. On a $900,000 purchase, that is $2,250.

Two things most buyers do not know about this right.

First, the cooling-off period cannot be waived by the seller. A vendor cannot include a clause in the contract that removes it. Under the Property Occupations Act 2014 (Qld), it is a statutory right that exists regardless of what the contract says.

Second, auction contracts have no cooling-off period. If you are the winning bidder at an unconditional auction, you are legally committed the moment the hammer falls. There is no five-day window to reconsider.

The cooling-off period is designed to give buyers a final opportunity to get independent legal advice, complete a building and pest inspection, or simply reconsider after the pressure of the offer process has passed. It is not a substitute for due diligence. A buyer who uses the cooling-off period to exit a contract has still paid the 0.25% fee and has lost whatever time the campaign took.

The better use of the cooling-off period is as a safety net, not a primary strategy. Do your due diligence before signing wherever possible. But know the right exists.
