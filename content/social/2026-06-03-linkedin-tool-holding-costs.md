## Caption

Yesterday's poll asked what holding costs ran to on your last Brisbane sale. The early responses are roughly split across the four bands, which is consistent with what I see in practice: most sellers underestimate the figure by about a third because the secondary costs accumulate quietly.

Holding costs break down into four buckets. Interest on the outstanding mortgage is usually the largest, sitting somewhere between $2,500 and $5,500 per month on a typical inner-east balance. Council rates and water average around $350 to $500 monthly when annualised. Insurance and utilities together add another few hundred. Then there are presentation costs that vary widely, including cleaning, gardening, and any styling left in place for the campaign.

For a home worth around $1.5 million, the combined monthly figure typically lands between $4,000 and $8,000. A 90 day campaign quietly carries somewhere in the range of $12,000 to $24,000 in holding cost before settlement arrives.

There is a calculator that walks through these inputs in plain English. It is useful for modelling whether a slightly shorter campaign at a more realistic price would actually net more than a longer campaign at an aspirational one. Link in the comments.
