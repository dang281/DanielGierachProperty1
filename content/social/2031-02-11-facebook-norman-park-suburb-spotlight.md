## Caption

Norman Park is the kind of suburb that holds its identity across market cycles. The Whites Hill State College catchment pulls family buyers from a wide radius. Whites Hill Reserve gives the suburb a green boundary that is not available to most inner east addresses at this price point. The ridge streets with CBD views are the address that buyers describe first when they are explaining to someone else why they chose Norman Park over Camp Hill or Hawthorne.

The housing stock is the other part of the answer. Character homes on generous blocks, streets that have not been subdivided to their limit, and a neighbourhood scale that feels deliberate rather than accidental. Norman Park sits above the flat grid of inner Brisbane in a way that buyers who have lived there know and buyers who have not yet lived there understand the moment they inspect.

Demand here does not arrive in waves. It is constant. The Whites Hill State College catchment is one of the mechanisms. The character housing stock is another. The proximity to Camp Hill's Old Cleveland Road strip and to Hawthorne's Oxford Street access means Norman Park residents can reach two of Brisbane's best inner east lifestyle strips without committing to the full premium of either address.

February in Norman Park is when the autumn market starts. Buyers who have been tracking this suburb through summer arrive at the first open homes of the year already decided. Sellers who have prepared are ready to meet them.
