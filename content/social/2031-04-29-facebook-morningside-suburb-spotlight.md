## Caption

Morningside is the suburb that sits just behind the headline names of the inner east and consistently outperforms what its price point might suggest. Buyers who cannot access Hawthorne or Bulimba at their budget look at Morningside and find a suburb that shares the same Cross River Rail corridor, the same general precinct character, and a housing stock of character homes that is genuinely appealing rather than a compromise.

Wynnum Road is the main artery, and the suburbs personality is shaped by the streets that run off it to the north and south rather than by the road itself. Apollo Road and the surrounding character home streets are where buyers focus their attention, and the Morningside State School catchment draws families who want the inner-east lifestyle without the premium that the river-adjacent suburbs command.

The Cross River Rail stations at Morningside and Cannon Hill have changed the daily transport calculation for residents. A fast, reliable train connection to the CBD and beyond is infrastructure that buyers now expect in this precinct, and it removes the commute friction that previously made the extra distance from the CBD feel more significant.

As the tail of autumn approaches, the Morningside market tends to see vendors who delayed their listing through the Easter period bring properties to market. For buyers who have been patient, late April can produce options that were not available a month earlier.
