## Caption

Q4 2031 is effectively done for the inner east. Here is what the quarter actually looked like.

October was the peak month. Buyer competition was at its deepest, open home numbers were high, and well-prepared properties that launched with accurate pricing moved quickly. The competitive end of the market, family homes in strong school catchments in the $1M to $1.5M range, saw genuine multi-offer situations. The $2M-plus range was more selective throughout.

November was a transition. The casual buyer pool thinned out and the remaining buyers were more focused. Properties that had been sitting from October with an overpriced guide saw their position clearly: the buyers had moved on to other properties and the price adjustment conversations became unavoidable. Properties that launched fresh in November with correct pricing into that motivated buyer pool transacted well.

December has been smaller in volume but decisive. The buyers who are still at open homes in December have a reason to be there. Transactions have been moving faster than usual because both sides want to be settled before Christmas.

The theme across the quarter, consistent with what I have observed in previous years, is that preparation was the dividing line. Properties that entered the campaign ready, with accurate pricing and genuine presentation, performed well regardless of which month they launched. Properties that cut corners, launched ahead of their preparation, or priced ahead of comparable sales struggled across all three months.

The market does not reward optimism. It rewards work.
