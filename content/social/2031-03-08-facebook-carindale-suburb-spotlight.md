## Caption

Carindale offers something that has become genuinely scarce across Brisbane's inner east: residential blocks with room for a pool, a shed, and a backyard where children can actually play.

Westfield Carindale anchors the suburb's identity for most of Brisbane. But the residential streets behind it tell a different story from the shopping centre car park. The housing stock runs to larger post-war homes with generous land, the kind of blocks that families in Morningside or Coorparoo look at and recognise as a trade the inner east no longer provides. The Gateway Motorway proximity makes Carindale practical for buyers who work across the city or need access to the airport corridor.

Autumn brings a specific buyer type to Carindale: the family that has been looking closer in, that has done the open home circuit through Camp Hill and Norman Park, that has recalibrated its expectations against its budget and arrived at the conclusion that space is the priority. These buyers are not settling for Carindale. They are choosing it because what they want, land, privacy, practical access to Westfield and the Gateway, is precisely what Carindale provides.

The market here is competitive in March because the supply of larger family homes with good presentation is constrained relative to the buyer pool. Vendors who present their properties properly and price accurately tend to find the autumn market works in their favour.
