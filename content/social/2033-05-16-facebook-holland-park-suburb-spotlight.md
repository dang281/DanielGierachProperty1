## Caption

Holland Park's position about seven kilometres south-east of the CBD puts it within reach of the city while keeping it firmly in the family suburb category. The suburb is not trying to be the inner city; it is doing something different, offering space, school catchment access and a residential environment that suits families who are past the phase of prioritising proximity to bars and restaurants.

The Cavendish Road State High School catchment is the suburb's most powerful property driver. Parents who have researched state schooling in Brisbane's south-east understand the school's reputation, and many purchasing decisions in Holland Park are made specifically to secure enrolment. That underlying demand is structural rather than cyclical, which means it supports property values even when sentiment softens.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war brick and timber on blocks that are larger than the inner east average. Renovation activity has been steady for years, with many homes now presenting well after significant investment. Those that have not been touched remain attractive to buyers with renovation intentions, particularly where the block size justifies the spend. Current medians in Holland Park reflect a market that has been buoyed by catchment demand and by families upgrading from more expensive inner suburbs.

Everyday retail is accessible via Cavendish Road and nearby Greenslopes, and bus services connect residents to the CBD.
