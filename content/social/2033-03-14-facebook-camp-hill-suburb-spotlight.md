## Caption

Camp Hill in the third week of March is where the autumn season earns its reputation. The buyer pool is at its most concentrated, the comparable sales from the previous six weeks have established the market benchmark, and the vendors who started their preparation in January are now on market and competing in the most active window of the year.

The streets off Holland Road and Cavendish Road carry a character that is consistent from block to block. Elevated positions, canopy trees, and the quiet that comes from measured street activity rather than through traffic. It is a suburb that rewards buyers who slow down and look carefully, and those buyers tend to be exactly the profile that produces strong auction outcomes. ⚠️ VERIFY

The Coorparoo State School catchment draws one of the most specific and financially committed buyer cohorts in the inner east. These buyers have made a school decision and they are working within the geography that decision requires. When the right property appears within the catchment zone, they act.

Camp Hill in mid-March is also the point at which vendors who have not yet listed are starting to run out of peak season. The Easter disruption is three to four weeks away. A property that lists after Easter is in a different market from one that lists this week.

If you have been thinking about selling in Camp Hill and have not yet started the preparation conversation, this post is the prompt to do so.
