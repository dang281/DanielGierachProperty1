## Caption

Oxford Street is the kind of retail and café strip that other Brisbane suburbs try to describe themselves as having. In Bulimba, it is simply the main street. Coffee at one end, dinner at the other, river access a few blocks south. The suburb has a character that is specific to this bend in the Brisbane River, and the buyers who want it know exactly what they are looking for.

The elevated streets above Oxford Street carry river views that command premiums with genuine structural support. These are not views that depend on trees staying trimmed. The character housing stock, predominantly timber and tin homes with wide verandahs and established gardens, is the kind that presents well and holds value through market cycles.

Bulimba's ferry access at Merthyr Road connects directly to the CBD without a car. Oxford Park provides a green corridor in the heart of the suburb. The school catchment options nearby, including Bulimba State School, serve families who have made the decision to be here for the long term.

Stock has remained tight. Bulimba is a suburb where properties are held across generations. When they do trade, the buyer pool is concentrated and prepared. Conditional offers are often not competitive here. Buyers arrive knowing the market.

For Bulimba owners thinking about 2031, the preparation conversation is worth starting in January.
