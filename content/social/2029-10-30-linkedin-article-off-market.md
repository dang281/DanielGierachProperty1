## Caption

"Off-market" has a premium sound to it. In practice, for a vendor, it almost always means reducing the number of buyers who know your property is available. Fewer buyers means less competition. Less competition means less pressure on price.

"We have a buyer ready to go" is the version of this you'll hear from an agent. What that sentence typically means is that the agent has a buyer they can transact with quickly, at a price the agent can negotiate, without the effort of a full marketing campaign. That is a good outcome for the agent. Whether it is a good outcome for you depends on what you could have achieved with the full buyer pool in the market.

Off-market sales do sometimes make sense. In very specific circumstances, including situations where privacy is a genuine requirement, where a property has very limited buyer appeal and would struggle on market, or where a vendor needs speed above price outcome, the trade-offs can work in their favour. But these are narrow situations, and the vendor needs to enter them with clear eyes about what they're giving up.

The more common situation is a vendor who is told they can avoid the campaign process without understanding what the campaign process is actually designed to do. The purpose of a well-run marketing campaign is to create competition. Competition is the mechanism that drives price above reserve.
