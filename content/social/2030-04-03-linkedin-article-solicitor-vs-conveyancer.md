## Caption

Most buyers in Queensland sign the contract first and call their solicitor second. That sequence is backwards, and it regularly costs people money.

The contract you sign is binding from the moment both parties execute it. Special conditions, inclusions, finance deadlines, and settlement dates are all locked in at signing. If your solicitor reviews it before you sign, they can catch the things that matter: a finance deadline that is too tight, an exclusion you thought was staying, a special condition that is not standard and could work against you.

The difference between a solicitor and a conveyancer comes down to complexity. A licensed conveyancer handles the title transfer and standard settlement process well. A solicitor can advise on contract risk, title issues, easements, and anything that falls outside the straightforward path.

For most inner east purchases, the choice comes down to the property itself. Standard house on a clean title in an established suburb: a good conveyancer is fine. Heritage overlay, easement through the yard, unusual contract condition, or deceased estate: get a solicitor before you sign, not after.
