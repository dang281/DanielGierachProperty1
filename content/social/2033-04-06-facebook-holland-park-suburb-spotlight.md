## Caption

Holland Park's elevated position, about seven kilometres south-east of the CBD, is one of its most underappreciated qualities. The land sits higher than surrounding suburbs in parts, which gives many homes good breezes and, where the block allows, pleasant outlooks. That topography combined with generous post-war lot sizes makes Holland Park an attractive target for families looking to renovate and stay for the long term.

The suburb is well served by Cavendish Road State High School, one of the more sought-after state school catchments in Brisbane's south-east. This drives consistent demand from families who want access to quality public education without paying private school fees, and it underpins property values even during softer market periods.

The housing stock leans heavily toward post-war brick and timber homes on good-sized blocks. Many have already been renovated; others still present genuine opportunity for buyers willing to put in work. Current medians in Holland Park reflect a market that rewards the well-presented property, with buyers competing seriously for anything in good condition with usable outdoor space.

Retail and café options are accessible via Cavendish Road and nearby Greenslopes, and the bus network connects residents to the CBD in reasonable time. It is a suburb that appeals to families prioritising space, catchment access and relative affordability compared to the inner east.
