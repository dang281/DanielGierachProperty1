## Caption

The gap between a well-prepared property and an unprepared one in Brisbane's inner east is not a matter of renovation. It is a matter of presentation, and the difference in final price regularly runs into five figures.

Preparation starts four to six weeks before the first open home, not in the week the listing goes live. Photography is the first impression for most buyers, who research online before attending in person. Properties with strong photography attract more enquiry, more open home attendance, and more competition. That competition produces better results.

What strong photography requires is a property that is genuinely ready: furniture appropriately arranged or styled, surfaces cleared, gardens presentable, minor defects fixed, and every room lit correctly. A professional property stylist is not a luxury for inner east campaigns at the $1.2M+ level; it is a standard part of a well-run campaign.

The preparation list is longer than most vendors expect. Fixing a dripping tap, repainting scuffed doors, cleaning gutters, and having carpets professionally cleaned are not optional extras. They are the baseline that buyers expect when they walk through a property in this price range.

The article below covers every step of the preparation process in the order it needs to happen, with timelines and what each step actually involves.
