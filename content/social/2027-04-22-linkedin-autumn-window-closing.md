## Caption

The buyers who have been active in Brisbane's inner east since February are not casual. By late April, they have been through fifteen to twenty properties. They know the price points. They know which streets trade above suburb median and which do not. When something well-presented comes to market in the next three weeks, these buyers do not need much time.

That is what makes this part of the year so productive for sellers. You are not dealing with browsing. You are dealing with buyers who have done their homework and are ready to commit.

By June, attendance at open homes in Brisbane's inner east typically drops as the colder months reduce urgency. Some buyers pause. The pool thins. It does not stop, but the character of it changes. The concentrated, motivated buyer pool of autumn is a different condition to what mid-year looks like.

This is not a reason to rush a campaign before it is ready. It is a reason to have the conversation now, rather than in three months. If you have been watching the market, thinking about timing, or wondering whether to list before or after winter, the answer for most inner-east sellers is that the next three to five weeks are the most favourable window of the next six months.

If that is relevant to your situation, I am happy to give you a straight read on current conditions in your street.
