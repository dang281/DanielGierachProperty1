## Caption

Running a campaign in November for a December or early January settlement is not the right move for every vendor, but for those it suits, it is worth understanding what actually works in its favour.

Three things I consistently observe about the November campaign.

The first is buyer quality. By November, the casual inspectors have largely gone. The people attending open homes are buyers who have been searching since the middle of the year. They have seen a large number of properties. They know the market well. They know what things are worth. That means less time on price education and more direct negotiation.

The second is the settlement appeal. Vendors who settle before Christmas arrive in the new year without that transaction hanging over them. They do not carry the weight of an unsettled property through December and January. For many vendors, particularly those who are downsizing or relocating, that clean start is genuinely valuable.

The third is a premium for speed. Buyers who are motivated to complete before Christmas will sometimes pay a small but real premium for the certainty of a quick, clean handover. They are not looking for a drawn-out negotiation. They want the property and they want it done. A vendor who can offer that certainty is in a strong position.

This window does not suit vendors who are underprepared, who need to find their next property first, or who are not comfortable with the compressed timeframe. But for the right vendor, it is a strong window. And the buyer pool in it is arguably the most committed of the year.
