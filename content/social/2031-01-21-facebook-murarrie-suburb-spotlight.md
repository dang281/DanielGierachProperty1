## Caption

Murarrie sits at the affordable end of the inner east corridor and that position has been attracting a specific, purposeful buyer type for the past several years. First-home buyers who want an inner east postcode at an entry price that the suburbs to its west no longer offer. Investors who are watching where the development trajectory is heading as industrial land near the Gateway Motorway continues to transition.

The suburb's geography places it well. Cannon Hill is immediately to the west. Morningside is close to the north. The Bulimba Ferry is accessible, putting the CBD within a practical commute without the inner city price point. The Gateway Motorway access to the south and east matters for buyers who travel that corridor for work.

The character of Murarrie is still forming in parts. That is not a criticism. It is the honest description of a suburb in transition, and buyers who enter a suburb during transition, rather than after it, tend to capture the movement that the next cohort will pay for. The residential pockets away from the industrial boundary are more established than the suburb's overall profile suggests.

For investors and first-home buyers, the question Murarrie poses is straightforward: do you want the address at today's price, or at the price it will carry once the transition is complete?
