## Caption

There is a straightforward case to be made for Mt Gravatt East as one of Brisbane's better-value middle-ring options. Ten kilometres from the CBD, the suburb offers land sizes that have largely disappeared from suburbs closer to the city, school and retail access that makes day-to-day life practical, and price points that remain within reach for families who have been priced out of the inner east.

Cavendish Road runs through the suburb and provides the commercial backbone: everyday retail, takeaway, medical services and small business all concentrated along a corridor that residents can access without significant travel. The TAFE Queensland campus in the area supports the rental market and means investors have a consistent pool of tenant demand to draw from, alongside family renters and owner-occupiers.

The housing stock is a mix of original post-war homes on good blocks and more recently renovated or extended properties. The unrenovated home still exists here in meaningful numbers, and that supply of renovation potential is part of what draws buyers who see the opportunity to add value over time. Current medians in Mt Gravatt East offer a notable contrast to comparable land sizes in Morningside or Cannon Hill, and buyers who run that comparison often land in Mt Gravatt East as a result.

Road access to the CBD and to Garden City is direct and uncomplicated, and bus services fill the public transport role.
