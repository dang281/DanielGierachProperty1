## Caption

Hawthorne offers a combination of lifestyle assets that most inner east suburbs can only partly match, and buyers who have specifically targeted this suburb know exactly what they are coming for.

Oxford Street is the centrepiece of daily life here. The dining and cafe strip has genuine depth, and it functions as a local high street rather than a destination. Residents walk to it. That kind of walkable amenity is increasingly rare in Brisbane's inner east and the market prices it accordingly.

The Hawthorne ferry terminal adds a City Cat connection to the CBD that takes the morning commute off the roads entirely. For buyers who are comparing Hawthorne to other riverside or near-riverside suburbs, the ferry access is often the detail that tips the decision.

Most streets fall within the Bulimba State School catchment, which is a draw for families looking at this part of the inner east. Combined with the lifestyle offering on Oxford Street and the river access nearby, the catchment adds another layer to what is already a strong case for the suburb.

The housing stock matters here too. Character homes command a significant premium over units in Hawthorne, and the buyer profile reflects it. Upsizers and interstate arrivals who have researched the suburb arrive with a clear brief and are comparing individual properties within it, not the suburb against alternatives.

If you own in Hawthorne and are thinking about your options, I can give you a clear picture of where the market sits right now.
