## Caption

An appraisal is a data conversation, but the decision it leads to is almost never purely about data.

I have done enough appraisals to recognise the real question underneath most of them: is now the right time for us? That is not a question about comparable sales or days on market. It is a question about life stage, financial position, what the next chapter looks like, and whether the numbers are close enough to make that chapter possible.

The appraisal that is useful to a vendor is the one that gives them an honest number, a clear sense of the current buyer pool for their property, and an honest assessment of what preparation would change about both.

What the appraisal is not: a negotiation opener, a figure designed to win the listing, or a number that cannot be explained with evidence. Vendors who have received inflated appraisals elsewhere know what I am describing. A figure that does not survive first contact with the market costs sellers time, campaign momentum, and money.

The vendors who make good decisions at appraisal time are the ones who come prepared. They have thought about their timing, they have a rough sense of where they want to be, and they are looking for a number they can trust rather than one that tells them what they want to hear.
