## Caption

Most buyers have a property they still think about. The one they inspected three times, did the numbers on, and then didn't act on in time. Someone else moved faster, or they waited for a second open that never came, or they simply weren't sure enough.

That experience shapes how buyers approach every subsequent decision, sometimes for the better, sometimes not. Occasionally it drives overcorrection: moving too quickly on the next property out of fear rather than conviction.

Curious how many people here have been through it.
