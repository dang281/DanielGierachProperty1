## Caption

Most sellers in Queensland do not know they are required to provide a disclosure statement before a contract is entered into.

Under the Property Law Act 2023 (Qld), sellers must complete a Seller Disclosure Statement covering matters including body corporate information, encumbrances, and other prescribed matters. This is not a formality. If the disclosure statement is not provided correctly, the buyer has the right to terminate the contract at any time before settlement.

What this means in practice: the disclosure statement needs to be ready before your campaign launches, not as an afterthought once a buyer is under contract. Agents and sellers who treat it as a post-offer step are creating unnecessary risk.

The other thing sellers underestimate is how much information the disclosure covers. A property with an easement, a heritage overlay, or an ongoing dispute with a neighbour requires careful handling.

I have written a full breakdown of what Queensland's seller disclosure obligations cover, what happens if they are not met, and how to prepare before launching a campaign.
