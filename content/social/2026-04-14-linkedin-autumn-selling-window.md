## Caption

April is not a season most sellers plan around.

Most of the conversation in real estate centres on spring: more buyers, more energy, more competition. That framing holds for many markets. Inner east Brisbane runs differently.

The buyers active in April have typically been searching since January. They have missed out on properties, narrowed their brief, and are ready to move. They are not browsing. Stock levels in April are also lower than they will be in September, which means less competition for the seller and less negotiating power for the buyer.

What this creates is a window. Not a secret, not a trick, just a supply and demand reality that plays out most years.

What I observe at open homes through March and April: attendance holds. Serious pre-approved buyers make up a higher proportion of the room than they do in October, when you also get the curious neighbours and the early researchers. Days on market tend to be shorter. Vendor discounting tends to be tighter.

None of this means every April listing reaches its best result. Price, presentation, and campaign quality still determine outcomes. But the seasonal context matters, and it tends to get overlooked in favour of the default spring narrative.

If you have been thinking about selling and holding off for the right time, the current window is worth a conversation.
