## Caption

LMI is a one-off premium paid by the borrower when the loan-to-value ratio exceeds 80%. It protects the lender, not the borrower.

On a $900,000 Brisbane property with a 10% deposit, LMI can cost $15,000 to $25,000 or more. That money adds to the loan balance and earns you nothing.

The argument for paying it: you enter the market earlier and capture more growth. In a market that has been rising, the growth gained from entering 12 months earlier often outweighs the LMI cost. The argument against: it's dead money on a cost that compounds over the life of the loan.

In Brisbane's inner east, where median prices have been moving, the entry-timing argument carries weight. But it depends entirely on what prices do from the point you buy.

What's your view?
