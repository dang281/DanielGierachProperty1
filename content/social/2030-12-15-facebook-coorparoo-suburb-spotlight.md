## Caption

Coorparoo heading into Christmas 2030 has a property market that reflects the broader inner east story: the properties with correct pricing and good presentation have resolved through spring, and the December stock is a mix of properties that came to market late and are now navigating the quieter buyer pool.

That pattern does not make December a bad time to be selling in Coorparoo. It makes it a time where the pricing strategy and presentation standard carry more weight than they do in the competitive spring market. The buyers who are active in December in Coorparoo are typically decisive. They have spent the year attending opens. They do not need multiple viewings to form a view.

The suburb's fundamentals have not changed through the market cycle. Old Cleveland Road access, proximity to Stones Corner and Camp Hill, Coorparoo State School for primary, and the suburb's natural diversity of housing stock from character homes through to apartment complexes have sustained its appeal to a wide buyer range.

The streets on the elevated section of Coorparoo, toward the boundary with Seven Hills and Camp Hill, consistently outperform the flat section around the shopping centre. Buyers who understand the suburb know this and target their search accordingly.

For Coorparoo homeowners considering a campaign before February, December is not too late.
