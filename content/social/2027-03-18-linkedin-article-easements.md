## Caption

Easements come up in almost every property transaction in Brisbane's inner east, and they cause more confusion than almost anything else in a contract.

An easement is a right that a third party has over part of your land. In the inner east, the most common types are drainage easements, utility easements, and access easements.

A drainage easement along the rear boundary is the most common type you will encounter. It is there because council or a utility has the right to access that strip of land to maintain drainage infrastructure. In practice, it affects what you can build over it, but it rarely affects the use, enjoyment, or value of the property materially.

An access easement is different. If another property has a right of way across part of yours, that affects how you can use that area and who can enter. These are less common but worth understanding clearly before you buy.

The key principle: not all easements are created equal. A drainage easement along the back fence is almost always a non-issue. An access easement through the middle of the backyard is not.

When a property you are interested in shows an easement on the title, ask your solicitor to explain what it covers, who benefits from it, and what you can and cannot do with that part of the land. Do not assume it is a problem. Understand what it actually is.
