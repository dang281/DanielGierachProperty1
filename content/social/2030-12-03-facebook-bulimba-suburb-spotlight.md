## Caption

Bulimba has remained one of the most consistently desirable addresses in the inner east through 2030. The reasons are straightforward: the Oxford Street lifestyle precinct, the river access and foreshore, the Bulimba ferry terminal connecting to the CBD and New Farm, and a residential street character that has resisted the density pressure visible in some other inner east postcodes.

The streets between Moray Street, Amelia Street, and the river are where the suburb's prestige market operates. River glimpses and river views from elevated positions in this precinct attract buyers who have come from the highest end of the Hawthorne, Balmoral, and Morningside markets and found Bulimba's concentration of lifestyle assets in a small geographic footprint compelling.

Bulimba State School has a strong local reputation, and the suburb's proximity to Churchie on Oaklands Parade in East Brisbane gives families with boys a private secondary option within comfortable distance.

December in Bulimba typically sees a small but highly motivated buyer pool. The buyers attending opens in December in this suburb are usually well-established, have completed their research over the spring, and are making a decision rather than exploring. For vendors with the right property, that focused buyer pool can produce a strong December result.
