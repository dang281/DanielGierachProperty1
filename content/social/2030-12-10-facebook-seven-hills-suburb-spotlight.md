## Caption

Seven Hills is one of the inner east suburbs where a summer listing can work well precisely because the buyer pool that remains active in December and January is self-selecting toward serious. The suburb does not attract the casual open home visitor in the same way that more prominent inner east addresses do, which means the buyers attending an open home in Seven Hills in December are usually there with a clear brief.

The elevated streets on Seven Hills' ridge, with views south across the Brisbane corridor, attract a buyer who is specifically after position and space. The block sizes on the better streets give residents the garden depth and setback that the flat suburbs closer to the river trade away in return for walkability. It is a different living proposition, and the buyers who choose it have usually thought carefully about what they are optimising for.

The proximity to Coorparoo's commercial and dining strip, reachable over the ridge, gives Seven Hills residents access to the lifestyle assets of a more active suburb while living in the quieter residential character that their own streets provide.

School catchments in Seven Hills cover a number of primary school options depending on the specific street, with Coorparoo State School serving parts of the suburb and other options available to the north.

For Seven Hills homeowners, December and January are lower-stock periods where a well-presented property faces limited competition.
