## Caption

On a $1 million purchase in Queensland, transfer duty adds approximately $38,025 to your costs. Most buyers underestimate this figure by $10,000 or more when they are working out how much deposit they actually need.

This matters because transfer duty is not a cost that adds to your equity. It is paid directly to the state government before you receive the keys. It does not come back. When buyers miscalculate it, they either find themselves short at settlement or they eat into a buffer they were counting on for other things.

Queensland uses a tiered rate system for transfer duty, which means the rate you pay is not flat, it increases as the purchase price climbs. Most online calculators will get you close, but understanding how the tiers work helps you see exactly where the number comes from and plan accordingly.

For first home buyers, there is a concession available for purchases up to $700,000 that can significantly reduce the duty payable. Above that threshold the concession does not apply, which is worth knowing if you are stretching to buy in the inner east. The rate structure also differs between owner-occupiers and investors, with investors paying a higher rate at most price points.
