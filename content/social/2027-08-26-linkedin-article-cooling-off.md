## Caption

Sellers in Queensland cannot waive the five-business-day cooling-off period. It is a statutory buyer right. It is not a negotiating point. A vendor who assumes it can be removed from the contract is mistaken, and so is the buyer who does not know they have it.

There are two things about the cooling-off period that regularly catch people off guard. The first is that auction contracts carry no cooling-off period at all. A buyer who signs a contract at auction is bound immediately, with no exit right. That distinction matters enormously for buyers who have not had their solicitor review the contract before auction day.

The second is the exit fee. If a buyer exercises their cooling-off right and walks away from a signed contract within the five-business-day window, they are not walking away for free. The exit costs 0.25% of the purchase price. On a $900,000 property, that is $2,250. Not a trivial amount, and not something most buyers are aware of when they assume cooling-off means they can simply change their mind without consequence.

Understanding these mechanics before you sign, whether you are buying or selling, is the kind of preparation that avoids costly confusion at a point in the transaction where there is very little room to move.
