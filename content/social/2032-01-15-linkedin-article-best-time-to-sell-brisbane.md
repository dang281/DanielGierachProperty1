## Caption

Most advice about when to sell property in Australia is built around Melbourne and Sydney data. Spring listings surge, autumn is the second season, and the rest of the year is quieter. That pattern does not transfer directly to Brisbane's inner east.

Brisbane has a different seasonal rhythm. Summer heat reduces buyer willingness to attend open homes in December and January, and competition from end-of-year holidays fragments the buyer pool. The spring surge that drives Sydney and Melbourne results is weaker here and less predictable.

What the inner east data consistently shows is that the autumn window, from late February through to the end of May, is the most reliable campaign period for vendors. Buyers who did not find what they wanted in late 2031 are active and financially ready. The competition from other listings is lower than in a typical spring campaign. Weather is agreeable. Properties photograph well in the autumn light.

The second pattern worth understanding is that the inner east does not have a single peak season in the way that a more homogeneous suburb does. Prestige riverfront stock and family homes on school catchment boundaries behave differently. Timing a campaign to the specific buyer pool your property attracts is more important than following a generic calendar.

In a Brisbane Olympic year, the first half of 2032 has additional buyer motivation from people who want to be settled before July. That shifts the optimal campaign window earlier than usual for many inner east vendors.

The full article covers the seasonal data, what drives it, and how to think about your specific property and buyer profile.
