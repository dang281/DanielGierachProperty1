## Caption

Most sellers think about the pre-settlement inspection as a formality. The buyer walks through, everything looks fine, settlement proceeds.

That is how it works when the preparation is done. When it is not, the pre-settlement inspection becomes a last-minute negotiation that creates delays, demands for compensation, and in some cases a threat to the contract itself.

Buyers are entitled to one pre-settlement inspection, and they are entitled to find the property in the same condition as when the contract was signed. That means the inclusions listed in the contract are still there, nothing has been damaged during the move-out, and all appliances and systems are working.

The issues I see most commonly: the seller has taken a light fitting that was never excluded in the contract, the moving company has damaged a door frame or wall, or the garden has been left in a significantly worse state than when the buyer inspected.

None of these need to happen. A methodical approach in the week before settlement prevents all of them.
