## Caption

Holland Park is coming back into focus for buyers who sat out the winter and the Games season and are now ready to move.

The post-Olympic period brings a particular type of buyer back to the inner south. They have been watching the market since autumn, they know what they want, and they have a budget that has been tested against Coorparoo and Camp Hill. Holland Park is often where they land when they want more than those suburbs are delivering at the price they can pay.

Echlin Street and the streets around Musgrave Park give the suburb a local character that is distinct from the main road corridors. The post-war homes here carry renovation opportunity that owner-occupiers with a long hold in mind respond to. The buyer who buys well in Holland Park and holds it typically does well over time.

Holland Park State High School is a secondary school catchment anchor that brings a specific buyer demographic to the suburb. Families who are making a ten-year decision based on secondary schooling need to be in the catchment before the relevant year, and that urgency drives purchases in a way that creates consistent demand.

Spring is when the renovation-ready buyer, the catchment-driven family buyer, and the value-seeking buyer from Coorparoo and Camp Hill all come back to the market at the same time. That overlap creates the conditions for strong competition on well-presented properties.
