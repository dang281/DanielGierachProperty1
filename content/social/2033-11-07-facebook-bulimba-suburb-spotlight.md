## Caption

Bulimba's appeal to the buyers I work with comes down to a combination that very few inner east suburbs can match: walkable lifestyle, direct CBD access without a car, a genuinely excellent primary school catchment, and a streetscape character that buyers do not find everywhere.

Oxford Street is one of Brisbane's best suburban dining and lifestyle strips. The concentration of cafes, restaurants, wine bars, and independent retailers means Bulimba residents genuinely use their suburb for daily life rather than driving elsewhere for most things. That walkability is a quality that buyers who have lived in Bulimba are reluctant to give up, and buyers arriving from other suburbs and interstate quickly understand why.

The City Cat ferry terminal provides CBD access that removes the commute friction most inner-city workers are trying to solve. For a professional who wants a suburb with river access and a real community feel, Bulimba resolves both requirements. The Bulimba Riverside reserve adds parkland that works for families, dog owners, and anyone who values daily access to the river.

Bulimba State School sits within the suburb and is one of the most sought-after primary catchments in the inner east. ⚠️ VERIFY. Families targeting this school have a specific geographic search, and Bulimba properties within the catchment are subject to that concentrated demand.

The buyer profile here tends to prioritise location and lifestyle over land size. They are typically willing to trade a larger block in an outer suburb for the access and community that Bulimba delivers.
