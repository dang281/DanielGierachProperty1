## Caption

Three weeks into the spring selling season, a pattern is establishing itself in the inner east that is worth naming directly.

Properties that arrive at auction with a competitive registered bidder pool are achieving strong results. Not necessarily surprising results, but results that validate well-researched pricing and reflect genuine market depth. The buyers are there. They are prepared. The ones who miss out at one auction are back the following week.

What is not performing is the outlier pricing. Vendors who have entered the market at a figure that requires a buyer to substantially exceed recent comparable evidence are sitting. The spring buyer pool in the inner east is informed. They have attended enough opens and seen enough results to know when a price guide is aspirational rather than evidential.

The clearest signal from the past three weeks is that correct pricing unlocks competition, and competition is what produces results above expectation. Overpricing removes competition at the point of maximum buyer activity, which is the worst possible time to lose it.

From an open home perspective, attendance is strong across the board. Morningside, Camp Hill, and Norman Park are seeing consistent numbers. The properties converting interest into registered bidders and contract buyers are, almost without exception, the ones where presentation and pricing are aligned with current market evidence.

If you are watching the spring market before deciding whether to list, what you are seeing is the market at its most active. More at danielgierach.com.
