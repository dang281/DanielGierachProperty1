## Caption

Mt Gravatt functions as a genuine hub for Brisbane's south-east, and that status shapes the property market in ways that are easy to underestimate. Garden City, one of Brisbane's larger shopping centres, anchors the suburb's commercial identity and draws foot traffic and services from a wide catchment. For residents, that means everyday convenience is simply part of the deal.

The housing stock is diverse. Post-war homes on reasonable lots sit alongside newer townhouse developments and medium-density infill, giving buyers at different life stages a realistic entry point. Families are drawn by the school options, including access to Griffith University which adds a student rental market that investors factor into their decisions. Downsizers also feature prominently, particularly those who want to simplify without leaving the area where they have lived for decades.

Current medians in Mt Gravatt reflect a market that has held up well, supported by the suburb's broad appeal across multiple buyer demographics. A well-presented home in good condition tends to attract genuine competition, particularly in the sub-$1.5 million range where the pool of qualified buyers is deep.

The bus network is the primary public transport link to the CBD, supplemented by good road access via major arterials. Proximity to Griffith, the Mater and other health and education employment hubs means the suburb benefits from a stable underlying rental and owner-occupier demand base.
