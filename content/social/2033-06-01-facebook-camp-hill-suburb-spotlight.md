## Caption

Camp Hill has a strong gravitational pull for families who have spent time in Coorparoo and reached the point where they need more space or want a better block position. The two suburbs share a boundary and a similar character, but Camp Hill's elevation and the sense of quiet that comes with it tends to attract buyers who are ready to step up from their first inner-south purchase.

The elevated terrain across parts of Camp Hill delivers something that matters in Brisbane's climate: natural ventilation and, on many blocks, a view across neighbouring rooftops toward the city or the hills to the east. Homes that benefit from this position attract buyers prepared to pay for that quality, and the price difference between a high-ground property and one in a lower pocket is a consistent feature of the market.

Coorparoo's café strip on Old Cleveland Road is accessible from Camp Hill, so residents can access the village atmosphere without living directly on top of the commercial activity. That balance of proximity and residential calm is part of what makes Camp Hill a popular destination for families at the second or third property stage of their buying journey.

Stock levels tend to be tight in Camp Hill, partly because owners who have found their ideal position in the suburb are reluctant to leave.
