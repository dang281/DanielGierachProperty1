## Caption

October buyers in Brisbane's inner east are a different proposition to the people who walked through properties in August.

By October, the buyer pool has been active for months. Many have attended five to ten open homes before they step into yours. They have read the comparable sales, they have a broker on speed dial, and in a lot of cases they have already missed out on one or two properties they wanted. That experience does something to a buyer. It sharpens them.

The dominant demographic in inner east spring campaigns is family buyers competing for homes in school catchment zones. These buyers have a deadline. They want to be moved in and settled before the Christmas school break, or positioned to start the new school year in January without disruption. That deadline is not abstract. It converts into decisions and into offers.

Motivated buyers with real deadlines are real buyers. When an agent tells a vendor that competition is strong in October, this is what they mean. The people walking through are not browsing. They are comparing your property against the two or three others they have already seen this month, and they are ready to move if the fit is right.

Properties that do well in October share a few consistent traits: space for children, proximity to parks and schools, and indoor-outdoor flow that makes family living feel easy. These are not selling points conjured by marketing. They are the features this specific buyer pool is actively seeking.

The window is real and the buyers are ready. danielgierach.com
