## Caption

A comparable sales report is the primary tool for understanding what a property is worth. Most people read them incorrectly.

The most common mistake is treating all comparables as equal. A three-bedroom home on Riverview Road in Balmoral is not comparable to a three-bedroom home on a busy road in the same suburb. A renovated character home is not comparable to the same floor plan unrenovated. A property that sold 18 months ago is not comparable to the current market in the same way a property that sold six weeks ago is.

The comparables that matter are the ones that share the same physical position (elevated vs flat, corner vs mid-block, quiet street vs main road), the same configuration (bedroom and bathroom count, garage), the same renovation level, and that sold within the last 90 days. That list is often shorter than it looks.

If there are no strong comparables in the immediate suburb, the analysis expands to the neighbouring suburb with similar characteristics. If those properties are consistently selling at a certain level, and the subject property is materially different in some way, that difference needs to be quantified and explained.

The agent presenting a comparable sales report should be able to explain every adjustment they have made. If they cannot explain why one comparable was included and another was not, the report is not doing the work it needs to do. Ask the question.
