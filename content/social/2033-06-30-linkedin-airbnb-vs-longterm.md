## Caption

Platform fees, cleaning, insurance, and maintenance add between $15,000 and $25,000 per year to the cost of running a short-term rental. The net return is often within a few thousand dollars of a well-managed long-term tenancy, with significantly more effort and unpredictability attached.

That is the number that changes the short-term rental conversation for most investors, and it is the one that rarely features in the headline comparisons.

The gross revenue from an inner east Brisbane property on Airbnb can look impressive, particularly in a good week or a strong holiday period. But the platform takes a cut. Every changeover requires a professional clean at a rate that adds up quickly over fifty-two weeks. Insurance for a short-term rental property is more expensive than landlord insurance for a long-term tenancy, and the policy conditions are different. Maintenance on a property with high turnover accumulates faster than on a stable tenancy. And the owner's time, or the cost of a manager who handles it all, is real.

When you net out those costs, many inner east properties end up within a narrow margin of what a quality long-term tenant would have paid. The difference is that the long-term tenant requires a fraction of the operational overhead.

The article also covers the council compliance picture for short-term rentals in Brisbane, the insurance requirements, and the questions to ask before deciding which path suits your property and your situation.
