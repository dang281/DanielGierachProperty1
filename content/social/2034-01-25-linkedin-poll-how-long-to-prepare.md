## Caption

One of the most consistent gaps I see in property campaigns is the difference between how long vendors think preparation will take, and how long it actually takes.

Most people initially estimate they need two or three weeks. Once they sit down and work through the actual list: the painting, repairs, decluttering, landscaping, styling, photography, and the time it takes to book and complete tradework, the realistic timeline is often double that.

A proper preparation window for a well-presented family home in the inner east is typically four to eight weeks. That is before you consider booking delays, which are common at the start of the year when tradespeople are fully committed.

Getting the preparation timeline right matters because it directly determines your launch date. And your launch date determines which buyer pool you are selling into.

If you were listing your home for sale in 2034, how long would you realistically need to prepare?
