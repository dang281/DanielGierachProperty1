## Caption

The October buyer in Brisbane's inner east is not browsing. They have typically been watching the market since late winter, attending opens since early September, and have finance confirmed. By October they are deciding, not researching. That's a meaningfully different person to deal with than the one who turns up to a September open just to see inside a house they walk past regularly.

In Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, Camp Hill, and Morningside, the October buyer profile tends to cluster around a few clear categories. There are local upgraders who know the suburb intimately and have been waiting for a property that fits a specific brief. There are buyers relocating from interstate, often from Sydney or Melbourne, who have visited multiple times and are ready to commit. And there are investors who track inner east yields closely and understand the land value story that underpins the precinct.

What these groups share is that they're not going to wait indefinitely. The buyer who misses the right property in October often recalibrates rather than paying more for the next one. They pause, reassess, and sometimes drop out of the market until autumn. Vendors who have a property that fits the brief and present it well in October are in front of the most motivated buyer pool of the year.

Understanding who is actually in the room at your open home changes how you think about preparation, pricing, and campaign strategy.
