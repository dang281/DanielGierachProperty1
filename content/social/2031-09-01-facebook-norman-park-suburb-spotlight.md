## Caption

Spring has arrived in Norman Park, and the suburb shows it well. The character homes along the ridge, the green reserves, and the gardens of the established streets come alive in September in a way that gives the suburb one of its most compelling presentations of the year. Buyers who have been delaying their inspections through winter are now active.

The Whites Hill State College catchment remains the most powerful single demand driver in the inner east. Families who have decided on the catchment and narrowed down to Norman Park are typically comparing a small number of properties at any given time, and they will move quickly when the right one appears. In spring, with buyer numbers at seasonal peaks, competition among those families intensifies in a way that delivers strong outcomes for vendors.

Norman Park's ridge streets deserve specific attention in September. The views available from elevated positions in the suburb are not available year-round in the same way; September's clear skies and longer days show those outlooks at their best. For any owner on the ridge considering selling, the month ahead is the time to do it.

The village around the train station continues to be a daily amenity that residents rely on. That walkable connection between home, coffee, and transport is a selling point that resonates with a broad buyer base, not just families.
