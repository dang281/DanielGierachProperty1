## Caption

East Brisbane sits at the inner city edge of what buyers mean when they say "inner east." The suburb borders Kangaroo Point and sits less than 3 kilometres from the CBD, which means its resident profile includes professionals who want city access without city address pricing, and buyers who have found that New Farm and Woolloongabba prices have moved beyond their ceiling.

The housing stock in East Brisbane is mixed. The character houses on the elevated streets toward the Dutton Park boundary are the most consistently competed-for. The townhouse and apartment supply is significant and serves a different buyer and renter profile. Understanding which of those product types you are in matters for expectations.

Churchie (Anglican Church Grammar School) is located in East Brisbane on Oaklands Parade, and the suburb's proximity to the school makes it a natural residential choice for families with boys in the Churchie system. That catchment effect on nearby residential streets is real and consistent.

The Kangaroo Point Bridge connection has improved active transport links between East Brisbane, the CBD, and South Bank, adding a walkability and cyclability premium to riverside and near-riverside properties. That infrastructure investment has been reflected in buyer enquiry for properties within easy walking distance of the bridge access.

For homeowners in East Brisbane's character home streets, November is a productive window.
