## Caption

If the target is a September or October listing in Brisbane's inner east, the preparation starts now. Not in August. Not when the weather turns. Now.

Here is what the timeline actually requires. A property going to market in September needs the photography done in August, when the gardens are at their best and the light is right. Styling needs to be arranged and delivered in the week before the shoot. Any pre-campaign repairs, from the compliance items to the cosmetic ones, need to be done and fully settled before the stylist arrives. The solicitor needs to be briefed and the contract and disclosure documents need to be in order before the campaign opens.

Working backwards, compliance issues that are identified in June give a vendor three months to resolve them. Identified in August, they become a problem that can delay the campaign or require price adjustments. The difference between early and late action is often measured in weeks of delay or thousands of dollars in concessions.

For inner east suburbs like Coorparoo, Hawthorne, and Camp Hill, spring is genuinely competitive. Multiple properties in the same price range will be launching at the same time. The campaigns that stand out are the ones where every detail is right from day one, not the ones that needed another week to get ready.

The vendors who start this conversation in June arrive at September in a fundamentally different position to those who treat June as too early.
