## Caption

There is a persistent belief among vendors that January open home traffic is made up of casual lookers, people with nothing else on after the beach or a family lunch. In the inner east, that is not accurate.

The buyers who turn up to open homes in the second and third week of January are typically people who spent the back half of 2031 watching the market, missed one or two properties they wanted, had the Christmas break to think about it, and are now ready to move. The decision timeline for this cohort is short. They are not browsing. They are comparing the shortlist they have been building for months.

In an Olympic year, that motivation is amplified for some buyers. The early-2032 buyer in the inner east has often made a deliberate choice to be in front of what they expect to be a more competitive market later in the year. They are informed, they have finance either approved or close to it, and they have a clear brief on what they want.

For vendors, this matters. A property listed in January with a realistic price and solid presentation is not entering a quiet market. It is entering a market with a concentrated, motivated buyer pool that has limited comparable stock to choose from. Competition for good properties in January is often stronger than people expect, precisely because supply is low and the buyers who are active are serious.

The open home count tells you less than the quality of the people in the room.
