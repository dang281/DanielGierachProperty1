## Caption

Mt Gravatt East is a suburb that family buyers tend to find after they have exhausted their options in Coorparoo and Camp Hill, and then find themselves wondering why they did not look here earlier.

Creek Road is the practical access spine of the suburb and connects it north toward the inner east and south toward the arterial network. Mt Gravatt East State School serves the primary catchment and is a consistent driver of owner-occupier demand among families making a purchase with the next six to ten years of schooling already mapped out.

The elevated ridge sections of the suburb carry views that buyers from the tighter inner-east markets do not expect to find at this price point. The blocks are generous. The street character in the residential pockets away from the main road is quieter than neighbours at comparable distances from the CBD.

The buyer who lands on Mt Gravatt East typically arrives through a budget conversation. They have been looking at Coorparoo or Camp Hill, they have a clear brief around land, lifestyle, and school catchment, and at some point the comparison with Mt Gravatt East becomes compelling on the numbers. When it does, they often move quickly.

Spring is when this buyer acts. The family buyer who watched through winter and the Olympics has been making decisions through September and October for years. The listing pool in Mt Gravatt East expands in spring, which gives buyers options and sellers competition.
