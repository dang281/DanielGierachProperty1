## Caption

On a $1 million owner-occupier purchase in Queensland, transfer duty adds approximately $38,025 to your purchase price. That figure does not appear in your mortgage. It does not add to your equity. It is a cost that must be funded at settlement, on top of your deposit.

Most buyers underestimate this by $10,000 or more. The gap between what people budget for stamp duty and what they actually pay is one of the more consistent surprises in the settlement process.

A few things affect the final number. Owner-occupiers pay a lower rate than investors purchasing the same property. First home buyers may qualify for a concession or exemption depending on the purchase price, which can significantly reduce or eliminate the duty payable. The rate schedule is tiered, so the duty does not scale linearly as the purchase price rises.

What this means practically: if you are budgeting for a purchase in the inner east, transfer duty should be a line item calculated from the actual rate schedule, not a round-number estimate. It is also worth confirming your eligibility for any applicable concession before you submit an offer, not after.

The full breakdown of Queensland transfer duty rates, first home buyer concessions, and how to calculate your specific liability: danielgierach.com/insights/stamp-duty-queensland
