## Caption

Morningside's position between Bulimba and Cannon Hill is not a limitation. For the right buyer, it is exactly the point.

The Wynnum Road corridor has changed over the past several years. The northern streets of Morningside, those sitting above Wynnum Road toward the river side, have benefited from that change and from the suburb's proximity to what Bulimba offers without carrying Bulimba pricing. Buyers who have looked at Bulimba and understood the price, then looked at Cannon Hill and wanted something slightly closer to the river and the Bulimba retail strip, arrive in Morningside's northern streets consistently.

Morningside State School catchment demand is a steady contributor to owner-occupier purchases. The families who are buying here with the school in mind are making a decision that keeps them in the suburb for years, which supports the holding patterns that characterise the street character in the northern sections.

The suburb's strength in spring is the buyer who has been in the Bulimba corridor for six months and has not found what they want at the prices they have been seeing. Morningside is frequently the suburb that resolves that search. The product is similar: Queensland character homes, good blocks, proximity to the river corridor. The pricing gap is real and consistent, which is what drives the buyer's final decision.

Q4 from Morningside tends to build on spring momentum. The listings that come to market in September and October are well-positioned to capture that buyer before the year end.
