## Caption

Under the Property Law Act 2023 (Qld), a vendor must provide a completed Seller Disclosure Statement to a buyer before any contract is entered into. If this is not done correctly, the buyer has the right to terminate the contract at any point before settlement.

Most vendors do not know this requirement exists until their solicitor raises it in the weeks before the campaign launches. By that stage, the disclosure documents need to be compiled, which takes time, and if the campaign is already running, there is a compliance exposure for every buyer who signs a contract before the documents are properly provided.

The Seller Disclosure Statement requires the vendor to provide current council and water rates notices, a search of the relevant local government planning certificates, and other prescribed information depending on the property type. Strata title properties have additional disclosure requirements under the Body Corporate and Community Management Act.

The practical implication is that the disclosure documents are not a box-ticking exercise. They are a legal prerequisite. A buyer who receives a compliant disclosure package and proceeds to contract has less room to terminate for administrative reasons. A buyer who can point to defective disclosure has a legitimate exit right that can be exercised at any time before settlement, regardless of how far through the process you are.
