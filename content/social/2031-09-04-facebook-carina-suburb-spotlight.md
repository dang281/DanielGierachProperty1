## Caption

Spring in Carina tends to be active for a specific reason: the suburb collects buyers who could not secure a property in Cannon Hill or Carindale and have decided that Carina offers the best available alternative at a price they can reach. That spillover demand is real and consistent, and it means the spring market here is driven by people who are motivated and ready to move.

For owners in Carina, the spring buyer pool includes families upgrading from smaller homes, investors looking for yield at a reasonable entry point, and first-home buyers stretching into a detached house rather than an apartment. That diversity of buyer type means a well-presented Carina property rarely sits unattended in September and October.

The housing stock in Carina rewards vendors who have made the right improvements. Kitchens, bathrooms, and landscaping in particular translate directly into buyer interest and price. Buyers at this price point are often making a stretch purchase, and a home that is ready to move into removes the uncertainty that renovation-in-progress properties carry.

September is the month where the Carina market moves most freely. Gardens that looked dormant in winter are beginning to show colour. Streets feel more welcoming. Buyer numbers are up, and inspection attendance at open homes reflects that.
