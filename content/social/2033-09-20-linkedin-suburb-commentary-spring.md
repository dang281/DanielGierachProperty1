## Caption

The mid-tier inner east suburbs are worth looking at separately from the riverside corridor in spring. Camp Hill, Holland Park, and Carina operate on different buyer motivations from Bulimba or Hawthorne, and the spring market reveals that difference.

Camp Hill has consistently attracted buyers whose primary filter is school catchment. The Whites Hill State College catchment and proximity to both Holland Park State School and Citipointe Christian College create a buyer pool that is specifically motivated and often less price-sensitive at the catchment boundary than buyers in other parts of the suburb. In spring, that catchment demand tends to show up in open home attendance and in the speed with which well-positioned properties move.

Holland Park is a suburb where price point and land size do the most work. Buyers who cannot afford Camp Hill or Coorparoo and who want a house on a reasonable block with access to the same corridor of services find Holland Park delivers those fundamentals at a lower entry. That price-point positioning becomes more visible in spring when the comparison set expands.

Carina sits at the eastern edge of the inner east's price band. The suburb's appeal is value relative to its neighbours, and the buyer profile reflects it: practical, research-led, focused on comparable sales evidence rather than lifestyle appeal. In spring, Carina campaigns that are accurately priced against recent comparable sales tend to move quickly.
