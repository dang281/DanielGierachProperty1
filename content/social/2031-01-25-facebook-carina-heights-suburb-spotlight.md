## Caption

Carina Heights is the suburb that buyers find when they have done the inner east search, priced out their first preferences, and started looking at what sits above Carina to the south. What they find is a quieter, more elevated version of the inner east offer at a lower entry price. The creek corridors running through the suburb provide green amenity and natural separation between streets. The residential character is established and low-density in the way that inner east buyers want.

The proximity to Westfield Carindale handles the practical needs. The distance to the Coorparoo and Camp Hill café strips is manageable. Families who buy in Carina Heights are typically making a deliberate trade: they are accepting a slightly longer drive to the inner east strip in exchange for a larger block, a better price, and a suburb that is settled rather than in transition.

The buyer competition in Carina Heights is not as visible as in Camp Hill or Norman Park, but it is consistent. Families who want the inner east address at an accessible price, upsizers from Carina who want the elevation, and buyers who are simply looking for a three or four bedroom home with a yard that does not require a competitive auction. Demand in this bracket has been reliable.

For owners thinking about where the 2031 market takes this suburb, the fundamentals here are sound.
