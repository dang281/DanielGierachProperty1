## Caption

The inner east has a buyer type that does not show up in aggregate Brisbane data: the family that has already sold and needs to settle into a specific catchment before the school year starts.

Queensland schools begin late January. For a family buying in Bulimba, Camp Hill, or Norman Park with a Year 3 and a Year 7 to enrol, settling in February is too late. They need to be in the property, organised, and registered at the school before the term one bell. That means contracts signed in November at the absolute latest.

This is not speculative pressure. These buyers have sold, cleared their mortgage, and are carrying the proceeds. They are not waiting on finance approval. They are waiting on the right property.

I see this in open home registers every October and November in the inner east. The profile is consistent: dual income, pre-teen or teenage children, looking in a specific school catchment, and operating on a fixed timeline. When a suitable property comes to market in October or November, this buyer type moves quickly and often competes.

It is one of the reasons a November campaign in Bulimba or Camp Hill can outperform a March campaign even though spring in Queensland does not have the same temperature logic it does in Sydney or Melbourne.

If you are weighing up November versus February, the school start deadline is a real factor in the inner east buyer pool that the February market simply does not have.
