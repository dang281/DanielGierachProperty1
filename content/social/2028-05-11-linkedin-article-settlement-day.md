## Caption

For most vendors, settlement day passes with almost no direct involvement. Since PEXA digitised the settlement process, the vast majority of Queensland property transactions now settle electronically. Most vendors find out it is done via a text message from their solicitor. There is no exchange of documents across a table. No handshake. Just a notification that the money has moved and the title has transferred.

That anticlimactic reality is actually good news. It means the settlement system works reliably when everything has been prepared correctly. The preparation that actually matters happens in the weeks before settlement day, not on the day itself.

What vendors need to handle ahead of time includes arranging the final inspection for the buyer, ensuring keys and remotes are ready, having utilities and council rates managed so there are no last-minute adjustments, and making sure the property is in the condition the contract requires. Solicitors handle the financial and legal coordination on the day, but the vendor's practical obligations are completed well before that.

The most common things that delay or derail settlement are not dramatic. They tend to be small: a bank that is slow to release a discharge, a vendor who has not organised keys, a discrepancy in final figures. Knowing what the solicitor is doing in the background while you wait for that text message makes the whole process less stressful.
