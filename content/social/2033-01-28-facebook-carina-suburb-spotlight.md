## Caption

Carina sits in a comfortable middle ground in Brisbane's inner east: accessible to the city, close to the Cannon Hill Coles corridor for everyday shopping, and priced at a point that brings both upsizing families and renovation-minded buyers to the same open homes.

The post-war housing stock is the defining feature of the suburb's streetscape. Weatherboard and fibro homes from the 1950s and 60s dominate, and many of them sit on blocks that reward renovation. The buyer who comes to Carina looking for original-condition property with land content and the scope to improve is a consistent presence in the market, and that buyer profile has supported price growth in the suburb as renovation results have become established comparables. ⚠️ VERIFY

Family buyers are drawn to Carina's school options and the relatively generous block sizes that have become harder to find at comparable price points closer to the CBD. Backyards that function, garages with room to spare, and a street culture that is residential in character without being remote from the services that matter.

The corridor between Carina and Cannon Hill has been showing steady activity, and buyers in the market for a first family home or a renovation project consistently include Carina on their shortlist. What the suburb offers to a patient vendor who has prepared well is a motivated, price-sensitive buyer pool that responds strongly to a well-presented, correctly priced campaign.
