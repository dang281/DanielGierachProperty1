## Caption

Cavendish Road State School is one of the catchment drivers that shapes buyer behaviour across several suburbs in Brisbane's inner south. In Mt Gravatt East, it sits at the centre of the family buyer conversation. Families who have identified the school as their target arrive at this suburb having already drawn the catchment boundary on a map, and they are not flexible about being outside it.

The suburb has two layers of demand. The school-driven family buyer who wants a three or four bedroom home within the Cavendish Road catchment. And the investor and first-home buyer who is watching Griffith University's Nathan campus influence on the rental market and the longer-term development trajectory near Garden City. Westfield Garden City sits adjacent, providing practical convenience that buyers in this suburb do not need to travel far to access.

The elevated ridge sections of Mt Gravatt East carry views toward the bay corridor that buyers from the inner east recognise when they see them. Properties on these sections sit in a different conversation to the flat blocks closer to Logan Road. The distinction matters at valuation time.

Demand in Mt Gravatt East has been driven by two consistent forces pulling in the same direction. School-driven owner-occupiers who stay for a decade or more, and investors attracted by the university and hospital proximity. The rental vacancy rate reflects the second group's judgement.
