## Caption

The strongest buyer profile in Brisbane's inner east right now is not the first home buyer. It is the upgrader: someone already in the suburb, or the next suburb over, who has built equity over the past several years and is ready to make a move.

These are buyers who know the area intimately. They know which streets in Camp Hill back onto Whites Hill, which pockets of Coorparoo sit inside the Whites Hill State College catchment, which parts of Norman Park have the river views and which do not. They are not researching the suburb from scratch. They are watching for the right property.

What this means for sellers: a genuinely well-positioned home in the inner east has a deeper buyer pool than the listing metrics suggest. The buyers are not always coming from interstate. They are often coming from the street behind you.

For sellers thinking about upgrading themselves, the logic works both ways. You are selling into a market that knows your suburb and will pay for quality. You are buying into a market where the upgrader you are competing against is equally motivated to transact.

The window for that equation to work well is autumn. Both sides of the transaction are motivated, qualified, and active. By June that changes. Winter narrows things.

If you are thinking about making a move this year, the conversation is worth having this month.
