## Caption

Carindale is entering its strongest selling window of the year, and the suburb is carrying more momentum into spring 2032 than it has in several years.

The Westfield Carindale precinct is the suburb's defining infrastructure anchor. In the post-Olympic period, that precinct is operating at full rhythm, which matters for buyers who are assessing the suburb's long-term retail and lifestyle fundamentals before committing to a purchase.

Gateway Baptist College serves the buyer profile that is making decisions based on secondary schooling. Families who have mapped out the next five to ten years of schooling before they make an offer represent consistent demand at the family home price point.

The elevated sections of the suburb are where the strongest land value resides. The Belmont Hills precinct and its views south are the kind of aspect that buyers from the tighter inner-east markets often did not expect to find at Carindale's price point. Large-block post-war brick homes on those elevated streets sit on land that is not easily replicated in the suburbs closer to the CBD.

The buyer who arrives at Carindale from Camp Hill or Norman Park and sees what the land size can look like here often recalibrates their thinking about what the budget can deliver. That recalibration, when it happens in spring with a motivated seller on market, tends to move quickly.

If you own in Carindale and have been considering timing, this is the season.
