## Caption

Early February open homes in the inner east have a specific quality. The January browsers are gone. The people walking through properties in the first two weeks of February have usually done their research over summer and are ready to make a decision.

What I observe at opens right now: group sizes are meaningful, not spectacular. You are not getting the twenty-group Saturdays that a peak March weekend delivers, but the buyers who are attending have already done their research. Offer conversations are starting faster than they were in November. A well-presented property generating ten to fourteen groups is drawing written interest within the first campaign week.

Free-standing houses and large townhouses in the $1.2m to $2m range are moving first. This is consistent with where buyer demand has been concentrated. Apartments and smaller townhouses are taking a week or two longer to develop offer momentum, though that gap narrows as March approaches and buyer numbers build.

The difference between a strong February result and a flat one is preparation. A vendor who goes to market in February with styling complete, professional photography done, and a clear price strategy is competing in a field that is thinner than March will be. A vendor who rushes the preparation to get to market early does not get the benefit of that timing advantage.

The autumn window is open. The buyers are in it. The question is whether the campaign is ready for them.
