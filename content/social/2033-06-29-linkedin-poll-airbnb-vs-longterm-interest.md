## Caption

Short-term rental platforms have reshaped how investors think about yield, but the headline numbers don't always survive contact with the full cost picture. Platform fees, professional cleaning between stays, higher insurance premiums, and the maintenance load of a property turning over weekly rather than annually all reduce the net return considerably. In many inner east Brisbane properties, the net difference between a well-managed short-term rental and a long-term tenancy is smaller than investors expect, with substantially more effort and unpredictability attached to the short-term option. For investors with a property that could go either way, I'm curious how they're thinking about it. If you owned an investment property in Brisbane's inner east, which rental strategy would you use?
