## Caption

Most sellers sign the Form 6 agency agreement without reading it. That is understandable: you have chosen an agent you trust, you are ready to get moving, and the document looks like paperwork. It is paperwork. It is also a legally binding contract.

The Form 6 sets out what you are agreeing to: the commission rate, whether the agent has exclusive authority or general authority to sell your property, the duration of that authority, any rebates the agent receives from third-party services, and the marketing costs you are committing to.

The rebate clause is the one that surprises sellers most. If the agent has an arrangement with a listing portal, a photographer, or a staging company where they receive a fee or commission, that must be disclosed in the Form 6 under Queensland's Property Occupations Act 2014. Many sellers never read this and do not know until after the campaign.

There is also a five-business-day cooling-off period after signing the Form 6 under listing conditions. Most sellers are not told this, and most agents do not raise it.

None of this means you should not trust your agent. It means you should know what you are signing. A good agent will walk you through it and encourage you to ask questions.

The full guide to what the Form 6 covers and what to look for is at danielgierach.com/insights/form-6-agency-agreement-queensland.
