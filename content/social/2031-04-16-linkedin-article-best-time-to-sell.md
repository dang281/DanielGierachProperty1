## Caption

Most advice about the best time to sell in Brisbane is written for Melbourne and Sydney. The inner east has a different seasonal rhythm, and the spring premium is smaller than most vendors expect.

The southern markets experience a genuine winter slowdown. Buyer numbers drop, open home attendance falls, and properties launched in winter often sit longer. Brisbane's climate does not produce the same effect. Buyer activity in the inner east continues through June, July, and August at levels that are meaningfully higher than what the seasonal narrative suggests. Vendors who delay to avoid a Brisbane winter are often waiting for a premium that does not materialise in the way it does further south.

The most consistent window for inner east sales is autumn, broadly running from late February through to May. The combination of stable weather, strong buyer motivation following the summer break, and lower listing volumes than spring creates conditions that suit well-prepared vendors. Spring does see higher listing volumes, which means more competition from other properties, and the price premium over winter is rarely as large as it looks when vendors compare spring headlines to winter results without controlling for property quality and preparation.

What preparation and a well-run campaign deliver in terms of outcome regularly exceeds what any seasonal timing advantage can produce. A vendor who waits for October and launches underprepared will typically achieve a worse result than one who launches in May with strong photography, accurate pricing, and a properly targeted campaign.

The article covers Brisbane's actual seasonal data and what it means for vendors deciding whether to act now or wait.
