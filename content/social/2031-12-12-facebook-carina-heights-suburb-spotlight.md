## Caption

The bigger blocks in Carina Heights make a real difference to how families live. When you've got enough backyard for a swing set, a garden, and a dog, the weekend rhythm of a family changes. That quality-of-life factor is what separates Carina Heights from its closer-in neighbours and it's something buyers with children notice within the first five minutes of a walk-through.

Carina Heights has been quietly repricing over the past few years as surrounding suburbs like Camp Hill have become increasingly out of reach for the mid-range family buyer. That repricing is not finished. The suburb sits within reach of multiple school catchments without being anchored to a single one, which gives families flexibility rather than a single pressure point.

The streets here are quiet. Traffic volumes are low. The character of the suburb, low fuss, owner-occupier oriented, and garden focused, remains consistent even as prices have moved. That consistency is what keeps the buyer profile here stable.

December brings a specific buyer to Carina Heights: the family that has been looking since winter, didn't find what they wanted in Camp Hill or Carina proper, and is now motivated to close before the new year. Those buyers are often in the best position to move quickly.
