## Caption

Holding costs are the running expenses a seller continues to pay on a property while it sits on the market: mortgage interest, council rates, water, insurance, plus utilities, lawn and cleaning if the home is being kept presentable for inspections.

For a typical inner-east property worth around $1.5 million, the monthly run-rate can sit anywhere between $4,000 and $8,000 depending on the loan balance and how the home is presented during the campaign. A 30 day campaign carries one figure. A 90 day campaign carries roughly three times that.

If you have sold a Brisbane home in the past few years, what did the total holding cost end up being as a rough percentage of the final sale price? Vote your gut answer.
