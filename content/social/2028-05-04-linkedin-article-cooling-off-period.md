## Caption

Queensland's 5-business-day cooling off period cannot be waived by the vendor. It is a statutory right that belongs to the buyer, and it exists regardless of what any contract says.

The cost to exercise it is 0.25% of the purchase price. On an $800,000 home, that is $2,000 to walk away after signing. For a buyer who has second thoughts, discovers something they missed, or simply changes circumstances, that is a relatively low exit price compared to the alternative.

Here is what both buyers and sellers frequently do not know: auction contracts carry no cooling off period at all. If you buy under the hammer, or sign a contract at the conclusion of an auction, the contract is binding from that moment. There is no 5-day window. No 0.25% exit. You are in, unconditionally, from the point of signing.

This distinction matters particularly in Brisbane's inner east, where auction is a common sale method for prestige and sought-after properties. A buyer who walks into an auction not knowing this is operating without a safety net.

The cooling off period itself is counted in business days, not calendar days. That means a contract signed on a Thursday may not start until the following Monday. Understanding exactly when the period begins and ends, what happens to your deposit if you do exit within it, and what triggers the period in the first place is all covered in the article below.
