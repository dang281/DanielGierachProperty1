## Caption

October open homes in the inner east have a different quality to them than spring listings would suggest.

The buyers I am seeing in the first few weeks of October are not casual. They have been watching the market through winter, they know what comparable properties have sold for, and they are moving with intent. The tyre-kicker volume is lower than it will be in November. The serious buyer volume is high.

What this means for sellers: the first two to three weeks of October represent one of the cleaner selling windows of the calendar year. You are marketing to buyers who are motivated, prepared, and not yet overwhelmed by the volume of choice that arrives by late October and into November.

The homes I have taken through October campaigns that were well-prepared and priced within their comparable evidence have moved quickly. The ones that were stretched on price or required work have sat, because spring buyers at this end of the market do their research.

If you have been thinking about selling before Christmas, the preparation window to launch in October is narrowing. A campaign launched in the second half of October is still viable. One launched in mid-November is targeting a much shorter buyer pool before the market quietens for the summer.

If you want to talk through your options for a pre-Christmas campaign, I am happy to have that conversation.
