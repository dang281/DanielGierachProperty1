## Caption

Camp Hill's spring market is defined by one of the most consistent pricing effects in the inner east: the Whites Hill State College catchment premium. Families who have identified the catchment as a non-negotiable arrive at Camp Hill with finance aligned to what the catchment commands, and they are not price-sensitive in the way that buyers in non-catchment suburbs tend to be. When a property within the verified catchment boundary comes to market in September, that buyer pool activates quickly and competes hard.

The Samuel Street strip is one of the inner east's best neighbourhood retail and dining precincts. Weekend mornings at the café cluster around Samuel Street represent the kind of daily life experience that buyers are trying to assess when they attend open homes. The visible community activity in this precinct is a reliable proxy for what living in Camp Hill actually feels like, and it is part of why buyers who attend open homes in September tend to make their decisions quickly.

The housing stock across Camp Hill's residential streets includes some of the inner east's best examples of post-war period character homes on generous blocks. Properties in the streets north and south of Samuel Street, particularly those within the catchment boundary, represent a specific combination of character housing, school access, and local amenity that buyers in this market have been targeting for years.

For Camp Hill vendors, spring is the year's strongest window.
