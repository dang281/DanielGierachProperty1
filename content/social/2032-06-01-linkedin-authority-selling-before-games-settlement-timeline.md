## Caption

June 1. The Games open in 55 days. Here is the honest version of what the settlement calendar looks like from here.

A property that goes to contract this week with a standard 30-day settlement completes around 1 July. A 45-day settlement takes you to mid-July. Both of those clear before the opening ceremony on 26 July, but they are tight. Any extension request, any delay at the bank, any issue with the title or the pre-settlement inspection, and you are suddenly settling during or after the Games period.

That does not mean you should not list in June. It means you need to go in with your eyes open about what the timeline actually looks like and choose your settlement terms thoughtfully. Some buyers will want more time. Some will want less. Knowing what you are prepared to agree to before you receive an offer is a better position than deciding under pressure on the day.

Properties going to auction in the first two weeks of June with shorter campaigns and a buyer pre-prepared to move fast can still work within the window. But it requires a coordinated approach: your solicitor or conveyancer needs to be briefed, your discharge of mortgage needs to be initiated early, and your agent needs to be managing buyer expectations clearly.

If you are a seller thinking about June, I am happy to map out exactly what the timeline looks like for your circumstances.
