## Caption

In a market where stock is limited relative to the buyer pool, competition does not always look like bidding at auction.

In Brisbane's inner east right now, the buyers who are winning are the ones who are financially ready to move. Pre-approved finance. A clear picture of their ceiling. A solicitor they can call to turn around a contract review in 24 hours.

What I see consistently is that the buyer who misses a property in this market rarely loses because they were outbid by a large margin. They lose because they needed two more days and the vendor accepted another offer before they were ready.

The properties that generate the most competition are the ones that are well-prepared, cleanly presented, and priced where the active buyers are already searching. The seller who puts in that work before the campaign creates the conditions where buyer competition drives the outcome.

The buyer who puts in that work before they start looking creates the ability to act when the right property appears, rather than watching it go under contract to someone who was ready.
