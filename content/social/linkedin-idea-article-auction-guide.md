## Caption

Auctions are the most transparent method of property sale in Queensland, and also the one that most buyers find intimidating. Most of that anxiety comes from not knowing what to expect.

Before auction day, registered bidders have completed their due diligence. There is no cooling-off period on an auction contract in Queensland, so a buyer who wins at auction is committing unconditionally. That means building and pest inspections, finance pre-approval, and title searches all happen before the auction, not after. Buyers who arrive unprepared cannot bid confidently.

On the day itself, the auctioneer opens proceedings, bids are placed, and the auction runs until the reserve is reached and met in the market. Once a bid is made above the reserve, the property is declared on the market and the highest bidder will purchase it. Below the reserve, the vendor can choose to negotiate with the highest bidder or pass the property in.

One detail that surprises many buyers: in Queensland, unlike Victoria, there is no statutory right giving the highest bidder first right of negotiation after a pass-in. The vendor can negotiate with any party they choose. Knowing this matters for bidding strategy.

After the hammer falls, contracts are exchanged immediately. The buyer signs, the deposit is paid, and the unconditional exchange is complete. Settlement follows at the agreed date in the contract.
