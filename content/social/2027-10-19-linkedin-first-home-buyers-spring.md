## Caption

First home buyers are a real and growing share of the buyer pool in Brisbane's inner east at the $700,000 to $900,000 price point. Understanding how this market works for them is worth spelling out clearly.

The QLD First Home Owner Grant applies to new builds, not established homes. Most first home buyers competing in the inner east are doing so without grant support and against buyers who have equity, greater borrowing capacity, or both. The competition is real and the conditions are not equal.

In open bidding at auction, first home buyers typically lose to families with more buying power and investors with unconditional positions. The path to winning in this market is not outbidding. It is arriving unconditional and being ready to move fast. A pre-approved buyer who can exchange on the day an offer is accepted has a significant advantage over a buyer who still needs to satisfy finance conditions, even if the unconditional buyer's offer is lower in dollar terms.

The most common mistake first home buyers make in this market is waiting. Waiting for a property that ticks every box. Waiting to see if prices soften. Waiting to save a slightly larger deposit. The buyers who succeed are the ones who have done their research across multiple open homes, know what a fair price looks like, and commit when the right property appears rather than continuing to look for something better.

Pre-approval in place before the first open home is not a nice-to-have. It is the minimum required to compete seriously. danielgierach.com
