## Caption

Holland Park sits in a position that buyers who have been priced out of Camp Hill and Norman Park keep rediscovering. The suburb is elevated, it has good school access, it is close to the Mt Gravatt East corridor, and it delivers the suburban fundamentals that inner east buyers want at a price point that still makes sense for a first or second family home. That gap between quality and price is what sustains demand here through varying market conditions.

The character of the suburb is established residential. Families who have been in Holland Park for ten years are raising children who will go to the same schools they did. The Holland Park Junior Cricket Club and the parklands along the suburb's green corridors provide the kind of community infrastructure that appears low-key but shapes a neighbourhood's long-term character.

School access is a consistent driver. The proximity to Mt Gravatt East and the Cavendish Road State School catchment boundary matters to families who are buying with an eye on their children's schooling path. The suburb also sits close enough to Garden City for practical convenience and well within the range of Brisbane's inner south employment centres.

For buyers who have been watching Camp Hill and decided the numbers no longer work for them, Holland Park is the suburb that answers the question of where to look next. For owners, that buyer profile translates into consistent demand.
