## Caption

Carina Heights offers something that has become genuinely scarce in Brisbane's inner east: large blocks with elevation and views at prices below the suburbs immediately to its north.

Sitting between Carina to the east, Camp Hill to the west, and Carindale to the south, the suburb's position is practical on every side. The elevated sections along Mt Cotton Road and the streets running along the ridge carry views toward the city and the ranges that buyers who have only driven through on the flat have not seen. This is a suburb where the aspect is worth exploring on foot before forming a view.

Carina Heights State School serves the family buyer segment. Buyers with secondary school-age children have options nearby without travelling far.

The buyers arriving in Carina Heights have usually been priced out of Camp Hill or Norman Park. They want the elevation, the space, and the aspect that those suburbs offer. Carina Heights delivers those things at a price point that reflects its lower profile rather than any genuine deficit in what it provides.

For owners in Carina Heights who have been sitting on a larger block with good views and considering whether now is the right time, the current buyer profile is arriving with that brief. They are motivated, they have done the comparison, and they know what they are looking for.
