## Caption

Murarrie sits at an interesting point in the inner east story for 2032. It has airport access via Tilley Road and direct Gateway Motorway connectivity that makes it genuinely convenient for buyers with logistics-heavy lifestyles. The suburb has been a quiet achiever in value growth as buyers have moved along the inner east corridor looking for relative affordability with strong fundamentals.

The residential pockets around Wynnum Road and the streets running toward the riverine edges of the suburb are where owner-occupier buyers focus. Industrial and commercial uses in the northern section of the suburb do not significantly affect the residential market but they are worth understanding when positioning a property to buyers.

Infrastructure investment in the area around Murarrie and the Portside precinct next door has reinforced confidence in the suburb's long-term trajectory. Buyers who are priced out of Cannon Hill or Morningside increasingly identify Murarrie as the next step along the access corridor.

The housing stock is varied: there are substantial lowset homes on good blocks, older post-war properties suitable for renovation or redevelopment, and some newer infill townhouse product. The buyer pool reflects that mix, with owner-occupiers, investors, and developers all active depending on the site.

In 2032, Murarrie's position near major arterials and the inner east fringe gives it airport and Games access that some buyers specifically seek.
