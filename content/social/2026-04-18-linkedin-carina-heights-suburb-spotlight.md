## Caption

Carina Heights does not get the headlines that Camp Hill or Morningside do. That is deliberate.

It sits directly between Camp Hill and Carindale, with Whites Hill State College on its doorstep and bus routes that get you into the CBD in under 30 minutes. The streets are quiet, the blocks are generous, and the price point is still 10 to 15 percent below comparable properties in the suburbs immediately to the north.

That gap has been closing.

Entry price for a three-bedroom house sits around $900,000 to $950,000. Renovated properties in the streets closer to Whites Hill are regularly clearing $1.1 million. The land content here is the story: 600 to 700 square metre lots are still achievable at a price that has already moved past reach in Morningside or Norman Park.

The buyer pool tends to be families who have done the maths and decided the value is better to the south. The Whites Hill State College catchment draws consistent demand even when other parts of the market soften.

If you own in Carina Heights and have been watching your neighbours sell, now is a reasonable time to understand what your property is worth.
