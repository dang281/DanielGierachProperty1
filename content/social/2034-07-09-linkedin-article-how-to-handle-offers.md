## Caption

The first offer on a property sets the tone for everything that follows. How you respond to it often determines whether the deal gets done.

Most sellers make one of two mistakes. They reject a low offer outright, which closes the negotiation entirely. Or they accept too quickly, not knowing whether the buyer had more to give.

What experienced sellers understand: an offer is the start of a conversation. Rejecting without countering is the equivalent of hanging up the phone. The buyer who opened at a number you found insulting may have a ceiling well above what they showed you. You will never find out if you walk away.

In Brisbane's inner east, 8 to 10 per cent below the asking price is a standard opening position. That is not a signal the buyer is not serious. It is a signal they are testing.

The other variable that changes everything is conditions. An unconditional offer at a lower number often represents a better outcome than a higher conditional offer carrying finance and building and pest risk for two to three weeks.

There is more on how to read and respond to offers at danielgierach.com/insights/how-to-handle-offers-when-selling.
