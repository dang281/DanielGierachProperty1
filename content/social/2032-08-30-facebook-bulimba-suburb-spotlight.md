## Caption

Bulimba's housing market divides into two distinct products, and understanding which one you hold matters for how you approach a sale.

The northern end of the suburb, the Oxford Street precinct and its surrounding residential streets, carries the suburb's lifestyle premium. Character homes within walking distance of cafes, the ferry, and Bulimba State School. These properties attract buyers who have done a thorough search of the inner east and arrived at a clear conclusion. That buyer is motivated and has usually been watching the suburb for some time before a property reaches them.

The southern end, toward Wynnum Road and the Heritage Lane corridor, offers a different product: townhouses and post-war homes at more accessible entry prices. The lifestyle access is still there. Oxford Street is not far. The school catchment is the same. But the buyer profile is different: younger purchasers entering the suburb, investors drawn to the rental demand that the precinct generates, and owner-occupiers who want the Bulimba address at a price that the northern streets have moved beyond.

What stays consistent across both segments is the pace at which buyers commit once they have decided on Bulimba. The ferry, the school, and the Oxford Street precinct is a package that has been drawing buyers to this suburb for over a decade, and the depth of that buyer interest does not diminish as supply tightens.
