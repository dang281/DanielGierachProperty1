## Caption

Buyer competition in Brisbane's inner east is not uniform. It varies sharply by price point, property type, and suburb, and treating it as one market leads to poor decisions on both sides of the table.

At the moment, the most competitive segment in suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Morningside is well-presented houses in the $1.8 million to $2.6 million range with four bedrooms and functional outdoor space. These properties are drawing multiple registered bidders at auction when they are priced correctly and presented well. The demand is not speculative. Most of these buyers are local, upgrading within the inner east, and have genuine equity behind them.

The segment where competition drops off is the high end above $3.5 million. There are buyers at this level, but the pool is smaller and the timeline to a decision is longer. Campaigns need to be planned accordingly. Auctions are not always the right method here.

Units are a different conversation. The apartment market across the inner east remains slower. Many buyers in this category are still contending with serviceability constraints despite rate movements, and investor appetite has not fully recovered. There are exceptions, particularly for well-located, larger two and three-bedroom apartments, but they need to be priced with precision.

For anyone thinking about listing this autumn, knowing which bucket your property sits in changes the strategy significantly. The method of sale, the campaign length, and the price guidance all follow from that.
