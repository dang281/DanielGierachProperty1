## Caption

Easter creates a natural break in Brisbane's property market. Buyers step back from decision-making for the long weekend and the week that follows. For sellers who are on the market but have not yet sold, Easter can feel like the market has switched off.

The three weeks before Easter are when the autumn window produces its best results. Buyer motivation peaks. Competition among buyers is highest. Auction rooms are full. And the sense of urgency that comes from a clearly defined seasonal endpoint pushes buyers to act.

Properties that go to market now, in mid-March, have time to run a proper three-week campaign and reach the auction room or close a private treaty deal before Easter. That is the window.

Properties that are still in preparation and have not launched will miss this window. They will either launch into the post-Easter market, which can be effective but is generally quieter, or they will hold until spring. Both are workable options. Neither is as strong as the pre-Easter autumn peak.

For inner east sellers who have been on the fence: if you want to capture the autumn window, the decision needs to happen this week. The preparation lead time is gone. What remains is the launch window, and it is short.

For sellers already on the market: the next three weekends are the ones that matter. Properties that are priced correctly and have strong presentation are best positioned for this period.
