## Caption

Holland Park sits at the point where the inner east transitions into the middle ring, and it carries the best of both. Good street character, established gardens, and a school catchment mix that includes Holland Park State School and Cavendish Road State High makes this suburb a consistent performer in the inner east market.

Buyers here are typically families prioritising land size and school access, often upgrading from units in Coorparoo or East Brisbane. The block sizes in Holland Park, regularly 600 to 800 square metres on the established streets east of Logan Road, offer what a lot of the denser suburbs closer to the river cannot.

Days on market in Holland Park have tracked in the low-to-mid twenties through 2030 for well-positioned houses, reflecting consistent buyer demand without the frenzied competition seen in some of the more prestige suburbs closer to the water. That steady demand suits vendors who price correctly from the outset.

Transport links via Logan Road and direct bus access to the CBD keep Holland Park competitive with other inner east suburbs at similar price points. The Evans Road and Logan Road café and retail strip gives the suburb a lived-in quality that buyers notice.

For homeowners in Holland Park considering a sale, the spring market through October and November is the period with the deepest buyer pool.
