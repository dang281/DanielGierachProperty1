## Caption

Seven Hills is one of the inner east's best kept positions for buyers who have worked out that elevation matters. The suburb rises sharply from the Old Cleveland Road corridor and the top streets, particularly along the ridge toward Whites Hill, deliver genuine north-facing views across the inner east at a land size and price point that buyers in Hawthorne and Bulimba gave up years ago.

The housing stock is a generation behind the suburbs closest to the river in terms of renovation penetration, which is part of what makes it interesting. There is still a meaningful proportion of original post-war homes on well-proportioned blocks where the land value and the topography are carrying the property rather than a finished product. For buyers who want to control their own outcome rather than pay someone else's renovation margin, Seven Hills is a rational choice.

The Whites Hill State College catchment runs through the suburb and provides the school-zone anchor that keeps family buyers engaged. The proximity to the Bulimba Creek linear park and the relatively low traffic density on the internal streets add a liveability quality that the suburb's profile does not always reflect.

Autumn 2032 is a sensible time to be selling in Seven Hills. Buyers who have been assessing the suburb against its neighbours are increasingly concluding the value case is real. Well-presented homes here are attracting a buyer pool with a longer research horizon and a clear sense of what they are committing to.
