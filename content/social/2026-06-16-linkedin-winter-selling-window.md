## Caption

Brisbane's winter does something interesting to the property market that most sellers don't expect.

Listings drop. Buyer motivation doesn't.

The people searching for homes in June and July are not casual browsers. They have been in the market for months, they have finance arranged, and they are ready to move. The casual lookers who showed up at every open home over summer have largely dropped off. What's left is a more concentrated pool of genuine buyers.

At the same time, fewer competing listings means your property isn't lost in a crowded spring grid. In suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Balmoral, a well-presented home with a solid marketing campaign in July often attracts sharper offers than the same home launched in October alongside 12 similar properties.

The common fear is that buyers stay home in winter. That hasn't been the experience in Brisbane's inner east. The city doesn't get cold enough to deter a motivated buyer from attending an open home.

If you've been waiting for spring to list, it's worth asking what you're actually waiting for. More competition. More comparable sales for buyers to lean on. A bigger pool of properties for buyers to consider before yours.

Winter isn't the wrong time to sell in Brisbane. For the right property, it's the right time.
