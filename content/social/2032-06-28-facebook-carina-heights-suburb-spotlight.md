## Caption

Carina Heights occupies the ridge south of Carina, and the elevation that gives the suburb its name is a consistent part of how buyers think about it. Streets on the higher contours carry a sense of space and quiet that the flatter suburbs nearby cannot replicate, and properties that make use of that elevation, whether through a deck, an outlook, or simply the airflow that comes with being above the creek corridor, attract a specific and motivated buyer group.

The suburb is bordered by Whites Hill Reserve to the west, and that reserve creates a genuine bushland edge for residents on the western streets. The walking tracks through Whites Hill connect through to Camp Hill and Norman Park, and active lifestyle buyers, particularly those who run or cycle, factor that access into their search in a practical rather than incidental way.

Carindale sits directly to the south, and the Westfield shopping centre is within a short drive, which handles the daily convenience question without the suburb needing its own high street. Bus routes along Old Cleveland Road and Carindale Street link northward to the CBD for those without a car or who prefer to leave one at home.

Housing in Carina Heights tends toward post-war brick and timber, and the blocks are generous by inner-east standards. Buyers who have been priced out of Camp Hill or Coorparoo often find that Carina Heights delivers similar lifestyle qualities at a different entry point.
