## Caption

The most common question I get about property styling is: does it actually work? The honest answer is yes, but not necessarily in the way most sellers imagine.

Staging works primarily as a photography investment. The goal of professional styling is not to impress buyers at the open home. It is to produce photographs that stop the buyer from scrolling past the listing online. The listing photo is the first inspection. Everything else is a follow-up.

Better photographs drive higher enquiry volumes. Higher enquiry volumes bring more buyers to the open home. More buyers at the open home creates competition. Competition is what moves a sale result above market value. The styling itself is three to four steps back in that chain.

This framing matters because it changes who benefits most. Vacant properties photograph significantly smaller and colder than furnished ones. A vacant inner east home in a competitive spring market is at a measurable disadvantage in online search. Styling a vacant property is almost always the right call.

An owner-occupied property with existing furniture is a different calculation. Selective decluttering and simple presentation improvements often deliver most of the benefit at a fraction of the cost.

For a well-presented three-bedroom inner east home, professional styling typically costs $3,000 to $6,000 for a four-week campaign. The question is whether that investment is likely to drive a result that justifies the cost.
