## Caption

Morningside is one of the inner east's more consistently active property markets. Turnover here is real, and the suburb draws buyers who have done the comparison work and found that Morningside delivers genuine value relative to the riverside suburbs nearby.

Colmslie Beach Reserve on the northern edge gives residents river access and parkland that most of the surrounding suburbs do not have at that proximity. For families and owner-occupiers who want outdoor access as part of daily life, the reserve is a drawcard that holds up when you live with it.

Wynnum Road creates two distinct precincts within the suburb. Properties north of Wynnum Road sit closer to the river influence and generally carry a different price point to those south of it. Buyers who know Morningside understand this distinction. Vendors who are not aware of it sometimes price without accounting for which side of the road they are on.

Morningside State School and the proximity of Cannon Hill Anglican College give families a school picture covering both public and private options without requiring travel far from home.

The buyer profile here is typically families upgrading from Camp Hill, Coorparoo, or Carina who are looking for more suburb at a price point that represents value relative to the riverside addresses. They have compared the options, understood the trade-offs, and concluded that Morningside makes sense.

If you own in Morningside and are thinking about the current market, I am happy to give you a straight answer on where your property sits.
