## Caption

The conventional wisdom in Australian real estate is that spring is the premier selling season, and for many markets that is accurate. Brisbane's inner east runs on a slightly different calendar. The autumn window from late February through May has historically been a strong campaign period in suburbs like Hawthorne, Bulimba, Norman Park, and Camp Hill, where the lifestyle appeal of the inner east draws serious buyers regardless of the headline season. Spring still brings the biggest listing volumes and the largest buyer pool, but autumn in the inner east produces competitive campaigns with a different buyer profile. Interested to know which season inner east sellers prefer and why.
