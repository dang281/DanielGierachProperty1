## Caption

The first open homes of January are always instructive. Not because the numbers are large, they rarely are, but because the people walking through are almost always serious. January browsers in Brisbane's inner east are not killing a Saturday afternoon. They are making decisions.

What I noticed across Hawthorne, Norman Park and Camp Hill in the opening days of 2033: buyer groups are smaller than the autumn peaks but the conversations are longer. People are asking about rates, asking about land size, asking when the vendor needs to settle. Those are buying questions, not browsing questions.

The Olympic year is behind us. 2032 brought a lot of attention to Brisbane and some of that translated into genuine demand. What 2033 looks like is a market finding its level. Prices in the inner east ⚠️ VERIFY have not collapsed, but the expectation that every property sells above reserve inside four weeks has settled down. That is actually healthy for sellers who prepare properly, because qualified buyers are still active and properties that are priced and presented well are still moving.

If you are planning to sell this year, January activity is worth paying attention to. The buyers in the market right now are the same cohort who will be attending your open home in March. Understanding what they care about, and what makes them hesitate, matters more than waiting for the autumn headlines.
