## Caption

Every January, the inner east property market goes quiet for about a fortnight. Then it switches on.

The people searching in early January are not casual browsers. They are buyers who spent December watching listings, attending a final inspection or two before Christmas, and using the break to make decisions. They come back in January with clear criteria and real intent. That is a meaningfully different buyer from the one who turns up to an open home in October out of curiosity.

What the first three weeks of the year also tend to look like is thin listing volume. Most vendors are still planning their campaign, organising tradework, or simply waiting for what they perceive to be the "proper" selling season. That lag between buyer re-entry and vendor re-entry creates a window that regularly benefits the sellers who are ready to move early.

A well-prepared property launching in late January faces a fraction of the competition it would face in March, when the bulk of the autumn campaign volume hits the market. The buyers are there. The competition often is not.

For anyone considering a sale in the first half of 2034, the question worth asking now is not whether January is the right month to sell. It is whether you can be ready in time to take advantage of the buyers who are already looking.
