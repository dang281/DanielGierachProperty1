## Caption

The inner east has had a strong run over the past several years. Values have moved significantly. The Olympics narrative has added a layer of attention and buying interest that has kept the market active well into 2031. Any honest assessment of what comes next has to weigh risk alongside opportunity.

There are a few legitimate risk scenarios for 2032 that I hear discussed in serious conversations: affordability reaching a point where the buyer pool genuinely narrows, rate movements that reduce borrowing capacity further, the question of what happens to inner east values after the Olympic attention fades, and the wildcard of development approvals that have been granted around Games infrastructure adding supply to the market over time.

None of these are certainties. All of them are real possibilities worth thinking about if you are making a buy, sell, or hold decision in the next 12 months.

What do you think is the single biggest risk to Brisbane's inner east property market heading into 2032?

## Poll options

- Affordability plateau after an extended strong run
- Rate movements reducing borrowing power further
- Post-Olympics price softening once the event passes
- Oversupply from development approvals around Games infrastructure
