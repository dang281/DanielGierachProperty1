**Caption:**

For most vendors in Queensland, settlement day passes without them doing a single thing. No bank counter, no handshake, no signing. PEXA handles the electronic transfer of funds and title in a matter of minutes, and the first notification most sellers receive is a message from their solicitor confirming it is done.

That surprises people who have been carrying the stress of a sale for weeks or months. They expect a ceremony at the end. There is not one.

What that quiet settlement day should not obscure is where the real preparation actually needs to happen, and it is not on settlement day itself. It is in the two to three weeks before it.

The property condition at the time of the contract must be maintained until the buyer's pre-settlement inspection. Fixtures and fittings that were included in the sale must still be there. Items that need to be removed must be gone. Damage caused during the move-out is the seller's problem, not a small one.

The article I have linked covers what settlement day actually looks like in Queensland, what PEXA does and does not handle, and where sellers most commonly create problems for themselves in the final stretch.

Most settlement complications I see come from the weeks before settlement, not the day itself. Understanding the sequence helps.
