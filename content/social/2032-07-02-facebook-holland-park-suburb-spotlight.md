## Caption

Holland Park is where buyers land when Camp Hill and Coorparoo have moved beyond their reach, and more often than not, they find what they were looking for.

The streets around Musgrave Park and along Echlin Street and Elphin Grove carry the kind of older Queenslanders and post-war brick homes that buyers have been chasing across the inner east. Blocks here regularly run over 600 square metres, and the opportunity to extend, renovate, or add a dual-income addition is genuine rather than theoretical.

The suburb's position is one of its most underrated features. You are sitting between Stones Corner to the north and Mount Gravatt East to the south, which means access to two distinct retail precincts without paying the premium attached to the postcodes directly east. The Gateway Motorway is close. The CBD is within practical commuting distance.

Buyers arriving in Holland Park have usually done a thorough search of the inner east. They know the price differential. They know what they are getting for the savings. Most of them stay.

For owners in Holland Park who have been thinking about what their property is worth in the current market, now is a good time to find out.
