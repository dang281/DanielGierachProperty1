## Caption

The number of groups through an open home is only one signal. The quality of buyer engagement tells you more.

Attendance figures are the number vendors tend to ask about first, and they are the number that is most easily misread. A property can attract thirty groups to its first open home and produce no offers. Another property can attract eight groups and produce four serious buyers. The difference is not volume. It is the depth of engagement underneath the foot traffic.

The signs of real competition are specific. Buyers who return for a second visit within the campaign are serious. Buyers who request the contract within the first ten days are telling you they are close to committing. Buyers who ask about settlement flexibility are solving a problem, which means they have already decided they want the property and are working out the logistics.

The signs of shallow competition are also specific. High attendance at the first open home followed by a drop to two or three groups by the second week is a warning sign. No contract requests by day fourteen means the campaign is not converting enquiry into intent.

In a well-run inner east campaign, three groups requesting the contract in the first two weeks is a reliable indicator that the campaign is working. When that number is zero, the conversation with the vendor needs to cover price, presentation, or both.

Vendors sometimes read strong early attendance as confirmation that the price is right. In practice, if attendance does not convert to enquiry and contract requests, the attendance figure is obscuring a problem rather than reflecting genuine demand. danielgierach.com
