## Caption

Most vendors find out settlement is done via a text message from their solicitor. That is usually the extent of their involvement on the day.

Settlement in Queensland is completed digitally through PEXA. The funds transfer, the title changes hands, and the keys are released. The vendor is not present. In most cases, there is nothing to attend.

This surprises a lot of people. But the more important point is this: the preparation that actually matters happens in the weeks before settlement, not on the day itself.

The things that cause settlement complications are almost never last-minute surprises. They are issues that were present earlier in the process and were not dealt with. The most common ones are straightforward to avoid if you know to look for them.

Missing inclusions cause more friction than most vendors expect. If it was in the house when the buyer inspected and it is not listed as an exclusion in the contract, the buyer can expect it to be there on settlement. Dishwashers, light fittings, and fixed shelving are frequent sticking points.

Removalist damage is another one. The pre-settlement inspection is the buyer's opportunity to confirm the property is in the same condition as when they signed. Damage that occurs during the move-out is your responsibility.

And lease complications on tenanted properties are worth addressing well in advance. Notice periods, bond lodgement, and condition report timing all need to align with the settlement date.

The article covers the full sequence and what to have confirmed before you hand over keys.
