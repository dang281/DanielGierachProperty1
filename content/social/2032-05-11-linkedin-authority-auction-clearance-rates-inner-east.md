## Caption

Clearance rates are one of those numbers that get quoted a lot and explained rarely. Here is what they actually tell you.

A high clearance rate does not just mean properties are selling. It means that when a seller and buyer are in the same room, under auction conditions, they are finding a number that works. That is a measure of real buyer depth, not just interest. People who register to bid have finance arranged, they have done their due diligence, and they have made a decision. When multiple registered bidders push a property past reserve, that is the market speaking clearly.

In the inner east through April and into May, the auction market has been operating with genuine competition at well-priced listings. Properties with good bones, presented well and priced from a credible starting point have been attracting registered bidders consistently. That is not the case in every market across Brisbane right now.

What I watch more than the headline clearance rate is the number of registered bidders per campaign. One registered bidder tells you one thing. Three or four tells you something quite different about where buyer appetite sits for a given price range and property type.

If you are weighing up whether auction is the right method for your property this year, the depth question is the one worth starting with, not the clearance percentage in isolation.
