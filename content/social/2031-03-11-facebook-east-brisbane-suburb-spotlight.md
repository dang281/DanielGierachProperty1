## Caption

At 2.5 kilometres from the Brisbane CBD, East Brisbane occupies a position on the inner ring that very few suburbs can claim. The consequence of that proximity is a buyer competition that compresses the time between listing and offer.

The suburb runs from the Gabba precinct on its western edge toward Mowbray Park on the river, with the Mowbray Park Ferry Terminal giving residents a commute option that bypasses the road network entirely. The mix of housing stock reflects the suburb's history: pre-war character homes on the older streets, infill townhouses and contemporary builds filling the gaps that subdivision has created over the past two decades. Both types attract buyers, for different reasons and at different price points.

The Gabba's proximity matters more than it once did. The surrounding precinct has developed consistently over recent years, with the changes to its urban environment drawing residents who want inner-city convenience without paying inner-city apartment prices. East Brisbane character homes on the streets above Vulture Street and Main Street give buyers that access without the trade-off.

In March, the buyer competition in East Brisbane reaches its clearest expression. The number of pre-approved buyers looking at inner ring character properties in the 2-3 kilometre band exceeds the supply of those properties on the market at any given time. That ratio is what drives results for well-prepared vendors.
