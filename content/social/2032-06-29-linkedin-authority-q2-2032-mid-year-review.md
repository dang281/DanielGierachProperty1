## Caption

Q2 is done. The Games open in four weeks. Here is what the past three months actually showed about the Brisbane property market.

The pre-Olympic narrative dominated conversation but buyer behaviour was more measured than the headlines suggested. Motivated sellers who launched well-priced, well-presented campaigns in April and May transacted cleanly. Properties that came to market optimistically priced, hoping the Olympic story would carry them past the market's judgement, generally did not. The market in the inner east remained discerning.

Buyer depth was genuine through Q2 at the owner-occupier price points that dominate the Bulimba, Hawthorne and Balmoral corridor. Auction competition was real. Interstate buyer enquiry stayed active, with the majority of that group focused on liveability and school access rather than any specific Games-driven calculus.

What Q2 also showed is that the market does not take shortcuts. Good preparation, credible pricing, and honest campaign management produced good results. Everything else produced the usual range of outcomes.

Going into the second half of the year, the interesting question is what the market looks like in September when listings return and buyers who sat out the Games period come back. I will be watching that closely and sharing what I see. If you are planning a spring campaign or thinking about buying later in the year, that September picture is the one to pay attention to.
