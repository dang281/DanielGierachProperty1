## Caption

Most vendors treat the pre-settlement inspection as a formality. The buyer walks through, ticks the box, and settlement proceeds. In practice, it is one of the most avoidable sources of last-minute disputes in a Queensland property transaction.

Removalist damage happens. Items listed as inclusions get packed by mistake. Fixtures that were present at the contract signing disappear. When a buyer identifies these issues at their inspection, the vendor is often scrambling to resolve them hours before settlement, under pressure, and with limited options.

The fix is straightforward. Vendors who do their own walkthrough two to three days before settlement can catch almost all of these issues before the buyer ever sees them. A missing light fitting is a five-minute hardware store trip when you have time. It is a potential delay to settlement when you do not.

I have written a full guide to pre-settlement inspections from the seller's perspective, covering what to check, what your obligations are under Queensland law, and how to handle it if an issue does arise. The link is below.
