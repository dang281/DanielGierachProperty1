## Caption

Hawthorne occupies a position in Brisbane's property market that is defined as much by scarcity as by quality. The suburb is small, well-regarded and located four kilometres from the CBD in the inner east corridor that consistently outperforms broader market trends. Owners in Hawthorne tend to stay, which means the pool of available properties at any given time is limited. When a well-presented home does come to market, it faces a buyer pool that has often been waiting for some time.

The character home is the defining feature of Hawthorne's streetscape. Queenslanders and post-war homes on blocks that are generous by inner east standards make up the majority of the housing stock. These are not the compact lots of East Brisbane or the redeveloped sites of newer inner suburbs; they are homes with genuine land, mature gardens and the kind of scale that buyers at this life stage are often trying to find again after years in a smaller property.

Proximity to Bulimba's Oxford Street and to New Farm means residents have access to two of Brisbane's most active café and restaurant precincts without those precincts being in Hawthorne itself. That balance of access and residential calm is a consistent theme in how buyers describe why they want to be in Hawthorne rather than in the more commercially active suburbs nearby.
