## Caption

Q1 2030 is nearly complete in the inner east. Here is an honest assessment of what it looked like.

What sold well and why: Properties that launched in late January and through February with accurate pricing, good presentation, and proper preparation were the consistent performers. In suburbs like Camp Hill, Norman Park, and Hawthorne, well-presented four-bedroom family homes that were priced within buyer expectations saw strong inspection numbers and offers. The buyer pool in these suburbs ran deep through February and into March.

The pattern for what didn't perform: Properties that launched at prices above what comparable sales supported, or with presentation that undersold the property. In a buyer pool this informed, overpriced stock sits. Buyers know the street values. They will wait for the price to adjust rather than offer up.

The broader pattern across Q1: sellers who acted in Q1 got the buyers who were ready, motivated, and had been searching since the back end of 2029. Those buyers have largely transacted now. Q2 sellers face a buyer pool that is different in composition, still genuine, but less homogenously motivated.

Heading into Q2: the autumn window narrows through April and May. Properties listing in the next four weeks will still find buyers, but the competitive dynamic that defined the February and March peak is easing. Sellers entering Q2 should price accordingly and not set expectations against the results they saw their neighbours achieve in March.

For sellers still deciding, the honest message: Q1 was the stronger window. Q2 can still produce good results with the right strategy. Q3 (winter) requires a different approach entirely.
