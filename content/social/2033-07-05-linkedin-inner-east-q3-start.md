## Caption

The pattern going into Q3 in Brisbane's inner east is the one this market follows almost every year. Transaction volumes compress. Listing stock thins. Buyer numbers fall. And then, within that smaller pool, the buyers who are still active turn out to be the most qualified and motivated of the year.

That is not bad news for vendors who understand what it means. A smaller pool of genuine buyers competing for fewer properties is often a more controlled environment than the noise of an autumn peak. The autumn campaigns that underperformed in Q2 were largely a pricing and preparation problem. The winter market does not create those problems. It makes them more visible.

The suburbs that carry well through winter in the inner east are the ones with inherent demand drivers that don't depend on season. Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Balmoral bring buyers who are committed to the riverside corridor regardless of the time of year. Camp Hill and Norman Park draw buyers with school catchments in mind, and school decisions rarely pause for July. Coorparoo and Morningside attract buyers from wider Brisbane who want proximity to the CBD at a price point that still makes sense.

The vendors who will look back on Q3 2033 as a good quarter are the ones who understood the winter market on its own terms rather than waiting for spring to solve something the market was ready to resolve now.
