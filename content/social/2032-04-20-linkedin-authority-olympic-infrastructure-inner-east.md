## Caption

I want to give you my honest read on this, because there's been a lot of noise about it for years.

The inner east has benefitted from infrastructure investment that was tied to or accelerated by the Olympics: transport connections, public space upgrades, road improvements, and the general liveability work that comes with a city preparing to host the world. That investment is real and it has improved the area as a place to live. Whether it has directly moved property values is much harder to pin down.

Valuers don't write "Olympics infrastructure" into their reports. What they assess is land size, location, comparable sales, condition, and improvements. The liveability improvements that came with Games preparation feed into desirability over time, which feeds into demand, which feeds into prices. But that's a long chain and it's very difficult to separate from broader market conditions.

What I can tell you from being on the ground is that buyers consistently reference the improved transport access and the upgraded public amenity as things they value about the area. That translates to a wider buyer pool than you'd have seen a decade ago, and a wider buyer pool generally supports prices.

I'm not going to give you a number. Anyone who tells you the Olympics will add $X to your property value is speculating. What I'll say is that the inner east is a better-connected, more liveable area in 2032 than it was in 2022, and that does matter to buyers.
