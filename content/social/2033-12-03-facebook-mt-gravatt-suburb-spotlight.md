## Caption

Mt Gravatt has a commercial and employment base that gives it a daily-life quality that purely residential suburbs often lack. Logan Road runs through the heart of the suburb with a well-established spine of retail, restaurants, cafes, and professional services that caters to residents without requiring a trip elsewhere for most daily needs.

The healthcare and medical precinct along this corridor is a meaningful local employer and also a draw for residents who value proximity to quality health services. For a suburb at this price point, the depth of local amenity is above average, and that is something buyers notice and value.

Griffith University's Nathan campus is close enough to add an education and employment layer to the suburb's appeal. Whether residents are connected to the university or not, the presence of a major institution brings a level of surrounding investment and services that benefits the broader area.

What stands out to me about Mt Gravatt's market is the owner-occupier retention rate. Residents who buy in Mt Gravatt tend to hold. The churn is lower than you might expect for a suburb at an accessible price point. That consistent holding rate reflects something about the suburb's liveability that the numbers alone do not fully capture. When properties do come to market, they typically attract buyers who have already decided that this is where they want to be.
