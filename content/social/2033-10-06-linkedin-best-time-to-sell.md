## Caption

The best-time-to-sell advice that circulates every spring is built entirely around Melbourne and Sydney data. It does not apply to Brisbane's inner east.

This is one of the more consequential pieces of misinformation that moves through property conversations every year, and I wrote about it in detail because I see it affect vendor decisions in ways that are genuinely costly. The short version is this: Brisbane's seasonality is different to the southern capitals, and the assumptions people carry about when to sell are mostly borrowed from markets that behave differently.

Brisbane's autumn window, from late February through to May, consistently performs as well or better than spring for inner east sellers. That is not because spring is weak. It is because the Brisbane market does not follow the same seasonal compression that Melbourne and Sydney do. Fewer competing listings, buyers who have been looking since Christmas, and weather that presents properties well. The combination is strong.

The more important point is that a well-prepared property launched in any month will outperform an unprepared property launched in the supposed peak month. Preparation is the variable a vendor actually controls. The calendar is a distraction from that question.

When the decision to sell is driven by readiness rather than the season, the results are consistently better. That holds across autumn, spring, and the months in between.
