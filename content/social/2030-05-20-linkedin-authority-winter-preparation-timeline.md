## Caption

If you are thinking about a July or August campaign, the preparation should already be underway. Here is the timeline that works.

Weeks one to three before launch: address anything structural or cosmetic that affects presentation. Cracked plaster, perished caulking, garden that needs attention, any deferred maintenance that a buyer's inspector will flag. This is not about a full renovation. It is about removing the easy objections.

Week two to three: book your photographer, confirm your staging if you are using it, and have your Form 6 agent agreement ready to sign. If you need to arrange somewhere to stay during open homes, sort that now.

Week one before launch: photography day, floor plan capture, signage installed. Your listing should be live with strong photos and a compelling price guide before the first open home.

Opening weekend: your best chance to create competition. The first two open homes set the tone. A property with 25 groups through on day one signals to the market that demand is real.

The sellers who consistently achieve the best results in winter are the ones who treat the preparation period as carefully as the campaign itself. A rushed launch with mediocre photos and a cluttered interior is a much harder campaign than a well-prepared property with a clear brief and properly set expectations.

If you are targeting July, the time to talk is now.
