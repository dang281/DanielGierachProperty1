## Caption

The pre-Christmas buyer urgency in Brisbane's inner east is not a marketing phrase. It is a genuine shift in buyer psychology that happens every year in late October and early November, and it has a practical effect on how negotiations run.

Buyers who have been searching since August understand the calendar. They know that if they are not under contract by late November, they are unlikely to be settled by Christmas. That awareness creates a decision pressure that does not exist in the same way in February or May.

What this looks like in practice: buyers attending opens are more decisive. Offers come earlier in the campaign. The negotiation window is shorter because both sides understand the timeline. Buyers who have been "thinking about it" for three weeks on a property tend to accelerate when they realise the deadline for a Christmas settlement is genuinely close.

For vendors, this dynamic is worth understanding before deciding whether to launch now or wait until February. The buyers in the market through November are motivated in a way that the February buyer pool is not. They have a hard stop. That hard stop works in a vendor's favour if the property is ready.

The window for a pre-Christmas launch is closing. Properties entering the market in November will still attract strong buyer attention, but the settlement arithmetic becomes tighter. A property launching in the first week of November with a 35-day settlement ends up at December 7 at the earliest, assuming a quick contract.
