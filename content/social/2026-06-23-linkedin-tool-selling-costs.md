## Caption

Most Brisbane sellers underestimate the total cost of selling by about a third. The headline numbers are familiar, but the secondary costs are where the underestimate tends to live.

Agent commission is the biggest single item, generally in the range of 1.8 to 2.5 per cent plus GST for inner-east property in 2026. Marketing campaigns for a standard four-week campaign sit somewhere between $3,000 and $8,000 depending on the level of premium media included. Conveyancing typically runs $1,500 to $2,500. Then there are presentation costs, including styling, repairs, gardening, and the cleaning required for inspections.

For a property selling around $1.5 million, total selling costs commonly run between $40,000 and $55,000 by the time you reach settlement. That is real money worth modelling carefully before you list, particularly if the proceeds are going toward a deposit on the next home.

I built a calculator that itemises everything so you can see exactly where the cost is concentrated and where there is room to negotiate. Link in the comments.
