## Caption

Spring is at its seasonal peak and open home numbers across the inner east are running high. But competition does not move evenly across the market. A well-presented family home in a strong school catchment at the right price point can generate 8 to 12 groups at an open and multiple offers in week one. A different property in a neighbouring suburb at a different price point might attract 3 groups over the same weekend.

The inner east is not one market. Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, Camp Hill, Coorparoo, and Balmoral all have their own dynamics right now, and the $800K to $1.5M range behaves differently to the $1.5M-plus range even within the same street.

So from where you are sitting, as a buyer, seller, or just a market observer, how would you describe buyer competition in Brisbane's inner east this spring?

## Poll options

- Very competitive, multiple offers on most well-priced properties
- Moderate, some competition but not for everything
- Light, less competition than previous springs
- Depends entirely on the suburb and price point
