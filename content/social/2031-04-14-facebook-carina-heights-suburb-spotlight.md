## Caption

Carina Heights sits at a useful remove from its busier neighbours, and the buyers who settle here tend to know exactly why they chose it over Carina or Camp Hill. The streets are quieter. The blocks are larger. The suburb does not press itself on you with heavy retail traffic or arterial road noise, and for families who have found that the inner east feels increasingly dense, that space and calm is worth paying attention to.

The access to Westfield Carindale from Carina Heights is straightforward without requiring residents to live adjacent to it. The retail and services infrastructure of Carindale is available within a short drive, while the suburb itself retains a residential character that proximity to a major shopping centre can erode. That combination is more deliberate than it appears in the buyer conversations I have with people considering this precinct.

Cannon Hill Anglican College is close enough to be relevant for families who are weighing school options. For buyers who are not exclusively focused on state school catchments, the proximity to that school adds a private education option without requiring a long daily commute for students.

The elevated sections of Carina Heights, where the street names reflect the ridge position, offer aspects and outlooks that the flatter parts of Carina to the east do not. Properties on those elevated streets have a price premium that is consistent and explicable.
