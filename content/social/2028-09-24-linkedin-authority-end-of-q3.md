## Caption

September closes the third quarter, and it is a reasonable moment to take stock of what the inner east market has done since the start of the year.

The pattern I have watched through Q3 is consistent with what I saw in Q2: properties that are priced within their comparable evidence and presented well are selling, often within two to three weeks. Properties that are priced above the comparable range are sitting, regardless of how strong the campaign is. The market is not broken. The pricing gap is.

The spring start has been reasonably active. Buyer volumes are up from the August trough, which was itself not as quiet as some years. The clearance rate for the inner east has been holding at a level that signals a functional market, not a frothy one.

For sellers thinking about Q4, the pre-Christmas window runs from October to mid-November. That is a genuine window with motivated buyers, and it is shorter than people expect. The properties that launch in the first two weeks of October with good preparation are well-placed. The ones that launch in late November are competing for a market that is already starting to think about January.

For buyers, the spring competition is real but not extreme. The buyers who are prepared and decisive are getting properties. The ones waiting for prices to fall are still waiting.
