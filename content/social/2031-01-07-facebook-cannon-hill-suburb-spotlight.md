## Caption

The corridor from Cannon Hill through to Morningside has been one of the quieter consistent performers in Brisbane's inner east. Not the loudest market, not the suburb that generates headlines every quarter. But one that has delivered steady capital growth and held its buyer demand through varying conditions.

Cannon Hill's elevated sections carry city and bay views that buyers notice when they arrive at an inspection. The Cannon Hill Anglers Club sits alongside Bulimba Creek, providing a recreational anchor that adds character to the suburb beyond the standard residential offer. School access connects into the broader inner east catchment network, with Murarrie and Morningside both close enough to be relevant when families are drawing their search boundaries.

The buyers drawn to Cannon Hill tend to be practical. They want inner east proximity without inner east pricing at its peak, they want land with some elevation, and they want a suburb that is not still in the process of figuring out what it is. Cannon Hill has that settled quality. The Morningside boundary is a short drive. The Gateway access to the south is useful for buyers who also need to travel that corridor.

For owners in Cannon Hill, the first quarter of 2031 will be a considered one. The buyers are there.
