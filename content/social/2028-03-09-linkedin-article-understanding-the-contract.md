## Caption

The REIQ contract of sale is the document that governs every residential property transaction in Queensland. Most buyers and sellers sign it without reading it carefully.

Two specific details in that contract cause more disputes than anything else.

The first is the finance condition. If you have a finance condition in your contract, it lapses on the date and time specified, regardless of whether the bank has given you a formal approval or not. Miss the deadline and you are either unconditional without having chosen to be, or in default. The notification process has to be done correctly and on time.

The second is inclusions and chattels. What stays with the property must be listed in the contract before signing. If a light fitting, a curtain rod, or a built-in unit is not explicitly excluded, it is assumed to be included in the sale. Sellers who remove items they considered personal property after signing create disputes that can delay or unwind a settlement.

These are not edge cases. They are the two issues that come up most often in the weeks between contract and settlement.

I have written a full breakdown of how the REIQ contract works and what both parties need to understand before they sign.
