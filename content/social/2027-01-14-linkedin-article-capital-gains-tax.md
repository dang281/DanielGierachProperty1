## Caption

Most homeowners assume that selling their primary residence means no capital gains tax. In most cases, they are right. But the main residence exemption is not absolute, and the situations where it does not fully apply are more common in Brisbane's inner east than many people realise.

If you rented your home for a period before moving in, rented it out after moving out, or used any part of the property for income-producing purposes, your CGT position is more complicated than a straightforward full exemption.

The six-year absence rule is one of the most useful provisions in the tax code for homeowners who have moved out of their home but not sold it. If you have not nominated another property as your main residence, you may be able to treat the original home as your main residence for up to six years after moving out, even if it was rented. But the rule has conditions, and the interaction with other properties you own matters.

For investors in the inner east who bought years ago and are now considering selling, the decision of when to sell and how to structure the timing relative to your tax year can affect your net position significantly.

This is accountant territory, not agent territory. But knowing the questions to ask before you sign a contract is the starting point.
