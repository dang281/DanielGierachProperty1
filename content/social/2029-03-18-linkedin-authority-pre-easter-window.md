## Caption

The three weeks before Easter are among the most competitive weeks of the Brisbane inner east calendar for property sales.

Buyers who need to be settled before school term 2 ends in late June are doing their arithmetic. A May or June settlement requires a contract signed in March or April. An Easter break in the middle of the campaign is not an obstacle if the property is well-positioned and the campaign started early enough.

What I typically see in the two weekends before Easter is a slight compression of buyer decision speed. Buyers who have been attending open homes since February have a shortlist. Properties they are serious about get a second inspection. Offers come before the long weekend rather than after it.

For vendors with campaigns already underway, the pre-Easter period is when serious offers tend to materialise from buyers who have been watching. The window to convert genuine buyer interest into a contract before the market pauses for four days is worth paying attention to.

For vendors who have not listed yet: a campaign launched the week after Easter enters the market with approximately four good selling weekends before May conditions start to soften. That is enough time for a well-prepared property. It is not a lot of time for a property that still needs work.
