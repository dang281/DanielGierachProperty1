## Caption

The vendors who do best in spring are rarely the ones who decided to sell in September. They are the ones who had the conversation in June or July.

Campaign preparation for an inner east property typically takes four to eight weeks. Photography, styling, disclosure documentation, building reports, repairs to address presentation issues, and the pricing conversation all take time when done properly. Vendors who start the preparation process in late June and early July are in a position to launch in late August or early September, at the front of the spring market rather than competing with the peak-October listing surge.

The practical advantage is significant. A property that launches into the spring market before the listing supply peaks has the buyer pool largely to itself for the first two to three weeks of the season. Those are the weeks when buyer numbers are highest relative to available stock. That ratio drives competition. Competition drives results.

Vendors who launch in mid-October are entering a market where listings have accumulated through September and early October. The buyer-to-listing ratio is less favourable and the campaign has to work harder for the same result.

If you are considering a spring campaign in Brisbane's inner east and have not yet had the conversation about what preparation involves and how long it takes, June is the time to start. Not September.
