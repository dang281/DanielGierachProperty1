## Caption

Most Brisbane investors I speak with assume short-stay is the better earner. The numbers often tell a different story once you account for the full picture.

The biggest mistake I see is comparing gross Airbnb revenue against a long-term rent figure and stopping there. That comparison ignores cleaning costs, platform fees, vacancy between bookings, higher insurance, furnishing, and the time cost of managing constant turnover. A property generating $1,800 a week on short-stay can net less than one sitting at $700 a week on a standard lease.

There's also a regulatory layer that has changed considerably. Queensland has tightened short-term rental rules in a number of council areas, and body corporate bylaws have become an active factor in strata properties. What was permitted when you bought may not be permitted now.

The other consideration is what your investment goal actually is. If you are planning to sell in the next two to three years, a settled tenancy with reliable rent history reads better to a buyer's valuer than a short-stay income stream that is harder to verify and project. That matters more than most investors expect in finance and price negotiation.

Neither model is universally better. The right answer depends on your property type, location, hold period, and appetite for active management.
