## Caption

When vendors ask how long selling will take, they're almost always thinking about days on market. The active campaign period, the opens, the negotiations, the contracts. That phase matters, but it's only one stage in a sequence that starts much earlier and ends later than most people expect.

The full timeline from deciding to sell to settlement funds in your account typically runs ten to sixteen weeks. That's a wide range, and the preparation phase is why. Vendors who compress the preparation window to get on market faster almost always pay for it in the result. Styling decisions get rushed. Minor repairs that would have been invisible to buyers become visible. Photography is done before the garden is presentable. The property launches before it's ready, and first impressions in the listing photos are impossible to undo.

The preparation phase, done properly, takes four to eight weeks. That's when the campaign is actually won or lost. What happens in week one and two of the campaign, the enquiry volume, the open home groups, the early offers, all of that is a direct reflection of the decisions made before the listing went live.

The article linked below walks through each stage in detail: what's typically involved, where the time goes, and which parts are genuinely flexible versus which parts hurt you if you rush them. If you're thinking about selling in spring, the preparation window for a September or October launch starts now.
