## Caption

August and September in Brisbane's inner east is appraisal season. Not because agents decide it is, but because the natural rhythm of the seller decision cycle brings homeowners to that first conversation in the weeks before spring.

An appraisal is a market assessment, not a commitment. It involves an agent walking through the property, reviewing recent comparable sales in the immediate area, assessing the property's condition and position in the market, and producing a price range that reflects what a well-run campaign would be likely to achieve. The outcome is a number, a method recommendation, and a campaign framework. The vendor can choose to proceed, hold, or do nothing with that information.

The useful appraisals are the ones that are honest rather than optimistic. An agent who tells a vendor what they want to hear to win the listing is not serving that vendor's interests. The comparable sales data is what the market will use to assess the property. It is the most reliable reference point available.

In the inner east, where comparable sales in the same street or within two hundred metres are often available, the gap between an accurate appraisal and the eventual sale price is usually narrow when the property is well prepared and appropriately priced. The gap between an inflated appraisal and the eventual sale price is the period of market time and price reduction the vendor absorbs while the market corrects the expectation.
