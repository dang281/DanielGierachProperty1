## Caption

Late September in Carindale is a practical time to reflect on what the spring market has delivered and what the final quarter of the year holds. For owners who have been observing the market rather than participating in it, the spring season is producing the buyer activity and price results that were anticipated. Carindale's consistent family demand has once again proven resilient.

The suburb's spring presentation is not dramatic by the standards of more character-rich suburbs, but it is genuine. Gardens on the larger blocks come into colour, outdoor entertaining areas benefit from the warmer weather, and the practical appeal of Carindale's family infrastructure, schools, Westfield, open streets, is as visible and real as ever. Buyers in September are imagining summer in the back garden and school mornings that do not require a long drive.

The window between now and Christmas carries specific weight for Carindale. Families who do not secure a property in September or October face the prospect of moving through the disruption of December or waiting for February, when the market reopens after the holiday period. That calendar pressure is a genuine motivator for buyers currently in the market, and motivated buyers produce competitive auction results and strong private treaty offers.

For vendors who prepared early and launched in August or September, Carindale's spring season has provided the conditions promised. For those still deciding, the final weeks of September remain a viable window.
