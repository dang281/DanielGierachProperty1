## Caption

After hundreds of conversations with inner east vendors over the years, one thing is consistently true: the decision to sell is rarely about the market.

People sell because they have found somewhere they want to buy. Or because a relationship has changed, a family has grown, a job has moved, or a health situation has shifted the priorities. Or because they have quietly been thinking about it for a long time and they are finally ready.

The vendors who have the clearest, most grounded experience of the process are the ones who know exactly why they are selling before the campaign begins. That clarity shapes how they respond to offers, how they handle a drawn-out negotiation, and how they assess the right result.

The vendors who struggle most are often the ones whose trigger is vague: they thought the market seemed good, or they heard prices were up, or someone told them it was a good time. When the process becomes harder than expected, that vague motivation is not enough to carry them through cleanly.

If you are planning to sell your home in 2034, what is the single biggest factor driving your decision to list?

## Poll options

- I have found somewhere I want to buy
- A life change: family, work, or health
- Financial: equity review or investment rebalancing
- I have been thinking about it for a long time and I am ready
