## Caption

June 30 is a date that concentrates the mind for anyone selling an investment property. It is not a soft deadline. For sellers in the inner east with a property worth $1 million or more, the financial year in which a capital gain lands is a real difference in real dollars.

The timing works like this. A capital gain is assessed in the financial year in which a contract becomes unconditional, not the year of settlement. That is the date that matters. A seller who accepts an offer and goes unconditional in late June has crystallised a gain in the current financial year, regardless of when settlement occurs. A seller who goes unconditional in early July has pushed that gain into the next financial year, with twelve months of deferral and the potential to use the 50 per cent CGT discount if the asset has been held for more than a year.

For an investment property with $400,000 or more in capital gain, the difference in timing is not trivial. It is a conversation worth having with a good accountant before accepting any offer in May or June.

What this means practically: sellers with investment properties in the inner east often have more flexibility on timing than they realise. A buyer who wants to settle quickly can be accommodated with a standard settlement. A buyer willing to take a longer settlement in exchange for a firm price can give the seller certainty on the contract date while pushing settlement well into the new financial year.

This is the kind of structuring conversation that does not happen if the agent is not asking the right questions upfront. danielgierach.com
