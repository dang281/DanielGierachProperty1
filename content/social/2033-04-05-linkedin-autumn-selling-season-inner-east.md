## Caption

Autumn is consistently one of the better selling windows in Brisbane's inner east, and most vendors don't plan for it.

The reason is simple. School-zone decisions have been made by February, buyers who missed out over summer are still active, and the heat has broken enough that open homes actually get proper attendance. Conditions that align in April and May don't always align again until the following autumn.

What tends to happen instead is that vendors in suburbs like Hawthorne, Bulimba, and Morningside wait for spring. They assume spring is always the peak. It often is for volume, but volume means more competing listings too. Autumn buyers are frequently better qualified and more motivated. They've been looking for months. They know what good looks like.

The data from the inner east over the past several years shows clearance rates holding well into April and May, particularly for houses in the $1.5 million to $2.5 million range. These are not slow months. They are simply quieter on the listing side, which works in a vendor's favour.

If you are considering selling in 2033 and autumn feels early, it is worth at least having the conversation now. The preparation window, styling, photography, solicitor briefing, and campaign planning, takes four to six weeks minimum to do properly.

What you don't want is to be ready in June and find yourself holding off until September.
