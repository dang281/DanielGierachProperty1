## Caption

Carindale doesn't turn up in many conversations about Brisbane's inner east. That's partly geography and partly the fact that it sits closer to Westfield than to a riverside café strip. But the data doesn't reflect a suburb that's been overlooked.

The median house price is currently $1.8 million, up 10.6 per cent over the past twelve months. Average days on market sits at 29 days. That's a suburb where qualified buyers are making decisions quickly.

Who buys here: typically families trading up from Carina or Camp Hill, seeking more land and proximity to Whites Hill State College and Carindale State School. The 11km drive to the CBD and direct access to the Gateway Motorway matter to households with two working adults. Westfield Carindale provides the retail and dining infrastructure that other suburbs rely on weekend trips to find.

The supply side is tight. Carindale's housing stock skews toward larger four and five-bedroom homes on the eastern fringe of Brisbane's inner east. Properties with dual-living potential have been particularly in demand from buyers who want the suburb but need the rental income to support the mortgage.

What's not selling quickly: unrenovated properties priced as if the renovation has already happened. Buyers here are financially sophisticated. They know what a project costs and they price their offers accordingly. The vendors who get clean results are the ones whose presentation matches the price expectation.

If you're thinking about what your Carindale home might be worth in this market, I'm happy to talk through the recent sales data.
