## Caption

Selling a property is consistently ranked as one of the most stressful life events. The reasons tend to cluster around a few common themes, and they are not always the ones you would predict.

Price uncertainty is the obvious one. But in practice, I find sellers who have done their research and have a realistic price expectation manage the campaign with far less anxiety than sellers who are hoping for a number that the market has not validated.

The part that surprises people is how stressful the open home process feels. The house has to be clean, the pets have to be somewhere else, and strangers are walking through the bedroom. That is a lot, especially if you are still living there.

What has been the most stressful part of selling for you, or what would you be most worried about?

## Poll options

- Not knowing what price you will get
- Keeping the home campaign-ready for weeks
- Negotiations and the offer process
- Settlement timing and what comes next
