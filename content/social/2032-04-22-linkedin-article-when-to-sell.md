## Caption

"When is the right time to sell?" is probably the question I get asked most often. And the honest answer is that there is no universal right time. There is, however, a right time for your specific situation, and the two things are often confused.

Most people think about selling timing in terms of the market. When prices are up, sell. When interest rates drop, buyers come back. That logic is not wrong, but it misses something important: you can never sell at the exact peak, and waiting for perfect conditions often means waiting for conditions that never arrive.

What actually matters is the intersection of market conditions and your personal position. A property in good condition, in a suburb with real demand, sold by an owner who has done their preparation, will perform well across most market conditions. A poorly prepared property sold at the "right time" will underperform regardless.

There's also a consideration that doesn't get talked about enough: the cost of not selling. Holding a property you intend to eventually sell means carrying costs, opportunity costs, and sometimes emotional costs. Those don't feature in the "wait for the market" calculation, but they should.

The article below lays out a framework I use when I'm talking with owners about timing. It's worth reading if you're in the "thinking about it" phase.
