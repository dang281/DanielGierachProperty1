## Caption

The day after Christmas, Bulimba has a particular energy. Oxford Street is busy in a loose, unhurried way, the river is full of weekend activity, and the suburb feels like itself: unpretentious, well-located, and genuinely enjoyable to live in. There are not many inner-Brisbane suburbs where that description holds on December 26.

What makes Bulimba a consistently strong market is the tightness of supply. Character home stock here does not turn over often, and when it does, the pool of buyers who have been waiting is well-established. Bulimba State School keeps the family buyer anchored to the suburb, and the Oxford Street corridor gives the area a day-to-day quality of life that buyers from outside the inner east consistently underestimate until they actually visit.

Post-Christmas is when some of the most motivated buyers in this market are at their peak. Decisions made over the holiday table get actioned in the days that follow. For owners in Bulimba, the summer period between Boxing Day and Australia Day can be a more active selling window than it appears from the outside, precisely because supply stays low and buyer intent stays high.

If you own in Bulimba and have been sitting on a decision about 2033, the new year is a practical moment to revisit it with fresh eyes.
