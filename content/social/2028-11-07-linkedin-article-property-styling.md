## Caption

Property styling is primarily a photography investment. The furniture you rent for the campaign is not what buyers are buying. What they are buying is the photograph that made them book the inspection, and the first impression that confirmed what the photograph showed them.

That sequence matters because it explains why staging works for some properties and adds less value for others. A vacant property almost always benefits from styling: empty rooms read smaller than furnished ones in photographs, and they feel disconnected in person. A well-furnished owner-occupied home with good natural light and clean presentation may photograph just as well without replacement furniture.

The cost for a three-bedroom inner east home runs roughly $3,000 to $6,000 for a four-week campaign. That figure covers furniture hire, a stylist's time, and installation. It does not include the agent's marketing budget or photography, which are separate.

What changes the equation is the buyer pool. For a property targeting the upgrade family buyer who has been looking since spring, the photography quality and the presentation at inspection are the two moments in the campaign where you either gain their attention or lose it. Styling that lifts the photography quality has a direct line to the offer.

The full article covers when professional styling adds clear value, when it does not, and how to decide for your specific property.
