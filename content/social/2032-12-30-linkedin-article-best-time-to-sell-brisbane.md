## Caption

The most common piece of timing advice vendors receive is to list in spring. It is also the advice most likely to be wrong for Brisbane's inner east.

The spring-selling narrative is built on Sydney and Melbourne data. In those markets, October and November have historically been peak transaction months. Brisbane runs on a different cycle. The inner east's most consistent selling window is autumn (late February through May), when buyer numbers are strong, the post-summer attention span is focused, and competition at campaign level tends to be genuine rather than seasonal.

That does not mean listing in January is wrong. It means the decision should come from the property and the buyer pool it will attract, not from a Sydney-shaped assumption about when the market is best.

The more relevant question for most vendors is not what month to list in, but whether the property is ready. A well-prepared campaign launched in any month of the year will outperform an unprepared campaign in peak season. The gap between those two outcomes in the inner east regularly runs into six figures.

The article below covers the Brisbane-specific timing data, the suburbs where autumn consistently outperforms spring, and the preparation questions worth asking before any decision about timing is made.
