## Caption

Since 1 August 2025, sellers of residential property in Queensland must provide a completed Seller Disclosure Statement to the buyer before a contract is entered into.

This is not optional. It is a legal requirement under the Property Law Act 2023 (Qld), and it sits with the vendor to complete accurately through their solicitor.

The statement covers a broad range of matters: title and encumbrances, planning and zoning designations, pool safety certificate status, smoke alarm compliance, whether there are neighbourhood disputes on record, and environmental matters including whether the property appears on contaminated land registers.

The critical part for sellers: if the statement contains a false or misleading statement about a material matter, the buyer may be entitled to terminate the contract and recover their deposit in full. Getting it right at the outset is not just good practice, it is protection.

Separate from the formal statement, sellers also have ongoing obligations to disclose material facts. Anything a reasonable buyer would consider important when deciding whether to buy and at what price. The obligation exists whether or not the specific matter appears on the disclosure statement template.

This changed significantly in 2025. If you are preparing to sell and you have not spoken to your solicitor about the current disclosure requirements, that conversation should happen before the campaign starts.
