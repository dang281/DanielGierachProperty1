## Caption

Murarrie's value story has been building quietly for long enough that it's now attracting buyers who wouldn't have considered it three years ago. The residential streets in the eastern section of the suburb have been improving steadily, and the price gap between Murarrie and Morningside to the west has been closing.

The Trade Coast employment hub nearby gives the suburb a structural rental demand advantage that most residential inner east suburbs don't have in quite the same way. Workers in trade and industrial roles who want to live close to their employment base find Murarrie practical in a way that justifies the rental premium relative to more distant suburbs.

Gateway Motorway access shapes the commute options for residents here in a way that benefits buyers heading southeast or to the airport. That directional flexibility is worth something, and buyers who work in those corridors factor it into their suburb selection.

Investors who found Morningside and Cannon Hill at affordable prices a decade ago are looking at Murarrie with similar logic now. The fundamental case for the suburb is improving supply of good rental stock, strong proximity to employment, and a price point that still allows reasonable yield.

Owner-occupiers who buy in Murarrie now tend to be the type who see what the suburb can become rather than requiring it to already be there.
