## Caption

East Brisbane is the kind of suburb that rewards patience in the market. Properties here do not stay available for long, and buyers who have been watching know that when a well-presented home comes up, moving decisively matters. That dynamic reflects the persistent imbalance between the number of buyers who want to be in the suburb and the relatively small number of homes that come to market in any given period.

At three kilometres from the CBD, the suburb's position is its most fundamental asset. The distance is short enough that residents feel genuinely connected to the city without being inside the density of the inner core. South Bank's cultural and dining precinct is accessible without a car, as are the major health institutions at Mater and QEII. For buyers whose lives revolve around the health, education or creative sectors, East Brisbane's location is often near-optimal.

The housing stock reflects the suburb's history: heritage character homes, compact Queenslanders and some post-war buildings across the older residential streets, with pockets of newer development in some areas. The character home remains the most sought-after product, and the market tends to price it accordingly.

Current medians in East Brisbane place it at the premium end of the inner south-east ring but typically at a discount to New Farm and Teneriffe. That discount narrows consistently over time as buyers continue to identify East Brisbane as a rational alternative to those more expensive neighbours.
