## Caption

Off-market sales come up regularly in Brisbane's inner east. An agent calls a vendor and says they have a buyer ready to go. No campaign, no open homes, no marketing costs. The vendor gets a quick result and the buyer gets access they would not otherwise have had.

The trade-off is competition. A full campaign creates competition between buyers. Off-market removes that competition entirely. And limiting competition almost always affects the price outcome.

There are legitimate reasons to sell off-market: a tenant in place, personal circumstances, a vendor who values privacy above price. But the idea that an agent with a buyer ready to go is doing the vendor a favour is one of the more durable myths in real estate.

In your view, which approach typically produces a better result for the seller?

My position on this is straightforward: if a vendor has the ability to run a full campaign, a full campaign will almost always produce a better result. The exceptions are real but they are exceptions, not the standard. Off-market makes sense in specific circumstances. It is not a selling strategy for properties with no complications.
