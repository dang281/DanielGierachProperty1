## Caption

Queensland calls it transfer duty, not stamp duty. The name barely matters. What matters is how much it adds to the cost of a property purchase, and how often buyers underestimate it.

On a $1 million owner-occupier purchase, Queensland transfer duty is approximately $38,025. That figure does not add to your equity. It does not reduce your loan balance. It is an upfront government cost that must be funded from savings, separate from your deposit.

Most buyers roughly estimate it and get the number wrong by $10,000 or more in either direction. Getting it wrong by $15,000 upward means the deposit you thought was sufficient is actually $15,000 short. In a fast-moving market where a conditional pre-approval is already stretching your LVR, that gap matters.

The Queensland Revenue Office publishes a transfer duty calculator. It takes two minutes to run the exact figure for your purchase price. Before you make an offer, run that calculator and confirm your buying costs are fully accounted for.

First home buyers have access to concessions that can significantly reduce transfer duty on eligible purchases. Whether those concessions apply depends on the purchase price, the buyer's status, and whether the property is a home or investment. The detail on all of that is at danielgierach.com/insights/stamp-duty-queensland.
