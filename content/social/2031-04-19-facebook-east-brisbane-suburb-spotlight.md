## Caption

East Brisbane is the closest residential suburb to the Gabba precinct, and that proximity is reflected in the buyer mix more than in a single buyer type. Owner-occupiers who want to walk to South Bank via Vulture Street, investors who understand the rental demand that precinct generates, and downsizers leaving the inner-south who want to remain within the inner ring all compete for the same housing in East Brisbane. That diversity of demand is a stabilising force.

The character homes in East Brisbane, particularly on the streets that run between the Gabba precinct and the river, represent an inner-city walkability that is difficult to price against suburban alternatives. A buyer who can step outside their front gate and reach three restaurants, a bottle shop, and a bus to the CBD within five minutes is purchasing a lifestyle arithmetic that the outer suburbs genuinely cannot replicate at any price.

Flood overlay is a real consideration in parts of East Brisbane, and buyers who have done their research will ask about it. Properties that sit outside the overlay, or on elevated positions that mitigate flood exposure, carry a measurable premium over the suburb median. That distinction matters significantly for vendor positioning.

The apartment market in East Brisbane runs alongside the character home market. Each attracts its own buyer type, and the two markets have different seasonal patterns. In April, owner-occupier demand for character homes is typically more active than the investor-driven apartment segment.
