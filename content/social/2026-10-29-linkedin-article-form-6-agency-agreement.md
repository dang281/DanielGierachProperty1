## Caption

Before a real estate agent in Queensland can market or sell your property, you have to sign a Form 6. It is the legal document that appoints them as your authorised representative. Without it, they cannot advertise, hold open homes, or negotiate on your behalf.

Most vendors sign it at the end of an appraisal, with the agent still in the room, without having read it in full.

That is worth understanding, because several clauses have real financial consequences. Whether the appointment is exclusive or open, and for how long. When commission is triggered and exactly what the rate applies to. What marketing costs you are committing to and whether they are refundable if the property does not sell. What happens at the end of the appointment period if you have not yet sold.

Queensland law gives you a five-business-day cooling-off period after signing, during which you can withdraw without penalty. Most sellers are not aware of that right when they sign.

None of this means you should distrust your agent. It means you should understand the agreement before you enter it, the same way you would any other contract involving a property worth $900K or more.

I have put together a plain-English guide to the Form 6 on danielgierach.com, covering what each section means, what to look for before you sign, and the five questions worth asking any agent before you commit.
