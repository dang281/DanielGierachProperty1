## Caption

The buyers who show up in January are not the same buyers who show up in March.

By January, they have usually been looking for three to six months. They know what they want. They have spoken to their broker, they understand what they can borrow, and they are ready to move. What they do not have is a lot to choose from, because most sellers wait until autumn to list.

That gap between supply and demand is what makes early-year campaigns in the inner east worth understanding. A motivated buyer pool sitting on limited stock produces competitive conditions that have nothing to do with the volume you see later in the season.

What I notice at open homes in January is that groups are smaller but the conversations are more direct. People who are just browsing have not come back from holidays yet. The people who show up in the first two weeks of the year are almost always genuine.

If you are considering selling and want to understand whether January or autumn makes more sense for your specific property, I am happy to walk through the numbers with you.
