## Caption

Almost all advice about the best time to sell in Australia is built around Melbourne and Sydney data. Brisbane's inner east follows a different seasonal pattern, and the conclusions drawn from southern markets do not transfer cleanly here.

Spring is active in Brisbane. Buyers are out, and stock is moving. But the data from the inner east over recent years points to autumn as the more consistent selling window, particularly for houses. The buyer pool in March and April tends to be smaller but more decisive. Stock levels are lower, which reduces competition. Buyers who are active in autumn have typically been in the market for months and are ready to commit.

Spring has its strengths too. Listings photograph better, open homes draw larger crowds, and the pre-Christmas settlement deadline adds a natural urgency in October and November. But high buyer volumes also mean more competition from other vendors. A crowded spring market can dilute the demand that should be focused on your property.

The more important variable is preparation. A well-prepared property with clean contracts, a building and pest report, and a coherent campaign can sell in any month. An unprepared property in peak spring is still an uphill campaign. Timing matters, but it is the second conversation, not the first.
