## Caption

The inner east is not one market. The buyer profile, price points, and absorption rates vary suburb by suburb, and those variations matter when you are setting a campaign strategy.

What I have been observing across the key inner east suburbs through March 2034.

Bulimba and Hawthorne continue to attract buyers who are making a clear suburb choice, not just a price point choice. The lifestyle proposition of the Oxford Street precinct and the river proximity sustains a buyer pool that is motivated even when the price range is at the top of the inner east spectrum.

Norman Park and Morningside are performing with consistency across the family home category. Properties that present well and are priced with accuracy relative to recent comparable sales are attracting competitive offers. The buyer profile here is predominantly families making a long-term decision.

Camp Hill is showing strong absorption for free-standing houses with good land content. The suburb has a deep buyer pool that responds quickly to quality campaigns.

Coorparoo is the most price-diverse of the inner east suburbs and the activity reflects that range: the sub-$1m segment moves differently from the $1.5m-plus segment, and the marketing strategy needs to account for it.

Balmoral commands a premium for its river and park proximity that the market has consistently supported. Campaigns here are typically longer but resolve at strong prices when the buyer is found.

The pattern across all suburbs: vendor expectations calibrated to the current buyer pool outperform those set in isolation from recent comparable results. That calibration conversation is the most important one to have before the campaign launches.
