## Caption

Balmoral sits on a ridge above the Brisbane River, and the homes that capture views from that elevation are among the most sought-after addresses in the inner east. The suburb is quieter than neighbouring Bulimba and Hawthorne, which is itself a selling point for the buyers it attracts: people who want proximity to everything the Oxford Street corridor offers but prefer a slower pace on their own street.

Listing volumes in Balmoral are structurally low. The suburb is predominantly owner-occupied, long-held, and residents tend to move only when life circumstances require it. When quality homes do come to market, competition at auction is concentrated and serious. Buyers who have been watching Balmoral for months will bid accordingly.

The housing mix runs from substantial character Queenslanders on elevated blocks to well-built post-war homes with genuine land content. Balmoral State School catchment underpins family buyer demand, and the river access at Lores Bonney Riverwalk adds a lifestyle component that buyers at this price point consider non-negotiable. Tingal Road and Wynnum Road (Balmoral section) both produce strong results when properties are presented well and priced accurately.

If you own in Balmoral and have been considering your options, low stock levels work in your favour. An appraisal costs nothing and tells you exactly where you stand.
