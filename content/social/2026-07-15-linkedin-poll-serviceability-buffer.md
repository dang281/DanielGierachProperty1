## Caption

APRA requires banks to test your ability to repay a mortgage at your actual interest rate plus 3%.

If you're borrowing at 6.5%, the bank tests your repayment capacity at 9.5%. This serviceability buffer is one of the most significant reasons people find they can borrow less than expected.

It was introduced to ensure borrowers could withstand rate rises. In an environment where rates have already moved significantly, some economists argue the 3% floor is too conservative. But as of mid-2026, it remains in place.

Did you know about the serviceability buffer before reading this?
