## Caption

Mt Gravatt East sits on elevated ground, and from many streets you get city views that buyers are genuinely surprised to find at this price point. The suburb offers something that is hard to find in the inner east: character homes on larger blocks, with the kind of space that has been priced out of Camp Hill and Hawthorne for most buyers.

The buyers I work with in Mt Gravatt East are typically families. They have usually looked at suburbs closer to the city, priced the block sizes and come to the conclusion that they get more for their money here without sacrificing the quality of the lifestyle. Westfield Garden City is close for daily shopping and dining, and the broader amenity of the area is genuinely well served.

The Mount Gravatt State High School catchment is a consistent driver of demand in this suburb. Families specifically searching for catchment access know what they want, and when the right property comes up they move decisively. Properties within the catchment boundary hold their own well against comparable properties in adjacent suburbs at higher price points, which tells you something about how seriously that catchment is rated.

The street-by-street variation in this suburb is worth understanding. Elevated streets with city aspect consistently perform above the suburb median, and the gap between a well-positioned block and a flat street can be meaningful. If you are thinking about what your property might achieve in the current market, I am happy to give you a clear picture.
