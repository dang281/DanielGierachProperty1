## Caption

Cannon Hill sits in a position that not many inner east suburbs can match: it is close enough to the CBD to be genuinely convenient, far enough out to still have proper land sizes, and it catches buyers who have been priced out of Bulimba and Hawthorne without feeling like a compromise.

The suburb's proximity to the Cannon Hill train station and the Pacific Motorway gives it strong commuter appeal. Families are drawn to the corridor around Samuel Griffith Drive and the established streets running off Old Cleveland Road. The shopping precinct at Cannon Hill Plaza has added retail diversity that supports lifestyle for residents rather than just passing trade.

In an Olympic year, the connection to arterial routes heading toward the inner city venues matters more than it used to. Buyers who want inner east access without inner east pricing have been looking at Cannon Hill more seriously since infrastructure timelines became clearer.

Properties here tend to be post-war character homes and lowset brick, with a growing number of modern infill builds and dual occupancy sites. Lot sizes are generally larger than you find in Bulimba, which still draws renovators and owner-occupiers with long-term plans.

Vendor preparation matters here. Well-presented, well-documented homes in Cannon Hill attract strong buyer pools. Rushed campaigns on unrealistic prices sit. The difference is in the strategy before the sign goes up.
