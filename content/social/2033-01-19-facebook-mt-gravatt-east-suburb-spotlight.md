## Caption

Mt Gravatt East occupies a practical position in Brisbane's inner south-east that has made it consistently attractive to investors and owner-occupiers alike. Westfield Garden City is a short drive, Griffith University's Nathan campus is on the doorstep, and the Mount Gravatt Road commercial strip provides the everyday conveniences that reduce the friction of suburban living.

The proximity to Griffith University shapes the rental market significantly. Student and academic accommodation demand provides yield-focused investors with a reliable tenant base, and that has historically kept vacancy rates in the suburb low. ⚠️ VERIFY For owner-occupiers, the same proximity means that the suburb's services and infrastructure are well maintained and accessible.

The housing stock in Mt Gravatt East is a mix that accommodates a range of budgets. Post-war homes that have been renovated sit alongside original-condition properties that offer genuine renovation upside. The elevation in parts of the suburb provides views and cross-ventilation that improve liveability significantly.

For vendors in 2033, the dual buyer pool, investors and owner-occupiers competing for the same stock, is a structural advantage. Properties that are well-maintained and sensibly priced tend to attract genuine competition because two different buyer types are assessing them against different benchmarks.

Understanding which buyer your property is most likely to attract shapes how you present it and how you set your price guide.
