## Caption

Seven Hills sits between Morningside and Murarrie, and it shares the access profile of both without carrying either suburb's price premium. The Gateway Motorway connection is close, the inner city is within easy reach, and Morningside State School catchment gives families a strong public education argument for choosing this pocket over suburbs that cost noticeably more.

The housing stock in Seven Hills is predominantly post-war homes alongside newer builds, which means buyers are looking at real land content and genuine development or renovation potential. That combination draws two distinct buyer types: families who want to move in and improve over time, and buyers with a clear renovation or build strategy from day one. Both categories are active in this market right now.

The suburb is less discussed in broader Brisbane property conversations than its neighbours, and that has historically kept entry prices more accessible. Buyers who have done the homework recognise that the access story, the school catchment, and the land content compare well to suburbs that command significantly higher prices. That is a gap that tends to close as suburbs mature.

If you own in Seven Hills and are considering your next move, now is a good time to understand what your property is worth in the current market.
