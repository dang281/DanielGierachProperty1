## Caption

Murarrie is one of the inner east's most interesting stories right now. For years it sat in the shadow of Morningside, carrying an industrial tag that kept buyer interest modest. That narrative is shifting.

Buyers priced out of Morningside have been looking east, and Murarrie keeps coming up. The Gateway Motorway access is genuinely useful for anyone commuting north or south, and the drive to the CBD is shorter than many people expect. Entry price points remain lower than the suburbs immediately to the west, which is exactly what draws first-home buyers and investors looking for yield in a tightening rental market.

New residential development has added housing stock with a more contemporary feel, and that is gradually changing how people see the suburb. The mix of new builds alongside older homes gives buyers options at different price levels, which creates a more active market than you might expect from a suburb still establishing its identity.

Mid-winter is a practical time to assess Murarrie. Competition from other listings is quiet. Buyers who are active in July are serious, and serious buyers make decisions. If you own here and have been wondering what your property is worth in this market, the answer may surprise you.
