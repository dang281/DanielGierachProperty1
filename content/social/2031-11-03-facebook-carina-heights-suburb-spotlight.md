## Caption

Carina Heights has a quieter feel than Carina and generally comes with larger blocks. That difference in block size is what draws the owner-occupier profile that defines this suburb. Buyers who are choosing between Carina and Carina Heights are almost always making a decision about land, and Carina Heights tends to win that comparison.

There's no single school that dominates buyer decisions here the way Whites Hill State College does in Camp Hill, but Carina Heights sits within reach of several catchments, and that breadth gives families options. The quieter streets and the larger gardens appeal to buyers who are done with the inner city intensity and want something that functions well as a long-term family home.

The trajectory for Carina Heights has been improving steadily as Carina itself strengthens. That flow-on effect is typical of how Brisbane's inner east reprices: the headline suburb moves first, and the adjacent suburbs follow. Carina Heights is now in the follow phase.

What moves fastest here is the home on a well-maintained larger block with a usable backyard and good indoor-outdoor flow. What sits longer is the property where the block size advantage is undermined by a poor layout or difficult access.
