## Caption

Most buyers in Queensland call their solicitor after they have already signed the contract.

That sequence is backwards.

The contract is where your legal exposure is set. Finance condition deadline, inclusions and exclusions, special conditions, settlement date. Once signed, renegotiating any of these is difficult and sometimes impossible. Reviewing before signing takes a few hours and can catch issues that cost weeks to resolve after the fact.

The distinction between a solicitor and a licensed conveyancer matters most in complex transactions: properties with unusual title conditions, units with complicated body corporate structures, or homes where the inclusion schedule needs careful attention. Both are qualified to handle a standard residential purchase. But only a solicitor can advise on matters that go beyond the conveyancing process itself.

For most inner east Brisbane purchases, either works well. The question to ask is not which one is cheaper. It is whether the property you are about to sign for has any complexity that warrants a solicitor's broader remit.
