## Caption

Clearance rate figures get reported and discussed as if they tell you something simple. They do not. The number that appears in the weekly headline averages across property types, price ranges, and suburb profiles that behave very differently from each other.

What I pay attention to at the suburb level is the pattern over three to four weeks, not the single-week result. A clearance rate that moves between 68 and 74 percent over a month tells a different story from one that drops week by week from 72 to 58 percent. The direction matters more than the absolute number.

Across the inner east through Q1 2034, the auction clearance pattern has been consistent with a market where vendor expectations and buyer willingness to pay are broadly aligned. Properties that went to market priced with reference to recent comparable sales have been clearing. Properties priced in hope of achieving something above the comparable set have been passing in or sitting.

The practical implication for vendors: the autumn market is rewarding accuracy. A property positioned correctly in relation to what buyers have actually paid in the suburb in the last 90 days is competing well. One priced to a peak that has passed is not finding buyers willing to bridge the gap.
