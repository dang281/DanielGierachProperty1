## Caption

Professional styling in Brisbane typically costs between $2,000 and $6,000 depending on the size of the property and how much furniture needs to come in. The question vendors ask is whether they can afford it. The more useful question is whether they can afford not to.

The first meaningful interaction most buyers have with your property is not the open home. It is the listing photos on REA or Domain. That's where decisions about whether to enquire, whether to attend, and what emotional position to bring to the inspection are formed. A property that photographs well drives more enquiry. More enquiry brings more groups through the door. More groups create the competition that drives price. Styling, in this framing, is not a cosmetic expense. It is the first link in a chain that ends at your sale price.

There's a compounding effect that vendors sometimes miss. A styled property doesn't just attract more buyers, it signals care, quality, and a vendor who is serious about the process. Buyers read that. It affects their offer psychology in ways that are hard to quantify but consistently observable.

The properties I've seen styled well, photographed well, and launched with strong marketing consistently outperform equivalent unstyled listings. Not always by the cost of the styling, but often by substantially more.

The article goes into what Brisbane stylists actually deliver, how to assess whether full styling or partial staging is appropriate for your property, and what the cost-benefit looks like across different price brackets.
