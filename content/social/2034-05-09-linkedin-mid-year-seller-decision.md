## Caption

The conventional logic says to wait for spring. Sell in September or October when buyer volumes peak and the market feels active. There are rational reasons to follow that logic.

There are also rational reasons not to.

Spring brings more buyers. It also brings more properties. In a typical inner east spring, the number of listings coming to market in September and October rises sharply. The buyer-to-listing ratio, which determines how much competition a property generates, does not always improve simply because buyer numbers are up. If listing supply rises in proportion, the competitive dynamics for any individual property are not significantly better.

A vendor who goes to market in May or June faces a smaller active buyer pool but also faces less competition from other listings. The buyers who are active in winter are often motivated and decisive. They are not browsing. They are committed to transacting.

The mid-year window does not suit every property or every vendor. But for well-presented properties in suburbs with consistent demand, a June or July campaign run properly can produce results that compare favourably with the autumn or spring peaks.

If you are considering whether to act now or wait, the specific calculation depends on your suburb, your property, and the current listing supply. That conversation is worth having before spring arrives.
