## Caption

Receiving a low offer is frustrating. The instinct for many vendors is to reject it and move on. That instinct is understandable, and it is usually the wrong move.

A low offer is rarely the buyer's ceiling. It is almost always their opening position. In Brisbane's inner east, an offer sitting 8 to 10 per cent below asking price is a fairly standard opening from a buyer who is genuinely motivated to purchase. Rejecting it outright closes the negotiation entirely. Countering keeps it open, and countering almost always reveals where the buyer can actually go.

The vendors who handle low offers well treat the offer as information, not an insult. What is the buyer's finance position? What is their timeline? Have they made offers elsewhere? The answers to those questions change how you respond. A buyer who is pre-approved, has no other properties under offer, and needs to be in before school starts is not the same as a buyer who is still exploring the market.

I have written a detailed guide on handling low offers when selling in Brisbane, covering how to counter, what to do if a buyer goes quiet, and when to accept that an offer is genuinely at the buyer's limit.
