## Caption

Norman Park is one of the few suburbs in the inner east where the state school catchment commands a comparable level of buyer commitment to what private school proximity generates elsewhere. The Whites Hill State College catchment is not a soft demand driver. Families who have identified it as their secondary school of choice will make location decisions years in advance and will not compromise on the catchment boundary when they buy.

The village feel around the Norman Park train station is one of the defining characteristics of the suburb that long-term residents reference consistently. The station gives the suburb a pedestrian rhythm that most of the inner east does not have, and the streets that run up toward Whites Hill Reserve from the station carry a residential quality that is visible in the property values there.

Whites Hill Reserve itself is the suburb's green lungs. For families with children, or for residents who structure part of their week around walking or trail running, the reserve is not a passive amenity. It is actively used, and that use reinforces the sense of community that Norman Park has maintained through significant periods of price growth.

Camp Hill buyers who are narrowing their search within the Whites Hill State College catchment will often find that Norman Park satisfies the same criteria at a similar or slightly higher price, depending on the street. Understanding that intersection of buyer behaviour is useful for vendors thinking about their competition.
