## Caption

Three days before the new year, Norman Park is doing what it always does: sitting quietly at the top of the inner east family suburb consideration list for buyers who've thought carefully about where they want to land.

The summer holiday period in Norman Park has a particular quality. Whites Hill Reserve is well used, the streets are safe and quiet enough for kids to be outside, and the general tempo of the suburb is relaxed in a way that reminds buyers who visit in December exactly why they wanted to be here.

Norman Park State School's catchment is not just a school decision. It is, for many families, the foundational reason for the address decision. That means buyers who target this suburb are highly motivated and not easily redirected. They come in knowing the suburb, knowing the streets they prefer, and knowing what recent comparable sales looked like. That research depth among buyers is one of the defining characteristics of the Norman Park market.

The reserve-adjacent streets remain the most contested. Properties on Whites Hill Road, Maryvale Street, and the blocks that back onto the reserve boundary regularly achieve the suburb's highest individual sale outcomes.

End of year listings in Norman Park are rare, which is exactly why they attract attention when they do appear. The motivated buyer pool that has been building through the second half of the year doesn't disappear in December.
