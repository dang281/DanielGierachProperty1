## Caption

Most Queensland buyers sign the contract first and call their solicitor second. By then, the window to negotiate conditions has usually closed.

A solicitor can advise you on whether the contract terms are appropriate for your situation. A conveyancer processes the transfer. They are not the same service.

In Queensland, the contract is legally binding once signed. Reviewing it before you sign, not after, is when professional advice has the most value.

The cooling-off period of five business days applies to residential property but comes with a 0.25 per cent penalty. It is not a free exit.
