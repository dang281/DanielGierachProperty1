## Caption

Brisbane 2032 is three years away. The conversation has shifted from anticipation to observation, because the infrastructure investment tied to the Games is already visible and, in some cases, already complete.

Cross River Rail has changed commute times from inner east suburbs materially. The Gabba precinct redevelopment has altered the land use and amenity calculus for East Brisbane, Woolloongabba, and the surrounding fringe. Active transport corridors connecting the inner east to the CBD and South Bank have been upgraded in ways that affect how buyers think about car dependency. These are not speculative improvements. They are done, or close to it.

The buyer sentiment effect is real but easy to overstate. The properties benefiting most directly are those within walking or cycling distance of completed infrastructure, particularly the new rail connections and precinct upgrades. Buyers from interstate, in particular, are highly attuned to this. For someone relocating from Sydney or Melbourne, improved public transport connectivity is not a bonus, it's a baseline requirement.

What the Olympic period typically does in host cities is pull forward development activity rather than create entirely new demand. In suburbs like Bulimba, East Brisbane, and the Woolloongabba fringe, that pull-forward effect is already embedded in current values to some degree. Whether there is further repricing as 2032 approaches is a function of how the broader market moves, not just the Olympic calendar.

For vendors in the inner east, the relevant point is that the infrastructure story is a genuine selling point, not a speculative one.
