## Caption

Bulimba wraps around a bend in the Brisbane River, and that geography sets the terms for almost everything in the suburb's property market. Riverview homes on streets like Honour Avenue and the riverfront reaches of Oxford Street command a different conversation entirely from the character housing further in, but the suburb maintains a coherent identity across both. Oxford Street is the spine: Sunday markets, well-regarded restaurants, and a neighbourhood bottle shop culture that residents describe as part of daily life rather than an occasion.

The Bulimba ferry terminal, with its cross-river link to the Norman Park side and onward to the CBD, is a genuine commuting asset that buyers from other parts of Brisbane tend to underestimate. Combined with the Bulimba District State High School catchment and proximity to Anglican Church Grammar School at East Brisbane, the suburb attracts families who have done thorough research and arrived with a specific brief.

Older Queenslanders on the elevated streets between Oxford Street and Hawthorne Road tend to generate competitive buyer fields, particularly where the original character has been preserved or the renovation has respected the structure rather than concealed it. Buyers in this suburb are typically informed and motivated, and well-presented properties move quickly.
