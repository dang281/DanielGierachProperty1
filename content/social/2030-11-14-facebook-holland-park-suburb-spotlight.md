## Caption

Holland Park is not a suburb that announces itself loudly. Its appeal is practical and consistent: established streets, reasonable land sizes, and a position that gives residents access to the inner east's lifestyle assets without the inner east's highest price points.

The streets north of Logan Road, particularly those approaching the Camp Hill and Coorparoo boundaries, are where the suburb's best-performing residential stock sits. Buyers who have done their research understand that the northern section of Holland Park has a different character to the southern section, and that the school catchment implications differ accordingly.

Cavendish Road State High School is the key secondary catchment driver for families in Holland Park. The school's reputation draws buyers who specifically target the catchment zone, and the boundary line between in-zone and out-of-zone addresses has a measurable effect on pricing.

The Holland Park West shopping centre and the retail access on Logan Road give the suburb practical day-to-day amenity without requiring a trip to the larger shopping centres nearby. For dual-income families with limited time for long shopping trips, that localised convenience is a quiet asset.

For homeowners in Holland Park's northern streets, the November buyer pool remains active and motivated.
