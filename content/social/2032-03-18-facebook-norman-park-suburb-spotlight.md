## Caption

Norman Park is one of the few inner east suburbs where the relationship between location and price is immediately legible to buyers. The ridge above Norman Park, running along the streets that back onto the escarpment with unobstructed city and river views, commands a consistent premium. Properties there are not comparable to the flatter streets near Norman Creek, and buyers understand that distinction before they arrive at open homes.

The streets off Bennetts Road and Old Cleveland Road, particularly those that climb toward the elevated plateau in the suburb's interior, attract buyers who have already looked at Balmoral and Camp Hill and are seeking the same quality of house and streetscape at a price that reflects a slightly different location. The results in Norman Park in recent years have confirmed that gap is narrowing.

Norman Park State School has a strong local reputation and is a fixture in buyer conversations at open homes with families. The suburb's green infrastructure, including the Norman Creek corridor and the Whites Hill Reserve access, is relevant to a specific and growing buyer cohort.

March campaigns in Norman Park perform consistently. The suburb draws a concentrated buyer pool with genuine financial capacity, and the autumn window, before Easter compresses open home attendance, is the clearest run of the year.
