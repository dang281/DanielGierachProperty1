## Caption

Carina Heights in the final week of September is a suburb showing its seasonal advantage to full effect. The elevated streets look genuinely beautiful with spring gardens in colour, and the quieter residential character that distinguishes Carina Heights from busier neighbouring suburbs is palpable on an inspection walk. Buyers who have been running between open homes in Carindale and Cannon Hill frequently find Carina Heights provides a contrast that feels like a positive discovery.

The family demographic that drives Carina Heights is at its most active right now. School proximity to Cannon Hill Anglican College and the larger lot sizes that allow children genuine outdoor space are the two selling points that resonate most clearly with the buyers in this market. Both are visible and tangible on a September inspection in a way that photographs partially capture but in-person experience confirms.

Late September is also the point where serious buyers who have not yet secured a property start to feel genuine urgency. Spring was supposed to deliver more choice, and for some buyers, it has. But for buyers specifically looking for Carina Heights' combination of size, quiet, and school access, the supply remains genuinely tight. That creates the competitive conditions where well-prepared vendors achieve the outcomes they planned for.

If you are an owner in Carina Heights who has been holding back, this is the window the market has been building toward all year.
