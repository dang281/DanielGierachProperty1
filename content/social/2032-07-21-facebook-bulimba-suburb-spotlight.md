## Caption

Oxford Street is the reason buyers come to Bulimba. A 400-metre stretch of cafes, restaurants, and locally-owned retail that creates daily walkability of a kind that most of Brisbane cannot offer at any price point. Buyers who have lived close to it know what they are giving up when they leave. Most do not leave voluntarily.

The Bulimba ferry to the CBD changes the commute calculation entirely. Under 30 minutes on the water, no road congestion, and a direct connection to the city without a car. That is a product that buyers who work in the CBD or who value their commute time assign real value to.

Bulimba State School is one of the most sought-after public school catchments in the inner east, and competition at enrolment time reflects that consistently.

The housing stock at the premium end runs to post-war Queenslanders and contemporaries on full blocks in the northern and central streets. The southern end of the suburb, toward Wynnum Road and Heritage Lane, carries townhouse stock and post-war homes at more accessible price points.

Buyers who choose Bulimba have usually arrived at that decision from a considered search of the inner east. The suburb rarely needs to sell itself.
