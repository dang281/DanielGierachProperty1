## Caption

The winter data in Brisbane's inner east is worth reading carefully before the spring market opens. Not because it predicts what spring will look like, but because it reveals the state of the buyer pool and the vendor position that spring will launch from.

What the winter period typically shows in the inner east is a compression of supply alongside a persistence of genuine demand. The open home numbers fall, but the buyers who attend are more qualified and more motivated than the broader pool that appears in September. The properties that cleared in winter, particularly those in school catchment suburbs like Camp Hill, Norman Park, and Coorparoo, cleared because the demand was specific and ready. That is useful market intelligence.

The vendors who did not transact in winter fall broadly into two groups. Those who tested the market and withdrew because the price gap was too wide. And those who held deliberately to benefit from the spring buyer pool. The first group will re-enter spring with updated expectations. The second group will enter spring competing with each other and with the new listings that always appear in September.

The buyers who were patient through winter and did not find the right property are now moving into the spring market with clear intent and confirmed capacity. That combination is what makes the first six weeks of spring in the inner east the most productive window of the year for well-prepared vendors.
