## Caption

Seven Hills sits on elevated ground, and from many streets you get views across the surrounding inner east that buyers are not always expecting when they first attend an open home here. The established tree canopy along the residential streets gives the suburb a leafy, settled character that feels different from the denser inner-ring suburbs, and that combination of elevation, views, and greenery is what many buyers are consciously seeking when they start looking in this area.

The suburb sits between two strong amenity precincts: Camp Hill Village on one side and the Old Cleveland Road shopping and dining strip on the other. For daily convenience, the residents of Seven Hills are well positioned relative to most inner east suburbs at a comparable price point.

Mount Gravatt State High School catchment covers Seven Hills, bringing the same family buyer profile that drives demand in Carina and Mt Gravatt East. Families researching catchment access find Seven Hills a compelling option that provides genuine value relative to its neighbours.

The buyer profile I see in Seven Hills is typically one that has done a careful comparison with Camp Hill and Norman Park, concluded that the price point in Seven Hills delivers comparable liveability and school access at a more accessible cost, and decided that the relative value is the deciding factor. These buyers know the suburb, know the comparable sales, and are ready to act.

If you are thinking about selling in Seven Hills, I am happy to walk you through what the current market looks like.
