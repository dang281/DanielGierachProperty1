## Caption

On a $1 million purchase as an owner-occupier in Queensland, transfer duty adds approximately $38,025 to your settlement costs. That figure does not build equity. It does not reduce over time. It is a cash cost that sits alongside your deposit, your conveyancing fees, and your inspection costs, and it is due at settlement.

Most buyers know stamp duty exists. Very few have an accurate number in their head until it appears on the settlement statement, at which point the surprise is unwelcome. In my experience, buyers underestimate the actual figure by $10,000 or more, particularly in the $800,000 to $1.2 million range where the duty brackets move quickly.

The practical consequence is straightforward: if you are buying at the $1 million mark and you have budgeted your deposit carefully, you need to have accounted for transfer duty separately. It cannot come from your deposit without reducing your loan-to-value ratio in ways your lender may not accept. It needs to be cash on top, or financed in a way your broker has specifically structured.

There are concessions available in Queensland for first home buyers, and the thresholds for those concessions are worth understanding if you are purchasing your first property. The article below covers what you will pay at different price points, how the duty is calculated, and what concessions apply and under what conditions.
