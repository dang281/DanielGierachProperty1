## Caption

Seven Hills has been the answer for a specific buyer for several years now: the one who wants inner east proximity, a genuine outdoor lifestyle, and a block that can actually fit a family. As Camp Hill and Norman Park become increasingly difficult to enter, the flow of buyers into Seven Hills has been consistent and growing.

Whites Hill Reserve is accessible from the suburb and defines the outdoor experience here. Trail running, walking, and the general sense of bush within the city are lifestyle features that attract a buyer who has thought carefully about where they want to live and made a values-based decision about what they're trading off by not being closer in.

Seven Hills State School handles the local primary. The suburb's block sizes are among the most generous for the inner east, which means buyers who need four bedrooms with actual separation between living and sleeping zones find what they're looking for here without the compromises that tighter suburbs force.

The December timing has an interesting dynamic in Seven Hills. Buyers who have been through the spring rush in Camp Hill and Norman Park without finding what they want, and who've extended their search radius, arrive in Seven Hills in November and December with a different perspective. They've done the comparison work, and often they're ready.

Gardens here present well in December. The outdoor space that distinguishes Seven Hills is at its best.
