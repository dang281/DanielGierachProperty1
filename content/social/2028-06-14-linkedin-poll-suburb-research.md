## Caption

The gap between what online data tells you about a suburb and what you understand from actually spending time there is significant. CoreLogic and Domain give you medians, days on market, and historical growth rates. They do not tell you which streets are affected by flight paths, which blocks back onto a drainage reserve, or which pockets are quietly being repriced because of a new cafe strip two streets over. Some buyers figure this out before they make an offer. Others discover it afterwards. The mix of data and on-the-ground intel matters, and the order in which people approach it says a lot about how they buy. How do you research a suburb before buying?

## Poll options

- Drive around and attend open homes
- Data first, CoreLogic or Domain, then inspect
- Ask a local agent who specialises there
- All of the above, in roughly that order
