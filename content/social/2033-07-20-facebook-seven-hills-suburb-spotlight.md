## Caption

Seven Hills is one of the least discussed but most consistently performing suburbs in Brisbane's inner east. Located just south of Coorparoo and immediately north of the Mount Gravatt escarpment, the suburb offers elevated blocks, leafy streets, and a quiet residential character that attracts buyers who have done the comparative work on the surrounding areas and recognised the value.

The suburb's appeal is built on its position within the inner east without the premium attached to the river-adjacent or higher-profile catchment suburbs. Buyers who want a character home on a reasonable block within ten kilometres of the CBD, with good road access to the Pacific Motorway and the convenience of the Upper Mount Gravatt retail strip nearby, find Seven Hills consistently delivers those fundamentals.

The housing stock in Seven Hills reflects its interwar and postwar development history: timber Queenslanders alongside brick lowsets, on blocks that are generous by inner east standards. The elevation provides outlooks across the surrounding suburbs that buyers with an eye for the long-term understand translate to desirability that persists.

Seven Hills remains underrepresented in most suburb ranking conversations, which means it is still possible to find value here relative to what comparable fundamentals cost in Coorparoo or Holland Park.
