## Caption

Most Brisbane homeowners selling their primary residence will never pay capital gains tax.

The main residence exemption under Australian tax law is broad. If you've lived in the property for the entire period you've owned it, never rented it, and the land is under two hectares, there is no CGT liability. No threshold, no limit. Sell for $2 million more than you paid: still zero.

But several scenarios create a partial CGT liability that catches vendors who assumed they were fully exempt.

If you rented the property for a period before moving in, or moved out and rented it before selling, the exemption only applies to the portion of the ownership period when it was your main residence. The CGT is calculated on the proportion of time you held it as an investment.

The 6-year absence rule provides some relief: if you move out and rent the property, you can continue to treat it as your main residence for up to six years provided you don't nominate another property as your main residence during that time.

If you've ever rented any part of your home, operated a home-based business, or owned the property through a company or trust, the position gets more complicated quickly.

The fix: talk to your accountant before you list, not after exchange. By the time the contract is signed, the options narrow.
