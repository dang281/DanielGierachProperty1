## Caption

For a seller aiming to launch a campaign in mid-April, eight weeks out is now. Here is what that preparation period actually looks like.

Weeks one and two: the appraisal and strategy conversation. Pricing, method of sale, and campaign structure. The agent should walk the property with you and give you a specific view on presentation, not a general one.

Weeks three and four: conveyancing. Your solicitor or conveyancer needs time to prepare the contract, run the title search, and put together the seller disclosure statement that Queensland law now requires before a buyer signs. Four to six weeks is realistic. Starting in week five is too late.

Weeks five and six: property preparation. Minor repairs, painting if needed, garden tidying, any cosmetic work that will be visible in photos. A stylist consultation, if you are using one, should happen before the photographer books.

Week seven: photography, floorplan, and copy. The campaign materials are produced. Premium photography is the single highest-return spend in a marketing campaign. It drives enquiry. Enquiry drives attendance. Attendance drives competition.

Week eight: campaign launch. Private database alert to registered buyers, then public listing.

Eight weeks sounds like a long time. In practice, with tradespeople to book, legal documents to prepare, and a household to organise around inspections, it fills quickly.

If you are planning an autumn campaign and have not started the conversation with your agent, the time is now.
