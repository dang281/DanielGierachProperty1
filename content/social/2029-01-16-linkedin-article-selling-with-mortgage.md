## Caption

The number most vendors focus on when they are thinking about selling is the sale price. The number that actually matters is the net proceeds after all costs are paid.

Net equity is not sale price minus loan balance.

By the time you add agent commission, conveyancing, marketing costs, and the mortgage discharge fee, selling costs typically run to 2.5 to 4 per cent of the sale price. On a $1.4 million inner east property, that is $35,000 to $56,000 before you receive a dollar.

If you are on a fixed rate, there is one more number to confirm before you launch a campaign. Fixed-rate break costs can run to several thousand dollars depending on how far through the fixed term you are and where rates have moved. Lenders calculate this differently, and the figure changes as rates shift. The time to ask your bank for a break cost estimate is before you commit to a campaign, not after you have accepted an offer.

Once you have accepted an offer, notify your lender immediately. They need time to prepare the discharge documentation for settlement, and delays on that side can create problems at settlement that have nothing to do with your buyer.
