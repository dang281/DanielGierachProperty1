## Caption

At open homes in the inner east, buyers almost always say the same things.

They want 4 bedrooms. They need a double garage. The kitchen has to be updated. Outdoor entertaining is non-negotiable.

Then the offers come in.

The properties attracting competition in Hawthorne, Norman Park, Bulimba and surrounds right now are not always the ones that tick every box. They are the ones that do one or two things brilliantly: the kitchen that makes people stop, the courtyard that feels like a different world, the street position that does not need explanation.

What I see most often is a buyer who says they will not compromise on space ending up in a slightly smaller home in a better position. Or a buyer who put parking as their top priority choosing to park on the street because everything else about the property fits their life.

Buyers compromise in private. They just do not talk about it at inspection.

If you are preparing to sell in the inner east, the question is not whether your home ticks every box. It is whether it excels at something. Most buyers can live with imperfect. What they cannot look past is mediocre in every direction.
