## Caption

Selling a tenanted property in Queensland is not simply a matter of giving notice and running the campaign. The rules around tenant access, open home scheduling, and settlement timing are specific, and getting them wrong costs either time or money.

The part that surprises most investors: every open home requires a written entry notice served at least 24 hours in advance. Not a courtesy call. Written notice, every time. For a campaign running four or five open homes, that is four or five separate notice obligations. If your property manager is not across the campaign schedule, this becomes a compliance issue.

The settlement timing is equally important. If your tenant is on a fixed-term lease that runs past the settlement date, the buyer takes over that lease. That is not a problem if the buyer is also an investor, but it eliminates owner-occupier buyers entirely. The decision about how to align the settlement date with the lease expiry should happen before the campaign launches, not after an offer comes in.
