## Caption

Mt Gravatt East in June is a suburb at its most legible from the perspective of a family buyer who is operating on a school-year timeline. The decisions made now about where to purchase will determine which school a child attends in the following year, and the buyers in Mt Gravatt East at this time of year tend to be working against exactly that timeline.

The suburb's value relative to Coorparoo and Holland Park has been a consistent feature of the market rather than a temporary anomaly. The extra distance from the CBD is real, but for households where the Garden City precinct or the Griffith University campus is the primary daily destination rather than the CBD, the traditional inner-city premium is less relevant. Those buyers are making a geographic calculation that is different from the standard inner-east framework.

The established tree canopy on the older streets of Mt Gravatt East is a quality that is genuinely difficult to replicate in newer precincts, regardless of budget. Streets where established trees meet overhead and filter winter sun onto the footpath are a kind of infrastructure that no developer can install in a new suburb, and buyers who have spent time in established leafy suburbs know the difference.

The end-of-financial-year period can bring some buyers to the market who are motivated by investment considerations alongside lifestyle ones. Mt Gravatt East's rental yield story, relative to its price, is worth having assessed before listing in June.
