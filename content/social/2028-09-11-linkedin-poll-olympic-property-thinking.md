## Caption

Four years before the Brisbane 2032 Olympics, the conversation about property in the inner east and surrounding areas has been running for a while. Some buyers moved specifically to be in the path of infrastructure improvements. Others bought before the announcement and have watched their suburbs become part of a national narrative about Brisbane's growth.

The question of how much the Games have actually influenced property thinking is one I find genuinely interesting. The infrastructure benefits are real and permanent. The event itself is temporary. And the property market has a habit of pricing in announcements well before delivery, which means some of the Olympic premium may already be embedded in current prices.

Has the Brisbane 2032 Games changed how you think about buying or holding property here?
