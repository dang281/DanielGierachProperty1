## Caption

A winter campaign in Brisbane's inner east runs differently from an autumn or spring one. The vendors who succeed in winter understand this rather than running the same campaign at a different time of year.

The buyer pool is smaller and more deliberate. Open home numbers will be lower. The buyers who attend are typically serious rather than browsing. The campaigns that work in winter lean into this by following up directly with every genuine enquiry rather than relying on volume to generate competition. In winter, one well-qualified buyer attending an open home is worth more than ten casual browsers in October.

Photography and presentation matter more in winter, not less. The first digital impression determines whether a buyer drives to the open home. A property that photographs well in winter conditions, with good natural light management and a warm, lived-in presentation, converts more online enquiries to physical inspections than one photographed poorly.

Pricing accuracy is non-negotiable in winter. The smaller buyer pool means there is no room to sit at a price and wait for someone to bridge the gap. Properties that are correctly positioned relative to recent comparable sales resolve. Those positioned in hope of achieving above-market do not, and they accumulate days on market that become a problem when spring arrives and buyers ask why the property has been listed so long.
