## Caption

Murarrie's access story is one that buyers often discover late, and then wonder why they did not look sooner.

The suburb sits at a practical intersection: Gateway Motorway to the north and south, East-West Arterial connecting it to the CBD, Cannon Hill train station within reach for those who prefer rail. The Murarrie Recreation Reserve provides green space that the density of the inner east often cannot match. These are infrastructure facts, not marketing claims.

The residential streets in Murarrie are separate from the industrial precinct. Post-war homes on full blocks sit in a residential pocket that many buyers from outside the suburb do not realise exists until they drive through it. The block sizes are real. The street character is quieter than neighbouring suburbs at comparable price points.

The buyer who lands on Murarrie usually arrives there through a process of elimination and then stays through conviction. Buyers who work north of Brisbane and commute regularly on the Gateway find the access practical in a way that most inner-east suburbs cannot offer. Buyers who need airport access regularly understand the same logic. Buyers who commute south on the Motorway find it works in both directions.

In the post-Olympic period, Murarrie's position on the transport and road network carries renewed relevance. The suburb is often a late addition to the inner-east buyer's shortlist and frequently becomes the first choice once the full access picture is mapped.

If you own in Murarrie, spring is when that buyer is looking.
