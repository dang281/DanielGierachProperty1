## Caption

Oxford Street is one of the best eating and drinking strips in inner Brisbane, and it belongs entirely to Bulimba. The restaurants, wine bars, and weekend foot traffic on Oxford Street create a suburb atmosphere that buyers pay a meaningful premium for, and the market reflects it consistently.

The Bulimba Ferry crossing to Teneriffe gives residents a direct connection to the inner north without touching a road. That is a rare amenity. Bulimba State School services the local families, and the suburb draws owner-occupiers who want to be within the inner east but also want genuine lifestyle infrastructure on their doorstep.

Character homes are the dominant stock type. Well-presented Queenslanders, particularly those with outdoor entertaining on the Oxford Street side of the suburb, attract the strongest competition. The closer to the river, the more the price conversation changes. River access on this stretch is some of the most valuable residential land in the inner east.

What takes longer here is the home that doesn't match the suburb's expectations. Buyers coming to Bulimba have usually done significant research. They know the streets, they know the recent sales, and they know what condition means.
