## Caption

Not everything that sells goes online. In the inner east, a meaningful portion of transactions happen before a property ever reaches realestate.com.au, and in 2032 that pattern has been more pronounced than usual.

There are a few reasons for this. Some sellers in an Olympic year do not want the disruption of a four-week campaign with open homes every Saturday. They would prefer a quieter process: a small number of pre-qualified buyers, a short due diligence period, and a clean contract. They trade a degree of market exposure for convenience and privacy.

From a buyer's perspective, being in the off-market pool means less competition and, often, more time to consider a decision without the artificial pressure of an auction countdown. The trade-off is that you do not always know what else might have been available if the property had gone to full market.

What makes off-market work is agent relationships. A buyer who is known to an agent, whose finance position is understood, and whose brief is clear enough to match a seller's property without a formal campaign is the buyer who gets the call. That requires consistent communication over time, not just registering on a website.
