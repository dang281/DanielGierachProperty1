## Caption

Coorparoo State School is one of the most sought-after primary catchments in Brisbane's inner south. That fact sits at the centre of Coorparoo's property market and explains a significant portion of the buyer competition in the elevated pockets to the north and west of Old Cleveland Road. Families who have done their research on Brisbane schools arrive at the inner south already knowing Coorparoo's name, and they arrive with a clear boundary in mind.

The pre-war housing stock in those elevated pockets is the character element. Timber homes with wide verandahs, blocks that give families room, streets that have retained their original form while the rest of the suburb has grown. The Old Cleveland Road café strip provides the day-to-day amenity. Coorparoo Station on the Cleveland line gives the suburb a CBD connection that not every inner east address can match.

Garden City to the south is useful without being the defining feature. The defining feature is the combination: proximity to the CBD, train access, a premium primary school catchment, and pre-war housing stock that does not get built anymore. That combination has supported Coorparoo through every market cycle in the past two decades.

Long-term performance here is structural, not cyclical. Owners who have held for five or more years understand this. The buyers who want Coorparoo in 2031 understand it too.
