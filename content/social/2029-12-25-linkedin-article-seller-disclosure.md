## Caption

Seller disclosure obligations in Queensland changed significantly when the Property Law Act 2023 came into effect. Under the current framework, vendors are required to provide a completed Seller Disclosure Statement to the buyer before any contract is entered into. This is not a technicality. It is a legal requirement with real consequences if it is not handled correctly.

The risk for vendors who do not comply is substantial. If a disclosure statement is not provided in the right form or at the right time, the buyer has the right to terminate the contract at any point before settlement. That is a significant exposure at any stage of a transaction, but particularly damaging if it surfaces after the vendor has already exchanged and started planning their next move.

The disclosure statement itself covers a range of matters including title issues, body corporate information where applicable, and certain known defects or matters affecting the property. Vendors do not need to speculate about future issues, but they do need to be accurate about what they know.

Getting this right is not difficult when you understand the process, but it does require proper preparation before marketing begins, not the day before exchange. I have written a full guide to seller disclosure obligations in Queensland at the link below.
