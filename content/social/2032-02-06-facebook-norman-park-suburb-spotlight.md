## Caption

Norman Park is one of those suburbs where the buyer pays for the address as much as the property. The streets running south from the riverline, particularly the elevated sections of Bennetts Road and the blocks above Old Cleveland Road near the Norman Park State School boundary, hold a loyal owner-occupier base that rarely sells. When something comes to market here, buyers who have been watching the suburb pay attention quickly.

The school catchment is a significant factor. Norman Park State School draws consistent demand from families, and the combination of catchment access and the suburb's character housing stock produces a buyer profile that is less price-sensitive than comparable markets further from the river. The Coorparoo Secondary College catchment also overlaps into parts of Norman Park, which adds a second layer of school-zone competition.

The suburb's retail amenity on Old Cleveland Road is modest, but proximity to Camp Hill's Stones Corner strip and the quick drive to the Norman Park train station keeps it well connected. Buyers who have looked at Coorparoo and found the price point tighter than expected will often shift their attention here.

In autumn 2032, well-presented character homes in Norman Park are meeting a buyer pool that has already done their research. That preparation, on both sides of the transaction, tends to produce clean campaigns.
