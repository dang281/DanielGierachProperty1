## Caption

Camp Hill has earned its reputation as one of the most consistently competitive markets in the inner east. The combination of Stones Corner on its northern edge, a strong community identity along Martha Street and the surrounding ridge streets, and a housing stock that leans heavily into elevated post-war and Queenslander character has made it one of the most recognisable addresses in this part of Brisbane.

The Coorparoo Secondary College catchment runs through parts of Camp Hill, and buyers who understand the school zone value have consistently placed a trackable premium on properties that fall inside it. That catchment-aware buyer pool is more disciplined and more motivated than the broader market, which is why Camp Hill auctions under good campaign conditions tend to produce strong clearance rates.

The blocks on the elevated ridge looking west toward the CBD have been among the most closely watched in the suburb. Street appeal here is about views and Queenslander rooflines in equal parts, and buyers who arrive at open homes in Camp Hill have usually already driven the street.

In autumn 2032, Camp Hill is meeting a serious buyer. The open home traffic here is less speculative than in some parts of the inner east because the suburb has a clear identity that self-selects the audience. For well-presented homes arriving in February and March, the conditions are as strong as they have been in this cycle.
