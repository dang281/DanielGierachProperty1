## Caption

Seven Hills sits between Coorparoo and Camp Hill, and its elevated position is one of the first things buyers mention when they describe why they targeted this suburb specifically.

The outlook from many streets is a genuine drawcard. City views at this price point, in a suburb this close to the inner east amenity belt, attract a buyer who has done the comparison work and found that the combination is not easily replicated. You can pay more for a similar view closer to the river, or you can find a flatter suburb at a lower price point, but replicating the elevation, outlook, and proximity to established amenity in one package is harder than it looks on a map.

The suburb has a quiet, leafy residential character that holds onto its appeal long after buyers move in. Proximity to Camp Hill Village and the shops along Old Cleveland Road means daily convenience is covered without requiring the suburb itself to carry a commercial function. Residents are close to good cafes, local shops, and the established infrastructure of Camp Hill without paying Camp Hill prices.

The Mount Gravatt State High School catchment adds a layer of family appeal that strengthens the buyer pool further. Families who want the school and are looking for relative value compared to the immediate Camp Hill and Coorparoo catchment areas find Seven Hills sits in a genuinely useful position.
