## Caption

Bulimba's property market operates at a different register from most Brisbane suburbs. The combination of Oxford Street's village atmosphere, the Brisbane River foreshore, the Bulimba State School catchment and a level of community identity that has built over decades creates a suburb that buyers approach with serious intent. People who want to be in Bulimba understand that they need to be prepared to compete for the properties they want.

The Oxford Street strip is not simply a convenience feature; it is a social infrastructure that residents build their lifestyle around. The quality of independent cafes, restaurants and specialty retailers along that corridor shapes how people spend their weekends and how they experience the suburb day-to-day. Buyers who have lived without it and then moved to Bulimba consistently cite it as transformative to their daily life.

Waterfront properties along the river represent the suburb's premium tier, but even non-waterfront homes benefit from the general quality and demand that defines the market. The CityCat ferry provides a genuine and pleasant commute option to the CBD and South Bank, and cycling infrastructure along the river corridor is practical for those willing to use it.

Current medians in Bulimba reflect the full premium that the suburb commands. Long-term owners have typically experienced strong capital growth, and properties in good condition tend to achieve results at or above comparable inner suburbs. Well-presented character homes remain the standout performer.
