## Caption

Coorparoo's Old Cleveland Road café strip is the kind of walkable, daily-use amenity that buyers will pay a premium for, and the suburb's school catchment options are among the strongest in Brisbane's inner east. Coorparoo State School, Villanova College, and Loreto College all sit within or very close to the suburb boundary, which means the family buyer pool here is both deep and competitive. When a well-positioned house hits the market in a tight street, it is not unusual for buyers to have been watching that specific block for months.

The housing stock is genuinely varied, from character Queenslanders on elevated blocks to post-war homes with renovation scope, alongside a solid apartment and townhouse market that attracts downsizers and professional buyers. That variety keeps demand broad across price points, which is one of the reasons Coorparoo has maintained consistency through different market conditions.

Sellers in Coorparoo benefit from a buyer pool that is highly informed and motivated. These are not casual enquirers. Families in particular tend to have done their research on school catchments, have a clear timeline, and are ready to act when the right property appears.

If you own in Coorparoo and are weighing up whether now is the right time to sell, a current appraisal will give you a clear picture.
