## Caption

A seller will often accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one.

Most buyers find this counterintuitive until it is explained. An unconditional offer removes every exit clause: no finance condition, no building and pest condition, no subject-to-sale condition. Once the seller accepts, the contract is binding immediately. For a vendor who has made plans around a certain settlement date, or who has had contracts fall over before, that certainty is worth money.

In Queensland, buyers can only make an unconditional offer responsibly when two things are true. Finance is confirmed: not just pre-approval, but a full unconditional letter from the lender based on the specific property. And the building and pest inspection has been completed before the offer is made, not after.

Skipping either step and making an unconditional offer anyway is how buyers get into serious trouble. If you cannot complete the purchase, you forfeit the deposit and may face further legal action from the vendor. There is no cooling-off period for auction purchases. For private treaty, the standard cooling-off period does not apply once it has been waived.

Understanding how unconditional offers work, and when they make strategic sense, gives buyers a meaningful advantage when other bidders are conditional.
