## Caption

Coorparoo sits 4km from the Brisbane CBD with direct train access via Coorparoo station, and that combination of proximity and connectivity is what puts it on the list for buyers who want to be close to the city without paying the prices that the suburbs immediately inside the inner ring command.

Coorparoo Square has added a genuine dining and retail precinct to a suburb that was already well served commercially along Old Cleveland Road. The depth of food, shopping, and services available within the suburb means most daily needs are met without leaving the area, which is the kind of amenity quality that buyers at this price point are actively comparing across suburbs.

The school catchments here are a consistent driver of family demand. Coorparoo State School and Coorparoo Secondary College both sit within the suburb, meaning families can make a long-term decision about where to be without needing to factor in a catchment change at any stage of their children's schooling. For families weighing up their options across the inner east, that continuity matters.

The housing stock in Coorparoo is more diverse than most suburbs in this part of Brisbane. Heritage Queenslanders sit alongside mid-century homes, modern townhouses, and apartment buildings. That diversity means the suburb attracts a broader buyer profile than more architecturally homogeneous suburbs. Young professionals, families, downsizers, and investors all find something in Coorparoo that suits their brief.
