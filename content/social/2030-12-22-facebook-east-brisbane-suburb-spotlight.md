## Caption

East Brisbane's proximity to the CBD makes it a different property proposition to most of the inner east. At under 3 kilometres from the city, the suburb sits in the commuting sweet spot where buyers who need regular CBD access prioritise it over the further-out inner east suburbs, even when the price-per-square-metre calculation goes against them.

The Kangaroo Point Bridge, which has changed the active transport connection between East Brisbane, Kangaroo Point, and the South Bank precinct, has made the riverside and near-riverside streets of East Brisbane more walkable and cyclable than they were in the pre-bridge era. That infrastructure improvement has been reflected in sustained buyer interest for properties within the bridge's catchment radius.

Churchie on Oaklands Parade makes East Brisbane a natural residential zone for families with boys enrolled there. The walking proximity from the residential streets to the school gates is a practical lifestyle factor that parents value, particularly during busy school week mornings.

The character home streets in East Brisbane, particularly in the elevated sections toward the Greenslopes boundary, are the suburb's most consistent performers. Properties in the flat sections close to the main roads and the unit precincts serve a different market.

December in East Brisbane brings fewer competitors to market, which can work in a motivated vendor's favour heading into the new year.
