## Caption

The buyers who will have the best spring are the ones doing the work right now, in winter, when nothing obvious is happening.

What that looks like in practice: finance pre-approval is current and the lender has confirmed borrowing capacity based on current income and rates. The brief is specific, not a wishlist spanning four suburbs and two property types. The building inspector has been identified and is on call. The solicitor has been appointed and the buyer understands the contract process before they need to use it.

These buyers are also attending open homes consistently, even for properties they don't intend to buy. Not because they are wasting Saturdays, but because they are calibrating. They know what $1.8 million looks like in Hawthorne versus Coorparoo. They know which streets they won't compromise on and which ones they thought they wanted but discovered they don't. They have removed the variables that slow down decision-making.

When spring comes and a property they want hits the market, this buyer can make an offer within forty-eight hours. They don't need a weekend to "think about it." They've already thought about it. That speed, combined with their ability to move without conditions if needed, puts them in a category most other buyers in the same market cannot match.

The spring market in the inner east will have genuine competition. The buyers who thrive in it are building that capability right now.
