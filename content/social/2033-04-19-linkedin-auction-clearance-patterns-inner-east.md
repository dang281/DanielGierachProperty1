## Caption

Auction clearance rates are reported weekly and quoted constantly, but they are frequently misread.

The headline number tells you what percentage of properties that went to auction sold on the day. It does not tell you how many of those sales happened under the hammer versus immediately after. It does not tell you how many properties were withdrawn before auction because the vendor accepted a pre-auction offer. And it does not tell you the ratio of listed-to-sold across all methods, only those taken to auction.

In the inner east, the relevant numbers to watch are not the Brisbane-wide figures. They are the suburb-level data for Hawthorne, Bulimba, Morningside, Camp Hill, and Coorparoo specifically. A 75% clearance rate across Brisbane can mask a 55% rate in one suburb and a 90% rate in another. These are different markets requiring different strategies.

What the autumn clearance data from the inner east actually shows most years is a tight and competitive market through April, a small softening in May as some buyers pause ahead of the financial year end, and then a clear floor established in June as the winter listing volume drops and serious buyers become a larger share of a smaller pool. The vendors who understand this cycle prepare differently to those who just look at the headline number and decide spring must be better.

If you are weighing up method of sale or campaign timing, the clearance rate conversation needs to go deeper than the weekly report.
