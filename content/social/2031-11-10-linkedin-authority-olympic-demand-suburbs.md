## Caption

With the Brisbane 2032 Games less than 12 months away, the Olympic conversation has moved from speculation to real buyer behaviour. Here is what I am actually observing at open homes.

Interstate buyers who have been watching Brisbane for years are now treating the Olympic timeline as a decision trigger. They have done the research. They know the suburbs they want. They are not here for a browse. They are here because the window they have been waiting for feels like it is closing.

The suburbs drawing the most interstate attention are specific. Bulimba and Hawthorne are attracting buyers who want the river lifestyle and have done their research on what it costs. Balmoral has a similar profile. Norman Park and Camp Hill are appearing on the radar of families moving for the school catchments, particularly those relocating from Sydney where equivalent catchment access costs considerably more.

The buyers from these interstate enquiries are generally well-financed. They have often been watching comparable sales for 12 to 18 months before they book a flight. When they attend an inspection, they are often ready to make a move within the same trip.

For local vendors, this matters. The buyer pool for a well-presented inner east property in Q4 2031 is not limited to local Brisbane buyers. It includes a meaningful segment of interstate buyers who have made a deliberate decision to be in this market now, ahead of what they see as the peak Olympic attention window.

That does not change the fundamentals of a good campaign. Price accurately, prepare properly, present well. But it does mean the audience for that campaign is broader than it was two years ago.
