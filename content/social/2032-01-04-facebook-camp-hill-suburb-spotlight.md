## Caption

Camp Hill has held a consistent position in the inner east market for years: school catchments that buyers plan around, character streetscapes that photograph well, and a price point that attracts owner-occupiers with long time horizons.

The Camp Hill State School catchment draws families from further afield who want to be inside the zone before enrolment closes. Streets like Doris Street, Riding Road, and the elevated sections above Wellington Road offer some of the better elevated positions in the inner east, with city skyline views on clear blocks. The Old Cleveland Road spine gives residents quick access to both the CBD and Coorparoo's retail strip.

The housing stock is predominantly Queenslander and post-war character homes, with a strong renovation culture. Buyers here are often spending a significant part of their budget on the purchase itself, planning to stage improvements over five to ten years. That means they are looking at bones: allotment size, aspect, flood overlay, and structural condition.

In 2032, Camp Hill is attracting buyers who want inner east character and school access without the waterfront premium of Bulimba or Hawthorne. That is a sizeable and motivated buyer pool.

Vendors who take time to prepare, declutter, and address minor defects before listing consistently see stronger results here than those who rush to market. The suburb rewards effort.
