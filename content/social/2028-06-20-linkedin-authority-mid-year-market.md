## Caption

Six months into the year is a useful point to separate the signal from the noise. There has been no shortage of commentary about where the market is headed. Most of it is forecast. Not much of it is observation.

The measures that actually tell you something about the state of the inner east market are straightforward. Days on market tells you whether sellers and buyers are finding each other at a pace that reflects strong demand or a market that is thinking harder before committing. Auction clearance rates tell you whether properties are selling at the price vendors expected or whether expectations are being adjusted at the fall of the hammer. The ratio of properties that sell at first open home versus those that go to a second week is one of the cleaner measures of whether week-one demand is meeting week-one pricing.

In offer rounds, the gap between the first offer and the final accepted price tells you whether buyers are testing the market or coming in hard. In a strong market, buyers come in close to their ceiling early because they know competition is real. In a softer market, first offers carry more room, and vendors who understand this negotiate accordingly rather than holding on principle.

The mid-year picture in the inner east from where I sit: properties that are well-prepared and honestly priced are still finding buyers with genuine intent, and the time from list to contract on those properties has not blown out. Properties that came to market hoping the market would meet them are taking longer, sometimes much longer.

That gap between well-positioned and not is the story of the first half. danielgierach.com
