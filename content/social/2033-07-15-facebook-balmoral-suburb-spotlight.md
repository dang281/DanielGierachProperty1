## Caption

Balmoral is the suburb in Brisbane's inner east where character housing stock commands a premium that is detached from the suburb's price-per-square-metre story. A well-maintained pre-war Queenslander on a standard lot in Balmoral attracts a buyer who is not comparing it to a contemporary build at the same price. They are comparing it to the other character homes they have inspected over the past several months, and they know what they want.

The suburb sits along the northern bank of the Brisbane River with access to the Balmoral Esplanade and Lores Bonney Park, one of the river corridor's more quietly excellent public spaces. The combination of water access, elevated ridge streets with city and river views, and the close proximity to the Hawthorne dining strip and Norman Park precinct gives Balmoral a lifestyle completeness that buyers who have identified the suburb are willing to pay for.

The housing stock ranges from modest post-war cottages to significant character homes on elevated blocks. The buyer who pursues the character housing in particular tends to have been searching for some time and arrives with a specific knowledge of the market and of the heritage overlay implications that apply to street-facing changes.

Balmoral is not a suburb with a large listing pool at any time of year. When the right property appears, the buyer pool for it tends to be concentrated and motivated.
