## Caption

Bulimba's Oxford Street is one of the most active café and restaurant strips in the inner east, and it sets the tone for a suburb that has positioned itself as a lifestyle destination as much as a residential address. That positioning shows up in both the buyer profile and the price premium the suburb commands relative to nearby postcodes.

The residential streets behind Oxford Street, particularly in the triangle between Amelia Street, Moray Street, and the river, are where the suburb's strongest auction results have historically come from. River glimpses and river access from the ferry terminal at the end of Oxford Street are factors that generate genuine buyer competition at the prestige end of the market.

Bulimba State School is well-regarded, and the suburb's proximity to Churchie (Anglican Church Grammar School) in East Brisbane makes it a natural choice for families with private secondary school options in mind.

The suburb benefits from the Bulimba ferry terminal, offering an alternative commute route to the CBD that buyers from outside Brisbane find unexpectedly practical. In a city where road congestion is a daily reality, river access to the CBD is a lifestyle premium that does not appear in the address but shows up in the price.

For homeowners considering a Bulimba campaign, the spring buyer pool through October and November is typically deep and active.
