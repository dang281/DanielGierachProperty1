## Caption

Most vendors ask the same question before they list: is now a good time to sell?

The honest answer is that timing the market perfectly is almost impossible. A more useful question is: given your personal circumstances and what is actually happening in your suburb right now, does selling now make sense?

The broader headlines rarely help. National property figures obscure enormous local variation. Your street can be outperforming the suburb average while other parts of Brisbane sit flat. The right frame for timing is always local and specific.

Here is the thing the data keeps confirming: preparation almost always matters more than timing. In Brisbane's inner east, the gap between a well-prepared campaign and a rushed or poorly executed one regularly amounts to $50,000 to $150,000 at a comparable price point. The month you list has far less influence on your result than the four weeks of work you do before the photographer arrives.

Spring gets all the attention. But in many inner Brisbane suburbs, the strongest results come from campaigns that launch in late summer and early autumn, when genuine buyer demand is still strong but the volume of competing listings has not yet climbed. Low inventory in a high-demand environment consistently produces strong prices.

If you are upsizing, downsizing, or managing a change in circumstances, the right time to sell is usually when those circumstances call for it. Waiting indefinitely for a perfect market while your situation demands action rarely produces a better net outcome.
