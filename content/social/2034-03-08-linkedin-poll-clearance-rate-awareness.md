## Caption

Brisbane's weekly clearance rate is one of the most cited property statistics and one of the least understood. It tells you the proportion of properties that sold at auction or by auction campaign in a given week. It does not tell you about private treaty results, expression-of-interest outcomes, or the properties that were passed in and subsequently sold.

In the inner east, where auction is not the dominant sale method for all property types, the clearance rate as a standalone figure can be misleading. A week with a low clearance rate does not necessarily mean the market is soft. It can mean a higher proportion of high-value or unique properties went to auction that week, properties that statistically pass in more often and are then sold in the days following.

The more useful question is not what the clearance rate is, but whether the properties that matter to your specific decision are selling and at what pace. Days on market, the ratio of sale price to original price guide, and the depth of the buyer pool at opens are more specific signals for inner east activity than a single weekly percentage.

How closely do you follow Brisbane's weekly clearance rate?
