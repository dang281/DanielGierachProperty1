## Caption

When an offer arrives on your property, the instinct is to look at the price. That is the wrong place to start.

Price is one variable. Conditions, settlement date, and the reliability of the buyer completing are the others. A higher conditional offer and a lower unconditional offer are not directly comparable on price alone. The conditional offer introduces a 14 to 21 day window during which the deal can unwind through finance or building and pest outcomes. The unconditional offer eliminates that risk entirely.

For most inner east vendors, the question is not which offer is higher. It is which offer is most likely to reach settlement. A lower unconditional offer that settles reliably is often a better outcome than a higher conditional offer that produces three weeks of uncertainty and then falls over.

The other variable that vendors underweight is settlement flexibility. A buyer who needs a 60-day settlement and another who can settle in 30 days represent materially different outcomes if you have a dependent purchase, a lease obligation, or a specific financial date in mind.

Rejecting a low offer outright without counter-offering is the most common vendor mistake I see. It closes the negotiation entirely and removes the possibility of discovering what the buyer's real ceiling is.
