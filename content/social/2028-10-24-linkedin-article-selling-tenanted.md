## Caption

Selling a tenanted property in Queensland requires 24 hours written notice for every inspection. Every single one. Not a general notice at the start of the campaign that covers all subsequent appointments.

That requirement adds a layer of coordination to a tenanted campaign that owner-occupier sales do not have. It also means open homes are typically by appointment rather than public open for inspection, which changes the buyer experience and can affect the depth of the offer pool.

The more significant issue is the lease itself. If your tenant is on a fixed-term lease that extends past the proposed settlement date, you cannot offer vacant possession to a buyer without the tenant's agreement. You are selling a tenanted investment property, not a property with vacant possession. Those are different products with different buyer pools, and they often achieve different prices.

Timing a campaign to end around the lease expiry, or negotiating an early surrender with your tenant before launching, requires planning well in advance of the campaign. Investors who want to achieve vacant possession and have not confirmed the lease position before appointing an agent typically find out too late.

The full article covers notice requirements, what you can and cannot do with a tenant in place, and how to structure the timing of a tenanted sale to protect your result.
