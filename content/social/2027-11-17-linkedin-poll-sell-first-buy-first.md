## Caption

One of the most common questions I hear from owner-occupiers in Brisbane's inner east is whether to sell their existing home before buying the next one, or to buy first and then sell.

Both approaches carry risk. Selling first means you know your exact budget but you may end up in bridging accommodation if you cannot find the right property quickly. Buying first removes that pressure but exposes you to the risk of not achieving your price on the sale, which affects borrowing capacity and deposit.

The right answer depends on market conditions, your financial position, and your personal tolerance for uncertainty. In a rising market, buying first is more often correct. In a softening market, selling first protects you.

What would you do?

## Poll options
