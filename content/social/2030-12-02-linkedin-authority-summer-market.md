## Caption

Brisbane summer property is quieter than spring, but it is not inactive. Understanding the difference matters for both buyers and vendors planning their next move.

Stock supply drops significantly from mid-December through January. Most vendors with properties not yet under contract pause their campaigns over the Christmas period or delay their launch until February. This contraction in supply has an effect on the buyers who are still active: they face less competition for properties that remain on the market, but they also have less to choose from.

For buyers who have been searching since August and have not yet found the right property, the summer period can be a useful recalibration. Attending any opens that do run over December and January gives a clearer picture of what genuine market value looks like without the spring buyer competition compressing time to decide.

For vendors who are considering a January campaign, there is a window between roughly January 15 and February 15 where a property can enter the market ahead of the main autumn supply. Buyer demand in that window is often underestimated. The buyers who are active in mid-January are serious, frequently in a position to act quickly, and are not competing with the field that will appear in March.

The standard advice to "wait for spring or autumn" is right for most properties and most vendors. But for a motivated vendor with a well-presented property, the summer and early autumn window is worth understanding as an option rather than dismissing as an off-season liability.
