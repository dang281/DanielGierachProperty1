## Caption

I want to be straightforward about what Brisbane's inner east property market looks like between Christmas and Australia Day.

It is quiet. Most agents take annual leave. Open home volumes drop substantially. The buyers who were competitive through October and November are largely settled or resting. Vendors who were considering a summer listing have generally either committed or deferred to February.

But the buyers who are active in late December and January are worth understanding, because they are different from the autumn pool.

The first group are professionals who have accepted a job offer and need to be housed before February. This is a real category in Brisbane's inner east, particularly with the volume of interstate movement the city sees in January. These buyers are motivated by deadline, not desire. They are decisive and they do not need convincing.

The second group are upsizers who made the decision over Christmas. A summer break at home, a conversation with a partner, a look at the space and a reckoning with what the family needs in the next five years. These buyers enter January with clarity they did not have in November.

The third group are buyers who have been watching the market for months and have decided they are not going to miss the start of another autumn season. They are pre-approved, they know the streets they want, and they are ready.

A property listed in January does not need a large buyer pool. It needs the right one. If you are considering a Q1 2033 campaign, early January can be worth a conversation before the February rush starts.
