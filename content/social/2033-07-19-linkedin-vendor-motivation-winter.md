## Caption

The vendors who list in winter are not, for the most part, people who miscalculated the calendar. They are people whose circumstances have created a decision point, and that decision point happens to fall in July or August.

The most common reasons are predictable. A job relocation. A separation or estate settlement. An upgrade purchase that requires selling the current property to proceed. A decision to downsize that has finally reached the point of action after months of consideration. In most of these cases, the vendor is not choosing between winter and spring. They are choosing between July and a delay that may not serve their actual interests.

What is less discussed is that some vendors make a deliberate choice to come to market in winter after weighing the conditions carefully. A realistic assessment of the listing competition in their suburb in July versus October. A buyer pool they understand, perhaps from having sold in the area before or having watched it closely. A property with characteristics, a north-facing aspect, a school catchment, a specific street position, that attract buyers regardless of the time of year.

The framing that winter selling is a concession to circumstance does not hold up across all of the inner east. In some streets and some property types, the vendor who lists in July is making a deliberate tactical decision, not a reluctant one.
