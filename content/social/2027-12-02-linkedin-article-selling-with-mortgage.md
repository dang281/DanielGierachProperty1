## Caption

Most vendors who contact me still have a mortgage on the property they are selling. The single most common misconception is that net proceeds equals sale price minus loan balance. It does not.

The actual calculation includes several costs that most vendors have not fully factored. Agent commission, typically between 2 and 2.5 percent of the sale price in the inner east. Conveyancing fees. Marketing budget. And the mortgage discharge fee, the fee the bank charges to release the property from the mortgage, which can range from a few hundred dollars to over a thousand depending on the lender and loan structure.

If the property is on a fixed-rate loan, there is a further calculation required: the break cost. Fixed-rate break costs apply when a mortgage is discharged before the fixed term expires. On a loan of $800,000 with two years remaining on a fixed term, the break cost can be significant, sometimes tens of thousands of dollars. It is not always disclosed upfront and vendors sometimes find out at settlement.

The practical step is to contact your lender before you list and ask for a payout figure. That figure should include the discharge fee and any applicable break cost. Then calculate your true net proceeds before you commit to your next move.
