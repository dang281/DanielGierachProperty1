## Caption

The comparable sales conversation is the one most vendors want to skip.

I understand why. The number that comes out of a genuinely rigorous comparable sales analysis is sometimes lower than what a vendor hoped for. It can feel like the agent is working against you. It is not. It is the agent doing their actual job.

Here is how comparable sales work in practice for a house in the inner east.

A genuinely comparable sale is recent (within the past 90 days ideally, 120 at most), in the same or a directly comparable street or pocket of the suburb, and similar in land size, bedroom count, and condition. It is not the best result in the suburb over the past two years. It is not the house three streets away that sold for a record because a developer wanted the block. It is properties that the buyer of your home will also be considering.

When comparable sales are assembled honestly and presented clearly, the price that comes out of the analysis is the price the market will support. Launching above that number does not change the market. It just accumulates days on market while the vendor waits to find out what the agents already knew at appraisal.

The vendors who come back to me after a campaign with a suboptimal result are almost always the ones who launched too high because they chose the agent who told them what they wanted to hear, not what the market would support.
