## Caption

Mt Gravatt heading into the final days of 2030 is a suburb where the summer pause creates a useful opportunity for homeowners planning a 2031 sale. The noise of the spring market has faded. The buyer pool is forming its 2031 brief over the summer break. And the properties that launch in late January or early February ahead of the main autumn supply typically face their first two weekends with limited competition.

The elevated streets on Mt Gravatt's ridge have produced some of the suburb's strongest 2030 results. Bunny Street and the streets running east from Hammett Road toward the ridge deliver the views across the southern Brisbane corridor that buyers specifically target when they decide that Mt Gravatt is the suburb for them. Those buyers have done their research. They know the streets. They know which positions have the views and which do not.

The Westfield Garden City centre continues to function as one of the suburb's strongest practical assets. For residents approaching retirement or with young children, full-service retail, medical, and food access within a five-minute drive of the residential streets is a genuine quality-of-life factor that registers in open home feedback.

For Mt Gravatt homeowners considering a January or February launch, the window to have the preparation conversation is now.
