## Caption

Most buyers sign the contract first and call their solicitor second. That sequence is backwards, and it is one of the most common process mistakes I see in the inner east.

The cooling-off period in Queensland gives you five business days to exit a signed contract for a fee of 0.25% of the purchase price. But if there is something in the contract that should have been negotiated before signing, that window is not always enough time to unwind it. Special conditions, inclusions and exclusions, settlement dates, and finance clause deadlines are all easier to change before you sign than after.

The question buyers often ask is whether a solicitor or a conveyancer is the right choice for a standard residential purchase. Both can handle the legal transfer of property in Queensland. The difference is in scope. A conveyancer handles the title transfer and the mechanics of settlement. A solicitor can advise on the contract itself, negotiate special conditions, and handle disputes if they arise. For a straightforward purchase of an established home, a licensed conveyancer is usually sufficient. For anything with complexity, including tenanted properties, off-the-plan, or development potential, a solicitor is the better choice.

The cost difference between the two is rarely significant relative to the purchase price. The difference in outcome when something goes wrong can be.
