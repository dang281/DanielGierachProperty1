## Caption

Most sellers sign the Form 6 agency agreement without reading it. This is the document that governs the entire relationship between the seller and the agent, and it is worth understanding before you sign.

The Form 6 is not a formality. It sets the commission structure, including when it becomes payable and on what basis. It defines the campaign scope, the advertising spend, and who is responsible for what costs. It establishes the exclusivity period, which determines whether you can engage other agents during the campaign and under what conditions. It sets the term of the agreement, which determines how long you are bound to the agent if the property does not sell.

These are not small details. The commission structure alone can represent tens of thousands of dollars. The exclusivity period has direct consequences if the relationship with the agent is not working and you want to make a change. The term of the agreement affects your flexibility across the entire campaign.

Under the Property Occupations Act 2014 (Qld), sellers have a five-business-day cooling-off period after signing the Form 6. This right is not widely known. It exists to give sellers time to seek independent advice on the agreement they have just signed. Using it does not mean the relationship with the agent has broken down. It simply means the seller is treating the Form 6 with the same care they would apply to any significant contract.

The right time to ask questions about a Form 6 is before you sign it, not after the campaign is underway.
