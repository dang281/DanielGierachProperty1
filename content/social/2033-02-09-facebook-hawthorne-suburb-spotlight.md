## Caption

Hawthorne is where the Oxford Street lifestyle and the Hawthorne ferry terminal combine with one of Brisbane's most recognisable housing types: the renovated inner east Queenslander. The suburb has a clarity of identity that makes it easy for buyers to fall in love with and hard for vendors to overprice without consequence.

The Hawthorne ferry connects the suburb to the broader CityCat network, and for residents who commute to the CBD or the University of Queensland, the river access is not just amenity. It is a genuine transport option. The ferry terminal draws buyers who want the inner-city lifestyle without the inner-city density, and the combination of walkable retail and river access is hard to find at any price point in Brisbane. ⚠️ VERIFY

The renovated Queenslanders that define Hawthorne's streetscape command a premium that reflects both the quality of the housing stock and the character of the suburb. Buyers in this market have typically inspected dozens of properties and have a sophisticated view of what represents value. Presentation is expected to be at a high standard. Anything less does not fail quietly; it affects the result visibly.

For vendors in autumn 2033, Hawthorne is a market where the right campaign, executed at the right standard, draws the kind of buyer competition that the suburb's reputation supports. The preparation conversation is the starting point.
