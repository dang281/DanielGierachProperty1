## Caption

Q2 2033 in Brisbane's inner east has been a useful market to observe. Not because it surprised anyone who watches this area closely, but because the patterns that showed up confirmed a few things worth carrying into winter and spring.

Autumn performed. The well-prepared properties that came to market in April and May in Hawthorne, Bulimba, and Coorparoo cleared well. The buyer pool was qualified and motivated. The campaigns that had done the preparation work in March achieved results that reflected it.

The properties that sat were largely the ones that arrived underprepared or overpriced against current comparable sales evidence. That gap between vendor expectation and market reality is not a market problem. It is a pricing and preparation problem, and it showed up consistently across the quarter.

What the June data is showing is expected: transaction volumes are lower, open home numbers are down, and some vendors have chosen to hold for spring rather than campaign in winter. That creates the opportunity for vendors who do come to market now. The buyer pool is smaller but the listing competition is equally thin.

Going into July and August, the market is in a holding pattern that is familiar for this part of Brisbane. It is not stalled. It is loaded for spring. The vendors and buyers who understand that distinction and act accordingly will be the ones who remember Q3 2033 as a good quarter for them.
