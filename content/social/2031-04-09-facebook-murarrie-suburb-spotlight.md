## Caption

Murarrie is in the middle of becoming something. That transition is visible in the mix of housing on its streets: older industrial-adjacent stock sits alongside newer residential development, and the buyers who have been purchasing here over the past decade have largely been people who identified the suburb's trajectory before the wider market had priced it in.

The Gateway Motorway connection is the most practical feature of Murarrie's location. For buyers who need fast access to the airport, the port precinct, or the southern motorway corridors, Murarrie removes a problem that costs residents of Morningside and Cannon Hill time every morning. That infrastructure advantage is not glamorous, but it is consistent and reliable.

Entry price relative to Morningside is the conversation that comes up most often with buyers considering Murarrie. The price gap between the two suburbs reflects Morningside's more established residential identity and its closer walking connections to retail and transport. For buyers who are primarily focused on getting into a house in the inner east and who are less dependent on walkable amenity, Murarrie offers a foothold that Morningside no longer does at equivalent budget.

New development in the suburb is worth monitoring. As the residential population grows and the suburb's identity continues to consolidate, the amenity gap between Murarrie and its neighbours tends to close.
