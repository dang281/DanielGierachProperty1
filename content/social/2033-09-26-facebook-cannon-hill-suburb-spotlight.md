## Caption

Cannon Hill's spring market is grounded in a buyer proposition that is more practical than aspirational, and that is its strength. Buyers who arrive at Cannon Hill open homes in September and October have typically resolved the lifestyle versus utility trade-off in utility's favour. They want rail access to the CBD, proximity to a functioning retail precinct, a school catchment that covers their children's ages, and a price that does not require them to stretch their borrowing capacity beyond what they are comfortable carrying. Cannon Hill satisfies all four.

The Cannon Hill Kmart Plaza precinct is not a glamorous retail address, but it is one of the most functional shopping destinations in the inner east. Residents consistently describe the ability to handle the full range of household requirements within a five-minute drive as a quality-of-life advantage that they underestimated before moving to the suburb and value highly after. That functional amenity is part of what keeps Cannon Hill's buyer demand sustained across market conditions.

The Cleveland line train at Cannon Hill station connects residents to Central station in under 15 minutes during standard off-peak periods. For buyers who are making a household decision that includes a daily CBD commute, that direct rail access is a practical argument that Cannon Hill can make cleanly against comparable suburbs that require a bus transfer or longer journey.

Spring is when Cannon Hill properties attract the widest and best-qualified buyer pool of the year.
