## Caption

Cannon Hill has completed a transition that has been building for over a decade. It is no longer a suburb buyers settle for; it is a suburb they actively seek out. The Cross River Rail has accelerated that shift, with Cannon Hill Station now offering genuine, fast connectivity to the CBD. Buyers who previously looked only at suburbs with existing rail links have added Cannon Hill to their shortlist.

Cannon Hill Plaza and the Gateway Motorway access make practical daily life straightforward. Cannon Hill Anglican College adds a private education option that families increasingly factor into location decisions. Together, those infrastructure and amenity layers create a suburb that serves multiple buyer profiles simultaneously.

The housing stock has diversified too. Established homes and newer builds now sit alongside each other, which gives the market a price range broad enough to capture both first-home buyers stepping up and families looking for a longer-term address.

Mid-winter is a useful time to watch Cannon Hill. The buyers in this suburb are driven by commute and school logic, not by garden aesthetics or spring sentiment. That means winter demand holds up better here than in lifestyle-led suburbs. If you own in Cannon Hill and have been weighing up your timing, the winter window deserves consideration.
