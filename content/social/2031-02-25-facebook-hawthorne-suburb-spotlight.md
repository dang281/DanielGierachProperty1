## Caption

Hawthorne lists fewer properties in a given year than almost any comparable inner east suburb. That is not a coincidence. It is the defining characteristic of the market here, and it explains almost everything about how Hawthorne performs at auction.

The suburb sits between Bulimba to the north and Balmoral to the south, borrowing the best of both. The Oxford Street end of Hawthorne gives residents immediate access to the café strip and the Hawthorne Ferry Terminal without the density of Bulimba proper. The elevated streets behind, running up through character Queenslanders on blocks that rarely come up for subdivision, offer a residential quiet that the suburb guards carefully.

When a Hawthorne property does come to market, the competition is disproportionate to the volume. Buyers who have missed previous campaigns in the suburb, and there are always buyers in that position, arrive at open homes alert and prepared. They have often been watching for eighteen months or more. The result is that well-presented Hawthorne properties tend to perform at the upper end of vendor expectations, because the scarcity itself generates urgency.

For Hawthorne homeowners, the autumn window represents the clearest alignment between active buyer demand and the kind of considered selling process that a premium property deserves. Supply will not suddenly increase. The buyers who want Hawthorne will still be there in March.
