## Caption

Almost all spring-selling advice in Australian media is built around Melbourne and Sydney. It reflects the seasonal patterns of those markets, not Brisbane's. Applying it here is a reasonable way to pick the wrong window.

In Brisbane's inner east, autumn, late February through to May, is the most consistent and productive selling window. The combination of cooler weather that makes the city genuinely pleasant, high buyer activity from people who want to be settled before the financial year, and reduced competition from other listings creates conditions that repeatedly produce strong results. Inner east properties that campaign in autumn historically perform well on both price and days on market.

The spring argument for Brisbane tends to rest on the idea that warm weather brings out buyers. What it actually brings out is more listings. Increased supply in spring dilutes buyer competition rather than concentrating it. The spring window is not bad in Brisbane, but it is not the standout that national media implies.

More important than either season is preparation. A property that is presented, priced, and photographed well in August will outperform an unprepared property in October. The timing advantage only matters when everything else is in order. If your preparation timeline is driving the decision, focus on getting the property right before you focus on the calendar.
