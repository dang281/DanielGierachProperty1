## Caption

The White Rock Conservation Area on Holland Park's southern edge is one of the things buyers mention before they mention anything else. Bushland access, quiet streets, and you're still 7km from the CBD. That combination is hard to find at this price point.

Buyers coming to Holland Park are typically families who want a post-war character home with a real backyard. Holland Park State School draws strong local demand, and the Mt Gravatt catchment further up gives secondary options that parents take seriously. Griffith University nearby also makes this suburb work for academics and university-adjacent professionals.

The market here has tended to reward renovated Queenslanders on larger blocks. Homes that present well on streets like Cavendish Road and Bengar Street attract competitive interest. Properties that need significant work still move, but they take longer and the discount expectation from buyers is real. The post-war brick homes on smaller lots sit in the middle of that spectrum.

Median house prices in Holland Park have been tracking upward alongside inner south demand. ⚠️ VERIFY current median and days on market before posting.

Spring is traditionally when Holland Park sees its best listings quality. If you own here and have been considering your options, now is a good time to understand what your property is worth.
