## Caption

The "sell in spring" advice that gets repeated constantly in Australian property is built around Melbourne and Sydney. Those markets have cold winters that genuinely suppress buyer activity, and spring represents a real awakening in buyer energy. Brisbane's seasonal pattern is fundamentally different.

Brisbane winters are mild. Buyers keep attending opens. Stock does thin out a little, but buyer motivation doesn't drop anything like it does further south. The season that actually suppresses activity here is summer. December through February is when the heat, the Christmas break, and the school holiday calendar collectively reduce open home attendance and buyer urgency in ways that winter simply doesn't.

The practical consequence of this is that autumn, specifically late February through to May, is Brisbane's most consistent performing window for inner-east properties. You're catching buyers who are motivated and active before the heat has passed, competing supply is typically lower than spring, and the school year calendar has families making decisions. Autumn campaigns in the inner east consistently produce strong results.

Spring is not a bad time to sell in Brisbane. September in particular can be excellent for the reasons I've written about this week. But if you've been told that spring is simply "the" time and autumn is a lesser option, that's Sydney advice applied to a Brisbane market. The two cities don't behave the same way.

The article at danielgierach.com/insights/best-time-to-sell-brisbane covers this seasonal analysis in detail, including what the data and on-the-ground observation suggests about each window of the year.
