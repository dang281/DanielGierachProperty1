## Caption

Carina Heights sits above Carina on the slope that faces north and west, which gives the suburb a distinct character from its lower neighbour. The streets are quiet and predominantly owner-occupied, and the housing stock runs toward the post-war character range with larger blocks than buyers find in the inner east suburbs further north.

The creek corridors that run through and around the suburb provide the kind of green buffer that is difficult to replicate in denser suburban environments. Residents can walk through the Belmont Hills State Forest fringe areas, which gives families with children a recreational resource that does not require driving. The suburb is well-positioned for access to Old Cleveland Road and the Carindale Shopping Centre, without sitting directly adjacent to either in a way that affects amenity.

In March, the families who are making decisions about the next school year start their search in earnest. Carina Heights draws this buyer because it offers the inner east fundamentals, manageable distance to the CBD, good school options, and established residential character, at a price point that remains accessible relative to Camp Hill or Norman Park. The families who find themselves working within a budget that does not quite reach those suburbs find Carina Heights offers the same quality of daily life at a price that makes the numbers work.

For homeowners here, the autumn market is the clearest opportunity.
