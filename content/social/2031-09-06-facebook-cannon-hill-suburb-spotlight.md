## Caption

September in Cannon Hill is the month where everything the suburb has been building toward becomes visible in the market. Buyer numbers are at their seasonal high. The Cross River Rail connection to the CBD brings a new wave of buyers who have recalibrated their acceptable commute based on fast train access. And Cannon Hill Anglican College creates its own stream of families who need to secure an address before the school year turns.

The suburb's shift from transitional to established in buyer perception is now complete. Buyers are not approaching Cannon Hill as a compromise or a stepping stone. They are approaching it as a destination, and that confidence is reflected in how aggressively they compete for the right properties.

Cannon Hill Plaza handles the daily retail needs of residents, and the Gateway Motorway access is genuinely useful for anyone whose work or family commitments involve travel north or south of the city. Together with the train station, those infrastructure layers mean Cannon Hill serves a wide range of daily life patterns, which broadens the buyer pool considerably.

Spring is the season where listing competition intensifies, but Cannon Hill's buyer depth typically absorbs that additional supply without a significant price impact. The suburb's appeal is broad enough to support multiple campaigns simultaneously without buyers being divided too thinly.
