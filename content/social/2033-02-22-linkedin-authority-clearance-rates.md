## Caption

Clearance rates make good headlines. They are a poor guide to what your specific property will do in your specific suburb.

A weekend clearance rate for Brisbane is an aggregate of results across an enormous range of locations, price points, and property types. A clearance rate of ⚠️ VERIFY 70-something per cent in the inner east means something different from the same figure recorded in an outer ring suburb with different buyer demographics and stock levels.

What matters for a vendor in Hawthorne or Coorparoo is not how Brisbane performed on Saturday. It is how properties comparable to yours, similar land size, similar condition, within two kilometres of your address, performed over the past sixty to ninety days. That data set is small enough to be meaningful and specific enough to give you a genuine read on buyer appetite.

The other variable most sellers miss is the composition of what sold in that clearance figure. A weekend where ten properties went to auction and seven sold will have a different character depending on whether those seven sales were front-loaded (sold under the hammer) or came later (sold post-auction). The timing of an offer relative to the auction says something about buyer confidence.

Suburb-level clearance data, read over time rather than week by week, is a useful signal. City-average clearance rates, presented without context, are mostly noise.

If you are planning to list this autumn, ask your agent to show you the suburb-specific comparable sales, not the city headline.
