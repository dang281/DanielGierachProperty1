## Caption

Buyer demand in Brisbane's inner east has multiple drivers pulling at once. Understanding which one is doing the most work in a given season matters for pricing strategy, campaign timing, and understanding what buyers are willing to stretch for.

Interest rates shape the borrowing capacity of finance-dependent buyers and therefore the ceiling of what the market will pay at the mid-tier. Population growth and interstate migration bring buyers into the inner east who are unfamiliar with the suburbs and willing to pay for location. Olympic infrastructure left some tangible improvements in Brisbane's transport and amenity landscape. And limited stock supply remains a structural feature of the inner east, where land availability is genuinely constrained.

All four are real. But in February 2033, one of them is probably doing more work than the others.

What do you think is the biggest factor driving buyer demand in Brisbane's inner east right now?
