## Caption

Two properties on the same street. Similar land size, similar layout, similar build era. One sells for what the market says it should. The other sells for materially more. The difference is almost never location. It is presentation.

In Brisbane's inner east, the gap between a well-presented property and an average one is not cosmetic. It is financial. ⚠️ VERIFY Buyers making decisions at the top of their budget are not being sentimental when they pay more for a clean, styled, well-photographed home. They are reducing their perceived risk. A property that looks cared for tells them there are fewer surprises behind the walls.

Street appeal compounds this. The first image a buyer sees, whether online or driving past on a Saturday, sets the frame for everything that follows. A freshly painted fence, trimmed gardens and a clean driveway cost a fraction of what they return in buyer perception. In Camp Hill and Balmoral, where price points are high and buyers are comparing multiple properties every weekend, street appeal is often the reason a buyer walks in rather than past.

Photography is the final multiplier. Most buyers in the inner east shortlist properties online before attending in person. A property with flat, poorly lit photography gets fewer clicks, fewer inspections, and ultimately a smaller buyer pool. A smaller buyer pool almost always means a lower result.

None of this is about spending large amounts of money. It is about understanding where buyers pay attention.
