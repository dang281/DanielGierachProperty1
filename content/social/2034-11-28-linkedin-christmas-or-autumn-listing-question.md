## Caption

The question I get most often in late November is whether it is better to go to market now or wait for autumn. The honest answer depends on factors specific to your property and your situation, but there are some patterns worth knowing.

Properties that tend to work well in a December or early January campaign: homes where the vendor is not in a rush, where the property shows well in summer light, and where the target buyer is someone who has been watching the suburb and will act when the right property appears regardless of season.

Properties that tend to work better in autumn: family homes where the competition for buyers is intense and you want the widest possible pool, properties that need extensive preparation that cannot be completed before Christmas, and situations where the vendor needs to sell and buy simultaneously and wants the confidence of a larger buyer pool for both transactions.

Autumn in the inner east, specifically the February to May window, consistently delivers strong clearance rates. That is partly seasonal and partly because it catches the buyers who had a quiet Christmas thinking about property and are now in action mode.

The decision between Christmas and autumn is not about market conditions in the abstract. It is about what your property needs to perform at its best and when your personal timeline allows it.
