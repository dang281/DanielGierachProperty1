## Caption

Appraisals in Brisbane's inner east are not guesses. But they're also not guarantees. The final sale price routinely comes in above or below a well-reasoned appraisal, and the gap is almost always traceable.

Three things consistently push sale prices above the appraisal range in the inner east.

The first is buyer competition. A property that generates three or more genuine offers does not sell at the first offer. It sells at the highest offer the vendor is prepared to accept. Creating genuine competition requires the right price point, the right marketing reach, and enough time to let competing buyers identify each other. A vendor who accepts the first offer rarely finds out what they left behind.

The second is presentation. Not renovation. Presentation. Cleaned, decluttered, styled for photography and inspection. The difference in how a home photographs when it has been prepared versus when it hasn't is measurable in the depth of the buyer pool it attracts. More buyers at open homes means more potential for competition.

The third is timing the negotiation correctly. The moment a vendor becomes visibly motivated to accept any offer, the negotiating dynamic shifts. Experienced buyers read urgency in days-on-market, in price reductions, in agent communication. Maintaining the discipline to negotiate from a position of patience, even when you want to move, changes the outcome.

None of these are secrets. But they require preparation, discipline, and an agent who manages the process rather than just running it.
