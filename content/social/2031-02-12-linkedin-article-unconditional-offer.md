## Caption

A seller will often accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one. Not always, but often enough that understanding this changes how a buyer approaches a negotiation.

An unconditional offer removes the 2 to 3 week risk window that every conditional contract carries. During a finance condition period, the buyer can exit. During a building and pest period, the buyer can exit. For a seller who has already exchanged on their next purchase, or who is working to a settlement timeline driven by an external factor, that risk window is a real and meaningful cost. Certainty is worth a price difference, and in competitive markets, buyers who understand this can use it deliberately.

The decision to go unconditional is not a casual one. It requires pre-approved finance, a clear understanding of the property's condition, and confidence in the title. In practice, a buyer who has done a pre-purchase building inspection through their own inspector before making an offer, has unconditional finance approval rather than pre-approval, and has had a solicitor review the contract can make a credible unconditional offer. Most buyers do not have all three of those things ready before they identify a property they want.

The article covers what unconditional actually means in a Queensland residential contract, how to prepare to make one, the circumstances where it makes strategic sense, and where the genuine risks sit for a buyer who goes in without the preparation the position requires.
