## Caption

The Coorparoo State School catchment does something measurable to property prices on the streets that fall inside it. Buyers who have school-age children or are planning ahead make decisions based on that boundary in ways that sustain demand through every phase of the market cycle.

Coorparoo sits close enough to the CBD for daily commuting to be practical, and far enough from the inner ring to offer land sizes that the suburbs closer in cannot match. The pre-war character housing in the elevated pockets, particularly on the streets that rise above Old Cleveland Road, gives the suburb an architectural identity that infill suburbs built around townhouse stock cannot replicate. The Old Cleveland Road strip itself runs the length of the suburb's commercial spine, offering the mix of cafes, restaurants, and independent retail that buyers at this price point expect.

The train station at Coorparoo provides a commute option that removes the car dependency that affects many comparable suburbs. For buyers who are comparing Coorparoo with Holland Park or Camp Hill, the train access is frequently the point that tips the decision.

As autumn reaches its peak in March, the catchment-driven demand in Coorparoo concentrates. Families who want to be settled before the next school year start their search now and make decisions quickly.
