## Caption

Christmas Eve in Mt Gravatt is a good time to think about what the suburb looks like in the new year. The buyers who've been through an active spring market, who missed out on properties, who have their finance sorted and their criteria locked in: many of them return in January with the same intent. Knowing your property's value before that wave arrives is worth doing now.

Mt Gravatt's diversity of buyer types is one of its strengths. The Griffith University campus at Upper Mt Gravatt draws academic and professional buyers. The South East Freeway access draws practical commuters. The views from Mt Gravatt Outlook attract prestige buyers who want outlook without paying Hawthorne or Bulimba prices. All of those groups are active across the year.

The Plaza shopping precinct means residents rarely need to leave the suburb for daily needs. That containment, combined with good freeway access, appeals to buyers who want lifestyle efficiency as much as suburb character.

Properties on the elevated streets with eastern outlook are consistently the most contested in this suburb. They attract buyers who've specifically looked for this position and rejected alternatives that didn't have it. That conviction in the buyer profile produces strong competition when the right property comes up.

For vendors considering a summer campaign, the buyer pool refills quickly in January. The window between Christmas and the Australia Day long weekend has historically been productive.
