## Caption

Holland Park sits in a part of Brisbane's south-east that has been quietly appreciating for a long time, supported by fundamentals that do not change with market sentiment. The suburb is elevated, it has good school catchments and it has the block sizes that families need when children, outdoor space and practical storage are all competing for room. Those factors attract a particular buyer who is not influenced by suburb prestige so much as by what the suburb actually delivers.

Cavendish Road State High School remains a central driver of buyer behaviour in Holland Park. Families who have researched state secondary education in Brisbane's south-east know the school, and many of the purchasing decisions made in the suburb each year are directly tied to securing enrolment. That pattern of school-driven buying provides a structural demand base that is resilient across market cycles.

The housing stock in Holland Park gives buyers options across a meaningful price range. Original post-war homes on large blocks offer renovation upside for buyers prepared to invest, while the growing number of renovated and updated properties cater to those who want to move in without a major project. Both buyer types are active in the market. Current medians in Holland Park represent a reasonable premium over comparable suburbs without the school catchment advantage, reflecting the value the market places on that access.

Bus connections to the CBD along Cavendish Road are reliable, and road access to surrounding centres is straightforward.
