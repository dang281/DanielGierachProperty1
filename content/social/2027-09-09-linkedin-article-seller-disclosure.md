Under the Property Law Act 2023 (Qld), sellers are required to provide a completed Seller Disclosure Statement before any contract is signed. If that statement is not provided, the buyer can terminate the contract at any time before settlement. No fault required, no reason given. Just termination.

Most sellers are not aware of this until their solicitor raises it, usually well into the campaign. By that point, the statement needs to be prepared quickly, which increases the risk of errors or gaps.

The Seller Disclosure Statement covers a significant amount of ground: title details, encumbrances, any notices affecting the property, body corporate matters for lots in a scheme, and more. Getting it right at the outset protects the contract.

The article I have linked explains what the disclosure statement needs to include, who prepares it, when it must be provided, and what happens if it contains a material deficiency. There are also specific items that many sellers overlook when completing it, particularly around easements and infrastructure charges, and those gaps are the ones most likely to give a buyer grounds to exit.

If you are planning to sell in Queensland, understanding your disclosure obligations before you list is not optional. It is part of the preparation.
