## Caption

The most common mistake sellers make with styling is treating it as decoration. It is not. It is about removing the reasons a buyer hesitates.

Buyers make decisions quickly and often emotionally. If they walk into a room and the furniture is oversized, the bench is cluttered, or the spare bedroom looks like a storage dump, they discount. Not consciously, not always verbally, but they do. The property feels like someone else's problem to solve rather than an opportunity they want.

Professional staging changes that. It is not about making a property look like a magazine shoot. It is about presenting rooms at their correct scale, removing personal noise that distracts from the bones of the place, and helping buyers imagine living there rather than moving someone else out.

In Brisbane's inner east, where comparable properties are often genuinely similar in size and condition, presentation becomes one of the few controllable variables in a campaign. Buyers shortlisting from online searches do not always get to a second inspection if the first impression does not land.

I have written in detail about what styling actually costs, what it typically returns, when it is worth it and when it is not, and how to find a reliable stager in Brisbane. If you are preparing a property for sale this year, it is worth reading before you decide.
