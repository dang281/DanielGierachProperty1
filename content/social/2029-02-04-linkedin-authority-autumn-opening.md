## Caption

The autumn campaign window in Brisbane's inner east opens properly in February, and the buyers who have been sitting on pre-approval since late November are ready to move.

This is the part of the year where the buyer pool is most motivated and the competition for well-presented, correctly priced properties is most consistent. It is not a spring hype cycle or a pre-Christmas rush. It is the period where owner-occupier families making planned, deliberate moves are most active.

What I see at open homes in February and March is different from what I see in September and October. The February pool is smaller in number but faster to decide. They have been doing the research for months. They know the streets. They have already done the building and pest on two other properties that did not come together. When they find the right one, they move.

For vendors, that buyer profile is the one you want to find. Not the browser who attends fifteen open homes and never commits. The buyer who has done the work and is ready to sign.

If you are sitting on a property you have been thinking about listing for the past six months, the conditions right now are supportive. The buyers are ready. The question is whether the property is ready for them.
