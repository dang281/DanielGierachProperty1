## Caption

The buyers showing up to open homes in early September are not the same as the buyers who show up in October. Understanding that difference matters if you are planning a spring campaign.

The early spring buyer has usually been searching since June or July. They have been to 15 open homes across multiple suburbs. Their finance is approved, their brief is clear, and they have a strong sense of what comparable properties have been selling for because they have been tracking it for months. When the right property appears, they do not need three weeks to decide. They will make an offer in week one or two of a campaign.

These buyers fall into a few clear groups. Families who want to be settled before the end of school term four are the most time-sensitive. They are not browsing. They need a contract signed and a settlement date that works. Upgraders who have been preparing since mid-winter are similarly ready, often with their own property either sold or ready to list. Investors who are ahead of the peak competition curve also tend to move early, preferring to act before October when buyer numbers swell and competition for each property intensifies.

What this means for a vendor launching in late August or early September is that these committed, prepared buyers are your primary audience. They are not waiting for the market to warm up. They are ready now, and they are evaluating every property against everything they have already inspected. A property that presents well, is priced with reference to recent comparable sales, and is marketed properly will get an offer from this group. That is not a small pool of buyers.
