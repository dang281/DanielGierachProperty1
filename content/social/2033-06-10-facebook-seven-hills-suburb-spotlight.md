## Caption

Seven Hills is a suburb where the physical setting does a lot of the selling. The elevated ridge position at around five kilometres from the CBD delivers something that flat inner suburbs cannot: unobstructed views toward the city skyline and the natural ventilation that comes with height. In Brisbane, where summer heat is a real consideration in how people live, that ridge position translates into a daily comfort advantage that buyers put a concrete value on.

The character home is the suburb's dominant housing type. Queenslanders and post-war homes with the proportions that come from a period when lots were built with space in mind define the streetscape across most of Seven Hills. The suburb has not been heavily redeveloped relative to some inner east neighbours, which means the character has been preserved and the sense of continuity across the street is noticeable. That consistency is a quality that buyers who have been looking seriously across the inner east come to appreciate.

Whites Hill State College covers the secondary catchment for much of the suburb and is a genuine factor in purchasing decisions for families with teenagers. The security of knowing a well-regarded state secondary school is within catchment removes one variable from an already complex decision, and it keeps a steady stream of family buyers engaged with the market in Seven Hills.

Current medians reflect the elevation premium and the character quality of the suburb. Stock availability is limited, which concentrates competition when good properties are available.
