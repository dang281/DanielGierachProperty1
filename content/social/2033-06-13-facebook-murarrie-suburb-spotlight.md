## Caption

Murarrie sits eight kilometres from the CBD in a position that gives it practical advantages that are not always fully priced in. Access to the Gateway Motorway is direct and uncomplicated, which means that for residents who commute to the airport, the Port or employment nodes to the south and north, the suburb's location is genuinely efficient. That connectivity appeals to buyers whose work does not take them primarily into the CBD.

The suburb's residential fabric is evolving. Newer townhouse and unit developments have introduced a younger demographic and a different type of buyer into a suburb that previously had a more industrial-residential character. That evolution has been gradual, and the market has absorbed it without the volatility that can accompany rapid change. Current medians in Murarrie represent an accessible entry point into the broader inner east corridor, a point of difference that buyers who have compared the suburb to Morningside or Cannon Hill note quickly.

Investors have been attracted to Murarrie by the price point and by rental demand that draws from employment in the surrounding industrial and commercial precincts. Vacancy rates across the broader inner east corridor have historically supported investors, and Murarrie benefits from proximity to that demand without carrying the same price point as its more prestigious neighbours.

As the suburb continues to develop and as Brisbane's population growth drives demand further out along the inner east corridor, Murarrie's position becomes progressively more interesting to buyers.
