## Caption

The last post of the year is a good place for honesty rather than forecasts. Here is my honest read on the inner east heading into 2030.

The Olympic Games are roughly two and a half years away. Infrastructure works are now visible across the inner east corridor. Buyers who made decisions in 2026 on the basis of what the precinct might become are watching that thesis play out. That does not make 2030 a guaranteed boom, but it gives the inner east a supporting story most Brisbane precincts do not have.

Land supply remains constrained. New housing stock in established suburbs like Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, and Balmoral is limited almost entirely to small infill developments. Freestanding homes on decent land continue to attract competition from a buyer pool larger than the available stock.

For sellers in Q1 2030: preparation matters more than timing. A property properly prepared and priced will perform in March. One rushed to beat an imagined seasonal window will not. For buyers: waiting for meaningful softening before acting has not been a rewarding strategy in this precinct over the past decade.

I am not making price forecasts. The conditions that could shift things quickly are real, and any agent claiming certainty is not being straight with you. What I can say is that the fundamentals for the inner east are as solid as they have been at any point in recent years.

It has been a good year. Thank you to everyone who engaged with this content or referred a client. I am looking forward to what 2030 brings.
