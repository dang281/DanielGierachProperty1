## Caption

August buyers in Brisbane's inner east are a specific type. Understanding who they are and what drives them matters for any vendor deciding whether to launch now or hold until September.

By the time August arrives, the buyers attending inner east open homes have typically been searching since late autumn. They went through June without finding what they needed. They attended opens through July and either missed out or kept waiting. They have made their suburb decision. They have made their price decision. In many cases, they have made their product decision and are simply waiting for the right property to come to market.

That is a very different buyer from the one who starts their search fresh in September because the weather is getting warmer and the weekend listings look appealing. The September buyer is motivated, but they have options. The August buyer has been waiting for months and knows that spring will bring more competition from other buyers as well as more listings.

A vendor who launches into August with a well-prepared property is often entering a market where the buyer has already done the work. They are not educating a buyer about the suburb or about what comparable properties have sold for. They are presenting to someone who already knows those things and is ready to transact when they find the right fit.

The practical implication is that August campaigns, when well prepared and realistically priced, often produce a rapid result because concentrated demand meets limited supply. That dynamic does not last once spring listings arrive and alternatives expand.

The August window is underused. Most vendors wait for spring because that is what conventional advice says. Vendors who move in August and get it right regularly achieve results the spring crowd watches with envy.
