## Caption

A seller who lists in July has made a deliberate choice. They have looked at the competing stock, assessed the buyer pool, and concluded the conditions suit their property and their timeline. That deliberateness tends to produce better outcomes.

A seller who lists in September because "spring is when you sell" has not made the same analysis. They have followed convention. And they arrive in a market where every other seller who followed the same convention is now competing for the same buyers.

There is nothing wrong with a spring campaign. The buyer pool does expand. Qualified buyers who were sitting out winter do re-enter the market. But so does the competition, and for most sellers the net effect of increased supply is more pronounced than the boost from additional demand.

What winter sellers consistently find: the buyers they meet in July are decisive. They are not window-shopping. They are buying. And a well-prepared property listed in a thinner market reaches a committed buyer faster and with less campaign fatigue.

That is not a guarantee. It is a pattern. The guarantee only ever comes from the preparation.
