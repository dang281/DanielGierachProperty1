## Caption

Net equity on a property sale is not sale price minus loan balance. That calculation misses three costs that are always present and sometimes substantial.

Agent commission sits between two and three percent of the sale price on most inner east campaigns. On a $1.2 million sale, that is $24,000 to $36,000 before anything else. Conveyancing adds another $1,500 to $3,000. And if you are on a fixed-rate loan, the break cost for discharging the mortgage before its term ends can run from a few hundred dollars to several thousand depending on how much of the fixed period remains and how rates have moved since you locked in.

The lender needs to be notified when you accept an offer, not at settlement. The sooner they are in the process, the sooner the discharge paperwork is initiated and the less risk of settlement delays on that side.

The practical advice for anyone selling a mortgaged property: get the net proceeds calculation done before you set your price expectation. Know what the loan discharge will cost. Know the timeline your lender needs. That information changes the campaign strategy in some cases and almost always changes the vendor's understanding of what they will walk away with.
