## Caption

Body corporate levies are one of the most commonly underestimated costs in Brisbane's inner-east unit and townhouse market.

The annual figure varies significantly depending on complex type, age, and facilities. For context, realistic ranges in the inner east: a small townhouse complex of four to eight lots runs around $2,000 to $4,000 per year. A standard unit block with a pool and garden management: $4,000 to $8,000. A mid-rise complex with a lift, gym, and pool: $6,000 to $14,000.

For an investor, the body corporate levy comes straight off the net yield calculation. A property returning $30,000 per year in rent with $10,000 in levies looks very different from one with levies of $3,000. That difference needs to appear in the numbers before you make an offer.

For buyers, there are two things worth checking before exchange. The first is the current levy amount, which your solicitor obtains from the body corporate records. The second is the sinking fund balance. A sinking fund that is unusually low relative to the age and size of the complex often signals that a special levy is coming. Special levies can run from a few thousand to tens of thousands, and once you own the lot, you're liable.

Request the body corporate records before going unconditional. It's a standard step, but one that buyers under time pressure sometimes skip.
