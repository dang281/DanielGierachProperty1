## Caption

Understanding who is buying in the inner east right now shapes everything about how a campaign should be run. The buyer profile this autumn is worth being specific about.

The family upgrader remains the dominant buyer type across the core inner east suburbs: Norman Park, Morningside, Camp Hill, Carina, and Cannon Hill. These are buyers making a 10-plus year decision, school catchment is a primary filter, and they are motivated by certainty rather than bargains. A property that presents well, is priced with reference to recent comparable sales, and offers clear value in terms of land and living space converts these buyers.

The downsizer cohort is active across Balmoral, Hawthorne, and Bulimba. These buyers are asset-rich, patient, and selective. They are not competing on emotion. They want a property that justifies a premium on its merits, and when they find it they move decisively.

The investor buyer is present but more selective than in previous cycles, particularly in the sub-$900,000 price range. Yield expectations have adjusted and due diligence is rigorous.

Each buyer type responds to different campaign signals. The vendor who understands which profile their property will appeal to runs a more targeted campaign than one treating the whole market as a single audience.
