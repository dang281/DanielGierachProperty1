## Caption

In Queensland, a buyer is entitled to carry out one pre-settlement inspection of the property before settlement takes place. It is not a second building and pest inspection. It is a check that the property is in the same condition as when the contract was signed, that all inclusions listed in the contract are present, and that the property is ready for handover.

For sellers, the pre-settlement inspection is a straightforward step that becomes complicated when the property is not left in the condition required.

The most common issues: fixtures that were in the contract have been removed. Damage has occurred since the building and pest inspection was done. The property has not been cleaned to the standard expected. The garden has not been maintained.

The contract requires the property to be in the same condition at settlement as it was when the contract was signed, fair wear and tear excepted. That clause is there for a reason. If the pre-settlement inspection reveals something that does not meet that standard, the buyer has grounds to delay settlement or seek compensation.

For sellers, the practical steps are simple. Leave all contract inclusions in place. Do not remove anything that was present at the time of signing. Leave the property clean. Maintain the garden. Ensure all keys, remotes, and access items are ready for handover.
