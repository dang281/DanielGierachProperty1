## Caption

On a $1.1 million owner-occupier purchase in Queensland, transfer duty adds approximately $43,350 to the purchase cost.

That number does not go into equity. It does not come back. It is a transaction cost that leaves your account on settlement day and does not appear on the title.

In my experience, most buyers underestimate it by $10,000 or more when they first start budgeting. They think about the deposit, the borrowing capacity, perhaps the conveyancing fee. The duty calculation tends to come as a late-stage surprise, at a point where the budget is already set and the property search is well underway.

First home buyers in Queensland have access to a concession, with duty relief available on homes up to a tapering threshold. But for everyone else purchasing at inner east Brisbane prices, this is a fixed cost that has to be factored into the deposit calculation and the borrowing structure before the search begins, not after the contract is signed.

The practical implication is straightforward: if you are budgeting for a purchase in the $900,000 to $1.5 million range, run the duty calculation first. It changes the deposit requirement and may change the price range you are genuinely able to access.

The full Queensland transfer duty calculation, including the current thresholds and first home buyer concession details, is covered in the article.
