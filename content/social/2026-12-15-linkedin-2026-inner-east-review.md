## Caption

A few honest observations about Brisbane's inner east in 2026, as the year closes out.

Stock remained consistently tight. The suburbs I work across, Bulimba, Hawthorne, Balmoral, Norman Park, Camp Hill, Morningside, and Cannon Hill, all saw listings running below their five-year averages for most of the year. That structural shortage did more to support prices than any other single factor.

Buyer demand was durable, not frantic. The competition I saw at open homes through winter and spring was from buyers who had been in the market long enough to know what they were prepared to pay. The bidding environments were competitive, but they were not chaotic. Buyers with clear finance and clear criteria moved. Buyers who were testing the market without conviction tended to miss.

The price ceiling shifted upward in several suburbs. Properties that would have struggled past $1.4 million in 2024 found buyers at $1.5 million and above in 2026, particularly in Balmoral and Hawthorne where quality of presentation and site conditions matter most.

Going into 2027, the question is whether the supply constraint persists. My expectation is that it does, at least for the first half of the year. The inner east does not have significant new residential development to release into the market, and the owners who did not sell in 2025 or 2026 are not under obvious pressure to do so.
