## Caption

Carindale is a suburb that delivers a specific set of trade-offs, and the buyers who land here have usually worked through those trade-offs deliberately. The block sizes are generous, the streetscape is quiet, and the proximity to Westfield Carindale means daily retail and services access is unmatched in the inner east. What you give up is the walkable village character of Camp Hill or Bulimba. What you gain is space and a price point that reflects it.

The streets to the north of Carindale, backing toward the creek corridors and the Chandler Athletics Centre precinct, are the ones that buyers with children tend to prioritise. Schools in the catchment, including Carindale State School and the independent options along the Logan Road corridor, are a consistent part of buyer conversations.

The autumn campaign season suits Carindale's buyer profile well. The families and upgraders who dominate buyer enquiry in this suburb tend to make decisions in March and April, before the school year settles and before Easter disrupts the inspection calendar. A well-presented property on a good block in Carindale in March tends to find its buyer within a standard campaign cycle.

Properties that have been updated with practical buyers in mind, good kitchens, outdoor living, and adequate garaging, consistently outperform cosmetically renovated homes that sacrifice functionality for aesthetics.
