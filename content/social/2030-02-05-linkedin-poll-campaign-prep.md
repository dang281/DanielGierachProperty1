## Caption

One of the most common miscalculations sellers make is underestimating how long proper preparation takes.

Four to eight weeks is the realistic window for a well-prepared campaign in the inner east. That covers decluttering, repairs, building inspection, styling, photography, and the marketing setup. Rush any part of that and it shows, either in the photography or in the open home presentation or in buyer feedback that is polite but doesn't convert to offers.

The sellers who get it right are the ones who planned four to six weeks ahead. The ones who say "we'll be ready in two weeks" are often still on the market eight weeks later.

If you decided to sell your home tomorrow, how long would you need to prepare before going to market?
