## Caption

Holland Park sits along the Logan Road corridor, one of Brisbane's great inner south-east arterials, and the suburb has always drawn buyers who want inner east access without inner east pricing. That equation is gradually changing as the suburb's appeal becomes harder to ignore.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war: weatherboard and fibro homes from the 1950s through to the 70s, many of them on elevated blocks with views across the suburban canopy. The renovation opportunity in Holland Park has attracted buyers who want to buy value and add to it, and the results of well-executed renovations here have consistently outperformed. ⚠️ VERIFY

Wellers Hill State School sits within the Holland Park catchment and draws school-zone buyers who have done the calculation and found it advantageous compared to closer-in alternatives. The Logan Road strip provides retail and dining access that has improved steadily over the years.

What makes Holland Park interesting for vendors in 2033 is the ongoing transformation of the suburb's character. A street that had unrenovated homes five years ago may now have three or four well-presented properties that have reset the comparable sale benchmark. If you have been in your Holland Park home for more than a decade, your perception of what comparable properties sell for may be significantly behind where the market has moved.

An updated appraisal might tell you something worth knowing.
