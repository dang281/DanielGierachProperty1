## Caption

The first full weekend of September marks the start of Brisbane's spring selling season in a way that is visible at open homes, not just on a calendar. Attendance lifts, enquiry quality improves, and buyers who were sitting on the sidelines through winter begin to move.

What I observed at open homes this past weekend was consistent with what I have seen in previous spring openings. Buyer groups were larger. More people were asking for contracts rather than just walking through. The quality of the conversation shifted from exploratory to serious. For properties that had been prepared through August and launched at the right price, the first weekend delivered everything a spring campaign should.

That said, spring is not a guarantee of result. It is the best time to be presenting a well-prepared property, but the market still separates well-prepared from poorly-prepared campaigns with the same clarity it does in any other season. A property that launches in September with rushed photography, no styling, and an aspirational price does not benefit from the spring effect. It competes against properties that took preparation seriously, and it loses.

The vendors who are seeing the benefits of spring right now are the ones who started their preparation conversations in August. If your property is one of those, this is a strong market to be operating in. If you are still making decisions about preparation, the window is narrowing. Spring does not wait, and the best buyer activity is typically concentrated in the first three to four weeks.
