## Caption

The autumn selling window in Brisbane's inner east has a specific shape. It opens in late February when buyer numbers recover from the summer lull, builds through March as serious buyers commit to their search, and starts to soften from mid-May as the market quiets ahead of winter.

That is roughly ten weeks of peak buyer activity. We are currently four weeks in.

The vendors who launched in late February and March have been the primary beneficiaries of the strong buyer numbers this autumn. Clearance rates across the inner east have been solid and days on market for well-priced properties has remained short. Buyer competition at open homes has been genuine, not manufactured.

For vendors who have been weighing whether to go to market this autumn or wait until spring, the calculus is straightforward. The remaining window before the May softening is around four weeks. A well-run campaign needs three to four weeks from launch to auction or negotiation. That means vendors who want to capture autumn buyer numbers need to be making decisions now, not in three weeks.

The question is not whether autumn is a good time to sell. It is whether there is enough time left in this autumn to run the campaign properly.
