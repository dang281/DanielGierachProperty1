## Caption

In spring, every property is competing for the same buyer. What distinguishes one listing from another in an online search result is the photography. Styling is the investment that makes the photography better. It is not primarily an in-person experience. Most buyers have formed a strong view about whether a property is worth inspecting before they have ever set foot through the door, and that view is formed entirely from photographs.

Vendors who think of styling as a presentation tool for open homes are missing where most of the value is generated. The open home matters, but the decision to attend the open home is made from a screen, often on a phone, often at night, often in under ten seconds of scrolling. The styled property that photographs well appears in that ten seconds and earns the inspection. The unstaged property with existing furniture and flat lighting does not make the shortlist.

Mid-September is the right moment to make this decision properly. Vendors planning October and November campaigns still have time to engage a stylist, allow the work to be done correctly, and have photography taken under the right conditions. Vendors who leave this decision until the week before their first open home are not getting the same quality of result. A rushed styling job with borrowed furniture does not produce the photography that a considered styling engagement produces.

The full article explains what property styling involves, how to evaluate whether it is the right investment for a specific property, and how to select a stylist in the Brisbane market.
