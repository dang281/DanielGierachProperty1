## Caption

Most vendors do this calculation early: sale price minus outstanding mortgage balance equals what they walk away with. It's a reasonable starting point. It's also consistently wrong.

By the time you account for agent commission, conveyancing fees, the mortgage discharge fee, and potentially a fixed-rate break fee if you're exiting a fixed-rate loan early, the gap between the number on the contract and the amount that arrives in your bank account is meaningfully larger than most people plan for. Selling costs for a typical Brisbane property generally run somewhere in the range of 2.5 to 4 per cent of the sale price when you include everything. On a property that sells for $1.5 million, that's $37,500 to $60,000 before a dollar of mortgage is repaid.

This isn't a reason not to sell. It's a reason to do the maths properly before you commit to a price expectation or a simultaneous purchase.

The most common version of this problem I see is vendors who set a floor price based on what they need to net, without factoring in the full cost of selling. They're then surprised at settlement when the proceeds are lower than expected. Getting that number right upfront changes how you approach the campaign.

I've covered the full breakdown of costs in detail, including when break fees apply and how to estimate discharge costs, in an article on my website.
