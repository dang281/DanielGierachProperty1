## Caption

The standard advice is not to list in December. I understand why that advice exists and I've seen it play out poorly when it's ignored without good reason. But in Brisbane's inner east, a well-run December campaign targeting the right buyer can outperform what you'd get in January, and sometimes by more than vendors expect.

Here's the logic. By late November, the number of new listings drops sharply. Vendors who planned spring campaigns have largely sold. Vendors who are waiting for autumn haven't started preparing. That means the buyer who attends a Bulimba or Hawthorne open in late November or early December is doing so against a very thin supply of alternatives. They have fewer properties to choose between, and their motivation tends to be genuine rather than exploratory.

The buyer who turns up to an inspection on a warm Saturday morning in the first week of December is not casually browsing. They've often exhausted the spring listings, they want to be settled, and they're calculating whether a December contract can get them into the property before summer properly begins or, at minimum, by early in the new year.

This doesn't apply to every property or every situation. A poorly prepared campaign in December will suffer from thin attendance and low urgency, the same as in any other month. But a well-presented property, correctly priced, with quality marketing and strong agent communication, can perform very well in this window precisely because the competition is minimal.

If you're considering a late November or December listing, it's worth having a specific conversation about whether your property is the type that suits it.
