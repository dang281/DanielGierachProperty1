## Caption

East Brisbane's position on the city fringe, directly adjacent to The Gabba, puts it in a category that very few Brisbane suburbs occupy. The CBD is genuinely close, and the connectivity to Kangaroo Point cliffs and South Bank via bridge means residents are walking distance from two of Brisbane's most-used recreational and cultural precincts.

The housing stock reflects the suburb's density and proximity. Apartments and townhouses dominate, which shapes who buys here. The typical purchaser in East Brisbane is a professional or downsizer who has made a deliberate choice to prioritise city proximity and walkability over land size. That is a buyer who has consciously moved away from the suburban brief, and they are comparing East Brisbane to other city-fringe options rather than to detached housing suburbs.

The rental market in East Brisbane is consistently active. City workers and students who want inner-city access without CBD prices sustain rental demand in a way that keeps vacancy low and makes investor returns a straightforward calculation. For investors comparing this end of the market, East Brisbane's combination of proximity, walkability, and transport access is a reliable anchor for yield.

For owners of apartments and townhouses in East Brisbane who are thinking about timing, the buyer pool that pays attention to city-fringe stock tends to be year-round rather than seasonal. The seasonal dynamics that affect detached housing in the outer suburbs apply less here.
