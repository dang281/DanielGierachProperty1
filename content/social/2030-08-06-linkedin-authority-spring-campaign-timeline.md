## Caption

The vendors who get the best spring results are not the ones with the best properties. They are the ones who started the preparation work in August.

Week one looks like this: the agent is engaged, a pricing strategy is agreed based on comparable sales, and the property is walked through properly so that a maintenance list can be put together. Not a cosmetic list, but an honest one. Buyers notice every leaking tap, every sticking door, every soft board underfoot. Week one and two are for getting the trades in to fix those things before the camera arrives.

Week two is also when the styling quote gets booked. A good stylist in September is fully committed by mid-August. If you wait until your maintenance is finished to call, you may find yourself waiting three weeks for a booking that should have been locked in early.

Week three is declutter and pre-style preparation. This is not glamorous work. It is removing the furniture that makes rooms look smaller, clearing out the garage, and creating the space the stylist needs to work with. It is also when a lot of vendors realise they needed an extra two weeks.

Week four and five are photography, floor plans, and marketing copy. Week five and six are launch.

That sequence takes six weeks when everything goes to plan. It rarely goes exactly to plan. The vendors who build in buffer time in August are the ones who launch in September without rushing the photography because the painter finished late.

Preparation is almost always the variable. Not the season, not the market, not the agent. If you are considering a spring campaign, now is the right time to start.
