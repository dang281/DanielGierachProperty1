## Caption

The question most vendors ask is whether they need to style their home before selling. That is almost always the wrong question.

The right question is: how many buyers will book an inspection because of your listing photos?

Styling is not about impressing people when they walk through the door. It is about earning the inspection in the first place. A staged room photographs differently to an empty or dated room. That difference in online engagement translates directly to inspection numbers. More inspections mean more competition. More competition produces better results.

For a standard three-bedroom home in Brisbane's inner east, staging typically costs $3,000 to $6,000 for a four-week campaign. Compare that to the gap between a well-attended open home and a poorly attended one. The spend almost always earns back many times over in the final result.

The case for staging is clearest in vacant properties. Empty rooms photograph smaller than they are in person. That mismatch costs inspection bookings, and inspection bookings are where campaigns are won or lost.
