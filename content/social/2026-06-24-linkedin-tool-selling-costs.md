## Caption

Yesterday's poll asked what total selling costs came to on your last Brisbane sale. The votes have tended to cluster around the 2 to 3 per cent band, which matches what I see when sellers run the full breakdown rather than estimating.

A few notes on where the figure actually goes.

The commission line is the largest by some margin, but it is also the most negotiable. Tiered models with a lower base and an incentive on results above a target can produce a meaningfully different total than a flat percentage, particularly when the campaign performs strongly. Worth asking your agent for both options before signing the agency agreement.

Marketing is the next largest variable. A premium media package can add several thousand dollars without producing a better outcome if the property sits in a tightly-held suburb where saved-search alerts and the agent database already do most of the buyer reach. The right marketing spend is a function of the property's reach problem, not a standard package.

Presentation costs deserve careful thought. Styling, pre-sale repairs, and gardening return well when a property is being positioned to a competitive offer, less so when the buyer pool is renovation-focused and pricing the home below cosmetic decisions.

There is a calculator that itemises everything by line and lets you stress-test where the cost is concentrated. Link in the comments.
