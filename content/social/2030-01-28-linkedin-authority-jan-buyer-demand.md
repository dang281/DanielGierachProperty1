## Caption

Post-holiday enquiry is the best leading indicator of the coming quarter. Not commentary, not forecasts. The actual behaviour of buyers at open homes and through private inspection requests tells you what is coming.

What I am seeing in January 2030 in the inner east:

Buyers who prequalified in late 2029 are active. They have approval, they have clarity on what they want, and they are moving quickly when the right property comes to market. They are not waiting.

December-listed stock has had its open home attendances and buyer feedback established. The buyer pool for those properties has largely shown itself. January-fresh stock gets a new audience, including buyers who weren't actively searching in December but returned from the break with a clear decision made.

The difference between December-listed and January-fresh is meaningful for sellers. A property that launched in December into a quieter pool and didn't sell is not a property that failed. It is a property that needs to be assessed again in the context of the January buyer pool.

The profile of buyers active in January in the inner east is skewed toward the motivated end. They are not casual inspectors. They are people who have spent the summer break making a decision, and who come back into the market ready to transact.

If you have a property that didn't sell in November or December, or if you are preparing to launch in the next four to six weeks, the January read is useful information for your pricing and marketing conversation.

I am happy to share what I am seeing in specific suburbs.
