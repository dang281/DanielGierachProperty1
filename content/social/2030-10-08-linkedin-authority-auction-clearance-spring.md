## Caption

Auction clearance rates get reported every weekend and misunderstood almost as often.

A high clearance rate tells you that buyer demand is outpacing the volume of available stock at current pricing. It does not guarantee that every property sells for what its vendor hoped. It tells you the market is clearing, not the margin at which it is clearing.

In Brisbane's inner east, spring clearance rates historically track above the annual average. October and November tend to be the strongest consecutive months of the year for auction performance. The reason is straightforward: motivated buyers who spent winter doing research are now ready to commit, and the window before Christmas creates a practical deadline that focuses decision-making.

What clearance rates do not tell you is whether your specific property, with its specific configuration and position, will perform to the median. A character home on a good street in Balmoral behaves differently to a unit in a block of twelve in Coorparoo, regardless of what the suburb-level clearance rate says.

The practical use of clearance rate data is as a direction indicator. When rates are trending above 70 percent, it tells you the balance of negotiating power sits with vendors. When they are below 55 percent, that balance shifts. It is context, not a guarantee.

If you are thinking about a spring campaign and want to understand how your property is likely to sit within the current market, that is a conversation worth having before you commit. More at danielgierach.com.
