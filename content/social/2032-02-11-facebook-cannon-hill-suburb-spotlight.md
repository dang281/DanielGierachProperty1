## Caption

Cannon Hill sits in a position that more people should pay attention to. It is one stop on the train from Morningside, has its own retail centre anchored by Cannon Hill Kmart and the adjoining dining strip, and it backs up against the Bulimba Creek corridor in a way that gives some sections of the suburb a genuine green buffer that buyers in denser inner suburbs would find difficult to access.

The housing stock is predominantly post-war timber, with enough land on the typical block to have attracted a consistent stream of renovators over the past 15 years. The streets running toward the creek, particularly around Wynnum Road and the blocks behind the Cannon Hill Anglican College precinct, have seen steady value appreciation as the suburb's identity sharpened. The school, which draws enrolments from across this part of the eastern corridor, is a regular reason buyers give for choosing Cannon Hill specifically over a suburb half a kilometre away.

For buyers in the 2032 market who are finding Morningside and Hawthorne at full stretch, Cannon Hill is the suburb that makes structural sense. The fundamentals are real, the amenity is established, and the price point still offers room.

As autumn opens, sellers in Cannon Hill are meeting a buyer pool that is increasingly deliberate about this suburb. That is a different conversation from 10 years ago.
