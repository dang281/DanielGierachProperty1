## Caption

The gross revenue from a short-term rental property in Brisbane's inner east can look significantly higher than the equivalent long-term rental yield. The net revenue after accounting for platform fees, professional cleaning between stays, specialist short-term rental insurance, and ongoing maintenance on a high-turnover property tells a different story.

Platform fees typically run between 15 and 20 percent of gross revenue. Professional cleaning at $150 to $250 per changeover, with multiple changeovers per week in a well-occupied property, accumulates quickly over a year. Insurance for a short-term rental is a specialist product at a premium well above a standard landlord policy. And maintenance on a property turning over weekly degrades faster than one with a stable tenancy.

When those costs are netted out, the return gap between a well-managed short-term rental and a high-quality long-term tenancy in the same inner east property is often narrower than investors expect. The short-term option carries significantly more operational complexity and time cost, or the cost of a management company to absorb that complexity, which itself reduces the net return further.

The article covers the full cost comparison with worked examples, the BCC compliance requirements for short-term rentals in Brisbane, and the questions investors should work through before choosing between the two strategies.
