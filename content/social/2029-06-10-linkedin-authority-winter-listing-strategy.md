## Caption

The question I get most often in June is whether winter is the right time to list. The honest answer is that it depends on the property and the vendor's situation, not on a general rule about seasons.

Brisbane winters are mild. The standard argument about cold weather deterring buyers does not apply here the way it does in Melbourne or Sydney. A well-presented inner east home in June will still attract motivated buyers, and those buyers are typically more purposeful than the weekend browsers common in spring.

Winter works best for certain property types. Character homes that are warm, well-lit, and cosy present exceptionally well in Brisbane's mild winter months. Properties with good artificial lighting, usable indoor entertaining areas, and a sense of homeliness often outperform expectations in June and July campaigns.

Winter is more challenging for properties that rely on outdoor lifestyle appeal: pool-focused marketing, alfresco entertaining areas, and expansive gardens tend to make a stronger impression from September to November. For those properties, the spring campaign window often produces better competition.

The other factor is vendor motivation. A vendor who is genuinely ready to sell in June, with a realistic price expectation and a well-prepared home, will almost always do better than a vendor who waits until spring and then rushes the preparation phase.

Readiness matters more than season.
