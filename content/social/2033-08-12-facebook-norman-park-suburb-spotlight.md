## Caption

Norman Park's spring preparation season is when the suburb's demand character becomes most visible. Buyers who have identified Norman Park as their target location and who have been watching through winter tend to arrive at September open homes with a clear sense of what they will pay and what they are comparing it to. That specificity of buyer intent produces the competitive open home conditions that well-prepared Norman Park vendors benefit from most.

Whites Hill Reserve continues to be the suburb's most distinctive amenity. A bushland reserve within walking distance of the CBD that offers mountain biking, walking trails, and genuine natural separation from the surrounding urban fabric is not something most inner east suburbs can claim. Buyers who value that access seek it out deliberately.

The Bennetts Road dining strip provides the neighbourhood dining character that buyers increasingly expect from their suburb. What was a modest collection of local eateries has become a genuine weekend destination, and the properties within walking distance of that strip reflect it in their comparable sales evidence.

Norman Park State School catchment properties continue to attract motivated family buyers at the spring market open. The August to October period is when that buyer pool activates most strongly, and vendors with catchment properties who are campaign-ready going into September are well positioned.
