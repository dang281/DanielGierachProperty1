## Caption

There is a micro-geography to Coorparoo that is worth understanding if you own here, because it affects how buyers price different streets and positions within the suburb.

The streets closest to Coorparoo State School command the tightest pricing and the most competitive buyer fields. Properties within comfortable walking distance of the school carry a premium that reflects the simple logic of school-run convenience. Further from the school, prices adjust, but the broader suburb catchment still applies, and buyers who are zoned in but a few streets further away accept that trade-off readily.

Coorparoo Square has added a retail and dining dimension that lifted the suburb's liveability profile. Old Cleveland Road bus access remains one of the most reliable public transport connections in the inner south-east. Those two infrastructure elements mean the suburb functions well for people who do not own a car, which is a buyer demographic that has grown in relevance over the past decade.

Pre-spring in August is the moment where vendors who are genuinely committed separate themselves from those who are still thinking about it. Buyers are motivated and supply is tightening ahead of spring. If you are in the school catchment and your property is ready to present, the conditions in August favour a vendor who is organised and decisive.
