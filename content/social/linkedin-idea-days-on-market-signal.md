## Caption

Days on market is the most readable signal in any property market, and most buyers and sellers do not pay enough attention to it.

A property that sells in under 21 days in Brisbane's inner east has usually attracted competitive interest. Multiple buyers attended, price expectations were realistic, and the campaign created urgency. A property still listed at 45 days or more is telling a different story. Either the price does not reflect the market, the property has a condition issue that buyers are walking away from, or the campaign strategy has not connected with the right buyer pool.

This matters for buyers because a property with 60+ days on market often has room to negotiate that a freshly listed property does not. Understanding why it has sat is the key question. If it is a price issue and the property itself is sound, that can be an advantage for a well-prepared buyer.

For sellers, days on market data on comparable properties is the most honest guide to where your price needs to be. If the properties that sold quickly were priced at a certain level, and the ones that sat were priced higher, the market has already given you the answer.

In the inner east, the gap between a well-received campaign and a slow one is usually a pricing conversation that needed to happen before launch, not after.
