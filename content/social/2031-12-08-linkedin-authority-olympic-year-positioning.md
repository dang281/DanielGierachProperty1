## Caption

The Olympic year starts in a few weeks. It is worth being clear about what it actually means for inner east property decisions and what it does not mean.

Three things I observe heading into 2032.

The first is about vendor timing. Vendors who are waiting for the Olympics to "arrive" before deciding to sell often find they have waited too long. By the time the Games are in full swing, the strongest buyer interest has usually already moved through the market in the months prior, when the attention was building and buyers were motivated to get settled before the event. Waiting for the opening ceremony is not a strategy.

The second is about fundamentals. The strongest positioning for an inner east property in 2032 is the same as in any year: accurate price guide, genuine preparation, honest presentation. Properties that are overpriced or underprepared do not get saved by Olympic attention. The buyers attending open homes in an Olympic year are no less discerning than any other year.

The third is about the buyer mix. Buyers who are drawn to Brisbane specifically because of the Olympics are a real segment but they are not the primary market. The inner east market in 2032 will still be driven mainly by local buyers upgrading, downsizing, and relocating. Interstate buyers are an additional layer, not the base. Decisions built around the idea of a flood of Olympic buyers are usually built on a thinner foundation than the people making them realise.
