## Caption

Flood overlay in Brisbane's inner east is one of the questions I get asked most consistently, from both buyers and vendors. It is also one of the most misunderstood considerations in the market.

The overlay is not a binary dealbreaker. It is not a signal that a property flooded in 2011 or 2022, nor is it a guarantee that it will flood in future. The overlay covers a range of risk categories, from properties that experienced inundation in major flood events to properties that carry a theoretical risk based on modelling but have no flood history at all. The distinction between those two situations matters enormously for price.

Properties outside the overlay carry a measurable premium in the inner east. That premium reflects genuine buyer preference and is unlikely to disappear. Properties within the overlay sell regularly and attract genuine buyer competition. The discount that applies to overlay properties varies considerably depending on three things: the floor level of the dwelling relative to the Q100 flood level, the elevation of the lot, and which subcategory of overlay applies.

A property on a high set with a habitable floor well above any modelled flood level in an overlay area presents very differently to a low-set on a flat lot in a regularly inundated area. Treating both as equivalent because the overlay notation appears on both property reports is a mistake that costs buyers and vendors real money.

Buyers who have done their research ask about this directly, and rightly so. Vendors who know their position clearly before a campaign launches are in a much stronger negotiating position than vendors who discover these details mid-negotiation.

If you own a property in an overlay area and are considering selling, knowing your specific position before appointing an agent is worth the time.
