## Caption

The pre-spring window is when the market signals tend to be clearest. Sellers who are serious about listing before Christmas are making their decisions now. The open home traffic picking up in August is not yet spring volume, but it is motivated volume.

What I am watching is the gap between listing price and sale price on the properties that have been sitting. In the inner east, a property that has been on the market for more than thirty days without an offer is almost always a price story, not a market story. The suburbs are not the problem. The pricing is.

The properties that are selling quickly are the ones priced off genuine comparable evidence and presented well. That has been true in every market I have operated in, and it remains true now. The ones sitting are invariably the ones where the seller's expectation and the buyer pool's evidence are not aligned.

The other thing I notice is that motivated sellers are increasingly willing to be realistic about price earlier in the campaign rather than waiting for the market to tell them. That is a mature response to a market that has more information available to buyers than at any previous point. Buyers arrive at inspections having already run the comparables. They know when a property is priced past its evidence.

If you are thinking about listing before the end of the year, August is the right time to have the conversation.
