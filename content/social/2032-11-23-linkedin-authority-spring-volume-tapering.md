## Caption

By the time late November arrives, the inner east property market has already changed shape. The volume of new listings coming to market drops noticeably from the mid-spring peak. Vendors who were going to list in spring largely have, and the next meaningful wave of stock will not appear until late January or February. That shift is predictable and it happens every year without exception.

For buyers still active right now, the practical effect is a narrowing of choice. There are fewer properties to inspect each weekend. But the buyers who remain in the market are not casual lookers, they are people with a genuine reason to purchase before Christmas. That sustained demand against thinning supply means well-located properties in the inner east continue to attract real competition even at this point in the calendar.

For sellers who are already listed, the picture is worth understanding clearly. Your buyer pool is smaller now than it was in October. But the buyers remaining are more decisive. They are not browsing. They have typically done months of research and are ready to act when the right property and the right price align. A vendor with honest pricing and a property presented in good condition has a genuine pathway to a result in the next few weeks.

This is not a worse market than spring. It is a different one. Fewer inspections, fewer competing properties, more focused buyers. The sellers who tend to struggle in this window are those still expecting spring-volume inquiry on a November timeline. Adjusting that expectation, not the price, is usually what opens the door.
