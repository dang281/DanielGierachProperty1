## Caption

A buyer's agent works exclusively for the buyer. Their job is to find, evaluate, and negotiate a property on behalf of someone who is either too time-poor, too unfamiliar with the market, or too emotionally invested to negotiate effectively themselves.

For buyers, the value proposition depends on the situation. Interstate buyers purchasing in a market they do not know well benefit significantly. First-home buyers who have not been through a negotiation before often find the fee worthwhile. Experienced local buyers who have done several transactions may not need one.

The fee structure varies. Most buyer's agents in Queensland charge either a flat fee or a percentage of the purchase price. On a $1.2M property, a 1.5% fee is $18,000. That fee needs to be weighed against what the buyer's agent actually achieves in negotiation and access, not just what they say they will achieve.

For sellers, understanding that your buyer may be represented changes how you approach negotiation. A buyer's agent who presents an offer is working to minimise the price their client pays. That is their job. Knowing who is actually on the other side of the negotiation, and what their brief is, is useful information for any vendor.

Buyer's agents do not have access to any information a diligent buyer could not find themselves. What they have is time, market experience, and emotional detachment. Whether those are worth the fee depends on the buyer.
