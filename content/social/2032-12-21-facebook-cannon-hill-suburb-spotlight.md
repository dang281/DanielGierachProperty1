## Caption

Summer in Cannon Hill has its own character. The character homes on the residential streets sit well in the heat, the rail line keeps the suburb connected to the city without the car dependence that weighs on outer areas, and Cannon Hill Anglican College continues to draw families who have thought carefully about their schooling options and made a deliberate choice.

What distinguishes Cannon Hill from some of its neighbours is that combination of rail access and genuine family infrastructure. Buyers here tend to be people who want a proper house with outdoor space, reasonable proximity to the city, and a school option they feel good about. That is a focused brief, and when a property fits it, buyers tend to act.

The week before Christmas is a quieter moment in most markets, but it is not an inactive one. Buyers who have been searching since winter often reach December with a sharpened sense of urgency. They know the calendar, they know that supply thins in January, and they are watching. Owners who list in this window often find the pool is smaller but the quality of inquiry is better. The casual browsers have dropped off; the people making contact are serious.

If you own in Cannon Hill and want to understand what your home would achieve in the current market, I am available to talk before the year ends.
