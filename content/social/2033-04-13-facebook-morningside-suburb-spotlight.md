## Caption

Morningside sits five kilometres from the CBD and occupies an interesting position in the Brisbane market: close enough to the city to attract inner-suburb buyers, yet priced accessibly enough to draw families who have been edged out of Norman Park or Hawthorne. That tension between the two buyer pools is part of what keeps demand in Morningside consistently active.

The suburb's elevated sections, particularly those above Wynnum Road, command a premium for the views and breezes they offer. Homes on the high ground attract buyers who are willing to pay for the lifestyle benefit of outlook and airflow in a Brisbane climate. Morningside State School has a solid reputation, and the school catchment is a genuine factor in purchasing decisions for families with primary-school-age children.

Turnover in the suburb has historically been reasonable, which means there is data to work with when pricing a home. The housing stock mixes original Queenslanders, post-war homes and some more recent infill development. The well-presented renovated character home remains the standout performer at auction and in private treaty campaigns.

Current medians in Morningside reflect the suburb's proximity premium relative to suburbs further south and east, while still offering a meaningful price advantage over the tightly held suburbs immediately north. Bus services along Wynnum Road connect residents to the CBD and to Cannon Hill.
