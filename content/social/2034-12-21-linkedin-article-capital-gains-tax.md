## Caption

The main residence exemption from capital gains tax is not automatic. If the property was ever used to produce income, partial CGT liability almost certainly applies.

The main residence exemption fully covers a property that has been your principal place of residence for the entire period of ownership and was never used to generate income.

If you rented the property for part of the ownership period, the exemption is calculated proportionally. The years it was rented attract CGT on the capital gain for that period.

Settlement date, not contract signing date, determines which financial year the capital gain falls into. In cases where the gain is large, settlement timing relative to 30 June can have a material effect on the tax outcome.
