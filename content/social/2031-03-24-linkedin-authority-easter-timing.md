## Caption

The week before Easter is typically the final concentrated push of the Brisbane autumn selling window. Buyers who have been active since February are aware that the break is coming, that travel and school holidays will disrupt their focus, and that the post-Easter market will feel different. That awareness concentrates buyer urgency in the days leading up to Good Friday in a way that agents working a late March campaign can use.

For vendors who launched in late February or early March and have a genuine buyer pool, the period between now and Easter is the time to close the gap. Buyers who have inspected twice and not yet made an offer often need a trigger. The approaching break, combined with a clear signal from the agent that competing buyers are still active, can be that trigger.

The post-Easter question, whether to wait until after Easter to launch, is one that vendors ask regularly. The honest answer depends on the property and the vendor's circumstances. The post-Easter market is not dead, but it is thinner than March. The buyer pool fragments between April and the winter slowdown, and the sense of urgency that characterises the February and March window dissipates. Properties that launch in mid to late April are competing for a smaller pool of buyers who are not under the same time pressure.

That does not mean post-Easter launches are wrong. For a vendor who is not yet ready, launching properly in May is better than launching underprepared in March. But if preparation is in order, the window before Easter is worth targeting.
