## Caption

Settlement timing is one of the most overlooked negotiating points in a property transaction.

Sellers often have a strong preference for settlement timing, particularly when they are simultaneously buying. Buyers, especially those with finance conditions, often need more time than they initially indicate. Bridging these preferences is part of what an experienced agent manages during the negotiation.

A 30-day settlement benefits sellers who need to transact quickly and buyers who have unconditional finance. It is practical and clean but leaves almost no buffer for anything to go wrong.

A 45 to 60-day period is the most common in Queensland and works well for most situations. It gives both parties time to arrange finance, plan a move, and manage the practical realities of changing homes.

A 90-day or longer settlement suits sellers who are looking for their next property and need time to secure it before they hand over the keys. In a competitive inner east market, finding and buying the next property in 45 days can be stressful.

When selling your home, what settlement period would you prefer?
