## Caption

Seven weeks after the Brisbane 2032 Olympics closed, the inner east property market looks materially different from what it did in August.

Open home attendance has been rebuilding steadily through September and is now genuinely active. The buyers who put their search on hold during July and August, whether because of the Games calendar, the disruption to normal life around the venue precincts, or simply the noise of a global event in the city, have come back. Most of them came back with pre-finance in place and a clear sense of what they want.

What I am seeing on the ground is a buyer pool that is purposeful. These are not casual lookers. At well-priced homes in Bulimba, Balmoral, Camp Hill and Norman Park, the follow-up after open homes has been fast. Buyers are making second-visit requests within 24 hours. Qualified inquiry is coming from people who have been watching specific streets and property types for months.

The properties that are attracting genuine competition right now share a few consistent characteristics: good land content, honest presentation, and pricing that reflects actual comparable sales rather than aspirational numbers from two years ago. The buyers in this market know what they are looking at and they have seen enough campaigns to recognise when a price is realistic.

Spring in the inner east has its own rhythm. October is typically the strongest month for motivated buyer volume, and this year that pattern is holding. The post-Olympic rebound has run into what would have been a strong spring market regardless. The combination is producing a meaningful level of buyer activity across the precinct.

If you are considering a sale before Christmas, the motivated buyer pool you are competing for is active right now.
