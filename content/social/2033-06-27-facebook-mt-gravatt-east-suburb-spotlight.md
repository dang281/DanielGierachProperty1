## Caption

Mt Gravatt East holds a position in Brisbane's property market that is driven by a clear value argument. The suburb is ten kilometres from the CBD, practical to live in, well-served by retail along Cavendish Road and priced at a meaningful discount to inner east suburbs with comparable land sizes. For buyers who have been working through their options and comparing what their budget buys across different postcodes, Mt Gravatt East often ends up looking like the most rational answer.

The TAFE Queensland campus in the area contributes to the rental market and ensures there is a consistent pool of tenants for investors who purchase in the suburb. Alongside the family rental market that the suburb's good school options and practical layout support, that institutional demand gives investors a reasonable foundation for their purchase decisions. Owner-occupiers and investors are both active in Mt Gravatt East, which keeps the market liquid.

The housing stock still includes a meaningful number of original post-war homes that have not been substantially updated. These attract buyers at the more affordable end of the market, as well as developers and renovators who see the opportunity to add value. The renovated segment has grown, reflecting investment in the suburb that has tracked its improving profile over recent years. Current medians show a market that has moved but still offers a genuine comparison with inner east alternatives.
