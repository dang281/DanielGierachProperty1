## Caption

The most common misunderstanding I encounter with vendors who have a mortgage is how they calculate their net proceeds from a sale.

The instinct is to subtract the loan balance from the sale price and treat the difference as what they walk away with. That calculation is missing several line items that reduce the figure materially.

Agent commission on a $1.2 million sale at a standard rate runs to approximately $18,000 to $24,000. Conveyancing costs add $1,500 to $3,000. Marketing costs for a well-run campaign, depending on the package and the digital and print components, add another $4,000 to $8,000. Mortgage discharge fees, which most lenders charge when you pay out a home loan, add $300 to $500. If you are on a fixed-rate loan, a break cost may apply that can run to several thousand dollars depending on how far through the fixed term you are and how rates have moved.

Before calculating how much equity you will have to deploy into your next purchase, add up those costs. On a $1.2 million sale, the real cost of selling runs to $24,000 to $36,000 or more. That is money that does not transfer to your next deposit.

For anyone who is on a fixed-rate loan, the break cost conversation with your lender should happen before you commit to a launch date. Not after you have signed the agency agreement. The number can change the entire timing of the campaign decision.
