## Caption

The third open home of a spring campaign tells me more about a seller's position than any conversation with the vendor.

By week two, the curious buyers have had their look. What remains on the register are people who have already inspected two or three comparable properties in the same suburb. They are comparing floor plans in their heads. They have looked at the sold data. They know the street.

In October, this type of buyer moves faster than at any other time of year. Not because the market is hot or cold, but because the preparation window is closing. Anyone who wants to settle before Christmas needs to sign a contract in the next four to six weeks. That timeline is real and it concentrates minds.

This changes how I read a campaign. Twelve groups at a week-two open home is a different story to twelve groups at a week-four open home. Volume matters less than timing inside the campaign.

I see this same pattern in Bulimba, Hawthorne, Camp Hill, and Norman Park every October. The buyers showing up in spring are not browsing. They have already spoken to their mortgage broker.

If you are thinking about listing in the next few months, the lead time between now and a spring campaign is tighter than most sellers expect. Preparation, styling, photography, and marketing take four to six weeks before day one.

Worth a conversation before the window closes.
