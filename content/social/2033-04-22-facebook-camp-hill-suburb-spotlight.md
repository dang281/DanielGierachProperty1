## Caption

Camp Hill's elevated position, around six kilometres from the CBD, gives the suburb a physical advantage that buyers recognise and pay for. Homes on the higher ridges capture views and breezes that make a real difference in a Brisbane summer, and that natural asset underpins pricing in the streets that benefit most from the elevation.

The suburb has become a natural progression point for families who have outgrown Coorparoo. Those buyers already know the broader area, they are familiar with the school catchments, and they are willing to pay the step-up in price to get more space or a better position. That pattern of move-up buying keeps demand in Camp Hill consistently present, even when the broader market softens.

Stock levels tend to be tight, partly because the suburb is popular and partly because many owners are long-term residents who are not in a hurry to leave. When well-presented homes do come to market, they draw genuine competition. Current medians in Camp Hill reflect its status as a suburb that has continued to appreciate over time, supported by the quality of its housing stock and its proximity to Coorparoo's café and retail strip without being in the middle of it.

The suburb is bus-dependent for public transport to the CBD, but road access is straightforward via Old Cleveland Road and surrounding arterials.
