## Caption

Every open home on a tenanted property requires 24 hours written notice to the tenant. That is Queensland law. It is not optional, and it cannot be waived by agreement between the landlord and the agent.

A spring campaign with weekly open homes means weekly written notices, each with the correct timing and format. If a tenant declines to allow access for a given inspection, the open home cannot proceed. If a tenant is uncooperative, you can have the best campaign in the world and still lose weeks of buyer access.

The conversation that matters most before launching a campaign on a tenanted property is not with your agent. It is with your property manager. Specifically: how is the tenancy going? Is the tenant likely to cooperate? When does the lease end? Are they likely to want to stay or move on? Is the property being maintained in a condition that photographs well?

The answers to those questions shape the entire campaign strategy. A cooperative tenant who is happy to have the property presented and inspected weekly is an entirely workable situation. A tenant on a fixed lease who is unhappy about the sale and unwilling to cooperate is a campaign problem that needs a plan before the first open home, not after.

Investors who are thinking about selling while the property is tenanted should have this conversation with their property manager, ideally a few months before they plan to list.
