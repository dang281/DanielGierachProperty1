## Caption

Morningside has a buyer profile that is broader than most sellers expect, and in November that breadth works in a vendor's favour.

The suburb draws from three distinct groups at this time of year. First are the investor buyers, steady in Morningside through all seasons because the rental demand along the Wynnum Road corridor is structural and not dependent on market sentiment. Second are the young families who have been priced out of Bulimba and have spent several months working through the northern streets here. Third are the downsizers from within the inner east who want a manageable home and a walkable neighbourhood without moving to the outer ring.

Each of those buyer types has a pre-Christmas motivation. The investor wants a settled purchase before the calendar flips. The family wants to be in the catchment for the school year starting in late January. The downsizer has made a decision and wants it resolved.

The gentrification that has been reshaping Morningside's streetscapes over the past several years has created a suburb where a renovated character home on a decent block sits alongside post-war original builds that hold strong land value. Sellers in both product categories are in a position where November buyer activity is higher than the quieter July and August months by some margin.

If your Morningside property has been sitting as a consideration rather than a plan, November is the month to make the move.
