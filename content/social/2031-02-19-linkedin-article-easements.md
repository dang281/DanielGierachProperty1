## Caption

Most buyers cannot tell the difference between a drainage easement along the rear boundary and an access easement through the middle of the yard. The difference at resale time is material.

Both show up on a title search. Neither is explained in the listing. The agent is not always across the practical implications, and the building inspector is not looking at the title. The result is that buyers sometimes purchase a property with an easement they do not fully understand, and discover the significance later when they want to build, extend, or sell.

A drainage easement along the rear fence is usually a manageable constraint. It restricts what can be constructed over or across it, but the land is otherwise usable. A utility or council drainage easement through the body of a property is a different consideration entirely. An access easement that benefits an adjoining lot or grants rights to a utility company over a meaningful section of the property is different again. The practical effect on what you can build, where you can build it, and how a future buyer will value the land depends entirely on the type of easement, its location, and the rights it creates.

Queensland title searches contain the detail, but reading them without knowing what to look for produces a false sense of clarity. Your solicitor should be identifying every registered interest on the title and explaining the practical implication of each one before you exchange contracts.
