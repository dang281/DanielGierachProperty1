## Caption

Most vendors have bought property before. Fewer have sold it, and even fewer understand what a well-run sales campaign actually looks like from the inside.

A campaign is not a single moment. It is a sequence of decisions and events spread over eight to twelve weeks, starting well before the first open home.

The preparation phase comes first: property presentation, professional photography, building and pest inspection if required for vendor disclosure, pricing strategy discussions, and method of sale. Vendors who treat preparation as a short step before the real work begins often get results that reflect that decision.

The listing phase follows: the property goes live on the major portals, the agent's buyer database is contacted directly, the first weekend open homes are held. The first two weekends are the highest-traffic period of any campaign. Buyer attention peaks at launch and does not return at the same level later.

Offers, negotiation, and contract execution come next. In a competitive campaign, multiple parties may be negotiating simultaneously. In a slower market, the agent's work is to maintain vendor confidence and buyer engagement until the right offer arrives.

Once a contract is signed, the due diligence period begins: finance condition, building and pest condition, and any other special conditions agreed in the contract. Satisfying those conditions moves the property to unconditional status and sets the path to settlement.
