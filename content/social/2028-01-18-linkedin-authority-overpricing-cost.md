## Caption

The cost of overpricing a property is rarely visible on the day you set the price.

It shows up three to four weeks later, when the enquiry has dried up, the price reduction conversation starts, and everyone who was watching the listing knows it sat. Days on market is public information. Buyers notice when a property has been on for six weeks and the price has dropped twice. Their opening position adjusts accordingly.

In the inner east, the first two weeks of a campaign are when a well-priced property attracts its strongest buyers. That window does not come back once it is gone. A price reduction brings in a different buyer, at a different level of motivation, in a different negotiating position.

The properties I see sell for the strongest outcomes are not always the best presented. They are the ones where the price was set correctly from the start, the campaign was properly resourced, and the seller was ready to move when the right buyer showed up.

Price guides get set in the first meeting. The outcomes they produce play out over the following six weeks.
