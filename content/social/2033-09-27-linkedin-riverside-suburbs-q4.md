## Caption

The riverside corridor from Balmoral through Hawthorne and into Bulimba is worth reading as its own market rather than as a component of the broader inner east. The buyer profile, the price points, and the competitive dynamics in these suburbs have specific characteristics that become visible in the spring data.

The buyers who pursue Hawthorne and Bulimba are, by a significant margin, families who want proximity to the river, access to the Oxford Street and Bulimba Riverside dining and lifestyle precinct, and school access to Bulimba State School or Balmoral State High. That demand profile is consistent across market cycles. It does not disappear in winter. It intensifies in spring when more stock gives the buyer pool something to compete over.

Balmoral is a suburb where the character housing stock, pre-war Queenslanders in particular, commands a premium that is detached from the suburb's price per square metre story. A well-positioned, well-maintained character home in Balmoral attracts a buyer who has been looking for that specific combination for a period measured in months, not weeks. When that buyer appears, they are motivated and they know what the comparable sales evidence looks like.

Heading into Q4 2033, the riverside corridor is positioned for the campaigns that prepared through August to benefit from September and October buyer activity before the December market slowdown.
