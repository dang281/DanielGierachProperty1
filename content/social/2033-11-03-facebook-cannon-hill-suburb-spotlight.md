## Caption

Cannon Hill sits in a position that buyers from neighbouring suburbs are starting to pay more attention to, and the reason is straightforward: it offers most of what Bulimba and Morningside deliver at a price point that still has room.

Cannon Hill Plaza anchors the suburb's daily convenience, covering grocery, dining, and services in a format that removes the need for regular trips further afield. The Doomben Line provides direct rail access to Brisbane CBD, and with stations at both Cannon Hill and Murarrie on the same line, residents have a genuinely workable commuter option without needing a car. That combination of local amenity and rail access is not universal across the inner east, and buyers who prioritise both notice it.

The emerging Morningside Racecourse mixed-use precinct adds a long-term layer to the suburb's appeal. As that development matures, the walkable lifestyle options within proximity of Cannon Hill will expand, which matters to buyers thinking about the suburb's trajectory over a five to ten year period rather than just its current state.

Cannon Hill State School sits within the suburb and draws families who want that primary catchment without the competition for properties that comes with the most sought-after inner east schools. The buyer profile here tends to be families and owner-occupiers who have done the comparison work, understand what they are getting relative to the price, and are ready to act when the right property appears.
