## Caption

Off-market sales are a regular feature of the Brisbane inner east. An agent calls with a buyer ready to go, no campaign required, settle quickly, no open homes. For a vendor who values privacy and convenience, it sounds compelling.

The trade-off is buyer competition, and limiting buyer competition almost always affects price.

A property that sells off-market transacts with one buyer. That buyer's offer reflects what they are prepared to pay without competition. A property that runs a proper campaign and attracts three or four serious buyers will almost always produce a higher result, because competitive buyers commit to their best number rather than an opening position.

The gap between an off-market result and a campaigned result in the inner east regularly runs to tens of thousands of dollars on a standard property. On a riverfront home or a large character block, it can be significantly more.

There are scenarios where off-market makes sense. A vendor with genuine privacy concerns, a property with circumstances that make a campaign difficult, or a case where the vendor has a long-standing relationship with a buyer and the price is agreed at a level both parties find fair. These are real situations.

What they are not is an instruction from an agent that "we have a buyer ready to go." That is a service to the agent's buyer, not to you as the vendor. The question to ask is: what price does this buyer become competitive?
