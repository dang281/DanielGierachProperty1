## Caption

First impressions at a property are formed in about four seconds. The garden, the facade, the letterbox, whether the paint looks fresh. Most buyers walk through the gate having already made a preliminary call before they open the front door.

Whether that first impression is enough to move the needle on price is a more contested question. Plenty of experienced buyers have trained themselves to look past it. Plenty of vendors spend money on landscaping and repainting on the theory that it returns more than it costs.

Curious where people in this network actually sit.

How much does street appeal factor into your decision when you are buying a property?

## Poll options

- It is one of my top three factors
- Important, but secondary to the house itself
- I would buy an ugly facade if the bones were right
- I tend to discount it and focus on the numbers
