## Caption

Cannon Hill sits at the quieter end of Brisbane's inner east corridor, and that relative understatement is precisely what draws a particular kind of buyer.

The suburb is bordered by Morningside to the north and Carina to the south, and its residential streets give little away from the outside. But the fundamentals are solid. The elevated sections above Kianawah Road and Creek Road offer genuine city and district views. Cannon Hill State School and the nearby Cannon Hill Anglican College draw families who want school access without the price premium attached to the Whites Hill or Coorparoo State School catchments. The proximity to the Wynnum Road arterial and the Cannon Hill train station means commute options are practical.

Autumn is when Cannon Hill tends to see its clearest buyer activity. Pre-approved buyers who have watched the inner east market through 2030 arrive at open homes in February with a clear sense of what they can get at each price point. Cannon Hill consistently appeals to the buyer who has looked at Morningside, done the maths, and found that the value sits one suburb south.

Supply in Cannon Hill remains tight relative to demand, particularly for character homes in the elevated pockets. Vendors who list well-prepared properties in February tend to attract competitive attention.

If you own in Cannon Hill and are thinking about the 2031 autumn window, now is the right time to understand what your property is worth.
