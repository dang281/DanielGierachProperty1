## Caption

March is the highest-volume listing month of the year across Brisbane's inner east. Morningside, Norman Park, Camp Hill, Coorparoo, and Bulimba all see their largest influx of new properties in the first three weekends of March. Vendors who have been preparing since January now go to market. The field fills quickly.

What that means for buyers: more choice than at any other point in the year, but also a shorter window to be decisive. Properties that are well-presented and competitively positioned generate multiple-buyer dynamics fast. Buyers who spend two weekends attending opens before making a decision often find that a property they wanted has exchanged unconditionally.

What that means for vendors: the rising supply of competing listings means differentiation becomes critical. A property that might have attracted a small pool of buyers in February, when the field was thin, now needs to stand out in a larger group. Presentation, pricing, and campaign strategy all need to be sharper, not looser, as listing numbers increase.

Campaign pacing in March tends to compress. Buyers who have been watching since January are motivated and ready. Offer conversations that might have taken three weeks in February can move to exchange in ten days when the buyer has done their research across multiple properties and knows what they want.

The pattern I consistently see: vendors who are surprised by the speed of a March result are the ones who did not expect the autumn peak to be as deep as it is. Vendors who plan for it come out of the campaign with clarity and confidence.
