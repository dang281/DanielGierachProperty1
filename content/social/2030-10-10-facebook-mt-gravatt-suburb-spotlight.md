## Caption

Mt Gravatt is anchored by the Westfield Garden City complex but it is defined by what lies on the residential streets beyond the retail precinct. The elevated land around Bunny Street and Nindoomalha Road gives buyers the position, the block size, and the natural light that the lower-lying suburbs in this corridor cannot offer.

The buyer profile in Mt Gravatt is broad. Families targeting Mansfield State High School drive consistent demand in the catchment zone. Investors looking for rental returns at a price point below the inner east prestige suburbs find yields that work. Downsizers from larger nearby properties see the suburb as a practical and familiar move.

The Westfield access is a genuine lifestyle factor. Buyers who work from home value proximity to full-service retail, food, and services within walking or a short drive. That convenience factor is reflected in open home feedback consistently.

Mt Gravatt has benefited from the southern corridor infrastructure investment over the past several years, with improved public transport links connecting the suburb to the CBD and the broader inner east network. That infrastructure improvement has been reflected in sustained buyer demand.

For homeowners on the elevated streets of Mt Gravatt, spring through October and November is historically the most active buyer period.
