## Caption

The conventional wisdom in Brisbane is that winter is slow. For some parts of the market, that is true. For the inner east, it is more nuanced, and the nuance matters for anyone making a decision about what to do between June and August.

Winter reduces listing volumes. That is real. Fewer properties come to market in the inner east during June, July, and August than in spring or autumn. But buyer pools don't disappear. The buyers who are still active in winter are often the most committed and the best qualified. They are not browsing. They have been looking for months, they have missed out on properties, and they want to buy. When they find the right property, they move.

This creates a specific dynamic that benefits a certain type of vendor. If you have a well-prepared property in a suburb like Hawthorne or Bulimba and you can bring it to market in June or July, you face far less competition from other listings than you would in September. You are not dividing the buyer pool with twenty comparable properties. You may have two or three.

What winter is also good for is preparation. If spring is the target, June is when the decisions should be made. Choosing the agent, getting the property assessed, planning any pre-campaign works, briefing the stylist. The vendors who do this work in winter arrive at spring in a fundamentally better position than those who start thinking about it in August.
