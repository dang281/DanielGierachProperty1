## Caption

The buyer profile in the inner east shifts through October in a way that matters to sellers who are deciding whether to launch now or wait.

October buyers are predominantly upgrade buyers and local downsizers who have been watching the market since the start of the year. They have a good read on what comparable properties have sold for. They are not guessing at prices. They are working from evidence.

What this means in practice: the October buyer is harder to stretch beyond comparable evidence than the spring buyer of a couple of years ago. They will not pay a premium just because there are other people at the open home. They will pay a premium for a property that is genuinely better than its comparable evidence: better aspect, better renovation quality, better street position.

The interstate buyer is also present in October in the inner east, more so than in winter. Families who are relocating for a January school start have a firm timeline. They need to be under contract by November to have any confidence in a December or January settlement. That buyer has real urgency, and they tend to be decisive.

Knowing who your likely buyer is before the campaign launches informs how the property is presented, how it is priced, and how the negotiation is managed. That is a conversation worth having at the start, not partway through.
