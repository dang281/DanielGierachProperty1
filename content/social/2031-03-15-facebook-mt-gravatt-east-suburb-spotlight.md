## Caption

Mt Gravatt East has two distinct buyer conversations running simultaneously, and understanding both is what separates well-advised vendors from those who leave money on the table.

The first conversation is about Cavendish Road State School. The catchment for Cavendish Road State High School pulls a consistent stream of family buyers to Mt Gravatt East, specifically to the streets that fall within the zone. These buyers are not negotiating on school access as an amenity. For them, it is the primary criterion of the purchase, and it anchors their decision in a way that makes them less price-sensitive than the comparable buyer in a non-catchment suburb.

The second conversation is about Westfield Garden City. The shopping centre sits at the western boundary of the suburb and provides the day-to-day infrastructure that makes Mt Gravatt East practical. Combined with the Griffith University campus nearby, the suburb has an institutional density that supports a secondary buyer type: the investor and the first-time buyer looking for a suburb with structural demand drivers that extend beyond the family market.

The elevated streets in Mt Gravatt East, running toward the ridge with district and bushland views, give the suburb a lifestyle credential that the catchment conversation sometimes overshadows. Buyers who discover those streets often find the suburb makes more sense than they initially assumed.

Autumn is when the catchment buyers in particular are most active and most decisive.
