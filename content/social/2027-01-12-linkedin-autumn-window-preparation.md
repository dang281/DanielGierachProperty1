## Caption

Brisbane's autumn selling window runs roughly from mid-February to the end of April. In the inner east, it is consistently the period where the strongest results happen. The combination of cooler weather, serious buyers back from the holiday period, and low competition from other listings creates conditions that favour sellers.

Eight weeks from now puts us at early March. Which means the preparation window for an autumn campaign is open right now, and it will close faster than most sellers expect.

Here is what eight weeks allows. Time to have a proper conversation about pricing and strategy without being rushed. Time to sort the pre-listing work: minor repairs, staging consultation, photos, and the legal documents your conveyancer needs to prepare. Time for your agent to build awareness among the buyers already registered in the suburb before the campaign launches publicly.

What eight weeks does not allow is a decision-making process that takes four weeks, followed by three weeks of preparation, leaving one week before the campaign needs to launch. That sequence produces reactive decisions and rushed preparation.

Sellers who are thinking about autumn 2027 and want to give themselves the best chance of a strong result should be having conversations with their agent in January.

I am talking to several owners in Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, and Camp Hill right now about what a February or March campaign would look like. If you are in a similar position, reach out.
