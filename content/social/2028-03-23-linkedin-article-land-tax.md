## Caption

Land tax in Queensland catches investors by surprise more often than it should.

The calculation is based on the unimproved land value of all taxable Queensland land you own, combined. Your primary residence is exempt. Investment properties are not. And as inner-east land values have risen significantly over the past several years, investors who were comfortably below the tax-free threshold a few years ago may now be above it without realising.

The Queensland land tax thresholds are not indexed to land value growth. They were set at specific dollar amounts and have not moved at the same pace as the market. What this means in practice: investors who have held inner-east properties for five to ten years may have crossed the threshold as a result of capital growth alone, not because they bought more property.

The notification when you owe land tax comes from the Queensland Revenue Office as an assessment, not in advance. The first sign many investors have that they are in the land tax zone is an unexpected bill.

Getting ahead of this is straightforward if you know to look. I have written a full breakdown of how Queensland land tax is calculated, who it applies to, and what to check.
