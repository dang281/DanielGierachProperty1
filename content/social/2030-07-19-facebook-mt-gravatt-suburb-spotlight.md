## Caption

Mt Gravatt's Logan Road precinct has quietly become one of the more complete lifestyle strips in Brisbane's inner south-east. The café culture, restaurant options, and retail variety there have improved substantially, and Griffith University's Nathan campus sitting adjacent to the suburb brings a consistent population of students, academics, and professional staff who need housing nearby. That resident base supports the local economy and gives the suburb a vitality that is noticeable on a Saturday morning.

The buyer profile in Mt Gravatt is broader than in some of its inner east neighbours. You have families drawn by the school catchments, investors who understand the university-driven rental demand, and apartment buyers who want the lifestyle access that Logan Road provides without the house price. That multi-segment demand is a genuine asset for sellers: your property is being considered by more than one type of buyer at any given time.

Long-term capital growth in Mt Gravatt has been a consistent story, underpinned by that infrastructure position and the dual residential and rental markets running alongside each other. For owners who have held here for a number of years, the equity position is often stronger than they realise.
