## Caption

The common assumption is that nothing happens in real estate between Christmas and New Year. In Brisbane's inner east, that is not entirely accurate.

The market does thin out. Open home volumes drop, casual enquiry slows, and the majority of buyers are genuinely pausing. But within that quieter period, there is a specific category of buyer who is still very much active: the relocator, the buyer under contract pressure who needs to settle before a particular date, and the person who has just made a decision over the Christmas break and is ready to act on it.

These buyers are not browsing. They are often making decisions with limited competition from other buyers. The market is thin, but the intent is high. A vendor with a property on the market in this window who has a motivated, qualified buyer in front of them is in a strong position.

January typically sees the first wave of genuine 2030 buyer activity before the broader spring planning takes hold. That makes the pre-January period useful for vendors who want to be ready for that first wave rather than scrambling to prepare once it arrives.
