## Caption

Mt Gravatt's elevated plateau position is a feature that becomes particularly apparent in June, when the cooler, clearer winter days open up the views that the summer haze reduces. The properties on the higher sections of the suburb with northerly or westerly aspects receive the winter sun in a way that makes the indoor-outdoor living spaces functional throughout the cooler months, not just in the Queensland warmth of spring and summer.

The Cavendish Road State High School catchment is the secondary school anchor for the suburb. Families who have been living in Mt Gravatt with younger children, and who have been watching the catchment position closely as their children approach secondary school age, represent a segment of the buyer market that is deeply reluctant to leave. The desire to remain in the catchment rather than purchase elsewhere is a consistent force that supports the suburb's pricing through market cycles.

The Westfield Garden City proximity is practical rather than aspirational. The centre's scale means that Mt Gravatt residents are not making a significant journey to access major retail, medical services, or specialty shopping, and the frequency with which residents use it means the centre functions as a genuine neighbourhood amenity rather than a regional destination they visit occasionally.

The winter market in Mt Gravatt tends to see fewer casual browsers and more purposeful buyers. For vendors with a property that meets the brief of a Cavendish Road High catchment buyer, winter campaigns can deliver concentrated buyer attention rather than broad but shallow enquiry.
