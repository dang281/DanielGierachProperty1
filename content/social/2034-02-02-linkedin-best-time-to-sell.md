## Caption

Most Brisbane vendors assume spring is the best time to sell. The data from the inner east tells a more complicated story.

Buyer demand in Morningside, Norman Park, Camp Hill, and Bulimba does not disappear in February. What actually changes with the seasons is the volume of competing listings, not the number of active buyers. A well-prepared campaign in February is competing against fewer properties than the same campaign in October.

The vendors who achieve the strongest results are not the ones who timed the calendar perfectly. They are the ones whose preparation was complete before the campaign launched. That means styling done, maintenance finished, marketing assets ready, and price strategy calibrated. Buyers in any month of the year respond to a property that is clearly ready for a decision.

Waiting for spring to arrive when everything else is already done often means holding a prepared property for three to six months while a motivated buyer, who is looking right now, buys something else.

The full article steps through how to assess the right time based on your own preparation timeline, not the season.
