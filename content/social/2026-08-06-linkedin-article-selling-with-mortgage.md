## Caption

The part that catches most Brisbane vendors off guard is not the discharge process itself. It's not calculating their net proceeds accurately before they've committed to what comes next.

The common mistake: treating net equity as sale price minus remaining loan balance. That number looks good on a spreadsheet. The real number, after agent commission, conveyancing fees, and the mortgage discharge, can look materially different. Discharge fees on a variable loan are typically $150 to $400 and easy to absorb. The figure that can genuinely change the calculus is a fixed-rate break cost. Depending on the rate differential and how much time remains on the fixed term, break costs can run into the tens of thousands. Not every vendor checks this before they're in contract.

Simultaneous settlement, where you sell and buy on the same day, adds another layer. The timing has to be coordinated carefully between your conveyancer, lender, and the other side's solicitor. When it works it's efficient. When it doesn't, you're either bridging or delaying.

The article I've linked covers how discharge fees are calculated, what triggers significant fixed-rate break costs, and how to model your actual net position before you make any decisions about your next purchase. If you're carrying a mortgage and thinking about selling in the next six months, this is worth reading before you commit to anything.
