## Caption

Most vendors say price matters most when selling their home. In practice, the answer shifts the moment a life event or a deadline enters the picture.

A vendor selling because they have exchanged on a purchase they love will tolerate a longer campaign if it produces the right price. A vendor selling because they have accepted a job in another city, or need to settle an estate, or has already committed to a retirement move, will start to reassign value to speed as the deadline approaches. Both positions are rational. Neither is wrong.

What I observe is that vendors who are honest with themselves about where they sit on this spectrum before the campaign starts make better decisions throughout the process. They respond to offers differently. They assess price guides more clearly. They do not hold out for a number that their actual situation does not give them the time to chase.

The campaign strategy should reflect the vendor's real position, not an idealised one. That conversation happens before the sign goes up.

When selling your home, which matters more?
