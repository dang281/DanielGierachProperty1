## Caption

The conventional advice to sellers is to list in autumn or spring, when buyer numbers are highest and competition drives prices up. But from a buyer's point of view, that same advice flips. Winter in Brisbane means fewer competing buyers at inspections, and vendors who are on the market during a quieter period are often there for a reason. Motivated sellers, less competition, and more room to negotiate. The trade-off is less choice. Spring brings more stock but also more competition for the good ones. Some buyers are entirely driven by when the right property appears, regardless of season. Where do your instincts sit? In Brisbane, when is the better time to buy?
