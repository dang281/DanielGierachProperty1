## Caption

Every vendor wants the best possible result from their campaign. Most have a strong view on what will get them there. The answer to this question often determines whether they achieve it.

Presentation and styling matters because buyers make emotional decisions quickly, and a property that looks right in photography and on inspection creates competition. Marketing reach matters because a campaign that does not find all the buyers in the pool is leaving money on the table. Reserve and asking price strategy matters because an unrealistic price guide reduces the number of buyers who will step forward. Choosing the right agent and sale method matters because execution determines how the competitive dynamics of a campaign are managed.

None of these factors operates in isolation. A beautifully styled property with weak marketing and a wrong price strategy will underperform. A well-priced property with the right sale method and average presentation will outperform expectations in a competitive pool.

The vendors who achieve the strongest results in autumn 2034 will be the ones who get all four of these right, not just the one they believe in most strongly.

In a strong autumn market, what is the most important factor for getting the best result when selling?

## Poll options

- Presentation and styling (how the property looks)
- The marketing reach and campaign strategy
- The reserve price or asking price strategy
- Choosing the right agent and sale method
