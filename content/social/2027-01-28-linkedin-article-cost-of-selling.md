## Caption

Most sellers approach the appraisal conversation with a number in their head. They know what they want to walk away with. The gap between that number and what they actually net after costs is often a surprise.

Selling a property in Brisbane involves costs that are easy to underestimate. Agent commission. Marketing spend. Conveyancing. If the property is tenanted, a potential lease break. If there is a mortgage, a discharge fee and any break costs. Styling if you use a stylist. Repairs identified during pre-sale preparation.

Added up, selling costs in Brisbane typically sit between 2.5% and 4% of the sale price, depending on the marketing campaign, the agent fee, and the individual property situation.

On a $1.4 million sale, that is between $35,000 and $56,000 in costs before you receive the proceeds. A seller who walks into an appraisal expecting to net $1.4M is in a different position to one who starts from a sale price of $1.4M and works backwards.

This matters for property decisions. If you are selling to buy, the net proceeds determine what you can spend on the next purchase. Getting that calculation right before you set your expectations protects against disappointment later.

I have written a breakdown of what the full cost of selling looks like in Brisbane, including where the costs sit and where there is room to negotiate.
