## Caption

With six weeks left in 2034, the pattern of what worked for inner east sellers this year is becoming clear.

The sellers who achieved the strongest results shared a few characteristics. They began preparation earlier than they thought they needed to. They had an accurate read on their buyer pool before the campaign launched. They priced to attract competition rather than to leave room for negotiation. And they chose a sale method that matched the specific appeal of their property.

The sellers who achieved average or below-average results typically had one element out of place. Most often it was pricing: starting above the range that buyers in their suburb had already mapped from comparable sales, then reducing mid-campaign when momentum had already slowed.

The suburb-specific outcomes within the inner east have been notably varied this year. Properties in Hawthorne and Balmoral have continued to attract competitive buyer pools. Camp Hill and Norman Park have seen strong results for well-presented character homes. Murarrie and Seven Hills have benefited from buyers who were priced out of the more central suburbs.

If you are planning to sell in 2035, the most useful thing you can do before the end of 2034 is get an accurate read on what your property is worth in the current market and what preparation it needs. That conversation costs nothing and changes how you approach the next six to twelve months.
