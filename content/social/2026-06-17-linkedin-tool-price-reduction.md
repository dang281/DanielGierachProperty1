## Caption

Yesterday's poll asked what a seller should do first after four weeks on the market with no real interest. The vote has tended to split between holding the price and adjusting it down, which mirrors the conversation most sellers have with their agent at that point.

The useful frame here is to separate two questions that often get confused. The first is whether the property is wrong for the buyer pool. The second is whether the price is wrong for the buyer pool that has seen it.

By week four, almost every genuinely interested buyer who would have responded at the current price has already inspected. The portals send saved-search alerts the moment a property lists, and engaged buyers move quickly. So if four weeks of inspections have not produced an offer that respects asking, the issue is rarely that the right buyer has not seen the listing yet. The issue is usually that the buyers who have seen it have priced it differently to the seller.

There is a tool that helps work through the decision rationally rather than emotionally. It looks at where the property sits against comparable sales, the feedback collected so far, and what changes in the buyer pool a price adjustment of a given size would unlock.
