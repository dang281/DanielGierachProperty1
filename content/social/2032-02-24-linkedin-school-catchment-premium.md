## Caption

School catchment premiums in Brisbane are not a theory. They are a measurable price difference between comparable properties on either side of a catchment boundary, and in the inner east they are significant enough to show up clearly in the data.

The Bulimba State School, Coorparoo Secondary College, and Whites Hill State College catchments each carry a premium that is specific to that catchment and to the style of buyer those schools attract. A three-bedroom house in the Bulimba catchment competes against a different buyer profile than a three-bedroom house in the same street configuration just outside it. The competition is more intense, the days on market is typically shorter, and the final price relative to initial price guidance is consistently stronger inside the boundary.

What makes this interesting in the autumn 2032 market is that the premium is showing up at a time when affordability pressures are real for many buyers. The buyers paying a catchment premium are not casual about it. They have done the maths on school zones versus private fees, they have considered the commute to work, and they have decided the address matters. That level of commitment in a buyer produces competitive bidding even when the market elsewhere is more patient.

For sellers, knowing whether your home sits inside or adjacent to one of these catchments affects your campaign strategy, your buyer targeting, and how you should be positioning the property in marketing.
