## Caption

A seller in a strong market cannot waive the cooling off period. Full stop.

This comes up regularly. A vendor receives an offer, the buyer seems committed, and the vendor asks whether they can get the buyer to sign away the cooling off period to make the contract more certain. The answer is no. In Queensland, the five-business-day cooling off period is a statutory right that belongs to the buyer. It cannot be waived by negotiation, by agreement, or by any clause in the contract.

The only situation where a buyer loses the cooling off right is if they purchase a property under auction conditions. A buyer who bids at auction and wins does so unconditionally. A buyer who signs a contract in the 72 hours after a passed-in auction, under specific legislative rules, also loses the right. Outside of those circumstances, the cooling off period applies.

There is a second misunderstanding worth addressing. Many vendors believe the cooling off period means the contract is not real. It is real. A buyer who walks away during the cooling off period forfeits 0.25% of the purchase price as a termination penalty. That is the deterrent built into the legislation. The contract is binding; it just comes with a prescribed exit right.

If you are preparing to sell and want to understand exactly what your contract protections look like from day one, the full article explains the timeline, the penalty, and the exceptions clearly.
