## Caption

Coorparoo has a settled confidence that comes from decades of being one of Brisbane's most consistently sought-after inner south-east suburbs. The Logan Road strip anchors the suburb's commercial life: cafes, independent retailers, and the kind of walkable neighbourhood character that buyers from the southern states consistently seek out when they move north.

Federation homes and older Queenslanders line many of the side streets off the main thoroughfares. The housing stock here has more character than volume, which is precisely why buyers competing for it tend to pay well. The Coorparoo Secondary College catchment draws significant family buyer interest at the mid to upper end of the market. ⚠️ VERIFY

Proximity to Stones Corner adds a second walkable retail node to the east, and the frequency of bus services along Logan Road makes the suburb accessible for households that are not entirely car-dependent.

What Coorparoo offers to a seller in 2033 is a deep, consistent buyer pool with strong median values and relatively fast days on market for well-presented properties. The suburb does not have the boutique scarcity of Bulimba or Hawthorne, but it has a breadth of demand that makes it a reliable market for vendors who prepare correctly.

If your property sits within a school catchment zone, your agent needs to be actively marketing to that fact. Catchment buyers are motivated and specific in what they are looking for.
