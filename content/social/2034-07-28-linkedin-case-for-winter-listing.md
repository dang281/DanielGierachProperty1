## Caption

I want to make a specific case for July and August as campaign months in Brisbane's inner east.

The argument is not that winter is always better than spring. It is that winter is often better than sellers expect, and spring is often more competitive than sellers anticipate.

Brisbane's inner east benefits from a quirk that most of the spring-selling advice misses: the climate here is mild enough that a well-presented home shows well in every month. The "wait for spring" logic was developed in Melbourne and Sydney, where cold, grey winters genuinely depress buyer activity. That is not the Brisbane experience. Clear winter mornings, dry air, comfortable temperatures from mid-morning. These are inspection conditions most cities would call ideal.

The buyer pool in July and August includes two groups that get overlooked. First, the buyers who missed out in autumn and are still in the market, pre-approved and motivated. Second, the corporate transferees and interstate movers who buy on their company's timeline, not the seasonal calendar.

For properties with good natural light, solid bones, and a presentation that does not depend on a lush summer garden, July and August offer a genuinely competitive opportunity. Fewer listings, engaged buyers, decisive outcomes.

The question worth asking before spring: what am I waiting for? danielgierach.com.
