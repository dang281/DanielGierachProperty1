## Caption

Whites Hill State College is one of the most in-demand public school catchments in all of south-east Queensland, and it sits right at the heart of Camp Hill. Families come from across the city for this catchment, and the streets within it attract a level of competition that buyers from other suburbs often find surprising until they see it firsthand.

The Stones Corner cafe strip on the southern edge of the suburb is where Camp Hill residents do their weekend. Good coffee, a cluster of restaurants, and the general feel of a neighbourhood that has a clear identity. That strip has been strengthening for years.

The owner-occupier base here is deeply established. Long-term residents hold their homes, which means supply is naturally tight. When a character home comes to market on streets like Boundary Road or Riding Road, the buyer pool tends to arrive in force. Unrenovated Queenslanders in original condition still attract significant interest from buyers willing to do the work themselves.

What takes longer here is the modern infill or the home with an awkward floorplan. Buyers in Camp Hill are buying a suburb as much as a house, and they have standards. Presentation and positioning matter.
