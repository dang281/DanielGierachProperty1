## Caption

The inner east buyer pool in late May is not the same group that was active in February and March, and understanding the difference is useful if you are considering a June launch.

The discretionary upgrader, the buyer who could live in any of several inner east suburbs and was weighing up options through autumn, has largely made their decision by now. That buyer either purchased earlier in the season, went under contract on something suitable, or stepped back to reassess. They are not as present in late May.

The buyers who remain active in the inner east in late May fall into two broad profiles. The first is the buyer who missed out on something specific earlier in the year and is waiting for the right property to come to market in a suburb or street they have already committed to. This buyer has a clear brief, has usually done multiple inspections on properties that did not end up being right for them, and will act quickly when the property they have been waiting for becomes available. They are not browsing.

The second profile is the buyer with a hard deadline: a lease expiry requiring them to be under contract by a certain point, a school enrolment that requires an address within a specific catchment by term one, or a job relocation with a fixed start date. These buyers are motivated by circumstance rather than market sentiment, which means they are less likely to wait out a price disagreement and more likely to transact at a price that works for both parties.

Both profiles are active buyers. A vendor who launches in June with a well-prepared property at a price grounded in current evidence is entering a smaller but genuinely motivated market.
