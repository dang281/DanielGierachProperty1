## Caption

Buyer energy peaks in March. By April it is fading, and by May it has shifted in a way that is difficult to recover.

This is a pattern I see play out year after year in the inner east. It is not a theory about the market. It is what I observe at open homes, in offer volumes, and in the negotiating dynamic between buyers and sellers across the two months.

In March, buyers who have been searching since January or February are at peak motivation. They have missed out on other properties. They have recalibrated their price expectations upward. They know the market and they know what they want. When the right property arrives, they compete.

By April, a portion of those buyers have bought something else. The pool is smaller. The buyers who remain are often more patient, more cautious, and less inclined to stretch for a property they like but can live without.

The sellers who hold off for "one more week of preparation" in early March often find themselves in a less competitive April market with a buyer pool that has been refreshed but not deepened.

The practical message: if you are close to ready right now, launching in March is the highest-probability path to a strong result. Not in theory, in practice.

If you are genuinely not ready, the right move is not to rush and produce a campaign that doesn't do the property justice. A well-executed April campaign is better than a poorly executed March one. But "I want to do one more thing" is rarely a reason to miss the window.

If you are thinking about this, the time to call is today.
