## Caption

Most sellers underestimate the timeline from decision to settlement. Understanding the full process changes how you plan.

The campaign itself is typically four to six weeks. But the campaign is only one phase of the process.

Before the campaign: four to eight weeks of preparation. Decluttering, repairs, photography, styling, marketing sign-off. A seller who starts in September for an October campaign launch needed to begin in August.

After the campaign: the accepted offer triggers the conditional period. Finance conditions typically require seven to fourteen business days. Building and pest is usually five to seven business days. Settlement is commonly thirty to forty-five days after unconditional exchange.

Total timeline from the day you decide to sell to the day settlement completes: ten to sixteen weeks is the realistic range for a well-organised inner east campaign in normal market conditions. A simple property with straightforward conditions can move through in ten weeks. A more complex property, or one where multiple conditional periods run in parallel, can extend to sixteen weeks or beyond.

The phase that most vendors underestimate is the preparation phase. A property that is well-prepared before launch performs better in the campaign and typically reaches an unconditional offer faster. The time spent in preparation is not added to the timeline: it is often recovered on the other end through a faster campaign and cleaner contract.
