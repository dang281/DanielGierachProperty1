## Caption

The Village on Old Cleveland Road is one of Brisbane's most established and genuinely well-used suburban lifestyle precincts. The concentration of cafes, restaurants, independent retailers, and daily-use services that have built up along that strip over many years creates a walkability and community quality that buyers who move to Camp Hill almost universally mention as a reason they chose the suburb. It is the kind of precinct that is hard to manufacture and harder to replicate.

Camp Hill shares the Whites Hill State College catchment with Norman Park, and the combination of that catchment access and The Village lifestyle draws a specific buyer type: families who have been looking specifically for this suburb, not a category of suburb, but this one. When the right property comes up in Camp Hill, these buyers tend to act quickly. Days on market for well-presented character homes in this suburb are consistently low, and the competition at open homes reflects the depth of that motivated buyer pool.

The character housing stock in Camp Hill, the Queenslanders and post-war homes with their elevated positions and indoor-outdoor living potential, is what that buyer profile has been searching for. They are typically upsizers who have been in the market for some time, know the comparable sales evidence, and have finance in place.

If you own in Camp Hill and have been thinking about whether now is the right time to sell, the short answer is that the buyer pool for well-presented properties in this suburb remains strong.
