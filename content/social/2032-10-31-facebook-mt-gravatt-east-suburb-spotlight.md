## Caption

Mt Gravatt East draws a broader buyer mix than some of its neighbouring suburbs, and the two anchors that explain most of it are Greenslopes Private Hospital and Griffith University's Nathan campus. Healthcare workers and university-connected households feature consistently in the buyer and rental demand here, and that creates a different dynamic from the pure family-buyer suburbs further east.

The elevated blocks give the suburb genuine appeal beyond the institutional demand. A number of streets offer district outlooks that justify attention from buyers who are prioritising the land and position rather than proximity to a school catchment. Character homes on those higher blocks have shown consistent interest from buyers who want more than a flat block in a comparable price range.

The family buyer base is real as well. Quiet streets away from the main arterials suit households at the family stage, and the mix of housing types across the suburb creates accessible entry points for buyers at different budget levels.

October brings the suburb into its best physical presentation, and that matters for elevated properties where the views and garden read well in photography. If you own in Mt Gravatt East and want to understand the full buyer picture before deciding whether to sell, I am happy to have that conversation.
