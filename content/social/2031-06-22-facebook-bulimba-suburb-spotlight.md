## Caption

A winter morning on Oxford Street in Bulimba is one of the better arguments for the suburb's lifestyle offer. The tables outside the cafes are in use from early. The park at Bulimba Reach is occupied by people who are using the river foreshore rather than driving to it. The ferry terminal is within walking distance of most of the character home streets, and the commute to the city by water is, in clear June weather, something that residents who work near the CBD refer to with genuine appreciation.

The price Bulimba commands over neighbouring suburbs is not solely a function of Oxford Street and the ferry. It is also a function of what is not there: no arterial roads cutting through the residential core, no apartment tower block at the end of a character home street, no retail strip attracting noise and traffic into residential precincts. The suburb's physical layout protects its character in a way that is difficult to price but easy to feel when you spend time there.

For vendors in Bulimba preparing for a spring campaign, the winter period is the time to complete any deferred maintenance, engage a stylist, and ensure the property is presenting at the level the buyer profile expects. The buyers who come through a Bulimba open home have typically seen a significant number of properties and are not easily impressed by superficial presentation.

Spring preparation beginning now means a September or October launch that captures peak buyer attention with a fully prepared property.
