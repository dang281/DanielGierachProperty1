## Caption

Morningside has carved out a clear identity in Brisbane's inner east property market. At five kilometres from the CBD, it sits at a distance that appeals to buyers who want genuine city proximity but cannot justify the price premium of Hawthorne, Norman Park or Bulimba. The result is a suburb that absorbs demand from buyers who have been priced out of those tighter markets while still offering the lifestyle benefits of the inner east corridor.

The Wynnum Road corridor is the suburb's main artery, connecting it to the CBD and the broader south-east. Properties on or near the road have bus access; those further back enjoy quieter residential streets. The elevated parts of Morningside sit noticeably higher than the surrounding area, delivering views and ventilation that buyers factor into their offers, often significantly so. A home with outlook and a good breeze is in a different bracket from a comparable property in a lower position.

Morningside State School is well-regarded in the area, and the primary school catchment influences buyer decision-making in the way that strong school catchments consistently do. Families who identify the school as a priority buy into the catchment and stay, which contributes to the suburb's reasonable turnover rather than destabilising it.

Current medians in Morningside offer a meaningful step below the neighbouring prestige suburbs, making it one of the more rational entry points into the inner east.
