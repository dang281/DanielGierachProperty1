## Caption

Three weeks into peak autumn campaign season in the Brisbane inner east, a clear pattern has emerged across the properties I have been watching and managing.

Properties where the vendor expectation was set at or close to the market at campaign launch are resolving quickly. Offer conversations that are starting in the first week of a campaign are reaching exchange in the second or third. Vendors in this group are not necessarily leaving money on the table by being realistic about price. They are generating competition because the price position attracts multiple buyers, and that competition is producing results at or above the campaign target.

The properties taking longer to resolve share a narrower set of characteristics. Price expectations set above what the buyer pool will absorb in the current condition of the property. Presentation that asks buyers to imagine something rather than experience it. Or properties in a sub-category of the market where the buyer pool is genuine but thin, requiring more campaign time to find the right match.

Free-standing houses with original character in the $1.3m to $2m range across the inner east suburbs are showing the strongest buyer competition. Larger family homes above $2.5m are moving more slowly and require more targeted marketing to find their buyer.

Q1 2034 is tracking as a productive autumn for well-prepared vendors. The depth of buyer demand in the inner east is evident in the pace at which quality campaigns are resolving.
