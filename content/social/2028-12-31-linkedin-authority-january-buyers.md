## Caption

January gets underestimated as a selling month in Brisbane's inner east.

The buyers who return from the Christmas break with a property search intention are not the casual browsers of spring. They have been thinking about this over the holiday period. They have had the kitchen table conversation about where they want to live and what they are prepared to do about it. By the time the first open home hits their calendar in mid-January, many of them have been ready to act for weeks.

What January lacks is listing supply. Most vendors who intended to sell in the first quarter list in late January or February. The weeks between school returning and the February surge represent a window where motivated buyers and limited competition from other sellers exist simultaneously. That is a structural advantage for the vendor who is ready to go in mid-January.

The buyer profile in early January also has a specific urgency driver that spring buyers do not: school catchments. Families who are relocating for a school year that starts in late January are operating on a timeline with no flexibility. An unconditional offer from a buyer who needs to be in by end of January is the most motivated offer you will receive all year.

If you are thinking about listing in the first quarter of 2029 and your preparation is in order, the January window is worth a conversation.
