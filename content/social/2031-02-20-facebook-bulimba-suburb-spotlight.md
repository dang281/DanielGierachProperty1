## Caption

Oxford Street on a Saturday morning is its own argument. The cafes along the strip, the foot traffic spilling out from Oxford Park, the ferry terminal a short walk away: Bulimba built a version of inner-city life that most Brisbane suburbs still aspire to.

The autumn selling season suits Bulimba well. Buyers who arrive in February are typically the ones who have done the research, who understand that character housing on the river side of Bulimba, the Queenslanders with elevated verandahs and original hardwood floors, rarely comes up twice in the same year. The ferry connection to the CBD via the Bulimba Ferry Terminal gives the suburb an access story that no amount of road widening could replicate for neighbouring suburbs.

Oxford Park anchors the suburb's open space. The river frontage sections on Wyandra Street and surrounds remain among the most sought-after addresses in the inner east. Owner-occupiers who buy in Bulimba tend to stay, which compresses the available stock and concentrates competition when properties do come to market.

The premium buyer profile in Bulimba is consistent: professional couples and families who prioritise walkability, character, and liveability over land size. That buyer is active in February, and they are prepared.

For homeowners in Bulimba considering the 2031 autumn window, the time to start the preparation conversation is now.
