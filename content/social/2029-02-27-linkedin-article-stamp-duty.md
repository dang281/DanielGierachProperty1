## Caption

The most consistently underestimated cost in a property purchase is transfer duty.

Most buyers build their deposit, factor in conveyancing costs, and have a rough idea of building and pest fees. Transfer duty gets treated as a line item to worry about closer to settlement. By then, the number is fixed and there is no room to adjust.

On a $1 million owner-occupier purchase in Queensland, transfer duty adds approximately $38,025. Most buyers who have not looked it up recently underestimate that by $10,000 or more. On a $1.2 million purchase the figure is higher again. At inner east Brisbane price points, where $1.1 million to $1.6 million is a typical range for a family home in suburbs like Camp Hill or Carina Heights, transfer duty is a meaningful cost that belongs in the calculation from day one.

First home buyers in Queensland may be eligible for a concession on properties below a threshold value, but that threshold is below the median for most inner east suburbs. Verify the current threshold at the Queensland Revenue Office before relying on any concession.
