## Caption

The cooling-off period is one of the most commonly misunderstood protections in a Queensland property contract. Buyers know it exists. Most could not tell you exactly how it works.

Four facts worth having clearly in your head before you sign.

One: the cooling-off period is five business days from the date of contract, not from the day you receive your copy of the signed contract. The clock starts at signing.

Two: if you withdraw within the cooling-off period, the penalty is 0.25% of the purchase price. On a $1.5m contract, that is $3,750. It is not a deposit forfeiture.

Three: to exercise the cooling-off period, the buyer must serve written notice before 5pm on the fifth business day. A phone call to the agent is not sufficient. The written notice must be served.

Four: properties purchased at auction have no cooling-off period at all. The contract is unconditional from the moment the hammer falls and the contract is signed. This is material if you are planning to bid at an inner east auction and have not arranged finance and building and pest inspection in advance.
