## Caption

Autumn in Brisbane's inner east is not a calendar event. It is a market condition. And based on what I am seeing right now, it has arrived.

Open home numbers have climbed each weekend through February. The groups attending are not browsing. They have pre-approval, they have done their suburb research, and they are ready to move on the right property.

Auction clearance rates in the inner east are worth watching closely in March. When clearance rates run above 60% over three consecutive weekends, it tells you that buyer demand is outpacing available supply. That environment is what most sellers are hoping to list into.

A few observations from the ground. Properties with genuine lifestyle appeal, walkability, school catchments, or river proximity continue to generate strong competition regardless of rate conditions. The buyer who wants to be in Hawthorne or Bulimba is not going to pivot to a different suburb because rates have not moved. Scarcity of genuinely good properties in genuinely good locations is what drives the premium.

Properties that are priced well and presented well are going in the first one to two weeks of a campaign. Properties that come to market with questions around pricing or presentation are sitting longer and often accepting less than they could have achieved.

For sellers still considering whether to list: the window that produces the best results in the inner east typically runs until late April. After Easter, buyer attention begins to fragment.
