## Caption

In Brisbane's inner east, median days on market for houses has tracked between 25 and 45 days across recent windows, depending on price range. Once a property crosses 45 days, buyer perception shifts. Enquiry drops, second inspection requests slow, and the property begins to carry a stigma that is difficult to reverse without a price adjustment or fresh campaign. Overpricing is the most common cause, but it is not the only one. Presentation, marketing quality, and genuine location or structural issues all play a role. When you see a property sitting beyond six weeks, what does your mind go to first?
