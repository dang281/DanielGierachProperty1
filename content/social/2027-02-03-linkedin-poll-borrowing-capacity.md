## Caption

APRA requires lenders to test your ability to repay a mortgage at your actual rate plus 3%. So if you are borrowing at 6%, the bank tests you at 9%. That buffer alone eliminates a significant portion of what most people expect to be able to borrow.

On top of that, lenders apply their own conservative income assumptions. Overtime and bonuses are discounted. Investment income is treated cautiously. Rental income from a property you own often only counts at 80% of its actual figure.

First-time borrowers are regularly surprised by the gap between what they thought they would be approved for and what the bank actually offers. So are people who have not borrowed for five to seven years and are returning to the market with a changed income picture.

When you last applied for a home loan, how did the approved amount compare to your expectation?
