## Caption

The standard advice to sell in spring was written for Melbourne and Sydney. It does not apply to Brisbane's inner east.

Spring selling season logic is built around a specific set of conditions: cold winters suppress buyer activity, gardens look better once they bloom, and the school year creates a natural buyer urgency before Christmas. Those conditions describe Melbourne. Brisbane's inner east follows a different calendar entirely, and vendors who plan their campaign around national advice rather than local patterns often miss the window that actually matters.

The most consistent selling period in the inner east is autumn, running from February through to May. The reasons are layered. Families who have been settled since the start of the school year are now in a position to commit. Couples making financial decisions before the end of the financial year are active and motivated. Downsizers whose thinking has been crystallising quietly since Christmas have often reached the point where they are ready to act. That combination of buyer types, all moving at the same time, creates genuine competition.

Summer, by contrast, often brings high enquiry volume that does not translate to competitive offers. Buyers browse during December and January. They are not always ready to commit. The gap between interest and action is wider in summer than the enquiry numbers suggest.

The article covers how to read seasonal conditions specific to the inner east, what to watch in the February market before committing to a campaign start date, and how buyer life stage drives demand more than weather or garden aesthetics.
