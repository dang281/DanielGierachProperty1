## Caption

Under the Property Law Act 2023 (Qld), sellers in Queensland are required to provide a completed Seller Disclosure Statement to the buyer before any contract is entered into. This is not a post-contract obligation. It is a pre-contract requirement.

The disclosure statement covers matters including the property's title, any statutory charges, encumbrances on the title, pool compliance status, and other prescribed matters. The buyer must receive the completed statement before they sign. If they do not, they have the right to terminate the contract at any time before settlement.

The practical issue is that most vendors approach the disclosure statement as a document to prepare when an offer arrives. By that point, the statement needs to be ready within hours. Any outstanding items, a pool compliance certificate that has not been renewed, an encumbrance that needs clarification, create delay that can cool a buyer's motivation precisely when that motivation needs to be sustained.

The vendors who avoid this problem are the ones who treat the disclosure statement as part of campaign preparation rather than contract administration. When an offer comes in, the statement is signed, correct, and ready. The contract exchange is clean and the buyer's commitment is maintained.
