## Caption

Most buyers and sellers imagine settlement day as the moment everything comes together. In practice, it is closer to twenty minutes of confirmation calls, a funds transfer, and a phone call from the solicitor saying you can collect the keys from the agent.

Here is what actually happens. The buyer's lender releases funds to the buyer's solicitor. The solicitor confirms receipt, then authorises the transfer to the vendor's solicitor. The vendor's solicitor confirms receipt and notifies the agent. The agent releases the keys. The entire sequence, on a clean settlement, runs in twenty to forty minutes.

What determines whether settlement runs cleanly is not what happens on the day. It is what happens in the week before. Outstanding requisitions on title, late bank document issues, body corporate certificates that have not been ordered, and final inspection disputes are the categories most likely to push a settlement past its scheduled time.

The article at the link below steps through the full settlement process from both the buyer's and vendor's perspective, including what to check in the final week and what the agent's role is once the matter is in the solicitor's hands.

If you are buying or selling in 2034, this is worth reading before settlement day arrives.
