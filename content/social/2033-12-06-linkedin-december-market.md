## Caption

December has a reputation in property that I think it does not deserve. The assumption is that the market goes quiet, buyers disappear, and nothing worth doing happens until the new year. What I actually observe is something quite different, and it is worth understanding if you are a vendor thinking about timing.

The buyers who are still attending open homes in mid-December are not casual. They are not browsing. They have typically been searching for most of the year, have not yet found what they are looking for, and have a very specific brief. Finance is approved. They know the comparable sales evidence in their target suburbs. They are not coming to open homes out of curiosity. They are coming because they want to buy before the year ends or are willing to commit to a January settlement.

Compare that to a September open home, where the pool is broader but also shallower. September brings out buyers at various stages of readiness. Some are early in their search. Some are just getting a feel for the market. The motivated buyer is in that pool, but so is a lot of noise around them.

In December, the noise has largely gone. What remains is a buyer pool with concentrated intent.

For vendors, the other factor is competition. Listings thin out significantly from late November. A well-presented property entering the market in early December is competing against far fewer alternatives than a comparable property launched in October. Lower competition and a highly qualified buyer pool is not a bad combination.

The idea that December is a dead market does not match what I see in Brisbane's inner east. It is a different market, and for a prepared vendor, it can be a very productive one.
