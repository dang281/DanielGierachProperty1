## Caption

Most inner east buyers underestimate stamp duty by at least $10,000. On a $1 million owner-occupier purchase, transfer duty in Queensland is approximately $38,025, and it contributes nothing to equity.

That figure sits outside the loan, outside the deposit, and outside the purchase price. It is a cash cost due at or before settlement and it needs to come from somewhere. Buyers who have calculated their purchasing capacity as deposit plus borrowing capacity, without separately accounting for transfer duty, discover the shortfall either when they see the full cost statement from their solicitor or, in some cases, after they have already committed to a price they cannot comfortably complete.

Transfer duty in Queensland is tiered and the effective rate increases with the purchase price. On a $1.5 million purchase the figure is meaningfully higher than on a $1 million purchase, and the rate increments are not always intuitive to buyers who are working from a single rounded estimate. First home buyer concessions reduce the duty payable on purchases up to $700,000 and taper above that threshold. Most inner east buyers are purchasing well above that level and the full duty rate applies. The concession should not be assumed.

Any estimate from a listing, an agent, or a general online tool is a starting point only. The correct figure for a specific purchase should be confirmed at the Queensland Revenue Office before a buyer uses it for budget planning.

The article covers how transfer duty is calculated, what concessions apply and to whom, and how to verify the figure correctly.
