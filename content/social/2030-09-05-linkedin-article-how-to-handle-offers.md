## Caption

Rejecting a low offer outright is almost always the wrong move. It ends the conversation entirely. A counter, even a firm one, keeps the buyer at the table.

A buyer who opens at $950K on a $1.05M property is not necessarily a time-waster. In the inner east, an 8 to 10 per cent gap between the asking price and the opening offer is standard. The buyer who starts at $950K is often capable of $1.02M or more if the negotiation stays open long enough to find it. Rejection closes that ceiling off permanently.

The second principle most vendors miss: speed matters as much as price in the early stages. A buyer who submits an offer within 48 hours of their first inspection is motivated. That motivation is an asset in a negotiation. Treating the offer as an insult, or sitting on it for a week waiting for something better, can cool that motivation faster than any counter-offer.

The third principle is separating price from terms. A vendor who insists on $1.05M might get there if they are willing to move on settlement date, or to include inclusions the buyer values. Understanding what the buyer needs beyond price is often what unlocks the gap.

Offer handling is where many sales are won or lost, and it happens well before the auction or the final contract is signed. The full picture is at danielgierach.com/insights/how-to-handle-offers-when-selling.
