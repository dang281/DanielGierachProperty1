## Caption

The buyers who want to settle before Christmas are making decisions this week. Not next week, this week.

A standard 30-day settlement from mid-November lands in mid-December, which is the edge of the workable Christmas window. Most buyers know this, and the ones who are serious about a year-end settlement have done the arithmetic. If they do not have a contract executed by the end of this week or early next, their chances of settling before Christmas close off quickly.

From a seller's perspective, this buyer type is distinct and worth recognising. They call within 24 hours of an open home, sometimes the same evening. They ask direct questions: what does the vendor want, is there flexibility on settlement, what is the situation with the property. They are not browsing. They have already made up their mind that this is the type of property they want, they know their finance, and they are working against a self-imposed deadline.

The risk for sellers is underestimating this buyer's urgency and treating them the same as an early-campaign inquiry that needs time to develop. A motivated pre-Christmas buyer who feels they are not being taken seriously will move to the next property. There are usually two or three options on their list.

The other thing worth knowing: this buyer type will often accept less favourable conditions, tighter settlement terms, or fewer inclusions if it means locking in the deal. Their priority is certainty and timing. That combination, when handled well, produces good outcomes for sellers who are ready to move.
