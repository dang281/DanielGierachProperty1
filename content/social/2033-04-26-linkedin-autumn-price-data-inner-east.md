## Caption

Price data for autumn campaigns in Brisbane's inner east consistently shows one pattern worth paying attention to: well-prepared properties that come to market in April and May are not discounting to sell. They are selling at or above reserve when the preparation has been done properly.

That is not a universal statement about the market. It is specific to properties that are genuinely ready. Styling completed, photography done at the right time of day, pricing anchored to comparable sales rather than aspirational numbers. When those elements align, autumn campaigns in suburbs like Coorparoo, Camp Hill, and Morningside are performing.

Where the data tells a different story is with properties that come to market underprepared or overpriced. These sit. Days on market climb. Price reductions follow. And the vendor ends up holding through winter and reconsidering for spring, which is exactly what they were trying to avoid by listing early.

The gap between a well-prepared autumn campaign and a poorly prepared one is larger than most vendors expect. It is not primarily a function of market conditions. It is a function of the work done in the four to six weeks before the first open home.

If you are looking at the autumn data and wondering whether your property fits the profile of what is clearing well, that is the conversation worth having now, not in August.
