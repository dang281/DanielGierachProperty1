## Caption

Body corporate levies on a townhouse or unit in Brisbane's inner east are not a single fixed cost. They vary significantly depending on the size of the complex, the age of the building, the facilities, and the health of the sinking fund.

A small townhouse complex with no pool or lift typically runs levies in the range of $2,000 to $4,000 per year. A mid-rise building with a pool, lifts, and a larger common area can run $6,000 to $14,000 per year or more. The listing usually shows one or two figures. It rarely explains what drives them.

The more important number is the sinking fund balance. The sinking fund is the reserve held for major capital works: roof replacement, external painting, lift refurbishment. A building with a healthy sinking fund is likely to handle major works without a special levy. A building with a depleted sinking fund is a building where owners may face a significant unplanned expense when major works arrive.

The body corporate disclosure statement, which is provided as part of the contract process, contains the levy schedule, the sinking fund balance, and the minutes from recent meetings. Most buyers receive this after they have already signed the contract. Reading it before signing gives you the ability to ask questions while you still have options.

The full article covers how to read a body corporate disclosure statement and what to look for before you commit.
