## Caption

Holland Park sits on the southern fringe of what most people consider the inner east, and it benefits from that position without being overshadowed by higher-profile suburbs closer to the river. The suburb has a strong owner-occupier culture, large established blocks, and a buyer base that tends to be deliberate in its choice.

The elevated terrain through the suburb's central ridge produces some well-positioned allotments with CBD or bush outlooks, particularly along Cavendish Road and the streets running off Abbotsford Road to the west. Holland Park's proximity to Greenslopes Private Hospital and the Mount Gravatt TAFE corridor gives it a professional and healthcare worker buyer base that is not always visible in aggregate suburb statistics.

The housing stock covers well-maintained post-war homes, some Queenslander character properties, and a growing number of renovated homes where owners have invested substantially. Lot sizes are generous compared to inner city suburbs, and there is development potential on some sites. That dual buyer pool of owner-occupiers and developers keeps the market active across price points.

For families, Holland Park State School and the secondary school options along the Logan Road corridor are part of the buying calculation. The suburb's position between the inner city and the southeastern suburbs gives residents practical access in both directions.

In 2032, Holland Park offers inner east proximity without inner east pricing, which is a meaningful position in a year when buyer competition in core inner east suburbs is elevated.
