## Caption

Morningside sits in a position that most buyers have to work hard to find at this price point: inner-east access, a walkable retail strip, and a family school catchment, all without the pricing that comes with the Bulimba and Hawthorne addresses immediately to its north.

The Wynnum Road retail corridor anchors the suburb's daily life. The streets north of Wynnum Road are where the character home premium sits, and buyers who know the suburb well target those streets specifically. Pre-war and post-war Queenslanders on full blocks, with the Oxford Street precinct within reach and the Bulimba ferry not far behind.

Morningside State School drives consistent family buyer demand into the suburb. At enrolment time, the catchment is regularly the deciding factor for buyers who are weighing Morningside against Balmoral or Norman Park at a lower entry price.

There is also a genuine investment and rental presence around the units and townhouses along the Wynnum Road corridor, which keeps the suburb relevant across multiple buyer types rather than relying on one segment.

For owners in Morningside who have been sitting on a well-presented Queenslander or a post-war home and watching the market, the current period is worth paying close attention to.
