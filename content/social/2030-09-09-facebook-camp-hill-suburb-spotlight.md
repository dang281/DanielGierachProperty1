## Caption

Camp Hill is consistently one of the most competitive markets in the inner east, and the reason is straightforward: supply is structurally low, school catchments are strong, and Wynnum Road delivers the kind of walkable café strip that buyers in this price bracket are no longer willing to compromise on. Days on market for well-presented homes are short, and competition at auction is genuine.

The geography of the suburb matters. Streets north of Wynnum Road, particularly those with elevation and city glimpses, attract a measurable premium over comparable homes to the south. Buyers who know the suburb know this, and they price their offers accordingly. Camp Hill State Infants and Primary School and Whites Hill State College are both within the catchment boundary, which adds further weight for families.

Renovated Queenslanders dominate the top end of the market, but solid post-war homes on decent blocks have their own buyer pool: people who want the address and the school catchment without taking on a full character renovation. Both categories tend to move quickly when the price is right and the presentation is clean.

Sellers in Camp Hill are in a strong position right now. If you are thinking about timing, an accurate appraisal is a useful starting point.
