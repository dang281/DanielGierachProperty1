## Caption

Under Queensland's Property Law Act 2023, sellers are required to provide a completed Seller Disclosure Statement to the buyer before any contract of sale is entered into. This is not optional, and it is not a formality.

The disclosure statement covers information the buyer needs to make an informed decision: details about the property's title, encumbrances, body corporate obligations if applicable, and any notices or orders affecting the property. If it is not provided correctly, the buyer has the right to terminate the contract at any point before settlement.

That last point is significant. A buyer who is past the cooling-off period, who has finance approved, who is three days from settlement, can still walk away if the disclosure was not properly provided. That is a genuine risk, and it is entirely avoidable.

The practical implication for sellers: get your solicitor to prepare the disclosure statement as part of the contract preparation, before your campaign launches. Do not treat it as something you will sort out once an offer comes in. By the time an offer arrives, you should be ready to exchange immediately.
