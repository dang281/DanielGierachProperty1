## Caption

The price differential between the right side and the wrong side of a school catchment boundary in Brisbane's inner east is one of the most consistent premiums I observe. It's not abstract. In certain streets, it's a meaningful and reliable difference in what buyers will pay, simply based on which school zone the title sits in.

The catchment zones that generate the most consistent buyer activity in this part of Brisbane include Whites Hill, Coorparoo State, Balmoral State, and Mt Gravatt East. When a property falls inside a sought-after zone, buyers with school-age children move differently. They've often already done the research, they know exactly what they want, and they make decisions faster than buyers who are more flexible on location.

The timing dimension is worth understanding if you're selling. The school year calendar drives buyer urgency in two distinct windows. Families who missed the February intake are already looking for July entry, and that search is active from around May through to October. The September-October window is also when families who want their children starting at the right school in the following year begin making their property moves. That's active buyer intent tied directly to a non-negotiable deadline.

For vendors with a property in a strong catchment zone, positioning the campaign to align with that buyer urgency is part of the strategy. It's not something to mention in passing in the marketing, it's a core part of the buyer audience you're speaking to and the timeline they're working to.

If your property sits in one of these zones and you're thinking about selling, the conversation about timing and positioning is worth having now. Start at danielgierach.com.
