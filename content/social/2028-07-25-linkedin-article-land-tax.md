## Caption

Queensland land tax is calculated on unimproved land value, not on what you paid for the property. That distinction matters more in the inner east than almost anywhere else in Brisbane.

The threshold for individual investors is relatively modest. But inner east land values have moved significantly over the past decade, and a property that sat comfortably under the threshold when it was purchased may have crossed it without the owner realising. Land tax assessments arrive from the Queensland Revenue Office after the fact. The first time many investors find out they have a liability is when the bill arrives.

There is another layer that catches people off guard: the threshold applies across all taxable Queensland landholdings combined, not per property. An investor who owns two inner east properties with individually modest land values may have a combined holding that pushes them well into a taxable position.

The primary residence is exempt. But if the home has ever been rented or used for business, that exemption may be partial.

None of this is complicated once you understand the structure. The issue is that most investors learn the structure after they have already built a portfolio.

The full article explains how the threshold works, how combined holdings are calculated, and when to review your position.
