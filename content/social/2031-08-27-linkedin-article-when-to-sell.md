## Caption

Vendors who wait for the perfect market conditions often wait longer than they intend. The preparation phase is the variable the vendor controls. Market conditions are not. A well-prepared property launched in a moderate market regularly outperforms a poorly prepared one launched at what everyone agrees was the peak.

Late August is the moment this becomes concrete rather than theoretical. Vendors who want to launch in October have approximately six weeks before their campaign should begin. That is enough time to style, photograph, complete minor repairs, and price accurately if the process starts now. It is not enough time to do any of that if the decision to sell is made the week before the first open home.

The vendors who consistently achieve the strongest results in spring are the ones who made their preparation decisions in August. The vendors who struggle in spring are typically the ones who waited until September to start everything. The difference is not the market conditions they launched into. Both groups launched into the same spring market. The difference is the condition of the property at the point of launch and the accuracy of the price positioning.

Preparation also includes the conversation with an agent. Understanding the current market, the realistic price range for the property, and the right method of sale are all decisions that are better made with time to consider them than under the pressure of a campaign that has already begun.

The full article explains the variables that actually determine sale timing and outcome in the Brisbane market.
