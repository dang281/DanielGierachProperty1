## Caption

One of the most practical questions in property is also one of the least settled: when you are making an offer, does the number on the page matter more than the terms attached to it?

In a competitive inner east market, an unconditional offer at slightly below asking can beat a higher offer loaded with conditions. But not always. A seller with a long settlement in mind, or one who needs the certainty more than the extra dollars, weighs things differently. The right answer depends on who is sitting across the table.

When making an offer on a property, which carries more weight: price or conditions?
