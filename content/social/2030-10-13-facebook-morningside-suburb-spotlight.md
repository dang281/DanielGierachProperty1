## Caption

Morningside is one of the inner east suburbs where the nuance between streets matters as much as the suburb label itself. The elevated houses above Wynnum Road, with their city and bay views, attract a different buyer to the flat streets around the shopping centre. Both are Morningside. The market treats them differently.

The character housing stock on the higher streets, particularly around Martin and Thynne Road, has been a consistent performer through 2030. Families value the school options, including Morningside State School and the proximity to Cannon Hill Anglican College. The suburb's walkability to the Morningside café strip on Wynnum Road is a factor buyers mention consistently at open homes.

The Cross River Rail network and existing Morningside train station give the suburb a transport profile that is increasingly valued as CBD commute times become part of the buyer calculation. Morningside sits at approximately 6 kilometres from the CBD by road, with direct rail access.

For investors, Morningside has maintained healthy rental demand through 2030, driven by the suburb's combination of lifestyle amenity and transport access. Vacancy rates have tracked low by historical comparison.

For homeowners considering a sale, spring is when Morningside's buyer depth is at its strongest.
