## Caption

Cannon Hill has undergone a shift in how buyers classify it over the past decade that is worth naming plainly. It moved from being described as a transitional suburb to being described as an established one, and that reclassification has been reflected in prices, in the calibre of renovations buyers bring to the housing stock, and in the confidence with which vendors approach the market.

The Cannon Hill train station is the infrastructure anchor that made a significant part of that shift possible. With the Cross River Rail upgrade delivering fast, reliable connections to the CBD, South Bank, and the inner north, the suburb's commute story changed. Residents who previously weighed Cannon Hill's distance from the city now have a compelling public transport argument that they can make to buyers.

Cannon Hill Anglican College adds a private school option to the suburb that draws families from surrounding precincts into Cannon Hill, and that arrival of school-motivated buyers has broadened the demand base. The combination of the train station and a well-regarded Anglican college in the suburb is a profile that many inner-south-east suburbs would not refuse.

Cannon Hill Plaza meets the day-to-day retail needs of the suburb reliably. The proximity to the Gateway Motorway makes the suburb genuinely functional for households with work or family commitments north or south of the city.
