## Caption

Carindale is one of Brisbane's most recognisable family suburbs, and the reason is not particularly complicated. The suburb has the ingredients that families at the main home-buying stage prioritise: good school access, practical land sizes, manageable distance to the CBD and Westfield Carindale on the doorstep for everyday convenience. That combination has supported consistent demand from owner-occupiers who are buying to stay rather than to trade.

Westfield Carindale is one of the city's larger regional shopping centres, and its presence shapes life in the suburb in practical ways. Residents can handle major retail, dining, medical appointments and entertainment within a short drive. That level of amenity at the suburban fringe is not something every suburb at this distance from the CBD can offer, and buyers factor it into their decisions.

The housing stock in Carindale tends toward detached homes on reasonable blocks, a product of the suburb's development era when land was available and density was not a priority. Many homes have been extended and updated over the years, reflecting owners who have invested in the property to suit a growing family. Others remain closer to their original form, offering buyers a more accessible entry point with upside through improvement.

Current medians in Carindale offer a compelling comparison to the inner east, with buyers receiving more space for less money.
