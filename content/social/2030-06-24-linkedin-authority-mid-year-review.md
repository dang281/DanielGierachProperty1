## Caption

The first half of 2030 played out in the inner east largely the way the fundamentals suggested it would. Strong autumn activity through March and April, a measured transition into winter, and now a buyer pool that is smaller but serious.

A few things worth noting from the past six months.

The $1.1M to $1.6M range remained the most competitive segment in suburbs like Camp Hill, Carina Heights, and Morningside. Properties in this range with good presentation and school catchment access continued to attract multiple buyer interest, even in quieter months.

Above $2M, days on market extended. Not every seller adjusted their expectations to match the slower pace, which led to some properties sitting longer than the vendor wanted. The ones that cleared did so because the price guide was credible from the start.

The upgrader pool, which I commented on in April, remained active through May. A number of Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Norman Park properties transacted in the $1.8M to $2.4M range to buyers who had owned elsewhere in the inner east for five years or more.

Looking into Q3: the spring preparation cycle is already underway for some sellers. The vendors I am working with now who plan to launch in August and September are using June and July to complete preparation, stage, and photograph while the competition pool is thin.

If you are thinking about spring, the window to start that preparation conversation is now.
