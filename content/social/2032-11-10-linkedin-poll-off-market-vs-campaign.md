## Caption

Off-market sales are common in Brisbane's inner east. No public marketing, no open homes. The seller avoids the process entirely. The buyer avoids competition. The outcome is almost always that the seller accepts less than a well-run campaign would have achieved. But not always. There are genuine buyers who appear off-market at the right price, and sometimes the seller's circumstances make the simplicity worth more than the extra dollars a campaign might deliver. Which approach do you think gets a better result for the seller?
