## Caption

The inner east is frequently referenced as if it were a single market. It is not. The price spread across the core inner east suburbs is significant, and the reasons for that spread are specific and worth understanding.

Balmoral and Hawthorne command a premium that the market has consistently supported. River proximity, the Oxford Street precinct, and a buyer profile that is choosing the suburb rather than compromising toward it combine to sustain prices that sit above comparable properties in the surrounding suburbs.

Norman Park, Morningside, and Camp Hill sit in the family home core of the inner east. School catchment drives significant buying decisions here. Whites Hill College, Brisbane State High, and East Brisbane State School all have catchment implications that affect specific addresses and are priced accordingly by buyers who have done the research.

Coorparoo covers a wider price range than any other inner east suburb because the suburb itself spans multiple price points, schools, and property types. A well-positioned Coorparoo house and a lower-land Coorparoo unit are in different markets, and treating them as comparable produces inaccurate expectations.

Cannon Hill, Murarrie, and Seven Hills represent strong value relative to their proximity to the CBD and the amenity they offer. Buyers who know these suburbs well tend to buy confidently here.

Understanding where a property sits within this spread, not just which suburb it is in, is the foundation of a realistic campaign strategy.
