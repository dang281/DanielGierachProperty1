## Caption

Murarrie sits just east of Morningside, bounded by the Gateway Motorway corridor and the river. It is a suburb in transition, and buyers who understand that have been paying attention for a while.

The residential pockets in Murarrie, particularly those closer to the Wynnum Road boundary with Morningside, offer house-and-land at entry points that reflect the suburb's industrial-residential mix rather than pure residential demand. That pricing gap is closing. As buyers priced out of Morningside and Cannon Hill look for the next suburb east, Murarrie keeps appearing on the shortlist. ⚠️ VERIFY

Gateway Motorway access is a practical drawcard for households with a car-dependent commute or trade workers needing north-south movement. For buyers who work in the port corridor or the logistics sector, the location is close to genuinely useful.

The mix of housing stock here ranges from post-war timber homes to newer infill development. The character of the streets near the residential core has improved steadily as renovation activity has tracked the interest from buyers. Proximity to Morningside's retail and cafe strip is a selling point that more buyers are recognising.

If you own in Murarrie and have been wondering how the market here compares to the suburbs that get more attention, the answer is: it compares better than the price gap currently suggests.
