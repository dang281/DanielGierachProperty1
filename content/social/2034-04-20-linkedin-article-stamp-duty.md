## Caption

Transfer duty in Queensland is a tiered charge that most buyers underestimate, often by $10,000 or more. The most common mistake is running the calculation using a rough percentage rather than the actual tiered rate schedule.

On a $1 million owner-occupier purchase, transfer duty adds approximately $38,025. That figure does not change based on suburb, property type, or how long the buyer has been saving. It is a fixed cost of the transaction that needs to be factored into borrowing calculations before an offer is made, not after a contract is signed.

The first home buyer concession provides meaningful relief on eligible purchases under the applicable threshold, but the concession tapers and then disappears as the purchase price increases above the threshold. Buyers who are purchasing near or above the concession ceiling should verify the exact figure applicable to their situation before relying on any estimate.

Investors purchasing in Queensland pay full transfer duty with no concession available. For a $1 million investment purchase, transfer duty is the same $38,025. That cost does not appear in any gross yield calculation but it is real and it is payable at settlement.

The full current transfer duty schedule and how to calculate the exact figure for any purchase price is at danielgierach.com/insights/stamp-duty-queensland
