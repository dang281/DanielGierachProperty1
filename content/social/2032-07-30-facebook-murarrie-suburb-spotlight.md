## Caption

Murarrie is a suburb that the market has been reassessing, and the reassessment is overdue.

Positioned between Cannon Hill to the west, Morningside to the south, and the airport corridor to the north, the suburb offers access that is genuinely difficult to replicate at its price point. The Gateway Motorway and the East-West Arterial are both within reach. The airport precinct is close. Bulimba is a short drive south. All of that without the pricing that comes with a Bulimba or Hawthorne address.

The Murarrie Recreation Reserve and the proximity to the Bulimba Riverside Parklands give the suburb outdoor appeal that buyers who have not looked closely at Murarrie sometimes miss. This is not a suburb that relies entirely on the access story for its liveability.

The residential streets carry post-war homes on large blocks. The established industrial area in the suburb's north is a separate land use that does not impinge on the residential character. Buyers who have walked the residential streets know the distinction.

The buyers who arrive in Murarrie have usually done the calculation: Bulimba access, airport access, Gateway access, at a price point that reflects the suburb's lower profile rather than any deficit in its practical appeal.

For owners in Murarrie, that access story is a selling point that resonates with a specific and motivated buyer type.
