## Caption

Eight weeks sounds like plenty of time to prepare a property for sale. Most vendors who have been through the process will tell you it goes faster than expected.

Here is the sequence in practice for an inner east Brisbane property going to market in April.

Weeks one and two: appraisal and honest conversation about the market, comparable sales, and what the property can realistically achieve. This conversation shapes the entire campaign. Getting it wrong at this stage costs money at the other end.

Weeks three and four: minor maintenance items addressed. Not a renovation. The things a buyer will notice: a gate that does not close properly, a bathroom grout that needs resealing, a fence panel that has been sitting on the list for three years. These take less time than most vendors expect but need to be done before the stylist comes through.

Weeks five and six: stylist engaged, disclosure statement prepared. The disclosure statement is a legal requirement and catches title issues early. Better to find them six weeks before your campaign launch than two weeks in.

Week seven: photographer in, listing prepared. Photographs are the first thing a buyer sees. They are the campaign.

Week eight: live on the market. First open home on Saturday.

Vendors who follow something close to this timeline end up with better-prepared properties and better results. The ones who compress it to four weeks are usually managing avoidable issues mid-campaign.
