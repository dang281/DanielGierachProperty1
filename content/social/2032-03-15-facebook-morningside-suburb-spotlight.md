## Caption

Morningside is one of the inner east suburbs that has shifted most noticeably in buyer perception over the past decade. It sits between Bulimba and Cannon Hill, has its own train station, and the Morningside Precinct shopping strip on Wynnum Road has matured into a daily-use retail and café area that buyers with young families specifically mention.

The streets north of Wynnum Road, running toward the river end of the suburb and backing up toward the Balmoral boundary, are where the most competitive buyer interest concentrates. The character homes here, predominantly Queenslanders and postwar chamferboards on elevated lots, consistently draw multiple groups at open homes.

South of Wynnum Road the streetscape is more mixed, with postwar and contemporary infill housing at a generally lower price point. This part of the suburb attracts investors and first-home buyers alongside owner-occupiers, and the proximity to the train line is a genuine asset for that buyer group.

March is a productive month to campaign in Morningside. The suburb's combination of liveability and accessibility means the buyer pool includes people who have already looked at Bulimba and Hawthorne and have decided that the price gap is worth examining more seriously.
