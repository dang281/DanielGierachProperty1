## Caption

Before a vendor commits to a campaign, there are signals worth reading. Not forecasts. Not predictions. Observable data from the current market.

Days on market for comparable properties. If homes matching your profile are selling in under two weeks, buyers are competing for limited supply. If they are sitting for six to eight weeks, something is out of alignment: price, presentation, or buyer expectation. Understanding which of these is the issue before your campaign launches matters.

Enquiry rates on current listings. The number of enquiries a listing receives in the first week tells you how large the active buyer pool is for that property type. An agent with multiple active listings in your suburb has this data. An agent who does not operate in your suburb is estimating.

Clearance rates at recent auctions. Not Brisbane-wide rates. Clearance rates for your suburb or adjacent suburbs in the last four weeks. A clearance rate above 70 per cent in your target suburb suggests a competitive buyer pool. Below 55 per cent suggests a buyer's market where price expectations need to be calibrated carefully.

These are not numbers you need to compile yourself. They are numbers a good local agent should be able to put in front of you at an appraisal, without prompting. If they cannot, that tells you something too.
