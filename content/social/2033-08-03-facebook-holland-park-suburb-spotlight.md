## Caption

Holland Park is one of the inner east suburbs that consistently attracts buyers who have done the comparison work on the surrounding area and arrived at a considered decision. The suburb delivers a residential character, access to the inner east's school and services infrastructure, and block sizes that reflect its 1950s and 1960s development history, at a price point that remains below the more prominent neighbouring suburbs.

The buyer who chooses Holland Park over Coorparoo or Camp Hill is typically making a value judgement: the same fundamentals, less competition at individual properties, more square metres of land and house for the budget. That is not a compromise decision. It is a deliberate one from a buyer who has weighed the options carefully.

The suburb's access to Wellers Hill State School and its proximity to Camp Hill State Infants and Primary School create a catchment dimension to Holland Park buyer demand that sustains a proportion of family interest year-round. The Old Cleveland Road corridor and its retail strip connects Holland Park to the broader inner east amenity network that buyers from outside the area often underestimate until they are living in it.

Holland Park's price point relative to its neighbours makes it one of the better stories in the inner east for buyers who are willing to look past suburb prestige toward property fundamentals.
