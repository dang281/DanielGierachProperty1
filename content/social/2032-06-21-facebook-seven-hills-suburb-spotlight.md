## Caption

Seven Hills is one of the smaller suburbs in Brisbane's inner east, and its size is part of what makes it distinctive. Bordered by the Seven Hills Bushland Reserve to the north and Riding Road to the south, the suburb has a contained residential character that buyers who discover it tend to feel strongly about. Properties here rarely turn over at the rate of busier neighbouring suburbs, which means when something comes to market, attentive buyers move.

The bushland reserve is the defining natural feature, with walking tracks that connect through to Balmoral and provide a genuine bushland edge within minutes of the home. For buyers coming from inner-city or suburban areas without meaningful green space nearby, this is a practical lifestyle consideration rather than a marketing point.

Riding Road provides access to everyday retail at Hawthorne and direct bus routes toward the CBD, and the suburb's position between Balmoral and Hawthorne means it draws comparison buyers from both sides. Homes on streets with reserve-edge positions, particularly those with elevated outlooks across the tree canopy, attract a buyer profile that is typically patient, specific, and willing to compete when the right property appears.

The housing stock runs predominantly to post-war character homes with generous block sizes, and the renovation potential on several streets in the suburb remains a consistent conversation with buyers who are looking one cycle ahead.
