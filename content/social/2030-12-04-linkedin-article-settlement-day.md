## Caption

Most vendors expect settlement day to be a significant event they need to be present for, involved in, and anxious about. In the majority of transactions, settlement happens almost entirely without the vendor's direct involvement.

Queensland property transactions are processed through PEXA, the national electronic settlement platform. The financial components, including the balance of the purchase price, mortgage discharge, and transfer of title, are all managed digitally through PEXA on the nominated settlement date. Your solicitor or conveyancer represents your interests in that process.

What most vendors experience on settlement day is a phone call or text message from their solicitor confirming completion. In a straightforward transaction, that is it.

The work that determines whether settlement day is smooth or complicated happens in the weeks before. The pre-settlement inspection, the confirmation that all inclusions are in place, the discharge of the mortgage, the signing of transfer documents: these are the things that need to be done correctly ahead of time. Settlement day itself is the output of that preparation, not the event where problems are solved.

Where things do go wrong is when preparation has been poor. An inclusion that was not handed over. A mortgage discharge that was not initiated with enough lead time. A special condition that was not satisfied and not formally waived. Those issues create delays and disputes that the PEXA platform cannot resolve, because the problem is not the platform, it is the underlying transaction.
