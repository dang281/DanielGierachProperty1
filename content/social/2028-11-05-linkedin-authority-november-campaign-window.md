## Caption

November has a reputation in real estate as the last viable month before Christmas. In the inner east, that reputation undersells what November actually delivers.

The buyers who are still in the market in November are not casual. The ones who have been attending open homes since September and have not yet bought are either highly specific about what they want or competing on the same few properties. When a November listing comes to market that matches what they have been looking for, they move.

What November removes is the casual buyer. The person who was curious in October but not committed. In a sense, that is a market cleaner than October: lower volume, but higher intent per buyer.

What November requires from sellers: preparation that is complete before the campaign launches. There is no time to run a half-ready listing and hope buyers warm to it. A November buyer who has been looking since August has seen forty properties. They know what a well-presented property looks like and what a rushed one looks like.

A campaign launched in the first two weeks of November with a well-prepared property, priced within its comparable evidence, still has a full four weeks before the market starts to quieten for summer. That is enough time to run a proper campaign.

If you are thinking about November, the decision needs to happen now.
