## Caption

The buyers who are still active in the inner east in the third week of March have usually been looking since October or November last year.

They are not browsers. They have inspected 30 or 40 properties. They know the market in the suburbs they are targeting better than most sellers do. Their finance is in place. Their solicitor is on standby. When the right property appears at the right price, they respond within 24 hours.

This is the buyer group that produces strong outcomes for sellers who launch campaigns in February and March. By the time April arrives, some of these buyers will have purchased. The pool thins as the weeks go on, not because demand disappears, but because the most motivated buyers have committed.

The end of the autumn window in the inner east is not a cliff. It does not suddenly go quiet. But the profile of buyers who are active in late April and May is different from the ones who are circling right now.

For sellers who have been holding off, the time to have the "should we list now or wait until spring?" conversation is this week, not next month.
