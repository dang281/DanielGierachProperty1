## Caption

The number most vendors have in their head when they start thinking about selling is the sale price minus the loan balance. That figure is not the net equity. It is the starting point before the deductions that most people either underestimate or have not factored in at all.

Selling costs in Brisbane sit between 2.5 and 4 per cent of the sale price for a well-run campaign. On a $1.4 million property, that is between $35,000 and $56,000 before the net proceeds calculation. Agent commission, conveyancing, marketing budget, and settlement costs all come out before what remains goes to you.

The one that catches vendors with fixed-rate mortgages most off guard is the early exit fee. If your fixed-rate term has not expired and you settle before it does, the lender will charge a break cost that reflects the difference between your fixed rate and the current rate for the remaining term. In some interest rate environments, that figure can add tens of thousands to your cost of sale. It is worth getting the number from your lender before you commit to a campaign timeline.
