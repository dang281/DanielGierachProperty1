## Caption

Rejecting a low offer outright closes the negotiation entirely. It signals to the buyer that there is no path forward and they move on. In most cases, that is the wrong response.

Most opening offers in the inner east sit 8 to 10 per cent below asking price. That is not an insult. It is a negotiating position. The buyer is not telling you your property is worth 10 per cent less. They are testing where the vendor is willing to land. It is the opening move in a conversation, not the final word.

The response to that first offer is where a transaction either begins or ends. A vendor who rejects without counter-engagement removes themselves from the table. A vendor who counters, even firmly, keeps the conversation moving. There are buyers who submit a low first offer and then close the gap significantly when they receive a serious counter. There are also buyers who submit a low offer and mean it. You do not know which you are dealing with until you respond.

An agent's job at this point is to give you accurate information about the buyer's position: what they have been pre-approved for, how long they have been searching, how serious their interest is relative to other buyers in the campaign. That intelligence should be shaping your response, not just the number on the offer.

How a vendor handles the first offer is one of the biggest variables in whether a campaign ends in a sale.

The full article walks through the negotiation sequence and how to respond at each stage.
