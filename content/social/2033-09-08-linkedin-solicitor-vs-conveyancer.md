## Caption

The most common sequencing error I see from buyers in Queensland is signing the contract first and calling their solicitor or conveyancer second. The contract defines what you are buying, what conditions you have, and what the timeline is. Reviewing it before signing is the point at which issues can be addressed without cost. Reviewing it after signing is the point at which changing anything requires negotiation with the other party.

Both solicitors and licensed conveyancers can handle the legal work for a property transaction in Queensland. The distinction matters when the transaction is straightforward or when it is not. A standard REIQ residential contract in uncomplicated circumstances is work a licensed conveyancer handles efficiently. A contract with unusual special conditions, a property with a complex title, or a situation where the buyer needs legal advice on their rights and options in a dispute is work that requires a solicitor.

Most inner east residential transactions fall into the first category. But buyers often do not know which category their transaction is in until they have read the contract carefully. That is the argument for having a professional review the contract before signing rather than after.

The article covers what each practitioner does, the situations where a solicitor is the right choice versus a conveyancer, and the questions to ask when you are choosing who to instruct.
