## Caption

The spring selling season in Brisbane's inner east effectively closes in late November. By the first week of December, buyer activity slows significantly as attention shifts to Christmas and end-of-year commitments. The window is not closing tomorrow, but it is closing.

A property that launches in October with a well-run four to five week campaign can settle before Christmas. That timeline is still achievable right now. A property that launches in November is pushing it. One that launches in December is almost certainly carrying into the new year.

The pre-Christmas buyer has the same deadline as the vendor. They want to be moved in and settled before the new year begins. That shared urgency is not a soft motivation. It produces decisions. Vendors who meet that buyer pool with a correctly priced, well-prepared property find that the final weeks of spring are among the most efficient selling conditions of the year.

The alternative is to carry the property into January, which means contending with the holiday period slow-down before the autumn season begins to build. The vendors who hold over winter in the hope of a better spring result sometimes find it. The ones who hold over Christmas in the hope of a better February result are giving up a motivated buyer pool for a quieter one.

The inner east fundamentals are strong. Buyer depth is genuine. The vendors who have listed in October with realistic price expectations have consistently out-performed the ones who have deferred. Ready now, with a well-prepared property and a clear price strategy, beats ready later. danielgierach.com
