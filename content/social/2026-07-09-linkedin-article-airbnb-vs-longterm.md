## Caption

Short-term rental beats long-term rental on gross income. Most people who run the comparison stop there.

The comparison that matters is net cash flow after costs. And that calculation looks very different.

A conservative estimate for additional annual costs on a short-term Brisbane listing compared to long-term: $15,000 to $25,000 per year. Platform commissions of 15 to 20% (versus 8 to 10% for a property manager). Cleaning at $80 to $180 per turnover. Furnishing costs of $15,000 to $30,000 upfront, amortised over time. Utilities that the tenant pays on a long-term lease, but the owner pays for short-term guests. Specialist insurance. Higher wear and maintenance from frequent changeovers.

That gap erodes most of the gross income advantage.

There is also a regulatory question for Brisbane. Under BCC CityPlan 2014, investment properties used exclusively as short-term rental, where the owner does not reside on site, may need a development application in some zones. It is worth checking before you list.

Long-term rental offers lower gross income, but the management overhead is lower, the income is more predictable, and the regulatory position is straightforward.

Neither model is universally better. The right answer depends on the property, the location, and how much management work you want to take on.
