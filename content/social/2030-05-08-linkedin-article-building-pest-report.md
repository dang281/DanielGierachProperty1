## Caption

Most buyers read the summary page of a building and pest report first. That is the wrong place to start.

The summary is designed to give a quick traffic-light assessment of the property's condition. It is useful for understanding the broad picture. But it groups issues in ways that can make a major defect look like a minor concern, or make a routine maintenance item look alarming.

The section to read first is the one that identifies active defects, specifically any finding that describes current timber pest activity, rising damp, structural movement, or waterproofing failure. These are the issues that cost real money and can affect whether you should proceed with the purchase at all.

General maintenance items, by contrast, are the ones that most Brisbane properties carry: minor cracking, perished silicone, surface rust on gutters. These are not reasons to pull out of a purchase; they are items to factor into your renovation budget.

The other thing worth knowing: a pest report that says "no active termite activity detected" is not the same as "no termites." Inspectors can only assess what is accessible and visible. A positive detection warrants immediate action. A clear report warrants continued monitoring.
