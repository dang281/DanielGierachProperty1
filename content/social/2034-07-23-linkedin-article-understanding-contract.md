## Caption

Most buyers spend more time reading the listing than the contract. That is the wrong way around.

The REIQ Contract of Sale is the document that actually governs the transaction. Everything in the listing is marketing. The contract is law.

Inside that contract, two dates carry more weight than anything else: the finance condition deadline and the settlement date.

The finance condition deadline is the date by which you must confirm unconditional finance approval. Miss it, even if the bank has not finished its assessment, and the condition lapses. You may lose your deposit. Most buyers who find themselves in this situation assumed they were still protected. They were not.

The settlement date is when you must be ready to complete. Adjusting it after contracts are exchanged requires written consent from both parties. If you need more time, negotiate it before you sign, not the week before settlement.

The contract also determines what stays with the property. Inclusions not specifically listed as excluded are assumed to stay. The light fitting you planned to take, the dishwasher, the garden shed: if they are not excluded in writing, they are the buyer's.

Understanding the contract before signing is one of the most practical things a buyer can do. The full guide is at danielgierach.com/insights/understanding-the-reiq-contract-of-sale.
