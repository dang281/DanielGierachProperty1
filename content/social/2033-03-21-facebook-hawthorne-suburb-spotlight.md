## Caption

Hawthorne in the final weeks of peak autumn is a suburb where vendors who prepared well are seeing their preparation rewarded. The buyer pool that has been active since February is now fully formed. The comparable sales are established. And the properties that arrive on market this week are entering a market that knows what Hawthorne is worth.

The Hawthorne ferry terminal continues to be the suburb's most distinctive asset. The ability to step off a CityCat at the bottom of the street and be in the CBD within twenty minutes, without touching a car, is something buyers price specifically and consciously. The commute calculus in Hawthorne is genuinely different from most Brisbane suburbs, and that difference sustains the premium. ⚠️ VERIFY

The renovated Queenslanders that anchor Hawthorne's streetscape set a standard that all campaigns in the suburb are measured against. Buyers who have been looking at Hawthorne for any period of time have seen the top of what the suburb offers. When a new property arrives on market, it is compared against that benchmark first.

What this means for vendors is that the gap between well-presented and average-presented is wider in Hawthorne than in less visually distinctive suburbs. The first week of a campaign matters more here. The photography, the styling, and the street presentation are all doing more work than a vendor arriving from another suburb might expect.
