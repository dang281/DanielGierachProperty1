## Caption

Some investors swear by buying the worst house in the best street. Others say renovation risk isn't worth it. The reality: unless you're managing the project well, renovations almost always cost more and take longer than expected. The equity upside can still be real, but the gap between the budget and the final invoice has a way of closing fast. Where do you land on this?

When buying an investment property, which do you prefer?
