## Caption

We are in the window where inner east vendors start making serious decisions about whether to list before Christmas or wait for the autumn market. It is not a simple call. The pre-Christmas window is shorter, buyer numbers are lower in December than October, and it requires being genuinely ready to campaign and settle in a compressed timeframe.

But there are real reasons to go before Christmas rather than waiting. Motivated buyers who are still searching in November and December are often the most committed buyers of the year. Less competition from other listings. A clean settlement before the new year.

The decision tends to come down to a few specific factors, and different vendors weight them differently.

If you are considering listing your inner east home before Christmas, what would most influence your decision?

## Poll options

- Whether the price expectation would be met
