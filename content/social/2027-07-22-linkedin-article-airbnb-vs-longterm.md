## Caption

The short-term rental comparison in inner east Brisbane almost always looks compelling until you account for the full cost structure. The gross income figures from Airbnb listings in suburbs like Bulimba and Hawthorne can appear significantly higher than the equivalent long-term rental. That difference is real. What is also real is the cost gap that sits between gross income and what actually lands in the owner's account at the end of the year.

Short-term rental in a well-located inner east property carries a cost profile that long-term rental does not: higher cleaning costs per turnover, more frequent maintenance from higher occupancy turnover, platform fees, more active management, and in many cases professional property management rates that are meaningfully higher than standard residential management.

When those costs are modelled accurately, the net difference between a well-managed short-term rental and a well-managed long-term tenancy in the inner east is often far narrower than the gross figure suggests. For some investors the short-term premium survives the cost deductions and the additional management intensity is worth it. For many it does not, and the simplicity of a stable long-term tenant at a market rate is the better financial outcome.

The article below works through the comparison in detail, including where the short-term premium holds and where it evaporates.
