## Caption

April open homes in Brisbane's inner east have a specific character that is different from any other time of year.

By now, the buyers who were watching from January have had two months to refine their brief. They have been through fifteen or twenty properties. They know exactly what a four-bedroom home in Camp Hill looks like for the money. They know which streets they prefer and which floor plans do not work for them. When a well-presented property comes to market in April, these buyers are ready.

What I notice at this point in the autumn cycle is that the quality of buyer engagement tends to be higher than at any other time of year. Less casual browsing, more people with pre-approval, more people bringing their architect or builder to a second inspection. The population of "just looking" buyers thins out, and the buyers who remain are serious.

For sellers, this matters. A buyer who has done their homework and walked through twenty homes is not going to be deterred by a small list of maintenance items. But they will adjust their offer for anything that looks like it was not disclosed. Transparency about what the property needs is more valuable in an April campaign than it is at any other time of year, because the buyers you are dealing with are experienced enough to find it themselves.

The window does not stay open indefinitely. By June, the Brisbane market typically starts to slow as the colder months reduce open home attendance and buyer urgency. If selling is on your agenda for 2027, the next six weeks are worth taking seriously.
