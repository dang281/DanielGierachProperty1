## Caption

Norman Park is one of those inner east suburbs where the fundamentals are so well established that the market rarely needs to be explained to buyers who have already identified it. Whites Hill Reserve, one of the best bushland parks within five kilometres of the Brisbane CBD, backs onto the suburb's eastern boundary. Norman Park State School sits within the suburb and has a strong reputation. And the housing stock, predominantly post-war Queenslanders and brick lowsets on generous blocks, offers the land content that inner east buyers have increasingly competed for over the past decade.

The buyers who pursue Norman Park are largely households making a deliberate choice about where to raise a family. School catchment access is a primary motivator. The reserve access, for families with dogs, children, and an appetite for outdoor space within walking distance, is a secondary filter that narrows the competition to buyers who genuinely value it. That specificity of demand tends to sustain prices in Norman Park through cycles that affect more generic suburbs more strongly.

The suburb's proximity to the Cannon Hill Kmart Plaza, the Norman Park dining strip on Bennetts Road, and train access at Cannon Hill and Morningside stations completes the practical picture for buyers weighing lifestyle convenience alongside the natural amenity.
