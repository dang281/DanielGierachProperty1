## Caption

January in Brisbane property is genuinely different to what happens in Sydney or Melbourne at the same time of year.

In the southern cities, the first two weeks of January are almost completely dormant. The conventional advice is to wait for February before launching a campaign. That advice does not translate to Brisbane, and it especially does not translate to the inner east.

Here is what I actually see in January. Buyers who spent time in the inner east over the Christmas break, looking at suburbs for the first time or reconnecting with areas they have been tracking, tend to make decisions in early January. They have had two weeks with their family to think it through. They come back motivated.

At the same time, very few sellers list in January. So a property that goes live in the first two weeks of the month faces almost no direct competition from other listings. That combination, active buyers and limited supply, can be a strong environment for a seller who is ready.

The sellers who are best positioned for January are the ones who spent December doing their preparation: styling conversations, conveyancing documents sorted, photos booked. The preparation window for a January launch has passed for most. But for a February or March campaign, the preparation window is right now.
