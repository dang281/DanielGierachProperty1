## Caption

I have conversations with homeowners every week who have been thinking about selling for six to twelve months and still have not listed. When I ask what is holding them back, the most common answers are: not sure of the timing, not sure of the price, worried about where to buy next.

These are rational concerns. But here is what I notice: they are almost never about the market itself.

The timing question is solvable. The inner east has two good selling windows per year: autumn and early spring. Most of the time the relevant question is not "when is the best time" but "am I prepared enough to make the most of the next window."

The price uncertainty is also solvable. A credible appraisal from someone who actually knows the street and the comparable sales is the starting point. The figure that comes back from that conversation usually resolves the hesitation faster than any amount of watching the market.

The "where do I buy next" question is the most genuinely complex. It requires a clear sense of what you are moving toward, not just what you are moving from. But even that has a practical path: buy first if the market allows it, or sell with a flexible settlement and rent briefly while you search.

None of these concerns are unsolvable. The thing I see most often is homeowners sitting on the question rather than having the conversations that would answer it.
