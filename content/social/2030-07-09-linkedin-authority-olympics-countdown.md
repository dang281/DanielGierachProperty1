## Caption

The Brisbane 2032 Olympics is under two years away. The conversation around it has shifted.

For most of the last decade, the questions were about infrastructure: would Cross River Rail be delivered, would the precinct construction stay on schedule, would the city actually be ready. Those questions are largely settled. The infrastructure is in place. The focus now is on the event itself.

What that shift means for the inner east is subtle but worth understanding if you are thinking about selling in the next eighteen months. The buyers who were purchasing ahead of the infrastructure story have already moved. The market is not being driven by speculation about what will be built. It is being driven by buyers who want to be in a well-connected, established inner-city suburb in a city that is about to host a global event.

That is a different buyer type. It includes families who want school catchments and walkability. It includes investors who understand that low vacancy rates in the inner east are not a short-term condition. It includes interstate and international buyers who are looking at Brisbane with a level of attention the city has not always received.

None of this is a prediction about prices. What it is, is context. The inner east has multiple competing buyer groups at the same time, and the 2032 proximity is one of the reasons why. Vendors who understand their buyer pool before they go to market are better positioned to price and present their property correctly.

If you are considering selling in the inner east before the end of 2030, the timing context is worth a conversation. danielgierach.com
