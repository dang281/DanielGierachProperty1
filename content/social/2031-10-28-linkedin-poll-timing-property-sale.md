## Caption

The conversation about timing a property sale is everywhere right now in the inner east. The Olympics countdown, the spring market, interest rate movements, and general market sentiment are all feeding into vendor decisions about whether to go now, wait for autumn, or hold further into the Olympic year.

Some people are deliberate market timers. They watch conditions, wait for a specific signal, and make a move that is partly or entirely shaped by external factors. Others sell when their personal circumstances require it, and the market is whatever it is on the day.

Both approaches exist. Neither is wrong. But the outcomes people report vary a lot depending on which camp they are in.

Have you ever timed a property sale around market conditions or external events outside your normal plans?
