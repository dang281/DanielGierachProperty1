## Caption

Heritage overlays are one of the most misunderstood parts of property ownership in Brisbane's inner east. The common assumption is that a heritage listing restricts everything. The reality is more specific and considerably less limiting than most buyers assume.

The overlay applies to the street-facing character of a property. Changes to the facade, the roofline, or elements visible from the street require BCC approval. Internal renovations, rear extensions within BCC height and setback guidelines, and most landscape changes proceed without heritage approval at all. A heritage property in Hawthorne is not frozen in time. It is subject to a set of external character requirements that most owners manage without difficulty.

What the overlay does affect is the type of buyer who approaches the property. Buyers with a clear agenda to demolish and rebuild typically move on. Buyers who value the character, the street presence, and the craftsmanship of a pre-war home see the overlay as a feature rather than a constraint. That self-selection shapes the buyer pool in ways that sometimes benefit the vendor.

The article also covers how to check whether a property carries a heritage overlay before signing a contract, what the approval process involves for affected works, and how inner east buyers and vendors have navigated it in practice.
