## Caption

September opens are well attended. October is when people start making decisions.

That distinction matters for vendors. The buyers walking through Hawthorne, Bulimba, Norman Park, and Camp Hill right now are not the same cohort as six weeks ago. They've done the research. They've attended enough opens to know the difference between a property that represents fair value and one that doesn't. They're pre-approved, and they're ready to move if the right property comes up. That's a meaningfully different buyer than the one browsing in early spring.

Open home volumes across the inner east tend to peak between late September and late October. Properties launching campaigns now are reaching buyers at peak engagement, before the December calendar compresses decision-making and before some buyers pause for the holiday period. By comparison, a property that launches in the second week of November is competing for diminishing buyer attention at the same time vendor supply tends to increase.

Median days on market in Brisbane's inner east has been running below the broader Brisbane average for well-positioned stock in this period, which reflects how quickly motivated buyers act when supply is tight. That gap is worth understanding before you decide whether to launch now or hold until February.

The competition for buyer attention is real, but the pool right now is deep. Vendors who launch in October with a well-prepared property and a clear price strategy are operating in conditions that work in their favour.
