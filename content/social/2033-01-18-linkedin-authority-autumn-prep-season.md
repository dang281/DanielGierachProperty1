## Caption

The sellers who list in early March and achieve strong results in 2033 are not deciding in February. They are deciding now.

A well-run autumn campaign in Brisbane's inner east requires six to eight weeks of preparation before your first open home. That means if you want to be on the market by late February or early March, mid-January is when the work starts. Not when you call an agent for the first time, when you have already had those conversations and are ready to move.

What does preparation look like in practice? For most inner east properties it involves at least one set of maintenance items to attend to before photography: the gutter that needs clearing, the fence panel that should be replaced, the bathroom silicone that has gone dark. None of these are expensive individually, but together they signal to a buyer whether the property has been looked after.

Presentation is the other piece. In suburbs like Camp Hill, Norman Park and Coorparoo, where competition among similar homes is real, a well-styled property consistently draws more traffic and more offers than an empty or under-presented one. The investment in professional styling is modest against what it returns in buyer attention.

Vendors who start these conversations in January can make considered decisions. Vendors who start in February are making rushed ones.

If you are thinking about selling this autumn, the time to talk is now, not in four weeks.
