## Caption

Carina Leagues Club has been a community anchor in this suburb for decades, and the streets around it have the feel of a suburb that people actually live in rather than just pass through. Carina doesn't make a lot of noise in the market, but its fundamentals are solid.

Between Camp Hill and Carindale, Carina sits at a price point that still attracts first-home buyers and investors while also drawing families who want the Carina State School catchment and freeway access for the commute south or north. That mix of buyer types keeps demand reasonably broad, which helps in softer market conditions.

The character of older homes here is noticeably improving through renovation. Original post-war homes that were unremarkable five years ago are coming back to market looking completely different, and the price outcomes are reflecting it. Buyers who bought and renovated have done well. The homes that sit longest tend to be the ones with deferred maintenance and optimistic vendor pricing.

Supply in Carina has been tightening as surrounding suburbs like Camp Hill become harder to enter at any price. That's a pattern worth watching.
