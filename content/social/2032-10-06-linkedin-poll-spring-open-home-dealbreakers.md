## Caption

It is spring in Brisbane's inner east and open homes have more visitors than August and more available stock than September. Buyers are covering ground quickly. When they find the right property, they move fast. When they do not, they walk out without leaving their details.

After years of running campaigns across Bulimba, Balmoral, Camp Hill and Norman Park, certain things come up repeatedly as the reason a buyer disengages. Sometimes it is visible maintenance that reads as a future cost. Sometimes the floor plan photographs well but does not actually work for how a family lives. Often it is a price that is out of step with what comparable properties have actually sold for.

What is the thing most likely to make you leave a spring open home without registering?

## Poll options

- Deferred maintenance (it reads as hidden cost)
- Garden and street presentation
- Floor plan or room proportions
- Overpriced relative to comparable sales
