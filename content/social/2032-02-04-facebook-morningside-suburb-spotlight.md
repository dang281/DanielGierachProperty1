## Caption

Morningside has spent the last decade moving quietly from a suburb people discovered by accident into one buyers target deliberately. The character housing stock along Wynnum Road and the streets running north toward Hawthorne has a density of original Queenslanders and post-war chamferboard homes that renovators have worked through steadily, raising the floor on presentation across the whole suburb.

The proximity to the Morningside retail strip, the Oxford Street amenity in Bulimba a short walk away, and the Morningside train station all contribute to a walkability profile that buyers in the inner east now factor into their pricing calculations. For a suburb that sits comfortably within 6 kilometres of the CBD, that combination of character and infrastructure is not easy to replicate.

In the Olympic year, Morningside's position within the broader inner east corridor has drawn additional attention from buyers who want a permanent address near the Games precinct without paying the Bulimba waterfront premium. The streets between Wynnum Road and the riverline have seen consistent enquiry from that profile.

As autumn opens, homes presenting at a high standard in Morningside are competing for a buyer pool that genuinely wants to be in the suburb. That is the condition where well-run campaigns perform at their best.
