## Caption

Inner east Brisbane investment properties are facing a different set of calculations in 2033 than they were three years ago. Land values have moved significantly, land tax thresholds are catching more investors than expected, and the rental yield conversation is more complex when you factor in management costs and compliance. Some investors are deciding to hold and absorb the costs. Others are deciding this is the right moment to realise the capital gain and redeploy. Both decisions can be correct depending on the individual situation. For those who hold investment property in Brisbane, what is your current thinking?
