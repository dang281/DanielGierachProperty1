## Caption

In a competitive spring market, an unconditional buyer does not just have power on price. They have power on settlement terms, access flexibility, and certainty, and that power is worth more than most buyers understand when they are structuring an offer.

A conditional buyer offering $50,000 above asking price can be worth less to a vendor than an unconditional buyer at the asking price, depending on what the vendor actually needs. A vendor downsizing with a firm next move needs certainty. A vendor who wants a 30-day settlement instead of the standard 60 needs speed. A vendor with a tenanted property needs certainty that the deal will not fall over at finance assessment. An unconditional buyer delivers all of that. A conditional buyer, even at a higher price, does not.

This matters for buyers who are genuinely in a position to go unconditional. If you have your finance fully assessed and approved, not just pre-approved in principle, and you are comfortable with the property after a building and pest inspection, there is a scenario where waiving finance and pest conditions in favour of an unconditional offer gives you a price outcome that is better than what you would achieve with a conditional offer at a higher headline figure.

The decision to go unconditional carries real risk if your financial position is not fully assessed. The article below explains what unconditional means in Queensland, when it makes sense, and what preparation is required before a buyer can genuinely offer unconditional terms.
