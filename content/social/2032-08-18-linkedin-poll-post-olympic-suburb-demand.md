## Caption

The 2032 Games are over and the spring selling season is about to open in Brisbane's inner east.

The inner east covers a wide range of suburbs, each with a distinct buyer profile. The river and lifestyle corridor around Bulimba, Hawthorne, and Balmoral attracts a different buyer to the family-home and school-catchment market in Camp Hill and Norman Park. The value and access story in Coorparoo, Carina, and Carina Heights draws a different group again. And the infrastructure and proximity case for Cannon Hill and Murarrie has been building for several years.

Now that the post-Games market is opening up, buyers who have been waiting are making decisions about where to focus.

Which inner east suburb group do you think will see the most renewed buyer demand this spring?

## Poll options

- Bulimba, Hawthorne, or Balmoral (river and lifestyle corridor)
- Camp Hill, Norman Park, or Morningside (family homes and schools)
- Coorparoo, Carina, or Carina Heights (value and access)
- Cannon Hill or Murarrie (infrastructure and proximity)
