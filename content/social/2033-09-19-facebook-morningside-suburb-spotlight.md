## Caption

Morningside's spring market is worth watching closely this year. The suburb has been accumulating buyer demand through winter from buyers who want Hawthorne or Bulimba proximity without the Hawthorne or Bulimba price. That stored demand tends to activate quickly once listings appear in September, particularly for Queenslanders and post-war character homes in the streets between Wynnum Road and the railway line.

The Morningside café strip along Wynnum Road has developed into a genuine neighbourhood destination. Weekend mornings in this precinct have the kind of pedestrian energy that buyers interpret as a signal about the quality of daily life in a suburb. Properties within walking distance of the strip carry a lifestyle premium that buyers arrive ready to pay.

The Morningside train station connects residents to the CBD in under 20 minutes and to the inner north suburbs via the loop. For buyers who are managing a household with multiple work locations across the inner city, that rail connectivity is a practical argument for Morningside that influences the final suburb decision. The combination of rail access, character housing stock, and the Wynnum Road precinct creates a proposition that continues to attract first-home buyers and upgraders in roughly equal measure.

Spring is Morningside's strongest selling window. Vendors who launch in September find the buyer pool at its deepest.
