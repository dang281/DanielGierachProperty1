## Caption

Norman Park is one of Brisbane's inner east suburbs where the land does the talking. Elevated blocks, wide frontages on quiet streets, and the green buffer of Whites Hill Reserve to the east give the suburb a residential quality that is difficult to replicate closer to the CBD.

Norman Park State School is one of the key demand drivers for family buyers. The school's catchment zone is actively sought and buyers who need to be within it will often stretch their budget to secure the right property. This creates a consistent floor of demand in the mid to upper price range that holds across different points in the market cycle. ⚠️ VERIFY

Character homes dominate the stock here. Elevated Queenslanders and post-war timber homes with good land content are what buyers expect when they come to Norman Park, and that expectation has shaped the renovation culture in the suburb. Properties that have been updated to respect the original character while adding modern liveability consistently achieve strong results at auction.

The proximity to Camp Hill and Hawthorne gives Norman Park buyers access to two of Brisbane's best lifestyle corridors without the price premium that attaches to those suburbs directly.

For vendors in 2033, Norman Park is a market with deep, motivated demand. The buyers here are committed to the suburb and they have done their research. A well-prepared campaign in this suburb rewards vendors who have invested in their property and are realistic about their price expectations.
