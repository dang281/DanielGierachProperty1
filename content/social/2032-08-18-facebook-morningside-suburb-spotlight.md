## Caption

The transformation along Morningside's Wynnum Road corridor has been gradual and consistent over many years, and the result is a suburb that now presents a different picture to buyers than it did a decade ago.

The former industrial and warehouse buildings between Morningside and the Bulimba terminal have given way progressively to mixed-use and residential development, changing the character of the northern edge of the suburb in ways that are still playing out.

The eastern residential streets, particularly those running north off Wynnum Road toward the Bulimba boundary, carry Morningside's most sought-after housing stock. Pre-war and post-war character homes on full blocks with a short trip to both Oxford Street's cafes and the Bulimba ferry. That combination is the suburb's ceiling product, and buyers who know Morningside well look for it specifically.

Morningside State School is a constant driver of family buyer demand. The catchment is the reason many buyers draw the line at Morningside rather than looking further east, and it has been that way across multiple market cycles.

For buyers who want the river-corridor lifestyle story without Hawthorne or Bulimba pricing, Morningside is where the calculation tends to resolve. For owners in those eastern streets, your position in the market is stronger than the suburb's profile sometimes suggests.
