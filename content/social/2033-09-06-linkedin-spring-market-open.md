## Caption

The spring market in Brisbane's inner east does not open on a single date, but the shift is recognisable when it arrives. Open home attendances lift. Pre-approved buyers who held through winter start enquiring with more urgency. Listing volumes begin to increase, slowly at first and then more quickly through October.

For vendors who have been preparing through August, September is the moment the preparation either shows or does not. The photography is already done. The presentation is set. The method of sale and the price guidance are in place. The campaign launches into a market that is ready to receive it.

For vendors who are still in the preparation phase in September, the window is not closed but it is narrower. The early spring market, the first three to four weeks of September, often produces the strongest results of the season because the buyer pool is motivated and the listing competition is still manageable. Later in October, that ratio shifts as more stock arrives.

The vendors who consistently achieve the best spring results in the inner east are the ones who are ready when the market opens, not the ones who launch when they feel ready. The market's readiness and the vendor's readiness are not the same timeline, and understanding the difference is part of what determines the outcome.
