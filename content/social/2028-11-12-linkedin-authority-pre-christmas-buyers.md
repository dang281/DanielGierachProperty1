## Caption

There is a consistent assumption in the Brisbane market that November is too late to list and get a strong result before Christmas.

In my experience, that assumption is wrong in the inner east.

The buyers who are still attending open homes in November are not the buyers who were curious about the market in September. Those buyers have either purchased or decided to wait for the new year. The buyers who are still active in November are the ones who have a specific requirement, a firm timeline, or both.

The interstate family relocating for a January school start needs to be under contract in November. They cannot wait for February. The upgrade buyer who missed out on three properties in October is carrying real urgency into November. The investor who has been circling a specific street for six months is not going to walk away because it is mid-November.

What this means for sellers: the buyers you are marketing to in November may be smaller in number, but they are higher in intent than the general spring buyer pool.

The risk is not selling in November. The risk is launching a property in November that is not ready, overpriced, or poorly presented to a buyer pool that has seen everything on the market and has a clear view of value.

Preparation and accurate pricing matter in any month. In November, they matter more, because the buyers who are still looking have nothing left to forgive.
