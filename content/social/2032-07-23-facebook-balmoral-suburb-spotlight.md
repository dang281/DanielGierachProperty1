## Caption

Balmoral sits at the southern end of the Bulimba-Hawthorne-Balmoral corridor, and it carries a character that is different from its northern neighbours: quieter, more residential, with larger lots and an outdoor living emphasis that the suburb's terrain naturally supports.

The elevation through the suburb's central and northern streets delivers views, in many cases toward the river or back across the ranges. Wynnum Road marks the southern boundary. The river frontage at the suburb's north is some of the most valued land in the inner east.

Balmoral State School anchors the primary catchment, and buyers with school-age children arrive at Balmoral having worked through the options in Hawthorne and Bulimba and found that Balmoral's entry price into the character home market is the more practical step up.

The buyers who arrive here from interstate, particularly from Sydney and Melbourne, tend to have a specific brief. They want character homes within practical distance of the CBD with genuine outdoor living rather than a nominal backyard. Balmoral delivers that more reliably than most addresses at comparable distance from the city.

For owners in Balmoral who are considering a sale, the buyer profile that arrives here is a motivated and researched one. The suburb speaks for itself when you bring the right buyers to the table.
