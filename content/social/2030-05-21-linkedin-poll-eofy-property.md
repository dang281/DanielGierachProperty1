## Caption

End of financial year has a real effect on property decisions, particularly for investors. Settlement timing relative to 30 June determines which financial year a capital gain falls into, which can make a meaningful difference to the tax outcome. Negative gearing claims also cease at settlement, which affects the year-end position for properties that have been running at a loss.

For owner-occupiers, the EOFY effect is subtler but still present. Some buyers push to settle before 30 June to lock in deductions on investment loans taken out during the year. Others prefer to settle in the new financial year for cash flow reasons.

Does end of financial year influence your property decisions?
