## Caption

Two houses on the same Hawthorne street can be $200,000 apart in value. Not because one is fundamentally different on paper, but because aspect, renovation quality, flood overlay, and the specific buyers attracted to each property produce very different results at sale.

This is why automated estimate tools, while useful as an opening reference, cannot give you an accurate picture of what your property is worth right now. These tools pull historical comparable sales and apply a formula. They do not know whether your property catches the north-east breeze, whether the renovation was done to a high standard or cut to a minimum, whether there is a drainage easement on the title, or whether your street has been particularly strong or soft over the past six months.

An automated estimate tells you the general price range a property type in your suburb tends to achieve. It cannot tell you where your specific property sits within that range.

The full article walks through how comparable sales analysis actually works, what separates a strong comparable from a weak one, and what to ask an agent before relying on any number from a portal. Worth reading before making any decisions based on an estimate.
