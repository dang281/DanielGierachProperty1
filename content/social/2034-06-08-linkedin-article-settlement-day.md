## Caption

Settlement day causes anxiety in proportion to how unfamiliar the process is. For most vendors, the day itself is quiet, largely handled by solicitors and PEXA, and over before lunch.

The practical sequence: the buyer's solicitor releases the funds through the PEXA platform. The vendor's solicitor confirms receipt, the mortgage is discharged, and the title transfers electronically. The keys, which have already been handed to the agent, are released to the buyer. Most vendors find out settlement has occurred via a text from their solicitor.

What happens before settlement day is where the vendor's attention actually belongs. The pre-settlement inspection, which the buyer is entitled to conduct, needs to leave the property in the condition it was at contract date. Removalists creating damage on move-out day and missing inclusions that were supposed to stay with the property are the most common sources of last-minute disputes.

Vendors who walk through the property themselves two to three days before settlement with the contract inclusions list in hand almost never have a problem. Those who leave it to chance occasionally do.

The settlement sequence, what PEXA does, what the solicitors handle, and what the vendor actually needs to do, is explained in full at danielgierach.com/insights/what-happens-on-settlement-day-queensland
