## Caption

Choosing an agent is one of the decisions sellers spend the least time on, relative to its importance.

The agent controls the narrative around your property during the campaign. They manage the relationship with every buyer who walks through. They handle the negotiation at the most consequential point in the process. The wrong choice costs money. The right choice typically earns it back many times over.

Local knowledge matters because comparable sales are the foundation of everything. An agent who knows which street sells for a premium over the one parallel to it, and why, is using real intelligence in the pricing conversation. One who doesn't is guessing.

How they present the property matters because first impressions are formed from the listing photos before a buyer has ever walked through the door. Most buyers decide whether to inspect based on what they see online.

Communication matters because a silent agent during a campaign is an anxious one. The sellers I work with hear from me after every open home and every private inspection. That feedback loop is how you know whether the strategy is working.

When choosing an agent to sell your home, what matters most?
