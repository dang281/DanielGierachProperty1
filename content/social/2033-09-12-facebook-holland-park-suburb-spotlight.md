## Caption

Holland Park's spring market produces a buyer pool that is notable for its range. The suburb attracts buyers from both directions: those upgrading from the more affordable eastern suburbs who identify Holland Park as the best inner east suburb they can reach at their budget, and those coming from the closer-in suburbs who are prioritising space and block size over proximity. That dual flow produces genuine buyer competition in spring, particularly for houses in the middle of the price range.

The proximity to the Ekka Arterial and the Pacific Motorway gives Holland Park residents transport flexibility that pure inner suburbs cannot match. Buyers with one or two car-dependent commutes, or those who manage regular regional travel, find Holland Park's motorway access a practical advantage that they weight alongside the suburb's other attributes.

The Holland Park shops and the surrounding café and dining strip give the suburb a local character that residents tend to describe with genuine affection. The weekend morning pedestrian activity around the local cafes is a reliable indicator of the kind of community environment that family buyers use as a proxy for their future experience of a suburb.

Vendors preparing a spring campaign in Holland Park typically find September their most productive window. The buyer pool is broadest and best prepared in the first half of spring.
