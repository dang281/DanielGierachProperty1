## Caption

Carindale is a suburb built around Westfield Carindale, one of the largest shopping centres in Queensland, and that retail anchor shapes daily life here in a way that buyers either find immediately appealing or decide is not for them. For households that value walkable access to a full retail, dining, and services precinct, the suburb delivers in a way that few others in Brisbane can match at this price point and distance from the CBD.

Beyond the centre, Carindale's residential streets are predominantly post-war brick and tile homes on substantial blocks, and streets like Carindale Street, Maygar Street, and those running through the Belmont Hills precinct carry a quiet character that is easy to miss if you only know the suburb from Old Cleveland Road. The elevated sections of the suburb carry long views south toward the ranges, and those properties attract buyers who have outgrown the tighter blocks of Camp Hill or Coorparoo.

Gateway Baptist College and a number of primary school options in and around the suburb give families a range of choices, and easy access to the Gateway Motorway makes the suburb practical for households with work or family commitments south of Brisbane. Carindale is also positioned well for buyers who want to stay connected to the inner east while taking on a larger home, given its proximity to Carina Heights and Norman Park.
