## Caption

Most vendors list with auction intending to run the full campaign. But the pre-auction offer conversation comes up on almost every inner east campaign, and how a seller thinks about it ahead of time often determines whether they make the right call on the day.

The case for running through to auction is strong: competition, transparency, and no cooling-off period. But a pre-auction offer that is significantly unconditional changes the maths. Not every seller thinks about this before a buyer puts one on the table.

If your property was scheduled for auction, what would make you accept an offer before auction day?

## Poll options

- A significantly above-reserve unconditional offer
- If the buyer asked and the numbers worked
- Never: I would always run the auction
- If buyer competition looked lower than expected going in
