## Caption

Carina Heights sits just far enough outside the spotlight that buyers often arrive here after starting their search somewhere else. It is elevated, quiet, and has bush reserves along its eastern edge that give the suburb a different feel from the denser inner east options. Families who find it tend to appreciate exactly that: the sense of space and the lower traffic compared with more prominent nearby suburbs.

The suburb has been attracting growing recognition from family buyers who have done a thorough search of Camp Hill and Carina and found Carina Heights offers comparable land content at a price that makes sense. The quiet streets suit the buyer demographic well, and the elevation provides pleasant outlooks from a number of blocks without the premium attached to the more established view suburbs.

October is a good time to be visible in this market. Buyers in the family-home stage who are doing serious research will look at Carina Heights when they understand the land and elevation story, and spring presentation conditions help tell it clearly. Properties that sit well on their blocks and have been looked after tend to move with less friction than in the cooler months.

If you are a Carina Heights owner considering your options this spring, I am happy to have a direct conversation about what the current market looks like from a seller's position.
