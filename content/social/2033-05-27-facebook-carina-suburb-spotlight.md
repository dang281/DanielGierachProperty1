## Caption

Carina sits at an interesting intersection in Brisbane's property market: it is close enough to the city at nine kilometres to be considered part of the inner east catchment, yet priced accessibly enough to attract families who have done the comparison with suburbs further in and decided the value trade-off is clear. That positioning has kept demand in Carina resilient across different market conditions, supported by a consistent pool of buyers who know the suburb and target it specifically.

The suburb's proximity to Westfield Carindale is a practical advantage that residents make use of daily. Major retail, cinema, dining and services available within a short drive changes the calculus of suburban living in a meaningful way, reducing the friction that can make outer suburb life feel inconvenient. Families appreciate that the shopping centre is close without being directly adjacent to the residential core.

Housing stock in Carina covers a range of conditions and eras, from 1960s and 1970s originals through to extensively renovated family homes. That breadth creates market segments at different price points and means buyers with different budgets and intentions can find their place. Current medians in Carina represent solid value relative to the inner east, and the suburb has shown consistent appreciation over the medium term as demand from families continues to underpin the market.

Public transport relies primarily on buses, and road access to the CBD via main arterials is reliable.
