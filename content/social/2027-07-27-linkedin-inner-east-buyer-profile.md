## Caption

The buyer who is actively searching in the inner east at the end of July is a specific type, and understanding that profile is useful information for anyone considering a campaign in the coming weeks.

They are typically owner-occupiers, not investors. They are buying for their household, usually because something has changed: a family growing into a property that no longer fits, a commute that has shifted, children approaching school age, or a stage of life that makes a different type of home more sensible. They are buying because they need to, not because the market has given them a reason to move.

They have finance sorted. The buyers who are still searching in July either have pre-approval in place and have been patient enough to wait for the right property, or they have been through the approval process enough times to know exactly what they can spend. They are not going to fall over because their lender needs another week.

They know the inner east. Not in a general way but specifically: they know the difference between the catchment boundary and the suburb boundary, they know which streets have flood overlays and which do not, and they have formed a view on which blocks represent genuine value at current prices.

For vendors with the right property, this buyer pool is ready to act. The question is whether the property and the campaign are ready for them.
