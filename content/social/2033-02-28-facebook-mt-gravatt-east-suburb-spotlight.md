## Caption

Mt Gravatt East in the peak autumn season is a suburb with two distinct buyer conversations happening simultaneously. The first is with investors who are focused on yield, vacancy, and the Griffith University proximity that provides a reliable tenant base. The second is with owner-occupiers who are weighing up the suburb's price point against what it offers in liveability and location.

The fact that both buyer types are active in the same suburb at the same time is a structural advantage for vendors. Properties that suit investors tend to attract competitive bidding from a pool that is assessing rental yield and capital growth potential. Properties that suit owner-occupiers draw buyers making a liveability calculation. When both types are competing for a home that bridges those two profiles, the outcome for a vendor is consistently better than when only one buyer type is active. ⚠️ VERIFY

The Westfield Garden City proximity reduces the practical friction of suburban life for residents in a way that is easy to underestimate. Access to quality retail, a cinema complex, and specialty services within a short drive removes one of the standard compromises of living further from the inner east.

For vendors approaching late February with a well-maintained property in Mt Gravatt East, the autumn season is active and the two-buyer-pool dynamic is working in your favour. Pricing correctly for where that combined demand sits is the critical decision.
