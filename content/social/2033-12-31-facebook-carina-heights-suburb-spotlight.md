## Caption

Carina Heights sits on elevated ground that looks out over the surrounding inner east suburbs, and that position gives many of its residential streets a sense of space and outlook that is hard to find in the flatter suburbs at a comparable price point. The quiet street character and strong owner-occupier community feel are things that buyers who arrive from Camp Hill or Coorparoo often notice immediately as a shared quality.

Westfield Carindale is one of the most convenient shopping and lifestyle precincts in Brisbane's south-east, and its proximity means Carina Heights residents have access to daily retail, dining, and services that rivals many inner-city options. For families, that practical convenience is a quality-of-life factor mentioned at open homes.

Mount Gravatt State High School catchment covers Carina Heights, and that is the school access that many families in this suburb are specifically seeking. It puts Carina Heights in the same catchment conversation as Carina, Seven Hills, and parts of Mt Gravatt East, and it draws the same profile of family buyer who has researched catchment access carefully before deciding where to buy.

The buyer profile I see most often in Carina Heights is families who have looked seriously at Camp Hill and Coorparoo and found those suburbs beyond their budget. They are choosing Carina Heights because the school access, elevated position, residential quality, and Westfield Carindale proximity deliver what they want at a genuinely accessible price point.
