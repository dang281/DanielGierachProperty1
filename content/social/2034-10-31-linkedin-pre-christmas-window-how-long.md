## Caption

If you want to be under contract before Christmas 2034, you have roughly four weeks of effective campaign time left from now.

Here is the maths. A standard inner east campaign runs four to six weeks from the first open home to contract. Buyers need time to do finance, building and pest, and make a decision. Add the typical one to two week preparation phase after you sign with an agent, and you are looking at a window that closes in early November for a pre-Christmas outcome.

That does not mean properties stop selling in December. They do sell. But the pool of active buyers with finance pre-approved and a decision-ready mindset thins out considerably from late November onwards. The buyers who remain tend to be either highly motivated or very patient, and motivations diverge in ways that make negotiation harder.

The most common mistake I see in October and November is vendors assuming they have more time than they do. They take another few weeks to make the listing decision, then rush the preparation, then wonder why the campaign did not have the same energy as the ones they saw earlier in spring.

If you have been thinking about selling, the decision you make this week has a direct effect on the quality of your campaign outcome.
