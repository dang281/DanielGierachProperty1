## Caption

The Brisbane 2032 Olympics is now approximately 18 months away. For inner east property, that proximity is worth understanding clearly rather than responding to emotionally.

The infrastructure effect on Brisbane has been building for several years. Cross River Rail, the Kangaroo Point Bridge, and transport upgrades across the inner east have materially changed how quickly buyers can access the CBD and inner city venues. That infrastructure is already priced into the market to some degree. The question is whether the final 18 months generates an additional uplift or whether the expectation of Olympic benefit has already been captured.

The more immediate effect for inner east homeowners is the accommodation demand story. With major event venues concentrated in and around Brisbane's inner suburbs, proximity to the Olympic precinct is a factor international buyers and property investors are actively researching. Accommodation demand during the Games themselves is a short-term event. Long-term price support comes from the sustained infrastructure, population growth, and international profile lift that tends to follow major Games cities.

For vendors considering a late 2030 or 2031 sale, the Olympic narrative is one context point among several. It supports Brisbane's long-term investment case. It is not a reason to rush to market or to inflate expectations above what current comparable sales support.

The data for the inner east remains what it always was: the right property, presented well, at a price supported by evidence, sells. More at danielgierach.com.
