## Caption

When a property sells quickly and above reserve in June or July, people tend to treat it as a seasonal anomaly. The market was slow, someone overpaid, unusual circumstances. That reading is almost always wrong.

A strong winter result is not an accident. It is evidence. Specifically, it is evidence that genuine demand for this type of property at this price point exceeds the genuine supply currently available. The buyer who paid above reserve in winter did not overpay relative to what the market was willing to bear at that moment. They paid what the competition required. That is the market doing its job.

For sellers preparing a spring campaign, these winter results deserve close attention. The buyers who competed on that winter auction, including the ones who lost, are still in the market. They have not all found something else. A buyer who bid to a strong number in June and missed has a very specific brief and a high level of commitment. They know the price. They have finance to the level required. They know what they want. When a comparable property comes to market in September, they will be among the first through the door and the first to make an offer.

The best intelligence for a spring campaign is not a forecast or a median price report. It is a clear picture of what cleared in winter, who was buying it, and which of those buyers is still looking. That information is available. It just requires someone who has been paying attention.

If you are planning a spring campaign, the research phase starts now, not in August. danielgierach.com
