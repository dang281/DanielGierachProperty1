## Caption

The decision to wait until February is rarely wrong. Autumn is a strong selling window in Brisbane's inner east. But it carries a cost that most vendors do not fully account for.

Waiting until February means carrying the property through December, January, and the first few weeks of February before a campaign even launches. That is roughly ten to twelve weeks of ownership costs, maintenance, and opportunity cost on the proceeds.

On a $1.3 million property, ownership costs including rates, insurance, and mortgage interest at current rates run at approximately $4,000 to $5,000 per month. Twelve weeks of holding adds $12,000 to $15,000 before the property earns a dollar. That figure is rarely in the calculation when vendors say they will wait and see.

The buyer pool available in late November is also different from the February pool in a useful way: they are time-pressured. A buyer who needs to settle before Christmas is not spending three weekends thinking about it. They are making decisions on the first or second inspection. That urgency, in a market with limited well-priced stock, consistently produces strong results.

February will be fine. But November, with a prepared property and realistic pricing, is not a lesser option. It is a different one with its own specific advantages.
