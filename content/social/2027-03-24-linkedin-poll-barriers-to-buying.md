## Caption

Brisbane property prices have grown significantly over the past four years. The entry point for the inner east has moved well beyond what many buyers expected when they started saving.

At the same time, buyer enquiry remains strong. The people who want to buy in suburbs like Norman Park, Carina, and Morningside are still active. Many of them are just working through barriers that did not exist three or four years ago.

Understanding what is actually stopping buyers from moving is a useful exercise. The barrier shapes the solution: whether that means a different suburb, a different property type, a different deposit strategy, or a different timeline.

What is your biggest barrier to buying in Brisbane right now?

## Poll options

- Deposit savings are not keeping pace with prices
- My borrowing capacity is not sufficient for the suburb I want
- I am waiting for prices to correct before buying
- I am ready but have not found the right property yet
