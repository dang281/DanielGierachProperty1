## Caption

The buyers at a winter open home are self-selecting. They left the house on a cold morning, found parking, and walked through your property. That is not casual browsing.

Volume drops in winter. The tyre-kicker pool stays home. What turns up on your doorstep in July is, on average, more committed than the same number of buyers in October. They are pre-approved or close to it. They have usually been looking for a while. They are frustrated by how little is on the market, which works in a seller's favour.

This is the part of the winter conversation most agents skip. They talk about fewer buyers without talking about better buyers. The clearance rate does not collapse in winter. Homes priced correctly and presented well still sell in 15 to 20 days. What changes is that you have less margin for error on price.

A seller who goes to market in July with a realistic appraisal and a clean, well-maintained home is not fighting the season. They are competing against almost nothing. The spring market has more buyers but also far more stock. Choosing between those two realities is a strategic decision, not a seasonal reflex.

If you are weighing up whether to list now or hold until September, the answer depends on your property and your buyer profile, not the month.
