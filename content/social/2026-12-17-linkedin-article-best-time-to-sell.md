## Caption

Most of what gets written about the best time to sell property in Australia is based on Sydney and Melbourne data. Brisbane's inner east market behaves differently, and the conventional advice does not always translate.

The spring narrative, that September to November is when buyers come out and sellers should list, holds broadly for cooler-climate cities where winter genuinely suppresses activity. In Brisbane, winter is one of the most active selling periods. The weather is mild, open home attendance is strong, and the buyers who have held back from the humidity of summer are back in the market from July.

What I consistently see in the inner east is that autumn, specifically late February through May, is Brisbane's most reliable selling window. The summer school holiday period is over, families are settled, interstate buyers who visited over Christmas are making decisions, and stock levels have not yet hit their autumn peak. Properties that launch in late February or March face a fresh buyer pool that has been largely inactive since December.

Spring is a strong window. But it is not uniquely so, and it brings more competition from other listings. Autumn often produces better individual outcomes with less competition.

For anyone planning to sell in 2027, this distinction matters when you are deciding whether to list in February versus waiting until the conventional September sprint.
