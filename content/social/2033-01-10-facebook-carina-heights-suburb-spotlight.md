## Caption

Carina Heights sits quietly between the suburban energy of Carindale and the older housing pockets of Carina and Camp Hill. It is the kind of suburb that does not appear on many people's shortlist by name, but that buyers end up in when they realise how far their budget goes relative to the suburb immediately to the west.

The streets here are predominantly residential and family-oriented. The housing stock is a mix of post-war homes, 1970s and 80s brick, and more recent infill. The proximity to Westfield Carindale is a practical advantage that buyers with families appreciate: quality retail, cinemas, and services within a short drive without the premium pricing of suburbs closer to the CBD. ⚠️ VERIFY

The buyers who typically look at Carina Heights are making a considered calculation. They have priced themselves out of Camp Hill or are not finding the land size they need in Carina proper. Carina Heights gives them a quieter street character, more generous block sizes in parts of the suburb, and access to the same school catchments that draw people to the broader area.

For vendors in 2033, Carina Heights represents a market with genuine demand from buyers who are pragmatic and motivated. They are not buying here for the postcode. They are buying for the value and the liveability.
