## Caption

Most vendors selling with a mortgage start the conversation by doing a quick mental calculation: sale price minus loan balance equals what they walk away with. That calculation is wrong, usually by tens of thousands of dollars.

The costs that sit between sale price and net proceeds include agent commission, conveyancing, marketing, and the mortgage discharge fee. On a property selling for $1.4 million, those combined costs typically sit between $35,000 and $56,000. The net position after mortgage discharge is materially lower than the initial estimate.

For vendors on a fixed-rate loan, there is an additional variable that often does not get calculated until the campaign is underway: the break cost. Fixed-rate break costs depend on the outstanding loan balance, the remaining fixed term, and current wholesale interest rates. On a large loan with meaningful term remaining, the break cost can reach five figures. This should be confirmed with the lender before the campaign launches, not after an offer is accepted.
