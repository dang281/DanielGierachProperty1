## Caption

Gross yield is annual rent divided by purchase price. Net yield strips out the costs: rates, insurance, body corporate fees, management fees, and maintenance.

The gap between gross and net is often $8,000 to $15,000 per year on a typical Brisbane inner-east property. That gap is the difference between an investment that looks good on paper and one that actually performs.

When you're assessing a rental property, which number do you work from?
