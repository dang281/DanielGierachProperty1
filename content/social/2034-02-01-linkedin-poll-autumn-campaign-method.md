## Caption

Brisbane's inner east sees all three sale methods used regularly: auction, private treaty, and expression of interest. Each suits a different property type, price point, and buyer pool.

Auction works well when buyer competition is high and the vendor wants an unconditional result on the day. Private treaty gives buyers room to include conditions, which can widen the pool for higher-value or unique properties. Expression of interest creates a structured discovery process without the public pressure of an auction room.

The right method is not about vendor preference alone. It is about the property's likely buyer profile and how many of them are in the market at that moment. Getting this wrong costs money.

If you are listing your inner east home this autumn, which sale method would you choose?
