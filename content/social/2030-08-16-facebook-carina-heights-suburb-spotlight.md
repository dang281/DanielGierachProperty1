## Caption

Carina Heights offers something that buyers coming out of Coorparoo and Camp Hill increasingly want: larger blocks, leafy streets, and genuine space, without moving to the outer suburbs. The housing stock here, largely built between the 1960s and 1980s, sits on sites that would be significantly more expensive closer to the river, and buyers who understand land value are working that equation carefully.

The buyer profile arriving in Carina Heights is dominated by families who have looked at Camp Hill and Coorparoo, found the blocks smaller than expected, and turned here to get the lot size they actually need. They are also finding good school catchments and quiet residential streets that suit the lifestyle they are after. This is not a suburb buyers stumble into. They research it specifically.

What sells best in Carina Heights is original homes on larger blocks that offer clear renovation or extension potential. Buyers in this suburb are often planning a longer-term project and they are looking at the land as much as the dwelling. Well-presented homes on well-sized lots attract genuine competition at inspection.

The suburb sits between two well-established and well-priced markets, which gives Carina Heights a logical position for buyers seeking the next step. That positioning is strengthening as inner east affordability continues to compress.
