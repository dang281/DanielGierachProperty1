## Caption

Listing descriptions in Brisbane's inner east are almost identical. Light-filled. Entertainer's layout. Sought-after pocket. These phrases tell a buyer almost nothing about whether a property solves their actual problem. They are filler.

What actually produces multiple offers in this market is specific. School catchments matter more than most sellers realise. A property sitting inside the Cannon Hill Anglican, Balmoral State High, or Lourdes Hill catchment is in a different conversation to one a street outside it. Buyers with school-age children do not treat that as a minor preference. It is often the primary filter.

North to rear aspect is not marketing language. In a Brisbane summer, a house oriented correctly runs 4 to 6 degrees cooler through the afternoon. Buyers who have lived in the wrong-facing house know this. They will pay for it.

Flat land on a quiet street is finite. The inner east does not have much of it. A 600 square metre flat block within a few kilometres of the CBD is not a standard commodity. Buyers who want to build or substantially renovate understand the premium.

The ability to renovate without heritage constraints removes a layer of cost and delay that is not trivial. Buyers with a clear renovation plan will pay more for a property that gives them a clean run.

A floorplan where the master bedroom is separated from young children's rooms, or where the living zones work for a couple with toddlers and a home office, does not show up in any listing description. But it is often the thing that converts an interested buyer into a competing one.

The properties that draw genuine competition have usually solved one of these problems clearly. danielgierach.com
