## Caption

Autumn in the inner east produces excellent results and disappointing ones. The difference is rarely timing. It is preparation.

The sellers who perform in March launched with proper groundwork: a building report reviewed before listing, a styled property where the brief matched the target buyer, photography done on a clear morning with the right lens, and a price that reflected the market rather than the seller's aspiration. That combination is not complicated, but it takes four to eight weeks to execute properly.

The sellers who are still on the market in May are typically the ones who rushed the preparation. They launched too quickly, with too little done to the presentation, and with a price that the buyer pool rejected quietly and collectively. By May, the best buyers from March have already bought something else.

Here is what four to eight weeks of proper preparation actually looks like in the inner east:

Weeks one and two: Declutter, remove excess furniture, complete any defect repairs identified in the building inspection. Engage a stylist and confirm the brief.

Weeks three and four: Styling installation, photography and video, floorplan completed. Marketing copy reviewed and approved.

Weeks five and six: Campaign launches. Open homes begin. Buyer feedback is gathered and assessed.

This is not rushed, but it is not padded either. Sellers who start this process now are positioned to launch in mid-March, which is historically the strongest window in the inner east calendar.

If you are thinking about autumn and you haven't had a preparation conversation yet, the right time to have it is this week.
