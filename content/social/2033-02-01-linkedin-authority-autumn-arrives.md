## Caption

The first weekend of February marks the start of the autumn selling season in Brisbane's inner east. Listings that have been sitting in pre-market preparation for weeks go live. Buyers who have been watching the calendar start attending open homes with intent.

What buyer turnout in the first week of autumn tells me: are groups larger than late January, which suggests latent demand was held back by holiday timing, or are they similar, which suggests the pool is steady rather than surging. Both have implications for how the season will unfold. ⚠️ VERIFY

Morningside, Cannon Hill and East Brisbane tend to attract the entry-level and mid-market buyers who are rate-sensitive. Their attendance numbers give a useful read on how the finance environment is sitting. Hawthorne, Bulimba and Balmoral attract buyers for whom rates are a consideration but not the primary constraint. Those groups are more driven by whether the right property has appeared.

The post-Olympic context for 2033 is worth noting plainly. Some sellers have been waiting since 2031 expecting an Olympic price surge that would justify listing. That surge, at the levels some anticipated, did not eventuate broadly. What Brisbane's inner east has is a fundamentally sound market with genuine demand, sensible days-on-market, and buyers who are doing their homework. That is a good market to sell into. It just requires realistic expectations and proper preparation.

The season is here. The time to act is now.
