## Caption

Three numbers matter in a property transaction, and confusing them is one of the most common mistakes buyers and sellers make. Market value, list price, and auction reserve are related but distinct, and understanding the difference changes how you approach a campaign.

Market value is the price a willing buyer and a willing seller agree on in an open, competitive market. It is derived from comparable sales, current buyer demand, and the specific attributes of the property. It is not what the vendor paid, not what they need to achieve, and not the figure on their rates notice. It is a current number based on current market evidence.

A list price or price guide is the figure used to attract buyers to the campaign. A well-set list price draws in the right buyer pool. An inflated list price filters out buyers who would otherwise transact and creates a campaign that stalls. In Queensland, agents must comply with underquoting legislation, which means the price guide must reflect the agent's genuine opinion of market value.

An auction reserve is the minimum the vendor will accept under the hammer. It is set before the auction, is not disclosed to bidders, and is agreed between the vendor and the agent. A reserve set above market value produces a pass-in. A reserve set at market value allows the auction to find the price the competitive buyer pool will pay.
