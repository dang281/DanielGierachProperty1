## Caption

The Brisbane Olympic Games are seven months away. That fact is worth holding in context without letting it distort your decisions.

There is real, documented buyer interest in the inner east suburbs heading into 2032. Bulimba, Hawthorne, Morningside, Cannon Hill and the surrounding areas are all within reasonable proximity to venues and transport corridors that will matter during the Games. That proximity has a genuine value signal attached to it, and buyers know it.

But the opportunity is not in July or August, when 3,500 athletes and 10,000 officials are trying to get around the city. The opportunity for buyers is right now, in the first quarter. People who settle before the Games avoid the disruption, the vendor expectations inflated by hype, and the risk that conditions have shifted by the time the closing ceremony runs.

For sellers, the advice is different. The vendors who maximise their result in an Olympic year are not the ones who wait for the crowds to arrive. They are the ones who have completed their preparation, chosen a clear strategy, and taken their property to market in the autumn window, when Brisbane's inner east historically performs best.

The Games will generate noise. Some of it will be useful signal. Most of it will not. The buyers who move with a clear brief in January and February will, on balance, be better positioned than the ones who wait to see what happens.

If you are thinking about buying or selling in the inner east this year, the first conversation is worth having now.
