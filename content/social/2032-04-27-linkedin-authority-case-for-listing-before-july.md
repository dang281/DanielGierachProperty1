## Caption

A few weeks ago I wrote about why Q2 2032 is a distinct selling window. Today I want to be more specific, because "list before July" is the right move for some sellers and the wrong move for others.

The case for listing now: if your property is ready, you have flexibility on settlement timing, and you want certainty over speculation, then running a campaign in May or June gives you a settled result before the Games begin. You avoid the unknown of what August looks like. Buyers who want to be in before the Olympics are still active and motivated. The comparable sales supporting your price are current and strong.

That case holds if your property presents well, your suburb has active buyer demand, and your personal timeline is flexible enough to move if an offer comes in on your preferred terms.

The case against: if your property needs work before it's market-ready, the preparation time means you can't realistically list until June anyway. A rushed campaign that starts too late and settles during the Games period is worse than a well-prepared campaign after August. Similarly, if your personal circumstances mean you can't settle until September or later, there's no benefit in rushing the listing.

There's also a property type consideration. Some properties, particularly larger family homes in well-established streets, tend to attract buyers who are not driven by Games timing at all. For those, the urgency argument is weaker.

My honest advice: assess your readiness before you assess the calendar.
