## Caption

The settlement date on an investment property sale is not just a logistics question. It determines which financial year the capital gain falls into. For investors who have held a property in Brisbane's inner east through a period of significant capital growth, that timing decision can shift a meaningful tax liability from one year to the next.

The calculation is straightforward in principle and frequently overlooked in practice. Settlement is the date the sale completes, not the date contracts are exchanged. If a property exchanges in late June with a 30-day settlement, it settles in late July, in the new financial year. The capital gain is assessed in the year of settlement. For an investor managing their overall tax position, the difference between settling on June 28 and settling on July 5 can be a full financial year of cash flow on a substantial gain.

This matters more for the inner east than for many other markets because the capital growth over recent years has been significant enough that the tax liability itself is a significant number. Getting the settlement date right is not an afterthought. It is part of the campaign planning.

The article covers the negative gearing position for investors who are approaching the decision to sell, what the CGT calculation involves, and how the timing of a campaign can be managed to align with a tax-efficient outcome.
