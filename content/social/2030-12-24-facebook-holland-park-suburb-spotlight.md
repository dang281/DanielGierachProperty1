## Caption

Christmas Eve in Holland Park. For the homeowners here who have spent 2030 thinking about whether next year is the year to sell, the summer period is when that decision tends to crystallise.

Holland Park's appeal is practical in a way that a lot of the more prominent inner east suburbs are not. The streets north of Logan Road deliver the block sizes and the school catchment access that family buyers target, without the premium that living in Hawthorne or Balmoral commands. That value proposition has been consistent through the market cycle.

Cavendish Road State High School is the key secondary catchment for the northern section of the suburb, and the catchment line has a visible effect on pricing that buyers with secondary school-aged children navigate carefully. Properties inside the catchment boundary on Holland Park's northern streets carry a premium that comparable properties outside the boundary do not.

For homeowners who are planning to list in February or March 2031, the preparation process, decluttering, minor repairs, photography planning, and the initial conversation with a solicitor about the disclosure statement, is best started in January. Not February.

The Holland Park market in the first quarter of 2031 will benefit from the buyer decisions being made right now, over the Christmas and New Year period. Those buyers will be ready when the new listings arrive in February.
