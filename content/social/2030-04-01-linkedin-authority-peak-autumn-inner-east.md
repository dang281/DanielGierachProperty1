## Caption

April is when the inner east autumn market sharpens. Open home numbers are consistently strong, buyers are pre-approved, and the properties generating the most competition are the ones where the preparation has been done properly.

I walked three open homes in Camp Hill and Coorparoo over the past fortnight. Every one of them had more than 20 registered buyers through the door. The common thread: well-presented, correctly priced, and positioned before the school holidays pulled attention away.

What I am watching right now is the gap between vendor expectation and market feedback. Sellers who listed in February and priced at the top of the comparable range have adjusted. Those who priced with precision from the start are settling at or above reserve.

The other thing worth noting: buyer profiles in the inner east are skewing older this autumn. Upgraders from Camp Hill to Bulimba, from Carina to Norman Park. Families with kids already in school catchments, not looking to move catchments, looking to stay in the suburb but move up the property ladder.

That profile is worth understanding if you are a seller. These buyers are not emotional. They know the suburb, they know the streets, and they will not overpay for poor presentation. What they will pay is a very strong price for the right property.

If you are thinking about listing before winter, the next four weeks are the window. Not the last week of April. Now.
