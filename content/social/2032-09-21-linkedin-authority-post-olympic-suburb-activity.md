## Caption

Seven weeks after the Closing Ceremony and the inner east is in a clear spring pattern.

The Bulimba to Hawthorne river corridor is seeing the strongest buyer activity at owner-occupier price points above $1.5 million. Well-presented homes with aspect, good floor plans, and strong school catchment are attracting multiple groups at opens and producing competitive results at auction. This corridor has held depth through the entire Olympic period and the spring rebound here has been genuine.

Camp Hill, Norman Park, and Morningside are attracting good competition in the family home segment. Buyers who were priced out of the closer river-corridor suburbs over the past two years have formed a solid pool in these streets. The price differential to Hawthorne and Bulimba is still enough to make these suburbs attractive to buyers who want the inner east without the river premium.

Coorparoo and Carina are active with buyers who are doing the same calculation one step further out. They want the inner east fundamentals, including good schools, suburb amenity, and access to the city, but need a price point that reflects the absence of a river outlook. Those buyers are there and they are competitive.

The common thread across all of these suburbs in September is buyer intent. The people walking through open homes are not browsing. They have been watching the market since at least June and they are ready to act on the right property at the right price.
