## Caption

An auction reserve is the minimum price a seller is willing to accept on auction day. Below the reserve, the property passes in. At or above it, the auctioneer can knock it down to the highest bidder.

How that reserve number actually gets set is one of the most consequential decisions in the campaign, and the way it lands differs noticeably from one seller to another. Some anchor it to the agent's initial appraisal range. Some hold to a personal bottom line set months earlier. Others let comparable sales from the past 60 to 90 days dictate it. A more recent practice is to let the registered bidder feedback in the final week shape the final number.

How should an auction reserve be set? Vote your gut answer.
