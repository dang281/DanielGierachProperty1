## Caption

The streets east of Logan Road in Holland Park have a quietness to them that buyers notice early. The suburb sits on elevated ground, and on a clear morning the city skyline sits on the horizon in a way that reminds you how close the CBD actually is. It is an area that rewards buyers who look a little further than the obvious inner-east postcodes.

Holland Park State School anchors the family market here, and the catchment has been a reliable draw for households making a considered move. Those buyers tend to be thorough and patient, and when they find the right property they move on it decisively.

December afternoons in Holland Park feel genuinely summer: longer light, the smell of grass, the kind of weather that photographs well and makes a property feel liveable. Owners who list at this time of year often find presentation comes naturally. The harder question is usually about timing: whether to push before Christmas or to hold until February. Both have merit, but a conversation about your specific property and position makes that decision much clearer.

If 2033 is the year you plan to move, getting a current appraisal before the holiday break is a sensible starting point.
