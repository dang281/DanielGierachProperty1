## Caption

Having Westfield Carindale on the doorstep is not a minor convenience. For families and owner-occupiers in Carindale, the level of daily accessibility that the shopping centre provides is something that shapes how residents experience the suburb in practical, everyday terms. Grocery, health, retail, and dining all within walking distance or a short drive. Very few Brisbane suburbs have a comparable anchor at that scale.

The housing stock here leans toward older character homes on larger blocks, and that combination is increasingly valued by buyers who want the footprint to renovate or extend without moving into the inner city price bracket. Carindale offers the block size and the renovation potential at price points that are genuinely accessible compared to what equivalent land costs in Bulimba, Hawthorne, or Camp Hill.

Owner-occupier retention in Carindale is strong. The suburb has low turnover, which reflects a resident base that has found what it was looking for and stayed. When properties do come to market, they tend to attract buyers who have been watching the suburb carefully and are ready to act.

The Mount Gravatt State High School catchment and good bus access to Brisbane CBD add to the practical case for families comparing Carindale to surrounding suburbs. The combination of daily convenience, land size, school access, and relative value is a straightforward story that buyers in this part of the market understand.

If you own in Carindale and are considering whether now is the right time to act, I am happy to give you a current and honest assessment.
