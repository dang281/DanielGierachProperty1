## Caption

A significant share of buyers at inner east open homes in recent years have come from interstate, primarily from Sydney and Melbourne. They bring a different frame of reference and it changes how negotiations run.

The Sydney buyer who has sold a semi-detached in Balmain and is moving to Brisbane with $2 million to spend does not look at a character Queenslander on 600 square metres in Hawthorne the way a Brisbane buyer does. They are not anchored to Brisbane's historical price points. They are anchored to what they paid in Sydney, and on that comparison, Brisbane feels like value.

That is genuinely good news for inner east sellers. When a high-quality property draws both a well-qualified Brisbane buyer and a well-qualified interstate buyer, competition develops in a way it might not otherwise.

What changes is the buyer education requirement. Interstate buyers sometimes misunderstand Queensland-specific contracts, the disclosure process, or the flood overlay system. They may not have a local solicitor or a clear sense of the school catchments. An agent who manages those conversations well converts interstate interest into conditional offers rather than losing them to unfamiliarity.

For sellers in the Hawthorne, Balmoral, and Bulimba price bracket, understanding the interstate buyer pool is not an optional insight. It is the market.
