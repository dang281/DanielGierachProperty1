## Caption

Coorparoo is one of the inner east's most active markets. It has the density of buyer interest that comes with strong school catchments, walkable commercial strips, and a housing mix broad enough to accommodate buyers at multiple price points.

The Coorparoo Square precinct and the Old Cleveland Road strip give residents retail and dining options that reduced the suburb's reliance on the CBD long before hybrid working reshaped commuter patterns. The train line running through Coorparoo station adds to the suburb's commuter credentials.

School catchment boundaries matter a great deal here. Coorparoo State School and the broader secondary catchment network draw families who are buying the school zone as much as the home. Buyers in this cohort research catchments thoroughly and will pay a premium to be inside the right boundaries.

The housing stock covers a wide range: renovated character Queenslanders and post-war homes at the top end, knockdown-and-rebuild sites for developers, and an established apartment and townhouse market along the major arterials. That diversity keeps the buyer pool large and the market more liquid than smaller, more homogeneous suburbs.

For vendors, Coorparoo rewards accurate pricing. A well-presented home at the right price moves quickly because the buyer pool is large. An overpriced home can sit even when demand is strong, because buyers in this suburb are typically experienced and have comparable sales memorised.
