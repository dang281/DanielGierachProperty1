## Caption

Most homeowners selling their primary residence have a mortgage in place. The process is straightforward in most cases, but there are a handful of things worth confirming before the campaign launches rather than finding out mid-settlement.

Net equity is not sale price minus loan balance. By the time you add agent commission, conveyancing fees, marketing costs, and the mortgage discharge fee, the actual net proceeds are meaningfully lower than that calculation suggests. For a seller who needs a specific amount from the sale to fund their next purchase, working from the wrong figure creates a problem.

The mortgage discharge fee is set by your lender and varies. On a standard variable rate loan it is typically a few hundred dollars. On a fixed rate loan with time still to run, the break cost can be several thousand dollars and is calculated using a formula that most lenders will provide only on request.

If you are on a fixed rate loan, your break cost should be confirmed before you commit to a settlement date. That cost goes up or down depending on the interest rate environment at the time of settlement. Getting the number at the start of the campaign and again when you are negotiating settlement terms gives you the most accurate picture.

The full article covers what to notify your lender, when to do it, and how to calculate your actual net proceeds.
