## Caption

Investor buyers in Brisbane's inner east do not take winters off. The fundamentals they are buying do not change with the season.

Vacancy rates in the inner east have remained consistently tight for several years. Rental demand is structural, not cyclical. The tenants the inner east attracts, professionals, couples, small families who want walkability and proximity to the city, are not a seasonal population. An investor buying in Hawthorne or Bulimba or Morningside is buying into that demand profile, and that profile is the same in July as it is in November.

What does change in winter is the composition of the buyer pool at auction. Owner-occupier buyers, particularly families with school-age children, are less active between June and August. For an investor at an inner east auction in July, that means less competition from the buyer type most likely to stretch on price. Investors tend to buy to numbers: yield, vacancy, depreciation, capital growth trajectory. They do not bid emotionally. When the emotional buyers are less present, the investor's numbers-based approach can land more effectively.

At inspections, the questions I hear from investor buyers are consistent regardless of season: current tenancy status and rental income, vacancy history, strata levy amounts for units, and proximity to transport. The property types that attract the most investor interest in the inner east are the ones with low maintenance requirements, strong tenant demand, and clear depreciation schedules.

If your property suits that buyer profile, July is a reasonable time to be in front of them. danielgierach.com
