## Caption

Setting an asking price is one of the most consequential decisions in a property sale. Get it wrong in either direction and the result is the same: a longer campaign, more stress, and usually a lower final price than a correctly priced property would have achieved.

There is a lot of disagreement among vendors about what matters most when landing on a price. Some defer entirely to the agent's advice. Others anchor on what the neighbour got. Others set a number based on what they need to cover their costs.

What actually matters most when you are deciding on an asking price?

## Poll options

- Comparable sales in the suburb
- The agent's professional recommendation
- What I need to cover mortgage and costs
- What I think the property is worth
