## Caption

The late October open home numbers are one of the more reliable signals I watch each year.

When registered buyer numbers hold or increase through the third and fourth weekends of October, it tells me the spring market has absorbed the new stock without being overwhelmed. Buyers are still looking, still engaged, and the competitive tension that drives good results is still present.

When those numbers start to soften, it usually means one of two things: the quality of new stock coming to market has declined, or the buyer pool has found what it was looking for and is making decisions rather than continuing to look.

In either case, what tends to happen from late October into November is a gradual compression of buyer attention. There are still motivated buyers, but they are more specific. They are not looking at everything. They are looking at the one or two properties that genuinely match what they want.

For sellers still to launch: the November market is not a write-off. It is a different market. The general buyer volume is lower, but the buyers who are present are more motivated. Properties that suit a specific buyer type and are priced to reflect the comparable evidence still sell well through November.

Properties launched in the second half of November with Christmas approaching are working against the clock.
