## Caption

Setting the reserve price is one of the most consequential decisions a vendor makes before auction day. Too high and you risk passing in with no bidding momentum, which weakens your negotiating position considerably. Too low and you leave money on the table before the competition even gets started.

Most sellers set the reserve in the week before auction and rarely revisit it as the day approaches, even as new information comes in from the campaign. The final open home attendance, the conversations buyers are having, the number of registered bidders: all of that is relevant to where the reserve should sit, and it is worth a fresh conversation with your agent the day before.

But despite careful preparation, auctions do pass in. When that happens, what you do in the next 30 minutes matters enormously.

If your property passed in at auction, what would you do next?
