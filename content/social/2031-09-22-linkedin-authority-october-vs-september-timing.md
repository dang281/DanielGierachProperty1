## Caption

The timing question I field most often at this point of the year is whether a vendor should launch now in September or hold on until October. Both months have genuine arguments behind them and I want to set those out honestly.

The argument for October is not imaginary. Buyer volume is higher. The weather is warmer, which affects the feeling of open homes and the state of gardens. More buyers are searching in October than September, and for some property types, that increased pool produces stronger competition on auction day or during the offer period.

The argument for September is structural. Competing listing numbers in September have not yet reached the October and November peak. Buyers who have been active since winter are in the market and motivated. They have already done their research on suburbs and price ranges and they are looking for the right property rather than still forming their preferences. A property that launches in late September is entering a market where buyer conviction is high and listing alternatives are still relatively limited.

My honest assessment, based on what I observe consistently across spring campaigns in the inner east, is that the September launch advantage typically outweighs the October buyer volume advantage for any property where preparation is complete and the vendor is genuinely ready to go.

The qualification matters. A property that is not fully prepared and launched in September to capture the window still does not benefit from the timing. The timing advantage only adds to an already sound campaign.

If you are ready, September is almost always the right answer. If you are not ready, spend the time to get there rather than rushing a launch into a timing window that works against you because the property is not yet at its best.
