## Caption

September in the inner east means the volume of listings starting to lift, the open home calendars getting busier, and the buyers who have been patient through winter starting to move faster.

What I watch at the start of spring is the stock quality, not the stock volume. Every spring brings more listings. The question is whether the listings are the deferred, well-prepared campaigns that chose spring deliberately, or the rushed campaigns that should have started in August but launched underprepared because September felt like the right month.

The well-prepared spring campaigns in the inner east compete on quality and win on price. The underprepared ones join a crowded field with a property that is not yet presenting at its best, and they tend to sit.

The other thing I watch early in spring is the auction clearance rate. It is the most immediate market signal available. A clearance rate above 70 per cent tells you buyers are competing and sellers are pricing within the evidence. A rate below 60 per cent tells you the opposite: sellers are coming to market at prices above what buyers are willing to pay under competitive conditions. Right now, September is the tell.

If you are watching the spring market, the clearance rate data for the next three or four weekends will tell you more about the direction of the inner east than any forecast.
