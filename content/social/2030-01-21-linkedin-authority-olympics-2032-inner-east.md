## Caption

The 2032 Olympics is now two years away. That is close enough that the questions buyers were asking in 2026 have answers.

The infrastructure is visible. The Gabba precinct works are underway. The Chandler corridor is active. Buyers who made decisions in 2026 and 2027 on the basis that Olympic investment would follow the pattern of previous host cities are watching it play out.

What does this actually mean for the inner east?

The Murarrie, Cannon Hill, and Morningside corridor sits closest to both the Gabba and Chandler venue precincts. These suburbs are not speculation territory anymore. Improved transport links, road upgrades, and the general infrastructure attention that comes with hosting a global event are real and visible.

This doesn't guarantee a price surge in the lead-up. What it does is give the inner east a narrative and a structural attention that most precincts in Brisbane don't have. When buyers are weighing up one suburb against another, the Olympic factor is a legitimate consideration that most comparable markets in Australia cannot point to.

Buyers who made decisions on the Olympic thesis in 2026 are, in the main, glad they did. Not because of the Olympics specifically, but because the fundamentals that attracted them, land supply constraints, proximity to the city, school catchments, character housing stock, held up independently of the Games.

The inner east has always been able to stand on its fundamentals without needing the Olympic headline. The headline is a bonus, not the investment case.
