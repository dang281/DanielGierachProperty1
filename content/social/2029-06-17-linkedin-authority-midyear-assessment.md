## Caption

Halfway through 2029, a few patterns have emerged in Brisbane's inner east that are worth noting.

Supply has remained tight in the premium pockets. Balmoral, Hawthorne, and Bulimba continue to see strong competition for quality stock because the natural limit on housing supply in those suburbs has not changed. When a well-presented home comes to market in those areas, motivated buyers who have been circling for months tend to act.

The middle ring, suburbs like Morningside, Coorparoo, and Camp Hill, has seen more listing volume, which has given buyers more choice and moderated competition in the $900,000 to $1.3 million price range. Vendors in those suburbs who priced realistically and prepared their homes thoroughly have still achieved strong results. Those who tested the market with ambitious price guides have generally had longer campaigns.

The first home buyer and investor markets have both remained active, with the investor pool showing particular interest in properties with dual living potential and suburbs close to Cross River Rail access.

The second half of the year typically sees a quieter winter followed by a spring acceleration. For vendors thinking about listing from September, preparation should start now, not in August.
