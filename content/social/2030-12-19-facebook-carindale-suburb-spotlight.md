## Caption

Carindale heading into summer is one of the more liveable inner east postcodes for residents who are still in Brisbane over the Christmas period. The Westfield centre provides full retail and dining access, the quietness of the residential streets picks up as the city empties, and the suburb's broad tree cover makes the summer heat manageable in ways that the denser inner suburbs do not.

For property buyers, the December and January period in Carindale is worth watching. Stock is low, buyer competition is reduced, and vendors who price correctly can find themselves in a negotiation with a highly motivated buyer who has been in the market for most of the year and needs to finalise a decision before the new year or the new school term.

The Norman Park boundary streets in Carindale's northern section are the suburb's most competed-for positions. The combination of school catchment proximity, larger block sizes, and the established tree cover on the rise approaching Norman Park creates a micro-market within Carindale that outperforms the suburb's median consistently.

For homeowners in the northern section of Carindale who have been considering a campaign, the January-to-March period is the best window to enter the market ahead of the autumn supply. The preparation conversation makes sense to start now.
