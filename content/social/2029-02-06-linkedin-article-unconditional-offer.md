## Caption

Sellers regularly accept a lower unconditional offer over a higher conditional one.

This is not irrational. It is a straightforward trade: certainty now versus risk later.

A conditional offer with a finance clause and a building and pest clause gives the buyer two exits from the contract. They can pull out if their bank declines (finance condition) or if the inspection finds something significant (building and pest condition). The two to three weeks during which those conditions are being satisfied are weeks of real risk for the seller. Settlement day is not guaranteed until the conditions are met or waived.

An unconditional offer removes that risk entirely. The contract is binding from the moment both parties sign. For a seller who needs certainty, who has already bought their next property, or who simply wants the process to be clean, that certainty has genuine value.

For buyers, converting to unconditional is possible if you have pre-approval from your lender and have already done an independent building and pest inspection before you make your offer. Both take a few days of preparation. That preparation can be the difference between winning a property at a lower price or losing it at a higher one.
