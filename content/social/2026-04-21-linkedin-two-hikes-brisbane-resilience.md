## Caption

The RBA lifted the cash rate twice in 2026, in February and March, bringing it to 4.10 per cent. That is 50 basis points added to every mortgage in eight weeks.

Brisbane property prices are at record highs.

That combination surprises people. The assumption was that rate hikes would cool the market. The data says something different, at least for now.

New listings coming to market are down around 3 per cent on the same period last year, according to CoreLogic data for the four weeks to early April. When supply is constrained and buyer demand remains, prices do not soften. The buyers who are active right now are pre-approved, committed, and competing for a smaller pool of stock than they expected to find.

Days on market in Brisbane sits at around 17 days. That is fast. Properties priced correctly in the inner east are not sitting.

What this means practically: sellers who have been waiting for the right window are operating in one right now. Buyers who have been waiting for a rate-driven price correction have not seen one materialise, and the next RBA meeting is 5 May. The board is split. Another hold is possible. Another hike cannot be ruled out.

The market between now and that decision is worth paying attention to.
