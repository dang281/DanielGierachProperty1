## Caption

End of year in Holland Park tends to surface a specific type of buyer: one who made a decision earlier in the year to move and has been waiting for the right property. By mid-December those buyers have seen most of what came to market in spring, and when a well-positioned home comes up now, they tend to act without the deliberation of earlier in the year.

Holland Park's appeal to families is consistent and doesn't depend on the season. Holland Park State School, the White Rock Conservation Area on the southern boundary, and the 7km CBD distance make the suburb function well for families at multiple life stages. The post-war character homes have been evolving through renovation across the suburb, and the streets that looked ordinary five years ago look noticeably better now.

For vendors, listing in December is sometimes dismissed as a quieter window. But the buyers who are active in December in Holland Park are not window shoppers. They have finance in place, they have a settlement timeline in mind, and they're not going to let Christmas get in the way of a decision they've been building toward all year.

The homes that work best in this market present well for the outdoor living season: gardens maintained, outdoor entertaining areas ready, and good natural light. December in Brisbane suits a well-presented property.
