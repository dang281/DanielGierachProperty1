## Caption

Mt Gravatt East sits in a position that buyers coming from the city-fringe suburbs will find genuinely surprising when they first look at price compared to what they are getting. The suburb is elevated and leafy, the streets are quiet, Westfield Garden City is accessible without being immediately adjacent, and Griffith University's Nathan campus is close enough to be relevant to buyers whose household includes academics, students, or those employed at the university.

The Griffith University connection shapes part of the buyer and renter mix in Mt Gravatt East in ways that do not apply to surrounding suburbs. Academic staff who want to live close to the campus, and families of postgraduate students who are buying rather than renting, represent a consistent buyer segment that arrives independently of the standard family-formation or upsizer cycles.

The leafy character of the streets is not accidental. The established trees, particularly on the wider lots in the older sections of the suburb, create a street presence that newer development precincts in the south-east cannot replicate. Buyers who have spent time in the suburb comment on the difference it makes to the experience of walking or cycling the streets.

Value relative to closer-in suburbs is the argument that has sustained buyer interest in Mt Gravatt East across multiple cycles. The suburb's distance from the CBD is real but the gap between its price and the price of comparable housing in Coorparoo or Camp Hill has remained attractive.
