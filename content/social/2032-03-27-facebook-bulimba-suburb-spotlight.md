## Caption

Bulimba is the suburb that defines the upper end of the inner east residential market. Oxford Street is the reference point for what the suburb feels like day to day: independent restaurants, cafes, a cinema, and a farmers' market that draws from across the inner east. But what buyers are actually buying here is a rare combination of river proximity, character housing quality, and a village-scale street life that is genuinely difficult to replicate in Brisbane.

The streets closest to the river, particularly those running off Oxford Street toward the Bulimba ferry terminal, represent the most sought-after addresses in the suburb. Poinciana-lined streets and elevated Queenslanders with heritage character consistently draw the highest attendance and the most competitive buyer interest. Properties in this part of Bulimba are not assessed against a broad inner east comparable pool. They are assessed against each other.

The Bulimba State School catchment and the proximity to Cannon Hill Anglican College are consistently cited by families at open homes. The school factors are considered before buyers make appointments, not during inspections.

March campaigns in Bulimba attract buyers who have made a considered decision to be here. This is not a suburb where buyers are comparing it with Holland Park or Mt Gravatt. The buyer pool is defined and self-selecting.

If you are considering a sale in Bulimba this autumn, I am happy to discuss what the current market looks like for your specific property.
