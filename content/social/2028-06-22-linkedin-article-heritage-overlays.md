## Caption

A heritage overlay on a Brisbane property does not mean the property cannot be renovated. It means Brisbane City Council approval is required for changes to the external character of the building. Internal renovations, new kitchen, updated bathrooms, reconfigured layout, typically proceed without heritage approval at all.

This is a widely misunderstood point, and the misunderstanding cuts both ways. Some buyers walk away from well-priced character homes because they assume a heritage overlay makes the property untouchable. Others buy without checking the overlay and discover that the extension or addition they had envisioned requires a Council approval process that is more involved than a standard development application.

What the overlay actually restricts depends on the specific overlay category and the nature of the proposed work. Changes to the facade, the roofline, significant external materials, or elements that define the property's character contribution to the streetscape are the types of work that typically require heritage approval. The process is not a prohibition. It is a review.

For buyers in Brisbane's inner east, character homes with heritage overlays are a significant part of the available stock. Knowing how to read the overlay, what to investigate before making an offer, and how informed buyers are actually approaching these properties is practical knowledge that changes how you assess the opportunity.

Checking whether a property has a heritage overlay is straightforward through Brisbane City Council's online mapping tools. What to do with that information once you have it is what the article below covers.
