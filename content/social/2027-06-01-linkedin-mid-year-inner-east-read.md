## Caption

Six months into 2027, the inner east market has developed a character that is fairly easy to read if you have been paying attention.

The properties that have sold well share a few things: they were priced honestly from the start, they were presented without apology, and they attracted buyers who had already been searching long enough to recognise value when they saw it. The typical campaign for a well-priced inner east property in the first half of this year ran around three to four weeks before going under contract. That is not slow. That is a functioning market doing what it should do.

The properties still sitting are a different story. Some came to market overpriced relative to what comparable sales were actually showing, and the vendors were waiting for a buyer who would pay a number the market had already moved past. Others were priced reasonably but had presentation issues that photographs could not hide: deferred maintenance, dated kitchens without any styling help, or floorplans that were hard to read in photos. Buyers in the inner east are active, but they are not desperate. They will pass on a property that asks them to do too much imagining.

June is when the year's market personality becomes clear. The properties that sold in the first half tell you what buyers valued most. The ones still available tell you where vendor expectations have not yet caught up to buyer behaviour.

If you have a property in the inner east and you have been watching the market from the sideline, I am happy to give you a straight read on where your property sits. No obligation, just an honest conversation.
