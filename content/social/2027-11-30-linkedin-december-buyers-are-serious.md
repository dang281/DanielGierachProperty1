## Caption

The most common misconception about the December property market is that nobody is buying. In Brisbane's inner east, the opposite is closer to true: the buyers who are still active in December are almost exclusively serious.

The buyers who were browsing in August and September without a firm timeframe have long since dropped out. What remains is a very specific group: people who have finance approved, who know exactly what they want, and who have a hard reason to transact before the new year. A job relocation. A school enrolment deadline. A settlement on their existing home. A financial year consideration.

These buyers are not making offers subject to twelve conditions and then sitting on their finance for six weeks. They are making clean offers and moving quickly.

For sellers, a December listing requires a tight campaign and an honest price. Open home numbers will be lower than spring. But the conversion rate, the proportion of people who inspect and then make offers, is consistently among the highest of any month.

The window between late November and mid-December is narrow and most sellers do not use it. That means the properties that do enter that window are competing with each other, not with a crowded field. Less competition for a motivated, time-pressured buyer pool is not a disadvantage.
