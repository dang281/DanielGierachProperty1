## Caption

Net equity after selling is not sale price minus your loan balance. That calculation is missing most of the costs.

The full cost of selling on an inner east home typically runs between 2.5 and 4 per cent of the sale price. On a $1.4 million property, that is $35,000 to $56,000 leaving the transaction before the proceeds reach the vendor. Agent commission, conveyancing fees, marketing costs, and loan discharge fees all sit on the cost side of the ledger. None of them are reflected in the sale price headline.

The practical consequence appears most often when a vendor is selling to fund the purchase of their next property. A vendor who has calculated their available funds as sale price minus mortgage balance, and used that number to determine what they can spend on the next home, may find their purchasing capacity is meaningfully lower than expected. By the time the proceeds statement from their solicitor arrives on settlement day, the campaign has run, the contracts are exchanged, and the next purchase decision has usually already been made.

There is also a loan discharge cost that regularly surprises vendors. A fixed-rate loan broken before expiry carries a break cost that can be substantial depending on the interest rate environment at the time. A variable-rate loan has a discharge fee that is smaller but still real. Neither figure is part of the standard conversation when a vendor is considering whether to sell.

The article covers how to calculate net proceeds accurately before a campaign begins, what discharge fees look like, and how to have a clearer conversation with your lender before you list.
