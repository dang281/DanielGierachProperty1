## Caption

Holland Park occupies a useful position between Coorparoo to the north and Mount Gravatt to the south, and buyers who know this part of Brisbane understand what that means: good access in multiple directions, strong school catchments, and a housing stock that still offers genuine renovation upside. The suburb does not get the same social media attention as Camp Hill or Norman Park, but the fundamentals are solid and the buyer demand has been growing steadily.

The homes here are mostly 1950s to 1970s construction, the kind of stock that responds well to considered renovation. A well-executed update on a good block in Holland Park can move the price meaningfully, and buyers coming from Camp Hill who have been priced out are arriving with that knowledge. They are looking for a property where they can add value rather than paying a finished premium elsewhere.

School catchments in Holland Park are a genuine asset, and proximity to both the Coorparoo café precinct on Old Cleveland Road and the retail and dining options at Westfield Carindale gives residents daily-use amenity in multiple directions.

For owners who have held here for five years or more, the growth picture has been strong. If you have not had a current appraisal done recently, now is a practical time to understand where your property sits.
