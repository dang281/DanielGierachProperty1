## Caption

Nine months into 2030, a few things in the inner east are worth naming clearly.

Properties that are well-presented, priced to current evidence and brought to market with a competent campaign have been selling. Properties that are overpriced relative to comparable sales, or that cut corners on presentation, have been sitting. That gap, between the properties that sell well and the ones that do not, has been more visible this year than in previous years where a rising market covered a multitude of campaign sins.

Buyer behaviour has shifted through the year. The buyers active in January and February were largely local, known to the market, and decisive. By mid-year, the interstate and international buyer cohort had grown. That group tends to move faster when they find something that fits their brief, because they have fewer alternatives and a firm timeline. The inner east benefited disproportionately from that cohort.

On the supply side, the vendors who achieved the strongest results in 2030 had one thing in common: they came to market when the buyer pool was ready, not when it was convenient for them. Timing relative to market depth mattered more this year than in previous cycles.

Heading into Q4, the practical takeaway is straightforward. Buyers who have been searching without success all year are still active and are increasingly motivated to close before December. Vendors considering a Q4 campaign are entering a market with a compressed timeline and a committed buyer pool. Both of those things are worth understanding before making a decision either way.
