## Caption

Brisbane's inner east has a cluster of suburbs that all look similar on paper but feel very different to buyers on the ground.

Norman Park, Hawthorne, Balmoral, Bulimba, Camp Hill, Coorparoo: each one scores differently depending on what a buyer actually prioritises. School catchments can push prices in one street but not the next. Walkability varies block by block. Transport access is uneven. And land size brings its own set of conversations around long-term potential.

When buying in Brisbane's inner east, which factor matters most to you when choosing a suburb?

## Poll options

- School catchment
- Walkability and lifestyle
- Transport access to the CBD
- Land size and development potential
