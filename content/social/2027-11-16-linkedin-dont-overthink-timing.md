## Caption

Every week I speak to homeowners who are waiting for the perfect time to sell. They are waiting for rates to settle. Waiting for the next suburb data to come out. Waiting to see what the neighbours get. By the time every condition aligns, they have waited through three or four legitimate selling windows.

The reality is that no market signals perfect timing before the fact. What looks like a great selling market in retrospect always had something uncertain happening at the time.

The vendors who consistently out-perform are not the ones who time the market. They are the ones who prepare well and price accurately. A property that is presented in its best condition, correctly priced for where the market is sitting today, will find a buyer in any reasonable market. One that is overpriced and underprepared struggles regardless of when it lists.

In Brisbane's inner east right now, demand remains genuine. The supply of well-prepared properties at realistic prices is not overwhelming. That combination consistently produces good outcomes for sellers who are ready to engage.

Preparation is the variable you control. Timing is not. The vendors who direct their energy toward preparation rather than prediction almost always come out ahead.

If you have been sitting on a decision for months, the honest question is whether anything that changes externally will actually make the difference, or whether the real variable is the preparation and decision you are already able to make.
