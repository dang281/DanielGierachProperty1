## Caption

The comparison between short-term rental platforms like Airbnb and a standard long-term tenancy usually starts with the gross income number. Short-term rental looks better at the top line. The question is what it looks like at the net line.

Short-term rental in Brisbane's inner east adds costs that long-term rental does not: platform fees typically running at 15 to 20 per cent of gross revenue, professional cleaning between every stay, linen and consumables, higher insurance premiums, more frequent maintenance turnover, and the cost of managing bookings either personally or through a short-term management company.

When you run the actual numbers, the net return from a well-managed short-term rental in a desirable inner east property is often within a few thousand dollars of a fully leased long-term tenancy. The gross income advantage is real. The net income advantage is frequently much smaller, and in some cases non-existent.

There are properties and situations where short-term rental clearly makes sense: a property with exceptional location and amenity that commands a premium nightly rate, or an investor who manages it personally and eliminates the management fee. But for most investors who are comparing options based on a gross income estimate from a platform calculator, the net return comparison is the one worth running.

The full article covers the cost structures, the occupancy assumptions, and what the realistic net return comparison looks like for inner east properties.
