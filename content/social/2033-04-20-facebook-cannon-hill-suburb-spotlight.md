## Caption

Cannon Hill sits eight kilometres from the CBD in the inner east and benefits from its position adjacent to Morningside and close to several well-regarded schools. Cannon Hill Anglican College is a significant drawcard for families and contributes to consistent owner-occupier demand that keeps the suburb's property market relatively stable through different market cycles.

DFO Brisbane, located at Cannon Hill, gives residents convenient access to retail without the traffic of major shopping centres, and it also generates local employment that supports the suburb's identity as a practical, liveable place. The Cannon Hill rail station connects the suburb directly to the CBD and to the inner south-east, which is a tangible advantage over comparable suburbs that rely solely on bus.

The housing stock in Cannon Hill is a reasonable mix of post-war homes, some with original character, alongside renovated family homes and more recent medium-density development. Buyers in the family segment tend to be looking for three or four bedrooms with a usable yard, and Cannon Hill still delivers those in reasonable numbers. Current medians reflect the suburb's status as a solid family performer rather than a prestige market, but well-presented homes attract competitive offers.

Investors also participate actively, drawn by the train access and the rental demand generated by school and DFO employment catchments.
