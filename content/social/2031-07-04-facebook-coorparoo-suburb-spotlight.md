## Caption

Coorparoo has a demand driver that most suburbs would envy: the school catchment. Coorparoo State School pulls families from a wide area, and that demand does not switch off in winter. Parents buying for school access plan ahead, and many are already in the market now to secure a property before the next enrolment window.

That consistent family demand gives Coorparoo a floor that purely investor-driven suburbs lack. Old Cleveland Road ties the suburb to the CBD with solid bus frequency, and Coorparoo Square provides the retail convenience that buyers increasingly treat as non-negotiable. The mix of character Queenslanders alongside post-war homes means there is genuine variety in what comes to market, from renovation projects to move-in-ready family homes.

July is a telling month for this suburb. The buyers active right now are not browsing. They have a timeline, often school-related, and they will act on the right property. Listings are thinner than spring, which shifts negotiating dynamics in favour of vendors who are prepared to move.

If you own in Coorparoo and are thinking about the year ahead, a mid-winter appraisal gives you a clear starting point before spring competition changes the landscape.
