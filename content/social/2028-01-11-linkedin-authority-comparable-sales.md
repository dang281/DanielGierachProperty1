## Caption

The comparable sales an agent uses to justify their price estimate tell you everything you need to know about how they work.

An agent who shows you the three highest recent sales in your suburb and says "your property is worth this" is not doing analysis. They are giving you a number that feels good. An agent who shows you a broader set and explains why certain sales are relevant and others are not is doing the actual job.

In the inner east, the gap between the highest and lowest sale on the same street can be $200,000 or more. Aspect, renovation quality, car access, flood risk, and block shape all affect value in ways that comparable sales on paper do not capture.

The purpose of comparable sales is to establish a floor and a ceiling. Within that range, the question is where your property sits and why. That is the conversation worth having with your agent before you agree to a price guide.

A price guide that cannot be defended with clear reasoning is a number someone made up.
