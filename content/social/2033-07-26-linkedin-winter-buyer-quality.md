## Caption

One of the more useful ways to think about the winter market in Brisbane's inner east is through the lens of buyer quality rather than buyer volume. The two are distinct and the distinction matters for vendors.

Volume is highest in spring. The open home numbers, the registered bidder counts, and the enquiry figures all peak in September and October. But volume includes a proportion of buyers who are early in the process, unsure of their capacity, still deciding whether to buy at all. In a busy spring market, that noise is part of the environment.

The buyers active in July and August have typically filtered themselves. The ones who attend winter open homes in Hawthorne, Camp Hill, or Carina are not doing a casual first-look. They have usually pre-approved finance, identified their target area, and decided they want to move before the spring competition intensifies. That buyer has done the work. They know what they want and they know what they can pay.

For the right property with the right preparation, that is a more productive negotiating environment than a busy spring campaign where five buyers are each waiting to see what the others do.

The number of offers at a property matters less than the quality of those offers. Winter provides fewer of the former and often more of the latter.
