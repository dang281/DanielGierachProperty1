## Caption

Clearance rates are a useful number. They are also easy to misread.

A 75% clearance rate in the inner east this autumn sounds strong. And in most conditions, it is. But what that figure does not tell you is which 25% passed in and why. A property that passed in because the vendor had an unrealistic reserve is a very different story from a property that passed in because the buyer pool was simply not there.

What I am seeing across inner east auctions this autumn is segmented, not uniform. Properties in the $1.1M to $1.5M range with good presentation and strong school catchment access are clearing well and in some cases ahead of vendor expectations. Above $2M, the buyer pool is thinner and campaigns are taking longer.

That segmentation matters if you are choosing a method of sale. Auction makes most sense when you have a broad buyer pool that will create genuine competition. If your property sits at a price point or condition where the pool is narrower, a well-run private treaty campaign with a clear price strategy often gets a better outcome.

The most important conversation to have before any campaign is not auction versus private treaty. It is: who are the real buyers for this property and what does that pool look like?

Happy to run through that thinking for your property.
