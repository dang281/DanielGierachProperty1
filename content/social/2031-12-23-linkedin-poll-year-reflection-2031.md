## Caption

With 2031 almost done, it is worth asking a direct question about what actually drove property decisions and thinking this year.

2031 had a number of things running in parallel: the Olympics countdown intensifying as the Games moved within 12 months, a Brisbane market that continued to run through the spring season, rate movements that continued to shape borrowing capacity for buyers, and for many people, simply the reality of their own life circumstances pushing property decisions one way or another regardless of what the market was doing.

Different people will have different answers. The rate question dominated headlines for most of the year. The Olympics narrative was loud, particularly in Brisbane-focused media. But I suspect that for most people who made a real property decision in 2031, the most important factor was personal rather than macroeconomic.

With 2031 almost over, what is the single biggest factor that shaped your property thinking this year?

## Poll options

- Interest rate movements and their effect on borrowing power
- The Brisbane 2032 Olympics countdown intensifying
- Personal circumstances rather than market conditions
- The overall strength or softness of the Brisbane market

## Notes for Daniel

This is a year-end question that is simple, genuine, and invites reflection rather than debate. It works at this time of year because people are in a reflective frame of mind. The "personal circumstances" option is usually the most honest answer for most people and often gets a strong vote.
