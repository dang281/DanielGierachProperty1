## Caption

Most people thinking about selling are not held back by the market. They are held back by one specific concern that they have not fully resolved.

Sometimes it is price: will the result justify the disruption? Sometimes it is timing: what if the market moves after we sell? Sometimes it is the practical side of what comes next, finding somewhere to buy before committing to sell, managing settlement dates, navigating the process with tenants in place.

The decision to sell is rarely made in one moment. It builds up over time, and the concern that sits in the background is usually the one that delays the decision the longest.

What's the thing that would give you most pause?

## Poll options

- Getting the price right
- Timing the market correctly
- Knowing where I'd move next
- The process itself (agents, contracts, settlement)
