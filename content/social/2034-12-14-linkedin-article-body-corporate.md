## Caption

The body corporate levy is only part of the cost. The sinking fund balance tells you more about the true financial health of the building than the annual levy rate.

Body corporate levies in Queensland cover administration, insurance, and maintenance. They range from approximately $2,000 per year for a small townhouse complex to $14,000 or more per year for mid-rise buildings with lifts and facilities.

The sinking fund is the reserve for major capital works: roof replacement, lift maintenance, external painting. A sinking fund with a low balance relative to the age of the building is a financial risk that should be priced into the purchase.

Buyers should request the body corporate records before going unconditional. These records include the sinking fund balance, recent levy history, any special levies, and any known or planned capital works.
