## Caption

The most common mistake I see in September is sellers who decide to list because it is spring, not because they are ready.

The logic is easy to understand. The market feels active. The neighbours listed. The gardens are at their best. September feels like the moment. So the call goes out, the photographer comes through the following week, and the property launches at the start of October with three weeks of preparation behind it instead of eight.

What three weeks of preparation actually looks like: the inside of the property is clear enough for good photography but not genuinely decluttered, the building and pest report has not been done so the seller finds out about the items the buyer's inspector will flag at the same moment the buyer does, and the Form 6 is signed the night before the property goes live because there was not time to read it properly.

The property might still sell. But it will sell for less than it would have with eight weeks of preparation, because buyers can see the difference between a property that has been prepared and one that has been rushed to market. They see it in the photos first, and they confirm it at the open home.

The sellers who regret not listing in September are almost never the ones who waited and prepared well for October. They are the ones who listed in September without being ready and watched their campaign stall.
