## Caption

East Brisbane is two to three kilometres from the CBD. That distance has a specific effect on property demand: it means the suburb sits inside the ring that Brisbane buyers who work in the city have drawn around their search, without carrying the inner city price premium at its full weight. The result is consistent, year-round buyer competition for a suburb that does not have excess stock.

Mowbray Park sits on the river in the suburb's south, providing green space and a ferry terminal connection to the CBD. The Gabba is a short walk, which matters for buyers who value urban walkability and an address embedded in Brisbane's sporting and events geography. The mix of character homes and modern townhouses gives the suburb a buyer profile that ranges from owner-occupiers who want the timber and tin original to buyers who want low maintenance in a well-located suburb.

The supply dynamic in East Brisbane is structural. It is a geographically small suburb. Infill development has been part of the landscape but the character home stock is finite and the owners of those homes do not sell frequently. When a well-positioned character home with a manageable yard comes to market in East Brisbane, the buyer pool responds quickly. Days on market at the premium end are short.

For owners in East Brisbane, the 2031 autumn window will be active. The location speaks for itself.
