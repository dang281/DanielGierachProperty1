## Caption

Coorparoo has one of the strongest school catchment profiles of any inner east suburb outside the riverside pocket. Coorparoo State School, Villanova College, and Loreto College all draw families from across Brisbane, and a large proportion of those families ultimately decide to buy in Coorparoo to remove the commute entirely. That pattern of buyer motivation keeps demand consistent across market cycles.

Old Cleveland Road anchors the suburb's café and retail strip, giving Coorparoo a walkable everyday lifestyle that appeals to both families and professionals. Buyers who want inner east quality without the premium that comes with a Bulimba or Hawthorne address consistently land here and find the trade-off works strongly in their favour.

The housing stock spans character Queenslanders and post-war homes through to a well-established apartment and townhouse market. That breadth of stock means there is rarely a quiet period in Coorparoo. Buyers come from multiple directions, at multiple price points, and they compete for well-presented properties at every level of the market.

Owners who have held a Coorparoo Queenslander or post-war home through the last decade are sitting on significant equity, and the question for many is whether now is the right time to act. The market conditions favour prepared, well-presented stock.
