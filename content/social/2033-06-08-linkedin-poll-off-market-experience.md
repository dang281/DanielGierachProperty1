## Caption

Off-market property sales generate strong opinions on both sides. Buyers who have purchased off-market often describe it as one of the better experiences they've had, because it was simpler and faster with less competition. Vendors who have sold off-market sometimes wonder later whether they left money on the table by not running a full campaign. The reality is that off-market works well in specific circumstances and is the wrong choice in others. Whether you've been through the process or not, I'm curious how you'd approach it. If the right property came up off-market, what would you do?
