## Caption

Hawthorne shares Oxford Street with Bulimba, and for most practical purposes that means access to one of Brisbane's best suburban dining, cafe, and lifestyle precincts is a short walk from most streets in the suburb. Buyers who are choosing between Hawthorne and Bulimba often do so on price and land size rather than lifestyle, because the lifestyle access is largely the same.

The Hawthorne ferry terminal is a point of difference from Bulimba. Having a second City Cat stop within the suburb means that CBD commuting by ferry is accessible without needing to walk to the Bulimba terminal. For buyers who prioritise that commuting option, the Hawthorne terminal is a genuine convenience that factors into the location decision.

Bulimba State School catchment covers most of Hawthorne's streets, and the combination of that catchment and the Oxford Street lifestyle brings the family upsizer buyer profile to this suburb. These buyers have researched the inner east thoroughly, identified Hawthorne as their target, and are prepared to wait for the right property in the right streets.

Character homes in Hawthorne consistently command a premium over units in the suburb, and the demand for that character stock drives a competitive environment at open homes that is reliable across market conditions. Interstate arrivals who have done their research before relocating frequently identify Hawthorne as a target suburb, which adds a second distinct buyer profile to the local pool.
