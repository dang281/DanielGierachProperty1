## Caption

Vendors often come away from a busy open home feeling good about where the campaign is heading. Thirty groups through is a lot of people. But attendance and qualified interest are not the same thing, and confusing the two is where expectations go wrong.

At any given inner-east open I'll see a mix: genuine buyers who are finance-ready, have been looking for several months, and are prepared to move when the right property appears; first-timers who are six to twelve months away from being in a position to buy and are using opens to educate themselves; and neighbours who want to see inside or check the comparable. All three of those groups show up on the attendance count. Only one of them puts an offer on the table.

The number that actually matters is qualified interest. A buyer who has pre-approval, has inspected comparable properties, and asks pointed questions about settlement timing is worth more than ten curious visitors. Eight of those buyers in a room is a genuinely competitive open. Thirty unqualified groups is a busy Saturday with no offers by Sunday afternoon.

What good agents do at opens is not just show people through. It's understanding who is real, following up with the right people, and building the information picture that tells the vendor where genuine demand actually sits. That intelligence is what shapes the campaign strategy in week two.

Open home numbers are a starting point, not a verdict. What comes after the open matters more.
