## Caption

Net equity is not sale price minus loan balance. That calculation is what people expect to find at the end of a sale, and it is almost never the actual number.

Selling costs between 2.5 and 4 per cent of the sale price. On a $1.5 million inner east property, that is $37,500 to $60,000 in costs before you calculate your net proceeds. Those costs include agent commission, marketing, conveyancing, and any outstanding council rates or body corporate levies that need to be settled at completion.

Then there is the mortgage itself. If you are on a variable rate, the discharge process is straightforward. If you are on a fixed rate, you need to confirm your break cost before the campaign launches, not after you have accepted an offer. Break costs on fixed rate loans can be significant, and the number can change based on the movement of wholesale interest rates between when you ask the question and when settlement actually occurs.

The other calculation most vendors miss is CGT exposure on investment properties. If the property is not your principal place of residence, there is a capital gains calculation to work through with your accountant before you commit to a settlement date.

None of this is complicated once you have the numbers in front of you. The problem is that most vendors do not get the full picture until they are already under contract and the surprises feel unavoidable.
