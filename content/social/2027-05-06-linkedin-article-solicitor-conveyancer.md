## Caption

Most buyers in Queensland call their solicitor after they have already signed the contract. That sequence is backwards, and it costs people money.

A solicitor or conveyancer who reviews a contract before you sign can catch things that are extremely difficult to unwind once your signature is on the document. Missing inclusions, finance deadline periods that are too short for your lender to actually turn around an approval, special conditions written in a way that does not protect you the way you think they do. These are not rare edge cases. They come up regularly, and the cost of fixing them after signing is almost always higher than the cost of getting advice before.

The distinction between a solicitor and a conveyancer in Queensland is worth understanding before you make the call. A licensed conveyancer can handle a standard residential property transfer competently and is often less expensive. A solicitor can do everything a conveyancer does, plus advise on contract clauses, handle disputes, and represent you if the transaction becomes complicated. If your purchase is straightforward, a conveyancer may be the right fit. If you are buying a property with a complex title, unusual special conditions, or any element that is outside the ordinary, a solicitor is the more appropriate choice.
