## Caption

Balmoral's property market has a characteristic that distinguishes it from most other inner east suburbs: the buyer who targets Balmoral has usually decided that this is the suburb they want before they start looking at individual properties. The combination of river proximity, foreshore access, school reputation, and the Riding Road café and dining strip creates a suburb identity that buyers buy into as much as they buy the property.

The foreshore along the Bulimba Reach is the suburb's centrepiece. Properties with foreshore access or direct river views from Laidlaw Parade and the surrounding streets operate in a market segment with relatively few comparable sales and buyers who are prepared to pay for position. Those sales, when they occur, set a tone for the suburb's overall pricing story.

Balmoral State School's reputation is one of the consistent pull factors for families. The school's community profile and results attract buyers from across the inner east who are willing to pay the Balmoral premium to secure the enrolment.

December is typically a quiet month for Balmoral open homes, but the buyers who attend in December are unusually focused. They have been in the market for the year and have narrowed their brief to a small number of properties. A well-presented Balmoral home entering the market in December faces limited competition and a motivated buyer audience.
