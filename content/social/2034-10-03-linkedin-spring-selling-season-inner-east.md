## Caption

Spring in Brisbane's inner east is not one market. It is a collection of micro-markets that move at different speeds depending on suburb, property type, and price point.

The character homes in Camp Hill and Hawthorne attract a buyer profile that has usually been watching the market for months. These buyers know comparable sales, they attend multiple inspections, and when they find the right property they move quickly. Competition between two or three of them is what drives a strong result.

The larger blocks in Seven Hills and Murarrie attract a different buyer: people prioritising land and space over the café strip lifestyle. That pool tends to be slightly smaller but more decisive once the property matches their criteria.

In October, the pattern that matters most is buyer volume at open homes. Early spring typically brings the curious buyers alongside the serious ones. The serious buyer is the one who has already spoken to their bank and has finance pre-approval in place. Your agent should be able to tell you, after the first two open homes, which group you are dealing with.

Sellers who list in October typically settle before Christmas. Sellers who wait until late November risk pushing settlement into January, which compresses buyer urgency in a different way.

If you are weighing an October campaign against waiting for autumn, the honest answer depends on your property specifically. Not on the market in general.
