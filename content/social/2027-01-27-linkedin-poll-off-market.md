## Caption

Off-market sales are common in Brisbane's inner east. An agent approaches a homeowner on behalf of a buyer before the property is listed publicly. No open homes, no marketing costs, no campaign to manage.

The seller avoids the process. But they almost always sell for less than a well-run campaign would achieve.

The reason is simple: without competing buyers, there is no price tension. A single buyer does not need to stretch beyond what they think the property is worth. A room of five motivated buyers in an auction tells a different story.

There are situations where off-market makes sense. The seller values privacy above price. The property needs work and would struggle in a public campaign. The seller is testing interest before committing to a campaign. But in most cases, limiting buyer access limits the result.

If you received a strong off-market offer on your home tomorrow, what would you do?
