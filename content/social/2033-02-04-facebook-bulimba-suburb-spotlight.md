## Caption

Bulimba is one of the most consistently desirable suburbs in Brisbane's inner east. The Oxford Street cafe strip, the Bulimba ferry terminal, and the character housing stock combine to create a suburb with a lifestyle identity that is difficult to replicate. Buyers who choose Bulimba know what they are buying. They are not discovering it. They have been planning for it.

The housing stock is predominantly character homes: elevated Queenslanders, post-war timber, and converted workers' cottages. The blocks tend to be narrower and deeper than other inner east suburbs, a legacy of the original subdivision pattern, and buyers have adapted well. The standard of renovation in Bulimba is high, and that sets the expectation for the buyer pool in terms of what a finished home should look like. ⚠️ VERIFY

The Oxford Street strip is the suburb's daily life corridor. Cafes, restaurants, a cinema, specialty retail, and the ferry that connects Bulimba to Norman Park and the broader inner east network. For buyers who value walkability and the ability to live most of their week without a car, this suburb delivers that with a consistency few inner east locations can match.

For vendors in 2033, Bulimba is a market where presentation standards are high and buyer expectations match them. A campaign here rewards vendors who have genuinely invested in their property and are prepared to present it to the standard the suburb commands.
