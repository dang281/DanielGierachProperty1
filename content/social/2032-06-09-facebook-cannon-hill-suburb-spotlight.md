## Caption

Cannon Hill holds a position in the inner east that buyers returning to Brisbane after time elsewhere tend to find straightforward: it is close enough to the river corridor to feel connected to Bulimba and Hawthorne, while its price point has historically offered more land for the dollar. Streets like Cannon Hill Road, Wynnum Road, and Riding Road carry the suburb's main character, and the blocks set back from those arterials are where buyer competition tends to concentrate.

The Cannon Hill Anglican College precinct anchors the suburb's reputation with families, and the school draws enrolments from a wide surrounding area. Cannon Hill train station, on the Cleveland line, is a practical commuting asset that buyers from outside Brisbane often underestimate when researching suburbs at this price point.

The local shopping precinct on Wynnum Road provides everyday retail without requiring a car trip, and that walkability component is increasingly part of the buyer conversation in this part of Brisbane. Homes with north-to-rear aspect and room for outdoor living consistently attract the strongest fields at open inspections, particularly where the original hardwood structure is intact and the renovation has been considered rather than hurried.
