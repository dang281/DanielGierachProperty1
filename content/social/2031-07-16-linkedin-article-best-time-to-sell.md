## Caption

The best time to sell is when you are ready, not when the season changes. A vendor who launches in July with a well-prepared property in the right price range will often outperform one who launches in October unprepared. The market does not reward patience. It rewards preparation.

Mid-winter is the moment vendors ask whether to wait for spring. It is a reasonable question and it has a specific answer: waiting for spring adds buyers to the pool, but it also adds competing listings. The net effect on a well-prepared property is often neutral. What is not neutral is the quality of the property at the time of launch. A vendor who uses the next two months to style, photograph, repair, and price accurately will be better positioned in October than one who waits for October and then begins that process.

The vendors who genuinely should wait are those whose property requires work they have not started yet, or those who are navigating a change in personal circumstances that needs to resolve before a campaign begins. For everyone else, the question is not whether July or October is better. It is whether the property is ready to compete on day one of the campaign, regardless of when that day falls.

Brisbane's inner east market has enough consistent buyer demand across the year that seasonal timing is a secondary consideration for most properties. Preparation is the primary one.

The full article explores the seasonal patterns in the Brisbane market and the variables that actually determine sale outcome.
