## Caption

Murarrie's infrastructure position is one of the strongest in Brisbane's inner east. The Gateway Motorway and Logan Motorway interchange sits on its doorstep, which means buyers who need city access in multiple directions are finding this suburb fits their lives in a way that more-discussed inner east addresses simply cannot match.

The suburb has undergone real transition over recent years. New residential developments are sitting alongside established homes, and the buyer profile arriving has shifted meaningfully toward owner-occupiers, particularly families and buyers priced out of Cannon Hill and Morningside who are not prepared to move further from the city. That shift in buyer type tends to be a reliable leading indicator of price trajectory.

What sells well in Murarrie right now is honest, well-maintained residential stock priced to reflect the suburb's position relative to its neighbours. Buyers here are doing their research and they are comparing Murarrie's access and value proposition directly against Cannon Hill and Morningside. The gap in price with those suburbs is narrowing.

Established homeowners in Murarrie who have held for five or more years are sitting on equity that warrants a serious look at their options. The market is active, buyers are motivated, and supply has not kept pace with demand.
