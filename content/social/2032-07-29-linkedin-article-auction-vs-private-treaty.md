## Caption

The method of sale should follow the property and the buyer pool. Not what the neighbour used, and not what the agent prefers to run.

Auction works best when there is a broad, genuinely competitive buyer pool and the property has broad appeal. The competitive tension of auction day is what drives prices above reserve. Without that depth of competing buyers, auction is just a deadline without a premium.

Private treaty gives buyers time for conditions. Finance clauses, building and pest inspections, and longer settlement periods are all easier to accommodate in a private treaty framework. For properties where the likely buyer pool is narrower, or where the purchase is more complex, private treaty often produces a cleaner transaction.

Expression of interest suits unique properties where the range of buyer values is genuinely wide. When you cannot predict what different buyers will pay because the property has no close comparables, EOI surfaces that range without locking in a price too early.

Each method changes the negotiation dynamic in a specific way. Auction creates urgency and public competition. Private treaty gives both parties more time and flexibility. EOI concentrates decision-making into a defined window without the pressure of a public bidding process.

The conversation worth having before you list is a direct one: which buyer pool is this property most likely to attract, and which method creates the best conditions for that pool to compete?
