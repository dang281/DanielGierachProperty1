## Caption

Hawthorne in mid-September is a suburb that gives buyers a complete picture of what inner-east living looks like at its best. The leafy streets, the ferry, Oxford Street humming with activity, and Lores Bonney Park framing the river come together in a presentation that is hard to find anywhere else in Brisbane at this price point.

The buyer competition in Hawthorne during spring is real, and vendors who understand this use it to their advantage. Buyers who have been tracking the suburb closely through winter arrive in September with clear budgets, clear preferences, and the fatigue of having missed earlier opportunities. They are not going to let the right property pass again. That emotional readiness, combined with genuinely limited supply, creates the conditions that produce strong sale prices.

The mix of character homes and newer builds in Hawthorne serves different buyers simultaneously. The character buyer is usually an owner-occupier with a long hold horizon, buying for lifestyle rather than short-term strategy. The newer-build buyer may be more lifestyle-flexible but values the suburb's amenity infrastructure equally. Both arrive in numbers in September.

Hawthorne State School catchment supports the family dimension. The school is within the suburb, and families with primary-school-age children factor that into their decision with the same weight as the ferry or Oxford Street.

September is the time to act. See the market at danielgierach.com/suburbs/hawthorne
