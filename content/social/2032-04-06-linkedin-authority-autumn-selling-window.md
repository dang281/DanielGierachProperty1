## Caption

April to June 2032 is a distinct selling window. Not because of seasonal momentum alone, but because of what follows it.

The Brisbane Games begin in late July. That means this autumn campaign season is the final full cycle before the city shifts into Games mode. Buyers, sellers, and agents will all be dealing with a different set of conditions from August onward: temporary population influx, short-term accommodation pressure, media attention on Brisbane, and then the post-Games correction period where some of that noise settles.

For sellers in the inner east, this matters in a practical way. A property listed in April or May can realistically settle before end of June. That gives you a clean exit before the uncertainty of what the Games period actually delivers for property values. It's not that the market will collapse after July. It's that nobody can tell you with confidence what conditions will look like in August or September, and that uncertainty is a real factor in negotiations.

What Q2 does offer is a buyer pool that is active, a market with established comparable sales data, and a window where you control the timeline. Once we're into Games period, some of those variables shift.

This isn't a reason to rush a decision. It's a reason to at least consider whether now fits your position.
