## Caption

For most vendors, settlement day is quieter than they expect. The anxiety around it is almost entirely about not understanding what is actually happening.

Here is what occurs. Your solicitor logs into PEXA, Queensland's electronic settlement platform, alongside the buyer's solicitor and the lenders. The transfer documents are lodged, the purchase price is adjusted for rates and body corporate levies, the mortgage is discharged, and the funds land in your account. You are not in a room. You are not signing anything on the day itself. For most sellers, settlement happens while they are doing something else entirely.

What your solicitor needs from you before the day: a signed discharge authority, confirmation of your bank account for the proceeds, and responsiveness when they ask questions in the lead-up week. Delays on the vendor side are one of the more common causes of settlement being pushed back, and they are entirely avoidable.

The one thing you should plan for is your final inspection. The buyer is entitled to inspect the property within one to two business days before settlement, usually to confirm it is in the same condition as when they signed the contract. Leave the property clean, make sure all included items are present, and ensure anything you are keeping is already removed.

I have written a full breakdown of what happens at each stage, from contracts going unconditional through to key handover.
