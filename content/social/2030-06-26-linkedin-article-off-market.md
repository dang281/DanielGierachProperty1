## Caption

The off-market conversation in property is always framed around convenience: less disruption, faster process, no need for open homes. What it is rarely framed around honestly is what the vendor is trading away.

The price a property achieves in a well-marketed campaign is almost always higher than the price it achieves in a private negotiation with a single buyer. The reason is simple: competitive tension drives price. When two or more buyers want the same property and are aware of each other's interest, they make decisions about how much they are willing to pay that they would not make in an uncrowded negotiation.

Off-market removes that dynamic. The buyer who approached you off-market did so because they identified an opportunity to buy before the market gets involved. They may well be prepared to pay a genuinely fair price, but they are not under any pressure to stretch. There is no competing offer to respond to.

The situations where off-market genuinely makes sense for a vendor are narrow but real: where privacy is a genuine concern, where tenants make marketing difficult, where a fast unconditional result matters more than an optimised price. If you are in one of those situations, it can be the right choice.

If you are not in one of those situations, and someone approaches you off-market, the question to ask is: what would a properly run campaign tell me about what this property is worth?

That is a conversation worth having before you accept.
