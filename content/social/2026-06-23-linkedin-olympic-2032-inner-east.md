## Caption

Six years out from Brisbane 2032, the inner east is already positioned differently to the rest of the city.

The Olympic and Paralympic Games are not a single event. They are a decade of infrastructure investment, population growth, and international attention concentrated on a city that was already growing faster than any capital in Australia. The inner east sits between the CBD and the key southern venues, with access to Cross River Rail, the upgraded Gabba precinct, and the arterial corridors that will carry the Games.

Property markets respond to confidence before the infrastructure is complete. The price movements that matter most happen in the years leading up to delivery, not the year of the event.

What that means for inner east homeowners: the period from 2026 to 2030 is when the area's value proposition gets repriced. Schools, transport, proximity to the CBD, and lifestyle assets are all present. The Games give global visibility to a city that investors had undervalued relative to Sydney and Melbourne for two decades.

If you own in Bulimba, Hawthorne, Norman Park, Camp Hill, or Morningside, you are already holding a property in the corridor that benefits. The question for anyone considering their timing is whether six years of continued growth changes their position.
