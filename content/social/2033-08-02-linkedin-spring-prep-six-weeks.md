## Caption

The spring market in Brisbane's inner east typically opens in the second and third weeks of September, when listing stock starts to increase and buyer attendance at open homes picks up noticeably. That timing is well understood. What is less well understood is that the preparation required to be ready for a September launch starts in August, not the week before the first open home.

Six weeks before a campaign launch is a reasonable horizon for the preparation work that makes the difference to presentation. Exterior painting on a Queenslander in Balmoral or Hawthorne takes two weeks to book, complete, and dry fully. Landscaping and garden work on a corner block in Norman Park or Camp Hill takes time to settle and green up before photography. Interior trades, a new bathroom fixture, fresh kitchen cabinet painting, replacing worn carpets, typically run one to three weeks depending on availability.

The vendors who arrive at the first open home with everything done and dry are the ones whose photography looked right. The vendors who rushed the preparation in the final ten days often end up with photography that shows the result of haste.

August is the preparation month for spring. The vendors who use it correctly are the ones who look like they planned it, because they did.
