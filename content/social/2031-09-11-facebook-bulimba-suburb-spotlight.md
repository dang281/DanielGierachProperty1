## Caption

Spring in Bulimba is a different experience from most Brisbane suburbs. The combination of Oxford Street coming alive with warmer-weather dining and the river glittering under September sun creates a suburb presentation that buyers feel before they have even looked inside a property. That ambient quality is a genuine selling advantage and experienced vendors know how to use it.

The supply constraint in Bulimba is as persistent in spring as it is at any other time of year. The suburb simply does not produce enough detached house listings to satisfy the demand that sits waiting. Upsizer buyers from the inner east who have been building equity for years arrive with genuine purchasing power and few properties to choose from. That imbalance does not shift meaningfully even when spring brings some additional listings to market.

Bulimba State School catchment appeals to families who want a primary school that reflects the community the suburb has built around it. The ferry at the Bulimba terminal gives owners a commute option that is genuinely enjoyable, not just functional. Those are the kinds of suburb attributes that make buyers attach emotionally to an address before they have settled on a specific property.

For vendors thinking about September, Bulimba's scarcity is your asset. The buyers in this suburb are not comparison-shopping across fifteen similar homes. They may be choosing between two or three.
