## Caption

Carina is a suburb that buyers come to when they have done the maths and decided that size and practicality matter more than address.

That is not a criticism. It is the reason Carina has held consistent buyer demand across different market conditions. Sandwiched between Carina Heights to the north and Carindale to the south, with the Gateway Motorway accessible and the Carindale retail precinct close, the suburb covers the practical requirements for most family households without asking them to compromise on block size.

Post-war housing stock on lots that consistently run over 600 square metres in the established residential streets gives buyers the room that Camp Hill and Norman Park now price many of them out of. Carina State School serves a broad primary catchment, and the suburb's positioning means the transition to secondary schooling at nearby options is straightforward.

The buyers arriving in Carina have often come from Coorparoo or Camp Hill. They know the inner east. They have made a considered call that the land and the price point in Carina represent better value for their current life stage than pushing into the premium suburbs.

For owners in Carina, that buyer profile is a strong one to be selling into.
