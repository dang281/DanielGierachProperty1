## Caption

The choice between auction and private treaty is not about what the vendor prefers. It is about which method best matches the buyer pool for that specific property.

A property with broad appeal in a competitive price bracket, where 15 to 25 registered buyers are a realistic expectation, benefits from the auction process. The method creates genuine competition, removes cooling-off rights for the winning bidder, and produces an unconditional result on the day. When the buyer pool is wide, auction earns its place.

A unique property is a different question. A home with an unusual configuration, a specific lifestyle requirement, or a narrow target market may only attract 3 to 5 serious buyers regardless of how well the campaign is run. In that case, an auction creates pressure without creating competition. Expressions of interest or private treaty allows buyers the time to get their finances and due diligence in order, which often matters more than the theatre of the auction room.

The mistake some vendors make is choosing a method based on what worked for a neighbour or what they are comfortable with. The method should follow the property. A competent agent should be explaining why a particular approach fits this property and this buyer pool, not defaulting to a house view.

The full article walks through how to think about this decision and what questions to ask your agent before signing a sales authority.
