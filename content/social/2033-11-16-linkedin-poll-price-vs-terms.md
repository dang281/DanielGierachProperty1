## Caption

Most vendors say price without hesitation when I ask this question. But when they are actually looking at two offers side by side, the answer often changes. A failed conditional sale does not just cost the vendor the gap between the two offers. It costs them time to re-launch, re-marketing costs, the psychological reset of starting again, and often a lower result the second time around because the property has been seen to fail on the market. Certainty has a real financial value that most vendor conversations do not account for until it becomes relevant. The vendor who dismisses a clean unconditional offer in favour of a higher conditional one is making a calculation that involves probability, not just arithmetic. When you think about it from that angle, which matters more to you?
