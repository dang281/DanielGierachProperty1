## Typography

**Headings:** Serif typeface — dignified, editorial, premium. Use Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, or Canva's equivalent serif. Weight: Regular or Light for large sizes, Semibold for small.

**Body:** Clean sans-serif. Inter, DM Sans, or Canva's default sans. Weight: Regular 400 for body, Semibold 600 for emphasis.

**Data / Stats:** Bold sans, oversized. Let the number breathe — white space around stats is intentional. Pair with small label text in cream-dim.

**Hierarchy:**
1. One dominant element (stat, headline, suburb name)
2. 2–4 supporting bullet points or subheading
3. Footer (name, contact, Ray White attribution)

## Voice and Tone

- **Australian English.** Colour, not color. Authorise, not authorize.
- **No em dashes.** Use a comma, full stop, or rewrite the sentence.
- **No AI slop.** No "in today's dynamic market", "I'm passionate about", "your dream home", "exciting opportunity".
- **Suburb-specific always beats generic.** "The streets north of Wynnum Road" beats "Morningside property".
- **Data-led.** Cite a source if you use a figure. "According to CoreLogic" or "REIQ data for Q1 2026" — name the source or don't use the number.
- **No price predictions.** Daniel does not make his own market forecasts. He can cite forecasts from named institutions (CoreLogic, REIQ, major bank economists).
- **Not salesy.** Never use "act now", "don't miss out", "limited time". Daniel's authority comes from being the most informed person in the room, not the loudest.

## Social Media Visual Style

### News-Reactive Posts (RBA, legislation, ABS data, market updates)

**When to use:** Any post reacting to a specific announcement or data release — publish within 24–48 hours of the news dropping.

**Layout:**
- Background: Charcoal (`#0a0806`) or deep navy
- Headline zone: 1 bold stat or key fact in Gold (`#f5d07a`), large serif or bold sans
- Body zone: 3–5 bullet points in Cream (`#f0ece4`), regular weight, clear line spacing
- Source line: Tiny, cream-dim — "Source: [organisation]"
- Footer: `Daniel Gierach · Ray White Bulimba · danielgierach.com`
- **Canva template:** Use brand kit `kAGjS7yZLr8` when generating

**Dimensions:** 1080x1080px (square) for LinkedIn and Facebook feed

**Example visual for an RBA rate hold:**
- Headline (gold): "RBA holds at 4.10%"
- Bullets (cream): What this means for Brisbane buyers' borrowing capacity, what the market is pricing in for next meeting, Daniel's one-line local take
- Source: "Source: Reserve Bank of Australia, May 2026"

### Suburb Spotlight Posts

**Layout:**
- Light or dark background (alternate for variety)
- Suburb name large in serif, dominant
- 1 key data point in gold (median price, days on market, or a local fact)
- 2–3 sentences of context in body
- Property or suburb lifestyle photo if available (streetscape, café strip, river)
- Footer with Daniel's name and contact

### LinkedIn Poll Posts

- Simple, clean layout — polls are about the question, not the design
- Question in large, legible sans
- 4 answer options clearly listed
- Keep design minimal so it doesn't distract from the engagement hook
- Small branding in footer — name + Ray White Bulimba

### Facebook Market Updates

- More visual than LinkedIn — can use property photos, suburb aerials
- Lead with the local angle: "What this means for [suburb] sellers"
- Slightly warmer tone than LinkedIn (same honesty, slightly more conversational)

## What the Visual Brief Must Include

Every piece of content produced by the Social Media Agent must have a **Visual Brief** section in the markdown file. The brief tells the person creating the visual (or the AI) exactly what to produce.

A complete Visual Brief includes:
1. **Format:** e.g. "1080x1080 square, dark background"
2. **Headline text:** The exact text for the hero element (stat, quote, question)
3. **Body text:** The exact bullet points or sentences to appear on the graphic
4. **Footer text:** Always `Daniel Gierach · Ray White Bulimba · danielgierach.com`
5. **Style:** Which template type (news-reactive / suburb spotlight / poll)
6. **Image direction:** If a photo is needed — what to show (e.g. "aerial shot of Morningside", "street with character homes", "no photo needed — text only")
7. **Source line (if applicable):** e.g. "Source: CoreLogic, April 2026"
